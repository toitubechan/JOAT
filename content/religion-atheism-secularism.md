## Card: What it is
image: religionAtheismSecularismHero
**Atheism** is the absence of belief in any god or gods, and **secularism** is the principle of keeping religion and government (or public life) separate. A large and growing share of people worldwide are non-religious. Understanding these worldviews completes the picture of how humans approach questions of belief — including choosing not to hold religious beliefs. This is a respectful, descriptive overview.

## Card: Why it matters
Non-religious people make up a significant and growing portion of humanity, so understanding atheism and secularism is part of grasping the full landscape of belief. Secularism also shapes how many modern societies are governed. Knowing what these terms mean — and what they don't — fosters mutual understanding between religious and non-religious people alike.

## Card: Key terms
- **Atheism**: the absence of belief in any god or gods.
- **Agnosticism**: holding that we can't know whether a god exists.
- **Secularism**: separating religion from government and public life.
- **Non-religious**: not following any organized religion.

## Card: Atheism and agnosticism
image: religionAtheismSecularismAtheismAndAgnosticismHero
**Atheism** is the lack of belief in a god or gods — atheists don't hold religious beliefs about the divine. It's distinct from **agnosticism**, which is the view that we can't know (or don't know) whether a god exists. Some people identify as one, some as both, and many simply consider themselves **non-religious** without a strong label. Importantly, lacking religious belief doesn't mean lacking values, morality, or meaning — non-religious people find these through other sources.

## Card: Secularism
**Secularism** is a separate idea: the principle of keeping religion and government (or public institutions) separate, so the state doesn't favor or impose any religion, and people are free to follow any faith or none. Many modern societies are secular in this sense. Secularism isn't the same as atheism — it's about how religion and public life relate, and it can be supported by religious and non-religious people alike, protecting freedom of (and from) religion.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: confusing atheism (no belief in gods) with agnosticism (can't know) — they differ; thinking non-religious people lack morality or meaning (they find these through other sources); conflating secularism (separating religion and state) with atheism (a personal belief stance); and assuming everyone is religious. A large, growing share of people are non-religious, a real part of the belief landscape.

## Card: Quick recap
Atheism is the absence of belief in any god, distinct from agnosticism (holding we can't know if a god exists); many people are simply non-religious. Lacking religious belief doesn't mean lacking morality or meaning. Secularism is a separate idea — keeping religion and government separate — supported by religious and non-religious people alike. Together, these complete the landscape of how humans approach belief.
