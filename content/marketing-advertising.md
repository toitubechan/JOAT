## Card: What it is
image: marketingAdvertisingHero
**Advertising** is paying to place your message in front of an audience — on TV, online, social media, search engines, and more. It's the most familiar form of marketing: paying for attention rather than earning it. Understanding the basics — including modern targeted digital advertising — helps you use it effectively and understand the ads that surround you daily.

## Card: Why it matters
Advertising is a major way businesses reach customers, and modern digital advertising is hugely powerful and precisely targeted. Understanding how it works helps you advertise effectively (and avoid wasting money), and understand why you see the ads you do. Whether you're marketing or just a consumer, grasping advertising is essential modern literacy.

## Card: Key terms
- **Advertising**: paying to place a message before an audience.
- **Targeting**: showing ads to specific, relevant people.
- **Reach vs. relevance**: how many see it vs. how well it fits them.
- **Return on investment (ROI)**: whether the ad earns more than it costs.

## Card: Paying for attention
image: marketingAdvertisingPayingForAttentionHero
Unlike content marketing (which *earns* attention), **advertising** *pays* for it — you pay to place your message where your audience will see it. The big modern shift is **targeting**: digital advertising can show ads to specific people based on their interests, demographics, and behavior, rather than broadcasting to everyone. This makes ads far more relevant and efficient than old mass advertising — you can reach precisely the people most likely to care, rather than paying to reach everyone.

## Card: Making advertising work
Effective advertising isn't just about **reach** (how many people see it) but **relevance** — reaching the *right* people with a message that resonates. The key measure is **return on investment (ROI)**: does the advertising bring in more value (customers, sales) than it costs? Good advertising targets the right audience, with a compelling message, and measures whether it's actually paying off. Spending on ads without targeting well or measuring results is how marketing budgets get wasted.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: chasing reach (lots of views) over relevance (the right people); not targeting well, so ads reach uninterested people; failing to measure ROI (so you don't know if it's working); expecting ads alone to fix a weak product or offer; and continuing to spend on ads that aren't paying off. Effective advertising targets the right people, with a resonant message, and measures whether it actually delivers a return.

## Card: Quick recap
Advertising is paying to place your message before an audience — paying for attention rather than earning it. Modern digital advertising's power is targeting: reaching specific, relevant people based on interests and behavior, not broadcasting to everyone. Effective advertising prioritizes relevance over raw reach and measures return on investment (ROI) — whether it brings more value than it costs. Target well, resonate, and measure, or budgets get wasted.
