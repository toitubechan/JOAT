## Card: What it is
image: howworksGpsHero
**GPS** (Global Positioning System) lets devices figure out their location anywhere on Earth, using signals from a network of satellites orbiting the planet. It's how your phone's maps know where you are. Understanding the basic idea reveals the clever science behind a technology we rely on daily for navigation and far more.

## Card: Why it matters
GPS underpins navigation, maps, ride-hailing, delivery, and countless apps and services. Understanding how it works demystifies a technology we depend on constantly, and reveals it's based on satellites and precise timing — not magic. It's a great example of advanced science quietly powering everyday convenience.

## Card: Key terms
- **GPS**: a system using satellites to determine location.
- **Satellite**: an object orbiting Earth, here broadcasting signals.
- **Triangulation**: finding a position using distances from several points.
- **Signal timing**: using how long signals take to travel.

## Card: Satellites and signals
image: howworksGpsSatellitesAndSignalsHero
A network of **satellites** orbits Earth, each constantly broadcasting signals that include its position and the precise time. Your device (like a phone) listens for these signals. By measuring **signal timing** — how long each signal took to arrive — the device calculates its distance from each satellite (since the signals travel at a known speed). It needs signals from several satellites at once to work.

## Card: Finding your position
With distances from several satellites, the device uses **triangulation** (more precisely, a 3D version) to pinpoint its location. Imagine: if you know your exact distance from several known points, there's only one spot you can be. Each satellite distance narrows it down, and combining several gives your precise position on Earth. The device does this calculation instantly and continuously, so your location updates as you move.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking GPS satellites *track* you or know where you are (your *device* receives their signals and calculates its own position — the satellites just broadcast); imagining it works by magic; not realizing it needs several satellites at once; and not knowing it relies on extremely precise timing. GPS is your device figuring out its own location from satellite signals and timing.

## Card: Quick recap
GPS works using a network of satellites that broadcast their position and precise time. Your device measures how long each signal took to arrive, calculating its distance from several satellites, then uses triangulation to pinpoint its exact location. Importantly, your device calculates its *own* position from the signals — the satellites don't track you. It's precise timing and geometry, powering everyday navigation.
