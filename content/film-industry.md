## Card: What it is
image: filmIndustryHero
The **film industry** is the business of making, distributing, and profiting from films and video. Behind the art is a massive global business with its own economics, players, and dynamics — now being reshaped by streaming. Understanding the basics reveals how films get funded, made, and seen, and the business realities behind the entertainment we enjoy. This is a general overview.

## Card: Why it matters
Film and video are huge global industries shaping culture and entertainment. Understanding the business side reveals how movies actually get made and reach audiences, why certain films get made (and others don't), and how streaming is transforming everything. It deepens understanding of the media we consume and the realities behind a famously glamorous but tough business.

## Card: Key terms
- **Film industry**: the business of making and distributing films.
- **Financing**: how films get the money to be made.
- **Distribution**: how films reach audiences.
- **Streaming**: online platforms transforming the industry.

## Card: How films get made and seen
image: filmIndustryHowFilmsGetMadeAndHero
Filmmaking is expensive, so **financing** is central — films need funding (from studios, investors, or other sources) before they're made, and the need to recoup that money shapes which films get made. Once made, **distribution** gets films to audiences — historically via cinemas, then home video and TV, and now increasingly streaming. The business runs on recouping costs and making profit, which is why commercial considerations heavily influence what gets produced, not just artistic merit.

## Card: A transformed landscape
**Streaming** has dramatically reshaped the film and video industry. Where films once relied on cinema releases and physical sales, much viewing now happens through streaming platforms, changing how content is funded, distributed, and consumed. This has shifted power, business models, and what kinds of content get made. Meanwhile, like music, the internet has also lowered barriers, letting independent creators reach audiences directly (e.g., online video). The industry is in significant flux, more accessible to enter yet still tough to succeed in.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking films get made purely on artistic merit (financing and commercial considerations heavily shape what's produced); ignoring how expensive and business-driven filmmaking is; not realizing how much streaming has transformed the industry; and imagining it's easy to succeed in (it's famously tough). The film industry is a massive, business-driven, rapidly changing field behind the entertainment we enjoy.

## Card: Quick recap
The film industry is the business of making, distributing, and profiting from film and video. Filmmaking is expensive, so financing is central, and the need to recoup costs shapes which films get made — commerce, not just art, drives production. Distribution gets films to audiences, increasingly via streaming, which has transformed how content is funded and consumed. The internet has also lowered barriers for independent creators. It's a massive, fast-changing, tough business.
