## Card: What it is
image: newUsedHero
One of the first car-buying decisions is **new versus used**. Each has clear trade-offs in price, reliability, and value. Understanding them helps you choose what fits your budget and needs. (General guidance for a major purchase; not financial advice.)

## Card: Why it matters
A car is one of the biggest purchases most people make, and the new-vs-used choice hugely affects the cost. Many people overspend on new cars without realizing the value trade-off, or fear used cars unnecessarily. Understanding both helps you make a smart, money-saving decision suited to you.

## Card: Key terms
- **Depreciation**: the loss of a car's value over time.
- **New car**: bought brand new from a dealer.
- **Used car**: previously owned.
- **Value retention**: how well a car holds its worth.

## Card: The depreciation factor
image: depreciationDrop
The biggest financial fact: **depreciation**. A new car loses a large chunk of its value in the first few years — often a significant percentage the moment you drive it off the lot. This is why a used car a few years old can offer most of the function for far less money. Someone else absorbed the steepest depreciation. For value, used often wins.

## Card: The trade-offs
**New cars** offer the latest features, full warranty, no prior wear, and that "new" appeal — but at a premium and with steep early depreciation. **Used cars** are much cheaper and have already taken the depreciation hit, but may have wear, a shorter or no warranty, and unknown history (so inspection matters). The right choice depends on your budget, priorities, and tolerance for risk versus newness.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: buying new without considering the massive depreciation cost; assuming used cars are unreliable (a well-chosen used car can be excellent); overstretching the budget for newness; and not factoring depreciation into the "value" of a purchase. Weigh the real trade-offs rather than defaulting to new for its appeal.

## Card: Quick recap
New cars offer the latest features and full warranty but lose value fast through depreciation, especially early on. Used cars are far cheaper and have already absorbed that depreciation, though they need careful inspection. For value, used often wins; for newness and warranty, new appeals. Weigh the trade-offs against your budget and priorities.
