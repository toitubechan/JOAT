## Card: What it is
image: immuneSystemHero
Your **immune system** is the body's defense force — a network of cells and barriers that detects and fights off harmful invaders like bacteria and viruses. It works around the clock, mostly without you noticing, protecting you from countless threats every single day.

## Card: Why it matters
This system is why you recover from most illnesses and stay healthy despite constant exposure to germs. Understanding it explains how vaccines work, why hygiene helps, and why rest and nutrition matter when you're ill. It's an invisible guardian worth appreciating and supporting.

## Card: Key terms
- **Immune system**: the body's defense against invaders.
- **Pathogen**: a harmful germ like a bacterium or virus.
- **White blood cells**: immune cells that fight invaders.
- **Antibody**: a protein that targets a specific pathogen.

## Card: Layers of defense
image: immuneDefenseLayers
Defense comes in layers. First, **barriers** like skin and mucus block invaders from getting in. If a **pathogen** breaks through, **white blood cells** hunt it down. The body can also make **antibodies** — proteins tailored to a specific invader — to neutralize it. It's a layered fortress, not a single wall.

## Card: Memory and immunity
A remarkable feature: the immune system **remembers**. After fighting a particular pathogen, it keeps a memory so it can respond faster next time — which is why you often catch some illnesses only once. **Vaccines** use this safely: they train the immune system to recognize a threat *without* you having to get sick first.

## Card: Common mistakes
A common myth is that you can dramatically "boost" immunity with a single product — the system is complex and isn't supercharged by quick fixes. Another is ignoring basics that genuinely help: sleep, nutrition, and hygiene. And many misunderstand vaccines, which simply teach the immune system to recognize a threat in advance.

## Card: Quick recap
The immune system defends you in layers: barriers like skin, white blood cells that hunt invaders, and antibodies tailored to specific threats. It remembers past invaders, which is how immunity and vaccines work. No single product magically boosts it — but sleep, nutrition, and hygiene genuinely support it.
