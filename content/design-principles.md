## Card: What it is
image: designPrinciplesHero
**Design principles** are the foundational guidelines that make design effective — things like balance, contrast, alignment, and hierarchy. They explain *why* some designs look good and work well, and others don't. Far from being mysterious artistic talent, good design largely follows learnable principles. Understanding them helps anyone create better visual work and see design more clearly.

## Card: Why it matters
Good design is everywhere and profoundly affects how things look, feel, and function — yet many think it's just innate "talent." Understanding that design follows learnable principles is empowering: it means you can create better visuals and judge design more sharply. These principles apply across all visual work, from documents to websites to art. They're the foundation of visual creativity.

## Card: Key terms
- **Design principle**: a guideline for effective visual design.
- **Hierarchy**: guiding the eye to what matters most.
- **Contrast**: differences that create emphasis and clarity.
- **Alignment & balance**: arranging elements for order and stability.

## Card: Core principles
image: coreDesignPrinciplesHero
A few foundational **design principles**: **Hierarchy** — arranging elements so the eye is guided to what matters most first (size, position, and emphasis create order of importance). **Contrast** — using differences (in size, color, weight) to create emphasis, clarity, and visual interest. **Alignment** — lining elements up creates order and a clean, intentional look. **Balance** — distributing elements so the design feels stable, not lopsided. These (and others like spacing and repetition) underlie effective design.

## Card: Principles make design work
The key insight: good design isn't random or purely intuitive — it largely follows these **principles**. When a design looks clean, clear, and pleasing, it's usually applying hierarchy, contrast, alignment, and balance well. When something looks "off" or amateurish, it often violates them (cluttered, no clear focus, misaligned, no contrast). Because they're learnable guidelines, anyone can improve their design by understanding and applying them — design is a skill, not just inborn talent.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking good design is just innate talent (it largely follows learnable principles); ignoring hierarchy (so nothing guides the eye); avoiding contrast (making designs flat and unclear); poor alignment (looking messy and unintentional); and cluttering without balance or space. Applying core principles — hierarchy, contrast, alignment, balance — is what makes design effective, and it's learnable by anyone.

## Card: Quick recap
Design principles are learnable guidelines that make design effective: hierarchy (guiding the eye to what matters most), contrast (differences creating emphasis and clarity), alignment (order and intention), and balance (stability), among others. Good design largely follows these — it's a skill, not just innate talent. When something looks clean and clear, it's applying them well; when it looks "off," it usually violates them. Anyone can improve by learning them.
