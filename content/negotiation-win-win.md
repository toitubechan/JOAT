## Card: What it is
image: winWinNegotiationHero
A **win-win negotiation** aims for an outcome where both sides come away satisfied, rather than one winning at the other's expense. Far from being naive, this approach often produces better, more durable agreements — and preserves the relationship for future dealings.

## Card: Why it matters
The "I win, you lose" mindset damages relationships and often misses better solutions. In ongoing relationships (colleagues, clients, employers), how you negotiate today affects every future interaction. Win-win thinking finds bigger, more creative outcomes and keeps people willing to work with you. It's both kinder and, usually, smarter.

## Card: Key terms
- **Win-win**: an outcome satisfying both sides.
- **Zero-sum thinking**: assuming one side's gain is the other's loss.
- **Expanding the pie**: finding extra value for both sides.
- **Trade-offs**: exchanging things each side values differently.

## Card: Beyond zero-sum
Many people fall into **zero-sum thinking** — assuming every gain for them is a loss for the other side. But many negotiations aren't fixed pies. **Expanding the pie** means finding extra value: things one side values cheaply that the other values highly. By understanding each side's real interests, you can often create more total value than fighting over a fixed amount.

## Card: Trade what you value differently
image: tradeValueHero
The engine of win-win deals is **trade-offs** — exchanging things the two sides value differently. You might happily give up something that costs you little but matters a lot to them, in exchange for something that matters a lot to you. Both come out ahead. This is why understanding each side's priorities, not just demands, unlocks better agreements.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: defaulting to zero-sum thinking and fighting over a fixed pie; trying to "win" at the other's expense, damaging the relationship; not exploring trade-offs that could benefit both; and hiding all your interests so the other side can't find creative solutions. Win-win needs some openness and curiosity about what each side truly values.

## Card: Quick recap
Win-win negotiation seeks outcomes satisfying both sides, which is often smarter and more durable than winning at someone's expense. Move beyond zero-sum thinking: expand the pie by finding extra value, and use trade-offs — exchanging things each side values differently. Understanding both sides' real priorities unlocks better deals and preserves relationships.
