## Card: What it is
image: passwordManagerHero
A **password manager** is an app that securely stores all your passwords and can create strong, unique ones for every account. You remember just one **master password** to unlock it; it remembers everything else. It solves the impossible task of recalling dozens of long, unique passwords.

## Card: Why it matters
The advice to use a long, unique password for every account is impossible to follow by memory — which is why people reuse weak ones. A password manager makes the secure way the *easy* way. Security experts widely recommend them as one of the best practical steps for protecting your accounts.

## Card: Key terms
- **Password manager**: an app that stores and generates passwords.
- **Master password**: the single password that unlocks your vault.
- **Vault**: the encrypted store holding all your passwords.
- **Autofill**: the manager entering login details for you.

## Card: How it works
image: encryptedVault
The manager keeps your passwords in an encrypted **vault**, locked by one strong **master password** that only you know. It can generate a unique, random password for each site and **autofill** logins for you. So you get maximum security — long, unique passwords everywhere — while only memorizing one.

## Card: Are they safe?
A common worry: isn't storing all passwords in one place risky? In practice, reputable managers encrypt your vault so even the company can't read it, and the alternative — reused weak passwords — is far riskier. The key is choosing a trusted manager and making the master password very strong and never reused.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: avoiding password managers out of unfamiliarity while continuing risky reuse; choosing a weak or reused master password (it must be your strongest); not enabling extra protection on the manager itself; and writing the master password somewhere insecure. Done right, the benefits vastly outweigh the risks.

## Card: Quick recap
A password manager stores and generates strong, unique passwords, so you only memorize one master password. It makes secure habits effortless and is widely recommended. Reputable ones encrypt your vault so even they can't read it. Just protect the master password fiercely — it's the one key to everything.
