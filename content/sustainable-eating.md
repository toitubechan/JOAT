## Card: What it is
image: sustainEatingHero
**Eating sustainably** means making food choices that reduce environmental impact — without needing a drastic diet overhaul. Food production is a major source of emissions, water use, and land use, so what and how we eat matters. Small, realistic shifts can meaningfully lighten your footprint.

## Card: Why it matters
Food is a surprisingly large part of most people's environmental impact. The good news is that sustainable eating often overlaps with healthier eating and saving money (especially by wasting less). Understanding the basics lets you make easy, realistic changes rather than feeling you must transform your whole diet.

## Card: Key terms
- **Food miles**: distance food travels to reach you.
- **Plant-forward**: eating more plants, not necessarily all-vegetarian.
- **Food waste**: edible food thrown away (a big hidden impact).
- **Seasonal and local**: food in season and grown nearby.

## Card: The biggest food levers
image: sustainEatingTheBiggestFoodLeversHero
Two changes have outsized impact. First, **reducing food waste** — binned food wastes all the resources used to grow, transport, and store it, so this is a huge, easy win. Second, eating more **plant-forward** — animal products (especially red meat) generally have a much larger footprint than plants, so even shifting *some* meals toward plants helps significantly. You don't have to go fully vegetarian.

## Card: Smaller factors
Beyond the big two, smaller factors include choosing **seasonal and local** food where practical (though **food miles** matter less than people assume — what you eat usually matters more than how far it traveled), and reducing heavily processed and over-packaged foods. These help, but reducing food waste and eating more plants are where the real impact lies.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: thinking you must go fully vegetarian or vegan to make a difference (any shift helps); overrating food miles while underrating *what* you eat; ignoring food waste, a huge easy win; and treating sustainable eating as all-or-nothing. Focus on wasting less and eating somewhat more plants — realistic shifts beat unsustainable extremes.

## Card: Quick recap
Eating sustainably is mostly about two levers: reducing food waste (a big, easy win) and eating more plant-forward (animal products, especially red meat, have a larger footprint). Local and seasonal help a little, but what you eat matters more than food miles. You needn't go fully vegetarian — realistic shifts add up, and often save money and improve health too.
