## Card: What it is
image: foodStorageHero
Storing food properly — in the fridge, freezer, or cupboard — keeps it safe and fresh for as long as possible. Good storage slows the bacteria and spoilage that make food go off, reduces waste, and saves money. A few simple rules cover most of what you need to know.

## Card: Why it matters
Poor storage is a major cause of both food poisoning and food waste. Knowing how and where to store things, and how long they keep, means fewer binned groceries and safer meals. It's a quietly valuable skill that pays off every single week in money saved and illness avoided.

## Card: Key terms
- **Refrigeration**: keeping food cold to slow bacteria.
- **Freezing**: pausing spoilage by keeping food very cold.
- **Use-by date**: a safety deadline — don't eat after it.
- **Best-before date**: a quality guide — food may still be safe after.

## Card: Fridge and freezer basics
image: rawMeatBottom
Keep the fridge cold enough (around 5°C / 40°F or below) to slow bacteria, and don't overfill it so air can circulate. Store raw meat at the bottom, covered. **Freezing** essentially pauses spoilage — great for extending life or saving leftovers — though label and date items, since freezing isn't forever and quality slowly drops.

## Card: Use-by vs. best-before
A vital distinction. **Use-by** is about *safety* — don't eat food after its use-by date, even if it looks fine, especially for risky foods. **Best-before** is about *quality* — food is often still safe to eat after it, just possibly past its peak. Confusing these two leads to either risk or needless waste.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: a fridge set too warm; overfilling it so air can't circulate; storing raw meat above other foods; treating use-by and best-before as the same (causing waste or risk); leaving food uncovered; and not cooling leftovers before fridging large amounts. Good storage habits prevent both illness and waste.

## Card: Quick recap
Store food to keep it safe and cut waste: keep the fridge cold and not overfilled, raw meat covered at the bottom, and use the freezer to extend life. Know the difference — use-by is a safety limit, best-before is about quality. Right storage saves money and prevents illness every week.
