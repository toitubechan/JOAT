## Card: What it is
image: taxesHero
**Taxes** are mandatory payments individuals and businesses make to the government. They fund the shared things society runs on — roads, schools, hospitals, defense, public services. In short, taxes are how a country collectively pays for the things no single person could fund alone.

## Card: Why it matters
Taxes quietly shape your take-home pay, the price of what you buy, and the services around you. Understanding the basics helps you read your payslip, avoid nasty surprises, and not overpay. This is general knowledge — for your specific situation, a qualified tax professional is the right source.

## Card: Key terms
- **Tax**: a mandatory payment to the government.
- **Revenue**: the money governments collect, largely from taxes.
- **Taxpayer**: a person or business that pays tax.
- **Tax authority**: the agency that collects taxes.

## Card: Where the money goes
image: taxFundsShared
Tax **revenue** funds collective goods: public roads, schools, healthcare, emergency services, courts, and welfare. These are things that benefit everyone but are hard to charge for individually. Taxes are essentially the membership fee for a functioning society — pooled money spent on shared needs.

## Card: How taxes reach the government
Taxes are collected in many ways. Some come straight out of your wages before you're paid. Some are added to things you buy. Others you calculate and pay yourself. A **tax authority** oversees the rules and collection. The variety can feel confusing, but each tax is just a different point at which money is gathered.

## Card: Common mistakes
A common misunderstanding is treating tax as optional or ignorable — unpaid taxes can lead to penalties and serious trouble. Another is not understanding your own payslip, so you don't notice errors. And many assume taxes are the same everywhere, when rules vary widely by country and even region.

## Card: Quick recap
Taxes are mandatory payments that fund shared services like roads, schools, and healthcare — the membership fee for a working society. They're collected in several ways, overseen by a tax authority. Understanding the basics protects you from surprises; for your specifics, consult a qualified professional.
