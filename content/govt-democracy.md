## Card: What it is
image: govtDemocracyHero
**Democracy** is a system of government in which power ultimately rests with the people, usually exercised by voting for representatives and leaders. The word means "rule by the people." It's the most common form of government in the modern world, though it takes many forms. This is a neutral, educational overview of how democracy works and what it requires.

## Card: Why it matters
Many people live in democracies, and understanding how they work — and what makes them function well — is key civic knowledge. It helps you engage meaningfully, value your role, and recognize what keeps democracy healthy or threatens it. Understanding democracy is foundational to being an informed participant in self-government.

## Card: Key terms
- **Democracy**: government where power rests with the people.
- **Representative democracy**: people elect representatives to govern.
- **Majority rule with minority rights**: the majority decides, but rights are protected.
- **Free and fair elections**: genuine, honest voting.

## Card: Rule by the people
image: govtDemocracyRuleByThePeopleHero
In a **democracy**, ultimate authority comes from the people. Most modern democracies are **representative** — rather than everyone voting on every issue directly, people elect representatives and leaders to govern on their behalf, and can vote them out. The core idea is that government answers to the people through regular, **free and fair elections**, giving citizens a genuine say in who holds power and how they're governed.

## Card: More than just voting
Healthy democracy involves more than elections. It typically requires **majority rule with minority rights** — the majority's decisions carry, but the rights and freedoms of everyone (including minorities) are protected, so the majority can't simply oppress others. It also relies on the rule of law, free expression and press, and limits on power. Voting matters enormously, but these supporting conditions are what make a democracy genuine and durable, not just elections alone.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking democracy is *only* voting (it requires rights, rule of law, and free press too); believing majority rule means the majority can do anything (minority rights are essential); assuming all democracies are identical (they take many forms); and undervaluing participation. Democracy is government by the people, sustained by elections *and* the conditions that protect rights and limit power.

## Card: Quick recap
Democracy means government where power rests with the people — usually as a representative democracy, where citizens elect leaders through free and fair elections and can vote them out. But it's more than voting: healthy democracy requires majority rule *with* minority rights, plus rule of law, free press, and limits on power. These supporting conditions make democracy genuine and durable. Understanding it is key civic knowledge.
