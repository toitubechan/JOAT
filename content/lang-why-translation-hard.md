## Card: What it is
image: langWhyTranslationHardHero
**Translation** — converting meaning from one language to another — is far harder than simply swapping words. Languages differ not just in vocabulary but in structure, idioms, culture, and concepts, so a good translation requires understanding and recreating *meaning*, not just substituting words. Understanding why translation is hard reveals deep truths about language and meaning.

## Card: Why it matters
We rely on translation constantly (subtitles, signs, apps, books, business), and it's increasingly done by machines. Understanding why it's genuinely hard helps you appreciate good translation, recognize the limits of automatic translation, and grasp how deeply languages differ. It reveals that meaning isn't just words, but something richer that doesn't always transfer neatly.

## Card: Key terms
- **Translation**: converting meaning between languages.
- **Idiom**: a phrase whose meaning isn't literal.
- **Context**: the surrounding meaning and culture that shapes interpretation.
- **Untranslatable**: words or ideas with no direct equivalent.

## Card: Not just swapping words
image: langWhyTranslationHardNotJustSwappingWordsHero
Translation isn't word-for-word substitution. Languages differ in *structure* (word order, grammar), so a direct swap often produces nonsense. **Idioms** are a classic trap — phrases whose meaning isn't literal ("it's raining cats and dogs") make no sense translated word-for-word. And **context** and culture shape meaning hugely. A good translator must understand the full meaning and intent, then recreate it naturally in the other language — which is a creative, interpretive act, not mechanical swapping.

## Card: When there's no equivalent
Sometimes a word or concept is essentially **untranslatable** — one language has a word or idea with no direct equivalent in another (capturing a feeling, situation, or concept that the other language has no single word for). Then the translator must work around it. Tone, humor, poetry, and cultural references are especially hard to carry across. This is why translation often involves trade-offs and judgment, and why even good machine translation can miss nuance, idioms, and context.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking translation is simple word-swapping (it requires recreating meaning); assuming machine translation is perfect (it can miss idioms, context, tone, and nuance); not realizing some words and concepts are essentially untranslatable; and underrating how much culture shapes meaning. Translation is a skilled, interpretive act because meaning is richer than words and doesn't always transfer neatly between languages.

## Card: Quick recap
Translation is hard because it's not word-swapping but recreating *meaning* across languages that differ in structure, idioms, context, and culture. Idioms make no sense translated literally, some words and concepts are untranslatable, and tone, humor, and culture are especially tricky. This is why translation needs skill and judgment, and why even good machine translation can miss nuance. Meaning is richer than words.
