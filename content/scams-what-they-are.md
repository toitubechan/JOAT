## Card: What it is
image: scamPhishingHero
A **scam** is a trick designed to steal your money or information. **Phishing** is the most common kind: fake messages pretending to be someone you trust — your bank, a delivery company, a friend — to fool you into handing over details or clicking a harmful link. The bait is trust; the goal is theft.

## Card: Why it matters
Scams are everywhere and increasingly convincing, targeting everyone regardless of age or tech skill. They cause huge financial and emotional harm. The best defense isn't technical — it's awareness. Learning how scams work makes you far harder to fool, because you recognize the patterns behind even new and clever attempts.

## Card: Key terms
- **Scam**: a trick to steal money or information.
- **Phishing**: fake messages impersonating someone trusted.
- **Social engineering**: manipulating people rather than hacking systems.
- **Bait**: the lure that gets you to act.

## Card: They target people, not computers
Most scams use **social engineering** — manipulating *you*, not breaking into systems. It's easier to trick a person into giving a password than to crack it. That's why scams rely on emotion and trust: pretending to be authorities, creating urgency, or offering something tempting. Understanding this shifts your guard to where attacks actually land — your judgment.

## Card: The basic anatomy of a scam
image: scamAnatomy
Most scams follow a pattern: they impersonate someone trusted (the **bait**), create a reason to act *now*, and ask you to do something — click a link, share details, or send money. Spotting any one of these steps lets you stop the whole thing. The pattern stays the same even as the disguises change.

## Card: Common mistakes
The biggest mistake is believing "I'd never fall for that" — overconfidence makes people drop their guard. Another is assuming scams are always obvious or badly written; many are now polished and convincing. And many trust a message just because it *looks* official, when logos and layouts are trivially easy to fake.

## Card: Quick recap
Scams trick you into giving money or information, and phishing — fake messages impersonating trusted sources — is the most common form. They target your judgment through social engineering, not your technology. Learn the pattern: impersonation, urgency, a request. Recognizing it beats any technical tool, and overconfidence is the real danger.
