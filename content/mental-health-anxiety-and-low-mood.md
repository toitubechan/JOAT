## Card: What it is
image: anxietyLowMoodHero
**Anxiety** (excessive worry and fear) and **low mood** (persistent sadness or flatness) are two of the most common mental health experiences. Most people feel some anxiety or sadness at times — that's normal. This card set explains the basics so they feel less frightening and more understandable.

## Card: Why it matters
These experiences are extremely common, yet often misunderstood or hidden. Knowing the basics helps you recognize them in yourself and others, respond with understanding, and know when it's worth seeking help. This is general education — diagnosing and treating conditions is a job for qualified professionals.

## Card: Key terms
- **Anxiety**: excessive worry, fear, or unease.
- **Low mood**: persistent sadness, flatness, or loss of interest.
- **Trigger**: something that sets off a feeling or reaction.
- **Coping strategy**: a healthy way to handle difficult feelings.

## Card: Understanding anxiety
image: anxietyAlarmLoop
**Anxiety** is the mind's alarm system overreacting — worrying about threats that aren't present or are unlikely. Normal in small doses (before an exam, say), it becomes a problem when it's persistent, intense, or interferes with daily life. Physical signs can include a racing heart, tension, and restlessness — the body's alarm misfiring.

## Card: Understanding low mood
**Low mood** is more than a bad day — it's persistent sadness, flatness, or losing interest in things you usually enjoy. Everyone has down periods; it becomes concerning when it lingers for weeks or affects daily functioning. It's not a character flaw or something to "just snap out of" — it's a real experience that deserves care.

## Card: Common mistakes
A frequent mistake is dismissing these as weakness or something to push through alone. Another is telling someone (or yourself) to "just cheer up" or "stop worrying," which rarely helps and can add shame. And many ignore persistent symptoms, hoping they'll pass, when professional support could make a real difference.

## Card: Quick recap
Anxiety (excessive worry) and low mood (persistent sadness or flatness) are common and human. Occasional feelings are normal; they warrant attention when intense, persistent, or disrupting daily life. They're not weaknesses or things to "snap out of." Understanding them helps — and professional support is there when needed.
