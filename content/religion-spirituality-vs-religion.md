## Card: What it is
image: religionSpiritualityVsReligionHero
**Religion** typically means an organized system of beliefs, practices, and community, while **spirituality** is a broader, more personal sense of connection to something larger than oneself — meaning, the sacred, or inner life — that may exist with or without organized religion. Many people today identify as "spiritual but not religious." Understanding the distinction clarifies a common but fuzzy area of belief.

## Card: Why it matters
A growing number of people describe themselves as spiritual but not religious, so understanding the difference reflects a real shift in how people relate to belief. It also clarifies a distinction that's often confused. Whether you're religious, spiritual, both, or neither, understanding these terms helps you make sense of how people approach meaning and the sacred today.

## Card: Key terms
- **Religion**: an organized system of belief, practice, and community.
- **Spirituality**: a personal sense of connection to something larger.
- **Spiritual but not religious**: holding personal spirituality outside organized faith.
- **Organized vs. personal**: structured tradition vs. individual experience.

## Card: The distinction
image: religionSpiritualityVsReligionTheDistinctionHero
**Religion** is usually *organized* — a structured tradition with shared beliefs, practices, sacred texts, institutions, and community. **Spirituality** is broader and more *personal* — an individual's sense of connection to something greater, a search for meaning, transcendence, or inner depth. The two often overlap (most religions involve spirituality), but they can come apart: you can be deeply religious, spiritual without religion, both, or neither. The key contrast is **organized versus personal**.

## Card: "Spiritual but not religious"
A growing number of people identify as **spiritual but not religious** — they feel a sense of meaning, connection, or the sacred, but don't follow an organized religion or its institutions. They might draw on various practices (like meditation or nature) and beliefs without committing to one tradition. This reflects a broader modern trend toward personal, individualized approaches to meaning, outside traditional religious structures. It's neither a religion nor non-belief, but its own category.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: using "spiritual" and "religious" interchangeably (they overlap but differ — organized vs. personal); thinking you must be either religious or non-believing (spiritual-but-not-religious is a real middle); assuming spirituality requires religion (it can exist without it); and dismissing personal spirituality as not "real" belief. The distinction reflects genuinely different ways people relate to meaning and the sacred.

## Card: Quick recap
Religion is typically an organized system of beliefs, practices, and community; spirituality is a broader, more personal sense of connection to something larger — meaning, the sacred, or inner life. They often overlap but can come apart, and a growing number of people identify as "spiritual but not religious," seeking meaning outside organized faith. The key contrast is organized versus personal — reflecting diverse modern approaches to belief.
