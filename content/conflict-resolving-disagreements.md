## Card: What it is
image: resolvingDisagreementsHero
**Resolving disagreements** is the practical process of working through a conflict to a solution both sides can accept. Beyond staying calm, it involves specific approaches: understanding the other side, focusing on the problem not the person, and finding common ground. These turn a clash into a resolution.

## Card: Why it matters
Knowing conflict is normal and staying calm isn't enough — you need to actually resolve the issue. These practical steps prevent disagreements from dragging on, repeating, or damaging the relationship. They help you reach genuine solutions rather than uneasy truces or one-sided "wins" that breed resentment.

## Card: Key terms
- **Common ground**: shared interests or goals both sides hold.
- **"I" statements**: expressing your view without blaming.
- **Problem vs. person**: attacking the issue, not the individual.
- **Understanding first**: grasping their view before pushing yours.

## Card: Seek to understand first
Before pushing your view, genuinely understand theirs — ask questions, listen, and reflect back what you hear. People dig in when they feel unheard; they soften when they feel understood. Often a conflict reveals you actually share **common ground** (a goal you both want), which becomes the basis for a solution. Understanding first defuses much of the tension.

## Card: Attack the problem, not the person
image: problemNotPersonHero
Frame the conflict as you and them *together* versus the *problem*, not you versus them. Use **"I" statements** ("I feel frustrated when..." rather than "You always...") to express your view without blame, which avoids triggering defensiveness. Keep it about the specific issue and behavior, not the person's character. This makes them a partner in solving it, not an opponent.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: pushing your view before understanding theirs; making it personal ("you always...") instead of about the problem; trying to "win" rather than resolve; bringing up old grievances; and seeking a one-sided victory that breeds resentment. The aim is a genuine, mutual solution — understand first, attack the problem, find common ground.

## Card: Quick recap
Resolve disagreements by understanding the other side first (people soften when they feel heard), using "I" statements to express your view without blame, and attacking the problem together rather than each other. Look for common ground as the basis for a solution. Aim for a genuine mutual resolution, not a one-sided win.
