## Card: What it is
image: medicineCancerHero
**Cancer** is a disease in which some of the body's cells grow and divide uncontrollably, instead of following the body's normal rules. These abnormal cells can form lumps (tumors) and spread, disrupting how the body works. "Cancer" is actually an umbrella term for many related diseases. (General educational overview, not medical advice.)

## Card: Why it matters
Cancer affects a large share of people directly or through loved ones, and it's widely feared and misunderstood. A clear, basic understanding reduces fear and confusion, helps you grasp health news and prevention, and clarifies what cancer actually is. This is general knowledge; for any health concern, a doctor is the right source.

## Card: Key terms
- **Cell**: the basic unit of the body.
- **Uncontrolled growth**: cells dividing without normal limits.
- **Tumor**: a lump of abnormal cells.
- **Spread (metastasis)**: cancer cells moving to other parts.

## Card: When cells break the rules
image: medicineCancerWhenCellsBreakTheRulesHero
Normally, the body's **cells** grow, divide, and die in an orderly, controlled way. **Cancer** begins when something damages a cell's instructions so it ignores these rules and undergoes **uncontrolled growth** — dividing endlessly. These abnormal cells can pile up into a **tumor** and, in many cancers, **spread** to other parts of the body, where they interfere with normal functions. It's essentially the body's own cells malfunctioning and multiplying.

## Card: Why it's many diseases
"Cancer" isn't one disease but many — there are numerous types, depending on where it starts and how the cells behave. This is why there's no single "cure for cancer": different cancers behave differently and need different treatments. Some risk factors are known (certain exposures, genetics, lifestyle factors), and detecting cancer early often improves outcomes, which is why screening and awareness matter.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking cancer is a single disease with one cure (it's many diseases needing different treatments); imagining it's always a death sentence (many cancers are treatable, especially caught early); thinking it's caused by outside invaders (it's the body's own cells malfunctioning); and falling for miracle-cure misinformation. Cancer is complex; understanding the basics counters fear and myths.

## Card: Quick recap
Cancer is when the body's own cells start growing and dividing uncontrollably, ignoring normal rules — forming tumors and sometimes spreading to disrupt the body. It's actually many different diseases, which is why there's no single cure. Many cancers are treatable, especially when caught early, so screening and awareness matter. Understanding the basics reduces fear and counters misinformation.
