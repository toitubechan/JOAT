## Card: What it is
image: animalMigrationHero
**Migration** is the regular, often long-distance movement of animals between locations — usually seasonal — to find food, breed, or escape harsh conditions. From birds flying thousands of miles to whales crossing oceans, migration is one of nature's most astonishing feats. Animals make these journeys with remarkable navigation abilities.

## Card: Why it matters
Migration is a spectacular and vital natural phenomenon, shaping ecosystems and revealing extraordinary animal abilities. Understanding why and how animals migrate deepens appreciation of nature's wonders, and shows how animals are finely tuned to their environments and the seasons. It's one of the great dramas of the natural world.

## Card: Key terms
- **Migration**: regular movement between locations, often seasonal.
- **Navigation**: how animals find their way over long distances.
- **Seasonal**: tied to the changing seasons.
- **Instinct**: inborn behavior guiding migration.

## Card: Why animals migrate
Animals migrate for survival reasons: to follow **seasonal** food supplies (moving where food is abundant), to reach better breeding grounds, or to escape harsh conditions (like cold winters). For example, many birds fly to warmer regions for winter and return to breed. Migration is risky and demanding, but for these animals, the journey is worth it — staying put would mean starvation or failure to breed.

## Card: Astonishing navigation
image: animalNavigationHero
How animals **navigate** vast distances — often to precise locations, sometimes places they've never been — is remarkable. They use a mix of cues: the sun and stars, Earth's magnetic field (some animals can sense it), landmarks, smells, and inborn **instinct**. Some species travel astonishing distances each year. This navigation, much of it instinctive and still not fully understood by science, is one of nature's great mysteries and marvels.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking migration is random wandering (it's regular, purposeful, and often precisely navigated); assuming animals learn routes entirely (much is instinctive); underrating the distances and precision involved; and not realizing how vital migration is for survival and ecosystems. Migration is a finely tuned, instinct-guided survival strategy, not aimless movement.

## Card: Quick recap
Migration is the regular, often seasonal movement of animals to find food, breed, or escape harsh conditions — like birds wintering in warmer regions. Animals navigate vast distances using the sun, stars, Earth's magnetic field, landmarks, and instinct, often with astonishing precision. It's a demanding but vital survival strategy and one of nature's most spectacular, still partly mysterious feats.
