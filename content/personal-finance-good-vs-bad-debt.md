## Card: What it is
image: financePersonalGoodVsBadDebtHero
**Debt** is money you borrow and must pay back, usually with interest. Not all debt is equal. **Good debt** helps you build something of lasting value, like skills or a home. **Bad debt** pays for things that lose value fast or vanish entirely, like impulse buys on a high-interest card.

## Card: Why it matters
Debt itself isn't evil — it's a tool, and like any tool it can build or destroy. Used wisely, borrowing lets you invest in your future before you've saved the full amount. Used carelessly, it quietly siphons your income into interest payments for years.

## Card: Key terms
- **Interest rate**: the yearly cost of borrowing, as a percentage.
- **Good debt**: borrowing for something that grows in value or income.
- **Bad debt**: borrowing for things that lose value or are consumed.
- **High-interest debt**: debt that costs a lot, like many credit cards.

## Card: What makes debt "good"
image: financePersonalGoodVsBadDebtWhatMakesDebtGoodHero
Good debt usually has two traits: a **low interest rate** and it funds something that adds long-term value — education that raises your earnings, or a home that may hold value. The borrowing is an investment that can return more than it costs. Even then, it's only "good" if the payments fit comfortably in your budget.

## Card: What makes debt "bad"
Bad debt funds things that shrink or disappear — a fancy dinner, gadgets, impulse shopping — often at high interest. The classic example is a credit card balance you don't pay off: the item is long gone while you're still paying interest on it. The higher the rate and the faster the thing loses value, the worse the debt.

## Card: Common mistakes
The biggest trap is carrying a credit card balance, where interest can be very high and compounds against you. Another is borrowing for wants while telling yourself it's an "investment." A useful test: would you still buy this if you had to pay the full price in cash today?

## Card: Quick recap
Debt is a tool. Good debt is low-interest and builds lasting value; bad debt is high-interest and funds things that fade. Pay off high-interest debt first, and before borrowing, ask whether you're investing in your future or just renting a want at a steep price.
