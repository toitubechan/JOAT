## Card: What it is
image: findingClientsHero
For a freelancer, **finding clients** is the lifeblood of the business — no clients, no income. It's an ongoing activity, not a one-time task. Knowing the main ways freelancers find work, and that it requires consistent effort, is essential to building a sustainable freelance career.

## Card: Why it matters
Many new freelancers can do the work but struggle to find it — and skill alone doesn't pay the bills. Understanding how clients are found, and building a steady pipeline, is what separates freelancers who thrive from those who give up. It's often the hardest and most important part, especially early on.

## Card: Key terms
- **Pipeline**: a steady flow of potential clients and work.
- **Portfolio**: examples of your work that showcase your ability.
- **Referral**: a client gained through a recommendation.
- **Marketplace**: a platform connecting freelancers and clients.

## Card: Where clients come from
Work comes from several channels: **freelance marketplaces** (platforms matching freelancers with jobs — easy to start but competitive); your **network** and word of mouth; **referrals** from happy clients; social media and content showing your expertise; and direct outreach to potential clients. Most established freelancers use a mix, with referrals often becoming the best source over time.

## Card: Portfolio and pipeline
image: clientPipelineHero
A strong **portfolio** — examples of your work — is essential; it proves you can do what you claim. And the key habit is keeping a **pipeline**: always looking for the next client, even when busy. Many freelancers make the mistake of finding work, going heads-down, then scrambling when it ends. Consistent outreach smooths the feast-or-famine cycle.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: assuming clients will just appear because you're skilled; stopping all marketing whenever you get busy, then facing a dry spell; having no portfolio to show; relying on a single client (risky if they leave); and undervaluing referrals by not asking happy clients for them. Keep the pipeline flowing, always.

## Card: Quick recap
Finding clients is a freelancer's lifeblood and an ongoing effort, not a one-off. Clients come from marketplaces, your network, referrals, content, and outreach — usually a mix. Build a strong portfolio and keep a steady pipeline, looking for the next client even when busy, to smooth the feast-or-famine cycle.
