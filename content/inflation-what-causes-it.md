## Card: What it is
image: econInflationWhatCausesItHero
Inflation has identifiable **causes** — it's not random. Broadly, prices rise when demand outpaces supply, when the costs of producing things go up, or when there's simply more money chasing the same goods. Understanding these causes explains *why* inflation happens, not just that it does.

## Card: Why it matters
Knowing what drives inflation helps you make sense of economic news and why prices surge in certain periods. It reveals that inflation isn't a mystery or mere "greed" but the result of understandable forces. This understanding is the foundation for grasping how governments and central banks try to manage it.

## Card: Key terms
- **Demand-pull inflation**: prices rise as demand outstrips supply.
- **Cost-push inflation**: prices rise as production costs increase.
- **Money supply**: the total money circulating in an economy.
- **Central bank**: the institution that manages money and rates.

## Card: Demand-pull and cost-push
image: econInflationWhatCausesItDemandPullAndCostPushHero
Two classic causes. **Demand-pull**: when people want to buy more than is available, sellers raise prices — too much demand chasing too few goods. **Cost-push**: when the costs of producing things rise (energy, materials, wages), businesses pass those costs on as higher prices. Many real-world inflation episodes mix both — strong demand plus rising costs.

## Card: Too much money
A third major cause involves the **money supply**. If the amount of money in an economy grows much faster than the amount of goods, there's "more money chasing the same goods," and prices rise. This is why **central banks** carefully manage money and interest rates — printing or injecting too much money can fuel inflation, a lesson seen in extreme cases throughout history.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: blaming inflation solely on "greedy businesses" (it's driven by supply, demand, costs, and money); thinking it has no real cause; assuming printing money is free with no consequences (it can cause inflation); and seeing a single price rise as proof of economy-wide inflation. Inflation has real, understandable drivers.

## Card: Quick recap
Inflation is caused by demand outpacing supply (demand-pull), rising production costs passed to prices (cost-push), or too much money chasing the same goods (money supply growth). Real episodes often mix these. Central banks manage money and rates partly to control it. Inflation isn't random or mere greed — it has understandable causes.
