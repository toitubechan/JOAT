## Card: What it is
image: weatherClimateHero
**Weather** and **climate** are often confused, but they're different things. Weather is what's happening *right now* in the atmosphere; climate is the *long-term average* pattern over many years. Understanding this distinction is essential — especially for making sense of climate discussions.

## Card: Why it matters
Confusing weather and climate leads to common errors, like thinking a cold day disproves long-term warming. Getting the distinction clear lets you understand climate science accurately and avoid being misled. It's one of the most important — and most often muddled — ideas in everyday science.

## Card: Key terms
- **Weather**: atmospheric conditions at a place and moment.
- **Climate**: the long-term average weather pattern of a region.
- **Trend**: the overall direction over a long period.
- **Variability**: normal short-term ups and downs.

## Card: The core distinction
image: weatherVsClimate
**Weather** is short-term and local — today's rain, this week's heatwave. **Climate** is the long-term average — what weather a region *typically* gets across decades. A simple way to remember: "weather is your mood; climate is your personality." One day's weather tells you little about climate, just as one bad mood doesn't define a person.

## Card: Why a cold day doesn't disprove warming
This distinction matters most in climate discussions. A single cold day, or even a cold winter, is **weather** — normal short-term **variability**. Climate is about the long-term **trend** across the whole planet over decades. So "it's cold today, so much for global warming" confuses weather with climate. You judge climate by long-term trends, not individual days.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: using a single day's weather to argue about climate (a classic error); thinking weather and climate are the same; expecting climate trends to mean *every* day or place changes uniformly (there's still variability); and not grasping that climate is measured over decades and globally, not from one local moment.

## Card: Quick recap
Weather is short-term, local atmospheric conditions; climate is the long-term average pattern over decades. "Weather is your mood; climate is your personality." A single cold day is weather and doesn't disprove long-term climate trends, which are judged over many years and globally. Keeping these distinct is essential for understanding climate.
