## Card: What it is
image: bodySystemsHero
Your body is a collection of **systems** that work together — circulation, digestion, breathing, nerves, immunity, and more. Each handles a job, but none works alone; they constantly cooperate to keep you alive. Seeing the body as a team of systems makes the whole thing far easier to understand.

## Card: Why it matters
A basic grasp of how your body works helps you understand health advice, recognize what's normal, and appreciate why habits like eating and sleeping matter. You don't need medical training — just a friendly mental map. This is general knowledge to satisfy curiosity, not for diagnosing anything.

## Card: Key terms
- **Cell**: the tiny basic unit all living things are built from.
- **Organ**: a body part with a specific job, like the heart.
- **System**: a group of organs working toward one function.
- **Homeostasis**: keeping the body's internal balance steady.

## Card: From cells to systems
image: cellsToSystemsHero
The body builds up in levels. Tiny **cells** are the basic units. Similar cells form tissues; tissues form **organs** (like the heart or stomach); and organs that share a job form a **system** (like the digestive system). It's like a company: individuals form teams, teams form departments, departments run the whole organization.

## Card: Keeping balance
A central job of all these systems is **homeostasis** — keeping your insides stable despite a changing world. Your body holds temperature, water, and energy levels within narrow ranges automatically. Shiver when cold, sweat when hot, feel thirsty when low on water: these are your systems constantly nudging you back to balance.

## Card: Common mistakes
A common misunderstanding is seeing organs as working in isolation, when they're deeply interconnected — a problem in one often affects others. Another is underestimating how much runs automatically without conscious thought. And many forget that lifestyle (food, sleep, movement) directly affects how well these systems do their jobs.

## Card: Quick recap
The body is a team of systems built from cells, tissues, and organs, all cooperating to keep you alive and in balance (homeostasis). No system works alone. Much of it runs automatically, and your daily habits directly shape how well it all functions. It's teamwork, all the way down.
