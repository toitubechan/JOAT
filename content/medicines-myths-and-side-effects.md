## Card: What it is
image: sideEffectsHero
Every medicine can have **side effects** — unwanted effects alongside the intended one — and medicines are surrounded by **myths**. This card set explains what side effects really are and clears up common misunderstandings, so you can use medicines with realistic expectations. As always, ask a professional about your specifics.

## Card: Why it matters
Misunderstanding side effects causes people to either panic and stop needed medicine, or ignore genuine warning signs. And medicine myths can lead to harmful choices. Knowing the basics helps you weigh benefits against risks sensibly and know when something needs a doctor's attention.

## Card: Key terms
- **Side effect**: an unwanted effect of a medicine.
- **Benefit-risk balance**: weighing a medicine's help against its risks.
- **Allergic reaction**: the immune system overreacting to a medicine.
- **Myth**: a common but false belief.

## Card: Understanding side effects
image: sideEffectBalance
A **side effect** is any effect beyond the one you want — often mild (like drowsiness) and temporary. Medicines are approved when their **benefit-risk balance** is favorable: the help outweighs the likely downsides. Most side effects are minor, but some are serious. The leaflet lists them, and a pharmacist can explain which matter.

## Card: Knowing what's serious
Most side effects are manageable, but some need prompt action — especially signs of an **allergic reaction** like a rash, swelling, or difficulty breathing, which can be an emergency. If a side effect is severe, unexpected, or worrying, contact a doctor or pharmacist. Don't just suffer through something that seems serious.

## Card: Common myths
A few persistent **myths**: "natural means safe" (natural substances can be potent and interact); "if some is good, more is better" (overdosing is dangerous); "side effects mean the medicine is bad" (most are minor and the benefit outweighs them); and "I can stop once I feel better" (often wrong for prescribed courses).

## Card: Quick recap
Side effects are unwanted effects, usually mild; medicines are approved when benefits outweigh risks. Watch for serious signs like allergic reactions, and seek help if worried. Ignore the myths: natural isn't automatically safe, more isn't better, and side effects don't automatically mean a medicine is bad. Ask professionals.
