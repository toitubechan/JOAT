## Card: What it is
image: electricityFlowHero
Home **electricity** flows like water through pipes: it travels along wires from the grid, through your home's circuits, to power your devices, then returns. The key idea for safety is that electricity always wants to flow to the ground — and it will use *any* path, including a person, to get there.

## Card: Why it matters
You don't need to be an electrician, but understanding the basics keeps you safe and helps you avoid dangerous mistakes. Electricity is invisible and unforgiving: it can injure or kill instantly and start fires. A little knowledge tells you what's safe to touch and what to leave well alone.

## Card: Key terms
- **Current**: the flow of electricity through a wire.
- **Circuit**: a loop wiring carries electricity around.
- **Voltage**: the "pressure" pushing the current.
- **Earth / ground**: a safety path that carries stray electricity away harmlessly.

## Card: The water analogy
image: voltageCurrentLoop
Think of **voltage** as water pressure and **current** as the flow rate. Electricity moves in a complete loop — a **circuit** — from source, through a device, and back. Break the loop and the flow stops (that's what a switch does). This simple picture explains why a cut or loose wire can be both useless and dangerous.

## Card: Why grounding matters
Electricity always seeks a path to the **ground**. The **earth** wire is a deliberate safe route for stray current, so if something faults, the electricity flows away harmlessly instead of through you. This is why you never bypass earth connections or use damaged plugs — they're your protection against a shock.

## Card: Common mistakes
A dangerous misunderstanding is thinking low voltage is always harmless — even household electricity can be lethal. Another is assuming a switched-off device is safe to work on while still plugged in. And many overload circuits or ignore the role of the earth wire, removing the very safety features designed to protect them.

## Card: Quick recap
Electricity flows in a loop and always seeks the ground — through any path, including you. Voltage is pressure, current is flow, and the earth wire is a safety route for stray electricity. Respect it: it's invisible and can be lethal. Understanding the basics is mainly about knowing what *not* to touch.
