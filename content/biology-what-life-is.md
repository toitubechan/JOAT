## Card: What it is
image: biologyLifeHero
**Biology** is the study of life. But what *is* life? Living things share certain features — they grow, use energy, respond to their surroundings, and reproduce. And all of them are built from **cells**, the basic units of life. From bacteria to blue whales, life is built on this shared foundation.

## Card: Why it matters
Understanding what life is and that everything living is made of cells is the foundation for all biology — including understanding your own body, health, and the natural world. It's a unifying idea: the same basic principles connect a bacterium, a tree, and you. That connection is one of science's most profound insights.

## Card: Key terms
- **Cell**: the basic building block of all living things.
- **Organism**: a complete living thing.
- **Single-celled vs. multicellular**: made of one cell vs. many.
- **Life processes**: the activities common to living things.

## Card: What makes something alive
Living things share **life processes**: they grow and develop, take in and use energy, respond to their environment, get rid of waste, and reproduce. No single one defines life — a flame "grows" and "uses energy" but isn't alive — but together these processes distinguish the living from the non-living. Life is a bundle of these activities working together.

## Card: Everything is made of cells
image: cellBuildingBlocks
Every living thing is built from **cells** — the smallest unit that's truly alive. Some **organisms** are **single-celled** (like bacteria), an entire life in one cell. Others, like plants and animals, are **multicellular**, built from trillions of cells working together. Despite the variety of life, this shared cellular basis links every organism on Earth.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking one feature (like movement) defines life, when it's a *combination* of processes; not realizing all life, from microbes to humans, shares the cell as its building block; and imagining cells are all the same, when they specialize into many types (muscle, nerve, leaf). Life is unified by cells but wonderfully varied.

## Card: Quick recap
Biology is the study of life, and living things share life processes — growing, using energy, responding, reproducing — that together distinguish them from non-living things. All life is built from cells, the basic unit, whether a single-celled bacterium or a trillion-celled human. This shared cellular foundation links every organism on Earth.
