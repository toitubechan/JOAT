## Card: What it is
image: philosophyExistentialismHero
**Existentialism** is a philosophy centered on individual freedom, choice, and creating your own meaning in a universe that doesn't hand us one. Its famous theme: rather than having a fixed purpose given to us, we are radically free — and responsible — to define our own lives through our choices. It's both liberating and daunting.

## Card: Why it matters
Existentialism speaks directly to modern questions of meaning, freedom, and authenticity — how to live when there's no obvious given purpose. Its ideas resonate with anyone wondering how to find meaning and live genuinely. Understanding it offers a powerful (if challenging) perspective on freedom and responsibility, and influences modern thought, art, and how many people approach life.

## Card: Key terms
- **Existentialism**: a philosophy of freedom, choice, and self-made meaning.
- **Radical freedom**: we are profoundly free to choose our path.
- **Authenticity**: living true to your own choices and values.
- **Responsibility**: owning the freedom and consequences of your choices.

## Card: We create our own meaning
image: philosophyExistentialismWeCreateOurOwnMeaningHero
A core existentialist idea: there's no pre-given purpose stamped on us — we exist first, then define who we are through our choices. This is **radical freedom**: we're profoundly free to shape our own lives and meaning, not bound to a fixed essence. This is exhilarating but heavy, because with that freedom comes total **responsibility** — we can't blame a script; we own our choices and who we become. Meaning is something we create, not find pre-made.

## Card: Authenticity and facing freedom
Existentialism prizes **authenticity** — living true to your own freely chosen values, rather than just conforming to what others expect or drifting through life on autopilot. It also honestly confronts the anxiety this freedom brings (the weight of being responsible for your own life and meaning). Rather than seeing this as bleak, existentialists often see it as the path to a genuine, self-determined life. Facing freedom honestly, and choosing your path, is the existentialist ideal.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking existentialism is purely gloomy or nihilistic (it's about creating meaning and living authentically, often hopeful); confusing it with believing "nothing matters" (it says *we* make things matter); seeing radical freedom as a license to do anything without responsibility (responsibility is central); and treating it as only abstract. Existentialism is a serious, empowering call to own your freedom and create meaning.

## Card: Quick recap
Existentialism centers on radical freedom: there's no pre-given purpose, so we define ourselves and create our own meaning through our choices. With this freedom comes full responsibility — we own who we become. It prizes authenticity (living true to your own values) and honestly faces the anxiety freedom brings. Both daunting and liberating, it's an empowering (not gloomy) call to create meaning and live genuinely.
