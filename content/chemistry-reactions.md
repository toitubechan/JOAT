## Card: What it is
image: chemicalReactionHero
A **chemical reaction** is when substances transform into different substances, as atoms rearrange and form new bonds. Burning, rusting, cooking, digestion — all are chemical reactions. They're how the world changes at the chemical level, turning one set of materials into another.

## Card: Why it matters
Reactions are happening all around and inside you constantly. Understanding them explains everyday transformations — why food cooks, metal rusts, fuel burns, and your body extracts energy. It's the dynamic side of chemistry: not just what things *are*, but how they *change* into something new.

## Card: Key terms
- **Chemical reaction**: a transformation into new substances.
- **Reactants**: the starting substances.
- **Products**: the new substances formed.
- **Energy change**: heat released or absorbed during a reaction.

## Card: Atoms rearrange
In a reaction, the starting substances (**reactants**) transform into new ones (**products**) as their atoms break old bonds and form new ones. The atoms themselves aren't created or destroyed — they're just *rearranged* into different combinations. A reaction is essentially a reshuffling of atoms into new molecules with new properties.

## Card: Energy and signs of reaction
image: reactionSigns
Reactions usually involve an **energy change** — many release energy as heat or light (burning, explosions), while some absorb it. Signs a reaction has occurred include a color change, gas bubbles, heat or light, or a new substance forming. Cooking an egg, iron rusting, and wood burning are all everyday reactions transforming reactants into new products.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking atoms are created or destroyed in reactions (they're only rearranged); confusing a physical change (ice melting to water — same substance) with a chemical reaction (which makes a *new* substance); and not recognizing everyday changes like rusting and cooking as chemical reactions. Reactions reshuffle atoms into new substances.

## Card: Quick recap
A chemical reaction transforms reactants into new products as atoms break old bonds and form new ones — the atoms are rearranged, never created or destroyed. Reactions usually involve an energy change and signs like heat, gas, or color change. Burning, rusting, and cooking are all everyday reactions making new substances.
