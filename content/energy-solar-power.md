## Card: What it is
image: energySolarPowerHero
**Solar power** generates electricity directly from sunlight, mainly using **solar panels** (photovoltaic cells). It's a renewable energy source that's grown explosively as costs have plummeted. Sunlight is free and abundant, making solar one of the leading clean-energy technologies of the energy transition.

## Card: Why it matters
Solar is a key part of moving away from fossil fuels — it's renewable, produces no emissions while operating, and has become remarkably cheap. Understanding how it works (and its limits) helps you grasp the energy transition, evaluate home solar, and follow clean-energy news. It's one of the most important technologies for a low-carbon future.

## Card: Key terms
- **Solar panel (PV)**: a device converting sunlight to electricity.
- **Renewable**: an energy source that doesn't run out.
- **Intermittent**: producing power only when the sun shines.
- **Storage**: batteries holding solar energy for later.

## Card: How solar works
image: energySolarPowerHowSolarWorksHero
**Solar panels** contain photovoltaic cells that convert sunlight directly into electricity — no moving parts, no fuel, no emissions while running. Sunlight hits the panel, and a physical effect generates an electric current. Panels can sit on rooftops or in large solar farms. Because the "fuel" (sunlight) is free, once installed, solar produces very cheap, clean electricity.

## Card: Strengths and the storage challenge
Solar's strengths: renewable, clean, increasingly cheap, and scalable from a rooftop to a giant farm. Its main limitation is that it's **intermittent** — it only generates when the sun shines (not at night or in heavy cloud). This is why **storage** (batteries) and a flexible grid matter, to use solar energy when the sun isn't shining. Solving storage is key to relying on solar more heavily.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking solar works only in hot climates (it works on light, not heat, so even cooler sunny places generate well); assuming it produces power at night (it doesn't, without storage); thinking it's still prohibitively expensive (costs have fallen dramatically); and underrating the storage challenge. Solar is cheap and clean but intermittent.

## Card: Quick recap
Solar power converts sunlight directly into electricity via solar panels, with no fuel or emissions while running. It's renewable, increasingly cheap, and scalable. Its main limitation is intermittency — it only generates when the sun shines — making storage (batteries) and a flexible grid important. Solar is a leading technology of the clean-energy transition.
