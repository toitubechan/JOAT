## Card: What it is
image: sustainShoppingHero
**Sustainable shopping** means making buying choices that reduce environmental and social harm — buying less, buying better, and choosing more responsible products. It's not about perfection or buying lots of "eco" labels; the most sustainable shopping habit is often simply buying less and choosing things that last.

## Card: Why it matters
Everything we buy has an environmental cost — in resources, energy, and waste. Shopping more thoughtfully reduces that cost and can save money too (durable things bought less often). Since consumption drives much environmental impact, how we shop is a powerful everyday lever for living more sustainably.

## Card: Key terms
- **Buy less, buy better**: fewer, higher-quality, longer-lasting items.
- **Durability**: how long something lasts before replacement.
- **Secondhand**: buying used items instead of new.
- **Conscious consumption**: buying thoughtfully, not impulsively.

## Card: Buy less, buy better
image: sustainShoppingBuyLessBuyBetterHero
The single most sustainable shopping principle is **buy less, buy better**. Fewer, higher-quality, durable items that last for years beat a stream of cheap things that break and get replaced. This reduces waste *and* often saves money over time. Before buying, ask: do I really need this, will it last, and will I actually use it?

## Card: Smarter choices
Beyond buying less: choose **secondhand** where you can (reusing existing items has near-zero new impact), favor **durable** and repairable products over disposable ones, and consider how things are made when it matters to you. Avoid impulse buying — **conscious consumption** means pausing before purchases. The greenest product is usually the one you already own or don't buy at all.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: thinking sustainable shopping means buying *more* "eco" products (buying less is greener); chasing green labels without checking if you need the item; impulse buying; and ignoring secondhand and durability. Also beware greenwashing — marketing that makes products *seem* green. Buy less, choose durable, consider secondhand, and pause before purchasing.

## Card: Quick recap
Sustainable shopping is mostly about buying less and buying better — fewer, durable items that last, bought thoughtfully rather than on impulse. Favor secondhand and repairable goods, and beware green-sounding marketing. The greenest purchase is often the one you don't make or already own. How we shop is a powerful everyday sustainability lever.
