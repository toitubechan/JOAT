## Card: What it is
image: heartCirculationHero
Your **heart** is a muscular pump, and your **blood vessels** are the pipes that carry blood everywhere. Together they form the **circulatory system**, delivering oxygen and nutrients to every cell and carrying away waste. It's a delivery and waste-collection network running through your whole body, every second of your life.

## Card: Why it matters
This system keeps every cell alive — interrupt it for even minutes and the body is in serious trouble. Understanding it explains why heart health, exercise, and blood pressure matter so much. It's the system most linked to major health conditions, so the basics are genuinely worth knowing.

## Card: Key terms
- **Heart**: the muscular pump driving blood around.
- **Arteries**: vessels carrying blood away from the heart.
- **Veins**: vessels carrying blood back to the heart.
- **Blood pressure**: the force of blood against vessel walls.

## Card: The pump and the loop
image: bloodLoopHero
The **heart** beats continuously, pushing blood out through **arteries** to the body, then receiving it back through **veins** to be sent to the lungs for fresh oxygen — a continuous loop. Blood drops off oxygen and nutrients, picks up waste, and returns to start again. Your heart does this around 100,000 times a day.

## Card: What blood carries
Blood is a delivery service. It carries **oxygen** from the lungs and **nutrients** from food to every cell, then collects **carbon dioxide** and waste to be removed. It also moves heat, hormones, and immune cells. This is why good circulation matters everywhere — every cell depends on this constant supply run.

## Card: Common mistakes
A common confusion is mixing up arteries and veins — arteries carry blood *away* from the heart, veins carry it *back*. Another is thinking the heart only matters in old age, when habits throughout life shape its health. And many ignore **blood pressure**, a quiet but important measure of how hard the system is working.

## Card: Quick recap
The circulatory system is a pump (the heart) and pipes (vessels) delivering oxygen and nutrients to every cell and removing waste, in a continuous loop. Arteries carry blood away from the heart, veins bring it back. Lifelong habits and blood pressure shape its health — and every cell depends on it.
