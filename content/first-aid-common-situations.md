## Card: What it is
image: commonFirstAidHero
Beyond major emergencies, there are everyday situations — sprains, faints, nosebleeds, minor knocks — where a calm, correct response helps a lot. This card set covers the basics of a few common ones. As always, this is general guidance; if you're worried or it's serious, seek medical advice.

## Card: Why it matters
These situations are common, and knowing the simple right response prevents you from making them worse or panicking unnecessarily. Many have well-known but *wrong* "remedies" passed around. A little accurate knowledge means you respond helpfully and recognize when something needs more than home care.

## Card: Key terms
- **Sprain**: a stretched or torn ligament around a joint.
- **RICE**: Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation for sprains.
- **Faint**: a brief loss of consciousness from reduced blood to the brain.
- **Recovery position**: a safe side-lying position for the unconscious-but-breathing.

## Card: Sprains and strains
image: sprainCare
For a **sprain** (a twisted ankle, say), a common approach is **RICE**: **R**est it, apply **I**ce wrapped in cloth (not directly on skin), use gentle **C**ompression with a bandage, and **E**levate the limb. This reduces swelling and pain. Severe pain, inability to bear weight, or suspected fractures need medical assessment.

## Card: Faints and nosebleeds
For a **faint**, help the person lie down and raise their legs to restore blood flow to the brain; they usually recover quickly. For a **nosebleed**, lean *forward* (not back) and pinch the soft part of the nose for about 10 minutes. Leaning back, a common mistake, just sends blood down the throat.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common errors: tilting the head *back* for a nosebleed (lean forward instead); putting ice directly on skin for a sprain (wrap it); moving someone with a possible serious injury unnecessarily; and ignoring warning signs that need a doctor. For an unconscious but breathing person, the **recovery position** keeps their airway clear.

## Card: Quick recap
For everyday mishaps: sprains respond to RICE (rest, wrapped ice, compression, elevation); faints to lying down with legs raised; nosebleeds to leaning forward and pinching the nose. Avoid the myths (don't tilt the head back, don't ice bare skin). When in doubt or it's serious, get medical advice.
