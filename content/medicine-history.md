## Card: What it is
image: medicineHistoryHero
The **history of medicine** is the story of how humans went from superstition and guesswork to modern, science-based healthcare. Over centuries, key discoveries — germs, anesthesia, vaccines, antibiotics — transformed medicine from often-harmful folk practices into something that genuinely heals. It's a story of dramatic, relatively recent progress.

## Card: Why it matters
Modern medicine works remarkably well, but it's surprisingly recent — for most of history, healthcare was ineffective or even harmful. Understanding this history reveals how science transformed medicine, why we trust it today, and how much progress was made in a short time. It also gives perspective on how far medicine has come and how it continues to advance.

## Card: Key terms
- **Germ theory**: the discovery that microbes cause many diseases.
- **Anesthesia**: making surgery painless.
- **Antibiotics**: drugs that fight bacterial infection.
- **Evidence-based medicine**: medicine grounded in scientific testing.

## Card: From superstition to science
image: medicineHistoryFromSuperstitionToScienceHero
For most of history, medicine was based on guesswork, tradition, and superstition — treatments were often useless or harmful (bloodletting, for example). A turning point was **germ theory**: the discovery that tiny microbes cause many diseases, which revolutionized hygiene, surgery, and prevention. Understanding the real causes of illness, rather than guessing, shifted medicine onto a scientific footing for the first time.

## Card: Breakthroughs that transformed healing
Several breakthroughs transformed medicine: **anesthesia** made surgery painless and survivable; antiseptics and hygiene (from germ theory) drastically cut deaths from infection; **vaccines** prevented once-deadly diseases; and **antibiotics** made many lethal infections treatable. Together with **evidence-based medicine** (rigorously testing what actually works), these advances — mostly within the last couple of centuries — turned medicine into the powerful, life-saving science it is today.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: assuming effective medicine has existed for a long time (modern scientific medicine is quite recent); not realizing how harmful much historical "medicine" was; underrating germ theory's revolutionary impact; and forgetting that evidence-based testing is what separates modern medicine from guesswork. Medicine's transformation into a healing science is remarkably recent.

## Card: Quick recap
The history of medicine is the shift from superstition and often-harmful guesswork to science-based healthcare. Germ theory (microbes cause disease) was a turning point, alongside breakthroughs like anesthesia, antiseptics, vaccines, and antibiotics — mostly within the last couple of centuries. Evidence-based medicine, testing what truly works, underpins modern healthcare. Medicine's transformation into a healing science is recent and remarkable.
