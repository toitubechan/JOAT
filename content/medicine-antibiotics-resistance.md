## Card: What it is
image: medicineAntibioticsResistanceHero
**Antibiotics** are medicines that kill or stop **bacteria**, treating bacterial infections. **Antibiotic resistance** is a growing global threat where bacteria evolve to survive these drugs, making infections harder to treat. Understanding both — and using antibiotics wisely — matters for everyone's health. (General educational overview, not medical advice.)

## Card: Why it matters
Antibiotics transformed medicine, turning once-deadly infections into treatable ones. But overuse is breeding resistant bacteria, which the world health community considers a serious threat. Understanding this helps you use antibiotics responsibly and grasp why doctors are careful with them — a genuine public health issue affecting us all.

## Card: Key terms
- **Antibiotic**: a medicine that fights bacterial infections.
- **Bacteria vs. viruses**: different germs; antibiotics only fight bacteria.
- **Antibiotic resistance**: bacteria evolving to survive antibiotics.
- **Completing the course**: taking all prescribed doses.

## Card: What antibiotics do (and don't)
image: medicineAntibioticsResistanceWhatAntibioticsDoAndDonTHero
**Antibiotics** kill or stop **bacteria** — so they treat bacterial infections (some chest, ear, urinary, or wound infections). Crucially, they do *not* work against **viruses**, which cause most colds, flu, and coughs. Taking antibiotics for a virus does nothing for the illness and contributes to resistance. This is why a doctor may correctly *not* prescribe antibiotics for a viral illness.

## Card: The resistance threat
**Antibiotic resistance** happens when bacteria evolve to survive the drugs — through natural selection, the toughest bacteria survive and multiply, especially when antibiotics are overused or misused. Resistant infections are harder or sometimes impossible to treat. Using antibiotics only when truly needed, and **completing the prescribed course** (so surviving bacteria don't breed resistance), helps slow this serious global problem. It protects not just you, but everyone.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: expecting antibiotics for colds and flu (they're viral, so antibiotics won't help); stopping a course early once you feel better (this can breed resistance); saving or sharing leftover antibiotics; and not realizing resistance is a shared, growing threat. Take antibiotics only when prescribed, exactly as directed, to keep them working for everyone.

## Card: Quick recap
Antibiotics fight bacterial infections, not viruses (so they don't help colds or flu). Overuse and misuse drive antibiotic resistance — bacteria evolving to survive the drugs — a serious global threat making infections harder to treat. Use antibiotics only when prescribed, complete the full course, and never save or share them. Wise use protects everyone's future health.
