## Card: What it is
image: contractRedFlagsHero
Some contract terms are warning signs — **red flags** that could cost or trap you. They often hide in the **fine print** people skip. Knowing what to watch for helps you catch unfair or risky terms *before* you sign, when you can still negotiate or walk away. (General guidance, not legal advice.)

## Card: Why it matters
The terms that hurt people most are often the ones buried in fine print or written in confusing language — sometimes deliberately. Knowing the common red flags helps you protect yourself from costly traps. Catching a problem before signing is far easier than dealing with it after you're bound.

## Card: Key terms
- **Fine print**: small-text details easy to overlook.
- **Auto-renewal**: the contract renewing automatically.
- **Penalty / cancellation fee**: charges for ending early.
- **Hidden fees**: costs not obvious upfront.

## Card: Common red flags
image: hiddenFeesFlags
Watch for: **auto-renewal** that locks you in unless you cancel in advance; steep **cancellation fees** or long lock-in periods; **hidden fees** and charges buried in the detail; vague or open-ended terms (especially around price changes); and anything that seems to heavily favor the other party. Clauses letting them change terms or charge more later are particularly worth scrutinizing.

## Card: Beware the fine print
The **fine print** exists partly because important catches are placed where people won't read them. If a contract is confusingly written, terms seem too good to be true, or you're pressured to sign quickly without time to read — treat these as warning signs in themselves. Genuine, fair agreements don't usually require rushing you past the details.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: skipping the fine print where the catches hide; ignoring auto-renewal and cancellation terms; not noticing clauses that let the other side change terms or fees; and signing under pressure without time to read. The fix: read the fine print, watch for these red flags, and never let yourself be rushed into signing.

## Card: Quick recap
Contract red flags include auto-renewal lock-ins, steep cancellation fees, hidden fees, vague terms, and clauses heavily favoring the other party — often buried in fine print. Being rushed to sign or confusing language are warning signs too. Read the fine print, watch for these, and never let yourself be pressured into signing quickly.
