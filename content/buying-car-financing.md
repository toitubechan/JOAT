## Card: What it is
image: financingHero
You can pay for a car **outright** (cash) or through **financing** (borrowing and paying over time). Financing makes a car affordable upfront but adds interest and commitment. Understanding the options and their costs helps you choose wisely and avoid expensive deals. (General knowledge; not financial advice — consider a professional for big decisions.)

## Card: Why it matters
How you pay for a car hugely affects its true cost. Financing can add a lot in interest, and some deals are far better than others. Understanding the basics helps you avoid overpaying, compare offers, and not be talked into an unaffordable arrangement. Car financing is where many people quietly lose money.

## Card: Key terms
- **Paying outright**: buying with cash, no borrowing.
- **Financing**: borrowing to pay over time, with interest.
- **APR**: the yearly cost of borrowing, for comparing deals.
- **Total cost**: the full amount paid including all interest.

## Card: Outright vs. financing
**Paying outright** means no interest and full ownership immediately — cheapest overall, but requires having the cash. **Financing** spreads the cost into manageable payments, making a car accessible sooner, but you pay *more* overall due to interest, and you're committed to ongoing payments. Neither is simply "right" — it depends on your finances, but understand that financing costs more in total.

## Card: Compare deals by total cost and APR
image: totalCostCompare
If financing, don't judge by the *monthly payment* alone — a low monthly figure can hide a long term and high **total cost**. Compare deals using the **APR** (the true yearly cost of borrowing), and always work out the *total* you'll pay including all interest. Dealers may push financing because it earns them money, so shop around and don't be rushed into the first offer.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: judging financing by the monthly payment instead of the total cost and APR; not shopping around for better loan terms; being pressured into dealer financing without comparing; stretching the term so long you overpay hugely; and forgetting financing adds interest on top of the price. Compare the total cost and APR, and don't be rushed.

## Card: Quick recap
You can pay outright (no interest, cheapest overall) or finance (affordable upfront but more in total due to interest). If financing, compare deals by APR and total cost — not just the monthly payment, which can hide a costly long term. Shop around, don't be rushed by dealers, and remember financing always adds interest to the price.
