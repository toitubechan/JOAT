## Card: What it is
image: realestatePropertyValueHero
**Property value** is what a property is worth — and it's determined by a mix of factors, not a fixed number. Understanding what drives value (location, condition, market forces, and more) helps you judge whether a property is fairly priced and how its value might change. This is general educational knowledge, not financial advice.

## Card: Why it matters
Property is often the biggest purchase or asset in a person's life, so understanding what drives its value is hugely important. It helps you avoid overpaying, recognize good and bad deals, and understand why prices change. Whether buying, selling, renting, or just curious, grasping property value is foundational knowledge for one of life's biggest financial areas.

## Card: Key terms
- **Property value**: what a property is worth.
- **Location**: often the biggest single factor in value.
- **Supply and demand**: how availability and desire set prices.
- **Comparables**: similar properties used to estimate value.

## Card: What drives value
image: realestatePropertyValueWhatDrivesValueHero
Property value comes from several factors. **Location** is famously the biggest — the same building is worth vastly different amounts in different places (due to desirability, amenities, jobs, schools, safety). Other factors: the property's size, condition, and features; the local **supply and demand** (limited supply plus high demand pushes prices up); and broader economic conditions (like interest rates and the economy). Value is the combination of all these, which is why "location, location, location" is the famous saying.

## Card: How value is estimated
Value isn't a fixed fact — it's ultimately what a buyer will pay. A common way to estimate it is **comparables** ("comps") — looking at recent sale prices of similar nearby properties, since they reflect what the market actually pays. Professional valuations and appraisals also use this. Importantly, value can change over time as locations develop, markets shift, and demand changes — so property value is dynamic, not permanent. Understanding the drivers helps you anticipate these changes.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking property value is a fixed, objective number (it's ultimately what a buyer will pay, and it changes); underrating location (often the biggest factor); ignoring supply and demand and broader economic forces; and not using comparables to judge fair price. Property value is a dynamic combination of factors — especially location — best judged against what similar properties actually sell for.

## Card: Quick recap
Property value is what a property is worth — driven by location (often the biggest factor), the property's size and condition, local supply and demand, and broader economic conditions. It's not a fixed number but ultimately what a buyer will pay, commonly estimated using comparables (recent sales of similar nearby properties). Value changes over time as markets and areas shift. Understanding these drivers helps you judge fair prices.
