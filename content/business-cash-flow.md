## Card: What it is
image: businessCashFlowHero
**Cash flow** is the movement of money in and out of a business over time. Critically, it's *not* the same as profit — a profitable business can still run out of cash and fail. Understanding cash flow is one of the most important and underappreciated skills for any business owner, because running out of cash is a leading cause of business failure.

## Card: Why it matters
Many businesses fail not because they're unprofitable, but because they run out of *cash* at the wrong moment. Cash flow is what keeps the lights on day to day — you can't pay bills with profit that hasn't arrived yet. Understanding it helps owners avoid the cash crunches that sink otherwise-healthy businesses. It's a survival skill, not just an accounting detail.

## Card: Key terms
- **Cash flow**: money moving in and out over time.
- **Profit**: revenue minus costs (which may not be cash yet).
- **Timing**: when money actually arrives versus when it's owed.
- **Running out of cash**: having no money on hand, even if profitable.

## Card: Profit isn't cash
image: profitIsntCashHero
The crucial insight: **profit** and **cash flow** are different. Profit is revenue minus costs *on paper*. But money has **timing** — you might have made a sale (profit) but not yet received the payment, while bills (rent, wages, suppliers) are due *now*. So you can be profitable on paper yet have no actual cash to pay today's bills. Cash flow tracks the *real money available*, which is what you actually need to operate.

## Card: Why cash flow kills businesses
**Running out of cash** is a leading cause of business failure — even for profitable businesses. A growing, profitable company can hit a cash crunch if money goes out (paying for stock, staff, expenses) faster than it comes in (waiting on customer payments). The fix is managing cash flow actively: tracking money in and out, ensuring enough cash on hand to cover what's due, getting paid promptly, and not letting timing gaps catch you out. Cash is what keeps a business alive.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: confusing profit with cash (a profitable business can still run out of cash); not tracking cash flow and getting caught by timing gaps; letting customers pay slowly while your own bills come due; and spending based on profit that hasn't actually arrived. Manage cash actively — it's the lifeblood of the business, and running out of it is a top reason businesses fail.

## Card: Quick recap
Cash flow is the real money moving in and out of a business over time — and it's *not* the same as profit. Because of timing (a sale made isn't cash until paid, while bills are due now), a profitable business can still run out of cash and fail. Running out of cash is a leading cause of business failure. Manage it actively: track money in and out, keep enough on hand, and get paid promptly. Cash keeps a business alive.
