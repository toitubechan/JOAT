## Card: What it is
image: datingTrustHero
**Trust** is the confidence that someone is reliable, honest, and has your best interests at heart — and it's the foundation of every close relationship. Trust is *built* gradually through consistent actions over time, and can be damaged quickly. Understanding how trust works helps you build and maintain it. This is general educational knowledge, not professional advice.

## Card: Why it matters
Trust underlies all close relationships — without it, intimacy, security, and connection are impossible. Understanding how trust is built (and broken and repaired) helps you create stronger relationships and navigate trust issues. Since trust is foundational yet often taken for granted until damaged, understanding it is genuinely valuable for any relationship.

## Card: Key terms
- **Trust**: confidence in someone's reliability and honesty.
- **Consistency**: reliable actions over time that build trust.
- **Vulnerability**: opening up, which both requires and builds trust.
- **Repair**: rebuilding trust after it's damaged.

## Card: Trust is built through consistency
A key understanding: trust isn't given instantly or declared — it's **built** gradually through **consistency**. Trust grows when someone's actions reliably match their words over time: keeping promises, being honest, showing up, and being dependable repeatedly. Each consistent, trustworthy action builds a little more trust. This is why trust takes time to develop — and why a single betrayal can damage it quickly (one breach undermines the pattern of reliability that trust depends on). Trust is earned through consistent action, not words alone.

## Card: Vulnerability and repair
image: vulnerabilityRepairHero
Trust and **vulnerability** are linked: trusting someone means opening up and being vulnerable, and as trust grows, deeper vulnerability becomes possible — creating the closeness of intimate relationships. When trust is broken, **repair** is possible but hard: it requires genuine accountability from the one who broke it, consistent trustworthy behavior over time to rebuild, and willingness from the other to give the chance. Repaired trust takes patience and sustained consistency. Understanding this helps both protect trust and rebuild it when damaged.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking trust can be demanded or declared rather than earned through consistency; underestimating how quickly a betrayal damages slowly-built trust; expecting broken trust to be instantly repaired (rebuilding takes sustained consistent action); and not realizing trust requires vulnerability. Trust is built gradually through consistent, reliable action, damaged quickly by betrayal, and repaired only slowly through genuine accountability and sustained consistency.

## Card: Quick recap
Trust — confidence in someone's reliability and honesty — is the foundation of close relationships, built gradually through consistency (actions reliably matching words over time), not declared or demanded. It enables the vulnerability that creates intimacy. Trust can be damaged quickly by betrayal, and rebuilt only slowly, through genuine accountability and sustained trustworthy behavior. Understanding this helps you build, protect, and repair trust.
