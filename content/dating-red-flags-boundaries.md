## Card: What it is
image: redFlagsBoundariesHero
**Red flags** are warning signs of unhealthy or harmful behavior in a relationship, and **boundaries** are the limits you set to protect your wellbeing. Recognizing red flags and maintaining healthy boundaries are essential for safe, healthy relationships. Understanding both helps you protect yourself and build better relationships. This is general educational knowledge, not professional advice. (If you feel unsafe, seek appropriate help.)

## Card: Why it matters
Recognizing red flags can protect you from harmful relationships, and healthy boundaries are essential for wellbeing in any relationship. Many people miss warning signs or struggle to set boundaries, leading to harm or resentment. Understanding both empowers you to make safer choices and maintain self-respect. This is important knowledge for protecting yourself and fostering healthy connections.

## Card: Key terms
- **Red flag**: a warning sign of unhealthy or harmful behavior.
- **Boundary**: a limit you set to protect your wellbeing.
- **Disrespect of boundaries**: ignoring your limits — itself a red flag.
- **Self-respect**: valuing your own wellbeing and limits.

## Card: Recognizing red flags
**Red flags** are warning signs that a relationship may be unhealthy or harmful. General examples include: disrespect (for you, your boundaries, or others), controlling behavior, dishonesty, lack of respect, intense possessiveness or jealousy, and patterns that consistently make you feel diminished, unsafe, or anxious. A key sign is someone repeatedly ignoring your **boundaries**. Red flags are about *patterns* of concerning behavior, not isolated imperfections. Recognizing them early helps you protect yourself before harm deepens.

## Card: Setting healthy boundaries
image: healthyBoundariesHero
**Boundaries** are the limits you set about how you'll be treated and what you will and won't accept — essential for protecting your wellbeing and maintaining **self-respect**. Healthy relationships respect boundaries; a major red flag is someone repeatedly **disrespecting your boundaries**. Setting boundaries means clearly knowing and communicating your limits, and being willing to uphold them. This isn't selfish or unkind — it's necessary for healthy relationships and self-respect. How someone responds to your reasonable boundaries reveals a lot about the relationship.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: ignoring or excusing red flags (hoping they'll change, or dismissing patterns); thinking boundaries are selfish or unkind (they're essential and healthy); not setting or upholding boundaries (leading to resentment or harm); and confusing intense or controlling behavior with love. Recognize patterns of concerning behavior (red flags), and set and uphold healthy boundaries — how someone treats your boundaries reveals much. (If unsafe, seek help.)

## Card: Quick recap
Red flags are warning signs of unhealthy or harmful behavior — patterns like disrespect, control, dishonesty, possessiveness, or repeatedly ignoring your boundaries (not isolated imperfections). Boundaries are the limits you set to protect your wellbeing and self-respect — essential, not selfish, and respected in healthy relationships. Recognizing red flags and upholding boundaries protects you and fosters healthy connections. If you ever feel unsafe, seek appropriate help.
