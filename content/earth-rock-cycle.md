## Card: What it is
image: rockCycleHero
The **rock cycle** is the continuous process by which rocks form, break down, and transform from one type into another over vast spans of time. Rocks may seem permanent, but they're constantly (if slowly) being created, worn away, buried, melted, and reformed. The rock under your feet has been many things before.

## Card: Why it matters
The rock cycle explains where rocks, soil, and landscapes come from and how Earth recycles its materials. It ties together many Earth processes — volcanoes, erosion, mountain-building — into one elegant cycle. Understanding it reveals our planet as a dynamic system that endlessly reuses its own materials over geological time.

## Card: Key terms
- **Igneous rock**: formed from cooled molten rock.
- **Sedimentary rock**: formed from compressed layers of particles.
- **Metamorphic rock**: rock changed by heat and pressure.
- **Weathering**: the breaking down of rock over time.

## Card: The three rock types
There are three main rock types, defined by how they form. **Igneous** rock forms when molten rock (magma or lava) cools and hardens. **Sedimentary** rock forms when particles (sand, mud, shells) pile up in layers and compress over time. **Metamorphic** rock forms when existing rock is changed by intense heat and pressure deep underground. Each type can transform into the others.

## Card: The endless cycle
image: endlessRockCycleHero
The cycle connects them all. **Weathering** and erosion break rocks into particles, which compress into sedimentary rock. Buried rock under heat and pressure becomes metamorphic. Deeper still, rock can melt into magma, which cools into igneous rock. Then erosion begins again. Over millions of years, the same materials cycle endlessly between the three types. There's no fixed start or end — it's a true cycle.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking rocks are permanent and unchanging (they constantly transform over geological time); not realizing the three types can turn into each other; imagining the cycle has a clear beginning or end (it's continuous); and thinking it's fast (it unfolds over millions of years). The "solid" rocks around us are part of an endless, slow recycling system.

## Card: Quick recap
The rock cycle is the continuous transformation of rocks between three types — igneous (cooled molten rock), sedimentary (compressed particles), and metamorphic (changed by heat and pressure). Weathering, burial, melting, and cooling cycle the same materials endlessly over millions of years. There's no fixed start or end; rocks are part of a dynamic, ever-recycling planet.
