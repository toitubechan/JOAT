## Card: What it is
image: religionChristianityHero
**Christianity** is the world's largest religion, centered on the life and teachings of **Jesus**, whom Christians regard as the son of God and savior. It grew from Jewish roots and spread worldwide. This is a respectful, general overview of its core beliefs and practices — descriptive, not promoting or judging.

## Card: Why it matters
With billions of followers, Christianity has profoundly shaped history, culture, art, law, and the lives of people across the world. Understanding its basics builds cultural literacy and respectful understanding of a faith central to so many. It's part of grasping world history and the beliefs of a large share of humanity.

## Card: Key terms
- **Christianity**: the religion centered on Jesus Christ.
- **The Bible**: the central Christian sacred text.
- **Monotheism**: belief in one God.
- **Denominations**: the major branches of Christianity.

## Card: Core beliefs
image: religionChristianityCoreBeliefsHero
Christianity is **monotheistic** (believing in one God) and centers on **Jesus**, whom Christians believe to be the son of God who taught love, forgiveness, and salvation. Core to the faith are beliefs about Jesus's life, death, and resurrection. The central sacred text is **the Bible**. Teachings emphasize love of God and others, forgiveness, and the hope of eternal life. These beliefs vary in emphasis across the faith's many branches.

## Card: A diverse faith
Christianity is far from uniform — it has many major **denominations** (broad branches such as Catholic, Orthodox, and Protestant, among others), each with distinct practices, traditions, and interpretations, though all share the central focus on Jesus. Practices include worship, prayer, reading scripture, and rituals that vary by branch. With its global spread, Christianity also takes many cultural forms. Recognizing this internal diversity is key to understanding it accurately.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: treating Christianity as a single uniform group (it has many diverse denominations); confusing the actions of some followers with the faith's core teachings; assuming all Christians practice or believe identically; and judging by stereotypes. Christianity is a large, diverse, monotheistic faith centered on Jesus — best understood respectfully and in its variety.

## Card: Quick recap
Christianity, the world's largest religion, is monotheistic and centered on Jesus, whom Christians regard as the son of God who taught love, forgiveness, and salvation; its sacred text is the Bible. It grew from Jewish roots and spread worldwide. It's highly diverse, with major denominations (Catholic, Orthodox, Protestant, and more) differing in practice but sharing the focus on Jesus. Best understood respectfully and in its variety.
