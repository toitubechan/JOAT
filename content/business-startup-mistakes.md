## Card: What it is
image: startupMistakesHero
**Startup mistakes** are the common, predictable pitfalls that cause new businesses to fail or struggle. Many failures aren't bad luck — they're avoidable errors made again and again by founders. Knowing the most common ones in advance helps you sidestep them, dramatically improving your odds. Learning from others' mistakes is far cheaper than making them all yourself.

## Card: Why it matters
Most new businesses fail, and often for the *same* recurring reasons. Understanding these common mistakes lets you avoid the predictable traps that sink so many startups — a huge advantage. You can't avoid every problem, but dodging the well-known pitfalls greatly improves your chances. Forewarned is forearmed in the risky early stage of a business.

## Card: Key terms
- **No market need**: building something people don't want.
- **Running out of cash**: poor financial management.
- **Premature scaling**: growing too fast too soon.
- **Avoidable mistakes**: predictable errors you can learn to sidestep.

## Card: The biggest pitfalls
image: biggestPitfallsHero
Some of the most common, deadly startup mistakes: building something with **no market need** (the top reason startups fail — no real demand); **running out of cash** (poor cash flow and financial management); the wrong team or poor early hires; ignoring customers and feedback; and **premature scaling** (spending and growing too fast before the business is proven). Notice these mostly aren't bad luck — they're predictable, avoidable errors.

## Card: How to avoid them
The good news: knowing these pitfalls helps you dodge them. Validate demand *before* building (avoiding no market need); manage cash flow carefully (avoiding running out); hire thoughtfully; stay close to customers and adapt; and don't scale until you've proven the business works. Most fatal startup mistakes have known antidotes. Combining awareness of common pitfalls with the discipline to act on them is what separates surviving businesses from the many that fail avoidably.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common meta-mistakes: thinking failure is mostly bad luck (much is predictable and avoidable); not learning from others' well-documented mistakes; ignoring early warning signs; and being overconfident that "it won't happen to me." The reality is that most startup failures repeat the same avoidable errors — no market need, running out of cash, premature scaling. Knowing them in advance is a real, cheap advantage.

## Card: Quick recap
Most startups fail for the same recurring, avoidable reasons: no real market need (the top one), running out of cash, the wrong team, ignoring customers, and premature scaling (growing too fast before proving the business). These mostly aren't bad luck — they're predictable. Avoid them by validating demand first, managing cash, hiring well, staying close to customers, and not scaling prematurely. Learning others' mistakes is cheap.
