## Card: What it is
image: seasoningHero
**Seasoning** is adjusting the flavor of food, above all with **salt**, but also acid, fat, herbs, and spices. It's the step that separates flat, bland cooking from food that tastes alive. Good seasoning isn't a fancy skill — it's mostly about salt, tasting, and a few simple flavor balancers.

## Card: Why it matters
The most common reason home cooking tastes "boring" is under-seasoning, especially too little salt. Learning to season well instantly improves everything you cook, often more than any other single skill. It's the cheapest, fastest upgrade to your food — and it's almost entirely about tasting and adjusting.

## Card: Key terms
- **Seasoning**: adjusting flavor, especially with salt.
- **Salt**: the key seasoning that makes flavors pop.
- **Acid**: sourness (lemon, vinegar) that brightens food.
- **Taste as you go**: sampling and adjusting while cooking.

## Card: Salt is the big one
**Salt** doesn't just make food salty — in the right amount, it makes food taste *more like itself*, waking up other flavors. Most home cooks under-salt. Season in layers as you cook, not just at the end, and **taste as you go**. The difference between bland and delicious is very often simply enough salt.

## Card: Balancing flavors
image: flavorBalance
Beyond salt, great flavor is about balance. A splash of **acid** (lemon juice, vinegar) brightens and lifts heavy or rich dishes. A little fat carries flavor and richness. Sweetness can balance sourness or bitterness. If a dish tastes flat even with enough salt, a squeeze of acid is often the missing piece.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: under-salting (the number one cause of bland food); seasoning only at the very end instead of in layers; never tasting while cooking, so problems aren't caught; and forgetting acid, which is the secret to brightening a dull dish. Cooking without tasting is like driving with your eyes closed.

## Card: Quick recap
Seasoning — above all salt — is what makes food taste vivid rather than bland. Salt makes flavors pop, so season in layers and taste as you go. A splash of acid brightens flat or rich dishes. Most "boring" home cooking is simply under-seasoned, so this is the highest-impact skill to master.
