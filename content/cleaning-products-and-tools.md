## Card: What it is
image: essentialKit
You don't need a cupboard full of specialized sprays. A handful of versatile **products** and **tools** handles almost all household cleaning. Knowing which few to buy — and one crucial safety rule about mixing them — covers you for nearly every surface and mess.

## Card: Why it matters
The cleaning aisle is designed to sell you a different bottle for every surface, most of which you don't need. A simple kit saves money and cupboard space. More importantly, knowing how to use products safely — especially never mixing certain ones — prevents genuinely dangerous accidents.

## Card: Key terms
- **All-purpose cleaner**: handles most everyday surfaces.
- **Microfiber cloth**: a reusable cloth that grabs dust and grime.
- **Abrasive**: a scrubbing product or pad for tough grime.
- **Disinfectant**: a product that kills germs.

## Card: The essential kit
A practical set: an **all-purpose cleaner**, a **disinfectant** for germ-prone areas, dish soap, a glass cleaner, a mild **abrasive** for stubborn grime, plus **microfiber cloths**, a sponge, a brush, and gloves. This small collection tackles the vast majority of cleaning jobs across the whole home.

## Card: Never mix these
image: neverMixCleaners
A vital safety rule: **never mix bleach with ammonia or other cleaners** — it can release toxic, potentially deadly gases. Use one product at a time, rinse between if switching, and work in a ventilated space. When in doubt, don't combine products. This is the one cleaning rule that can genuinely save your life.

## Card: Common mistakes
The dangerous mistake is mixing products, especially bleach with others. Common wasteful ones: buying a separate cleaner for every surface; using too much product (more isn't better and leaves residue); and using paper towels for everything when reusable microfiber cleans better and cheaper. Skipping gloves with harsh products is another.

## Card: Quick recap
A small kit — all-purpose cleaner, disinfectant, dish soap, glass cleaner, a mild abrasive, and microfiber cloths — handles almost everything. Use products sparingly, wear gloves with harsh ones, and never mix bleach with other cleaners, which can create toxic gas. Simple, safe, and effective beats a cupboard of bottles.
