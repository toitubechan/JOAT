## Card: What it is
image: econMarketsRoleOfMoneyHero
**Money** is the tool that makes modern markets work — a widely accepted medium for exchanging goods and services. Without it, we'd be stuck swapping goods directly. Understanding what money really is, and the jobs it does, reveals why it's one of humanity's most important inventions.

## Card: Why it matters
Money is so familiar we rarely think about *what it is* or *why it works*. Understanding its functions demystifies the economy, explains why money has value at all, and lays the groundwork for understanding banking, inflation, and finance. It's a foundational concept that's surprisingly profound once examined.

## Card: Key terms
- **Money**: a widely accepted medium of exchange.
- **Barter**: directly swapping goods without money.
- **Medium of exchange**: something used to trade for anything.
- **Store of value**: something that holds worth over time.

## Card: Money solves the barter problem
image: econMarketsRoleOfMoneyMoneySolvesTheBarterProblemHero
Before money, people used **barter** — swapping goods directly. But barter needs a "double coincidence of wants": you must find someone who has what you want *and* wants what you have. That's hugely inefficient. **Money** solves this by being a **medium of exchange** everyone accepts — you sell your goods for money, then use money to buy anything from anyone. It lubricates all trade.

## Card: The jobs money does
Money does three core jobs: a **medium of exchange** (accepted for trade), a **store of value** (holds worth so you can save and spend later), and a *unit of account* (a common measure for pricing everything). Crucially, money has value largely because people *trust* and accept it — modern money isn't backed by gold; its value rests on shared confidence and acceptance.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking money has inherent value (its value rests on trust and acceptance); not realizing barter's inefficiency is why money exists; assuming money must be backed by gold (modern money isn't); and confusing money (the medium) with wealth (what it can buy). Money is a tool built on shared trust.

## Card: Quick recap
Money is a widely accepted medium of exchange that solves barter's inefficiency — no need to find a double coincidence of wants. It serves as a medium of exchange, store of value, and unit of account. Its value rests largely on shared trust and acceptance, not on being backed by gold. Money is a foundational tool of markets.
