## Card: What it is
image: growthTimePrioritizationHero
**Prioritization** is deciding what to do first when everything competes for your time. The key skill is separating what's truly **important** (matters to your goals) from what merely feels **urgent** (demands attention now). Getting this right is the heart of effective time management.

## Card: Why it matters
Without prioritizing, you drift toward whatever's loudest — emails, notifications, others' requests — and your important work never gets done. Learning to prioritize means your time goes to what actually matters, not just what's shouting. It's the difference between a productive day and a busy-but-empty one.

## Card: Key terms
- **Urgent**: needs attention soon, time-pressured.
- **Important**: contributes to your goals and what matters.
- **Eisenhower matrix**: a tool sorting tasks by urgent and important.
- **The big rocks**: the most important tasks, done first.

## Card: Urgent vs. important
image: growthTimePrioritizationUrgentVsImportantHero
A classic insight: urgent and important are different. A ringing phone is **urgent** but maybe trivial; exercise or planning is **important** but rarely urgent — so it gets endlessly postponed. The **Eisenhower matrix** sorts tasks into four boxes by these two axes, helping you see what to do now, schedule, delegate, or drop.

## Card: Do the big rocks first
A useful image: if you fill a jar with sand first, the big rocks won't fit; put the **big rocks** (your few most important tasks) in first, and the small stuff fits around them. Practically: tackle your most important task early, before the day's urgent noise crowds it out. Protect time for what matters most.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: treating all urgent things as important and reacting all day; endlessly postponing important-but-not-urgent work (health, learning, planning) until it becomes a crisis; doing easy small tasks first to feel productive while the big one waits; and never deciding priorities at all, so everything feels equally pressing.

## Card: Quick recap
Prioritization means separating the truly important from the merely urgent — and they're often not the same. Use a tool like the Eisenhower matrix to sort tasks, and tackle your "big rocks" (most important tasks) first, before urgent noise takes over. Protect time for what matters, not just what shouts.
