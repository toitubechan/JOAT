## Card: What it is
image: fitnessFlexibilityMobilityHero
**Flexibility** is how far your muscles can stretch, and **mobility** is how well your joints move through their full range of motion. Together they're a key but often-neglected part of fitness, affecting how well you move, your comfort, and injury risk. Understanding them helps you move better and stay healthier. This is general educational knowledge, not medical or fitness advice.

## Card: Why it matters
Flexibility and mobility are often overlooked in favor of strength and cardio, yet they're crucial for moving well, staying comfortable, performing better, and reducing injury risk. Understanding them — and that they tend to decline without attention, especially with age and sedentary lifestyles — helps you maintain healthy movement throughout life. It's an underappreciated foundation of physical wellbeing.

## Card: Key terms
- **Flexibility**: how far muscles can stretch.
- **Mobility**: how well joints move through their range of motion.
- **Range of motion**: the extent a joint can move.
- **Use it or lose it**: movement maintained through regular use.

## Card: Flexibility vs. mobility
image: fitnessFlexibilityMobilityFlexibilityVsMobilityHero
**Flexibility** refers to how far your muscles can stretch (like reaching to touch your toes), while **mobility** is about how well your *joints* move through their full **range of motion** — including the strength and control to move freely. They're related but distinct: you can be flexible yet have poor joint mobility, or vice versa. Both contribute to moving well, comfortably, and safely. Good flexibility and mobility let you move through life's activities with ease and reduced strain.

## Card: Use it or lose it
A key principle is **use it or lose it** — flexibility and mobility tend to *decline* if not maintained, especially with age and sedentary lifestyles (lots of sitting). The good news: they can be maintained and improved through regular movement and appropriate stretching/mobility work. Neglecting them often leads to stiffness, discomfort, reduced movement, and higher injury risk over time. Incorporating regular movement and mobility work — not just strength and cardio — helps preserve healthy, comfortable movement throughout life.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: neglecting flexibility and mobility entirely (focusing only on strength and cardio); confusing flexibility (muscle stretch) with mobility (joint range of motion); assuming they'll stay fine without attention ("use it or lose it" applies); and not realizing sedentary lifestyles erode them. Maintaining flexibility and mobility through regular movement supports comfortable, healthy movement and reduces injury risk — a key, underappreciated part of fitness.

## Card: Quick recap
Flexibility (how far muscles stretch) and mobility (how well joints move through their range of motion) are related but distinct, and both are crucial for moving well, comfortably, and safely. They're often neglected in favor of strength and cardio, yet they decline without attention ("use it or lose it"), especially with age and sitting. Maintaining them through regular movement and mobility work preserves healthy movement and reduces injury risk throughout life.
