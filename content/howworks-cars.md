## Card: What it is
image: howworksCarsHero
A **car** turns stored energy (fuel or electricity) into motion through a chain of systems: an engine or motor makes power, a transmission delivers it to the wheels, and various systems steer, stop, and control the vehicle. Understanding the basic flow — from energy source to turning wheels — demystifies the machine most of us use but few understand.

## Card: Why it matters
Cars are central to daily life, yet how they work is a mystery to most. A basic understanding helps you grasp maintenance, diagnose problems, talk to mechanics, and understand the shift to electric vehicles. It turns the car from a black box into a comprehensible machine.

## Card: Key terms
- **Engine / motor**: produces the power to move the car.
- **Transmission**: delivers power to the wheels.
- **Combustion engine**: burns fuel to make power.
- **Drivetrain**: the system carrying power to the wheels.

## Card: From energy to motion
image: howworksCarsFromEnergyToMotionHero
The basic chain: an energy source (fuel or a battery) powers an **engine** (or electric **motor**) that produces turning force. The **transmission** and **drivetrain** carry that force to the wheels, turning them and moving the car. In a traditional car, a **combustion engine** burns fuel in small controlled explosions to push pistons, creating the turning motion. In an electric car, a motor turns the wheels directly using battery power — far simpler mechanically.

## Card: The other key systems
Beyond making the car go, several systems control it: **steering** turns the wheels to change direction; **brakes** create friction to slow and stop (converting motion to heat); **suspension** smooths the ride over bumps; and electronics manage much of the modern car. Together, these systems let you not just move, but control the vehicle safely. Each is a key part of the whole machine.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking a car is one single machine (it's many systems working together — engine, transmission, brakes, steering); not knowing fuel engines work by burning fuel in controlled explosions; imagining electric cars work the same way (they're mechanically simpler, with a motor turning wheels directly); and treating the car as an incomprehensible black box. It's a chain of understandable systems.

## Card: Quick recap
A car turns stored energy into motion: an engine (burning fuel) or electric motor produces turning force, and the transmission and drivetrain deliver it to the wheels. Other systems — steering, brakes (friction converting motion to heat), suspension, electronics — control the car. Electric cars are mechanically simpler. The car is a chain of understandable systems, not a black box.
