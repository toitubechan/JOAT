## Card: What it is
image: lawWhenYouNeedALawyerHero
Not every legal matter needs a lawyer — but some genuinely do. Knowing **when to get professional legal help** versus when you can handle something yourself saves money on the simple things and protects you on the serious ones. This is general educational guidance on recognizing the difference; when in real doubt about a significant matter, erring toward professional advice is wise.

## Card: Why it matters
Lawyers can be expensive, so people sometimes avoid them when they really need one (risking serious harm) — or hire them for things they could handle simply (wasting money). Knowing the difference helps you spend wisely and protect yourself when it truly matters. Getting professional help at the right moment can prevent costly mistakes on serious legal matters.

## Card: Key terms
- **Lawyer**: a qualified legal professional.
- **High-stakes matter**: a legal issue with serious consequences.
- **Complexity**: how legally complicated a matter is.
- **DIY-able**: simple matters you can often handle yourself.

## Card: When you likely need a lawyer
image: lawWhenYouNeedALawyerWhenYouLikelyNeedALawyerHero
Strongly consider a **lawyer** for matters that are **high-stakes** or complex: serious criminal charges; significant disputes or lawsuits; major contracts or business deals; buying property; complex family matters (like divorce or custody); large sums of money; anything where you face serious legal jeopardy; or any situation you genuinely don't understand and where getting it wrong could be very costly. The rule of thumb: the higher the stakes and **complexity**, the more a lawyer is worth it.

## Card: When you might not need one
Many smaller, simpler matters are often **DIY-able**: minor disputes (where small claims or direct resolution work), reading and understanding everyday contracts, simple forms, or low-stakes situations. For these, the cost of a lawyer may exceed the benefit. Some matters fall in between — where a one-off consultation or limited advice is enough rather than full representation. Match the level of help to the stakes and complexity.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: avoiding a lawyer on a serious, high-stakes matter to save money (risking far costlier outcomes); hiring expensive full representation for trivial matters; not even getting a one-off consultation when unsure; and assuming you can handle complex legal matters alone. The balance: handle simple, low-stakes things yourself, but get professional help for serious or complex matters — when in real doubt, lean toward advice.

## Card: Quick recap
Not every legal matter needs a lawyer. Get one for high-stakes or complex matters — serious charges, big disputes, major contracts, property, complex family issues, large sums, or anything you don't understand with costly risks. Handle simple, low-stakes things yourself (minor disputes, everyday contracts), where a lawyer's cost may exceed the benefit. Match help to the stakes; when in real doubt on something serious, lean toward professional advice.
