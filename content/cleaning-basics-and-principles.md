## Card: What it is
image: cleaningBasicsHero
Cleaning has a few underlying principles that, once understood, make it faster and more effective. It's not about scrubbing harder — it's about working *with* how dirt and cleaning products behave. A little method turns a dreaded chore into an efficient routine.

## Card: Why it matters
Most people clean inefficiently, redoing work and using more effort than needed. Understanding the basic logic — like why you clean top to bottom — saves time and gets better results. A clean home is also healthier, reducing germs, allergens, and pests, so doing it well genuinely matters.

## Card: Key terms
- **Top-to-bottom**: cleaning high surfaces before low ones.
- **Dwell time**: letting a product sit to do its work.
- **Cross-contamination**: spreading germs from one area to another.
- **Declutter**: removing items before cleaning surfaces.

## Card: Work top to bottom
image: topToBottomFlow
Gravity is your guide: clean from high surfaces **down** to the floor. Dust shelves before vacuuming, or you'll knock dirt onto a floor you just cleaned. Cleaning **top-to-bottom** means you never redo a finished area. This single principle saves more wasted effort than any product.

## Card: Let products do the work
Most cleaning sprays need **dwell time** — a minute or two sitting on the surface to dissolve grime — before you wipe. Spraying and instantly wiping skips the chemistry and means scrubbing harder for worse results. Apply, leave it briefly, then wipe. Patience replaces elbow grease.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common errors: cleaning bottom-to-top so you redo work; wiping products off instantly instead of letting them dwell; using the same cloth everywhere, spreading germs (**cross-contamination**); and trying to clean around clutter instead of clearing surfaces first. Each makes cleaning slower and less effective.

## Card: Quick recap
Clean smart, not hard: work top to bottom so you never redo areas, let products dwell before wiping, declutter surfaces first, and avoid spreading germs with one dirty cloth. Cleaning is mostly method — work with how dirt and products behave, and the chore gets faster and the results better.
