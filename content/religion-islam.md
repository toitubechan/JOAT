## Card: What it is
image: religionIslamHero
**Islam** is one of the world's largest religions, centered on belief in one God and the teachings revealed to the prophet **Muhammad**, recorded in the **Quran**. Its followers are called Muslims. This is a respectful, general overview of its core beliefs and practices — descriptive, not promoting or judging.

## Card: Why it matters
With well over a billion followers across the world, Islam has profoundly shaped history, culture, science, art, and the lives of a large share of humanity. Understanding its basics builds cultural literacy, counters stereotypes, and fosters respectful understanding. It's essential knowledge for grasping world history and the beliefs of many people.

## Card: Key terms
- **Islam**: the religion centered on submission to one God.
- **Muslim**: a follower of Islam.
- **The Quran**: Islam's central sacred text.
- **Five Pillars**: core practices of Islam.

## Card: Core beliefs
image: religionIslamCoreBeliefsHero
Islam is strictly **monotheistic** — believing in one God (called Allah in Arabic, the same word Arabic-speaking Christians and Jews use for God). Muslims believe God's guidance was revealed to a series of prophets, culminating in **Muhammad**, and recorded in **the Quran**, Islam's central sacred text. Islam shares roots with Judaism and Christianity (all Abrahamic faiths) and reveres many of the same prophets. Core values include submission to God, compassion, justice, and charity.

## Card: Practices and diversity
A well-known framework is the **Five Pillars** — core practices including the declaration of faith, prayer, charity, fasting (notably during Ramadan), and pilgrimage. Like other major religions, Islam is internally diverse, with different branches (such as Sunni and Shia) and many cultural expressions across the world's Muslim communities. Practices and interpretations vary, while the central beliefs in one God and the Quran are shared.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: treating Islam as a single uniform group (it has branches and great cultural diversity); confusing the actions of some individuals with the faith's teachings; judging it by stereotypes or extremes rather than understanding it; and not knowing it shares Abrahamic roots with Judaism and Christianity. Islam is a major, diverse, monotheistic faith best understood respectfully and accurately.

## Card: Quick recap
Islam is a major monotheistic religion centered on one God and the teachings revealed to the prophet Muhammad, recorded in the Quran; its followers are Muslims. It shares Abrahamic roots with Judaism and Christianity. Core practices are often framed as the Five Pillars (faith, prayer, charity, fasting, pilgrimage). It's internally diverse (e.g., Sunni and Shia) with many cultural forms, and is best understood respectfully and accurately.
