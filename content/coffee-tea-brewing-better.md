## Card: What it is
image: betterBrewingHero
A few universal principles make almost any coffee or tea taste better — the right water, the right ratio, and a little freshness and care. These small upgrades cost nothing or next to nothing, and often improve your daily cup more than any gadget. This card set gathers the simple wins.

## Card: Why it matters
People often assume better coffee or tea needs expensive equipment, when the real gains come from getting the fundamentals right. Knowing these principles means you can make a noticeably better cup today, with what you already have. It's the cheapest possible upgrade to a daily pleasure.

## Card: Key terms
- **Ratio**: the proportion of coffee or tea to water.
- **Water quality**: the taste and freshness of the water used.
- **Freshness**: how recently ingredients were opened or ground.
- **Consistency**: repeating what works to get reliable results.

## Card: Water matters more than you think
A drink that's mostly water depends heavily on **water quality**. If your tap water tastes off, your coffee or tea will too — filtered or fresh water can be a real improvement. Also use **freshly drawn** water heated once; repeatedly re-boiled water tastes flat. Good water is the easiest overlooked upgrade.

## Card: Ratio, freshness, and consistency
image: consistentBrew
Get the **ratio** roughly right — too little coffee or tea tastes weak and watery, too much wastes it and can taste harsh. Use **fresh** ingredients (recently opened or ground). And once you find what works, aim for **consistency** — repeat the same ratio and method, and you'll reliably get a good cup instead of a hit-or-miss one.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: using poor-tasting or repeatedly re-boiled water; using too little coffee or tea, then complaining it's weak; stale ingredients; pouring water at the wrong temperature; and changing everything each time so you never learn what works. Nail water, ratio, and freshness, and the rest follows.

## Card: Quick recap
Better coffee and tea come from fundamentals, not gadgets: use good, freshly heated water; get the ratio of coffee/tea to water roughly right; use fresh ingredients; and once something works, repeat it for consistency. These free or cheap tweaks improve your daily cup more than expensive equipment.
