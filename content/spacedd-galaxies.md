## Card: What it is
image: spaceddGalaxiesHero
A **galaxy** is an enormous collection of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter, bound together by gravity — often containing hundreds of billions of stars. Our Sun is just one star in our galaxy, the **Milky Way**, which is one of countless galaxies in the universe. Galaxies are the great building blocks of the cosmos.

## Card: Why it matters
Galaxies are how the universe organizes its stars on the largest scales. Understanding them puts our Sun and Earth in cosmic perspective — tiny parts of one galaxy among billions. They're central to astronomy, and grasping them is key to comprehending the universe's vast scale and structure.

## Card: Key terms
- **Galaxy**: a vast gravitationally-bound collection of stars.
- **Milky Way**: our home galaxy.
- **Spiral / elliptical**: common galaxy shapes.
- **Light-years**: the vast distances between galaxies.

## Card: Our galaxy and beyond
Our **Milky Way** is a galaxy of hundreds of billions of stars, with our Sun located in one of its spiral arms. On a dark night, the faint band of light across the sky is part of our own galaxy seen edge-on. Beyond it lie countless other galaxies, separated by enormous distances measured in **light-years**. The observable universe holds an estimated hundreds of billions of galaxies.

## Card: Galaxies come in shapes
image: galaxyShapesHero
Galaxies aren't all alike. Many, like the Milky Way, are **spiral** — flat, rotating disks with arms winding out from a bright center. Others are **elliptical** — rounded blobs of older stars. Some are irregular, with no clear shape. Most galaxies also have a giant black hole at their center. Galaxies can collide and merge over cosmic time, reshaping each other in slow-motion encounters.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: confusing a galaxy with the whole universe (a galaxy is one of billions); thinking our solar system *is* the galaxy (it's a tiny part of one); imagining galaxies are close together (they're separated by vast distances); and not realizing the faint band in the night sky is our own galaxy. Galaxies are the universe's grand building blocks.

## Card: Quick recap
A galaxy is a vast, gravity-bound collection of often hundreds of billions of stars, plus gas, dust, and dark matter. Our Sun sits in one spiral arm of the Milky Way, just one galaxy among an estimated hundreds of billions. Galaxies come in shapes — spiral, elliptical, irregular — often with central black holes, and can collide over cosmic time. They organize the universe's stars on the grandest scale.
