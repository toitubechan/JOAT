## Card: What it is
image: onlineNetworkingHero
**Online networking** uses professional platforms — most notably **LinkedIn** — to build and maintain career connections digitally. It lets you reach far beyond your immediate circle, stay visible to your network, and connect with people you'd never meet in person. Used well, it's a powerful complement to in-person networking.

## Card: Why it matters
Much professional networking now happens online, and a strong professional profile is increasingly expected. Recruiters search these platforms, and a good presence can bring opportunities to you. Knowing how to use them well — beyond just having an account — is a genuinely valuable modern career skill.

## Card: Key terms
- **Professional profile**: your online career page, like a living CV.
- **Connection request**: an invitation to link with someone.
- **Personalized message**: a tailored note, not a blank request.
- **Visibility**: how often your network sees you.

## Card: A strong profile
image: strongProfileHero
Your **professional profile** is a living CV that others find and judge. Make it complete and clear: a professional photo, a headline saying what you do, and a summary of your skills and experience. Recruiters and contacts form impressions from it, so a polished, up-to-date profile works for you around the clock, even while you sleep.

## Card: Connect and stay visible
When sending a **connection request**, add a brief **personalized message** rather than a blank invite — it dramatically improves acceptance and starts a real relationship. To stay useful, maintain **visibility**: occasionally share or comment on relevant content, so your network remembers you exist. A dormant profile no one sees does little for you.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: an incomplete or unprofessional profile; sending blank, generic connection requests; immediately pitching or asking for favors after connecting; being totally inactive so no one sees you; and treating online networking as a numbers game (collecting connections) rather than building real relationships. Quality and genuineness still win online.

## Card: Quick recap
Online networking via platforms like LinkedIn extends your reach and visibility. Build a complete, professional profile (it's a living CV recruiters find), send personalized connection requests rather than blank ones, and stay visible by occasionally engaging. Don't pitch instantly or chase raw numbers — genuine relationships matter online too.
