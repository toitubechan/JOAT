## Card: What it is
image: antibioticsHero
**Antibiotics** are medicines that kill or stop **bacteria** — they treat bacterial infections like some chest, ear, or wound infections. Crucially, they do *not* work against **viruses**, which cause most coughs, colds, and flu. Knowing this difference is key to using them properly. (General knowledge; a doctor decides if you need them.)

## Card: Why it matters
Antibiotics are one of medicine's greatest achievements, but overusing them is creating a serious global problem: **antibiotic resistance**. Understanding why they're not a cure-all — and why finishing a course matters — helps protect both your health and these vital medicines for everyone, now and in the future.

## Card: Key terms
- **Antibiotic**: a medicine that fights bacterial infections.
- **Bacteria vs. viruses**: different germs; antibiotics only fight bacteria.
- **Antibiotic resistance**: when bacteria evolve to survive antibiotics.
- **Course**: the full prescribed set of doses.

## Card: They don't work on viruses
A widespread misunderstanding: antibiotics only kill **bacteria**, not **viruses**. Colds, flu, and most sore throats and coughs are viral, so antibiotics do nothing for them. Taking them anyway won't help you recover and contributes to resistance. This is why a doctor may *not* prescribe antibiotics for a viral illness — that's correct, not neglect.

## Card: The resistance problem
image: antibioticResistanceHero
**Antibiotic resistance** happens when bacteria evolve to survive the drugs, making infections harder or impossible to treat. Overuse and misuse speed this up. Not finishing a prescribed **course** can leave the strongest bacteria alive to multiply. Resistance is a growing global threat, which is why using antibiotics only when truly needed matters for everyone.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common errors: expecting or demanding antibiotics for a cold or flu (they won't help); stopping a course early once you feel better; and saving leftover antibiotics or taking someone else's. Each fuels resistance or risks your health. Take antibiotics only when a doctor prescribes them, and exactly as directed.

## Card: Quick recap
Antibiotics fight bacteria, not viruses, so they don't help colds or flu. Overuse drives antibiotic resistance — bacteria evolving to survive them — a serious global threat. Take antibiotics only when prescribed, finish the full course, and never save or share them. Using them wisely protects everyone.
