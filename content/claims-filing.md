## Card: What it is
image: filingClaimHero
**Filing a claim** properly — with prompt notification, good evidence, and accurate information — gives you the best chance of a smooth, full payout. How you file makes a real difference to the outcome. Knowing the steps turns a daunting process into a manageable one. (General guidance; follow your insurer's specific process.)

## Card: Why it matters
A poorly filed claim can be delayed, reduced, or denied — even when you were genuinely entitled. Knowing how to file well means you get what you're owed with less hassle. Since claims happen at stressful times (after an accident or loss), having a clear sense of the steps reduces stress and improves the result.

## Card: Key terms
- **Notification**: telling your insurer about the loss.
- **Evidence**: proof of the loss (photos, receipts, reports).
- **Documentation**: the records supporting your claim.
- **Honesty**: giving accurate, truthful information.

## Card: Notify promptly and gather evidence
image: promptEvidence
Two essentials. **Notify** your insurer promptly — many policies require reporting within a certain time, and delay can jeopardize a claim. Then gather **evidence**: photos of damage, receipts proving ownership and value, police or incident reports where relevant, and any other **documentation**. Strong evidence is what supports a full payout; weak evidence is a top reason claims get reduced.

## Card: Be accurate and follow the process
File **honestly** and accurately — exaggerating or misrepresenting a claim is fraud and can void it entirely (and have legal consequences). Follow your insurer's specific process and forms, keep copies of everything you submit, and note who you spoke to and when. Being organized, truthful, and thorough makes the assessment smoother and protects you if any questions arise.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: delaying notification (risking the claim); providing weak or no evidence; exaggerating the loss (fraud, which can void everything); not following the insurer's process or keeping copies; and giving inconsistent information. File promptly, document thoroughly, be honest and accurate, and keep records of everything.

## Card: Quick recap
File a claim well by notifying your insurer promptly (delay can jeopardize it), gathering strong evidence (photos, receipts, reports), and being honest and accurate (exaggeration is fraud that can void the claim). Follow the insurer's process, keep copies of everything, and stay organized. Good filing means a smoother, fuller payout with less hassle.
