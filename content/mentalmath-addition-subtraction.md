## Card: What it is
image: dataMentalmathAdditionSubtractionHero
Adding and subtracting in your head becomes easy with a few **tricks** — mainly breaking numbers into friendly chunks and rounding. Instead of trying to mimic paper methods in your head, these approaches work *with* how the mind handles numbers. They make everyday sums quick and reliable.

## Card: Why it matters
Addition and subtraction are the most common everyday calculations — totaling a bill, working out change, adding up costs. Quick mental methods save time and help you check figures on the spot. These tricks are simple to learn and immediately useful, building confidence with the math you face most often.

## Card: Key terms
- **Left-to-right**: adding from the biggest digits first.
- **Breaking apart**: splitting numbers into easier chunks.
- **Rounding and adjusting**: rounding to an easy number, then correcting.
- **Counting up**: subtracting by counting forward.

## Card: Add left to right, in chunks
image: dataMentalmathAdditionSubtractionAddLeftToRightInChunksHero
Unlike paper (which goes right to left), mental addition is easier **left-to-right** and in chunks. For 47 + 38: add the tens (40 + 30 = 70), then the units (7 + 8 = 15), then combine (70 + 15 = 85). Or **break apart**: 47 + 38 = 47 + 40 − 2 = 85. Splitting numbers into friendly pieces and adding the big parts first makes mental addition smooth.

## Card: Round, adjust, and count up
Two more powerful tricks. **Rounding and adjusting**: to add 38, add 40 then subtract 2 — rounding to an easy number, then correcting. For subtraction, **counting up** is often easier than taking away: for 83 − 67, count up from 67 to 83 (67 → 70 is 3, → 83 is 13, so 16). This is exactly how you work out change, and it's far easier than mental "borrowing."

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: trying to do paper-style right-to-left methods in your head (left-to-right is easier mentally); not rounding to simplify (38 is hard, 40 is easy); struggling with subtraction by "taking away" instead of counting up; and not breaking numbers into manageable chunks. Work left-to-right, round and adjust, and count up for subtraction.

## Card: Quick recap
Add and subtract mentally with simple tricks: work left-to-right in chunks (add the tens, then the units), round to easy numbers then adjust (add 40 then subtract 2 for 38), and count up for subtraction (83 − 67 by counting forward, like working out change). These methods work with how the mind handles numbers, making everyday sums quick.
