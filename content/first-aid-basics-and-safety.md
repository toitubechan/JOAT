## Card: What it is
image: firstAidBasicsHero
**First aid** is the immediate help given to someone injured or ill before professional medical care arrives. The goal is simple: keep the person safe, prevent things getting worse, and support them until help comes. This is general awareness — a hands-on certified first aid course teaches the practical skills properly.

## Card: Why it matters
In an emergency, the first few minutes can matter enormously, and bystanders are often the only help present. Knowing the basics — and staying calm — can genuinely save a life or prevent further harm. Even simple knowledge, like what to check first, makes you far more useful than panicking.

## Card: Key terms
- **First aid**: immediate help before professionals arrive.
- **DRABC**: a common order of priorities (Danger, Response, Airway, Breathing, Circulation).
- **Casualty**: the injured or ill person.
- **Emergency services**: ambulance, fire, police — call them early.

## Card: Check for danger first
image: dangerFirst
Before rushing to help, check the scene is **safe** — for you and the casualty. A common rule starts with **Danger**: don't become a second casualty by ignoring traffic, fire, electricity, or other hazards. You can't help anyone if you're hurt too. Make the area safe, or move to safety, before acting.

## Card: A simple order of priorities
A widely taught sequence is **DRABC**: **D**anger (is it safe?), **R**esponse (is the person awake/responsive?), **A**irway (is it open?), **B**reathing (are they breathing?), **C**irculation (any severe bleeding?). This order tackles the most life-threatening things first. Call **emergency services** as early as you can.

## Card: Common mistakes
The biggest mistakes: rushing in without checking for danger and getting hurt; panicking and freezing instead of acting on basics; not calling emergency services early enough; and attempting things beyond your knowledge that could cause harm. When unsure, calling for help and following the dispatcher's instructions is often the safest move.

## Card: Quick recap
First aid is immediate help before professionals arrive: keep safe, prevent worsening, support the casualty. Always check for danger first — don't become a second casualty. Follow a priority order like DRABC, and call emergency services early. This is awareness only; take a proper course to learn the hands-on skills.
