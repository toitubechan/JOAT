## Card: What it is
image: filmDirectingHero
The **director** is the creative leader of a film — the person who guides the overall vision and brings together all the elements (performance, visuals, sound, story) into a coherent whole. Directing isn't about doing everything; it's about *vision and leadership*. Understanding the role demystifies what a director actually does and reveals why they're so central to a film's identity.

## Card: Why it matters
The director shapes a film's overall vision and is often credited as its key creative author, yet what they actually *do* is widely misunderstood. Understanding the role clarifies how films get their distinctive feel, why directors matter so much, and what creative leadership in a collaborative art looks like. It's essential for understanding filmmaking and for anyone wanting to make video.

## Card: Key terms
- **Director**: the creative leader guiding a film's vision.
- **Vision**: the overall creative idea for the film.
- **Leadership**: guiding and coordinating the whole team.
- **Working with actors**: directing performances.

## Card: Vision and leadership
image: filmDirectingVisionAndLeadershipHero
A director's core job is **vision** and **leadership** — not doing every task themselves. They hold the overall creative idea for the film (its look, tone, feel, and meaning) and guide everyone — actors, cinematographer, editor, and the whole crew — to realize it cohesively. The director makes countless creative decisions that shape the film's identity, ensuring all the collaborative elements serve one unified vision. They're the creative leader steering the ship, not the only person rowing.

## Card: Working with actors and the team
A major part of directing is **working with actors** — guiding performances to get the emotion and characterization the film needs (helping actors find the right portrayal, not just telling them where to stand). Equally, directing means communicating the vision to the whole team so everyone's specialized work aligns. Good directing is largely about *communication and judgment* — conveying what you want, making decisions, and bringing out the best in a large collaborative team to serve the film's vision.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking the director does everything or operates the camera themselves (they lead a team of specialists); imagining directing is just "telling people what to do" (it's vision, judgment, and communication); underrating working with actors and guiding performance; and not seeing the director as the unifying creative force. Directing is creative leadership — holding a vision and guiding a whole team to realize it.

## Card: Quick recap
The director is a film's creative leader, responsible for the overall vision and for guiding the whole team — actors, cinematographer, editor, crew — to realize it cohesively. Directing isn't doing every task; it's vision, judgment, communication, and leadership. A major part is working with actors to shape performances. The director makes the countless creative decisions that give a film its distinctive identity, unifying a collaborative art around one vision.
