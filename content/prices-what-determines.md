## Card: What it is
image: econPricesWhatDeterminesHero
The price of anything is shaped by several forces: supply and demand, the cost of producing it, competition, and what buyers are willing to pay. Prices aren't arbitrary or fixed — they emerge from these forces interacting. Understanding what determines a price helps you see the logic behind every price tag.

## Card: Why it matters
We see prices constantly but rarely think about *why* they're what they are. Understanding the forces behind prices helps you judge whether something is fairly priced, anticipate price changes, and shop more wisely. It pulls together economic ideas into a practical, everyday lens on the numbers you pay.

## Card: Key terms
- **Cost of production**: what it takes to make something.
- **Demand**: how much buyers want it.
- **Competition**: rival sellers affecting price.
- **Willingness to pay**: the most a buyer will pay.

## Card: The forces behind a price
image: econPricesWhatDeterminesTheForcesBehindAPriceHero
A price is shaped by: the **cost of production** (a seller usually needs to cover costs and make a profit), **demand** (high demand supports higher prices), **competition** (rivals push prices down), and buyers' **willingness to pay** (the ceiling). A price tends to settle where these forces balance — high enough to be worth selling, low enough that people will buy.

## Card: Value isn't just cost
A key insight: price isn't simply "cost plus a bit." It's also about *value to the buyer*. Something cheap to produce can command a high price if people value it highly and competition is limited (a popular brand, a unique experience). Conversely, something costly to make won't sell above what buyers will pay. Price reflects both cost *and* perceived value.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking price is just production cost plus a fixed markup (value and demand matter too); assuming prices are arbitrary or pure greed (they emerge from real forces); believing a high price always means high quality (it can reflect branding or scarcity); and not seeing competition's role in keeping prices in check. Prices are shaped by several interacting forces.

## Card: Quick recap
A price is shaped by the cost of production, demand, competition, and buyers' willingness to pay — settling where these forces balance. Price reflects both cost *and* perceived value, not just cost plus a markup. Prices aren't arbitrary or pure greed; they emerge from real forces. Seeing those forces helps you judge and anticipate prices.
