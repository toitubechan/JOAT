## Card: What it is
image: firstHiresHero
**Hiring your first people** is a major step in growing a business — bringing in others to help when you can't do everything alone. Your first hires have an outsized impact on a small business, so getting these decisions right matters enormously. Understanding the basics helps you hire well and avoid common, costly mistakes when building your early team.

## Card: Why it matters
For a small or new business, each early hire is a big deal — they shape the culture, do critical work, and represent a major cost and commitment. A great early hire can transform the business; a poor one can be very damaging when you're small. Understanding how to approach early hiring helps you grow your team wisely at a pivotal stage.

## Card: Key terms
- **First hires**: the earliest employees of a growing business.
- **Outsized impact**: how much each early hire matters in a small team.
- **Cultural fit**: how well someone aligns with the team's values and ways.
- **Hire slow, fire fast**: a common hiring principle.

## Card: Early hires have outsized impact
image: outsizedHireImpactHero
In a small business, each person is a large fraction of the team, so **first hires** have an **outsized impact** — far more than in a big company where one hire is a tiny part. They influence the work, the culture, and the business's direction. This is why early hiring deserves real care: the right people can accelerate everything, while the wrong ones can drag down a whole small team. Hire thoughtfully, not desperately.

## Card: Hiring well
Some principles for hiring well: hire for what you genuinely *need* (be clear on the role and why); consider both skills *and* **cultural fit** (how they work with others and align with your values), which matters hugely in a small team; don't rush out of desperation (a common mantra is "**hire slow, fire fast**" — be careful choosing, but act decisively if it's not working). And remember each hire is a real cost and commitment, so hire when the need and the finances genuinely support it.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: hiring desperately or too fast just to fill a gap; focusing only on skills while ignoring cultural fit (a poor fit harms a small team); hiring before the business can really afford it; keeping a clearly wrong hire too long out of reluctance; and unclear expectations about the role. Early hires matter enormously, so hire thoughtfully — clear on the need, mindful of fit, and not out of desperation.

## Card: Quick recap
Hiring your first people is a pivotal step, because in a small business each hire has an outsized impact on work, culture, and direction. Hire thoughtfully, not desperately: be clear on the role and why you need it, weigh both skills *and* cultural fit, and hire only when the need and finances support it. A common principle is "hire slow, fire fast." Getting early hires right can transform the business.
