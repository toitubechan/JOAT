## Card: What it is
image: businessModelHero
A **business model** is how a business creates value and makes money — what it offers, to whom, and how it earns revenue. Two businesses selling similar things can have very different models. Understanding business models clarifies how companies actually turn an idea into income, which is essential for building (or understanding) any business.

## Card: Why it matters
A great product with no viable way to make money isn't a business. Understanding business models helps you figure out *how* an idea will actually generate income, recognize different ways companies earn, and avoid the trap of building something with no path to revenue. It's the bridge between having an idea and running a sustainable business.

## Card: Key terms
- **Business model**: how a business creates value and earns money.
- **Revenue**: the money a business takes in.
- **Value proposition**: the benefit a business offers customers.
- **Revenue streams**: the different ways a business earns.

## Card: How value becomes money
image: valueToMoneyHero
A business model answers key questions: What **value proposition** do you offer (what benefit, to which customers)? And how do you turn that into **revenue** (money in)? The model connects *creating value for customers* with *capturing some of that value as income*. A viable business needs both — genuine value for customers *and* a workable way to get paid for it. One without the other isn't a sustainable business.

## Card: Different ways to earn
There are many **revenue streams** and model types. Common ones: selling a product or service directly (one-time sales); *subscriptions* (recurring payments for ongoing access); *freemium* (free basic version, paid premium); advertising (free to users, paid by advertisers); marketplaces (connecting buyers and sellers for a cut); and more. The same idea can sometimes use different models, and the choice greatly affects how the business works, grows, and earns. Choosing the right model matters as much as the product.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: focusing on the product while ignoring *how* it'll make money (a model is essential); assuming there's only one way to earn (many models exist); copying a model that doesn't fit your situation; and not realizing the model choice deeply shapes the business. A business needs both real value for customers and a workable way to get paid — the business model connects the two.

## Card: Quick recap
A business model is how a business creates value and makes money — its value proposition (benefit to customers) plus how it earns revenue. A viable business needs both genuine customer value *and* a workable way to get paid; one without the other fails. There are many revenue models — direct sales, subscriptions, freemium, advertising, marketplaces — and the choice deeply shapes how the business works. The model matters as much as the product.
