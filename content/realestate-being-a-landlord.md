## Card: What it is
image: realestateBeingALandlordHero
**Being a landlord** means owning property and renting it to tenants for income. It's often imagined as easy "passive income," but the reality involves real responsibilities, costs, and work. Understanding what being a landlord actually entails helps you decide if it's right for you and do it well. This is general educational knowledge, not legal or financial advice.

## Card: Why it matters
Many people consider becoming landlords, often drawn by the idea of passive rental income. Understanding the genuine responsibilities, costs, and challenges — beyond the rosy image — helps you make a realistic decision and, if you proceed, be a good and successful landlord. It prevents the common surprise of discovering it's more work and risk than expected.

## Card: Key terms
- **Landlord**: someone who rents out property they own.
- **Responsibilities**: the legal and practical duties of a landlord.
- **Not passive**: the reality that it requires real work.
- **Costs and risks**: the genuine downsides to account for.

## Card: It's not truly "passive"
image: realestateBeingALandlordItSNotTrulyPassiveHero
A common myth: renting out property is effortless passive income. In reality, being a **landlord** involves real work and **responsibilities**: finding and vetting tenants, maintaining the property and handling repairs (often urgently), dealing with issues and complaints, managing finances and legal obligations, and handling problems like late payment or difficult tenants. It can provide good income, but it's a genuine ongoing commitment — **not passive** in the way it's often imagined.

## Card: Costs, risks, and responsibilities
Landlords face real **costs and risks**: maintenance and repairs, periods with no tenant (no income but ongoing costs), problem tenants or non-payment, taxes and fees, and legal **responsibilities** (landlords have legal duties to tenants — safety, fair treatment, proper process — that vary by location and must be followed). Being a good landlord means meeting these responsibilities, treating tenants fairly, and budgeting for the costs and risks. Done well, it can be rewarding; done carelessly, it's stressful and costly.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: expecting truly passive, effortless income (it's real work and responsibility); underestimating costs and risks (maintenance, vacancies, problem tenants); not knowing or meeting legal responsibilities to tenants (which vary by location); poor tenant vetting; and not budgeting for the inevitable repairs and gaps. Being a landlord can be rewarding but is a genuine commitment with real duties — go in realistically.

## Card: Quick recap
Being a landlord means renting out property for income — but it's not the effortless passive income it's often imagined to be. It involves real work and responsibilities: finding and vetting tenants, maintenance and urgent repairs, finances, and legal duties to tenants (which vary by location). Real costs and risks include vacancies, problem tenants, and ongoing expenses. Done well it can be rewarding, but go in realistically, not expecting easy money.
