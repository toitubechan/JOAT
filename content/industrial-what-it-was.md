## Card: What it is
image: histIndustrialWhatItWasHero
The **Industrial Revolution** was the period (starting around the 1700s) when societies shifted from making things by hand to making them with machines in factories. Beginning in Britain and spreading worldwide, it transformed how people worked, lived, and organized society — one of history's greatest turning points.

## Card: Why it matters
The Industrial Revolution created the modern world — our economy, cities, technology, and ways of working all trace back to it. Understanding it explains how humanity went from a mostly rural, handcraft world to an industrial, urban one in a remarkably short time. It's essential for understanding modern life and where it came from.

## Card: Key terms
- **Industrial Revolution**: the shift to machine-based manufacturing.
- **Factory**: a building where goods are mass-produced by machines.
- **Mechanization**: replacing hand labor with machines.
- **Mass production**: making goods in large quantities.

## Card: From hand to machine
image: histIndustrialWhatItWasFromHandToMachineHero
Before, most goods were made slowly by hand, by individual craftspeople. The Industrial Revolution introduced **mechanization** — machines doing the work — gathered in **factories**. This enabled **mass production**: making goods in huge quantities, faster and cheaper than ever. A society that had changed slowly for millennia suddenly transformed within a few generations. It was a fundamental break with how humans had always lived and worked.

## Card: Why it started and spread
The revolution began in Britain, driven by a combination of new technologies (especially the steam engine), available resources (like coal), capital, and other factors. Once underway, it spread to other countries, each industrializing in turn. The pattern — mechanize production, build factories, mass-produce goods — repeated and reshaped nation after nation, eventually transforming much of the world.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking it was a single event rather than a decades-long, ongoing process; assuming it happened everywhere at once (it began in Britain and spread gradually); underrating how radically it broke from all prior human life; and seeing it as purely positive or purely negative (it was transformative, with both benefits and serious costs).

## Card: Quick recap
The Industrial Revolution, from around the 1700s, shifted societies from handcraft to machine-based factory production, enabling mass production of goods. Beginning in Britain and spreading worldwide, it transformed work, cities, and society within a few generations — a fundamental break from how humans had always lived. It created the foundations of the modern world.
