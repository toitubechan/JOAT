## Card: What it is
image: cuisineIndianHero
**Indian cuisine** is one of the world's richest food traditions, renowned for its masterful use of **spices** and its enormous regional diversity. Far from being a single "curry" cuisine, Indian food is a vast, sophisticated array of regional styles built on the art of layering spices. Understanding it reveals one of the most complex and varied culinary traditions on Earth.

## Card: Why it matters
Indian food is beloved worldwide but often reduced to a few dishes or the vague idea of "curry," missing its incredible depth and diversity. Understanding it — its spice mastery and regional variety — deepens appreciation and improves your cooking and eating. Given India's vast population and ancient culinary heritage, this is a major and rewarding food tradition to understand properly.

## Card: Key terms
- **Indian cuisine**: the diverse food traditions of India.
- **Spices**: the foundation of Indian flavor, used artfully.
- **Layering**: building complex flavor through combined spices.
- **Regional diversity**: the vast variation across India.

## Card: The art of spices
image: cuisineIndianTheArtOfSpicesHero
The heart of Indian cooking is the masterful use of **spices** — not just for heat, but for complex, layered flavor. Indian cuisine uses many spices in skilled combinations, often **layering** them (adding at different stages, toasting, grinding) to build deep, nuanced flavor. "Curry" is a vague western catch-all; authentic Indian cooking involves sophisticated, varied spice blends specific to each dish and region. This spice artistry is what gives Indian food its distinctive depth and complexity.

## Card: Enormous regional diversity
Indian cuisine has vast **regional diversity** — India is huge, and its food varies enormously by region, with distinct ingredients, dishes, spice blends, and styles (northern and southern Indian cooking, for instance, differ greatly). There's also wide variety in vegetarian and non-vegetarian traditions. What's served as "Indian food" abroad is often a narrow, altered slice. Authentic Indian cuisine is a vast, diverse family of regional traditions — far richer than the stereotype suggests.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking Indian food is just "curry" (a vague western term; real Indian cooking has sophisticated, varied spice blends); treating it as one cuisine (it's enormously regionally diverse); assuming it's all very spicy-hot (spices are about complex flavor, not just heat); and judging by limited westernized versions. Indian cuisine is a rich, diverse tradition built on the art of spices.

## Card: Quick recap
Indian cuisine is one of the world's richest food traditions, built on the masterful use of spices — layered artfully for complex, nuanced flavor, not just heat ("curry" is a vague western catch-all). It's also enormously regionally diverse, varying greatly across India in ingredients, dishes, and styles. Far beyond the narrow versions served abroad, authentic Indian food is a vast, sophisticated family of regional traditions centered on spice artistry.
