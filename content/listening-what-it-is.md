## Card: What it is
image: activeListeningHero
**Active listening** is fully focusing on understanding what someone is saying, rather than just hearing words while planning your reply. It's a deliberate skill, not the passive default of "hearing." Active listeners absorb the full message — words, meaning, and feeling — and the speaker can tell the difference.

## Card: Why it matters
Most people are poor listeners — distracted, interrupting, or just waiting for their turn to talk. Genuine listening builds trust, prevents misunderstandings, and makes people feel valued. It's one of the most underrated skills in relationships and work: being truly heard is rare, and offering it sets you apart.

## Card: Key terms
- **Active listening**: focusing fully on understanding the speaker.
- **Passive hearing**: registering words without real attention.
- **Listening to reply**: planning your response instead of understanding.
- **Presence**: being fully attentive in the moment.

## Card: Listening to understand, not reply
image: understandNotReplyHero
The core distinction: most people listen to *reply* — half-attending while forming their response. Active listening means listening to *understand* — giving full **presence** to the speaker, taking in their meaning before thinking about your answer. It sounds simple, but genuinely doing it is rare and surprisingly powerful. People feel the difference immediately.

## Card: Why it's so powerful
Being truly listened to is rare, so when you offer it, people feel respected, understood, and valued — which builds trust fast. Active listening also means you actually grasp what's being said, avoiding the misunderstandings that plague rushed, half-attentive conversations. It improves your relationships and your decisions, since you're working from real understanding.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: listening to reply rather than understand; interrupting or finishing people's sentences; getting distracted (especially by phones); and assuming you already know what they'll say. Each signals you're not truly present. The fix is intentional: focus fully on the speaker, and hold your response until you've genuinely understood.

## Card: Quick recap
Active listening is fully focusing on understanding someone, not just hearing them or waiting to reply. It's a deliberate, rare skill that builds trust, prevents misunderstandings, and makes people feel valued. Listen to understand, give full presence, and hold your response — being truly heard is rare, and offering it sets you apart.
