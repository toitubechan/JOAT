## Card: What it is
image: growthHabitsTheHabitLoopHero
Every habit follows a simple pattern called the **habit loop**: a **cue** triggers it, a **routine** is the behavior itself, and a **reward** makes the brain want to repeat it. Understanding this three-part loop is the key to building, breaking, and changing any habit on purpose.

## Card: Why it matters
You can't deliberately change something you don't understand. The habit loop reveals the "mechanics" behind your behaviors, so instead of vaguely trying to "be better," you can target the specific parts of the loop. It's the practical foundation for every habit-change technique that follows.

## Card: Key terms
- **Cue**: the trigger that starts a habit.
- **Routine**: the behavior you actually perform.
- **Reward**: the benefit that reinforces the habit.
- **Craving**: the anticipation of the reward that drives the loop.

## Card: The three parts
image: growthHabitsTheHabitLoopTheThreePartsHero
The loop: a **cue** (a time, place, feeling, or preceding action) triggers the brain; you perform the **routine** (the habit itself); and you get a **reward** (pleasure, relief, satisfaction). The brain notes "that felt good" and wires the loop tighter. Over time, the cue alone creates a **craving** for the reward, pulling you into the routine automatically.

## Card: Why the reward matters most
The **reward** is what makes a habit stick — it's why the brain bothers to automate the behavior. This is why healthy habits with delayed or invisible rewards (exercise, saving) are hard to form, while instant-reward habits (snacking, scrolling) form easily. Understanding this helps you engineer rewards into the habits you *want*.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: trying to change a habit without identifying its cue and reward; ignoring the reward, which is what truly drives the loop; assuming habits are just about the visible routine; and not realizing the same craving can be satisfied in different ways — which is exactly what lets you swap a bad routine for a better one.

## Card: Quick recap
Every habit runs on a loop: a cue triggers it, a routine is the behavior, and a reward reinforces it, building a craving over time. The reward is the engine — which is why instant-reward habits form easily and delayed-reward ones are hard. Understanding the loop lets you change habits by targeting its parts.
