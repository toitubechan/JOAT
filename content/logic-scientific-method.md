## Card: What it is
image: logicScientificMethodHero
The **scientific method** is a systematic way of figuring out how the world works — by forming ideas, testing them against evidence, and revising based on results. It's not just for scientists; it's a powerful way of thinking that anyone can use to separate what's true from what merely seems true. It's arguably humanity's best tool for reliable knowledge.

## Card: Why it matters
The scientific method is why modern knowledge is so reliable — it's a systematic way to test ideas against reality rather than just believing them. Understanding it helps you think more rigorously, evaluate claims, and apply evidence-based thinking to your own life and decisions. It's a foundation of critical thinking and the engine of human progress.

## Card: Key terms
- **Hypothesis**: a testable idea or proposed explanation.
- **Experiment / test**: a way to check a hypothesis against evidence.
- **Evidence**: observations that support or contradict an idea.
- **Revising beliefs**: updating ideas based on what the evidence shows.

## Card: Test ideas against reality
image: logicScientificMethodTestIdeasAgainstRealityHero
The core of the scientific method: instead of just believing an idea, you *test* it. You form a **hypothesis** (a testable idea), then design an **experiment or observation** to check it against **evidence** from the real world. Crucially, you let the evidence decide — if it contradicts your idea, you revise the idea, not the evidence. This willingness to test beliefs and follow the evidence is what makes it so powerful.

## Card: Self-correcting and humble
A key strength is that the scientific method is *self-correcting*. Conclusions are always open to being tested again and revised if new evidence emerges (**revising beliefs**). Science doesn't claim final certainty; it builds reliable knowledge by constantly checking and updating. This humility — holding beliefs provisionally and changing them when evidence demands — is a feature, not a weakness. It's why scientific knowledge improves over time rather than getting stuck.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking the scientific method is only for labs (it's a general way of thinking anyone can use); seeing science changing its mind as weakness (self-correction is its strength); believing it gives absolute certainty (it builds reliable, revisable knowledge); and skipping the testing step (the whole point is testing ideas against evidence, not just believing them). Test, follow the evidence, and stay open to revising.

## Card: Quick recap
The scientific method figures out what's true by forming testable ideas (hypotheses), checking them against real-world evidence, and revising beliefs based on results — letting evidence decide. It's self-correcting: conclusions stay open to revision as new evidence emerges, which is its strength, not weakness. It's not just for labs but a powerful, humble way of thinking that anyone can use to reason rigorously.
