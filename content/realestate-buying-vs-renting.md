## Card: What it is
image: realestateBuyingVsRentingHero
**Buying vs renting** a home is one of life's big financial decisions — and there's no universal "right" answer. Each has real advantages and trade-offs depending on your situation, finances, and plans. Understanding the genuine pros and cons (beyond the common myth that "renting is throwing money away") helps you decide wisely. This is general educational knowledge, not financial advice.

## Card: Why it matters
Housing is usually your largest expense, and the buy-vs-rent choice has major financial and lifestyle consequences. Many people decide based on myths or pressure rather than their actual situation. Understanding the real trade-offs helps you make a sound decision for *your* circumstances, rather than following oversimplified advice in either direction.

## Card: Key terms
- **Buying**: purchasing a property, usually with a mortgage.
- **Renting**: paying to live in someone else's property.
- **Equity**: the ownership value you build by buying.
- **Flexibility**: the freedom renting often provides.

## Card: The case for each
image: realestateBuyingVsRentingTheCaseForEachHero
**Buying** can build **equity** (you own an asset that may grow in value, and payments build ownership rather than going to a landlord), offers stability and freedom to modify your home, and can be cheaper long-term in some markets. **Renting** offers **flexibility** (easy to move), lower upfront costs (no large deposit or buying fees), no responsibility for maintenance and repairs, and freedom from property-market risk. Each genuinely suits different situations.

## Card: It depends on your situation
There's no universal answer — it depends on factors like: how long you'll stay (buying's high upfront costs usually need years to pay off, so renting often wins short-term); your finances (can you afford a deposit and the true costs of owning?); local market (prices and rents vary hugely); and lifestyle (do you value flexibility or stability?). The common claim that "renting is throwing money away" is a myth — renting buys flexibility and freedom from costs, and buying has large hidden costs too.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: believing "renting is always throwing money away" (it buys flexibility and avoids ownership costs; buying has big hidden costs too); assuming buying is always the smart move (it depends on situation and timeframe); ignoring the true costs of owning (maintenance, fees, interest); and deciding by pressure rather than your circumstances. The right choice depends on your finances, plans, timeframe, and local market.

## Card: Quick recap
Buying vs renting has no universal answer — it depends on your situation. Buying builds equity, offers stability, and can win long-term, but has high upfront and hidden costs. Renting offers flexibility, lower upfront cost, and no maintenance burden, often winning short-term. "Renting is throwing money away" is a myth — it buys flexibility, and buying has big costs too. Decide based on your finances, timeframe, plans, and local market.
