## Card: What it is
image: musicIndustryHero
The **music industry** is the business side of music — how music is produced, distributed, promoted, and monetized, and how artists and companies make money from it. It has been radically transformed by streaming and the internet. Understanding the basics reveals how music actually reaches you and how (and whether) artists earn from it. This is a general overview.

## Card: Why it matters
Music is a huge global industry, and how it works affects artists, listeners, and culture. The industry has changed dramatically with streaming, reshaping how money flows and how artists build careers. Understanding it helps you grasp how the music you love is made and monetized, why artists earn the way they do, and the realities behind a much-romanticized field.

## Card: Key terms
- **Music industry**: the business of producing and monetizing music.
- **Streaming**: accessing music online for subscription or ad-supported play.
- **Royalties**: payments artists earn from their music being used.
- **Independent vs. label**: self-managed artists vs. those signed to companies.

## Card: How money flows
image: musicIndustryHowMoneyFlowsHero
Music makes money in several ways: **streaming** (now dominant — listeners pay subscriptions or generate ad revenue, and rights-holders earn small per-play **royalties**), live performances and touring (a major income source for many artists), merchandise, licensing (music used in films, ads, games), and more. A key reality: per-stream royalties are tiny, so streaming income usually requires huge play counts, and many artists rely heavily on touring, merchandise, and other income beyond recordings.

## Card: A transformed landscape
Streaming and the internet have transformed the industry. Music is now mostly accessed (streamed) rather than owned, changing how money flows. And the internet has lowered barriers: artists can now record, release, and promote music **independently** — directly to listeners — rather than needing a record **label**. This means more artists can reach audiences, but also more competition and the challenge of standing out. The industry is more accessible than ever to enter, but earning a living from music remains genuinely hard for most.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: assuming streaming pays artists generously (per-stream royalties are tiny; it takes huge numbers); thinking recording income is artists' main earnings (touring, merch, and licensing are often bigger); believing you still need a label to release music (independent release is now viable); and imagining music is easy money (most artists earn modestly). The industry is accessible but financially tough for most.

## Card: Quick recap
The music industry is the business of producing, distributing, and monetizing music — transformed by streaming and the internet. Money flows from streaming (dominant, but tiny per-play royalties requiring huge play counts), live touring (often a major income source), merchandise, and licensing. The internet lets artists release independently without a label, lowering barriers — but competition is fierce and earning a living from music remains hard for most.
