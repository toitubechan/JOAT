## Card: What it is
image: currentVoltageResistanceHero
Three key quantities describe electricity: **current** (how much charge flows), **voltage** (the push driving it), and **resistance** (how much the material opposes the flow). Understanding these three, and how they relate, is the heart of understanding electricity in any practical sense.

## Card: Why it matters
These three terms appear everywhere — on batteries, chargers, appliances, and bills. Knowing what they mean makes electrical specs understandable and explains why devices behave as they do. They're the vocabulary of electricity, and they're far simpler than they sound once you have a good analogy.

## Card: Key terms
- **Current (amps)**: the rate of charge flow.
- **Voltage (volts)**: the push or pressure driving the flow.
- **Resistance (ohms)**: opposition to the flow.
- **Water analogy**: a pipe model for understanding all three.

## Card: The water analogy
image: electricityWaterAnalogy
The classic picture: imagine water in pipes. **Voltage** is the water *pressure* pushing it along. **Current** is the *amount* of water flowing. **Resistance** is how *narrow* the pipe is, opposing the flow. More pressure (voltage) pushes more flow (current); a narrower pipe (higher resistance) reduces flow. This analogy makes all three intuitive.

## Card: How they relate
The three are linked: for a given **voltage**, higher **resistance** means lower **current** (a narrower pipe lets less through). Crank up the voltage and current rises; add resistance and current falls. This simple relationship (formalized as Ohm's law) explains most basic electrical behavior — why some devices draw more power, why resistors limit current, and so on.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common confusions: mixing up voltage and current (pressure versus flow — they're different); thinking high voltage alone is what's dangerous, when it's the current through the body that harms; and not realizing resistance is what controls flow for a given voltage. The water analogy keeps these straight: pressure, flow, and pipe width.

## Card: Quick recap
Electricity is described by current (how much charge flows), voltage (the push driving it), and resistance (opposition to flow). The water analogy makes them intuitive: pressure, flow rate, and pipe width. They're linked — higher resistance means less current for a given voltage. These three terms are the vocabulary of electricity.
