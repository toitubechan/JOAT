## Card: What it is
image: realestateClosingCostsHero
When buying property, the purchase price isn't the only cost — there are **closing costs and fees** on top, which can add up to a significant amount. Many buyers are caught out by these extra costs. Understanding what they are helps you budget properly and avoid an unpleasant surprise. This is general educational knowledge; specific costs vary by location.

## Card: Why it matters
Buyers often focus only on the price and deposit, then are shocked by thousands in additional costs at purchase — sometimes leaving them short. Knowing about closing costs and fees lets you budget accurately and avoid a stressful, costly surprise. It also helps you compare the *true* cost of buying versus other options. It's essential practical knowledge for any property purchase.

## Card: Key terms
- **Closing costs**: the fees and expenses paid to complete a purchase.
- **Hidden fees**: costs buyers often don't anticipate.
- **Upfront costs**: money needed at or before purchase, beyond the price.
- **True cost**: the full cost of buying, not just the price tag.

## Card: Costs beyond the price
image: realestateClosingCostsCostsBeyondThePriceHero
Beyond the property price and deposit, buying involves various **closing costs** and **upfront costs** that vary by location but commonly include: legal/conveyancing fees, taxes or duties on the purchase (which can be substantial), inspection or survey fees, mortgage-related fees, valuation fees, and insurance. Individually some seem small, but together they can add up to a significant sum on top of the price — money you need available at purchase, not just for the deposit.

## Card: Budget for the true cost
The key lesson: budget for the **true cost** of buying, not just the price tag and deposit. Research the typical closing costs and fees for your situation and location *before* committing, so you know the full amount needed and aren't caught short. Some fees are negotiable or vary between providers, so it can pay to compare. Knowing the complete picture — price plus all the extra costs — lets you plan properly and avoid the common, stressful surprise of unexpected fees at the finish line.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: budgeting only for the price and deposit, forgetting closing costs (and being caught short); underestimating taxes/duties, which can be substantial; not researching the typical fees for your location in advance; and not comparing providers for negotiable fees. Always budget for the *true* cost — price plus all the extra closing costs and fees — to avoid an unpleasant, expensive surprise.

## Card: Quick recap
Buying property involves closing costs and fees on top of the price and deposit — commonly legal fees, taxes/duties, inspection and mortgage fees, valuation, and insurance — which together can be a significant sum needed at purchase. Many buyers are caught out by these. Budget for the *true* cost (price plus all extras), research typical fees for your location in advance, and compare providers where fees are negotiable.
