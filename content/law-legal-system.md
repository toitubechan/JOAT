## Card: What it is
image: lawLegalSystemHero
The **legal system** is how a society makes, applies, and enforces laws, and resolves disputes — through laws, courts, judges, and legal processes. It can seem complex and intimidating, but the basic structure is understandable. Knowing how it works helps you navigate legal situations and understand your rights. This is general educational knowledge; specifics vary by country, and for real legal issues, consult a professional.

## Card: Why it matters
The legal system affects everyone — it governs contracts, rights, disputes, crime, and more. Understanding the basics demystifies it, helps you know when and how to engage, and reduces the intimidation many feel around anything legal. It's foundational practical knowledge for navigating life, even if you never set foot in a court.

## Card: Key terms
- **Legal system**: how laws are made, applied, and enforced.
- **Civil vs. criminal**: disputes between parties vs. crimes against society.
- **Court**: where legal disputes are decided.
- **Jurisdiction**: the area or system whose laws apply.

## Card: Civil vs. criminal
image: lawLegalSystemCivilVsCriminalHero
A key distinction: **criminal** law deals with offenses considered crimes against society (like theft or assault), usually prosecuted by the state, with penalties like fines or imprisonment. **Civil** law deals with disputes between people or organizations (like contracts, property, or compensation), where one party sues another, typically seeking money or a remedy rather than punishment. Knowing which type a situation falls under shapes how it's handled — and they work quite differently.

## Card: Courts and jurisdiction
**Courts** are where legal disputes are decided, with judges (and sometimes juries) applying the law to the facts. There are usually different levels of courts, including the ability to appeal decisions to higher ones. Importantly, laws and legal systems differ by **jurisdiction** — the country, state, or region whose laws apply. What's legal or how things work in one place may differ elsewhere, which is why legal advice must be specific to your location.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: confusing civil and criminal matters (they work very differently); assuming laws are the same everywhere (they vary by jurisdiction); thinking the legal system is too complex to understand the basics of (the structure is graspable); and not seeking professional help when genuinely needed. Understanding the basics demystifies the system; for real legal issues, a qualified professional is the right source.

## Card: Quick recap
The legal system is how society makes, applies, and enforces laws and resolves disputes, through laws and courts. Key distinction: criminal law (crimes against society, prosecuted by the state) versus civil law (disputes between parties, where one sues another). Courts decide disputes, with appeal levels, and laws vary by jurisdiction. Understanding the basics demystifies it; for real issues, consult a professional.
