## Card: What it is
image: howworksElevatorsEscalatorsHero
**Elevators** (lifts) and **escalators** are machines that move people between floors — elevators in a cab that travels up and down, escalators on a continuous moving staircase. Both rely on clever, well-established engineering, and elevators in particular are far safer than common fears suggest. Understanding them demystifies machines we use constantly.

## Card: Why it matters
Elevators and escalators are everywhere, and they made tall buildings practical (skyscrapers would be unusable without lifts). Understanding how they work removes mystery and unfounded fear (elevators are very safe), and reveals the clever engineering behind everyday convenience. It's satisfying to understand machines we trust with our lives daily.

## Card: Key terms
- **Counterweight**: a weight balancing the elevator cab.
- **Cable and pulley**: the system that raises and lowers the cab.
- **Continuous loop**: the moving belt of an escalator.
- **Safety systems**: features preventing falls.

## Card: How elevators work
image: howworksElevatorsEscalatorsHowElevatorsWorkHero
Most elevators use a **cable and pulley** system: the cab hangs from strong cables, raised and lowered by a motor. Crucially, a **counterweight** (about the weight of the cab plus an average load) balances the cab — so the motor only needs to move the *difference* in weight, not the whole cab. This makes elevators efficient. Multiple strong cables and **safety systems** (like brakes that grip if the cab moves too fast) make them extremely safe — a snapped-cable freefall is essentially a movie myth.

## Card: How escalators work
An **escalator** is essentially a staircase on a **continuous loop**. A motor drives a chain that moves a series of linked steps in an endless loop — the steps emerge flat at the bottom, rise as a staircase, and flatten again at the top before looping back underneath. The handrail moves at the same speed on its own loop. It's a simple, robust design that continuously carries people without stopping.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: fearing elevators will plummet if a cable snaps (multiple cables plus safety brakes make this essentially impossible — it's a movie myth); thinking the motor lifts the whole elevator's weight (the counterweight balances most of it); not knowing escalator steps loop around continuously; and underrating how safe these machines are. Both are clever, well-engineered, and very safe.

## Card: Quick recap
Elevators hang from multiple strong cables, raised by a motor, with a counterweight balancing the cab so the motor only moves the difference — and safety brakes make freefall essentially a myth. Escalators are staircases on a continuous loop, with steps driven by a chain that flatten at top and bottom. Both are clever, robust, and very safe machines that move us between floors every day.
