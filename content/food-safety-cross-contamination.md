## Card: What it is
image: crossContaminationHero
**Cross-contamination** is when harmful bacteria spread from one food (usually raw meat) to another food, surface, or utensil. It's one of the most common and overlooked causes of food poisoning — and one of the easiest to prevent with a little awareness of how germs travel in a kitchen.

## Card: Why it matters
You might cook your chicken perfectly, but if the raw juices touched the salad, the knife, or your hands first, you can still get sick. Cross-contamination undoes good cooking. Preventing it is about keeping raw and ready-to-eat foods firmly apart, which stops invisible germs hitching a ride.

## Card: Key terms
- **Cross-contamination**: spreading bacteria from one item to another.
- **Raw foods**: uncooked items like meat, often carrying bacteria.
- **Ready-to-eat**: foods eaten without further cooking, like salad.
- **Separate boards**: using different cutting boards for raw meat and other foods.

## Card: How germs travel
Bacteria from **raw foods** spread easily: via hands, knives, **cutting boards**, plates, and surfaces. The classic mistake is chopping raw chicken, then using the same unwashed board or knife for vegetables eaten raw. The germs transfer, and since the salad isn't cooked, nothing kills them before you eat it.

## Card: Keeping things separate
image: separateBoards
Prevent it by separating raw and **ready-to-eat** foods at every stage. Use **separate boards** (or wash thoroughly between uses) for raw meat versus other foods, wash hands after handling raw meat, store raw meat below other foods in the fridge (so juices can't drip onto them), and never put cooked food back on a plate that held it raw.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: one cutting board for everything without washing; storing raw meat above other foods so juices drip down; reusing the plate that held raw meat for the cooked version; not washing hands after touching raw meat; and using the same utensils throughout. Each lets germs jump from raw food to your meal.

## Card: Quick recap
Cross-contamination spreads bacteria from raw foods to ready-to-eat ones via hands, boards, knives, and surfaces — undoing careful cooking. Prevent it: separate boards for raw meat, wash hands and tools between, store raw meat below other foods, and never reuse the raw-meat plate for cooked food. Keep raw and ready-to-eat apart.
