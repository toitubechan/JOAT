## Card: What it is
image: nutritionBasicsHero
**Nutrition** is the study of how food fuels and builds your body. Everything you eat is broken down into components your body uses for energy, growth, and repair. Good nutrition simply means giving your body the mix of these it needs to run well — no extreme diets required.

## Card: Why it matters
What you eat affects your energy, mood, weight, and long-term health more than almost anything else in daily life. Understanding the basics cuts through diet fads and marketing, helping you make sensible choices. This is general knowledge — for specific health conditions or diets, a doctor or dietitian is the right source.

## Card: Key terms
- **Nutrient**: a component in food your body uses.
- **Calorie**: a unit measuring energy in food.
- **Macronutrients**: nutrients needed in large amounts (carbs, protein, fat).
- **Micronutrients**: vitamins and minerals needed in small amounts.

## Card: Food as fuel and building blocks
image: foodFuelBuildingBlocks
Food does two big jobs. It provides **energy**, measured in **calories**, to power everything from breathing to running. And it supplies **building blocks** to grow and repair your body — muscles, bones, blood, and more. You eat both to *do* things and to *be* built and maintained.

## Card: The two kinds of nutrients
**Macronutrients** — carbohydrates, protein, and fat — are needed in large amounts and provide energy and structure. **Micronutrients** — vitamins and minerals — are needed in tiny amounts but are essential for countless body processes. A healthy diet supplies enough of both, which is why variety matters so much.

## Card: Common mistakes
A common mistake is thinking nutrition means strict dieting or "good vs. bad" foods, when balance and variety matter most. Another is focusing only on calories while ignoring nutrient quality. And many fall for fad diets and miracle claims, when the boring basics — varied, mostly whole foods — work best.

## Card: Quick recap
Nutrition is how food fuels and builds your body, providing energy (calories) and building blocks. You need macronutrients in large amounts and micronutrients in small ones, so variety is key. Skip the fads — balanced, varied eating is the foundation. For specific needs, consult a professional.
