## Card: What it is
image: fitnessHowMusclesGrowHero
**Muscle growth** is how muscles get bigger and stronger in response to training. The basic process: exercise stresses muscles, they repair and adapt during rest, and they grow back stronger. Understanding this reveals why both training *and* recovery matter, and dispels common myths. This is general educational knowledge, not medical or professional fitness advice.

## Card: Why it matters
Many people exercise without understanding how muscles actually grow, leading to ineffective training or frustration. Understanding the basic science — stress, repair, and adaptation — helps you train more effectively, value recovery, and have realistic expectations. It clears up myths and turns guesswork into informed effort, whether your goal is strength, fitness, or health.

## Card: Key terms
- **Muscle growth**: muscles getting bigger and stronger.
- **Progressive overload**: gradually increasing demand to keep adapting.
- **Repair and adaptation**: muscles rebuilding stronger during recovery.
- **Recovery**: rest that allows muscles to grow.

## Card: Stress, repair, adapt
image: fitnessHowMusclesGrowStressRepairAdaptHero
Muscle growth works through a cycle: when you exercise muscles harder than they're used to, you create small stresses (challenging them). Then, during **recovery** (rest), the body **repairs and adapts** — rebuilding the muscle slightly stronger to handle that demand better next time. Crucially, muscles grow during *rest and repair*, not during the exercise itself. So both the training stimulus *and* adequate recovery are essential — you can't grow muscle by training alone without letting it repair.

## Card: Progressive overload
A key principle is **progressive overload**: to keep growing, you must gradually *increase* the demand over time. Once muscles adapt to a given level of effort, that level no longer challenges them enough to drive further growth — so you progressively do more (more weight, reps, or difficulty). This is why doing the same easy workout forever stops producing results. Steady, progressive increases in challenge — within your capacity and with recovery — are what drive ongoing muscle growth and strength gains.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking muscles grow *during* exercise (they grow during rest and repair afterward); neglecting recovery (which is when growth happens); not progressively increasing the challenge (so progress stalls); and expecting instant results (growth takes consistent time). Muscles grow through the cycle of stress, repair, and adaptation, requiring both training *and* recovery, plus progressive overload over time.

## Card: Quick recap
Muscles grow through a cycle: exercise stresses them, then during recovery the body repairs and adapts, rebuilding them stronger — so muscles grow during *rest*, not the exercise itself, making recovery essential. Progressive overload (gradually increasing the demand over time) is needed to keep growing, since muscles stop adapting once a level becomes easy. Effective training combines challenging exercise, adequate recovery, and steady progression — and consistent time.
