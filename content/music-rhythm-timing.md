## Card: What it is
image: musicRhythmTimingHero
**Rhythm** is the pattern of sounds and silences in time — the timing element of music that makes you tap your foot or dance. Along with the **beat** (music's steady pulse), rhythm gives music its drive and feel. Understanding rhythm and timing reveals the foundation beneath every piece of music, from a simple drumbeat to a complex song.

## Card: Why it matters
Rhythm is fundamental — arguably the most essential element of music (you can have rhythm without melody, like a drumbeat, but rarely the reverse). It's what makes music move and what we instinctively respond to with movement. Understanding it deepens appreciation and is crucial for anyone making music. It's the heartbeat that everything else sits on top of.

## Card: Key terms
- **Rhythm**: the pattern of sounds and silences in time.
- **Beat**: the steady underlying pulse of music.
- **Tempo**: how fast or slow the beat is.
- **Time signature**: how beats are grouped.

## Card: Beat and tempo
image: musicRhythmTimingBeatAndTempoHero
Underlying most music is the **beat** — a steady, regular pulse (the thing you'd naturally clap or tap along to). The **tempo** is how fast or slow that beat goes (fast tempo feels energetic; slow feels calm or heavy). The beat is like music's heartbeat — a consistent foundation. Everything else in the music is organized around and relative to this steady pulse, which is why a clear beat makes music feel grounded and danceable.

## Card: Rhythm and grouping
**Rhythm** is the *pattern* of sounds and silences arranged over the beat — when notes are played, held, or paused. It's distinct from the steady beat: rhythm is the varied pattern *on top of* the pulse. Beats are also grouped into regular units (a **time signature** describes how many beats group together, giving music like a "waltz feel" or a "march feel"). Rhythm and these groupings give music its distinctive feel, groove, and movement — varied patterns riding the steady beat.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: confusing beat (steady pulse) with rhythm (the varied pattern on top); thinking rhythm is less important than melody (it's arguably more fundamental); not noticing tempo's huge effect on feel; and underrating rhythm's role in why music makes us move. Rhythm and timing — beat, tempo, and rhythmic patterns — are the foundation that gives music its drive and groove.

## Card: Quick recap
Rhythm is the pattern of sounds and silences in time — music's timing element that makes us move. Underlying it is the beat (a steady pulse you tap along to) at a certain tempo (speed). Rhythm is the varied pattern riding on top of the beat, and beats group into units (time signatures) giving music a particular feel. Arguably music's most fundamental element, rhythm and timing are the heartbeat everything else sits on.
