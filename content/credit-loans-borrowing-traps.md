## Card: What it is
image: financeCreditBorrowingTrapsHero
Some borrowing is designed to look helpful while quietly being very expensive. **Borrowing traps** are loans and habits that charge sky-high rates or keep you stuck in debt — like payday loans, rent-to-own deals, and paying only minimums. Spotting them early saves you from costly mistakes.

## Card: Why it matters
These traps target people who feel they have no other option, often in a tight moment. The cost is brutal: what looks like a small fee can translate to enormous annual interest. Recognizing the warning signs lets you pause and find a cheaper path before you're locked into a cycle that's hard to escape.

## Card: Key terms
- **Payday loan**: a tiny, short-term loan at extremely high interest.
- **Debt cycle**: repeatedly borrowing to repay earlier borrowing.
- **Rent-to-own**: paying in installments, often far above the item's value.
- **Rollover**: extending a loan, adding more fees.

## Card: The payday loan trap
image: financeCreditBorrowingTrapsThePaydayLoanTrapHero
A **payday loan** offers fast cash until your next paycheck, but the fees translate to staggering annual rates — often many times a credit card's. When the due date hits, many can't repay and **roll over** the loan, piling on more fees. This is how a small shortfall becomes a deepening **debt cycle**.

## Card: Other common snares
**Rent-to-own** lets you take an item home for small payments, but the total often far exceeds buying outright. "Buy now, pay later" can encourage overspending and hit you with penalties if you miss a date. And paying only **minimum payments** on any debt keeps interest compounding for years. The pattern: easy now, costly later.

## Card: Common mistakes
The core mistake is focusing on "can I make this small payment?" instead of "what's the total cost?" Another is using new debt to pay old debt without a real plan, which deepens the cycle. And many feel too embarrassed to seek cheaper help — a credit union, a payment plan, or a trusted person — when those options would cost far less.

## Card: Quick recap
Borrowing traps — payday loans, rollovers, rent-to-own, minimum-only payments — look easy but charge punishing costs and can lock you in a debt cycle. Always ask the total cost, not just the monthly bite. When stuck, seek cheaper alternatives before reaching for the most expensive money available.
