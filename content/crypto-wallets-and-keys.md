## Card: What it is
image: financeCryptoWalletsAndKeysHero
A crypto **wallet** is a tool that stores the secret codes proving you own your cryptocurrency. The coins themselves live on the blockchain; the wallet holds your **keys** — the passwords that let you access and move them. Lose the keys, and you lose access to the crypto, often forever.

## Card: Why it matters
With normal banking, a forgotten password is a quick reset away. Crypto is different: there's usually no helpline, no reset, no recovery. Whoever holds the keys controls the coins. This makes understanding wallets and keys not optional but essential — it's the difference between keeping your crypto and losing it permanently.

## Card: Key terms
- **Wallet**: a tool that stores your crypto keys.
- **Public key**: like an account number others can send to.
- **Private key**: the secret that lets you spend — keep it hidden.
- **Seed phrase**: a list of words that can restore your wallet.

## Card: Public vs. private keys
image: financeCryptoWalletsAndKeysPublicVsPrivateKeysHero
Your **public key** is like an account number — safe to share so people can send you crypto. Your **private key** is the secret that authorizes spending; anyone who has it can take your funds. The golden rule: share the public key freely, guard the private key with your life. Never type or paste it anywhere suspicious.

## Card: Seed phrases and self-custody
Many wallets give you a **seed phrase** — a set of words that can restore your entire wallet if your device is lost. Whoever knows it controls the funds, so it must be kept offline and private. "Self-custody" means you alone hold the keys: total control, but also total responsibility, with no one to bail you out.

## Card: Common mistakes
The deadliest mistake is sharing or losing your private key or seed phrase — there's no recovery. Storing the seed phrase as a screenshot or in cloud notes makes it hackable. And "if you don't hold the keys, you don't really own the crypto" — leaving everything on an exchange means trusting that company entirely.

## Card: Quick recap
A wallet stores your keys; the crypto lives on the blockchain. Share your public key, never your private key or seed phrase. Keep recovery words offline and private — there's no reset button. Self-custody means full control and full responsibility. Guarding your keys is guarding your crypto.
