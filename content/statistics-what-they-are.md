## Card: What it is
image: dataStatisticsWhatTheyAreHero
**Statistics** is the science of collecting, summarizing, and interpreting data to understand the world. A "statistic" is also a single number summarizing something — like an average, a percentage, or a total. Statistics turn messy real-world data into figures we can grasp and act on.

## Card: Why it matters
Statistics are everywhere — news, health advice, sports, politics, ads. They shape decisions and opinions, and they're often misunderstood or misused. Understanding the basics helps you make sense of the numbers thrown at you, judge them critically, and avoid being misled. It's essential literacy in a data-saturated world.

## Card: Key terms
- **Statistic**: a number summarizing data.
- **Data**: collected facts or measurements.
- **Sample**: a smaller group used to represent a larger one.
- **Population**: the whole group being studied.

## Card: From data to insight
image: dataStatisticsWhatTheyAreFromDataToInsightHero
Statistics works by taking **data** (collected facts) and summarizing it into useful figures. Often we can't measure everyone (the whole **population**), so we study a **sample** — a smaller group meant to represent the larger one — and draw conclusions from it. This is powerful but introduces a key caveat: the sample must actually represent the population, or the statistics will mislead.

## Card: Statistics describe, they don't decide
A useful mindset: statistics *describe* and *summarize* — they're tools, not truths handed down. The same data can be summarized in different ways, and statistics always involve choices (what to measure, how to sample, which figure to report). This doesn't make them useless; it means they should be understood and questioned, not blindly trusted or dismissed. Good statistics inform; they don't dictate.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: treating a statistic as absolute truth rather than a summary involving choices; assuming a sample always represents the whole (it may not); not asking how data was collected; and either blindly trusting or blindly dismissing all statistics. The balanced view: statistics are useful tools to understand and question, not magic numbers.

## Card: Quick recap
Statistics is the science of collecting, summarizing, and interpreting data; a statistic is a number summarizing something. Often a sample stands in for a whole population, which is powerful but only works if the sample is representative. Statistics describe and involve choices, so they should be understood and questioned — useful tools, not absolute truths.
