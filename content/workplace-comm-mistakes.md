## Card: What it is
image: workCommMistakesHero
Certain communication habits quietly damage your effectiveness and reputation at work. This card set gathers the common workplace communication mistakes — so you can spot and avoid them. Many are easy to make without realizing, and easy to fix once you're aware.

## Card: Why it matters
Communication shapes how competent and trustworthy you seem, so these mistakes cost you beyond the immediate misunderstanding. Avoiding them protects your reputation and relationships. Because they're often habits done unconsciously, simply naming them is half the battle to changing them.

## Card: Key terms
- **Assuming understanding**: believing you were understood without checking.
- **Reactive messaging**: sending messages in the heat of emotion.
- **Oversharing / undersharing**: giving too much or too little information.
- **Tone-deaf**: misjudging how a message will land.

## Card: Clarity and listening failures
image: listeningFailuresHero
Top mistakes: **assuming understanding** (thinking people got your point when they didn't — check, don't assume); not listening, so you talk past people; being unclear or burying the point; and **undersharing** (leaving people without information they need) or **oversharing** (drowning them in detail). Each causes confusion and rework.

## Card: Emotional and tone mistakes
Two big ones: **reactive messaging** — firing off an email or chat while angry or frustrated, which you usually regret (sleep on it, or draft and wait). And being **tone-deaf** — misjudging how a message lands, coming across as curt, dismissive, or inappropriate for the context. Pausing to consider the recipient's view prevents both.

## Card: Common mistakes
To gather them: assuming you were understood; poor listening; unclear or buried points; over- or under-sharing; sending emotional messages in the moment; misjudging tone; gossiping or speaking badly of colleagues; and "reply all" overuse. Most are fixed by slowing down, considering the recipient, and checking understanding.

## Card: Quick recap
Common workplace communication mistakes include assuming you were understood, poor listening, unclear messages, over- or under-sharing, reactive emotional messages, and tone-deafness. They quietly harm your reputation. The fixes: slow down, consider the recipient's perspective, check you've been understood, and never send messages in the heat of emotion.
