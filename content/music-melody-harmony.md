## Card: What it is
image: musicMelodyHarmonyHero
**Melody** is the tune — the sequence of notes you hum or whistle, the main line of a song. **Harmony** is what happens when notes are played *together*, adding richness and emotional color beneath the melody. Together they're central to how music sounds and feels. Understanding the difference clarifies two of music's most important elements.

## Card: Why it matters
Melody and harmony are at the heart of why music moves us — melody is what we remember and sing, harmony is much of what gives music its emotional depth. Understanding them deepens listening (you hear *what's* happening in your favorite songs) and is foundational for making music. They're key to music's expressive power.

## Card: Key terms
- **Melody**: a sequence of notes forming a tune.
- **Harmony**: notes played together for richness.
- **Chord**: several notes played at once.
- **Emotional color**: the feeling harmony adds.

## Card: Melody — the tune
image: musicMelodyHarmonyMelodyTheTuneHero
**Melody** is the sequence of single notes that forms the recognizable tune of a piece — the part you hum, whistle, or sing along to. It's usually the most memorable and prominent element, the "main line." A good melody is what we carry away from a song. Melody unfolds *horizontally* over time (one note after another), creating the line that leads the listener through the music. It's often what we think of as the song itself.

## Card: Harmony — the richness beneath
**Harmony** is what happens when multiple notes sound *together* (notably in **chords** — several notes at once), creating richness and **emotional color** beneath or around the melody. Where melody is horizontal (notes in sequence), harmony is *vertical* (notes stacked together). Harmony is much of what makes music feel happy, sad, tense, or warm — the same melody can feel completely different depending on the harmony under it. It adds depth and emotional shading to the tune.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: confusing melody (the tune, notes in sequence) with harmony (notes together, adding richness); thinking only melody matters (harmony deeply shapes emotional feel); not realizing the same melody can feel different with different harmony; and overlooking harmony's role in music's emotional power. Melody is the memorable tune; harmony is the rich emotional color beneath it.

## Card: Quick recap
Melody is the tune — a sequence of single notes forming the memorable main line you hum, unfolding horizontally over time. Harmony is notes played together (like chords), adding richness and emotional color beneath the melody, working vertically. Harmony is much of what makes music feel happy, sad, or tense — the same melody feels different with different harmony. Together, melody and harmony are central to music's expressive power.
