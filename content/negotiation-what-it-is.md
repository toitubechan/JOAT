## Card: What it is
image: negotiationBasicsHero
**Negotiation** is simply a discussion aimed at reaching an agreement when two sides want different things. It happens constantly — over salary, prices, deadlines, responsibilities, even chores at home. It's not a confrontation or a battle to "win," but a normal conversation to find terms both sides can accept.

## Card: Why it matters
People who never negotiate leave value on the table — money, time, better terms — often because they fear conflict or don't realize negotiation is even an option. Understanding it as a normal, expected conversation removes the fear and helps you advocate for yourself. The cost of *not* negotiating, over a career, is huge.

## Card: Key terms
- **Negotiation**: a discussion to reach a mutually acceptable agreement.
- **Leverage**: the strength of your position.
- **BATNA**: your best alternative if no deal is reached.
- **Interests**: what each side really wants underneath their demands.

## Card: It's a conversation, not a fight
image: conversationNotFightHero
The biggest reframe: negotiation isn't a hostile battle where one wins and one loses. It's a problem-solving conversation to find an agreement. Approaching it calmly and collaboratively — rather than aggressively or fearfully — usually produces better results *and* preserves the relationship. Most everyday negotiation is far less adversarial than people imagine.

## Card: Understand interests
A key idea: look past stated *positions* to underlying **interests** — what each side really wants. Someone demanding a higher price might really want to feel respected or secure. Understanding the real interests behind demands often reveals creative solutions that satisfy both sides, which rigid position-bargaining misses entirely.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: not negotiating at all (out of fear or not realizing you can); treating it as a hostile fight to win; focusing only on stated positions instead of underlying interests; and assuming the other side is an enemy. Negotiation is a normal, often collaborative conversation — and simply being willing to have it is half the battle.

## Card: Quick recap
Negotiation is just a conversation to reach agreement when sides want different things — not a battle. It happens everywhere, and not doing it leaves value on the table. Approach it calmly and collaboratively, look past positions to underlying interests, and remember: being willing to negotiate at all is most of the win.
