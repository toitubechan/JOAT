## Card: What it is
image: growthHabitsMakingThemStickHero
Starting a habit is one thing; keeping it going for months until it's truly automatic is another. Making habits **stick** is about consistency, identity, and resilience — the strategies that carry a new habit through the inevitable dips in motivation until it becomes part of who you are.

## Card: Why it matters
Most habits fail not at the start but a few weeks in, when novelty fades and motivation dips. Knowing how to stay consistent through that valley is what separates lasting change from another abandoned resolution. The goal is to reach the point where the habit feels automatic and effortless.

## Card: Key terms
- **Consistency**: repeating the habit regularly over time.
- **Identity**: seeing yourself as the kind of person who does it.
- **Never miss twice**: a rule to recover quickly after a slip.
- **Plateau**: a flat stretch where progress feels invisible.

## Card: Become the type of person
image: growthHabitsMakingThemStickBecomeTheTypeOfPersonHero
A powerful shift is tying the habit to your **identity**, not just a goal. Instead of "I'm trying to run," think "I'm a runner." When a habit becomes part of who you *are*, it sticks far better than chasing an outcome. Each time you do the habit, you cast a vote for that identity, reinforcing it.

## Card: Survive the dip
Motivation always fades, so rely on rules, not feelings. **"Never miss twice"** is gold: one missed day is fine, but don't let it become two — that's how habits die. Expect a **plateau** where progress feels invisible (especially with health or skills); push through, because results often lag behind the work before suddenly showing.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: relying on motivation, which inevitably dips; treating one missed day as total failure and quitting; expecting fast, visible results and giving up at the plateau; and not connecting the habit to identity, so it stays an external chore. Consistency through the dip, not intensity at the start, is what makes habits last.

## Card: Quick recap
Habits stick through consistency, identity, and resilience. Tie the habit to who you are ("I'm a runner"), not just a goal. Rely on rules like "never miss twice" instead of fading motivation, and push through plateaus where progress feels invisible. Surviving the dip is what turns a new habit into a permanent one.
