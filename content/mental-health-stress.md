## Card: What it is
image: mentalStressResponseHero
**Stress** is your body and mind's response to pressure or demands. In short bursts it's normal and even helpful — it sharpens focus and energy. The problem is **chronic stress**: pressure that stays switched on too long, which wears down both mind and body over time.

## Card: Why it matters
Some stress is unavoidable and fine. But ongoing, unmanaged stress harms sleep, mood, concentration, relationships, and physical health. Understanding how stress works — and that it's manageable — helps you respond to it deliberately instead of being controlled by it. This is general guidance, not treatment for severe cases.

## Card: Key terms
- **Stress**: the body's response to pressure or demands.
- **Acute stress**: short-term, often helpful stress.
- **Chronic stress**: prolonged stress that harms health.
- **Fight-or-flight**: the body's automatic alarm response.

## Card: The stress response
When you face a threat or demand, your body triggers **fight-or-flight** — heart rate up, senses sharpened, ready to act. This is **acute stress**, and it's useful in short bursts (a deadline, a danger). The system is designed to switch on, then *off* once the pressure passes, letting you recover.

## Card: When stress becomes harmful
image: chronicStressLoad
Problems arise when the response never switches off — **chronic stress**. The body stays in alarm mode, which exhausts it, disrupting sleep, mood, digestion, and immunity. Modern stressors (work, money, constant notifications) can keep the alarm running for weeks. Recognizing this is the first step to dialing it back.

## Card: Managing it
You can't remove all stress, but you can manage your response. Helpful basics: regular exercise, good sleep, slow breathing or relaxation, talking to someone, and tackling causes where possible. Even brief pauses help the body switch *off* the alarm. Identifying your main stressors lets you address them rather than just enduring them.

## Card: Common mistakes
A common mistake is treating all stress as bad — some is normal and useful. The bigger error is ignoring chronic stress, pushing through until burnout. People also reach for unhelpful coping (excess alcohol, withdrawal) that worsens it, and assume there's nothing they can do, when small, consistent habits genuinely help.

## Card: Quick recap
Stress is the body's response to pressure — useful in short bursts, harmful when chronic. The fight-or-flight alarm is meant to switch on then off; ongoing stress keeps it running and wears you down. Manage your response with exercise, sleep, rest, and connection, and address causes where you can.
