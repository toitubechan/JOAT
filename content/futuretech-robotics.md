## Card: What it is
image: futuretechRoboticsHero
**Robotics** is the field of building machines (**robots**) that can sense their surroundings, process information, and physically act in the world. Robots range from factory arms to vacuum cleaners to humanoid machines. Combining mechanics, electronics, and increasingly AI, robotics is transforming work and daily life.

## Card: Why it matters
Robots are increasingly part of factories, hospitals, homes, and beyond. Understanding robotics helps you grasp how automation is changing work and society, separate realistic robots from science-fiction hype, and follow a fast-advancing field. It's central to understanding the technological future.

## Card: Key terms
- **Robot**: a machine that senses, processes, and acts physically.
- **Sensors**: how a robot perceives its surroundings.
- **Automation**: machines doing tasks previously done by people.
- **Humanoid robot**: a robot shaped like a human.

## Card: Sense, think, act
image: futuretechRoboticsSenseThinkActHero
A **robot** generally does three things: it *senses* its environment (using **sensors** like cameras and touch), *processes* that information to decide what to do, and *acts* physically (moving, gripping, building). This sense-think-act loop is what distinguishes a robot from a simple machine. Increasingly, the "think" part uses AI, making robots more capable and adaptable than older, purely pre-programmed machines.

## Card: Where robots really are
Most real robots aren't humanoid sci-fi figures — they're specialized machines doing specific jobs: factory arms assembling products, robotic vacuums, surgical assistants, warehouse robots, and explorers in dangerous places. **Humanoid robots** exist but are hard to build well and less common. Robots excel at tasks that are repetitive, precise, dangerous, or done at scale — which is why **automation** is reshaping many industries.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: imagining robots are mostly humanoid sci-fi machines (most are specialized, non-humanoid tools); thinking robots are fully autonomous and intelligent (many are limited and task-specific); overrating how human-like they are; and either over-hyping or dismissing the field. Robotics is advancing fast but the reality is more specialized and practical than science fiction suggests.

## Card: Quick recap
Robotics builds machines that sense, process, and act physically — the sense-think-act loop that defines a robot, increasingly enhanced by AI. Most real robots are specialized tools (factory arms, vacuums, surgical assistants), not humanoid sci-fi figures. They excel at repetitive, precise, dangerous, or large-scale tasks, driving automation across industries. The reality is more practical than fiction suggests.
