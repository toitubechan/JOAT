## Card: What it is
image: diyProHero
Not every repair is a DIY job. Some are perfect for a beginner; others can be dangerous, illegal to do yourself, or cost far more to fix when an amateur attempt goes wrong. Knowing where the line sits is itself a valuable skill — it keeps you safe and saves money.

## Card: Why it matters
Doing it yourself saves money on the right jobs but can be disastrous on the wrong ones — think gas, major electrics, or structural work. A botched DIY repair can cost more than a pro would have, or worse, cause injury or a hazard. Good judgment about the line protects both your wallet and your safety.

## Card: Key terms
- **DIY**: do-it-yourself repairs you handle alone.
- **Licensed trade**: work legally requiring a qualified professional.
- **Hazard**: something with risk of injury or serious damage.
- **Scope creep**: a small job quietly turning into a big one.

## Card: Great DIY jobs
Beginner-friendly tasks are low-risk and reversible: filling holes, painting, tightening screws, replacing tap washers, unclogging drains, swapping a light bulb or a cabinet handle. If a mistake would only mean redoing the job — not danger or major cost — it's usually safe to try yourself.

## Card: Leave these to a pro
image: callProBoundary
Call a professional for anything involving gas, major electrical work, structural changes, or significant plumbing behind walls. In many places these require a **licensed trade** by law for safety. The rule of thumb: if a mistake could cause fire, flood, collapse, or injury, the small saving isn't worth the risk.

## Card: Common mistakes
The classic mistake is overconfidence — tackling a dangerous job to save money and creating a hazard. The opposite is paying a pro for trivial jobs you could easily do. Watch for **scope creep** too: a "quick" fix that reveals bigger problems; know when to stop and call someone.

## Card: Quick recap
DIY the low-risk, reversible jobs — filling, painting, tightening, simple swaps. Hand over anything risky or legally restricted: gas, major electrical, structural, or deep plumbing. If a mistake could cause fire, flood, collapse, or injury, hire a pro. Matching the job to your skill keeps you safe and saves money.
