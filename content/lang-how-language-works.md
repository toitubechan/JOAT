## Card: What it is
image: langHowLanguageWorksHero
**Language** is the system humans use to communicate — combining sounds (or signs) and symbols into words, and words into sentences, following rules (grammar) that let us express endless ideas. It's one of humanity's most remarkable abilities, so natural we rarely notice how extraordinary it is. Understanding how it works reveals the elegant system behind everyday communication.

## Card: Why it matters
Language shapes how we think, connect, and pass on knowledge — it's central to being human. Understanding how it works gives insight into communication, learning, and even thought itself. It also lays the foundation for appreciating the diversity of the world's languages and how we learn and use them. It's a window into a defining human capacity.

## Card: Key terms
- **Language**: a structured system for communication.
- **Grammar**: the rules for combining words into meaning.
- **Vocabulary**: the words a language uses.
- **Generativity**: the ability to create endless new sentences.

## Card: Building blocks and rules
image: langHowLanguageWorksBuildingBlocksAndRulesHero
Language works by combining small pieces into meaning. Sounds form words (**vocabulary**), and words combine into sentences following **grammar** — the rules for how words fit together. What's remarkable is **generativity**: from a finite set of words and rules, we can create and understand an *infinite* number of sentences, including ones never said before. This is why language is so powerful — it's not a fixed list of phrases but a creative, generative system.

## Card: More than words
Language is more than vocabulary and grammar. It also involves meaning (how words and context convey ideas), and it's deeply tied to culture and thought. Spoken language uses sound; written language uses symbols; sign languages use gesture — all are full languages. Humans seem uniquely capable of this rich, generative, rule-based communication, which lets us share complex ideas, abstract concepts, and accumulated knowledge across people and generations.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking language is just a list of words (it's a generative system of words *and* rules); assuming grammar is arbitrary "rules to obey" rather than the structure that makes meaning possible; thinking only spoken language counts (sign languages are full languages); and underrating how extraordinary the everyday ability is. Language is a remarkable, generative, rule-based system for communicating endless ideas.

## Card: Quick recap
Language is the structured system humans use to communicate, combining sounds or signs into words (vocabulary) and words into sentences via grammar (the rules). Its remarkable feature is generativity — from finite words and rules, we create infinite new sentences. It's tied to meaning, culture, and thought, and includes spoken, written, and sign languages. Understanding it reveals the elegant system behind a defining human ability.
