## Card: What it is
image: businessPlanHero
A **business plan** is a document that lays out what your business does, who it serves, how it'll make money, and how you'll run and grow it. It doesn't need to be long or formal — even a simple plan forces you to think through the key questions. Understanding the basics helps you plan clearly and spot problems before they cost you.

## Card: Why it matters
Writing a plan forces you to think through your business properly — clarifying your idea, exposing gaps and weak assumptions, and giving you direction. It's also often needed to raise money or get support. Even a simple plan turns vague ideas into a concrete, examinable strategy. The *thinking* it forces is often more valuable than the document itself.

## Card: Key terms
- **Business plan**: a document outlining how a business will work.
- **Executive summary**: a brief overview of the whole plan.
- **Financial projections**: estimates of money in and out.
- **Living document**: a plan that you update as things change.

## Card: What a simple plan covers
image: simplePlanCoversHero
A simple business plan answers key questions: *What* does the business do and what problem does it solve? *Who* are the customers (the market)? *How* will it make money (the model and pricing)? Who are the *competitors* and how will you differ? How will you *reach* customers (marketing)? And the *finances* — basic **financial projections** of expected costs, revenue, and what you need to break even. A short **executive summary** captures the essentials up front.

## Card: A tool for thinking, not a formality
The real value of a business plan is the *thinking* it forces, not the document. Writing it makes you confront questions you might otherwise gloss over — exposing weak assumptions and gaps while they're still cheap to fix. Keep it as a **living document**: update it as you learn and circumstances change, rather than writing it once and filing it away. A plan you actually use to think and decide beats a polished one gathering dust.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: skipping a plan entirely (missing the clarifying thinking); over-engineering a huge formal document when a simple one would do; treating it as a one-time formality rather than a living tool; being unrealistic in projections; and not updating it as things change. A simple, honest plan that forces you to think through the key questions — and that you keep updating — is what's valuable.

## Card: Quick recap
A business plan lays out what your business does, who it serves, how it makes money, and how you'll run and grow it. Even a simple one forces valuable thinking — clarifying your idea and exposing weak assumptions cheaply. Cover what, who, how (model and pricing), competitors, marketing, and basic finances, with a short summary up front. Keep it a living document you update. The thinking matters more than the document.
