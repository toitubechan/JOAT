## Card: What it is
image: batchCookingHero
**Batch cooking** means making a large quantity of food at once, then storing portions to eat over several days or freeze for later. Cook once, eat many times. It's one of the most effective ways to save time, money, and effort while still eating home-cooked meals.

## Card: Why it matters
Cooking from scratch every day is time-consuming, which is why people fall back on takeaways. Batch cooking concentrates the effort into one session, leaving you with ready meals for busy days. It saves money, cuts waste, and means a healthy home-cooked option is always within reach, even when you're tired.

## Card: Key terms
- **Batch cooking**: making large quantities to portion and store.
- **Portioning**: dividing food into single-meal amounts.
- **Freezing**: storing portions long-term to eat later.
- **Reheating safely**: warming leftovers thoroughly before eating.

## Card: How it works
image: portionFreeze
Pick dishes that scale and store well — soups, stews, curries, pasta sauces, chili. Cook a big batch, then **portion** it into containers. Keep a few days' worth in the fridge and **freeze** the rest, labeled with the date. Future-you just grabs and reheats. The effort of one meal yields many.

## Card: Storing and reheating safely
Food safety matters with batch cooking: cool food reasonably quickly before fridging or freezing, store it covered, and use fridge portions within a few days. When **reheating**, heat leftovers until piping hot all the way through. As a rule, don't reheat the same dish repeatedly — reheat only the portion you'll eat.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: cooking dishes that don't store or reheat well; not portioning, so you can't grab a single meal easily; forgetting to label and date frozen food (it becomes mystery freezer-ware); leaving food out too long before storing; and reheating food more than once. A little care keeps batch cooking safe and convenient.

## Card: Quick recap
Batch cooking means making a big quantity once, then portioning and storing it — cook once, eat many times. Choose dishes that store well, portion into containers, refrigerate some and freeze the rest (labeled). Cool and store food promptly, reheat until piping hot, and don't reheat repeatedly. Effort once, meals all week.
