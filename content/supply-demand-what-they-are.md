## Card: What it is
image: econSupplydemandWhatTheyAreHero
**Supply** is how much of something is available, and **demand** is how much people want it. Together, these two forces are the foundation of economics — they shape prices, drive markets, and explain countless everyday situations. Understanding them is like learning the basic grammar of how the economy works.

## Card: Why it matters
Supply and demand explain so much: why prices rise and fall, why some things are expensive and others cheap, and why shortages and gluts happen. It's perhaps the single most useful idea in everyday economics — once you see it, you'll notice it operating everywhere, from concert tickets to housing to your weekly shop.

## Card: Key terms
- **Supply**: the amount of a good available for sale.
- **Demand**: how much people want and will buy.
- **Scarcity**: limited availability relative to want.
- **Market**: where buyers and sellers interact.

## Card: The two forces
image: econSupplydemandWhatTheyAreTheTwoForcesHero
**Demand** reflects how much people want something and are willing to buy — generally, the lower the price, the more people want it. **Supply** reflects how much producers offer for sale — generally, the higher the price, the more they're willing to supply. These two forces pull in opposite directions on price, and their interaction is what a **market** balances out.

## Card: Scarcity drives value
A core insight: value comes largely from **scarcity** — the relationship between how much is available (supply) and how much is wanted (demand). Something abundant and little-wanted is cheap; something scarce and highly-wanted is expensive. This is why diamonds cost more than water despite water being far more essential — water is abundant, while diamonds are scarce relative to demand.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking price reflects only how *useful* something is (it reflects supply and demand — hence cheap water, costly diamonds); ignoring the supply side and blaming all price changes on demand or "greed"; and not realizing these two forces operate together everywhere. Price is the meeting point of supply and demand.

## Card: Quick recap
Supply is how much is available; demand is how much people want. These two forces interact in markets to set prices. Value comes largely from scarcity — the balance of supply and demand — which is why abundant water is cheap and scarce diamonds are dear. Spot these two forces, and much of economics clicks.
