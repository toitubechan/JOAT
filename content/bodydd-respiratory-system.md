## Card: What it is
image: respiratorySystemHero
The **respiratory system** — your lungs and airways — brings oxygen into the body and removes carbon dioxide, through the act of breathing. Every cell needs oxygen to release energy from food, and produces carbon dioxide as waste. The respiratory system handles this vital exchange, around 20,000 breaths a day.

## Card: Why it matters
Breathing is so automatic we forget it, but it's essential every moment — cut off oxygen for minutes and the body fails. Understanding the respiratory system explains why we breathe, how oxygen reaches our cells, and why lung health and air quality matter. It's one of the body's most fundamental life-support systems.

## Card: Key terms
- **Lungs**: the organs where gas exchange happens.
- **Oxygen and carbon dioxide**: the gases exchanged.
- **Gas exchange**: swapping oxygen in for carbon dioxide out.
- **Diaphragm**: the muscle that drives breathing.

## Card: How breathing works
image: breathingMechanicsHero
When you breathe in, the **diaphragm** (a large muscle below the lungs) contracts, expanding the chest and drawing air into the **lungs**. The lungs branch into tiny air sacs surrounded by blood vessels. Here, **gas exchange** happens: **oxygen** passes from the air into the blood, while **carbon dioxide** passes from the blood into the air to be breathed out. Then you exhale, and the cycle repeats.

## Card: Why we need it
Every cell needs **oxygen** to release energy from food (a process called respiration at the cellular level), and this produces **carbon dioxide** as waste. The respiratory system supplies the oxygen and removes the carbon dioxide, working hand-in-hand with the circulatory system: the lungs load oxygen into the blood, and the heart pumps that oxygen-rich blood to every cell. Breathing and circulation are a tightly linked team.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking we breathe in oxygen and breathe out "used-up air" with no oxygen (exhaled air still has oxygen, just less, plus more carbon dioxide); not knowing the diaphragm drives breathing; imagining the lungs work alone (they team up with circulation); and underrating how air quality affects lung health. Breathing is a vital, continuous gas exchange.

## Card: Quick recap
The respiratory system (lungs and airways) brings in oxygen and removes carbon dioxide through breathing, driven by the diaphragm. In the lungs' tiny air sacs, gas exchange swaps oxygen into the blood and carbon dioxide out. Every cell needs oxygen to release energy and produces carbon dioxide as waste, so breathing teams up with circulation to keep you alive every moment.
