## Card: What it is
image: financePersonalNeedsVsWantsHero
A **need** is something you genuinely must have to live and function — housing, basic food, utilities, transport to work. A **want** is something that makes life nicer but isn't essential — dining out, a newer phone, streaming services. Sorting your spending into these two buckets is one of the simplest, most powerful money skills.

## Card: Why it matters
When money gets tight, knowing what's a need and what's a want tells you exactly what to protect and what to trim. It also stops guilt: spending on wants is fine, as long as your needs are covered first and you've chosen the want on purpose, not by accident.

## Card: Key terms
- **Need**: essential for living or earning a living.
- **Want**: optional, adds comfort or enjoyment.
- **Lifestyle creep**: wants slowly turning into "needs" as you earn more.
- **Discretionary spending**: the flexible money left for wants.

## Card: The blurry middle
image: financePersonalNeedsVsWantsTheBlurryMiddleHero
Many things aren't purely one or the other. You *need* food, but a restaurant meal is a want. You *need* a phone, but the latest model is a want. The trick isn't to label items — it's to ask: *what's the cheaper version that still meets the actual need?* The gap between that and what you chose is the want.

## Card: Watch for lifestyle creep
**Lifestyle creep** is when rising income quietly turns yesterday's luxuries into today's "essentials" — a bigger apartment, premium everything, habits you'd struggle to give up. It's not wrong to upgrade, but if every raise gets fully spent, you never get ahead. Let some of each raise grow your savings, not just your standards.

## Card: Common mistakes
The first mistake is calling wants "needs" to justify them — "I *need* this vacation." The second is the opposite: cutting all wants so harshly that you burn out and binge-spend later. A sustainable budget protects needs, funds *some* wants deliberately, and leaves room to save.

## Card: Quick recap
Needs keep you alive and working; wants make life nicer. Cover needs first, choose wants on purpose, and watch out for lifestyle creep eating every raise. The goal isn't a joyless budget — it's spending with intention instead of on autopilot.
