## Card: What it is
image: psychMotivationMythsHero
Motivation is surrounded by **myths** that actually make it *harder* to stay driven — like the idea you must feel motivated before acting, or that some people are just "born motivated." This card set debunks these, replacing them with a more accurate and empowering view of how motivation really works.

## Card: Why it matters
Believing motivation myths leads to waiting passively, feeling like a failure during normal dips, and giving up. Clearing them up frees you to take action regardless of how you feel and to build motivation deliberately. The truth about motivation is far more empowering than the comforting but limiting myths.

## Card: Key terms
- **Myth**: a popular but false belief.
- **"Wait for motivation"**: the myth that the feeling must come first.
- **"Born motivated"**: the myth that drive is a fixed trait.
- **Discipline**: consistent action regardless of feeling.

## Card: Common motivation myths busted
image: psychMotivationMythsCommonMotivationMythsBustedHero
- "**Wait until you feel motivated**" — false; action often *creates* motivation, so waiting keeps you stuck.
- "Some people are just **born motivated**" — motivation is influenced far more by habits, purpose, and environment than by a fixed trait.
- "You need to feel motivated to do good work" — disciplined action beats fickle feeling; consistency matters more than mood.
- "Motivation should be constant" — it naturally fluctuates; dips are normal.

## Card: The empowering truth
The reality is liberating: you don't need to *feel* motivated to act — start anyway, and motivation often follows. **Discipline** (consistent action regardless of feeling), supported by habits and a clear purpose, outperforms waiting for inspiration. And motivation isn't a fixed gift — anyone can build it with the right systems. You have far more control than the myths suggest.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: waiting to feel motivated before starting; believing you "aren't a motivated person" (it's not a fixed trait); expecting constant motivation and feeling like a failure during dips; and relying on the feeling instead of discipline and systems. Letting go of these myths frees you to act and build motivation deliberately.

## Card: Quick recap
Motivation myths — that you must feel it before acting, that some are simply "born motivated," that it should be constant — make staying driven harder. The empowering truth: action creates motivation, discipline and systems beat waiting for the feeling, and motivation can be built by anyone. Letting go of the myths gives you real control.
