## Card: What it is
image: consumerTrapsHero
Beyond knowing your rights, being a savvy consumer means **avoiding the traps** that catch people out — misleading deals, pushy upsells, sneaky subscriptions, and pressure tactics. This card set covers common consumer pitfalls and how to sidestep them. (General guidance for everyday spending.)

## Card: Why it matters
Many consumer problems are avoidable if you recognize the traps before falling in. Knowing the common tactics protects your money and helps you make clear-headed decisions rather than being manipulated. It's the proactive side of consumer awareness — preventing problems, not just resolving them after.

## Card: Key terms
- **Upselling / pressure selling**: pushing extra or pricier purchases.
- **Subscription trap**: hard-to-cancel recurring charges.
- **Misleading deal**: an offer that isn't the bargain it seems.
- **Cooling-off period**: a window to cancel after buying.

## Card: Pressure and upsells
Watch for **pressure selling** — "today only," "limited stock," or pushy upsells to pricier models or add-ons (like extended warranties you may not need). These exploit urgency and on-the-spot decisions. The defense: never feel rushed. A genuine deal is rarely "now or never." Take time, sleep on big purchases, and decline extras you didn't come for and haven't considered.

## Card: Subscriptions and misleading deals
image: subscriptionTrap
**Subscription traps** — free trials that auto-charge, or services that are easy to join but hard to cancel — catch many people. Track what you're subscribed to and cancellation deadlines. And scrutinize **misleading deals**: inflated "was" prices, "free" offers with hidden costs, or bundles that don't actually save money. Check the real price and terms rather than trusting the headline offer.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: caving to pressure and urgency tactics; buying add-ons (like extended warranties) on the spot without thinking; forgetting about free trials until they auto-charge; trusting "was" prices and "deals" without checking; and not knowing about any cooling-off period. The defenses: don't be rushed, track subscriptions, verify deals, and know your right to cancel where it exists.

## Card: Quick recap
Avoid consumer traps by resisting pressure and urgency tactics (genuine deals are rarely "now or never"), being wary of on-the-spot upsells, tracking subscriptions and free-trial deadlines, and verifying "deals" rather than trusting headline offers. Know about any cooling-off period too. Recognizing the traps in advance protects your money and your decisions.
