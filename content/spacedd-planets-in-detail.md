## Card: What it is
image: spaceddPlanetsHero
The **planets** of our solar system are eight diverse worlds orbiting the Sun, falling into two main groups: the small, rocky inner planets and the giant outer planets. Each is strikingly different. This is a closer tour of these worlds — what they're like and what makes each distinct.

## Card: Why it matters
The planets are our cosmic neighbors, and knowing them brings the night sky and space news to life. Understanding their variety — from scorching Venus to giant Jupiter — reveals how different worlds can be, and puts Earth in context as one special planet among many. It's a foundation for all space curiosity.

## Card: Key terms
- **Rocky (terrestrial) planets**: small, solid inner planets.
- **Gas and ice giants**: large outer planets.
- **Orbit**: a planet's path around the Sun.
- **Habitable zone**: the region where liquid water can exist.

## Card: The rocky inner planets
image: rockyInnerPlanetsHero
The four inner planets are **rocky** and relatively small: **Mercury** (closest, scorched and airless), **Venus** (similar size to Earth but with a thick, super-hot toxic atmosphere — the hottest planet), **Earth** (our home, in the **habitable zone** with liquid water and life), and **Mars** (the cold, red desert world that's a focus of exploration). These solid worlds are very different despite being neighbors.

## Card: The giant outer planets
Beyond Mars lie the four giants, far larger and made mostly of gas and ice. **Jupiter** is the largest, a gas giant with a famous great storm and many moons. **Saturn** is famed for its spectacular rings. **Uranus** and **Neptune** are distant, cold "ice giants." These giants have no solid surface to stand on and host dozens of moons each. They dwarf the rocky inner worlds.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking all planets are similar to Earth (they're wildly diverse); assuming Venus is mild because it's Earth-sized (it's the hottest, with a crushing toxic atmosphere); imagining you could stand on the gas giants (they have no solid surface); and thinking the planets are evenly spaced or close together (distances are vast). Each world is strikingly unique.

## Card: Quick recap
The solar system's eight planets split into the small rocky inner worlds (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars) and the giant outer planets (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune). They're wildly diverse — scorching Venus, the red desert of Mars, ringed Saturn, giant Jupiter. Earth sits in the habitable zone with liquid water. Knowing the planets brings the cosmos to life.
