## Card: What it is
image: bodyddGeneticsDnaHero
**DNA** is the molecule carrying the genetic instructions for building and running your body — a kind of code stored in nearly every cell. **Genetics** is the study of how these instructions work and pass from parents to children. DNA is, quite literally, the instruction manual that makes you *you*.

## Card: Why it matters
DNA and genetics explain inheritance (why you resemble your family), how traits pass through generations, and the basis of much modern medicine, ancestry, and biology. Understanding the basics demystifies a topic that's increasingly in the news (genetic testing, gene therapy) and reveals the elegant code underlying all life.

## Card: Key terms
- **DNA**: the molecule storing genetic instructions.
- **Gene**: a section of DNA coding for a particular trait or function.
- **Genome**: the complete set of an organism's DNA.
- **Inheritance**: passing genes from parents to offspring.

## Card: The code of life
**DNA** is like a vast instruction manual written in a chemical code, stored in nearly every cell. A **gene** is a section of that manual with instructions for a specific feature or function (such as a step in making a protein that affects eye color). Your complete set of DNA is your **genome**. Remarkably, the same basic DNA code underlies all life on Earth, from bacteria to humans.

## Card: How traits are inherited
image: inheritanceMixHero
Through **inheritance**, you receive DNA from both parents — a mix of theirs — which is why you share traits with your family yet aren't identical to either parent. Genes influence countless features, but importantly, many traits result from genes *and* environment together, not genes alone. So DNA provides instructions and tendencies, not an unchangeable destiny — how genes express depends on many factors.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking DNA rigidly determines everything (genes interact with environment; they're not absolute destiny); imagining you're a copy of one parent (you inherit a mix of both); confusing genes with a fixed fate; and not realizing all life shares the same basic DNA code. DNA is an instruction manual that guides, influenced by environment, not an unchangeable script.

## Card: Quick recap
DNA is the chemical code storing genetic instructions in nearly every cell; a gene is a section coding for a trait, and your full set of DNA is your genome. You inherit a mix from both parents, sharing family traits. But genes interact with environment, so DNA guides tendencies rather than dictating an absolute destiny. The same basic code underlies all life on Earth.
