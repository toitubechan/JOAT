## Card: What it is
image: aiLimitsHero
AI is genuinely powerful at some things and surprisingly poor at others. Knowing where the line sits — what it can and can't reliably do — is the key to using it well. The reality is more nuanced than both the breathless hype and the dismissive skepticism suggest.

## Card: Why it matters
Misjudging AI's abilities leads to trouble: trusting it where it's unreliable, or ignoring it where it'd genuinely help. Calibrated expectations let you get real value from AI tools while avoiding their pitfalls. This judgment is fast becoming an everyday practical skill.

## Card: Key terms
- **Generative AI**: AI that creates text, images, or other content.
- **Hallucination**: when AI confidently states something false.
- **Strength**: a task AI does reliably well.
- **Limitation**: a task AI does poorly or unreliably.

## Card: What AI does well
AI shines at: handling huge amounts of information quickly, spotting patterns, drafting and summarizing text, translating, generating ideas, and automating repetitive tasks. **Generative AI** can produce useful first drafts of writing, images, or code in seconds. For brainstorming, summarizing, and getting started, it's a genuine productivity boost.

## Card: What AI struggles with
image: hallucinationCheck
Key **limitations**: AI can **hallucinate** — state false things with total confidence — so it's unreliable for facts without checking. It lacks real understanding, common sense, and up-to-date or private knowledge it wasn't trained on. It can't be trusted with high-stakes decisions alone, and it reflects biases in its data.

## Card: Common mistakes
The biggest mistake is trusting AI output as fact without verifying — hallucinations look just as confident as correct answers. Another is using it for high-stakes decisions (medical, legal, financial) without professional input. Conversely, some avoid it entirely, missing easy gains in drafting and brainstorming where accuracy matters less.

## Card: Quick recap
AI excels at processing information, drafting, summarizing, translating, and idea generation. It struggles with reliable facts (it can hallucinate), genuine understanding, and high-stakes decisions. Use it as a fast assistant for low-stakes drafting and brainstorming, but always verify facts and keep humans in charge of important choices.
