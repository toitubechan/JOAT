## Card: What it is
image: lawSigningDocumentsHero
Signing a document often creates a binding legal commitment — so **signing safely** means understanding what you're agreeing to before you put your name to it. Many problems come from people signing things they didn't read or understand. A few sensible habits protect you from costly mistakes. This is general educational knowledge; for important documents, professional advice is wise.

## Card: Why it matters
We sign documents regularly — contracts, agreements, forms — often binding ourselves legally. Signing carelessly can lock you into unfair terms, unexpected obligations, or commitments you didn't intend. Knowing how to sign safely protects you from these avoidable problems. It's practical knowledge that can save significant money and stress.

## Card: Key terms
- **Binding**: legally committing you once signed.
- **Read before signing**: understanding the document first.
- **Fine print**: small-text details easy to overlook.
- **Due diligence**: checking carefully before committing.

## Card: Read and understand first
image: lawSigningDocumentsReadAndUnderstandFirstHero
The golden rule: **read and understand before signing**. A signature usually makes a document **binding** — "I didn't read it" is rarely a valid excuse later. So read the whole thing, especially the **fine print** where catches often hide, and make sure you understand what you're committing to: what you must do, what it costs, how to exit, and any obligations or penalties. Don't sign anything you don't understand.

## Card: Sensible signing habits
Beyond reading: never let yourself be **rushed** or pressured into signing (pressure is a warning sign). Ask questions about anything unclear — a fair party will explain. For significant documents, consider getting advice or a professional review first. Keep a copy of everything you sign. And be wary of signing blank or incomplete documents. These simple **due diligence** habits, done before you sign, prevent most document-related problems.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: signing without reading or understanding; being rushed or pressured into signing quickly; ignoring the fine print; not asking about unclear terms; signing blank or incomplete documents; and not keeping a copy. The fix is consistent: read carefully, never be rushed, ask questions, get advice for important documents, and keep records — all *before* you sign, when you still have power.

## Card: Quick recap
Signing a document usually creates a binding commitment, so sign safely: read and understand the whole thing (especially the fine print) before signing, since "I didn't read it" rarely helps later. Never be rushed or pressured, ask about anything unclear, get advice for important documents, keep a copy, and never sign blank documents. These habits, done before signing, prevent most costly problems.
