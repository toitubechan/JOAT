## Card: What it is
image: interviewQuestionsHero
Most interviews feature a predictable set of **common questions** — "tell me about yourself," "what's your weakness," "why do you want this job." Knowing how to approach these well means you're rarely caught off guard, and your answers land with confidence rather than panic.

## Card: Why it matters
These questions trip up countless candidates who haven't thought them through. But because they're so predictable, you can prepare strong answers in advance. Handling them well makes you look composed and self-aware — and avoids the common stumbles that sink otherwise good candidates.

## Card: Key terms
- **"Tell me about yourself"**: an opener inviting a brief professional summary.
- **Weakness question**: a question probing self-awareness.
- **Behavioral question**: "tell me about a time you..." — wants a real example.
- **Why this role**: a question testing your motivation and fit.

## Card: The classic openers
**"Tell me about yourself"** isn't an invitation for your life story — give a concise professional summary leading to why you're here. For **"why do you want this job?"**, show you understand the role and connect it to your genuine interest and strengths. These openers set the tone, so a clear, confident answer starts you strong.

## Card: Tricky and behavioral questions
image: behavioralAnswersHero
The **weakness question** wants self-awareness — name a real but non-fatal weakness and what you're doing about it (avoid the fake "I work too hard"). **Behavioral questions** ("tell me about a time you handled conflict") want a real example — use the STAR structure. They're checking how you've actually behaved, since past behavior predicts future performance.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: rambling life stories for "tell me about yourself"; a cliché or arrogant weakness answer; vague responses to behavioral questions with no real example; badmouthing past employers; and answering "why this job?" generically, showing no real interest. Prepare these predictable ones, and stay specific, honest, and positive.

## Card: Quick recap
Common interview questions are predictable, so prepare them: keep "tell me about yourself" a concise professional summary; give a genuine but non-fatal weakness with how you're improving; answer behavioral questions with real STAR examples; and connect "why this job?" to real interest. Stay specific, honest, and positive.
