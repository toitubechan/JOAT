## Card: What it is
image: bodyddHeartCirculationHero
The **heart** is a muscular pump that pushes blood through a vast network of vessels, delivering oxygen and nutrients to every cell and carrying away waste. This is the **circulatory system**. The heart beats around 100,000 times a day, every day of your life, without rest — a tireless engine at your core.

## Card: Why it matters
The circulatory system keeps every cell alive, and heart health is one of the biggest factors in long-term wellbeing. Understanding how the heart and circulation work explains why exercise, blood pressure, and lifestyle matter so much, and demystifies one of the body's most vital systems. Heart disease is a leading cause of death, making this knowledge genuinely important.

## Card: Key terms
- **Heart**: the muscular pump driving blood circulation.
- **Arteries and veins**: vessels carrying blood from and to the heart.
- **Oxygenated blood**: blood carrying oxygen to the body.
- **Blood pressure**: the force of blood against vessel walls.

## Card: A double pump
image: doublePumpHero
The heart is really a **double pump** with chambers. One side pumps oxygen-poor blood to the lungs to collect oxygen and release carbon dioxide. The other side pumps this freshly **oxygenated blood** out to the whole body. **Arteries** carry blood away from the heart (under pressure), and **veins** return it. This continuous loop — body, heart, lungs, heart, body — never stops.

## Card: What circulation delivers
Blood is a delivery and collection service for every cell: it brings **oxygen** (from the lungs) and nutrients (from food), and carries away carbon dioxide and waste. It also transports heat, hormones, and immune cells. **Blood pressure** — the force of blood against vessel walls — is a key health measure; consistently high pressure strains the heart and vessels, which is why it's monitored.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: picturing the heart as a single simple pump (it's a double pump serving body and lungs); thinking blood only carries oxygen (it also delivers nutrients and removes waste); confusing arteries and veins (arteries away, veins back); and underrating how lifestyle affects heart health. The circulatory system is a tireless, vital delivery network every cell depends on.

## Card: Quick recap
The heart is a tireless double pump: one side sends blood to the lungs for oxygen, the other pumps oxygenated blood to the whole body, with arteries carrying it away and veins returning it. This circulation delivers oxygen and nutrients to every cell and removes waste. Blood pressure is a key health measure. Heart health, shaped by lifestyle, is central to long-term wellbeing.
