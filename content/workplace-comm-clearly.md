## Card: What it is
image: workplaceCommunicationHero
**Workplace communication** is how you share information, ideas, and needs with colleagues — through speaking, writing, and listening. Clear communication isn't a "soft" extra; it's a core skill that affects how effective you are and how you're perceived. Much of work success comes down to communicating well.

## Card: Why it matters
Misunderstandings waste time, cause errors, and create friction. People who communicate clearly get their ideas heard, avoid confusion, and are trusted with more. It's also one of the most visible skills — clear communicators stand out and advance. Strong communication often matters as much as technical ability for getting ahead.

## Card: Key terms
- **Clarity**: being easily understood.
- **Audience awareness**: tailoring your message to who's receiving it.
- **Concise**: getting to the point without waffle.
- **Active listening**: fully focusing on understanding others.

## Card: Clarity is the goal
The aim of communication is to be **understood** — not to sound clever or cover everything. **Clarity** means being concise, organized, and to the point. Before communicating, know what you actually want the other person to understand or do. Rambling, jargon, and burying the main point are the enemies of clarity, and they cost everyone time.

## Card: Know your audience and listen
image: clarityAudienceHero
Good communicators practice **audience awareness** — adjusting their message for who's receiving it (a technical colleague versus a busy manager need different levels of detail). And communication is two-way: **active listening** (genuinely focusing on understanding others, not just waiting to talk) prevents misunderstandings and shows respect. Listening well is half of communicating well.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: assuming people understood when they didn't; burying the main point under detail; using jargon that confuses; not tailoring the message to the audience; and not listening — talking past people instead of understanding them. Clear communication means leading with your point and checking you've been understood.

## Card: Quick recap
Workplace communication is a core career skill, not a soft extra. Aim for clarity — be concise, organized, and lead with your point. Practice audience awareness, tailoring the message to who's receiving it, and listen actively to truly understand others. Clear communicators are trusted, effective, and stand out.
