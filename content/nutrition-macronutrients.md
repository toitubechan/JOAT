## Card: What it is
image: nutritionMacrosHero
**Macronutrients** are the three nutrients your body needs in large amounts: **carbohydrates**, **protein**, and **fat**. Together they provide nearly all your energy and much of your body's structure. Understanding what each does clears up a lot of diet confusion in one go.

## Card: Why it matters
Most diet advice and food marketing revolves around these three — "low-carb," "high-protein," "low-fat." Knowing what each actually does for you lets you see through the hype and understand why your body needs all three, not just whichever one is currently fashionable to fear or favor.

## Card: Key terms
- **Carbohydrates**: the body's main quick energy source.
- **Protein**: builds and repairs muscles and tissues.
- **Fat**: stores energy and supports vital functions.
- **Fiber**: a carbohydrate that aids digestion.

## Card: Carbs and protein
image: carbsProteinFat
**Carbohydrates** are your body's preferred quick fuel — found in grains, fruit, vegetables, and sugars. Some carbs also contain **fiber**, which aids digestion. **Protein** is the builder — it repairs and grows muscles and tissues, found in meat, fish, eggs, beans, and dairy. Active people and growing bodies especially rely on it.

## Card: Fat is not the enemy
**Fat** has a bad reputation it doesn't fully deserve. It's a concentrated energy store, helps absorb certain vitamins, and supports vital functions like hormones and brain health. The body genuinely needs fat. The quality matters more than avoiding it — fats from foods like nuts, fish, and olive oil are valuable.

## Card: Common mistakes
A big mistake is demonizing one macronutrient entirely — cutting all carbs or all fat — when the body needs all three. Another is ignoring quality: sugary carbs and fried fats differ greatly from whole-grain carbs and nut fats. And many forget fiber, a carb crucial for digestion and feeling full.

## Card: Quick recap
The three macronutrients each have a job: carbs for quick energy (with fiber for digestion), protein for building and repair, and fat for stored energy and vital functions. The body needs all three — quality matters more than cutting any one. Balance beats banning whole food groups.
