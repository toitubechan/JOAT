## Card: What it is
image: atmosphereHero
The **atmosphere** is the layer of gases surrounding Earth — the air we breathe and much more. It's held to the planet by gravity, made mostly of nitrogen and oxygen, and arranged in layers. This thin shell of gas protects life, enables weather, and makes Earth habitable.

## Card: Why it matters
The atmosphere makes life possible — providing breathable air, shielding us from harmful radiation and space debris, trapping warmth, and driving weather and climate. Understanding it explains why Earth is livable, how weather works, and why issues like air pollution and the ozone layer matter. It's a thin, precious shell we depend on entirely.

## Card: Key terms
- **Atmosphere**: the layer of gases surrounding Earth.
- **Nitrogen and oxygen**: the main gases in the air.
- **Layers**: distinct bands of the atmosphere at different heights.
- **Ozone layer**: a layer that blocks harmful radiation.

## Card: What the air is made of
The atmosphere is mostly **nitrogen** (about four-fifths) and **oxygen** (about one-fifth), with small amounts of other gases like carbon dioxide and water vapor. Oxygen is what we breathe; the trace gases, though tiny in amount, matter enormously (carbon dioxide affects climate, for instance). This mix, held by gravity, is what makes Earth's air breathable and life-supporting.

## Card: Layers and protection
image: atmosphereLayersHero
The atmosphere is arranged in **layers** at different heights, each with different conditions. Weather happens in the lowest layer, where we live. Higher up, the **ozone layer** absorbs much of the sun's harmful ultraviolet radiation, protecting life. The atmosphere also burns up most incoming space debris (meteors) and traps enough heat to keep Earth warm enough for life. It's a multi-layered protective shield.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking air is mostly oxygen (it's mostly nitrogen); imagining the atmosphere is huge and limitless (it's a surprisingly thin shell); not realizing trace gases like CO₂ matter despite being tiny in amount; and not knowing the atmosphere protects us from radiation and debris. This thin layer does far more than just provide air to breathe.

## Card: Quick recap
The atmosphere is the layer of gases around Earth — mostly nitrogen and oxygen, held by gravity and arranged in layers. It provides breathable air, shields us from harmful radiation (the ozone layer) and space debris, traps warmth, and drives weather. Trace gases like CO₂ matter greatly despite being tiny. This thin, precious shell makes Earth habitable.
