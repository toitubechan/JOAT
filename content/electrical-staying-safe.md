## Card: What it is
image: electricalSafetyHero
Electrical safety is a set of simple, non-negotiable habits that keep you from getting shocked or starting a fire. Unlike many home topics, here the goal isn't fixing things — it's knowing the rules that protect you and recognizing danger before it harms you.

## Card: Why it matters
Electricity causes injuries, deaths, and house fires every year, almost always through avoidable mistakes. The hazards are invisible, so rules matter more than instinct. These habits cost nothing and take seconds, but they're genuinely life-saving. This is the one home topic where caution always wins.

## Card: Key terms
- **Shock**: electricity passing through the body, which can be deadly.
- **Short circuit**: electricity taking a wrong, unintended path.
- **Overload**: drawing more power than a circuit can safely handle.
- **Water + electricity**: a deadly combination to always avoid.

## Card: The golden rules
A few rules cover most danger: never touch electrical things with wet hands or near water; never overload a socket with too many adapters; don't run cords under rugs where they overheat unseen; and never poke metal objects into outlets. Water and electricity together are especially deadly — keep them firmly apart.

## Card: Spot the warning signs
image: warningSignsElectrical
Your home warns you before disaster. Take these seriously: scorch marks or a burning smell near outlets, warm or buzzing switches, frequently tripping breakers, flickering lights, or sparks when plugging in. Any of these means stop using that outlet and get it checked. They're signs of a fault that could cause a fire.

## Card: Common mistakes
The deadliest mistake is handling anything electrical near water or with wet hands. Another is **overloading** sockets with stacked adapters, which overheats wiring. People also ignore warning signs like burning smells, and use damaged cords or plugs with exposed wires — all of which are genuine fire and shock risks.

## Card: Quick recap
Electrical safety is about rules, not repairs: keep water away, never overload sockets, avoid damaged cords, and never poke metal into outlets. Heed warning signs — burning smells, buzzing, scorch marks, tripping breakers — and stop using a faulty outlet immediately. With electricity, caution always wins.
