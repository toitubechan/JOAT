## Card: What it is
image: sleepHabitsHero
**Sleep hygiene** is the set of daily habits and conditions that help you fall asleep and stay asleep. Good sleep isn't just luck — it's largely the result of what you do in the hours before bed and the environment you sleep in. These habits are simple and highly effective.

## Card: Why it matters
Most everyday sleep problems come from habits, not medical conditions — and habits can be changed. Improving sleep hygiene is the first and most powerful step toward better sleep, often fixing issues people assumed they were stuck with. It's free, drug-free, and within your control.

## Card: Key terms
- **Sleep hygiene**: habits that support good sleep.
- **Circadian rhythm**: your internal 24-hour body clock.
- **Blue light**: screen light that can delay sleepiness.
- **Wind-down routine**: calming activities before bed.

## Card: Keep a consistent rhythm
Your **circadian rhythm** craves regularity. Going to bed and waking at roughly the same times daily — even weekends — trains your body to feel sleepy and alert on schedule. Morning daylight helps set this clock. Consistency is one of the single most effective things you can do for sleep quality.

## Card: Build the right environment and routine
image: sleepEnvironmentRoutine
Make the bedroom **dark, quiet, cool, and comfortable**. Create a **wind-down routine**: dim lights and do something calming before bed. Cut **blue light** from screens in the last hour, as it can delay sleepiness. Avoid caffeine late in the day and large meals close to bedtime. Reserve the bed for sleep, not scrolling.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common sleep-wrecking habits: scrolling on a bright phone in bed; irregular sleep times that confuse your body clock; caffeine in the afternoon or evening (it lingers for hours); and using alcohol to fall asleep, which actually fragments sleep later in the night. Each is fixable with a small change.

## Card: Quick recap
Good sleep is built by habit: keep consistent sleep and wake times, get morning daylight, make the bedroom dark, quiet, and cool, and wind down with calm, screen-free time before bed. Avoid late caffeine and using alcohol as a sleep aid. These simple habits fix most everyday sleep problems.
