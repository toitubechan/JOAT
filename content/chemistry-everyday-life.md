## Card: What it is
image: everydayChemistryHero
Chemistry isn't confined to labs — it's happening constantly in your kitchen, body, and home. From cooking to cleaning to digestion, everyday life is full of chemistry. Recognizing it makes the science feel real and useful, not abstract. This card set connects the basics to things you do every day.

## Card: Why it matters
Seeing chemistry in everyday life makes it relatable and genuinely useful — it explains why baking works, why soap cleans, why food spoils, and more. It also builds practical intuition and helps you make sense of product labels and warnings. Chemistry stops being a school subject and becomes a lens on daily life.

## Card: Key terms
- **Acid and base**: opposite types of substance (like lemon vs. soap).
- **pH**: a scale measuring how acidic or basic something is.
- **Reaction in cooking**: heat-driven changes that create flavor.
- **Everyday chemistry**: chemical processes in daily life.

## Card: Chemistry in the kitchen
image: kitchenChemistry
Cooking is applied chemistry. Heat triggers reactions that brown food and create flavor; baking relies on reactions producing gas to make dough rise; and food spoils through chemical and microbial changes. Even taste involves chemistry — the sourness of lemon is an **acid**, the bitterness of some foods relates to their chemical makeup. Your kitchen is a chemistry lab.

## Card: Acids, bases, and cleaning
**Acids** (lemon, vinegar) and **bases** (soap, baking soda) are opposites on the **pH** scale, and they react with each other (vinegar and baking soda fizzing is a classic reaction). Cleaning often uses chemistry: soap works by grabbing both grease and water, lifting dirt away. Many household products are acids or bases doing chemical work — which is also why some should never be mixed.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking chemistry only happens in labs (it's everywhere); not realizing cooking and cleaning are chemistry; assuming "chemical" means artificial or bad (everything is made of chemicals, including water and food); and mixing household products dangerously without knowing they react. Everyday chemistry is real, useful, and worth respecting.

## Card: Quick recap
Chemistry is all around you: cooking is heat-driven reactions creating flavor and rise, food spoils chemically, soap cleans by grabbing grease and water, and acids and bases (lemon, soap) react. "Chemical" doesn't mean bad — everything, including water, is chemicals. Recognizing everyday chemistry makes the science real and useful.
