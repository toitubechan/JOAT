## Card: What it is
image: rentingProcessHero
**Renting** means paying a landlord to live in their property for a period, under an agreement. The process — finding a place, applying, paying upfront costs, and moving in — follows a fairly standard path. Understanding it helps first-time renters navigate confidently. (General guidance; rules vary by location.)

## Card: Why it matters
Renting is how most people first live independently, and the process can feel overwhelming with its costs, paperwork, and jargon. Understanding how it works helps you avoid scams, budget for the real upfront costs, and approach it with confidence rather than confusion. It's essential life-admin knowledge for independent living.

## Card: Key terms
- **Landlord**: the property owner you rent from.
- **Tenant**: you, the person renting.
- **Deposit**: upfront money held against damage or unpaid rent.
- **Upfront costs**: the total needed before moving in.

## Card: The standard process
The typical path: search for a place within budget; view it in person; apply (often involving references, proof of income, and ID checks); if accepted, sign a tenancy agreement; pay the **upfront costs**; and move in. Knowing this sequence helps you prepare the documents you'll need and not be caught off guard at each stage.

## Card: Budget for the real upfront cost
image: upfrontRentalCost
A common shock: the money needed *before* moving in is far more than one month's rent. You typically need a **deposit** (often equal to several weeks' or a month's rent or more) *plus* the first month's rent upfront, and sometimes fees. So budget for this larger total — many first-time renters underestimate it. Knowing the real upfront cost prevents a nasty surprise.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: underestimating the upfront costs (deposit plus first rent and fees); not viewing a place in person before committing (a scam risk); not having documents (references, ID, proof of income) ready; and rushing into the first option. Also beware rental scams — never pay before viewing and verifying the property and landlord are genuine.

## Card: Quick recap
Renting follows a standard path: search within budget, view in person, apply with documents, sign the agreement, pay upfront costs, and move in. Budget for the real upfront cost — usually a deposit *plus* first month's rent and fees, far more than one month alone. Always view before paying, and watch for rental scams.
