## Card: What it is
image: sustainCarbonFootprintHero
Your **carbon footprint** is the total amount of greenhouse gases — mainly carbon dioxide — produced by your lifestyle and choices. It's a way of measuring your personal contribution to climate change. Understanding it helps you see where your impact actually comes from, and where changes make the most difference.

## Card: Why it matters
With climate change a major issue, many people want to reduce their impact but don't know where to focus. Understanding your carbon footprint reveals which actions genuinely matter most — letting you spend effort where it counts rather than on token gestures. It cuts through the noise about what actually helps.

## Card: Key terms
- **Carbon footprint**: total greenhouse gases your lifestyle produces.
- **Greenhouse gases**: gases like CO₂ that drive climate change.
- **High-impact actions**: changes that cut the most emissions.
- **Carbon offset**: paying to compensate for emissions elsewhere.

## Card: Where it comes from
image: sustainCarbonFootprintWhereItComesFromHero
For most people, the biggest footprint sources are: **transport** (especially flying and driving), **home energy** (heating and electricity), and **food** (especially meat). These big three usually dwarf smaller items. Knowing this matters because it directs effort: cutting a long-haul flight or driving less does far more than, say, obsessing over plastic straws.

## Card: Focus on high-impact actions
The key insight: **high-impact actions** matter far more than token ones. Flying less, driving less (or switching to public transport, cycling, or efficient vehicles), reducing home energy use, and eating less meat typically cut the most. Many feel-good small actions (like avoiding straws) have minimal footprint impact. Focusing on the big levers, not the tiny ones, is how to genuinely reduce your footprint.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: focusing on tiny actions (straws, bags) while ignoring the big three (transport, home energy, food); thinking individual action is either pointless or sufficient alone (it's part of a bigger picture); guilt-driven token gestures; and over-relying on carbon offsets instead of reducing. Target the high-impact areas, and don't sweat the trivial ones.

## Card: Quick recap
Your carbon footprint is the greenhouse gases your lifestyle produces. The biggest sources for most people are transport (especially flying), home energy, and food (especially meat) — these dwarf small items. Focus your effort on these high-impact areas rather than token gestures. Knowing where impact really comes from lets you make changes that genuinely count.
