## Card: What it is
image: realestateMortgageProcessHero
A **mortgage** is a loan used to buy property, where the property itself secures the loan. The mortgage process is the journey from applying for the loan to owning the home. It can seem complex and intimidating, but the basic steps are understandable. This is general educational knowledge; specifics vary by country, and for real decisions, consult a professional.

## Card: Why it matters
Most people buying property need a mortgage, and it's likely the largest loan you'll ever take. Understanding how mortgages work — and the process — helps you avoid costly mistakes, compare options sensibly, and approach home-buying with confidence rather than confusion. Given the sums and decades involved, this knowledge is genuinely valuable.

## Card: Key terms
- **Mortgage**: a loan to buy property, secured by the property.
- **Down payment / deposit**: the upfront sum you pay yourself.
- **Interest rate**: the cost of borrowing, affecting total repayment.
- **Pre-approval**: a lender's estimate of what you can borrow.

## Card: How a mortgage works
image: realestateMortgageProcessHowAMortgageWorksHero
A **mortgage** lets you buy a property by borrowing most of the cost, paying it back over many years (often decades) with **interest**. You typically pay a **down payment** (deposit) upfront — a percentage of the price — and borrow the rest. The property *secures* the loan, meaning if you don't repay, the lender can take it. The **interest rate** hugely affects total cost over the years — even a small rate difference adds up to a large amount over decades.

## Card: The general process
The typical journey: check your finances and get **pre-approval** (a lender estimates what you can borrow, useful before house-hunting); find a property and make an offer; formally apply for the mortgage; the lender assesses you and the property (including valuing it); and on approval, complete the purchase. Throughout, comparing lenders and rates matters — they're not all the same, and shopping around can save large sums over the loan's life. The process takes time and paperwork, but follows a logical sequence.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: not comparing lenders and rates (small rate differences cost a lot over decades); borrowing the maximum offered without considering what's truly affordable; underestimating the total cost including interest and fees; skipping pre-approval before house-hunting; and not understanding the terms. A mortgage is a huge, long commitment — understand it, compare options, and consult a professional for real decisions.

## Card: Quick recap
A mortgage is a loan to buy property, secured by the property, repaid over many years with interest — you pay a deposit upfront and borrow the rest. The interest rate greatly affects total cost over decades. The process: check finances and get pre-approval, find a property, apply, the lender assesses you and the property, then complete. Compare lenders and rates (it saves large sums), and consult a professional for real decisions.
