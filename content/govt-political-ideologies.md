## Card: What it is
image: govtPoliticalIdeologiesHero
**Political ideologies** are broad sets of beliefs about how society should be organized — about the role of government, the economy, individual freedom, equality, and tradition. They shape political debates and parties. This is a strictly neutral, educational overview of *what* ideologies are and the kinds of questions they answer — not promoting or criticizing any.

## Card: Why it matters
Political debates and parties are shaped by underlying ideologies. Understanding the main ones — neutrally — helps you make sense of political discussions, recognize different perspectives, and think more clearly about political questions. It fosters understanding across disagreements. The goal here is comprehension, not persuasion: understanding *how* people think politically, whatever you believe.

## Card: Key terms
- **Ideology**: a set of beliefs about how society should be organized.
- **Left and right**: a common (if simplified) political spectrum.
- **Role of government**: how much government should do — a key dividing question.
- **Trade-offs**: most ideologies balance competing values differently.

## Card: What ideologies disagree about
image: govtPoliticalIdeologiesWhatIdeologiesDisagreeAboutHero
Political ideologies largely differ on a few core questions: How big a **role** should government play (more involvement versus less)? How to balance **individual freedom** versus **collective good or equality**? How much to value **tradition and stability** versus change and reform? And how should the economy be organized? Different ideologies answer these differently, reflecting genuinely different values and priorities — not simply "right" and "wrong" answers, but different **trade-offs**.

## Card: The spectrum (and its limits)
Politics is often described on a **left–right** spectrum — broadly, "left" tends to favor more government role in the economy and greater emphasis on equality and change, while "right" tends to favor less government economic role and more emphasis on tradition and individual freedom. But this is a simplification: real views are more complex and multidimensional, people hold mixes of positions, and the terms mean different things in different places. It's a rough guide, not a precise map.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking one ideology is simply "correct" and others wrong (they reflect different values and trade-offs); treating the left-right spectrum as precise (it's a rough simplification); assuming people fit neatly into one box (most hold mixed views); and demonizing those who disagree rather than understanding their perspective. Understanding ideologies neutrally fosters clearer thinking and mutual respect across political differences.

## Card: Quick recap
Political ideologies are broad sets of beliefs about how society should be organized, differing on core questions: the role of government, freedom versus equality, and tradition versus change. The left–right spectrum is a common but simplified guide, and real views are more complex and mixed. Ideologies reflect different values and trade-offs, not simply right and wrong. Understanding them neutrally fosters clearer thinking and respect across disagreements.
