## Card: What it is
image: bankHero
A **bank** is a business that keeps your money safe, lets you move it around easily, and lends some of it to others. When you deposit cash, the bank doesn't lock it in a vault with your name on it — it records that it owes you that amount, and puts most of the money to work elsewhere.

## Card: Why it matters
Banks are the plumbing of everyday money. Your salary, rent, bills, and card payments all flow through them. Understanding what a bank does — and doesn't do — helps you use it as a tool instead of treating it as a mysterious black box that just holds your cash.

## Card: Key terms
- **Deposit**: money you put into the bank.
- **Withdrawal**: money you take out.
- **Loan**: money the bank lends, expecting repayment with interest.
- **Reserve**: the portion banks keep available rather than lending.

## Card: How banks make money
image: interestSpread
Banks pay you a little interest on your savings, then lend that money to others at a higher interest rate. The difference is their profit. They also charge fees for services. So a bank is essentially a middleman: it gathers many people's deposits and channels them to borrowers who need funds.

## Card: Is my money safe?
Banks keep only a fraction of deposits on hand (the **reserve**) and lend the rest — this is normal and how the system works. To protect you, most countries run **deposit insurance**, which guarantees your money up to a set limit even if the bank fails. That's why a regulated bank is far safer than cash under a mattress.

## Card: Common mistakes
People often assume their exact banknotes sit untouched in a vault — they don't, and that's fine. A bigger mistake is keeping large amounts at a single bank above the insured limit. Another is ignoring that the bank is a business: its products are designed to earn from you, so the fine print matters.

## Card: Quick recap
A bank safely holds your money, moves it for you, and lends out most of it to earn from the gap in interest. Deposit insurance protects your funds up to a limit. Treat the bank as a useful, profit-seeking tool — convenient, but worth reading the fine print.
