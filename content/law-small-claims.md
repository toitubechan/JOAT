## Card: What it is
image: lawSmallClaimsHero
When you have a dispute over a relatively small amount of money or a minor issue, there are usually ways to resolve it without an expensive, full-blown lawsuit — including **small claims** processes designed to be simple, cheap, and accessible without a lawyer. Knowing these options empowers you to pursue fair outcomes. This is general educational knowledge; specifics vary by location.

## Card: Why it matters
Many people give up on legitimate disputes (a faulty product, unreturned deposit, unpaid debt) assuming legal action is too expensive or complex. Knowing that accessible, low-cost options exist — like small claims — empowers you to stand up for what you're owed. It can save or recover real money, and it demystifies a part of the legal system designed to be usable by ordinary people.

## Card: Key terms
- **Dispute**: a disagreement, often over money or an obligation.
- **Small claims**: a simplified court process for smaller amounts.
- **Resolution**: settling a dispute, ideally without court.
- **Escalation**: moving from informal resolution toward formal action.

## Card: Try to resolve it first
image: lawSmallClaimsTryToResolveItFirstHero
Before any formal action, the sensible first step is **resolution** through direct, calm communication: clearly state the problem and what you want, in writing where possible, and try to settle it directly. Many disputes resolve here. If that fails, there may be intermediate steps (like a formal complaint, mediation, or a relevant ombudsman or authority). Keeping records throughout — of the issue, communications, and evidence — strengthens your position at every stage.

## Card: Small claims as a last resort
If informal **resolution** fails, **small claims** processes exist in many places for disputes under a certain amount. They're deliberately designed to be simpler, cheaper, and usable *without* a lawyer — accessible to ordinary people. You typically file a claim, present your evidence, and a decision is made. It's the affordable path for smaller disputes where hiring a lawyer wouldn't make sense. Knowing this option exists means you're not powerless over modest sums.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: giving up on legitimate disputes assuming legal action is unaffordable (accessible options like small claims exist); jumping to formal action before trying direct resolution; not keeping records and evidence (which weaken your case); and not knowing your local small claims process. The path is usually: try to resolve directly, escalate sensibly, and use small claims as an affordable last resort.

## Card: Quick recap
For smaller disputes (a faulty product, unreturned deposit, unpaid debt), you don't always need an expensive lawsuit. First try direct, calm resolution in writing, then escalate sensibly (complaints, mediation, relevant authorities). As a last resort, small claims processes — simple, cheap, and usable without a lawyer — exist in many places. Keep records throughout. Knowing these options means you're not powerless over modest sums.
