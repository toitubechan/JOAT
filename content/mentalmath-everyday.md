## Card: What it is
image: dataMentalmathEverydayHero
This final card set applies mental math to **everyday situations** — tips, discounts, splitting bills, unit prices, and quick checks. It pulls the tricks together into the practical moments where they actually save you time and money. This is where mental math pays off in real life.

## Card: Why it matters
Knowing tricks is one thing; using them in daily life is where the value lies. Applying mental math to real situations saves money (spotting bad deals, checking bills), saves time, and builds lasting confidence with numbers. These everyday applications turn mental math from an abstract skill into a genuinely useful daily habit.

## Card: Key terms
- **Unit price**: the price per standard amount, for comparing value.
- **Tip calculation**: working out a percentage for service.
- **Splitting a bill**: dividing a total among people.
- **Quick check**: verifying a figure makes sense.

## Card: Shopping and dining
image: dataMentalmathEverydayShoppingAndDiningHero
Mental math shines when spending. **Tips**: 10% (move the decimal) is the base — 20% is double it, 15% is 10% plus half. **Discounts**: 30% off €40 is 30% (€12) off, leaving €28. **Unit price**: divide price by quantity to compare value (which pack is really cheaper?). **Splitting a bill**: divide the total by the number of people, perhaps rounding up for ease. These quick calculations make you a sharper, faster spender.

## Card: Quick checks everywhere
Beyond shopping, mental math is a constant **quick check**. Does this bill total look right? Is this "deal" actually good? Do I have enough cash? Is this statistic plausible? A fast estimate answers these on the spot, catching errors and bad deals before they cost you. Building the habit of a quick mental check — rather than blindly trusting figures — is the real everyday payoff.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: knowing the tricks but not actually using them in the moment; blindly trusting bills, prices, and "deals" without a quick check; not comparing unit prices (so missing which option is truly cheaper); and reaching for a calculator when a quick estimate would do. The habit is what matters: pause and do a fast mental check in everyday situations.

## Card: Quick recap
Mental math pays off in everyday moments: tips (10% base, scaled), discounts (the percentage off the price), unit prices (to compare real value), and splitting bills. Above all, use it as a constant quick check — does this bill, deal, or figure make sense? Building the habit of a fast mental check, rather than blind trust, is where mental math truly earns its keep.
