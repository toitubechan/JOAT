## Card: What it is
image: fitnessCardioVsStrengthHero
**Cardio** (cardiovascular exercise) and **strength training** are the two main types of exercise, and they do different things for your body. Cardio works your heart and lungs; strength training builds muscle and strength. Understanding the difference — and that both have value — helps you exercise effectively for your goals. This is general educational knowledge, not medical or fitness advice.

## Card: Why it matters
People often focus on one type of exercise (or wonder which is "better"), missing that cardio and strength training offer different, complementary benefits. Understanding what each does helps you choose exercise that matches your goals and build a balanced approach. It clears up a common point of confusion and helps you get more from your exercise.

## Card: Key terms
- **Cardio**: exercise that works the heart and lungs.
- **Strength training**: exercise that builds muscle and strength.
- **Complementary**: the two types working together for overall fitness.
- **Goals**: matching exercise type to what you want to achieve.

## Card: What each does
image: fitnessCardioVsStrengthWhatEachDoesHero
**Cardio** (cardiovascular exercise — like running, cycling, swimming) raises your heart rate and works your heart and lungs, improving cardiovascular fitness and endurance, and burning energy. **Strength training** (like lifting weights or bodyweight exercises) challenges your muscles, building muscle and strength. They target different systems: cardio mainly improves heart/lung fitness and endurance; strength training mainly builds muscle, strength, and supports metabolism and bone health. Each does something the other doesn't.

## Card: Both have value
Rather than "which is better," the key insight is that cardio and strength training are **complementary** — both offer distinct, valuable benefits, and a balanced fitness approach often includes both. Cardio supports heart health and endurance; strength training builds and maintains muscle and strength (increasingly important with age). Which to emphasize depends on your **goals** (endurance, strength, general health, etc.), but for overall fitness and health, combining both is generally more beneficial than either alone.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking one type is simply "better" (they're complementary, with different benefits); doing only cardio and neglecting strength (or vice versa); not matching exercise type to your goals; and not realizing strength training matters for everyone, not just bodybuilders (it supports muscle, metabolism, and bone health). Cardio and strength training do different things — both have value, and combining them suits overall fitness.

## Card: Quick recap
Cardio (running, cycling, swimming) works the heart and lungs, improving cardiovascular fitness and endurance; strength training (weights, bodyweight) builds muscle and strength, supporting metabolism and bone health. They target different systems and are complementary rather than one being "better." Which to emphasize depends on your goals, but for overall fitness and health, combining both is generally more beneficial than either alone.
