## Card: What it is
image: animalCommunicationHero
**Animal communication** is how animals send and receive information — to find mates, warn of danger, mark territory, coordinate, and more. They use an astonishing range of methods: sounds, body language, scent, color, even electricity and dance. Animals "talk" in ways far beyond what we usually imagine.

## Card: Why it matters
Communication is central to how animals survive and live together, and it reveals surprising intelligence and complexity in the animal world. Understanding it deepens appreciation of wildlife and challenges the idea that language is uniquely human. It's a window into rich animal lives we often overlook.

## Card: Key terms
- **Communication**: sending and receiving information.
- **Signal**: a sound, movement, scent, or display carrying meaning.
- **Vocalization**: communication through sound.
- **Pheromone**: a chemical scent signal.

## Card: Many channels of communication
image: communicationChannelsHero
Animals communicate through many **signals**: **vocalizations** (bird song, whale calls, wolf howls), body language and visual displays (a peacock's tail, a dog's wagging or bared teeth), **pheromones** (chemical scents — how ants leave trails and many animals mark territory), color changes, and even touch. Some methods are exotic: some fish use electric signals, and bees famously "dance" to tell others where food is.

## Card: What animals communicate about
Animals communicate for vital reasons: attracting mates (songs, displays, scents), warning of predators (alarm calls), defending territory (scent marking, displays), coordinating group activity (in social animals), and bonding. Some animals have surprisingly complex communication — different alarm calls for different predators, for instance. While it's not language in the full human sense, animal communication is rich, purposeful, and sometimes remarkably sophisticated.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking animals only communicate by sound (they also use scent, color, body language, dance, and more); assuming communication is simple or random (it's purposeful and sometimes complex); thinking only humans communicate meaningfully; and over-interpreting it as full human-like language. Animals have rich, varied communication systems suited to their needs.

## Card: Quick recap
Animals communicate through many channels — sounds, body language, visual displays, scent (pheromones), color, even electricity and dance — to attract mates, warn of danger, mark territory, and coordinate. It's purposeful and sometimes remarkably complex (like different alarm calls for different predators). Though not full human language, animal communication reveals rich, sophisticated lives across the animal world.
