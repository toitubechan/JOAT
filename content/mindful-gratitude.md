## Card: What it is
image: gratitudeHero
**Gratitude** is the practice of noticing and appreciating the good things in life, and **positivity** is a constructive, hopeful outlook. Both are linked to greater wellbeing — and importantly, gratitude is a *practice* you can cultivate, not just a mood. Understanding them (and their limits) reveals genuinely helpful habits for wellbeing. This is general wellbeing knowledge, not medical advice.

## Card: Why it matters
Gratitude and a positive outlook are associated with greater happiness and wellbeing, and gratitude in particular is a simple, free practice anyone can cultivate. Understanding how (and why) to practice gratitude — while avoiding the trap of forced "toxic positivity" — offers a genuinely useful tool for wellbeing. It's an accessible habit with real benefits, grounded in more than just wishful thinking.

## Card: Key terms
- **Gratitude**: noticing and appreciating the good in life.
- **Gratitude practice**: deliberately cultivating appreciation.
- **Positivity**: a constructive, hopeful outlook.
- **Toxic positivity**: forced positivity that denies real feelings.

## Card: Gratitude as a practice
A key insight: **gratitude** isn't just a feeling that comes and goes — it's a **practice** you can deliberately cultivate. Regularly noticing and appreciating good things (even small ones) tends to shift attention toward the positive and is linked to greater wellbeing. Simple practices — like regularly reflecting on things you're grateful for — can build this habit. Because our minds often dwell on problems and overlook the good, deliberately practicing gratitude counterbalances this, helping you notice and appreciate what's genuinely good in your life.

## Card: Positivity without denial
image: balancedPositivity
**Positivity** — a constructive, hopeful outlook — is linked to wellbeing, *but* with an important caveat: avoid **toxic positivity**. Healthy positivity isn't forcing yourself to feel happy or denying real difficulties and negative emotions. Pretending everything is fine, or suppressing genuine feelings, is unhealthy and ineffective. Genuine positivity means a hopeful, constructive outlook *while still acknowledging and feeling* real emotions and difficulties. The goal is appreciating the good and staying constructive — not denying the bad. Balance, not forced cheerfulness, is the healthy approach.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking gratitude is just a passing mood rather than a cultivable practice; expecting positivity to mean denying or suppressing real difficulties and feelings ("toxic positivity," which is unhealthy); using forced cheerfulness instead of genuine, balanced appreciation; and dismissing gratitude as empty wishful thinking (it's a genuinely beneficial habit). Healthy gratitude and positivity appreciate the good while still acknowledging real feelings and difficulties.

## Card: Quick recap
Gratitude is the practice of noticing and appreciating the good in life — a deliberate, cultivable habit (not just a mood) linked to greater wellbeing, which counterbalances the mind's tendency to dwell on problems. Positivity (a hopeful, constructive outlook) is also beneficial, but must avoid "toxic positivity" — forcing happiness or denying real feelings. Healthy gratitude and positivity appreciate the good while still acknowledging genuine difficulties and emotions.
