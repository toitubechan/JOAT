## Card: What it is
image: nutritionMythsHero
Nutrition is drowning in **myths** — half-truths and marketing claims repeated so often they feel like facts. This card set debunks some of the most common ones, so you can ignore the noise and trust the simple, well-established basics instead.

## Card: Why it matters
Diet myths waste money on useless products, cause needless guilt, and can even harm health through extreme behavior. Being able to spot a myth — usually anything promising fast, dramatic results from one trick — protects your wallet and your wellbeing. Skepticism is a genuine nutrition skill.

## Card: Key terms
- **Myth**: a widely believed but false claim.
- **Fad diet**: a trendy diet promising quick results.
- **Detox**: a marketing term; your liver and kidneys already do this.
- **Superfood**: a marketing label, not a scientific category.

## Card: Common myths busted
- "**Detox** teas/cleanses remove toxins" — your liver and kidneys already do that; these mostly drain your wallet.
- "Carbs (or fat) are inherently bad" — both are needed; quality and amount matter.
- "**Superfoods** are magic" — no single food transforms health; variety does.
- "Eating late directly makes you gain weight" — total intake matters more than timing.

## Card: How to spot a myth
image: mythFilterHero
Warning signs of a nutrition myth: it promises fast, dramatic results; it names a single "miracle" food or product; it demonizes or worships one nutrient; or it's selling you something. Real nutrition advice is usually unexciting — eat varied whole foods, mostly plants, in sensible amounts. Boring, but true.

## Card: Common mistakes
The core mistake is trusting confident claims because they're repeated everywhere, not because they're proven. Another is chasing **fad diets** for quick results, then rebounding. And many spend on "detox" and "superfood" products that do little, when ordinary varied eating would serve them better and cheaper.

## Card: Quick recap
Nutrition myths thrive on quick-fix promises, miracle foods, and product sales. Detoxes are unnecessary, no single superfood is magic, and carbs and fat aren't villains. Spot myths by their drama and sales pitch. The unglamorous truth wins: varied whole foods, mostly plants, in sensible amounts.
