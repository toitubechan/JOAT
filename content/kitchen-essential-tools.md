## Card: What it is
image: kitchenToolsHero
A workable kitchen needs surprisingly few **tools** — a handful of versatile basics handle the vast majority of cooking. Gadget shops sell endless single-use items, but a good knife, a couple of pans, and a few utensils will cook almost anything. Knowing the essentials saves money and clutter.

## Card: Why it matters
Beginners often think they need lots of equipment to start cooking — they don't. A small, well-chosen kit removes that barrier and your money goes toward quality where it counts. Knowing what's truly essential versus what's a gimmick keeps your kitchen functional and your drawers uncluttered.

## Card: Key terms
- **Chef's knife**: a large, versatile all-purpose knife.
- **Cutting board**: a surface for safe chopping.
- **Versatile tool**: one that does many jobs.
- **Single-use gadget**: a tool that does only one narrow task.

## Card: The true essentials
image: starterKitchenKit
A solid starter kit: a good **chef's knife** (does most cutting), a **cutting board**, one or two pans (a frying pan and a saucepan), a pot for boiling, a few wooden or silicone spoons, a spatula, measuring tools, a colander, and mixing bowls. With these, you can cook the great majority of everyday meals.

## Card: Invest where it counts
Spend on the things you use most and that affect results: a decent **chef's knife** and good pans are worth the money, as they last and perform. Save on rarely-used or **single-use gadgets** (avocado slicers, egg separators) — they clutter drawers and a basic tool usually does the same job. Quality basics beat a pile of gimmicks.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: buying lots of single-use gadgets that gather dust; cheaping out on the knife and pans you use daily; thinking you need a fully-equipped kitchen before cooking anything; and accumulating duplicates. Start with the essentials, add specific items only when you find you genuinely need them.

## Card: Quick recap
A functional kitchen needs only a few versatile tools: a good chef's knife, cutting board, a couple of pans and a pot, basic utensils, and bowls. Invest in the knife and pans you use most; skip single-use gadgets. Quality basics cook almost everything and beat a drawer full of clutter.
