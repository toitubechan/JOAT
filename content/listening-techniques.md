## Card: What it is
image: listeningTechniquesHero
Active listening has concrete **techniques** you can practice — ways of responding that show you're listening and deepen your understanding. They're simple, learnable behaviors that turn the intention to listen well into something the speaker can actually feel.

## Card: Why it matters
Wanting to listen isn't enough; specific techniques make it real and visible. These methods also genuinely improve your understanding and help the speaker open up. They're practical tools you can start using in your very next conversation, with immediate effect on how connected the other person feels.

## Card: Key terms
- **Paraphrasing**: restating what you heard in your own words.
- **Open questions**: questions that invite more than yes/no.
- **Reflecting**: acknowledging the speaker's feelings.
- **Minimal encouragers**: small cues like nodding or "go on."

## Card: Show you're listening
Small signals make a big difference. **Minimal encouragers** — nodding, "mm-hm," "go on," eye contact — show you're engaged and invite the person to continue. Crucially, give them space: don't interrupt or rush to fill silences. These simple cues tell the speaker you're present and listening, encouraging them to share more fully.

## Card: Check and deepen understanding
image: deepenUnderstandingHero
Two powerful techniques. **Paraphrasing**: briefly restate what you heard in your own words ("So you're saying...") — it confirms you understood and lets them correct you. **Open questions** ("How did that feel?" "What happened next?") invite deeper sharing, unlike yes/no questions. **Reflecting** feelings ("That sounds frustrating") shows you've grasped not just the facts but the emotion.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: staying silent and giving no signals, so the speaker can't tell you're engaged; interrupting to share your own view; asking closed yes/no questions that shut down conversation; and paraphrasing in a robotic, parrot-like way. Use the techniques naturally and genuinely — they're tools for real understanding, not a performance.

## Card: Quick recap
Active listening techniques make your listening real and visible: use minimal encouragers (nodding, "go on") and give space without interrupting; paraphrase to confirm understanding; ask open questions to deepen it; and reflect feelings to show you grasp the emotion. Use them naturally, and the speaker feels genuinely heard.
