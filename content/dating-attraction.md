## Card: What it is
image: attractionBasicsHero
**Attraction** is what draws people toward each other — a mix of physical, emotional, and psychological factors that's more complex and varied than often assumed. Understanding the basics of attraction demystifies a powerful but often confusing experience, and reveals that lasting connection depends on more than initial spark. This is general educational knowledge, not professional advice.

## Card: Why it matters
Attraction profoundly influences relationships, yet it's widely misunderstood — often reduced to looks or "chemistry." Understanding what really drives attraction (and that initial attraction differs from lasting compatibility) helps you navigate dating and relationships more wisely, with realistic expectations. It clarifies a powerful force that shapes who we connect with and why.

## Card: Key terms
- **Attraction**: what draws people toward each other.
- **Initial attraction**: the early spark or chemistry.
- **Compatibility**: deeper alignment that sustains relationships.
- **Beyond looks**: the many non-physical factors in attraction.

## Card: Attraction is more than looks
A key insight: **attraction** is far more than physical appearance. While looks play a role, attraction also draws on personality, shared interests and values, emotional connection, sense of humor, confidence, kindness, and how someone makes you feel — many factors **beyond looks**. This is why people are drawn to a huge variety of others, and why "chemistry" can be hard to predict. Understanding that attraction is multi-faceted explains why connection often defies simple formulas about appearance.

## Card: Spark versus lasting connection
image: lastingConnectionHero
An important distinction: **initial attraction** (the early spark or chemistry) is different from **compatibility** (the deeper alignment that sustains a lasting relationship). A strong initial spark doesn't guarantee a good long-term relationship, and great lasting relationships can grow from modest initial attraction. Compatibility — shared values, good communication, mutual respect, and genuine connection — is what sustains relationships over time. Understanding this helps you not overvalue initial chemistry or undervalue qualities that matter for the long term.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: reducing attraction to physical looks (it's multi-faceted — personality, values, connection, and more); assuming strong initial chemistry guarantees a good relationship (initial spark differs from lasting compatibility); undervaluing the qualities that sustain relationships; and treating attraction as a simple formula. Attraction is complex and varied, and lasting connection depends on compatibility, not just the initial spark.

## Card: Quick recap
Attraction — what draws people together — is far more than looks; it draws on personality, values, emotional connection, humor, kindness, and how someone makes you feel. Importantly, initial attraction (the early spark) differs from compatibility (the deeper alignment that sustains lasting relationships): strong chemistry doesn't guarantee a good relationship, and lasting bonds can grow from modest initial attraction. Understanding this supports wiser, more realistic dating.
