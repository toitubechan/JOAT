## Card: What it is
image: carMaintenanceHero
**Car maintenance** is the regular care that keeps a vehicle running safely and reliably — checking fluids and tires, following the service schedule, and noticing problems early. Most basic checks need no tools and a few minutes. It's the routine that stops small issues becoming roadside disasters.

## Card: Why it matters
A car is a big investment and a safety-critical machine. Neglect leads to breakdowns, expensive repairs, and real danger on the road. Simple, regular attention extends a car's life, keeps it safe, holds its value, and saves serious money. Prevention is dramatically cheaper than repair.

## Card: Key terms
- **Service**: a scheduled professional check and tune-up.
- **Fluids**: liquids like oil and coolant the car needs to run.
- **Tread**: the grooved pattern on a tire that grips the road.
- **Owner's manual**: the book explaining your specific car's needs.

## Card: The owner's manual is gold
image: ownerManualGold
Every car is different, and the **owner's manual** tells you exactly what *yours* needs and when — service intervals, correct tire pressure, fluid types. It's the single most useful and most ignored resource a driver has. Reading the relevant sections answers most maintenance questions specific to your vehicle.

## Card: What you can check yourself
Plenty is beginner-friendly: checking oil and coolant levels, tire pressure and tread, lights, and washer fluid. These quick checks catch the most common problems early. You don't need to be a mechanic — just to look regularly and know what "normal" looks like so you notice when it isn't.

## Card: Common mistakes
The biggest mistake is ignoring small signs — a warning light, an odd noise, a slow leak — until they become a breakdown or a costly repair. Another is skipping scheduled **services** to save money, which usually costs far more later. And many never read the **owner's manual**, missing simple guidance built for their exact car.

## Card: Quick recap
Car maintenance is routine care that keeps you safe and saves money: check fluids, tires, and lights regularly, follow the service schedule, and act on warning signs early. The owner's manual tells you what your specific car needs. A little attention prevents most breakdowns and big bills.
