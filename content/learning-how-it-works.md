## Card: What it is
image: growthLearningHowItWorksHero
**Learning** is the process of building and strengthening connections in your brain so new knowledge or skills become available to you. It's not about cramming facts into a passive container — it's an active process of forming and reinforcing mental links. Understanding this changes how you study.

## Card: Why it matters
Most people were never taught *how* to learn, so they use ineffective methods that feel productive but don't stick. Understanding how learning really works lets you study smarter, not just harder — learning faster, remembering longer, and wasting less time on techniques that don't work.

## Card: Key terms
- **Learning**: forming and strengthening brain connections.
- **Memory**: stored information you can retrieve.
- **Encoding**: getting information into memory in the first place.
- **Retrieval**: pulling information back out when needed.

## Card: Building connections
image: growthLearningHowItWorksBuildingConnectionsHero
Learning works by forming and strengthening neural connections through **encoding** (getting information in) and **retrieval** (pulling it back out). The more you actively retrieve and use knowledge, the stronger those connections become. This is why effortful recall builds memory far better than passively rereading, which barely strengthens the links.

## Card: Effort makes it stick
A surprising principle: learning that *feels* hard often works better. When your brain struggles a little to recall or apply something, it strengthens the memory more than easy, passive review. This "desirable difficulty" is why challenging yourself — testing, practicing, struggling productively — beats comfortable, effortless studying that feels good but fades fast.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: treating learning as passively absorbing information (rereading, highlighting), which feels productive but barely sticks; avoiding the effortful methods that actually work because they feel harder; and confusing *familiarity* (recognizing material) with *knowing* it (being able to recall and use it). Effort, not ease, builds real learning.

## Card: Quick recap
Learning is actively building and strengthening brain connections, through encoding and especially retrieval — not passively absorbing facts. Effort that feels hard, like recalling and practicing, builds stronger memory than easy rereading. Don't mistake familiarity for knowing. Study smarter by embracing productive difficulty.
