## Card: What it is
image: psychMotivationWhyItFluctuatesHero
Motivation naturally **fluctuates** — it rises and falls with mood, energy, circumstances, and time. This is completely normal, yet many people treat dips in motivation as personal failure. Understanding *why* motivation varies helps you ride out the lows and not depend on always feeling driven.

## Card: Why it matters
Almost everyone experiences motivation slumps, and misunderstanding them causes guilt and quitting. Knowing that fluctuation is normal — and what influences it — lets you plan around the dips rather than being derailed by them. It's the key to persistence: those who succeed keep going *despite* low-motivation periods, not because they never have them.

## Card: Key terms
- **Fluctuation**: the natural rising and falling of motivation.
- **Energy and mood**: factors that strongly affect drive.
- **Novelty**: the initial excitement that fades over time.
- **The slump**: a predictable dip in motivation.

## Card: Motivation is naturally variable
image: psychMotivationWhyItFluctuatesMotivationIsNaturallyVariableHero
Motivation isn't constant — it's affected by **energy and mood** (tired or stressed, you'll feel less driven), by **novelty** (the exciting start of something fades as it becomes routine), and by progress and setbacks. So the early enthusiasm for a new goal naturally cools — this isn't failure, it's normal. Expecting steady, high motivation sets you up for disappointment.

## Card: Plan for the dips
Since dips are inevitable, the answer is to *plan* for them rather than rely on motivation being there. This is where **habits and systems** shine — they carry you through low-motivation periods automatically. Lowering the bar on hard days ("just do a little"), maintaining energy through sleep and rest, and remembering your "why" all help you ride out **the slump** without quitting.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: treating a motivation dip as a personal failing (it's normal); expecting the initial excitement to last forever; relying solely on motivation instead of habits that survive the lows; and quitting during a slump rather than easing through it. Fluctuation is expected — successful people keep going during the dips, not just the highs.

## Card: Quick recap
Motivation naturally fluctuates with energy, mood, novelty, and circumstances — dips are normal, not failure. The early excitement of a new goal inevitably cools. Since lows are inevitable, rely on habits and systems to carry you through them, lower the bar on hard days, and remember your "why." Persistence comes from working through the dips, not avoiding them.
