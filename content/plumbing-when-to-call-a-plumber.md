## Card: What it is
image: callPlumberHero
Plenty of plumbing is DIY-friendly, but some jobs need a professional — for safety, skill, or legal reasons. Knowing which is which protects you from turning a small problem into an expensive disaster. The smartest plumbing skill is often recognizing when to stop and pick up the phone.

## Card: Why it matters
Plumbing mistakes can be costly and fast: a wrong move can flood a room or damage the structure. Hidden pipes, hot water systems, and sewer lines are high-stakes. Calling a pro at the right moment usually costs far less than fixing the damage from a DIY attempt gone wrong.

## Card: Key terms
- **Licensed plumber**: a qualified professional, often legally required.
- **Mains pressure**: the high pressure in supply pipes.
- **Sewer line**: the main pipe carrying waste away from the home.
- **Water damage**: harm caused by leaks, often expensive to repair.

## Card: Handle these yourself
Beginner-safe jobs include unclogging drains, replacing a tap washer, fixing a running toilet, sealing gaps with caulk, and swapping a shower head. These are low-pressure, low-risk, and easy to reverse. If the worst outcome is a bit of mess and a redo, it's usually fine to try.

## Card: Call a pro for these
image: minorMajorBoundary
Get a **licensed plumber** for burst or leaking pipes inside walls, anything involving the hot water system or boiler, low pressure across the whole house, sewage backups, or any work on **mains pressure** pipes. These carry real risk of flooding, scalding, or major damage — and are often legally restricted.

## Card: Common mistakes
The classic mistake is pressing on with a job that's clearly escalating, hoping to avoid a call-out fee, while the damage grows. Another is not shutting off the water before any attempt, risking a flood. And some hire unqualified help to save money on jobs that legally need a licensed plumber.

## Card: Quick recap
DIY the low-risk plumbing — drains, washers, toilets, sealing. Call a licensed plumber for hidden leaks, hot water systems, whole-house pressure issues, sewage, and mains work. Always shut off the water before attempting anything. Knowing when to stop is what prevents a small fix becoming a flood.
