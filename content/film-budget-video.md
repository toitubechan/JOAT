## Card: What it is
image: filmBudgetVideoHero
**Making videos on a budget** is creating good video without expensive equipment — increasingly possible as cameras (even in phones) have become capable and affordable. The key insight: great video comes more from *skills and choices* than from expensive gear. Understanding this empowers anyone to start making video now, with what they already have.

## Card: Why it matters
Many people assume you need costly professional equipment to make good video, and never start. In reality, capable cameras are now in everyone's pockets, and what matters most is technique, not gear. Understanding this removes the biggest barrier and reveals that quality video is achievable for anyone. It's empowering and practical in an age where video skills are increasingly valuable.

## Card: Key terms
- **Budget filmmaking**: making good video with limited money.
- **Skills over gear**: technique mattering more than equipment.
- **Lighting and sound**: cheap-to-improve, high-impact basics.
- **Accessible tools**: affordable cameras and free editing software.

## Card: Skills matter more than gear
image: filmBudgetVideoSkillsMatterMoreThanGearHero
The crucial principle: **skills over gear**. The most expensive camera won't make a poorly shot, badly lit, poorly told video good — while a skilled person can make excellent video with modest equipment (even a modern phone). Composition, lighting, sound, storytelling, and editing — all *skills* — matter far more than the price of your camera. So the path to better video isn't buying expensive gear; it's developing technique. This is liberating: you can start and improve with what you have.

## Card: Where to focus on a budget
With limited money, focus on the high-impact basics. **Lighting** — even cheap or natural light, used well, dramatically improves footage (good lighting is often what makes video look professional). **Sound** — clear audio matters more than people expect, and is cheaply improved (poor sound ruins video faster than modest visuals). Use **accessible tools** — a capable phone camera and free or cheap editing software go a long way. Prioritize learning composition, lighting, sound, and editing over buying gear.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: believing you need expensive gear to make good video (skills matter far more); ignoring lighting and sound (cheap to improve, huge impact) while obsessing over the camera; not realizing modern phones and free software are highly capable; and never starting because of perceived equipment barriers. Good video comes from skills and choices — lighting, sound, composition, storytelling — more than from costly equipment.

## Card: Quick recap
Making videos on a budget is very achievable: great video comes more from skills and choices than expensive gear. A skilled person can make excellent video with a modern phone, while costly equipment won't fix poor technique. On a budget, focus on the high-impact basics — lighting (even natural light, used well) and sound (clear audio matters hugely and is cheap to improve) — plus accessible tools like phone cameras and free editing software.
