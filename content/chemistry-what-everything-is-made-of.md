## Card: What it is
image: atomsHero
Everything around you — air, water, your body, this device — is made of **atoms**, incredibly tiny building blocks. Chemistry is the science of these atoms: what they are, how they combine, and how they change. Understanding atoms is the foundation for understanding all of matter.

## Card: Why it matters
Atoms make up literally everything, so understanding them is understanding the physical world at its most basic. It demystifies materials, reactions, and why substances behave as they do. This is foundational knowledge that makes the rest of chemistry — and much of science — click into place.

## Card: Key terms
- **Atom**: the tiny basic building block of matter.
- **Element**: a substance made of one type of atom.
- **Proton, neutron, electron**: the particles inside an atom.
- **Nucleus**: the dense center of an atom.

## Card: Atoms are the building blocks
**Atoms** are unimaginably small — millions would fit across a hair's width. Everything is built from them, like letters building all words. There are only about a hundred basic *types* of atom, yet they combine into the endless variety of substances in the universe. Grasping that all matter is just atoms arranged differently is a profound, simplifying idea.

## Card: What's inside an atom
image: insideAtom
An atom has a tiny dense **nucleus** containing **protons** (positive charge) and **neutrons** (no charge), surrounded by **electrons** (negative charge) zipping around. The number of protons defines what *type* of atom it is — what **element**. Most of an atom is actually empty space, with the nucleus a tiny speck at the center.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking atoms are solid little balls (they're mostly empty space); imagining there are countless types (only about a hundred basic ones); and not realizing that the *same* atoms rearranged make hugely different substances. Atoms are few in type but infinite in combination.

## Card: Quick recap
Everything is made of atoms — tiny building blocks, with only about a hundred basic types combining into all of matter. Each atom has a dense nucleus of protons and neutrons, surrounded by electrons, and is mostly empty space. The number of protons defines the element. All matter is just atoms arranged differently.
