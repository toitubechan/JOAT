## Card: What it is
image: galaxiesUniverseHero
Beyond our solar system lies the vast **universe** — billions of **galaxies**, each containing billions of stars. Our Sun is one star in one galaxy (the **Milky Way**), which is itself one of countless galaxies. Grasping this scale is humbling and awe-inspiring: it places everything we know in cosmic perspective.

## Card: Why it matters
Understanding the universe's scale reshapes your sense of place — Earth is a tiny speck in an almost unimaginably vast cosmos. It satisfies profound curiosity and gives context to all of astronomy. It's also genuinely awe-inspiring, one of those ideas that can shift how you see existence itself.

## Card: Key terms
- **Galaxy**: a vast collection of stars bound by gravity.
- **Milky Way**: our home galaxy.
- **Universe**: all of space and everything in it.
- **Big Bang**: the leading explanation for the universe's origin.

## Card: Galaxies upon galaxies
image: galaxyScaleHero
A **galaxy** is a colossal collection of stars (often hundreds of billions) held together by gravity. Our Sun and solar system sit in the **Milky Way**, our home galaxy. And the Milky Way is just *one* galaxy — the observable **universe** contains an estimated *hundreds of billions* of galaxies. Stars within galaxies, galaxies within the universe: the nesting of scale is staggering.

## Card: Origins and expansion
The leading scientific explanation for the universe's origin is the **Big Bang** — the idea that the universe began around 13.8 billion years ago from an incredibly hot, dense state and has been expanding ever since. It's still expanding today. This is the broad scientific picture, supported by extensive evidence, of where everything we see came from.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking our galaxy is the whole universe (it's one of billions); imagining the Big Bang as an explosion *in* space rather than the expansion *of* space itself; underestimating the scale (it's beyond intuitive grasp); and confusing the solar system, galaxy, and universe — which nest inside each other at vastly different scales.

## Card: Quick recap
The universe contains billions of galaxies, each with billions of stars. Our Sun is one star in the Milky Way, itself one galaxy among countless others. The leading explanation for its origin is the Big Bang, around 13.8 billion years ago, with the universe expanding ever since. The nested scale is humbling and awe-inspiring.
