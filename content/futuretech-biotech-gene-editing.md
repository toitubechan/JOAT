## Card: What it is
image: futuretechBiotechHero
**Biotechnology** uses living things and biology to make products and solve problems — from medicines to crops. **Gene editing** is a powerful new branch that lets scientists precisely change the DNA of living things. Together, they're transforming medicine, agriculture, and more, while raising profound ethical questions. (General educational overview.)

## Card: Why it matters
Biotech and gene editing could cure diseases, improve food, and reshape life itself — among the most powerful and consequential technologies emerging today. Understanding the basics helps you follow major developments, grasp both the promise and the serious ethical concerns, and engage thoughtfully with debates that will shape the future. It's science with profound stakes.

## Card: Key terms
- **Biotechnology**: using biology to make products or solve problems.
- **Gene editing**: precisely changing an organism's DNA.
- **DNA**: the genetic code of living things.
- **Ethics**: the moral questions a technology raises.

## Card: What biotech and gene editing do
image: futuretechBiotechWhatBiotechAndGeneEditingHero
**Biotechnology** has long used living systems — making medicines (like insulin), fermenting foods, and developing crops. The newer leap is **gene editing**: tools that let scientists precisely edit **DNA** — adding, removing, or changing genetic instructions. This is far more precise than older methods. It opens possibilities like correcting genetic diseases, developing better crops, and creating new treatments — directly altering the instructions of life.

## Card: Promise and serious concerns
The promise is huge: potentially curing inherited diseases, improving food security, and advancing medicine. But gene editing raises profound **ethical** concerns, especially when applied to humans: questions about safety, unintended effects, fairness and access, and especially editing genes that pass to future generations (changes that would affect descendants). Where to draw lines is a serious, ongoing global debate. The power to reprogram life demands careful ethical thought.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking gene editing means "designer babies" are routine (human gene editing, especially heritable, is tightly restricted and controversial); seeing biotech as only futuristic (it already makes medicines and foods); ignoring the serious ethical questions; and treating it as either pure miracle or pure menace. It's a powerful technology with immense promise and genuine ethical stakes.

## Card: Quick recap
Biotechnology uses living systems to make products (medicines, foods, crops); gene editing is a powerful new tool to precisely change DNA — adding, removing, or altering genetic instructions. It promises cures for genetic diseases and better food, but raises serious ethical concerns, especially editing human genes passed to future generations. It's a profoundly powerful technology demanding careful ethical thought.
