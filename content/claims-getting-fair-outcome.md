## Card: What it is
image: fairOutcomeHero
Getting a **fair claim outcome** means receiving the full amount you're genuinely entitled to — no more (which is fraud), no less (which many people accept). It comes down to good preparation, documentation, and knowing your policy. This card set ties together how to claim fairly and successfully. (General guidance.)

## Card: Why it matters
Many people unknowingly accept less than their fair payout, while a few wrongly try to inflate claims. Aiming for *fair* — getting exactly what you're owed — protects your finances and stays on the right side of the law. Knowing how to achieve a fair outcome is the practical goal of understanding claims.

## Card: Key terms
- **Fair claim**: getting exactly what you're genuinely owed.
- **Documentation**: thorough records supporting your claim.
- **Knowing your policy**: understanding your actual cover.
- **Fraud**: inflating or faking a claim, which is illegal.

## Card: Preparation before disaster strikes
image: preparedRecords
The biggest factor in a fair outcome happens *before* any loss: **knowing your policy** (what's covered, your limits and deductible) and keeping good records. For valuables, keep receipts, photos, and serial numbers; for your home, document its contents. This **documentation**, prepared in advance, is what lets you prove a claim fully later. Most underpaid claims stem from not being able to prove the full loss.

## Card: Claim fully but honestly
Aim for a **fair claim**: include *everything* you're genuinely entitled to (people often forget legitimate parts of a loss and under-claim), while never exaggerating or inventing (that's **fraud**, which voids claims and is illegal). Provide thorough evidence, understand what your policy actually covers so you don't accept less than your due, and be persistent if a fair payout is wrongly reduced. Fair means complete *and* honest.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: under-claiming by forgetting legitimate parts of a loss; not having documentation to prove the full value (leading to a reduced payout); accepting an unfairly low offer without question; and at the other extreme, exaggerating (fraud). Prepare records in advance, know your policy, claim everything you're genuinely owed, and stay honest.

## Card: Quick recap
A fair claim means getting exactly what you're owed — complete and honest. Prepare before any loss by knowing your policy and keeping records (receipts, photos, serial numbers), since proving the full loss is key. Claim everything you're genuinely entitled to (people often under-claim), never exaggerate (fraud), and push back on unfairly low offers. Fair is the goal.
