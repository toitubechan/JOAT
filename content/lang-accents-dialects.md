## Card: What it is
image: langAccentsDialectsHero
**Accents** are differences in how people *pronounce* a language, while **dialects** are broader variations including different words, phrases, and sometimes grammar. The same language can sound and even feel quite different across regions and groups. Understanding accents and dialects reveals the natural, fascinating diversity within any single language — and why "correct" speech is more complicated than it seems.

## Card: Why it matters
Accents and dialects shape how people speak and how they're perceived — sometimes leading to unfair judgments. Understanding them fosters appreciation of linguistic diversity, challenges the idea that one way of speaking is "correct," and reduces prejudice. It reveals language as living, varied, and tied to identity and culture, not a single fixed standard.

## Card: Key terms
- **Accent**: variation in pronunciation.
- **Dialect**: broader variation in words, phrases, and grammar.
- **Standard language**: a commonly accepted "official" variety.
- **Linguistic diversity**: the natural variation within a language.

## Card: Accents vs. dialects
image: langAccentsDialectsAccentsVsDialectsHero
The distinction: an **accent** is purely about *pronunciation* — how words sound (everyone has an accent, even if they think they don't). A **dialect** is broader — it includes accent *plus* differences in vocabulary (different words for the same thing), phrases, and sometimes grammar. So two people speaking the "same" language might pronounce it differently (accents) and even use somewhat different words and expressions (dialects), while still understanding each other.

## Card: No single "correct" way
A key insight: there's no single linguistically "correct" way to speak a language. The **standard** version (the one often taught or used officially) is just one variety that gained prestige, not inherently "better" than dialects, which follow their own consistent rules. Accents and dialects are natural, valid **linguistic diversity** tied to region, culture, and identity. Judging people as less intelligent or "wrong" for their accent or dialect is a prejudice, not a linguistic fact.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking some people "have no accent" (everyone has one); believing the standard version is inherently "correct" and dialects are "wrong" or "lazy" (dialects follow their own valid rules); confusing accent (pronunciation) with dialect (broader differences); and judging people by how they speak. Accents and dialects are natural, valid diversity within a language, tied to identity and culture.

## Card: Quick recap
Accents are differences in pronunciation; dialects are broader, including different words, phrases, and sometimes grammar. The same language varies naturally across regions and groups. There's no single "correct" way to speak — the standard version is just one prestigious variety, and dialects follow their own valid rules. Accents and dialects are natural linguistic diversity tied to identity, not signs of error or intelligence.
