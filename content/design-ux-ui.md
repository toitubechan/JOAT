## Card: What it is
image: uxUiHero
**UX** (user experience) and **UI** (user interface) design are about creating digital products — apps, websites, software — that are easy, pleasant, and effective to use. **UX** is the overall experience of using something; **UI** is the visual interface you interact with. Understanding the difference and the basics reveals the craft behind the apps and sites you use daily.

## Card: Why it matters
We use digital products constantly, and good UX/UI is why some feel effortless and delightful while others are frustrating. It's a major, in-demand design field. Understanding it helps you appreciate why some products work so well, create better digital experiences yourself, and grasp a craft central to modern technology. It reveals the intentional design behind everyday digital tools.

## Card: Key terms
- **UX (user experience)**: the overall experience of using a product.
- **UI (user interface)**: the visual elements you interact with.
- **Usability**: how easy and effective something is to use.
- **User-centered**: designing around the user's needs.

## Card: UX vs. UI
The distinction: **UX** (user experience) is the *overall experience* of using a product — is it easy, logical, efficient, satisfying? It's about how the whole thing *works* and feels to use. **UI** (user interface) is the *visual interface* — the buttons, screens, layout, and visual elements you actually interact with. Roughly: UX is how it *works and feels*; UI is how it *looks*. Both matter and work together — a beautiful UI with confusing UX still frustrates, and vice versa.

## Card: Designing for the user
image: userCenteredDesignHero
The heart of UX/UI is being **user-centered** — designing around the *user's* needs, not the designer's preferences. The goal is **usability**: making the product easy, intuitive, and effective to use. Good UX/UI designers understand how real users think and behave, then design so using the product feels effortless and obvious. When digital products are frustrating (confusing, hard to find things, awkward), it's usually a UX/UI failure to put the user first. Great design makes the experience feel seamless.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: confusing UX (overall experience/how it works) with UI (visual interface/how it looks); thinking good looks (UI) are enough (usability/UX matters just as much); designing around the designer's preferences rather than real users' needs; and not realizing frustrating products are usually UX/UI failures. Good UX/UI is user-centered, prioritizing usability so digital products feel easy and effortless to use.

## Card: Quick recap
UX (user experience) and UI (user interface) design create digital products that are easy and pleasant to use. UX is the overall experience — how it works and feels; UI is the visual interface — how it looks. Both matter and work together. The heart of it is being user-centered, prioritizing usability so products feel effortless. Frustrating digital products are usually UX/UI failures to design around real users' needs.
