## Card: What it is
image: compositionLayoutHero
**Composition and layout** are about how visual elements are arranged within a space — where things go and how they relate. Good arrangement makes a design clear, balanced, and pleasing; poor arrangement makes it cluttered or confusing. Understanding composition helps you organize any visual work — designs, documents, photos, presentations — so it looks intentional and works well.

## Card: Why it matters
How elements are arranged determines whether a design feels professional and clear or messy and amateurish — often more than the individual elements themselves. Understanding composition and layout helps you arrange anything visual effectively, guiding the viewer and creating a clean, intentional result. It's a fundamental skill across all visual creativity, and it's learnable.

## Card: Key terms
- **Composition**: how elements are arranged in a space.
- **Layout**: the structured arrangement of content.
- **White space**: empty space that gives designs room to breathe.
- **Focal point**: the element the eye is drawn to first.

## Card: Arranging with intention
Good **composition** arranges elements with intention, guiding the viewer's eye. Key ideas: create a clear **focal point** (where the eye goes first — the most important element); use **white space** (empty space) generously, since cluttered designs overwhelm while space gives clarity and elegance; and group related elements together. A strong **layout** structures content logically so viewers can navigate it easily. The goal is a clear, balanced, intentional arrangement — not cramming everything in.

## Card: The power of white space
image: whiteSpaceHero
A counterintuitive but crucial principle: **white space** (empty space) is your friend. Beginners often fear empty space and fill it, creating cluttered, overwhelming designs. But space gives a design room to breathe, makes it feel clean and elegant, and helps the important elements stand out. "Less is more" often applies — removing and spacing out elements frequently improves a design more than adding to it. Embracing white space is one of the biggest improvements in composition.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: cramming too much in and fearing empty space (clutter overwhelms; white space helps); no clear focal point (the eye doesn't know where to go); poor grouping and alignment (looking messy); and adding more when removing would help. Good composition uses intentional arrangement, a clear focal point, and generous white space — often "less is more."

## Card: Quick recap
Composition and layout are how visual elements are arranged in a space — and good arrangement (clear, balanced, intentional) often matters more than the elements themselves. Key ideas: create a clear focal point, group related elements, structure content logically, and embrace white space (empty space gives clarity and elegance). Beginners often over-fill; "less is more" frequently applies. Intentional arrangement is a fundamental, learnable visual skill.
