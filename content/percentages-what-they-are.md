## Card: What it is
image: dataPercentagesWhatTheyAreHero
A **percentage** is just a way of expressing a number as a part of 100. "Percent" literally means "per hundred." So 25% means 25 out of every 100, or a quarter. Once you grasp this simple idea, percentages — which appear everywhere in daily life — become far less intimidating.

## Card: Why it matters
Percentages are everywhere: discounts, interest, statistics, tips, taxes, news. Many people feel uneasy with them, leading to confusion or being misled. Understanding what a percentage actually *is* removes that anxiety and is the foundation for using them confidently in everyday situations — from shopping to understanding the news.

## Card: Key terms
- **Percent (%)**: a part out of 100.
- **Percentage**: a number expressed as parts per hundred.
- **Fraction**: a part of a whole (like 1/4).
- **Equivalent forms**: the same value as a percent, fraction, or decimal.

## Card: Per hundred
image: dataPercentagesWhatTheyArePerHundredHero
The key insight: **percent means "per hundred."** So 50% is 50 per hundred (half), 25% is 25 per hundred (a quarter), and 100% is the whole thing. Picturing percentages as "out of 100" makes them concrete. If you imagine 100 people or 100 cents, a percentage simply tells you how many of those 100 you're talking about.

## Card: Percentages, fractions, and decimals
Percentages, **fractions**, and decimals are just different ways of writing the *same* value (**equivalent forms**). 50% = 1/2 = 0.5; 25% = 1/4 = 0.25; 10% = 1/10 = 0.1. Seeing these connections makes percentages easier — sometimes a fraction is simpler to picture (a half, a quarter), and you can switch between forms whenever one is more convenient.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: treating percentages as mysterious rather than just "out of 100"; not realizing percentages, fractions, and decimals are the same thing in different forms; thinking percentages always require complex math (often a simple fraction works); and being intimidated rather than picturing the simple "per hundred" idea. Percentages are simpler than they seem.

## Card: Quick recap
A percentage is just a part out of 100 — "percent" means "per hundred." So 25% is 25 out of 100, or a quarter. Percentages, fractions, and decimals are equivalent forms of the same value (50% = 1/2 = 0.5). Picturing "out of 100" makes percentages concrete and removes the intimidation. They're simpler than they appear.
