## Card: What it is
image: psychEmotionsWhatTheyAreHero
**Emotions** are complex responses involving feelings, physical sensations, and urges to act — like fear, joy, anger, or sadness. They're not random or irrational; they're signals from your mind and body about what's happening and what matters to you. Understanding what emotions *are* is the foundation for handling them well.

## Card: Why it matters
Emotions influence nearly everything — decisions, relationships, health, and wellbeing. Many people misunderstand them as either to be suppressed or simply obeyed. Understanding emotions as informative signals, neither to ignore nor blindly follow, is key to managing them and living well. It's central self-knowledge.

## Card: Key terms
- **Emotion**: a response with feelings, body sensations, and action urges.
- **Trigger**: an event or thought that sparks an emotion.
- **Physical sensation**: the bodily side of an emotion.
- **Signal**: information an emotion conveys.

## Card: More than just feelings
image: psychEmotionsWhatTheyAreMoreThanJustFeelingsHero
An **emotion** isn't only a feeling in your head — it's a whole-body response. It typically has three parts: a *feeling* (the subjective experience), a *physical sensation* (racing heart with fear, warmth with joy), and an *urge to act* (to flee, approach, lash out). A **trigger** — an event or even a thought — sets it off. Recognizing these parts helps you understand and work with emotions.

## Card: Emotions as signals
A powerful reframe: emotions are **signals** carrying information, not commands to obey or noise to silence. Fear signals possible danger; anger signals a boundary crossed; sadness signals loss; joy signals something good. They point to what matters to you. The skill isn't to suppress or blindly follow them, but to *listen* to what they're telling you, then choose how to respond.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: seeing emotions as irrational and to be suppressed (they carry useful information); the opposite, treating every emotion as a command to obey instantly; thinking "negative" emotions are bad (they're informative too); and not recognizing the physical and action parts of emotion. Emotions are informative signals to understand, not enemies or absolute orders.

## Card: Quick recap
Emotions are whole-body responses with three parts — a feeling, a physical sensation, and an urge to act — triggered by events or thoughts. They're signals carrying information about what matters to you (fear flags danger, anger a crossed boundary), not commands to obey or noise to suppress. The skill is to listen, then choose how to respond.
