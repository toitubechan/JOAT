## Card: What it is
image: conflictBasicsHero
**Conflict** is a disagreement or clash of needs, ideas, or interests between people. It's a normal, unavoidable part of relationships and work — not a sign something is broken. **Conflict resolution** is the skill of handling these clashes constructively, so they're resolved rather than left to fester or explode.

## Card: Why it matters
Most people either avoid conflict (letting problems grow) or handle it badly (damaging relationships). Both make things worse. Handling conflict well preserves relationships, solves real problems, and can even strengthen trust. Since conflict is inevitable, learning to navigate it is one of the most valuable people skills there is.

## Card: Key terms
- **Conflict**: a clash of needs, ideas, or interests.
- **Healthy conflict**: disagreement handled constructively.
- **Avoidance**: dodging conflict so problems fester.
- **Escalation**: conflict intensifying and getting worse.

## Card: Conflict isn't inherently bad
image: healthyConflictHero
A key reframe: conflict itself isn't the problem — how it's *handled* is. **Healthy conflict** can surface problems, improve decisions, and clear the air. Disagreement is natural when people care. The goal isn't to eliminate conflict (impossible and undesirable) but to handle it constructively, so it leads to resolution rather than damage.

## Card: The two bad defaults
People tend toward two unhelpful extremes. **Avoidance** — dodging the conflict — feels safer but lets problems fester and resentment build. **Escalation** — getting aggressive or defensive — damages relationships and rarely resolves the real issue. Good conflict resolution is the middle path: addressing issues directly but calmly and respectfully.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: avoiding all conflict, so problems grow unaddressed; treating disagreement as a personal attack; escalating into aggression or defensiveness; and believing healthy relationships have no conflict (they have plenty — handled well). The shift is to see conflict as a normal problem to solve together, not a battle to win or flee.

## Card: Quick recap
Conflict is a normal clash of needs or ideas, not a sign something's broken — and handled well, it can even strengthen relationships. The problem is how we handle it: avoidance lets issues fester, escalation damages relationships. Aim for the middle path: address issues directly but calmly and respectfully.
