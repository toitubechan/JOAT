## Card: What it is
image: bodyddReproductionBasicsHero
**Reproduction** is how new human life begins — the biological process by which two parents' genetic material combines to create a new individual. This is a general, educational overview of the basic biology. (For health, medical, or personal questions, a doctor or qualified professional is the right source.)

## Card: Why it matters
Reproduction is fundamental biology that explains where each of us comes from and how genetic information passes to the next generation. A basic understanding supports general knowledge of the body, health, and life. This is foundational science, presented simply and respectfully as general education.

## Card: Key terms
- **Reproduction**: the biological process of creating new life.
- **Sex cells**: the special cells (egg and sperm) that combine.
- **Fertilization**: the joining of egg and sperm.
- **Inheritance**: passing genetic material to offspring.

## Card: How new life begins
image: fertilizationOverviewHero
Human reproduction involves two special **sex cells**: an egg (from the mother) and a sperm (from the father). Each carries half the genetic instructions. **Fertilization** is when a sperm and egg join, combining their genetic material into a single new cell with a complete set of DNA. That cell then divides and develops, over about nine months in the womb, into a baby. This is the basic biological beginning of every person.

## Card: Why offspring are unique
Because each parent contributes half their genetic material, and that material mixes and shuffles, every child is a *unique combination* — sharing traits with both parents but identical to neither (except identical twins, who come from one fertilized cell splitting). This **inheritance** of mixed genetic material is why family members resemble each other yet are all distinct individuals.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking a child is a copy of one parent (offspring are a unique mix of both); not understanding that each sex cell carries only half the genetic instructions; confusing reproduction biology with the many social and personal dimensions (this card covers the basic biology only); and assuming all twins are identical (most aren't). Reproduction combines two parents' genes into a unique new individual.

## Card: Quick recap
Human reproduction begins when two sex cells — an egg and a sperm, each carrying half the genetic instructions — join in fertilization, forming a new cell with complete DNA that develops into a baby. Because each parent contributes half, mixed and shuffled, every child is a unique combination, resembling both parents yet identical to neither. It's the biological beginning of every person.
