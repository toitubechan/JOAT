## Card: What it is
image: medicinePandemicsHero
A **pandemic** is an outbreak of disease that spreads across many countries or worldwide. Understanding how diseases spread — and how outbreaks grow — explains how a local illness can become a global event, and why public health measures matter. (General educational overview.)

## Card: Why it matters
Pandemics affect everyone, as recent history has shown. Understanding how diseases spread helps you grasp public health news, why certain measures are used, and how outbreaks are controlled. It cuts through fear and misinformation with a basic understanding of the science behind contagion and prevention.

## Card: Key terms
- **Pandemic**: a disease outbreak spanning many countries.
- **Contagious**: able to spread from person to person.
- **Exponential growth**: rapid spread where each case causes more.
- **Public health measures**: actions to slow or stop spread.

## Card: How diseases spread
image: medicinePandemicsHowDiseasesSpreadHero
**Contagious** diseases spread when a germ passes from one person (or source) to others — through the air, droplets, touch, contaminated surfaces, food and water, or animals, depending on the disease. How easily a disease spreads, how deadly it is, and how it transmits all shape an outbreak. A disease that spreads easily *and* before people show symptoms is especially hard to contain.

## Card: Why outbreaks can explode
The key to pandemics is **exponential growth**: if each infected person infects more than one other, cases multiply rapidly — one becomes a few, a few become many, and the numbers can explode surprisingly fast. This is why early action matters so much, and why **public health measures** (like hygiene, isolation when sick, and vaccination) aim to reduce how many people each case infects, slowing or stopping the spread.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: underestimating exponential growth (small numbers can explode fast); thinking all diseases spread the same way (transmission varies); not understanding why early measures matter (slowing spread early is far easier than later); and falling for misinformation during outbreaks. Understanding the basic science of spread helps you respond calmly and sensibly.

## Card: Quick recap
A pandemic is a disease outbreak across many countries. Diseases spread through air, droplets, touch, surfaces, or other routes, and outbreaks can explode through exponential growth — when each case infects more than one other. This is why early public health measures (hygiene, isolation, vaccination) matter: they reduce how many each case infects, slowing the spread. Understanding the science cuts through fear and misinformation.
