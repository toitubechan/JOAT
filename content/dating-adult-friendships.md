## Card: What it is
image: adultFriendshipsHero
**Adult friendships** are the friendships we form and maintain in adulthood — which is genuinely harder than in childhood, when friends came easily through school and proximity. Many adults struggle with making and keeping friends, often silently. Understanding why, and how to nurture friendships, helps you build the social connections vital to wellbeing. This is general educational knowledge, not professional advice.

## Card: Why it matters
Friendships are crucial for happiness, health, and wellbeing throughout life — yet adult friendships often quietly fade, and many adults feel lonely or struggle to make friends. Understanding that this is common and *why* it happens (not a personal failing) — plus how to nurture friendships — helps you build and maintain the connections that genuinely matter for a good life.

## Card: Key terms
- **Adult friendships**: friendships formed and kept in adulthood.
- **Effort and intention**: actively nurturing friendships.
- **Proximity and repetition**: the conditions that naturally build friendship.
- **Loneliness**: a common but often unspoken adult experience.

## Card: Why it's harder as an adult
Friendships came easily in childhood because of **proximity and repetition** — being around the same people regularly (school, neighborhood) naturally builds friendship. As adults, we lose these built-in conditions: we're busy, scattered, and no longer thrown together repeatedly. This is *why* adult friendships are harder — not a personal failing. Recognizing this is freeing: if you find making friends harder as an adult, it's a common, structural challenge, and **loneliness** is far more widespread than people admit.

## Card: Nurturing adult friendships
image: nurturingFriendshipsHero
Because the natural conditions are gone, adult friendships need **effort and intention**. Making friends as an adult often means deliberately creating proximity and repetition — joining groups, activities, or communities where you see the same people regularly (recreating the conditions of childhood friendship). And *keeping* friends requires ongoing effort: reaching out, staying in touch, making time, and being a good friend in return. Friendships fade from neglect, not just conflict. Investing intentional effort is what builds and sustains adult friendships.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking struggling with adult friendships is a personal failing (it's a common, structural challenge); expecting friendships to form effortlessly like in childhood (adulthood lacks the natural conditions); letting friendships fade through neglect rather than effort; and feeling alone in loneliness (it's widespread). Adult friendships need intention and effort — creating regular contact and actively maintaining connections.

## Card: Quick recap
Adult friendships are harder than childhood ones because we lose the natural proximity and repetition (school, neighborhood) that once built friendships effortlessly — so struggling is common and structural, not a personal failing. Building friends as an adult means deliberately creating regular contact (groups, activities, communities), and keeping them requires ongoing effort (reaching out, staying in touch). Friendships fade from neglect, so intention and effort are key to vital social connection.
