## Card: What it is
image: breakerHero
A **circuit breaker** (or older **fuse**) is an automatic safety switch that cuts power when a circuit draws too much electricity. They live in your **consumer unit** or "fuse box." Their whole job is to stop wires overheating and starting fires — they're your home's electrical guardians.

## Card: Why it matters
When a breaker "trips" and cuts power, it's not a malfunction — it's protecting you. Knowing where your fuse box is, and how to safely reset a breaker, means you can restore power confidently and recognize when a repeated trip is warning you of a real problem worth investigating.

## Card: Key terms
- **Circuit breaker**: a resettable switch that cuts power on overload.
- **Fuse**: an older one-time version that melts to break the circuit.
- **Consumer unit / fuse box**: where breakers are housed.
- **Trip**: when a breaker automatically switches off.

## Card: Why breakers trip
image: tripProtects
A breaker **trips** for good reasons: too many devices on one circuit (**overload**), a fault like a **short circuit**, or a damaged appliance. By cutting power instantly, it prevents the wires from overheating. So a trip is a feature, not a fault — it means the safety system did exactly its job.

## Card: Resetting safely
To reset, first unplug or switch off whatever may have caused the trip. Go to the **fuse box**, find the breaker that's flipped (it sits between on and off, or fully off), and switch it firmly off then on. If it holds, you're done. If it trips again immediately, stop — that's a sign of a real fault.

## Card: Common mistakes
The biggest mistake is repeatedly resetting a breaker that keeps tripping — it's warning you of a hazard, and forcing it risks a fire. Another is not knowing where the fuse box is. And some replace a blown fuse with the wrong rating or, dangerously, with foil or a coin — defeating the safety entirely. Never do this.

## Card: Quick recap
Circuit breakers and fuses cut power to prevent overheating and fires — a trip means they're working. Reset a breaker by clearing the cause, then flipping it off and on. But a breaker that trips repeatedly signals a real fault: stop resetting and call an electrician. Never bypass a fuse.
