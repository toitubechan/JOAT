## Card: What it is
image: parentingHowChildrenDevelopHero
**Child development** is how children grow and change — physically, mentally, emotionally, and socially — from birth through childhood. Development follows broad patterns but varies between children. Understanding the basics helps parents and caregivers support children well and have realistic expectations. This is general educational knowledge; for concerns about a specific child, consult a professional.

## Card: Why it matters
Understanding how children develop helps caregivers support them appropriately, set realistic expectations, and worry less about normal variation. It reveals that much "challenging" behavior is normal development, and that children grow in understandable patterns. This knowledge fosters patience, better support, and confidence — benefiting both children and the adults caring for them.

## Card: Key terms
- **Child development**: how children grow and change over time.
- **Domains**: areas of development (physical, cognitive, emotional, social).
- **Developmental stages**: broad phases children pass through.
- **Individual variation**: children developing at their own pace.

## Card: Development across domains
image: parentingHowChildrenDevelopDevelopmentAcrossDomainsHero
Children develop across several **domains** at once: *physical* (body, movement, coordination), *cognitive* (thinking, learning, language), *emotional* (feelings and managing them), and *social* (relating to others). These are interconnected and unfold together over time. Understanding that development is multi-dimensional — not just physical growth — helps caregivers appreciate and support the whole child. Each domain progresses through broad **developmental stages** as children mature.

## Card: Patterns and variation
Development follows broad, predictable **patterns and stages** — children generally reach milestones in a similar order, which helps caregivers know roughly what to expect. *But* there's significant **individual variation**: children develop at their own pace, and reaching a milestone earlier or later than average is usually completely normal. Comparing children anxiously, or worrying about minor timing differences, is often unnecessary. Understanding both the general patterns *and* the normal variation helps caregivers support children without undue worry.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking development is just physical growth (it spans cognitive, emotional, and social domains too); expecting all children to develop at the same pace (individual variation is normal); worrying excessively about minor timing differences; and comparing children anxiously. Child development follows broad patterns with normal individual variation across multiple domains — understanding this supports children well without undue worry. (Consult a professional for genuine concerns.)

## Card: Quick recap
Child development is how children grow and change across multiple domains — physical, cognitive, emotional, and social — which unfold together over time through broad developmental stages. Development follows predictable patterns (children reach milestones in a similar order), but with significant normal individual variation in pace. Understanding both the patterns and the variation helps caregivers support children appropriately and worry less. For specific concerns, consult a professional.
