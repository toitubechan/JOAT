## Card: What it is
image: dataPercentagesRealLifeHero
Percentages show up constantly in **real life** — discounts, tips, interest, taxes, and statistics. This card set connects the theory to practical, everyday uses, so you can handle these situations confidently and avoid being caught out. It's where percentage skills genuinely pay off.

## Card: Why it matters
Percentages affect your money and decisions daily. Being comfortable with them in real situations helps you spot good deals, calculate tips, understand loan and savings interest, and judge statistics in the news. It's practical numeracy that directly saves money and prevents you from being misled or overcharged.

## Card: Key terms
- **Discount**: a percentage off the original price.
- **Tip**: a percentage added for service.
- **Interest**: a percentage charged or earned on money.
- **Markup**: a percentage added to a cost to set a price.

## Card: Money situations
image: dataPercentagesRealLifeMoneySituationsHero
Everyday money is full of percentages. **Discounts** ("30% off") tell you how much you save — and stacking the 10% trick helps you check them quickly. **Tips** are a percentage added for service. **Interest** is a percentage on money borrowed or saved (key to loans, credit cards, and savings). Being able to estimate these on the spot helps you make better, faster decisions and verify what you're charged.

## Card: Statistics and being a savvy reader
Percentages dominate **statistics** in the news ("crime up 40%", "70% of people..."). Being percentage-literate helps you read these critically: ask *percent of what*, watch for misleading percentage changes, and be wary of figures designed to alarm or persuade. The same skills that help you check a discount help you avoid being misled by statistics. Percentage literacy is a defense against manipulation.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: not checking whether a "discount" is genuine; miscalculating tips or being unable to estimate them; not understanding interest as a percentage (leading to costly borrowing mistakes); and accepting statistical percentages uncritically. The fix: get comfortable estimating percentages, and always ask "percent of what?" when reading figures.

## Card: Quick recap
Percentages run through everyday life: discounts, tips, interest, and statistics. Being comfortable with them helps you spot deals, calculate tips, understand borrowing and saving, and read statistics critically (always ask "percent of what?"). It's practical numeracy that saves money and defends against misleading figures. The theory pays off in real, daily decisions.
