## Card: What it is
image: spaceddTelescopesHero
**Telescopes** are instruments that gather light (and other signals) from distant objects, letting us see far more of space than the naked eye can. From backyard telescopes to giant observatories and space telescopes, they're how almost everything we know about the universe was discovered. They're humanity's eyes on the cosmos.

## Card: Why it matters
Nearly all our knowledge of space comes from telescopes — they revealed planets, galaxies, and the expanding universe. Understanding how they work demystifies astronomy and explains how we can study objects we'll never visit. It also reveals a beautiful fact: telescopes are time machines, showing us the universe's past.

## Card: Key terms
- **Telescope**: an instrument that gathers light from distant objects.
- **Light-gathering**: collecting faint light to see dim, distant things.
- **Space telescope**: a telescope placed in orbit, above the atmosphere.
- **Looking back in time**: seeing distant objects as they were long ago.

## Card: How telescopes work
image: lightGatheringHero
A telescope's main job is **light-gathering** — collecting far more light than your eye can, so faint and distant objects become visible. Bigger telescopes gather more light and reveal fainter, more distant things. Telescopes also detect more than visible light: some "see" radio waves, infrared, X-rays, and other signals invisible to us, revealing aspects of space the eye could never perceive.

## Card: Space telescopes and time travel
Earth's atmosphere blurs and blocks some incoming light, so **space telescopes** placed in orbit get clearer, fuller views. A profound feature of all telescopes: because light takes time to travel, looking at distant objects means **looking back in time** — we see far-off galaxies not as they are now, but as they were when their light left, sometimes billions of years ago. Telescopes literally let us observe the universe's history.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking telescopes mainly *magnify* (their key job is gathering light, not just enlarging); assuming they only see visible light (many detect radio, infrared, X-rays); not realizing space telescopes exist to escape the blurring atmosphere; and missing that telescopes show the past, not the present. They're light-gathering time machines, not just magnifiers.

## Card: Quick recap
Telescopes gather light (and other signals like radio and infrared) from distant objects, revealing far more than the naked eye can — almost all our knowledge of space comes from them. Bigger ones gather more light; space telescopes escape the atmosphere's blur. And because light takes time to travel, telescopes show us the past — we see distant objects as they were long ago.
