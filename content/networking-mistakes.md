## Card: What it is
image: networkingMistakesHero
Networking can backfire when done badly — coming across as pushy, fake, or selfish does more harm than not networking at all. This card set gathers the common mistakes, so you can build relationships that genuinely help your career rather than damaging your reputation.

## Card: Why it matters
A bad networking impression sticks and spreads — people talk. Avoiding these mistakes protects your reputation and makes your networking actually effective. Since the errors are mostly about *attitude* rather than skill, simply being aware of them is enough to sidestep most.

## Card: Key terms
- **Transactional**: treating relationships as pure exchanges.
- **Over-asking**: requesting too much, too soon.
- **Authenticity**: being genuine rather than performing.
- **Reputation**: how others perceive and describe you.

## Card: The "what's in it for me" trap
The cardinal sin is being purely **transactional** — only valuing people for what they can do for you, and showing it. People sense this quickly and it repels them. Related: **over-asking** — requesting big favors from people you barely know, too soon. Build the relationship first; trust and willingness to help come with time, not on first contact.

## Card: Being fake or pushy
image: pushyNetworkingHero
Two more killers: inauthenticity (performing a "networking persona" instead of being genuine — people can tell) and pushiness (aggressive self-promotion, not listening, dominating conversations). Both damage **reputation**. **Authenticity** and genuine listening are far more magnetic than slick self-promotion. Be a real, interested person, not a walking sales pitch.

## Card: Common mistakes
To gather them: being transactional and self-serving; over-asking too soon; fake "networking persona" behavior; pushy self-promotion; never giving back; forgetting names and not following up; and only networking when job-hunting. The cure for nearly all is the same: be genuine, curious, generous, and patient.

## Card: Quick recap
Networking backfires when you're transactional, over-ask too soon, act fake, or come across pushy — all of which harm your reputation. The fixes are simple and mostly about attitude: be authentic, genuinely interested in others, generous, and patient. Build trust before asking, and be a real person, not a sales pitch.
