## Card: What it is
image: cloudRainStormHero
**Clouds**, **rain**, and **storms** are the visible drama of weather, all arising from water and air interacting in the sky. They follow understandable processes: water vapor rising, cooling, and condensing. Knowing how they form makes the sky readable — you can often tell what's coming.

## Card: Why it matters
Clouds and storms affect daily plans and safety, and understanding them turns the sky from a mystery into something you can interpret. It satisfies natural curiosity ("why is it raining?") and gives you a basic ability to read weather signs. Storms especially are worth understanding for safety.

## Card: Key terms
- **Water vapor**: water in gas form, invisible in the air.
- **Condensation**: vapor cooling into tiny water droplets.
- **Cloud**: a visible mass of condensed droplets.
- **Storm**: intense weather with heavy rain, wind, or lightning.

## Card: How clouds and rain form
image: cloudRainCycle
The Sun evaporates water into invisible **water vapor**, which rises with warm air. As it rises, it cools, and the vapor **condenses** into tiny droplets — a visible **cloud**. When droplets grow heavy enough, they fall as **rain** (or snow if cold enough). So clouds are condensed water aloft, and rain is those droplets growing too heavy to stay up.

## Card: How storms form
**Storms** form when there's lots of warm, moist air rising rapidly. Thunderstorms build tall clouds where strong updrafts and water create heavy rain, and the charge that builds up discharges as **lightning** (with thunder being the sound of the air it superheats). Bigger storms draw energy from warm, moist conditions — which is why they're more common in heat and humidity.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking clouds are weightless puffs (they hold tonnes of water); not realizing rain is just cloud droplets grown heavy; imagining thunder and lightning are separate events (thunder is the sound of lightning); and underestimating storm safety. The processes are understandable, and reading the sky becomes possible with the basics.

## Card: Quick recap
Clouds form when rising water vapor cools and condenses into droplets; rain falls when those droplets grow too heavy. Storms form from lots of warm, moist air rising fast, with lightning being an electrical discharge and thunder its sound. Knowing these processes makes the sky readable and storms more understandable.
