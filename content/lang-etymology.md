## Card: What it is
image: langEtymologyHero
**Etymology** is the study of where words come from — their origins and how they've changed over time. Every word has a history, often surprising: borrowed from other languages, shifted in meaning, or built from older roots. Exploring etymology reveals the rich, hidden stories behind the everyday words we use without a second thought.

## Card: Why it matters
Etymology makes language come alive, revealing connections between words, hints about history and culture, and why words mean what they do. It can also help you understand and remember vocabulary (knowing roots helps decode unfamiliar words). Beyond utility, it's simply fascinating — turning ordinary words into windows onto history. It deepens appreciation of the language you use daily.

## Card: Key terms
- **Etymology**: the study of word origins and history.
- **Root**: a base from which related words are built.
- **Borrowing**: taking a word from another language.
- **Meaning shift**: how a word's meaning changes over time.

## Card: Words have histories
image: langEtymologyWordsHaveHistoriesHero
Every word has an origin story. Many words come from older **roots** — base elements (often from ancient languages) that spawn whole families of related words (so knowing one root helps you understand many words). Languages also constantly engage in **borrowing** — taking words from other languages they contact, which is why your everyday vocabulary is a patchwork of origins from many languages and eras. Words are like fossils, carrying traces of history within them.

## Card: Meanings drift over time
Words also undergo **meaning shift** — their meanings change, sometimes dramatically, over time. A word might broaden, narrow, or completely change what it refers to across centuries. This is why old texts can use familiar words in surprising ways, and why etymology often reveals that a word once meant something quite different. Language is alive and constantly evolving, and tracing a word's journey shows how meaning shifts with culture and use.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking words have fixed, eternal meanings (meanings shift over time); assuming a word's current meaning reveals its origin (etymology often surprises); not realizing how much vocabulary is borrowed from other languages; and missing that knowing roots helps decode unfamiliar words. Etymology reveals words as living things with rich, often surprising histories.

## Card: Quick recap
Etymology is the study of where words come from and how they've changed. Words often build from older roots (knowing one root helps decode many words), are borrowed from other languages (making vocabulary a patchwork of origins), and undergo meaning shifts over time. Words carry hidden histories like fossils. Exploring etymology makes language fascinating, aids vocabulary, and reveals connections to history and culture.
