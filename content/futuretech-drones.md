## Card: What it is
image: futuretechDronesHero
**Drones** are uncrewed aircraft — flying machines controlled remotely or autonomously, without a pilot on board. Ranging from small hobbyist quadcopters to large industrial and military craft, they've rapidly become useful across many fields. Understanding drones reveals a fast-growing technology reshaping photography, delivery, agriculture, and more.

## Card: Why it matters
Drones have spread quickly into everyday life and industry — filming, delivery, surveying, agriculture, search-and-rescue, and more. Understanding what they are and do helps you grasp a transformative technology, its genuine benefits, and the real concerns (privacy, safety, regulation) it raises. They're a visible piece of the technological future.

## Card: Key terms
- **Drone**: an uncrewed aircraft, remotely or autonomously controlled.
- **Quadcopter**: a common drone with four rotors.
- **Autonomous**: operating without direct human control.
- **Regulation**: rules governing where and how drones can fly.

## Card: What drones do
image: futuretechDronesWhatDronesDoHero
Drones are versatile because they're cheaper, smaller, and safer (no onboard pilot) than traditional aircraft, and can go where it's hard or dangerous for people. Uses include: aerial photography and filming; delivering packages; surveying land, crops, and infrastructure; monitoring and search-and-rescue; and agriculture (spraying, mapping fields). Small **quadcopters** are popular for hobby and photography, while larger drones handle industrial and other tasks.

## Card: Benefits and concerns
Drones offer real benefits — access to hard-to-reach places, lower cost, new capabilities — and are increasingly **autonomous** (flying set routes or tasks on their own). But they raise concerns too: **privacy** (cameras flying anywhere), safety (collisions, falling drones), airspace congestion, and misuse. This is why drones face growing **regulation** governing where, when, and how they can fly. Balancing their benefits against these concerns is an ongoing challenge.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking drones are only toys or only military (they span hobby, commercial, and industrial uses); assuming they can be flown anywhere freely (regulations restrict them); overlooking privacy and safety concerns; and either over-hyping or dismissing them. Drones are a versatile, fast-growing technology with genuine uses and genuine concerns.

## Card: Quick recap
Drones are uncrewed aircraft, remotely or autonomously controlled, ranging from hobby quadcopters to industrial craft. They're cheaper, smaller, and safer than piloted aircraft, useful for photography, delivery, surveying, agriculture, and rescue. They bring real benefits but raise concerns about privacy, safety, and airspace — which is why they're increasingly regulated. A versatile technology with genuine uses and trade-offs.
