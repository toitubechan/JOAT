## Card: What it is
image: futuretechSpaceTechHero
**Space tech** is the technology used to explore, use, and benefit from space — rockets, satellites, probes, and the systems behind them. A new era is underway, driven by reusable rockets, private companies, and small satellites. Space tech also quietly powers much of daily life on Earth, more than most people realize.

## Card: Why it matters
Space technology underpins everyday services (GPS, weather, communications) and is advancing rapidly with private companies and falling costs. Understanding it reveals how much we already depend on space, where the field is heading, and why it's a major area of innovation and investment. It connects the cosmos directly to your daily life.

## Card: Key terms
- **Satellite**: an object in orbit, often providing services.
- **Reusable rockets**: rockets that land and fly again, cutting costs.
- **Small satellites**: cheaper, miniaturized satellites launched in numbers.
- **Space economy**: the growing commercial activity in space.

## Card: Space tech in your daily life
image: futuretechSpaceTechSpaceTechInYourDailyHero
Much space tech quietly powers everyday life through **satellites**: GPS (navigation, maps, ride apps), weather forecasting, television and communications, and Earth observation (monitoring climate, agriculture, disasters). When you use maps or check the weather, you're relying on space technology. This often-invisible role means space isn't remote — it's woven into modern daily life right now.

## Card: The new space era
A major shift is underway. **Reusable rockets** — which land and fly again instead of being discarded — have dramatically cut launch costs, and private companies now lead much activity alongside national agencies. **Small satellites** (cheaper, miniaturized, launched in large numbers) are expanding what's possible. This growing **space economy** is fueling ambitions from global internet coverage to Moon and Mars missions. Space is becoming more accessible and commercial than ever.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking space tech is remote and irrelevant to daily life (it powers GPS, weather, and communications you use constantly); assuming only governments do space (private companies now lead much of it); not realizing how reusable rockets changed the economics; and over- or under-hyping the new space era. Space tech is both an everyday utility and a fast-advancing frontier.

## Card: Quick recap
Space tech — rockets, satellites, probes, and their systems — quietly powers daily life through GPS, weather, and communications, so space isn't remote but woven into modern life. A new era is driven by reusable rockets (slashing launch costs), private companies, and cheap small satellites, fueling a growing space economy and ambitions from global internet to Mars. Space tech is both everyday utility and advancing frontier.
