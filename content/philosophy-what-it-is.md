## Card: What it is
image: philosophyWhatItIsHero
**Philosophy** is the careful, reasoned exploration of life's deepest questions — about existence, knowledge, right and wrong, meaning, and reality. It's not about having all the answers, but about thinking clearly and rigorously about questions that science can't fully settle. Philosophy is, at heart, the love of wisdom and the practice of examined thinking.

## Card: Why it matters
Philosophy tackles questions everyone wonders about: What's right? What's real? What's the point? Engaging with it sharpens your thinking, clarifies your values, and enriches how you see life. Far from being useless or abstract, philosophy underpins ethics, science, law, and how we live. It's the practice of thinking deeply and well about what matters.

## Card: Key terms
- **Philosophy**: reasoned exploration of fundamental questions.
- **Big questions**: deep questions about existence, knowledge, ethics, meaning.
- **Reasoned argument**: thinking carefully and logically, not just opining.
- **Branches**: major areas like ethics, knowledge, and existence.

## Card: Questions, not just answers
image: philosophyWhatItIsQuestionsNotJustAnswersHero
A key feature: philosophy values the *quality of thinking* as much as conclusions. It tackles **big questions** — Is there free will? What makes something right or wrong? How do we know what's true? What is reality? — that often have no simple, provable answer. Philosophy explores them through **reasoned argument**: careful, logical thinking, considering different views, rather than just stating opinions. The thinking itself is the point.

## Card: The big branches
Philosophy has major **branches**, each tackling a kind of question: *ethics* (what's right and wrong, how to live), *epistemology* (knowledge — how we know things), *metaphysics* (existence and reality — what's ultimately real), and *logic* (good reasoning). Over millennia, great thinkers have built on each other, debating these questions. You don't need to be an expert — just willing to think carefully about questions that matter.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking philosophy is useless or purely abstract (it underpins ethics, science, and how we live); expecting it to give final, provable answers (it values reasoned exploration of hard questions); confusing it with just stating opinions (it requires careful argument); and finding it intimidating. Philosophy is the accessible, valuable practice of thinking deeply and clearly about life's big questions.

## Card: Quick recap
Philosophy is the careful, reasoned exploration of life's deepest questions — about existence, knowledge, right and wrong, and meaning — that science can't fully settle. It values clear, logical thinking as much as answers, working through reasoned argument rather than mere opinion. Its branches (ethics, knowledge, reality, logic) tackle questions everyone wonders about. It's the accessible practice of thinking deeply and well.
