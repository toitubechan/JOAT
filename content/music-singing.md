## Card: What it is
image: musicSingingHero
**Singing** is using your voice to produce musical sound — and it's a skill that can be learned and improved, not just an inborn gift you either have or don't. Your voice is an instrument, and like any instrument, it benefits from technique and practice. Understanding the basics demystifies singing and reveals that most people can improve far more than they think.

## Card: Why it matters
Many people believe they "can't sing" and give up, when in fact singing is largely a learnable skill. Understanding the basics — breath, technique, and practice — opens up an accessible, joyful form of musical expression. Even if you never perform, understanding how the singing voice works deepens appreciation and shows that improvement is possible for almost anyone.

## Card: Key terms
- **Singing**: producing musical sound with the voice.
- **Breath support**: using breath properly to power the voice.
- **Pitch (in tune)**: singing the right notes accurately.
- **Learnable skill**: the idea that singing can be improved with practice.

## Card: The voice as an instrument
image: musicSingingTheVoiceAsAnInstrumentHero
Singing produces sound the same way any instrument does — through vibration (here, your vocal cords vibrating as air passes), shaped by your mouth and body. A foundation of good singing is **breath support** — using your breath properly to power and control the voice steadily (most singing technique relates to breath and control). So your voice is a physical instrument you can learn to play better, through understanding and developing technique, just like any other instrument.

## Card: Singing is largely learnable
The biggest myth is that singing is a fixed inborn talent you either have or don't. In reality, singing is largely a **learnable skill**: things like singing in **pitch** (in tune), breath control, and tone can be developed substantially with practice and technique. While natural aptitude varies (as with any skill), most people who think they "can't sing" can improve a great deal. Understanding this is freeing — singing is something to *develop*, not a gift you're simply born with or without.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: believing singing is a fixed inborn talent you can't improve (it's largely learnable); neglecting breath support (the foundation of vocal control); thinking you either have a "good voice" or don't, fixed forever; and giving up after assuming you "can't sing." Singing is a skill — your voice is an instrument that, with technique and practice, most people can develop far more than they expect.

## Card: Quick recap
Singing uses your voice — an instrument that produces sound through vibrating vocal cords, shaped by your body — and a foundation is breath support (using breath to power and control the voice). The biggest myth is that singing is a fixed inborn gift; in reality it's largely a learnable skill, where pitch, control, and tone improve substantially with practice. Most people who think they "can't sing" can improve far more than they expect.
