## Card: What it is
image: contractHero
A **contract** is a legally binding agreement between parties, setting out what each side promises to do. You sign them more often than you realize — jobs, rentals, phone plans, gym memberships, online terms. Understanding what a contract *is* and that it's binding is the first step to not getting caught out. (General knowledge, not legal advice.)

## Card: Why it matters
Contracts shape major parts of life — your job, home, money, and purchases. Signing without understanding can lock you into unfair terms, unexpected costs, or obligations you didn't intend. Knowing the basics helps you protect yourself, ask the right questions, and avoid costly mistakes. It's essential life-admin literacy.

## Card: Key terms
- **Contract**: a legally binding agreement.
- **Parties**: the people or entities involved.
- **Obligation**: what each side is required to do.
- **Binding**: enforceable, with consequences for breaking it.

## Card: A contract is binding
image: bindingAgreement
The key thing to grasp: a contract is **binding** — once you agree (often by signing, but sometimes by clicking "I agree" or even verbally), you're legally committed to its terms. "I didn't read it" is rarely a valid excuse. This is why understanding before you commit matters so much: the terms you agree to are the terms you're held to, whether or not you read them.

## Card: Contracts are everywhere
Contracts aren't just formal documents lawyers draft. They include employment agreements, tenancy agreements, loan and phone contracts, gym memberships, insurance policies, and the "terms and conditions" you click past online. Recognizing that all these are binding agreements — not mere formalities — helps you treat them with appropriate care rather than signing on autopilot.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: signing without reading or understanding (you're still bound); assuming "terms and conditions" don't really count (they can); thinking a verbal agreement isn't binding (it sometimes is); and treating contracts as mere formalities. The core lesson: a contract binds you to its terms, so understand before you commit.

## Card: Quick recap
A contract is a legally binding agreement setting out each side's obligations — and you enter them constantly (jobs, rentals, plans, online terms). Once you agree, you're committed, and "I didn't read it" rarely helps. Contracts aren't mere formalities. Understanding before you commit is the key protection. (For important contracts, professional advice is wise.)
