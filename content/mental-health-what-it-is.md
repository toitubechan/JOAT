## Card: What it is
image: mentalHealthHero
**Mental health** is your emotional, psychological, and social wellbeing — how you think, feel, handle stress, and relate to others. Just like physical health, everyone has it, it changes over time, and it can be looked after. It's not only about illness; it's about how well you're functioning and feeling.

## Card: Why it matters
Mental health affects every part of life — relationships, work, decisions, and physical health too. Understanding it reduces stigma and helps you care for yourself and others. This is general educational content, not a substitute for professional help; if you're struggling, reaching out to a doctor or counselor is a strong, sensible step.

## Card: Key terms
- **Mental health**: emotional and psychological wellbeing.
- **Stigma**: unfair negative judgment around mental health.
- **Wellbeing**: feeling and functioning well overall.
- **Self-care**: actions that support your wellbeing.

## Card: A spectrum, not a switch
image: mentalSpectrumHero
Mental health isn't simply "fine" or "ill." It's a **spectrum** that shifts with circumstances — a hard period can lower it; support and good habits can lift it. Everyone moves along this range throughout life. Recognizing this makes it normal to talk about and tend to, rather than something only "other people" deal with.

## Card: Breaking the stigma
**Stigma** — unfair judgment around mental health — stops people seeking help, often making things worse. The truth is that mental health struggles are common and human, not a weakness or failing. Talking openly, like we do about physical health, is part of the solution. Seeking support is a sign of strength, not weakness.

## Card: Common mistakes
A common mistake is thinking mental health only matters when something is seriously wrong, rather than something to maintain like physical fitness. Another is dismissing struggles as weakness or "just stress." And many wait far too long to seek help, when earlier support — as with physical health — usually makes things easier.

## Card: Quick recap
Mental health is your emotional and psychological wellbeing — everyone has it, it shifts over time, and it can be cared for. It's a spectrum, not a switch, and struggles are common and human. Reducing stigma and seeking support early are key. This is general knowledge; professionals help with the personal.
