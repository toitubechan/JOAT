## Card: What it is
image: brainHowItWorksHero
The **brain** is the body's control center — a remarkable organ that processes information, controls the body, and generates your thoughts, emotions, memories, and sense of self. Made of billions of nerve cells, it's the most complex object we know of. Everything you think, feel, and do runs through it.

## Card: Why it matters
The brain is, in a real sense, *you* — your mind, personality, and experience all arise from it. Understanding the basics demystifies how you think and feel, and why sleep, learning, and habits affect your mind. It's the foundation for understanding mental health, learning, and human behavior.

## Card: Key terms
- **Neuron**: a nerve cell that carries signals.
- **Neural networks**: connected neurons working together.
- **Regions**: brain areas specialized for different jobs.
- **Plasticity**: the brain's ability to change and adapt.

## Card: Billions of connected cells
image: neuronNetworkHero
The brain is made of around 86 billion **neurons** — cells that communicate via electrical and chemical signals. What makes the brain powerful isn't single neurons but their **connections**: each neuron links to thousands of others, forming vast **neural networks**. Your thoughts, memories, and skills live in the patterns of these connections. The brain is less like a computer chip and more like an unimaginably complex web.

## Card: Regions and adaptability
Different brain **regions** specialize in different tasks — vision, movement, language, emotion, memory — though they work together constantly. A remarkable feature is **plasticity**: the brain can rewire itself, forming new connections as you learn and adapt. This is why you can learn new skills throughout life, and why the brain can sometimes recover or compensate after injury. The brain isn't fixed; it's continually shaped by experience.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: the myth that we only use 10% of our brain (we use virtually all of it); thinking the brain is fixed and unchangeable (plasticity lets it adapt and learn lifelong); imagining a single spot does everything (regions specialize but cooperate); and treating the brain as just a computer (it's far more complex and adaptive). The brain is a dynamic, ever-changing web.

## Card: Quick recap
The brain is the body's control center, made of ~86 billion neurons whose vast connections form the networks behind your thoughts, memories, and self. Different regions specialize but work together, and the brain is plastic — able to rewire and adapt throughout life as you learn. We use virtually all of it (not 10%). It's the most complex object we know of.
