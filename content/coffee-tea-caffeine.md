## Card: What it is
image: caffeineHero
**Caffeine** is the natural stimulant in coffee and tea that makes you feel more awake and alert. It's the world's most widely used stimulant, and understanding how it works helps you enjoy these drinks while avoiding the downsides like jitters and disrupted sleep. (General knowledge, not medical advice.)

## Card: Why it matters
Caffeine affects energy, focus, and sleep, and people vary widely in how they handle it. Knowing the basics lets you time your drinks well, avoid sleepless nights and afternoon crashes, and enjoy coffee and tea as the pleasant ritual they are, rather than an accidental source of restlessness.

## Card: Key terms
- **Caffeine**: a natural stimulant that increases alertness.
- **Half-life**: how long it takes the body to clear half the caffeine.
- **Tolerance**: needing more for the same effect over time.
- **Sensitivity**: how strongly caffeine affects an individual.

## Card: How caffeine works
Caffeine blocks the brain signals that make you feel sleepy, so you feel more alert. But it has a long **half-life** — often around 5–6 hours — meaning an afternoon coffee can still be in your system at bedtime, disrupting sleep. This is why *when* you drink it matters as much as how much.

## Card: Everyone's different
image: caffeineSensitivity
**Sensitivity** to caffeine varies a lot — some people can drink coffee at night and sleep fine, others get jittery from one cup. Regular use also builds **tolerance**, so effects lessen over time. Tea generally has less caffeine than coffee. Pay attention to how *you* respond, rather than following generic rules.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: drinking caffeine late in the day and blaming poor sleep on something else; relying on more and more coffee as tolerance builds; ignoring that you might be unusually sensitive; and forgetting caffeine appears in other things too (energy drinks, some sodas, chocolate). Moderation and timing are the keys to enjoying it.

## Card: Quick recap
Caffeine is the stimulant in coffee and tea that boosts alertness, but its long half-life means afternoon cups can disrupt sleep. Sensitivity varies widely, and regular use builds tolerance. Enjoy these drinks sensibly: mind the timing, notice how you personally respond, and remember caffeine lurks in other foods too.
