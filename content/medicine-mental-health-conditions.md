## Card: What it is
image: medicineMentalHealthConditionsHero
**Mental health conditions** are health problems that affect thinking, mood, or behavior — such as depression, anxiety disorders, and others. They're common, real medical conditions, not personal weaknesses. This is a respectful, general overview to reduce stigma and increase understanding. (For diagnosis or help, a qualified professional is the right source.)

## Card: Why it matters
Mental health conditions are extremely common, yet often misunderstood and stigmatized, which stops people seeking help. Understanding them with accuracy and compassion reduces stigma, helps you recognize them in yourself or others, and encourages support. It's a crucial part of overall health that affects vast numbers of people.

## Card: Key terms
- **Mental health condition**: a health problem affecting mind and mood.
- **Stigma**: unfair negative judgment that discourages seeking help.
- **Depression**: persistent low mood and loss of interest.
- **Anxiety disorder**: excessive, persistent worry or fear.

## Card: Real, common conditions
image: medicineMentalHealthConditionsRealCommonConditionsHero
Mental health conditions are genuine health problems, just as real as physical ones, and they're **common** — affecting a large share of people at some point. Examples include **depression** (persistent low mood, loss of interest, and other symptoms beyond ordinary sadness) and **anxiety disorders** (excessive, persistent worry or fear that interferes with life). These have biological, psychological, and social roots — not character flaws or things people can simply "snap out of."

## Card: Stigma and seeking help
**Stigma** — unfair judgment around mental illness — is a major barrier, making people feel ashamed or reluctant to seek help, which can worsen things. The truth is these conditions are common, treatable, and nothing to be ashamed of. Effective help exists (talking therapies, sometimes medication, support). Reducing stigma and encouraging people to reach out — to professionals and trusted others — saves suffering and lives.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: seeing mental health conditions as weakness or something to "snap out of" (they're real health conditions); confusing everyday sadness or stress with clinical depression or anxiety disorders (these are more persistent and disruptive); thinking they're untreatable (effective help exists); and perpetuating stigma. Understanding and compassion, plus professional help, are what genuinely matter.

## Card: Quick recap
Mental health conditions like depression and anxiety disorders are real, common health problems affecting mind, mood, and behavior — not weaknesses or things to "snap out of." They have biological, psychological, and social roots, and effective help exists. Stigma is a major barrier that stops people seeking support. Understanding, compassion, and encouraging professional help genuinely matter. (For help, consult a professional.)
