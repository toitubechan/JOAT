## Card: What it is
image: marketingSeoHero
**SEO** (Search Engine Optimization) is the practice of improving your website or content so it appears higher in search engine results, bringing in visitors who are actively searching for what you offer. Since search is how many people find things online, ranking well can drive significant free traffic. Understanding the basics demystifies a key part of online marketing.

## Card: Why it matters
When people search for something, they usually click results near the top — so ranking well can bring a steady stream of people already looking for what you offer (high-intent visitors). This "free" traffic is hugely valuable. Understanding SEO basics helps you get found online, and cuts through the hype and myths around it. It's foundational for any online presence.

## Card: Key terms
- **SEO**: optimizing to rank higher in search results.
- **Keywords**: the terms people search for.
- **Quality content**: genuinely useful content that ranks well.
- **Organic traffic**: visitors from unpaid search results.

## Card: How search engines work
image: marketingSeoHowSearchEnginesWorkHero
Search engines aim to show users the most *relevant, useful* results for their search. SEO works *with* this: you help search engines understand your content and judge it useful and relevant for the **keywords** (search terms) your audience uses. The big shift in modern SEO is that genuinely **quality content** that truly serves searchers tends to win — there's no lasting trick to outrank genuinely better, more relevant content. Serving the searcher well is the heart of good SEO.

## Card: The basics that matter
Core SEO basics: understand the **keywords** your audience searches for, and create **quality content** that genuinely answers those searches better than alternatives. Make your site technically sound (fast, mobile-friendly, easy for search engines to read). Earn credibility (other sites linking to yours signals trust). The result is **organic traffic** — unpaid visitors from search. Crucially, SEO is a long game: it takes time to build, but provides lasting, compounding traffic, unlike paid ads that stop when you stop paying.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: chasing tricks or "hacks" to game search engines (modern search rewards genuinely good content, and tricks backfire); stuffing keywords unnaturally; ignoring content quality; expecting instant results (SEO is a long game); and neglecting the technical basics. Good SEO means genuinely serving searchers with quality, relevant content — there's no lasting shortcut around being actually useful.

## Card: Quick recap
SEO is improving your website and content to rank higher in search results, bringing in people actively searching for what you offer (valuable, high-intent organic traffic). It works by helping search engines see your content as relevant and useful for your audience's keywords — and modern SEO rewards genuinely quality content over tricks. It's a long game that builds lasting, compounding traffic. Serving the searcher well is the heart of it.
