## Card: What it is
image: financeSavingSaveConsistentlyHero
**Saving consistently** means setting money aside regularly, no matter how small, as a built-in habit rather than whatever happens to be left over. The big shift is timing: instead of saving what remains at month's end, you save *first* and live on the rest. This one change quietly transforms results.

## Card: Why it matters
Most people who "try to save what's left" find nothing is left — spending expands to fill the available money. By saving first and automating it, you remove willpower from the equation. Small, steady amounts saved automatically beat occasional big efforts, because consistency plus time is what really builds wealth.

## Card: Key terms
- **Pay yourself first**: save before spending on anything else.
- **Automation**: setting transfers to happen without manual effort.
- **Standing order**: a scheduled, recurring transfer you set up once.
- **Savings rate**: the share of income you save.

## Card: Pay yourself first
image: financeSavingSaveConsistentlyPayYourselfFirstHero
The core principle: treat saving like a bill you owe yourself. The moment income arrives, move a set amount into savings *before* you budget the rest. Living on what remains feels surprisingly normal within a month or two. This flips saving from a hopeful leftover into a guaranteed priority.

## Card: Automate it
Willpower is unreliable; **automation** isn't. Set up a **standing order** that moves money to savings the day after payday, and the decision makes itself every month. Out of sight, out of temptation. Even starting tiny matters — you can raise the amount later, and the habit is the hard part, not the size.

## Card: Common mistakes
The first mistake is waiting to "have enough spare" before starting — there's rarely a perfect time, so small-and-now beats big-and-later. The second is keeping savings in the same account you spend from, where it quietly gets absorbed. And some set up automation, then cancel it the first tight month instead of just lowering it.

## Card: Quick recap
Save consistently by paying yourself first — move money to savings before spending — and automate it with a standing order so it happens without thought. Keep savings separate from spending, start small if needed, and protect the habit. Consistency over time beats occasional large bursts.
