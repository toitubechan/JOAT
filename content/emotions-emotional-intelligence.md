## Card: What it is
image: psychEmotionsEmotionalIntelligenceHero
**Emotional intelligence** (EI or EQ) is the ability to understand and manage your own emotions, and to recognize and respond well to others' emotions. It pulls together emotional self-awareness, regulation, empathy, and social skill. It's increasingly recognized as just as important as traditional intelligence for success and wellbeing.

## Card: Why it matters
Emotional intelligence strongly affects relationships, work, leadership, and happiness. People high in EI navigate social situations, conflicts, and their own feelings far better. The encouraging news: unlike raw intelligence, EI can be developed at any age with practice. It's a learnable skill set with wide-reaching benefits.

## Card: Key terms
- **Emotional intelligence (EQ)**: understanding and managing emotions, yours and others'.
- **Self-awareness**: recognizing your own emotions.
- **Empathy**: understanding others' feelings.
- **Social skill**: handling relationships and interactions well.

## Card: The components
image: psychEmotionsEmotionalIntelligenceTheComponentsHero
Emotional intelligence has a few core parts: **self-awareness** (recognizing your own emotions), **self-management** (regulating them — the pause, reframing), **empathy** (sensing and understanding what others feel), and **social skill** (using all this to handle relationships and interactions well). Together, these let you navigate the emotional side of life — your own and others' — skillfully.

## Card: Empathy and social skill
The "other people" side of EI is powerful. **Empathy** — genuinely understanding how others feel — builds trust, defuses conflict, and strengthens relationships. Combined with **social skill** (communicating, reading situations, responding well), it makes you someone people connect with and want to work with. Much of success in relationships and work comes down to this emotional understanding of others, not just raw intellect.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking EI is fixed or innate (it's developable with practice); equating it only with being "nice" (it includes self-awareness and managing your own emotions too); neglecting empathy and the skill of reading others; and underrating EI compared to traditional intelligence. EI is a learnable, wide-reaching skill set worth deliberately developing.

## Card: Quick recap
Emotional intelligence (EQ) is understanding and managing emotions — your own and others' — through self-awareness, self-management, empathy, and social skill. It strongly affects relationships, work, and wellbeing, often as much as traditional intelligence. Crucially, it's learnable at any age with practice. Developing it pays off across nearly every area of life.
