## Card: What it is
image: buildingConnectionsHero
Building **connections** is the practical side of networking — how you actually meet people and turn a first conversation into a relationship. It's a learnable set of low-pressure behaviors, not a personality trait. The core is simple: show genuine interest in others and offer value, not just ask for it.

## Card: Why it matters
Knowing *that* networking matters isn't enough; you need to know *how*. Practical, comfortable ways to connect make the difference between intending to network and actually doing it. These approaches work even if you find it awkward — they're about being helpful and curious, which anyone can manage.

## Card: Key terms
- **Give first**: offering help or value before asking for anything.
- **Genuine interest**: real curiosity about others.
- **Warm introduction**: being introduced by a mutual contact.
- **Follow-up**: staying in touch after meeting.

## Card: Give before you take
image: giveBeforeTakeHero
The most powerful networking principle is **give first**. Offer help, share useful information, make an introduction, or simply show **genuine interest** in someone's work — before you ever need anything. People remember and reciprocate generosity. Approaching relationships as "how can I help?" rather than "what can I get?" makes connecting natural and welcome.

## Card: Start conversations and follow up
You don't need clever lines — **genuine interest** does the work. Ask people about their work and listen. **Warm introductions** (being introduced by a mutual contact) are easier and more effective than cold approaches. And crucially, **follow up**: a connection only becomes a relationship if you stay in touch, even occasionally. The first conversation is just the start.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: only reaching out when you need a favor; making it all about yourself instead of showing interest in others; never following up, so connections fade; using cheesy, transactional lines; and waiting until you urgently need a network to start building one. Give first, be curious, and follow up.

## Card: Quick recap
Build connections by giving first — offering help or genuine interest before asking for anything — which makes you welcome and memorable. Start conversations through real curiosity, use warm introductions, and always follow up to turn a chat into a relationship. It's not about clever lines; it's about being helpful and genuinely interested.
