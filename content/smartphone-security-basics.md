## Card: What it is
image: phoneSecurityHero
Your phone holds an enormous amount of your life — messages, photos, accounts, banking, even your identity. **Phone security** is the set of basic protections that keep all that safe if your phone is lost, stolen, or targeted. A few simple steps make a huge difference.

## Card: Why it matters
A lost or stolen unprotected phone is a goldmine for a thief — access to your accounts, contacts, and money. Most phone security basics are quick to set up and then work silently in the background. Given how much your phone holds, these protections are well worth the few minutes they take.

## Card: Key terms
- **Screen lock**: a PIN, password, or biometric to unlock the phone.
- **Biometrics**: fingerprint or face unlock.
- **Find My Device**: a feature to locate or wipe a lost phone.
- **Encryption**: scrambling data so it's unreadable without your key.

## Card: Lock it down
image: screenLockHero
The foundation is a **screen lock** — a strong PIN, password, or **biometric** (fingerprint/face). This is the single most important phone security step; without it, a thief has everything. Avoid obvious PINs like "1234" or your birth year. Modern phones also use **encryption**, so a locked phone's data is scrambled and unreadable to others.

## Card: Prepare for loss and stay updated
Set up **Find My Device** (built into both iPhone and Android) so you can locate, lock, or remotely erase a lost phone — a lifesaver if it goes missing. Keep your phone's software **updated**, since updates patch security holes. Together, a screen lock, find-my-device, and updates cover most of what matters.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: no screen lock, or a guessable one like "0000"; not enabling find-my-device, so a lost phone is unrecoverable; ignoring software updates that fix security flaws; and installing apps from unofficial sources. Also risky: leaving the phone unlocked and unattended in public, even briefly.

## Card: Quick recap
Phone security starts with a strong screen lock (the most important step), backed by encryption that protects locked data. Enable find-my-device to locate or wipe a lost phone, and keep software updated to patch flaws. Given how much your phone holds, these quick steps are genuinely worth it.
