## Card: What it is
image: financeCreditTypesOfLoansHero
A **loan** is a lump of money you borrow and repay over time with interest. But loans come in flavors, each shaped for a purpose: buying a home, a car, funding study, or general needs. Knowing the main types helps you pick the right one — and understand why their rates differ so much.

## Card: Why it matters
Using the wrong loan for a purpose can cost you dearly — like putting a long-term purchase on a high-interest card. Each loan type has typical rates, terms, and rules. Matching the loan to the need usually means lower interest and a structure that actually fits what you're buying.

## Card: Key terms
- **Secured loan**: backed by an asset the lender can take if you don't pay.
- **Unsecured loan**: no asset backing it, so usually higher interest.
- **Mortgage**: a long-term loan to buy property.
- **Collateral**: the asset that secures a loan.

## Card: Secured vs. unsecured
image: financeCreditTypesOfLoansSecuredVsUnsecuredHero
A **secured loan** is tied to **collateral** — an asset like a house or car the lender can seize if you stop paying. Because the lender has that safety net, rates are lower. An **unsecured loan** (like most personal loans and credit cards) has no collateral, so it's riskier for the lender and charges higher interest.

## Card: The common loan types
- **Mortgage**: long-term (often decades), secured by the home, low rate.
- **Auto loan**: secured by the car, medium term and rate.
- **Student loan**: funds education, often with flexible or low rates.
- **Personal loan**: usually unsecured, flexible use, higher rate.
Each suits its purpose — and the secured ones tend to be cheapest.

## Card: Common mistakes
A big mistake is financing everyday wants with high-interest personal loans or cards when cheaper options fit better. Another is borrowing the maximum offered rather than what you need — more loan means more interest. And with secured loans, people forget the asset is at stake: miss payments, and you can lose the home or car.

## Card: Quick recap
Loans come in types: mortgages, auto, student, and personal, split broadly into secured (backed by collateral, cheaper) and unsecured (no backing, pricier). Match the loan to the purpose, borrow only what you need, and remember secured loans put your asset on the line.
