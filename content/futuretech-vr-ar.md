## Card: What it is
image: futuretechVrArHero
**Virtual reality (VR)** immerses you in a fully digital world, usually through a headset that replaces what you see and hear. **Augmented reality (AR)** instead overlays digital content onto the real world (like information or images appearing through a phone or glasses). Both blend the digital and physical in new ways, with growing uses beyond gaming.

## Card: Why it matters
VR and AR are reshaping gaming, training, design, education, and more, and may become major ways we interact with technology. Understanding the difference between them, and their real uses and limits, helps you follow a developing field and see past the hype. They represent a shift in how we might experience digital content.

## Card: Key terms
- **Virtual reality (VR)**: full immersion in a digital world.
- **Augmented reality (AR)**: digital content overlaid on the real world.
- **Immersion**: the feeling of being "inside" a digital experience.
- **Headset**: the device delivering VR or AR.

## Card: VR vs. AR — the key difference
image: futuretechVrArVrVsArTheKeyHero
The core distinction: **VR replaces** your reality — a headset blocks out the real world and immerses you fully in a digital one, so you feel "inside" it (**immersion**). **AR adds** to your reality — it overlays digital information or images onto the real world you still see (through a phone screen or transparent glasses). Simply: VR takes you elsewhere; AR brings digital content into your actual surroundings.

## Card: Real uses beyond gaming
While gaming is the best-known use, both have broader applications. VR is used for training (flight simulators, surgery practice, dangerous-job rehearsal), virtual tours, therapy, and design visualization. AR overlays helpful information onto the real world — navigation arrows on streets, instructions on equipment, or digital objects in your room. As the technology improves and devices shrink, these uses are expected to grow well beyond entertainment.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: confusing VR and AR (VR replaces reality; AR adds to it); thinking they're only for gaming (both have training, education, and practical uses); over-hyping them as imminently everywhere (the tech is still developing, with cost and comfort limits); and dismissing them entirely. They're developing technologies with genuine uses and real current limitations.

## Card: Quick recap
Virtual reality (VR) fully immerses you in a digital world via a headset that replaces your surroundings; augmented reality (AR) overlays digital content onto the real world you still see. Simply: VR takes you elsewhere, AR adds to here. Beyond gaming, both are used in training, education, design, and more. They're promising, developing technologies — with real uses and current limits.
