## Card: What it is
image: datingConflictHero
**Navigating conflict** is handling disagreements in relationships constructively, so they strengthen rather than damage the bond. Conflict is normal and inevitable in any close relationship — what matters is *how* you handle it. Understanding healthy conflict reveals that disagreements, managed well, can actually deepen relationships. This is general educational knowledge, not professional advice.

## Card: Why it matters
Conflict is unavoidable in close relationships, and how it's handled often determines whether the relationship thrives or suffers. Many people fear conflict or handle it poorly, causing lasting damage. Understanding that conflict is normal — and how to navigate it constructively — helps you protect and even strengthen your relationships through inevitable disagreements. It's a key relationship skill.

## Card: Key terms
- **Conflict**: disagreement or tension between people.
- **Healthy conflict**: disagreeing respectfully and constructively.
- **Repair**: reconnecting after a conflict.
- **Issue vs. person**: addressing the problem, not attacking each other.

## Card: Conflict is normal
A reassuring truth: **conflict** is normal and inevitable in any close relationship — even healthy ones have disagreements. Conflict itself isn't the problem; *how* it's handled is. The goal isn't to avoid all conflict (impossible, and avoidance breeds resentment) but to have **healthy conflict** — disagreeing respectfully and resolving issues constructively. Handled well, conflict can actually strengthen a relationship by resolving problems and deepening understanding. So don't fear disagreement; learn to navigate it well.

## Card: How to navigate it well
image: navigateConflictWellHero
Keys to healthy conflict: focus on the **issue vs. person** — address the specific problem rather than attacking each other's character; stay respectful even when upset (no contempt, insults, or stonewalling); listen to understand the other's perspective, not just win; use "I" statements; and look for resolution or compromise, not victory. Afterward, **repair** matters — reconnecting and moving past the conflict. The aim is to solve problems *together* as a team, rather than treating the conflict as a battle to win against each other.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: avoiding all conflict (breeds resentment and unresolved issues); attacking the person rather than the issue; using contempt, insults, or stonewalling (very damaging); trying to "win" rather than resolve; and not repairing afterward. Conflict is normal — handle it constructively by addressing issues respectfully, listening, seeking resolution together, and repairing. Done well, conflict can strengthen a relationship.

## Card: Quick recap
Conflict is normal and inevitable in close relationships — what matters is *how* you handle it. Healthy conflict means disagreeing respectfully: focus on the issue not the person, stay respectful (no contempt or stonewalling), listen to understand, use "I" statements, and seek resolution together rather than victory. Repair and reconnect afterward. Handled well, conflict can actually strengthen a relationship rather than damage it.
