## Card: What it is
image: routineHabitHero
A **sustainable routine** is an exercise plan you can keep doing long-term, not just for a motivated month. The secret isn't willpower — it's designing exercise around your real life and preferences so it becomes a habit rather than a constant battle.

## Card: Why it matters
Fitness comes from consistency over months and years, not heroic short bursts. A routine you abandon delivers nothing, however perfect on paper. Building something realistic and enjoyable is what actually produces results — because the best exercise is, quite simply, the one you'll keep doing.

## Card: Key terms
- **Consistency**: exercising regularly over the long term.
- **Habit stacking**: attaching exercise to an existing routine.
- **Enjoyment**: choosing activities you actually like.
- **Realistic goal**: a target that fits your life.

## Card: Choose what you enjoy
The single biggest predictor of sticking with exercise is **enjoyment**. If you hate running, don't run — dance, cycle, swim, play a sport, or walk. There's no "best" exercise that beats one you'll actually do. Enjoyment turns exercise from a chore you dread into something you look forward to.

## Card: Make it easy to do
image: easyExerciseSetup
Lower the barriers. Use **habit stacking** — attach exercise to something you already do (a walk after lunch). Schedule it like an appointment, lay out kit in advance, and start with **realistic goals** that fit your week. Small and consistent beats ambitious and abandoned. Two short sessions you keep beat five you skip.

## Card: Common mistakes
The big mistake is relying on motivation, which always fades — habits and systems last longer. Another is an unrealistic plan (daily hour-long gym sessions) that collapses within weeks. People also do exercise they hate out of duty, then quit, and treat one missed session as total failure rather than just continuing.

## Card: Quick recap
A sustainable routine is built on enjoyment and ease, not willpower. Pick activities you like, attach them to existing habits, set realistic goals, and make starting easy. Consistency over time is what delivers results. The best exercise is the one you'll actually keep doing — so choose for the long game.
