## Card: What it is
image: solarSystemHero
The **solar system** is our cosmic neighborhood: the **Sun** and everything orbiting it — eight planets (including Earth), their moons, plus asteroids, comets, and dust. It's held together by the Sun's gravity, with everything circling this one central star. It's home, on the cosmic scale.

## Card: Why it matters
Understanding the solar system places Earth in context — one planet among many, orbiting an ordinary star. It satisfies deep curiosity about our surroundings and is the foundation for understanding space. Knowing the basic layout makes astronomy news, space missions, and the night sky far more meaningful.

## Card: Key terms
- **Solar system**: the Sun and everything orbiting it.
- **Planet**: a large body orbiting the Sun.
- **Orbit**: the path one object takes around another.
- **Gravity**: the force holding the system together.

## Card: The Sun and its planets
At the center is the **Sun**, a star so massive its **gravity** holds the whole system together. Eight **planets** **orbit** it, in order from the Sun: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars (the smaller, rocky inner planets), then Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune (the giant outer planets). The Sun contains the vast majority of the solar system's mass — everything else orbits this dominant center.

## Card: Other members and vast distances
image: vastSolarDistances
Beyond planets, the solar system holds **moons** (orbiting planets), **asteroids** (rocky chunks, many between Mars and Jupiter), and **comets** (icy bodies with tails). A key thing to grasp is the *scale*: distances are mind-bendingly vast and mostly empty space. The planets are tiny specks separated by enormous gaps — space is far emptier than diagrams suggest.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking planets are close together (they're separated by vast emptiness); imagining the solar system is crowded (it's mostly empty space); confusing stars and planets (the Sun is a star; planets don't produce their own light); and not realizing the Sun holds nearly all the system's mass. Scale is the hardest thing to grasp.

## Card: Quick recap
The solar system is the Sun and everything orbiting it — eight planets, their moons, plus asteroids and comets — held together by the Sun's gravity. The Sun holds almost all the mass. The inner planets are rocky, the outer ones giants. Above all, the distances are vast and the system mostly empty space.
