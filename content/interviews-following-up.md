## Card: What it is
image: interviewFollowupHero
The interview isn't over when you leave the room. **Following up** — asking good questions at the end, sending a thank-you, and reflecting afterward — can strengthen your impression and improve your next attempt. This card set covers finishing strong and the common end-stage mistakes to avoid.

## Card: Why it matters
Many candidates do well in the interview then neglect the ending and follow-up, missing easy chances to stand out. A thoughtful close and follow-up reinforce your interest and professionalism. And reflecting on each interview turns even rejections into practice that makes you better for the next one.

## Card: Key terms
- **Questions to ask**: thoughtful questions for the interviewer.
- **Thank-you note**: a brief follow-up message after the interview.
- **Reflection**: reviewing how the interview went to improve.
- **Follow-up**: any contact after the interview itself.

## Card: End strong with questions
Interviewers almost always ask "do you have any **questions** for us?" — and "no" is a weak answer. Prepare a few thoughtful questions about the role, team, or company. Good questions show genuine interest and that you've thought seriously about the job. This is also your two-way moment to learn whether the role truly suits you.

## Card: Follow up and reflect
image: followupReflectHero
A short **thank-you note** (often email) within a day reinforces your interest and keeps you memorable — a small effort many skip. Then **reflect**: what went well, what you'd improve? Every interview is practice. Even if you don't get the role, the lessons make your next interview stronger. Treat each as a step forward, not just a verdict.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: having no questions to ask at the end (looks disengaged); skipping the thank-you follow-up; pestering the employer too aggressively afterward; and not reflecting, so the same mistakes repeat. Also: taking rejection as proof of failure rather than as practice. Finish thoughtfully and learn from every round.

## Card: Quick recap
Finish interviews strong: ask a few thoughtful questions (never "no questions"), send a brief thank-you note within a day, and reflect afterward on what to improve. Don't pester, and don't treat rejection as failure — every interview is practice that makes the next one better. The ending and follow-up matter more than people think.
