## Card: What it is
image: dnaGeneticsHero
**DNA** is the molecule that carries the instructions for building and running a living thing — a kind of recipe book inside almost every cell. **Genetics** is the study of how these instructions are passed down and shape the traits of organisms. DNA is, quite literally, the code of life.

## Card: Why it matters
DNA explains how you inherit traits from your parents, why family members resemble each other, and how life passes information across generations. It underlies medicine, ancestry, and much modern science. Understanding the basics demystifies headlines about genes and gives you insight into what makes each living thing what it is.

## Card: Key terms
- **DNA**: the molecule carrying genetic instructions.
- **Gene**: a section of DNA coding for a particular trait.
- **Inheritance**: passing genes from parents to offspring.
- **Trait**: a characteristic like eye color or height.

## Card: The instruction manual
image: geneInstructionManual
**DNA** is like a vast instruction manual stored in your cells, written in a chemical code. A **gene** is a section of that manual with instructions for a particular feature or function — say, a step in making a protein that affects your eye color. Your full set of DNA contains the instructions to build and operate the whole organism.

## Card: How traits are inherited
Through **inheritance**, you receive DNA from your parents — a mix of both — which is why you share **traits** (eye color, height, and more) with your family. This passing-down of genetic instructions across generations is how characteristics are inherited. It also allows variation: the reshuffling of genes means offspring aren't identical to either parent.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking DNA determines everything rigidly, when many traits result from genes *and* environment together; imagining you're a copy of one parent (you inherit a mix of both); and seeing genes as a fixed "destiny," when their effects are often more complex and influenced by other factors. DNA is instructions, not an absolute script.

## Card: Quick recap
DNA is the molecule carrying life's instructions, stored in cells like a recipe book; a gene is a section coding for a particular trait. Through inheritance, you receive a mix of DNA from both parents, sharing traits with your family. But genes interact with environment — DNA is instructions that guide, not a rigid, all-determining script.
