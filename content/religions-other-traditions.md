## Card: What it is
image: histReligionsOtherTraditionsHero
Beyond the largest religions, the world holds many other **faith traditions** — from Sikhism and Judaism's neighbors to indigenous and folk religions — as well as a large and growing number of **non-religious** people. This card set respectfully broadens the picture beyond the most populous faiths.

## Card: Why it matters
Focusing only on the biggest religions misses the world's full diversity of belief. Recognizing the range of traditions, and that many people are non-religious, gives a more accurate and respectful picture of humanity. It fosters appreciation of how varied human approaches to meaning truly are.

## Card: Key terms
- **Faith tradition**: an organized system of religious belief.
- **Indigenous religion**: traditional beliefs of native peoples.
- **Atheist / agnostic**: not believing / unsure about the divine.
- **Spiritual but not religious**: holding personal beliefs outside organized religion.

## Card: Other faith traditions
image: histReligionsOtherTraditionsOtherFaithTraditionsHero
The world's religious landscape is rich beyond the largest faiths. **Sikhism**, for example, is a major religion originating in South Asia, emphasizing devotion to one God and equality. There are also **indigenous and folk religions** — traditional beliefs of native peoples worldwide, often tied to nature, ancestors, and community — plus many smaller and newer traditions. Each carries deep meaning for its followers.

## Card: The non-religious
A large and growing share of people are **non-religious**. This includes **atheists** (who don't believe in a god), **agnostics** (who are unsure or hold that it's unknowable), and many who consider themselves **spiritual but not religious** — holding personal beliefs about meaning without an organized faith. Non-belief and personal spirituality are significant parts of the global picture of how humans approach life's big questions.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking only a few big religions exist (there are many traditions); ignoring indigenous and folk religions; assuming everyone is religious (a large share isn't); and conflating atheism, agnosticism, and "spiritual but not religious" (they differ). The world's beliefs are wonderfully varied, including many who are non-religious.

## Card: Quick recap
Beyond the largest faiths lie many traditions — Sikhism, indigenous and folk religions, and others — plus a large, growing number of non-religious people: atheists, agnostics, and the "spiritual but not religious." Each represents a way humans approach meaning. Recognizing this full diversity, including non-belief, gives a more accurate and respectful picture of humanity.
