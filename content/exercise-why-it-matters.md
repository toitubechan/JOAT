## Card: What it is
image: exerciseBenefitsHero
**Exercise** is any physical activity that gets your body moving and working harder than rest. It ranges from a brisk walk to lifting weights. You don't need a gym or athletic ability — the core idea is simply moving your body regularly, because human bodies are built to move, not sit.

## Card: Why it matters
Regular movement is one of the most powerful things you can do for your health — it benefits your heart, muscles, bones, weight, mood, sleep, and brain. Few habits offer so much for so little. Understanding the benefits makes it easier to prioritize, even when motivation dips. (General guidance, not personal medical advice.)

## Card: Key terms
- **Exercise**: physical activity that works the body.
- **Cardio**: activity that raises your heart rate.
- **Strength**: activity that works your muscles.
- **Sedentary**: a lifestyle with too little movement.

## Card: Benefits beyond the body
Exercise isn't just about looks or fitness. It boosts **mood** by releasing feel-good chemicals, sharpens focus and memory, improves **sleep**, and reduces stress and anxiety. Many people start exercising for their body and keep going for their mind — the mental benefits are often felt fastest.

## Card: Movement is medicine
image: movementMedicineHero
A **sedentary** lifestyle — lots of sitting, little movement — is linked to many health problems. The good news: even modest activity helps a lot, and the biggest gains come from going from *nothing* to *something*. You don't need to become an athlete; consistent moderate movement delivers most of the benefit.

## Card: Common mistakes
A big mistake is thinking exercise only "counts" if it's intense or hours long — regular moderate movement matters more. Another is an all-or-nothing mindset: skipping entirely because you can't do a full workout. And many overlook everyday movement (walking, stairs), which adds up significantly over a day.

## Card: Quick recap
Exercise is simply moving your body regularly, and it benefits nearly everything — heart, muscles, bones, mood, sleep, and brain. The mental boosts come fast. The biggest gains are from doing *something* rather than nothing, so consistency beats intensity. Everyday movement counts too.
