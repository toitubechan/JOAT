## Card: What it is
image: growthFocusTrainingYourFocusHero
Focus isn't a fixed trait — it's more like a **muscle** you can strengthen with practice. If your attention feels scattered, that's largely a result of habits and constant stimulation, and it can be rebuilt. This card set covers how to train your focus back to strength.

## Card: Why it matters
Many people feel their attention spans have shrunk, blaming themselves. The encouraging truth is that focus is trainable: the same way it weakened, it can be rebuilt. Knowing focus is a skill, not a fixed limit, turns "I just can't concentrate" into "I can get better at this."

## Card: Key terms
- **Attention training**: deliberately practicing sustained focus.
- **Mindfulness**: practicing present-moment attention.
- **Boredom tolerance**: comfort with not being constantly stimulated.
- **Refocusing**: gently returning attention when it wanders.

## Card: Practice focusing
image: growthFocusTrainingYourFocusPracticeFocusingHero
Like any muscle, focus grows with **attention training**: deliberately practicing sustained concentration and gradually extending it. Start with short focused sessions and build up. Crucially, **refocusing** is the rep that builds the muscle — each time your mind wanders and you gently bring it back, you're strengthening focus, not failing.

## Card: Reclaim boredom and stillness
Constant stimulation (scrolling at every idle moment) erodes focus and **boredom tolerance**. Reclaim it: let yourself be bored sometimes without reaching for your phone, and try **mindfulness** — simply practicing present-moment attention. These rebuild your capacity to sit with one thing. Reducing the constant dopamine hits helps your attention recover its natural strength.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: believing a short attention span is permanent; expecting instant focus without training; filling every idle second with stimulation, which weakens focus; and treating mind-wandering as failure rather than as the rep that builds the muscle. Focus rebuilds gradually, with practice and less constant stimulation.

## Card: Quick recap
Focus is a trainable muscle, not a fixed limit. Strengthen it with deliberate practice (short focused sessions, extended over time), where each refocus after wandering is a rep that builds strength. Reclaim boredom tolerance by not filling every idle moment with stimulation, and try mindfulness. Scattered attention can be rebuilt.
