## Card: What it is
image: routineChecksHero
**Routine checks** are the quick, regular look-overs that keep a car healthy between professional services — lights, tires, fluids, wipers. Paired with following your car's **service schedule**, they form the backbone of car care. Little and often beats one big panic when something fails.

## Card: Why it matters
Cars rarely fail without warning; they give small signs first. Routine checks catch those signs, and scheduled servicing handles the wear you can't see. Together they prevent breakdowns, keep you safe, and protect resale value — a well-documented service history is worth real money when you sell.

## Card: Key terms
- **Service schedule**: the maker's plan of when to service the car.
- **Service history**: the record of completed services.
- **Routine check**: quick self-inspections between services.
- **Wear items**: parts that wear out, like brake pads and wipers.

## Card: Your quick check routine
image: walkaroundCheck
A simple regular walk-around takes minutes: are all **lights** working (ask someone to watch the brake lights)? Tires inflated and not worn? Washer fluid topped up and wipers clearing properly? Any new leaks under the car, or odd noises and smells? Doing this regularly makes problems obvious early.

## Card: Following the service schedule
The **service schedule** in your manual lists when to service the car, by time or distance. Services replace **wear items** and fluids and catch developing faults. Keeping to it, and keeping the **service history** documented, keeps the car reliable and safe — and reassures future buyers that it's been cared for.

## Card: Common mistakes
The first mistake is delaying or skipping services to save money, which usually backfires with bigger repairs. Another is never doing simple checks between services, so problems grow unseen. And many lose their **service history** records, which lowers the car's value and makes it harder to prove proper upkeep.

## Card: Quick recap
Keep a car healthy with two habits: quick routine checks (lights, tires, fluids, wipers, leaks) and following the service schedule in your manual. Services handle hidden wear; checks catch early signs. Keep the service history documented. Little and often prevents breakdowns and protects your car's value.
