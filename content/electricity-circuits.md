## Card: What it is
image: circuitLoopHero
A **circuit** is a complete loop that electricity flows around, from a power source, through a device, and back. Electricity only flows when the loop is unbroken — break it anywhere, and the flow stops. This simple idea of a closed loop explains how switches, batteries, and devices all work together.

## Card: Why it matters
Circuits are the basis of every electrical device. Understanding the loop explains everyday things: why a switch turns things on and off, why a battery has two terminals, and why a broken wire kills the whole thing. It's the foundational concept that makes electrical devices make sense.

## Card: Key terms
- **Circuit**: a complete loop electricity flows around.
- **Closed circuit**: an unbroken loop where current flows.
- **Open circuit**: a broken loop where current stops.
- **Switch**: a device that opens or closes the loop.

## Card: The loop must be complete
image: closedCircuitLoop
Electricity needs a **closed circuit** — a complete, unbroken path from the source, through the device, and back to the source. If the loop is broken anywhere (an **open circuit**), the flow stops entirely. This is why a single broken wire or loose connection can stop a whole device: the loop is interrupted.

## Card: Switches and components
A **switch** is simply a way to break or complete the loop on purpose — flipping it open stops the flow (off), closing it completes the loop (on). Along the circuit, components like bulbs or motors use the flowing electricity to do work. Everything in a device is arranged around keeping or controlling this essential loop.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking electricity flows out of one wire and just disappears (it must return to complete the loop); not realizing why two connections (terminals) are needed; and forgetting that any break stops the whole flow. Picture the unbroken loop, and circuits, switches, and batteries all make sense.

## Card: Quick recap
A circuit is a complete loop electricity flows around: source, through a device, and back. Flow happens only when the loop is closed (unbroken); break it anywhere and the flow stops. A switch deliberately opens or closes the loop to turn things off and on. The complete loop is the key idea behind every device.
