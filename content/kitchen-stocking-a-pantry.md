## Card: What it is
image: pantryHero
A **pantry** is your store of long-lasting basic ingredients — the staples you keep on hand so you can always cook *something* without a special shopping trip. A well-stocked pantry is the foundation of easy, spontaneous home cooking and a big money-saver.

## Card: Why it matters
With a good pantry, a quick meal is always possible — you just add a fresh item or two. It reduces takeaways, food waste, and last-minute shopping. Knowing which staples are worth keeping means you can throw together meals on busy days and rescue dinner when the fridge looks empty.

## Card: Key terms
- **Pantry staples**: long-lasting basics you keep stocked.
- **Shelf-stable**: food that lasts a long time without refrigeration.
- **Base ingredients**: building blocks like grains, oils, and tins.
- **Flavor boosters**: items that add taste, like spices and sauces.

## Card: The building blocks
Core **shelf-stable** staples: a carbohydrate or two (rice, pasta, potatoes), tinned goods (beans, tomatoes, fish), cooking oil, onions and garlic, stock cubes, and flour. These **base ingredients** form the backbone of countless meals. With these in, you're rarely more than one fresh ingredient away from dinner.

## Card: Flavor on standby
image: flavorBoosters
Just as important are **flavor boosters** that turn plain staples into real meals: salt and pepper, a few dried herbs and spices, and a couple of sauces or condiments (soy sauce, mustard, vinegar). These last for ages and add the seasoning that makes simple ingredients taste good. They punch well above their cost.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: buying lots of unusual ingredients for one recipe that then sit unused; not keeping the everyday basics stocked, so cooking feels impossible; ignoring expiry and letting things go off; and over-stocking perishables. A focused pantry of things you actually use beats a cupboard of one-off purchases.

## Card: Quick recap
A pantry of long-lasting staples — grains, tinned goods, oil, onions, garlic, stock, flour — plus flavor boosters like salt, spices, and a few sauces, means you can always cook something. It cuts waste, cost, and last-minute shopping. Stock what you actually use, and rotate it before it expires.
