## Card: What it is
image: logicDecisionTrapsHero
**Decision traps** are predictable mental pitfalls that lead us to make poor choices — often without realizing it. They come from cognitive biases and flawed thinking patterns. Knowing the common traps helps you recognize them in the moment and decide more wisely, especially on important choices.

## Card: Why it matters
We make countless decisions, and these traps quietly steer us wrong — costing money, opportunities, and good outcomes. Since they're predictable, knowing them is a powerful advantage: you can catch yourself before falling in. Better decision-making compounds over a lifetime, making this one of the most practically valuable critical-thinking skills.

## Card: Key terms
- **Sunk cost fallacy**: continuing because of past investment.
- **Confirmation bias**: favoring information that fits your beliefs.
- **Anchoring**: over-relying on the first piece of information.
- **Overconfidence**: overestimating your own judgment.

## Card: Common traps
image: logicDecisionTrapsCommonTrapsHero
A few that catch nearly everyone: the **sunk cost fallacy** (continuing something — a project, film, investment — because of what you've already put in, even when stopping is wiser); **confirmation bias** (seeking and favoring information that confirms what you already believe, ignoring the rest); **anchoring** (over-relying on the first number or fact you hear, like an initial price); and **overconfidence** (overestimating your own judgment and underrating risk). Each quietly distorts decisions.

## Card: Avoiding the traps
You can't eliminate these tendencies, but you can guard against them. Helpful habits: slow down on important decisions (traps thrive on fast, automatic thinking); actively seek information that *challenges* your view (countering confirmation bias); ignore sunk costs by asking "what's best from here, forward?"; question your first impressions (anchoring); and stay humble about your judgment. Awareness plus these habits, not willpower alone, is the defense.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking you're immune to decision traps (everyone is susceptible); believing awareness alone removes them (you need active habits too); only spotting traps in others; and not slowing down on important choices. The fix: know the common traps, slow down, seek challenging information, ignore sunk costs, and stay humble — turning predictable pitfalls into avoidable ones.

## Card: Quick recap
Decision traps are predictable mental pitfalls — the sunk cost fallacy (continuing due to past investment), confirmation bias (favoring agreeable information), anchoring (over-relying on the first input), and overconfidence (overrating your judgment). Guard against them by slowing down, seeking challenging information, ignoring sunk costs ("what's best from here?"), and staying humble. Awareness plus these habits leads to wiser decisions.
