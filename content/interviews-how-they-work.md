## Card: What it is
image: interviewsHero
A **job interview** is a conversation where an employer assesses whether you're right for a role — and you assess whether it's right for you. It's not an interrogation to survive but a two-way evaluation. Understanding what interviewers are *really* looking for transforms how you approach it.

## Card: Why it matters
Interviews make many people anxious, partly because they misunderstand the purpose. Knowing what's actually being assessed lets you prepare the right things and feel more in control. Since interviews are the gateway to most jobs, getting comfortable with them is one of the highest-value career skills you can build.

## Card: Key terms
- **Interview**: a conversation to assess mutual fit.
- **Competency**: a skill or quality the role requires.
- **Culture fit**: how well you'd work with the team.
- **Two-way street**: you're evaluating them too.

## Card: What they're really assessing
image: assessmentTwoWayHero
Interviewers typically weigh three things: **can you do the job** (skills and **competency**), **will you do the job** (motivation and reliability), and **will you fit in** (working well with the team, or **culture fit**). Most interview questions, however they're worded, are probing one of these. Knowing this helps you aim your answers.

## Card: It's a two-way street
An interview isn't just them judging you — it's also your chance to judge *them*. Is this a place you'd want to work? This reframe is powerful: it eases anxiety (you have power too) and helps you ask good questions. Treating it as a mutual conversation, not a one-sided test, makes you come across as confident and engaged.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: treating the interview as an interrogation to endure rather than a conversation; not realizing they assess fit and motivation, not just skills; forgetting it's two-way and asking no questions; and being so anxious about "passing" that you forget to evaluate whether the role suits you. Reframe it, and you'll perform better.

## Card: Quick recap
An interview is a two-way conversation assessing mutual fit. Employers weigh whether you *can* do the job, *will* do it, and will *fit in* — most questions probe one of these. Remember you're evaluating them too. This reframe eases anxiety and helps you come across as confident, engaged, and prepared.
