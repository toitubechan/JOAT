## Card: What it is
image: onlinePrivacyHero
**Online privacy** is your control over who can see and use your personal information on the internet — your data, activity, location, and habits. Much of online life is "free" precisely because your data is the payment. Understanding this trade is the first step to managing your privacy.

## Card: Why it matters
Your personal data is genuinely valuable — it's collected, combined, and used to profile, target, and sometimes manipulate you, and it can be exposed in breaches. Privacy isn't about having something to hide; it's about control. Knowing how it works lets you decide what to share, rather than giving it away unknowingly.

## Card: Key terms
- **Personal data**: information about you, from name to browsing habits.
- **Data collection**: gathering your information, often automatically.
- **Profile**: a detailed picture of you built from your data.
- **Targeted advertising**: ads aimed at you based on your data.

## Card: You are the product
A key insight: when an online service is free, your **personal data** is often what pays for it. Companies collect your activity to build a **profile** of your interests and habits, then use it for **targeted advertising** or sell access to it. "Free" rarely means free — it means you're paying with information instead of money.

## Card: Privacy is about control
image: privacyControlGate
Privacy isn't secrecy or having something to hide — it's about *control* over your own information. You might happily share some things and not others; the point is that *you* decide, knowingly. Lost privacy means decisions about your data are made for you, often invisibly, by companies you never chose to trust.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: thinking "I've nothing to hide, so privacy doesn't matter," which misses that privacy is about control and protection; assuming free services don't cost anything; and not realizing how much is collected automatically, beyond what you actively post. Awareness is the foundation of every other privacy step.

## Card: Quick recap
Online privacy is your control over who sees and uses your personal data. Free services are often paid for with your data, used to profile and target you. Privacy isn't about hiding — it's about deciding knowingly what to share. Understanding the data trade is the first step to protecting yourself.
