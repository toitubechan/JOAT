## Card: What it is
image: redFlagsTricksHero
Scammers rely on a small set of **psychological tricks** to override your judgment — urgency, fear, authority, and temptation. Learning these levers lets you recognize when one is being pulled on you, which is often the clearest sign of a scam, regardless of the story being told.

## Card: Why it matters
Scams work by getting you to act on emotion before thinking. If you can feel the emotional pressure being applied, you can pause — and pausing defeats most scams. Spotting the *technique* is more reliable than spotting the *disguise*, because the techniques rarely change even as the cover stories do.

## Card: Key terms
- **Urgency**: pressure to act immediately, with no time to think.
- **Authority**: posing as a bank, police, or official to command obedience.
- **Fear**: threatening loss, trouble, or danger.
- **Too good to be true**: an offer luring you with easy gain.

## Card: The pressure levers
image: pressureLevers
The big ones: **urgency** ("act in the next hour"), **fear** ("your account will be closed" or "you're in legal trouble"), and **authority** (pretending to be your bank, the police, or a government body to make you comply). These short-circuit careful thinking and push you to react. Feeling rushed or scared is itself the warning.

## Card: The temptation lever
The flip side of fear is greed: offers that are **too good to be true**. A prize you didn't enter, a guaranteed investment return, a stranger offering you money. The excitement is designed to stop you questioning. The rule holds firmly: if something seems too good to be true, it almost always is.

## Card: Common mistakes
The core mistake is letting emotion drive action — paying or sharing details while panicked or excited. Another is being swayed by claimed **authority** without verifying independently (call the bank's official number yourself). And many feel embarrassed to "waste time" checking, when taking time is exactly what defeats the scam.

## Card: Quick recap
Scammers pull psychological levers: urgency, fear, authority, and too-good-to-be-true temptation, all designed to make you act before thinking. Feeling pressured, scared, or thrilled is itself a red flag. Pause, and verify independently through official channels. Spotting the technique beats spotting the disguise.
