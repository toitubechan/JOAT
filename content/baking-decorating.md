## Card: What it is
image: decoratingBasicsHero
**Decorating** is finishing baked goods to look appealing — with icing, frosting, toppings, and presentation. Good decoration makes treats more inviting and special, and the basics are more achievable than elaborate showpieces suggest. Understanding fundamental decorating principles helps anyone make their bakes look as good as they taste, without needing professional skills.

## Card: Why it matters
We eat with our eyes first — attractive decoration makes baked goods more appealing and enjoyable, and turns ordinary treats into special ones. Many people feel decorating is beyond them, intimidated by elaborate cakes. Understanding that simple decoration done well looks great (and is achievable) empowers you to finish your bakes attractively. Presentation noticeably elevates baking with modest effort.

## Card: Key terms
- **Decorating**: finishing baked goods to look appealing.
- **Frosting/icing**: sweet coverings used to decorate.
- **Simplicity**: clean, simple decoration done well.
- **Presentation**: how a finished bake is shown.

## Card: Simple done well beats elaborate
image: simpleDecorationHero
A liberating principle: **simple decoration done well** usually looks better than ambitious decoration done poorly. You don't need elaborate skills — clean, neat, simple finishes (a smooth layer of **frosting**, a dusting of sugar, fresh fruit, a few tidy touches) look attractive and professional. Trying complex techniques before mastering basics often looks worse. So focus on doing simple things neatly: clean lines, even coverage, and restraint. **Simplicity** executed well is both achievable and genuinely appealing.

## Card: Presentation matters
Beyond decoration itself, overall **presentation** makes a big difference — how a finished bake is arranged and shown. Clean edges, a tidy plate, even portions, and a thoughtful final touch (a garnish, a dusting) elevate the whole impression. Since we "eat with our eyes," a little attention to presentation noticeably increases appeal and enjoyment, even for simple bakes. Good presentation is low-effort, high-impact — often mattering as much as elaborate decorating skills for making treats look special.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: attempting elaborate decoration before mastering basics (often looks worse than simple done well); messy or rushed finishing (clean and neat matters most); ignoring presentation entirely; and feeling decorating is beyond you (simple, neat decoration is achievable by anyone). The key is simplicity done well, clean execution, and thoughtful presentation — which make bakes look great without professional skills.

## Card: Quick recap
Decorating finishes baked goods to look appealing, and the key principle is that simple decoration done well beats elaborate decoration done poorly. Clean, neat, simple finishes — smooth frosting, a dusting of sugar, fresh fruit, tidy touches — look attractive and are achievable by anyone. Overall presentation (clean edges, thoughtful final touches) is low-effort, high-impact. Since we eat with our eyes, simple, well-executed decoration noticeably elevates any bake.
