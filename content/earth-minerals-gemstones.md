## Card: What it is
image: mineralsGemstonesHero
**Minerals** are naturally occurring solid substances with a specific chemical makeup and orderly structure — the building blocks of rocks. **Gemstones** are rare, beautiful minerals (or rocks) prized for jewelry. From the salt on your table to the diamond in a ring, minerals are all around us.

## Card: Why it matters
Minerals make up the rocks and soil of our planet and provide the raw materials for nearly everything we build and use — metals, electronics, construction, even toothpaste. Gemstones connect geology to art, value, and culture. Understanding minerals reveals the natural origins of the materials underpinning modern life.

## Card: Key terms
- **Mineral**: a natural solid with a defined composition and structure.
- **Crystal**: a solid with atoms arranged in an orderly pattern.
- **Gemstone**: a rare, attractive mineral used in jewelry.
- **Ore**: rock containing valuable minerals or metals.

## Card: What makes a mineral
image: mineralStructureHero
A **mineral** is naturally occurring, solid, and has a specific chemical composition and an orderly internal structure — its atoms are arranged in a repeating pattern, forming a **crystal**. This is why many minerals grow in geometric shapes. Rocks are typically made of one or more minerals packed together. There are thousands of minerals, from common quartz to rare gems.

## Card: Gemstones and ores
**Gemstones** are minerals (or sometimes rocks) that are rare, durable, and beautiful enough to be cut and polished for jewelry — diamonds, rubies, emeralds. Their value comes from rarity, beauty, and hardness. Meanwhile, **ores** are rocks containing valuable minerals or metals (like iron or copper) in concentrations worth extracting. So minerals range from precious gems to the everyday metals that build our world.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: confusing rocks and minerals (rocks are usually made *of* minerals); thinking all crystals are gemstones (most minerals aren't gem-quality); assuming gemstone value is arbitrary (it comes from rarity, beauty, and hardness); and not realizing how many everyday materials come from minerals and ores. Minerals are the natural foundation of countless things we use.

## Card: Quick recap
Minerals are naturally occurring solids with a specific composition and orderly crystal structure — the building blocks of rocks. Gemstones are rare, beautiful, durable minerals used in jewelry, valued for rarity and hardness. Ores are rocks holding valuable metals worth extracting. From table salt to diamonds to construction metals, minerals are the natural foundation of much of modern life.
