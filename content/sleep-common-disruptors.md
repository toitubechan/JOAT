## Card: What it is
image: sleepDisruptorsHero
Many things quietly sabotage sleep — substances, screens, stress, and routines — often without us connecting them to a bad night. This card set covers the most common **sleep disruptors** so you can spot which ones might be undermining your rest and remove them.

## Card: Why it matters
You can do everything else right and still sleep badly if a hidden disruptor is at work. Because their effects are delayed or subtle, the culprit isn't always obvious. Learning to recognize common disruptors helps you troubleshoot poor sleep instead of just accepting it or blaming yourself.

## Card: Key terms
- **Stimulant**: a substance that increases alertness, like caffeine.
- **Half-life**: how long a substance lingers in your body.
- **Stress / racing mind**: mental activity that blocks sleep.
- **Sleep environment**: the room conditions you sleep in.

## Card: Substances that sabotage
**Caffeine** is a **stimulant** with a long **half-life** — that afternoon coffee can still be in your system at bedtime. **Alcohol** helps you drop off but fragments later sleep. Large late meals and nicotine also disrupt rest. The timing matters: it's not just *what* you consume, but *when*.

## Card: Screens, stress, and environment
image: screensStressEnvironment
Bright screens late at night delay sleepiness and keep the mind engaged. A **racing mind** from **stress** is a top cause of lying awake. And a poor **sleep environment** — too warm, too bright, noisy, or an uncomfortable bed — undermines even a tired body. Each can quietly steal an hour of rest.

## Card: Common mistakes
A frequent mistake is not linking afternoon caffeine to a restless night hours later. Another is doom-scrolling or working in bed, then wondering why the mind won't switch off. People also keep bedrooms too warm or bright, and lie in bed anxious about not sleeping — which itself makes sleep harder.

## Card: Quick recap
Common sleep disruptors include late caffeine (it lingers for hours), alcohol, big late meals, bright screens, a stressed racing mind, and a too-warm or noisy room. Their effects are often delayed, so the cause hides. Troubleshoot poor sleep by checking these before assuming you simply "can't sleep."
