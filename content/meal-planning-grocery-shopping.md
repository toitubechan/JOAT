## Card: What it is
image: groceryShoppingHero
Smart **grocery shopping** is buying what you need efficiently, avoiding waste and overspending. A few simple habits — shopping with a list, knowing supermarket tricks, and buying sensibly — turn a costly, haphazard chore into a quick, money-saving routine.

## Card: Why it matters
Groceries are a major regular expense, and shops are expertly designed to make you spend more. Shopping smartly cuts your bill, reduces impulse buys and waste, and saves time. Small habits at the supermarket add up to meaningful savings over a year, for no real sacrifice.

## Card: Key terms
- **Shopping list**: a planned list of what to buy.
- **Impulse buy**: an unplanned purchase, often near the checkout.
- **Unit price**: the price per standard amount, for fair comparison.
- **Loss leader**: a cheap item used to lure shoppers in.

## Card: Shop with a list
image: listStopsImpulse
The single best habit: shop with a **shopping list** built from your meal plan, and stick to it. A list keeps you focused and slashes **impulse buys** — the unplanned extras (often placed at eye level and by the checkout) that quietly inflate the bill. Shopping when hungry is a classic trap, since everything looks tempting.

## Card: Get value, not just low prices
Compare using the **unit price** (price per 100g or per item), usually shown in small print — it reveals whether the bigger pack or the "offer" is genuinely cheaper, since it often isn't. Be wary of "deals" that make you buy more than you'll use. Cheaper own-brand versions are frequently identical to pricier names.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: shopping without a list and buying on impulse; shopping hungry; falling for "offers" that aren't real savings per unit; buying perishables in bulk that then spoil; and assuming pricier brands are better. Smart shopping is mostly about a list, the unit price, and resisting the shop's nudges.

## Card: Quick recap
Shop smart with a list from your meal plan and stick to it, avoiding impulse buys (and don't shop hungry). Compare unit prices to find true value, be skeptical of "offers" that push you to overbuy, and try own-brands. These simple habits cut your grocery bill and waste without any real sacrifice.
