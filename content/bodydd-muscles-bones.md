## Card: What it is
image: musclesBonesHero
Your **bones** form the rigid framework (skeleton) that supports and protects your body, while your **muscles** pull on those bones to create movement. Together, the muscular and skeletal systems give you structure, protection, and the ability to move. They work as a team in nearly everything you physically do.

## Card: Why it matters
Muscles and bones let you move, stand, lift, and protect vital organs. Understanding them explains how movement happens, why exercise strengthens both, and why bone and muscle health matter as you age. It's the foundation for understanding fitness, injury, and physical wellbeing throughout life.

## Card: Key terms
- **Skeleton**: the body's framework of bones.
- **Muscle**: tissue that contracts to create movement.
- **Tendon**: connects muscle to bone.
- **Joint**: where bones meet and allow movement.

## Card: Bones: more than a frame
image: bonesLivingTissueHero
The **skeleton** does more than hold you up. Bones provide structure, protect organs (the skull shields the brain, ribs guard the heart and lungs), allow movement at **joints** (where bones meet), and even produce blood cells inside them. Bones are living tissue — they grow, repair, and remodel throughout life, and they get stronger with use, which is why exercise benefits them.

## Card: Muscles: the movers
**Muscles** create movement by *contracting* (shortening) — they can pull but not push, so they often work in pairs (one pulls a joint one way, another pulls it back). Muscles connect to bones via **tendons**, so when a muscle contracts, it pulls the bone and you move. There are also muscles you don't consciously control, like the heart and those moving food through digestion. Muscles strengthen with use and weaken without it.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking bones are dead, dry structures (they're living tissue that grows and repairs); imagining muscles push (they only pull, hence working in pairs); not realizing muscles and bones strengthen with use and weaken without it; and forgetting some muscles work automatically. The muscular and skeletal systems are a living, adaptable team.

## Card: Quick recap
Bones form the living skeleton — supporting, protecting organs, enabling movement at joints, and even making blood cells. Muscles create movement by contracting (pulling, not pushing, so they work in pairs), connected to bones by tendons. Both are living and adaptable, strengthening with use and weakening without it — which is why exercise benefits them throughout life.
