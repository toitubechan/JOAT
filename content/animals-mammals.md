## Card: What it is
image: mammalsHero
**Mammals** are a group of animals that share key features: they're warm-blooded, usually have hair or fur, and feed their young milk. Humans are mammals, as are dogs, whales, bats, and elephants. Despite their huge variety, all mammals share these defining traits. They're among the most familiar animals to us.

## Card: Why it matters
We're mammals, and so are most of the animals we know best — pets, livestock, and many wild animals. Understanding what defines mammals helps you make sense of the animal kingdom and recognize surprising members (like whales and bats). It's a foundation for understanding animal biology and our own place in nature.

## Card: Key terms
- **Mammal**: a warm-blooded animal that feeds young milk.
- **Warm-blooded**: maintaining a constant body temperature.
- **Mammary glands**: produce milk for young (the group's namesake).
- **Diversity**: the wide range of forms mammals take.

## Card: What makes a mammal
Mammals share defining features: they're **warm-blooded** (maintaining a steady body temperature regardless of surroundings), most have **hair or fur**, they breathe air with lungs, most give birth to live young, and — the key trait — females have **mammary glands** that produce milk to feed their young (hence "mammal"). These shared features unite a hugely varied group.

## Card: Surprising diversity
image: mammalDiversityHero
Mammals are remarkably **diverse**. They include the largest animals ever (whales — yes, mammals, not fish), flying mammals (bats — the only mammals that truly fly), tiny mice, and giant elephants. They live on land, in oceans, and in the air, from poles to deserts. Despite this variety, every one shares the core mammalian traits — warm-blooded, milk-feeding, usually hairy. We humans are mammals too.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking whales and dolphins are fish (they're mammals — warm-blooded, breathe air, feed milk); assuming all mammals live on land (many are marine, and bats fly); not realizing humans are mammals; and thinking "mammal" means a specific look (the group is hugely varied). The defining traits — not appearance or habitat — are what make a mammal.

## Card: Quick recap
Mammals are warm-blooded animals that usually have hair or fur, breathe air, mostly give birth to live young, and feed their young milk (the defining trait). They're hugely diverse — from whales (mammals, not fish) and bats (flying mammals) to mice, elephants, and humans. Despite living on land, in oceans, and in the air, all share the core mammalian features.
