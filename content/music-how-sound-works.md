## Card: What it is
image: musicHowSoundWorksHero
**Sound** is a wave of vibrations traveling through the air (or another medium) that our ears detect and our brains interpret. Everything we hear — music, speech, noise — is these vibrations. Understanding how sound works reveals the physics behind hearing and music, explaining things like pitch, volume, and why sound behaves as it does. It's a foundation for understanding music and audio.

## Card: Why it matters
Sound is fundamental to music, communication, and daily life, yet how it actually works is rarely understood. Knowing the basics explains pitch and volume, how we hear, why sound needs a medium, and the physics behind music and audio technology. It's a satisfying piece of everyday science that connects music, hearing, and the physical world.

## Card: Key terms
- **Sound wave**: a vibration traveling through a medium.
- **Frequency**: how fast the wave vibrates, determining pitch.
- **Amplitude**: the size of the wave, determining volume.
- **Medium**: the material (like air) sound travels through.

## Card: Sound is a traveling wave
image: musicHowSoundWorksSoundIsATravelingWaveHero
**Sound** is created by vibration, which sends a **sound wave** traveling through a **medium** (usually air). The vibrating source pushes air molecules, which bump neighboring molecules, passing the vibration along as a wave until it reaches your ears, which detect it. Crucially, sound needs a medium — it can't travel through a vacuum (which is why space is silent). Sound isn't a substance that flies through the air; it's a wave of vibration passing *through* the air.

## Card: Pitch and volume
Two key properties come from the wave. **Frequency** — how fast the wave vibrates — determines **pitch**: high frequency (fast vibration) is a high-pitched sound, low frequency a low one. **Amplitude** — the size or intensity of the wave — determines **volume**: bigger amplitude is louder, smaller is quieter. So a high, loud note is a fast, large wave; a low, quiet note is a slow, small one. These two properties explain much of what we hear in any sound, including music.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking sound is a substance that flies through the air (it's a wave of vibration passing through a medium); not knowing sound needs a medium (it can't travel through a vacuum — space is silent); confusing pitch (frequency) with volume (amplitude); and not connecting these to what we hear. Sound is a traveling vibration wave, with frequency setting pitch and amplitude setting volume.

## Card: Quick recap
Sound is a wave of vibration traveling through a medium (usually air) to our ears — not a substance flying through the air, and it can't travel through a vacuum (space is silent). Two key properties: frequency (vibration speed) sets pitch (fast = high), and amplitude (wave size) sets volume (bigger = louder). Understanding this reveals the physics behind hearing, music, and audio — connecting sound to the physical world.
