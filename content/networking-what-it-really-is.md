## Card: What it is
image: networkingHero
**Networking** is building and maintaining professional relationships. Despite its slick reputation, it's not about schmoozing, using people, or collecting business cards — at its best, it's simply getting to know people in a genuine, mutually helpful way. Reframing it this way makes networking far less intimidating and far more effective.

## Card: Why it matters
A large share of jobs and opportunities come through people, not job ads — the famous "hidden job market." Beyond jobs, a network offers advice, support, and ideas throughout your career. Many people avoid networking because they misunderstand it; learning what it really is unlocks one of the most valuable career assets.

## Card: Key terms
- **Networking**: building professional relationships over time.
- **Hidden job market**: roles filled through contacts, not adverts.
- **Mutual benefit**: relationships that help both sides.
- **Authentic**: genuine, not fake or transactional.

## Card: It's relationships, not transactions
The biggest myth is that networking is slimy and transactional. Done right, it's **authentic** — building real relationships based on **mutual benefit** and genuine interest in people. You're not extracting favors; you're connecting, helping where you can, and staying in touch. Think "make genuine professional friends," not "collect useful contacts."

## Card: Why it opens doors
image: relationshipDoorsHero
Many opportunities never get advertised — they're filled through who people know and trust (the **hidden job market**). A strong network means you hear about things early, get recommended, and have people to turn to for advice. Over a career, *who* you know often matters as much as *what* you know.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: thinking networking is sleazy and avoiding it entirely; treating it as transactional (only reaching out when you need something); collecting contacts you never genuinely connect with; and believing it's only for extroverts. Authentic, low-pressure relationship-building works for everyone, including the shy and introverted.

## Card: Quick recap
Networking is building genuine professional relationships, not schmoozing or collecting contacts. It matters because many opportunities flow through people (the hidden job market) and a network offers lifelong advice and support. Approach it authentically and with mutual benefit — think "make professional friends," and it stops being intimidating.
