## Card: What it is
image: futuretechWearableTechHero
**Wearable tech** is electronic devices worn on the body — smartwatches, fitness trackers, smart glasses, health monitors, and more. They sense, track, and display information, often connecting to your phone. Increasingly common, they bring technology into closer, more constant contact with daily life and the body itself.

## Card: Why it matters
Wearables are now widespread, especially for health and fitness, and they're a growing way technology integrates into everyday life. Understanding them — their genuine benefits and the privacy considerations of body-worn sensors — helps you use them wisely and follow a developing field that increasingly blends technology with the human body.

## Card: Key terms
- **Wearable**: an electronic device worn on the body.
- **Sensors**: components that measure things like heart rate or steps.
- **Health tracking**: monitoring fitness and body metrics.
- **Data privacy**: protecting the personal data wearables collect.

## Card: What wearables do
image: futuretechWearableTechWhatWearablesDoHero
Wearables pack **sensors** into devices you wear, measuring things like steps, heart rate, sleep, and activity, then displaying or analyzing the data (often via a connected phone app). Common examples: smartwatches (notifications, apps, **health tracking**), fitness bands (activity and sleep), and health monitors. They put information and tracking on your wrist or body, making technology more constant and personal than a device in your pocket.

## Card: Benefits and privacy
Wearables offer real benefits — encouraging activity, tracking health trends, convenient notifications, and even alerting to potential health issues. But because they constantly collect personal and sometimes sensitive health data, **data privacy** is a genuine consideration: who can access your heart rate, location, and habits? Using wearables wisely means enjoying the benefits while being mindful of what data they collect and share. Body-worn tech makes privacy especially relevant.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: treating wearable health data as medical-grade certainty (it's useful for trends but not a substitute for medical advice); ignoring the privacy implications of constant body data collection; thinking wearables are only fitness gadgets (they span health, communication, and more); and over- or under-relying on their readings. Wearables are useful tools — best used wisely and with privacy in mind.

## Card: Quick recap
Wearable tech is body-worn electronics — smartwatches, fitness trackers, health monitors — packed with sensors that track steps, heart rate, sleep, and more, often via a connected phone. They offer real benefits for health, fitness, and convenience, but constantly collect personal data, making privacy a genuine consideration. Treat their health readings as useful trends, not medical certainty, and mind your data.
