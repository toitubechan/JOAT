## Card: What it is
image: hydrationHero
**Hydration** means having enough water in your body to function well. Water makes up most of you and is involved in nearly every bodily process — temperature control, digestion, circulation, and clearing waste. Staying hydrated is one of the simplest, most overlooked pillars of feeling good.

## Card: Why it matters
Even mild **dehydration** can cause tiredness, headaches, poor concentration, and low mood — symptoms people often blame on other things. Because thirst lags behind your actual needs, many people are mildly dehydrated without realizing. Getting hydration right is a cheap, easy win for energy and focus.

## Card: Key terms
- **Hydration**: having enough water in the body.
- **Dehydration**: not having enough water.
- **Thirst**: the body's signal it needs fluids (often delayed).
- **Electrolytes**: minerals that help balance body fluids.

## Card: How much and from where
image: waterSourcesHero
Needs vary by body, climate, and activity, so rigid numbers mislead — but water should be your main drink, topped up across the day. Food counts too: fruits and vegetables contain plenty of water. A practical guide is your urine color: pale yellow generally suggests good hydration, dark suggests you need more.

## Card: Thirst and timing
**Thirst** is a lagging signal — by the time you feel it, you're already a little low. So sip regularly rather than waiting to feel parched. You need more during exercise, hot weather, and illness. Heavy sweating also loses **electrolytes** (minerals), which is why intense, prolonged activity may need more than plain water.

## Card: Common mistakes
A common mistake is relying on thirst alone, staying mildly dehydrated all day. Another is getting most fluids from sugary or heavily caffeinated drinks rather than water. And some swing the other way, force-drinking far more than needed; balance is the goal, guided by your body and urine color, not extreme rules.

## Card: Quick recap
Hydration keeps nearly every body process running, and even mild dehydration saps energy and focus. Make water your main drink, sip through the day rather than waiting for thirst, and let urine color guide you. Drink more when hot, active, or ill. It's a simple, high-impact health habit.
