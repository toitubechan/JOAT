## Card: What it is
image: dataProbabilityWhatItIsHero
**Probability** is the measure of how likely something is to happen, usually expressed as a number from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain), or as a percentage. It's how we reason about uncertainty — weather, games, risks, decisions. Understanding probability helps you think clearly about chance in everyday life.

## Card: Why it matters
We constantly deal with uncertainty, and our intuition about chance is often badly wrong. Understanding probability helps you judge risks, make better decisions, avoid gambling traps, and interpret "70% chance of rain" correctly. It's a practical tool for thinking clearly in an uncertain world, where gut feelings frequently mislead.

## Card: Key terms
- **Probability**: the likelihood of an event, from 0 to 1.
- **Certain / impossible**: probability 1 / probability 0.
- **Outcome**: a possible result of a situation.
- **Likelihood**: how probable something is.

## Card: The scale of likelihood
image: dataProbabilityWhatItIsTheScaleOfLikelihoodHero
**Probability** runs from 0 to 1 (or 0% to 100%). **0** means impossible; **1** (100%) means certain; **0.5** (50%) means equally likely to happen or not, like a coin flip. Everything else falls in between. This simple scale lets us put a number on how likely an **outcome** is, turning vague feelings ("probably", "unlikely") into something more precise and comparable.

## Card: Probability isn't a guarantee
A crucial point: probability describes **likelihood**, not certainty. A 90% chance still fails 1 time in 10; a 10% chance still happens sometimes. Probability tells you what's *likely* over many tries, not what *will* happen in one instance. This is why a low-probability event occurring isn't "impossible" or proof the odds were wrong — unlikely things happen regularly, given enough chances.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: treating a high probability as a guarantee (90% still fails sometimes); thinking a low-probability event occurring means the odds were wrong (unlikely things do happen); confusing "probable" with "certain"; and trusting gut feelings about chance, which are often very wrong. Probability is about likelihood over many tries, not certainty in one.

## Card: Quick recap
Probability measures how likely something is, from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain), with 0.5 meaning a coin-flip chance. It describes likelihood over many tries, not a guarantee — a 90% chance still fails sometimes, and unlikely events do happen. Understanding this helps you reason about uncertainty far better than gut feeling, which is often badly wrong.
