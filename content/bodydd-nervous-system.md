## Card: What it is
image: nervousSystemDeepDiveHero
The **nervous system** is the body's communication and control network — the brain, spinal cord, and a vast web of nerves reaching everywhere. It carries electrical signals at high speed, letting you sense the world, move, think, and run automatic functions like breathing. It's how your body coordinates everything it does.

## Card: Why it matters
The nervous system controls and coordinates the entire body and underlies all sensation, movement, and thought. Understanding it explains how you react, why reflexes are fast, and how the conscious and automatic parts of your body work. It's central to understanding everything from injury to how the brain and body connect.

## Card: Key terms
- **Central nervous system**: the brain and spinal cord.
- **Peripheral nerves**: the nerves reaching the rest of the body.
- **Reflex**: an automatic, near-instant response.
- **Autonomic**: the part controlling automatic functions.

## Card: Central and peripheral
The nervous system has two main parts. The **central nervous system** — the brain and spinal cord — is the control hub that processes information and makes decisions. The **peripheral nerves** branch out to the entire body, carrying signals *in* from the senses and *out* to muscles and organs. Together they form a high-speed network linking your control center to every part of you.

## Card: Conscious, automatic, and reflexes
image: reflexShortcutHero
The nervous system handles both conscious actions (deciding to move) and **autonomic** functions you never think about — heartbeat, breathing, digestion — running automatically in the background. **Reflexes** are the fastest responses: touch something hot and your hand jerks back *before* the signal even reaches conscious awareness, because it takes a shortcut through the spinal cord. This split between deliberate and automatic control is key to how the body operates.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking the nervous system is just the brain (it includes the spinal cord and nerves throughout the body); imagining all responses are conscious (much is automatic); not knowing reflexes bypass the brain for speed; and underrating how fast nerve signals travel. The nervous system is a body-wide, high-speed communication network, not just the brain.

## Card: Quick recap
The nervous system is the body's high-speed communication network: the central nervous system (brain and spinal cord) processes and decides, while peripheral nerves carry signals to and from the whole body. It handles conscious actions and automatic (autonomic) functions like breathing, and reflexes take spinal shortcuts for speed. It coordinates everything you sense, do, and think.
