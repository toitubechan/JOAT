## Card: What it is
image: developingEyeHero
**Developing your eye** means training yourself to perceive and judge visual quality — to notice *why* designs work or don't, and to see details and choices most people overlook. It's a learnable skill that underlies all visual creativity. A "good eye" isn't a mysterious gift you're born with; it develops through attention and practice. Understanding how to develop it empowers your growth in any visual field.

## Card: Why it matters
A "good eye" is what lets designers, photographers, and artists make great choices and judge their own work — and it's often assumed to be innate talent. Understanding that it's *developed*, not inborn, is empowering: it means you can improve your visual judgment through practice. Developing your eye underlies improvement in all visual creativity and enriches how you see the visual world.

## Card: Key terms
- **Developing your eye**: training your visual perception and judgment.
- **Visual literacy**: the ability to "read" and evaluate visuals.
- **Active observation**: deliberately noticing and analyzing what you see.
- **Practice**: improving through doing and studying.

## Card: A developed skill, not a gift
The key insight: a "good eye" — strong **visual literacy** — is *developed*, not a born gift. Just as you can learn to read words, you can learn to "read" visuals: to notice composition, color, hierarchy, and the choices behind a design, and to judge what works. This develops through exposure and attention over time. Believing it's innate talent stops people from trying; understanding it's learnable frees you to develop it deliberately, like any skill.

## Card: How to develop it
image: practiceObservationHero
You develop your eye through **active observation** and **practice**. Actively study visuals you encounter — don't just glance, but ask *why* a design works or fails, what choices were made, how it uses the principles you've learned. Expose yourself to lots of good design and analyze it. Practice creating, and critique your own work and others'. Over time, this trains your perception: you start noticing details and quality others miss, and your judgment sharpens. Attention plus practice, consistently, develops the eye.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: believing a "good eye" is innate talent you either have or don't (it's developed through practice); passively glancing at visuals rather than actively analyzing them; not studying good design deliberately; and expecting visual judgment to appear without effort. Developing your eye is a learnable skill, built through active observation, studying good work, and consistent practice over time.

## Card: Quick recap
Developing your eye means training your visual perception and judgment — learning to notice why designs work and to see details others miss. It's a developed skill (visual literacy), not an innate gift: you can learn to "read" visuals like you learned to read words. Develop it through active observation (asking *why* designs work), studying lots of good design, and practicing and critiquing. Attention plus practice, over time, sharpens the eye.
