## Card: What it is
image: mathProbabilityVsStatisticsHero
**Probability** and **statistics** are closely related but distinct fields for dealing with uncertainty and data. Roughly: probability predicts the chance of outcomes from known rules (looking *forward*), while statistics draws conclusions from observed data (looking *backward*). Understanding the difference clears up two terms often confused, and reveals how we reason about chance and data.

## Card: Why it matters
Probability and statistics underpin science, medicine, finance, polling, and everyday decision-making about uncertainty. They're often muddled together. Understanding how they differ — predicting from rules versus learning from data — sharpens your thinking about chance, evidence, and the numbers you encounter constantly in news and life.

## Card: Key terms
- **Probability**: predicting outcomes from known rules.
- **Statistics**: drawing conclusions from observed data.
- **Forward vs. backward**: predicting outcomes vs. inferring from results.
- **Uncertainty**: incomplete knowledge that both fields handle.

## Card: Probability — predicting forward
image: mathProbabilityVsStatisticsProbabilityPredictingForwardHero
**Probability** starts with known rules and predicts the chance of outcomes. If you know a coin is fair, probability tells you the chance of getting heads (50%). It works *forward*: from a known setup to the likelihood of results. It answers "given these rules, what's likely to happen?" — useful for games of chance, risk, and modeling situations where the underlying odds are known.

## Card: Statistics — reasoning backward
**Statistics** works the other way — *backward*. It starts with observed **data** (real-world results) and draws conclusions about what's going on. If you flip a mysterious coin 1,000 times and get heads 700 times, statistics helps you infer the coin probably isn't fair. It answers "given this data, what can we conclude?" — used to analyze experiments, surveys, and real-world evidence where the underlying truth is unknown and must be inferred.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: using "probability" and "statistics" interchangeably (they're related but distinct); not grasping the forward/backward distinction (probability predicts from rules; statistics infers from data); thinking either gives certainty (both handle uncertainty, not guarantees); and confusing which applies. Probability predicts outcomes from known rules; statistics draws conclusions from observed data.

## Card: Quick recap
Probability and statistics both handle uncertainty but in opposite directions. Probability works *forward* — predicting the chance of outcomes from known rules (a fair coin gives 50% heads). Statistics works *backward* — drawing conclusions from observed data (1,000 flips landing mostly heads suggests the coin isn't fair). They're related but distinct, underpinning how we reason about chance, data, and evidence everywhere.
