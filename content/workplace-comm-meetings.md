## Card: What it is
image: meetingCommunicationHero
**Meetings** are where much workplace communication happens collectively — and where many people struggle, either dominating or staying silent. Knowing how to contribute well, speak up effectively, and not waste others' time makes you a valued participant and raises your visibility.

## Card: Why it matters
Meetings are highly visible — how you show up there shapes how colleagues and managers see you. Speaking up well gets your ideas heard and credited; staying silent means good ideas go unshared and you fade into the background. Yet many capable people hold back, missing chances to contribute and be noticed.

## Card: Key terms
- **Speaking up**: voicing your ideas and input.
- **Preparation**: thinking through your points beforehand.
- **Concise contribution**: making your point briefly and clearly.
- **Active participation**: engaging rather than passively sitting.

## Card: Speak up effectively
image: speakEffectivelyHero
Many hold back from **speaking up** out of fear their idea isn't good enough. But unshared ideas help no one, and contributing is how you're noticed. The trick is to make a **concise contribution** — say your point clearly and briefly, rather than over-explaining or apologizing for it. **Preparation** helps: think through your key points before the meeting so you're ready to contribute.

## Card: Participate without dominating
Good meeting communication is balanced. **Active participation** means listening, building on others' points, and asking questions — not just waiting to talk or, worse, dominating. If you tend to be quiet, aim to make at least one solid contribution. If you tend to dominate, practice making space for others. Both extremes weaken the meeting.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: staying silent and never sharing ideas; the opposite, dominating and not letting others speak; rambling instead of making a concise point; not preparing, then having nothing to add; and multitasking (visibly checking your phone) instead of engaging. Aim for prepared, concise, balanced participation.

## Card: Quick recap
Meetings are visible, so how you show up matters. Speak up — unshared ideas help no one — but keep contributions concise and prepare your key points beforehand. Participate actively by listening and building on others, without dominating. If you're usually quiet, contribute at least once; if you dominate, make space for others.
