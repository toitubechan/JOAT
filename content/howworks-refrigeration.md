## Card: What it is
image: howworksRefrigerationHero
**Refrigeration** is how fridges, freezers, and air conditioners make things cold. The surprising key: they don't "create cold" — they *move heat* from inside to outside. By pumping heat away from where you don't want it, they leave the inside cool. Understanding this flips a common intuition and reveals the clever physics behind everyday cooling.

## Card: Why it matters
Refrigeration is one of the most important everyday technologies — it keeps food safe, cools our homes, and transformed how we live and eat. Understanding how it works reveals an elegant principle (moving heat, not making cold) that explains fridges, freezers, and air conditioning all at once. It's a satisfying everyday physics insight.

## Card: Key terms
- **Refrigeration**: moving heat to make something cold.
- **Refrigerant**: a fluid that carries heat in the system.
- **Heat transfer**: moving heat from one place to another.
- **Compressor**: the pump that drives the cooling cycle.

## Card: Moving heat, not making cold
image: howworksRefrigerationMovingHeatNotMakingColdHero
The core idea: there's no such thing as "cold" to add — there's only heat to remove. **Refrigeration** works by **heat transfer**: a special fluid (**refrigerant**) flows through the system, absorbing heat from inside the fridge and carrying it outside, where it releases that heat. A **compressor** pumps the refrigerant around this cycle. So a fridge cools its inside by continuously moving heat *out* — which is why the back of a fridge feels warm.

## Card: The same principle everywhere
This one principle explains many appliances. A **freezer** moves even more heat out to reach freezing temperatures. An **air conditioner** does the same for a room — moving heat from inside to outside (which is why the outdoor unit blows warm air). Even a heat pump uses the same cycle, run in reverse, to *heat* a home. Once you grasp "move heat, don't make cold," all of these make sense.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking a fridge "creates cold" (it removes heat — there's no "cold" to add); not realizing the heat removed goes somewhere (out the back, which is why it's warm); confusing cooling with making cold air from nothing; and not seeing that fridges, freezers, and AC all use the same principle. Refrigeration is heat *removal*, not cold *creation*.

## Card: Quick recap
Refrigeration makes things cold by *moving heat*, not creating cold — there's no "cold" to add, only heat to remove. A refrigerant fluid, pumped by a compressor, absorbs heat from inside and releases it outside (hence the warm back of a fridge). The same principle powers freezers, air conditioners, and even heat pumps. Grasp "move heat, don't make cold," and all cooling tech makes sense.
