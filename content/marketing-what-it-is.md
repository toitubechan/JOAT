## Card: What it is
image: marketingWhatItIsHero
**Marketing** is often misunderstood as just advertising or pushy selling, but it's much broader: it's understanding what people want and connecting them with something that meets that need. At its best, marketing is about creating and communicating genuine value to the right people. Understanding what marketing *really* is reframes it from manipulation to meeting needs.

## Card: Why it matters
Marketing affects every business and surrounds us daily, yet it's widely misunderstood and often disliked. Understanding what it truly is — meeting needs and communicating value, not just ads or tricks — helps you market honestly and effectively, and see through poor or manipulative marketing. It's essential for any business and useful for understanding the world.

## Card: Key terms
- **Marketing**: connecting people with something that meets their needs.
- **Value**: the genuine benefit something provides.
- **Customer focus**: centering on the customer's needs, not just your product.
- **The 4 Ps**: product, price, place, promotion — the marketing mix.

## Card: More than advertising
image: marketingWhatItIsMoreThanAdvertisingHero
Marketing is much bigger than advertising (which is just one part). It encompasses understanding customers, creating the right product, pricing it, getting it to people, and communicating about it. A classic framework is the **4 Ps**: *Product* (what you offer), *Price* (what you charge), *Place* (where/how it's available), and *Promotion* (how you communicate it). Advertising is only the last P — real marketing is the whole effort of connecting a valuable offering with the people who want it.

## Card: It starts with the customer
Good marketing has a **customer focus** — it starts not with "how do I sell my product?" but with "what do people actually need and want?" When you genuinely understand your customers and offer real **value** that meets their needs, marketing becomes about *connecting* the right people with something that genuinely helps them, not tricking them. This is why the best marketing feels helpful rather than pushy: it's grounded in real customer needs and honest value.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: equating marketing with just advertising or pushy selling (it's far broader — understanding and meeting needs); starting from the product instead of the customer; thinking marketing means manipulation (good marketing communicates genuine value); and ignoring most of the marketing mix beyond promotion. Real marketing is connecting people with value that meets their needs — customer-focused and honest.

## Card: Quick recap
Marketing isn't just advertising or pushy selling — it's the broad effort of understanding what people want and connecting them with something that genuinely meets that need. It spans the whole "4 Ps" (product, price, place, promotion), not just ads. The best marketing starts with the customer's needs and communicates real value, feeling helpful rather than manipulative. Understanding this reframes marketing from trickery to meeting needs.
