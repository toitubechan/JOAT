## Card: What it is
image: hormonesEndocrineHero
**Hormones** are chemical messengers that travel through the blood to control and coordinate body processes — from growth and metabolism to mood, sleep, and stress. They're produced by the **endocrine system**, a set of glands. Hormones quietly run much of what happens in your body, often without you realizing.

## Card: Why it matters
Hormones influence enormous parts of life — energy, mood, hunger, sleep, growth, stress, and more. Understanding them explains why you feel certain ways, how the body regulates itself, and why hormonal balance matters for health. They're behind many everyday experiences and a key part of how the body coordinates itself.

## Card: Key terms
- **Hormone**: a chemical messenger carried in the blood.
- **Endocrine system**: the glands that produce hormones.
- **Gland**: an organ that releases hormones.
- **Regulation**: keeping body processes balanced.

## Card: Chemical messengers
image: chemicalMessengerHero
The **endocrine system** is a network of **glands** that release **hormones** into the bloodstream. Each hormone travels to specific parts of the body and triggers a response — like a chemical message. Unlike the fast electrical signals of the nervous system, hormones work more slowly but can have widespread, lasting effects. Together, the nervous and endocrine systems are the body's two great coordination systems.

## Card: What hormones control
Hormones **regulate** a huge range of functions: growth and development, **metabolism** (how you use energy), hunger and fullness, sleep cycles, the stress response (adrenaline and cortisol), reproduction, and mood. The body constantly adjusts hormone levels to stay in balance. When hormones are out of balance, it can affect energy, mood, weight, and health — which is why the endocrine system is so important.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking hormones only relate to puberty or reproduction (they control metabolism, mood, sleep, stress, and much more); imagining they act instantly like nerves (they're slower but far-reaching); not realizing how widely they affect daily life; and underrating the link between hormones and mood or energy. Hormones are the body's pervasive chemical coordination system.

## Card: Quick recap
Hormones are chemical messengers, made by the endocrine system's glands and carried in the blood to coordinate body processes — growth, metabolism, hunger, sleep, stress, reproduction, and mood. Slower than nerve signals but far-reaching, they keep the body in balance. Alongside the nervous system, the endocrine system is one of the body's two great coordination networks.
