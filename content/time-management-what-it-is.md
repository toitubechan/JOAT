## Card: What it is
image: growthTimeWhatItIsHero
**Time management** is the skill of using your time deliberately — choosing what to work on, when, and for how long. Despite the name, it's less about squeezing every minute and more about making good *choices* with your limited hours. Everyone gets the same 24 hours; management is about how you spend them.

## Card: Why it matters
Time is the one resource you can't make more of, yet it's easily frittered away. Good time management means more gets done with less stress, and more room for what matters to you. It's not about being busy — it's about being effective, and protecting time for what's genuinely important.

## Card: Key terms
- **Priority**: what matters most and deserves time first.
- **Productivity**: getting meaningful things done, not just being busy.
- **Time blocking**: assigning specific tasks to specific time slots.
- **Busywork**: low-value activity that feels productive but isn't.

## Card: Busy isn't the same as effective
image: growthTimeWhatItIsBusyIsnTTheSameAsEffectiveHero
A core insight: being **busy** is not the same as being **productive**. You can fill every hour and achieve little, lost in **busywork** — emails, small tasks, reacting to whatever shouts loudest. Real time management is about doing the *right* things, not merely many things. Effectiveness beats mere activity.

## Card: It's about choices
Since you can't add hours, time management is really *decision* management: choosing what deserves your time and what doesn't. Saying yes to one thing means saying no to another. The people who seem to "have more time" usually just make sharper choices about where theirs goes — guarding it for what matters.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: confusing busyness with progress; reacting to whatever's loudest instead of what's important; saying yes to everything, leaving no time for priorities; and trying to do everything rather than choosing. Time management isn't about cramming more in — it's about choosing better what goes in at all.

## Card: Quick recap
Time management is using your limited hours deliberately — really a matter of choices, not the clock. Busy isn't the same as productive; doing the right things beats doing many. Since you can't make more time, it's about deciding what deserves it. Guard your hours for what genuinely matters.
