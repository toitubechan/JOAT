## Card: What it is
image: sustainRecyclingProperlyHero
**Recycling** is processing used materials into new products instead of throwing them away. But it's more complicated than tossing things in a green bin — what's actually recyclable varies by location and material, and doing it wrong can do more harm than good. Recycling *properly* is a skill worth learning.

## Card: Why it matters
Recycling saves resources and energy when done right — but well-meaning mistakes can contaminate whole batches, sending them to landfill anyway. Understanding what genuinely gets recycled, and how, makes your effort actually count rather than backfire. It turns good intentions into real impact.

## Card: Key terms
- **Contamination**: non-recyclable items spoiling a recycling batch.
- **Wishcycling**: tossing in things hoping they're recyclable.
- **Sorting**: separating materials correctly.
- **Local rules**: what your specific area accepts.

## Card: Wishcycling backfires
image: sustainRecyclingProperlyWishcyclingBackfiresHero
A surprising problem: **wishcycling** — putting questionable items in recycling "just in case" — can cause **contamination**, where non-recyclable items spoil an entire batch so it all goes to landfill. A greasy pizza box or wrong plastic can ruin a load. When unsure, it's often better to bin an item than to risk contaminating the recycling. Recycling right beats recycling hopefully.

## Card: Know your local rules
What's recyclable varies hugely by **location** — different areas accept different materials and have different rules (rinsing, separating, which plastics). There's no universal answer. Check your *local* recycling guidelines rather than assuming. Common tips: rinse food residue off containers, and don't bag recyclables unless told to. Knowing your specific area's rules is what makes recycling effective.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: wishcycling (causing contamination); not rinsing food off containers; assuming all plastics are recyclable (many aren't); not checking local rules; and treating recycling as a cure-all when reducing and reusing matter more. Recycle the right things, the right way, for your area — and remember it's the last resort, after reducing and reusing.

## Card: Quick recap
Recycling properly means knowing what your local area actually accepts and following its rules (rinsing, sorting). Avoid wishcycling — tossing in hopeful items can contaminate a whole batch into landfill. When unsure, check or bin it. Recycling is valuable but it's the last resort, after reducing and reusing. Done right, your effort genuinely counts.
