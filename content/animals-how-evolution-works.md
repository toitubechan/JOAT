## Card: What it is
image: animalsEvolutionHero
**Evolution** is how living things change over many generations, producing the incredible diversity of life — from the tiniest insect to the largest whale. Its main engine is **natural selection**. Understanding evolution is the foundation for understanding all of animal life and how the living world came to be as it is.

## Card: Why it matters
Evolution is the unifying idea of biology, explaining why animals are so diverse yet related, how they're adapted to their environments, and how new species arise. It makes sense of the entire animal kingdom. Understanding it transforms a zoo of seemingly random creatures into a connected family tree shaped by understandable forces.

## Card: Key terms
- **Evolution**: change in living things across generations.
- **Natural selection**: survival and reproduction of the better-suited.
- **Adaptation**: a trait that helps survival.
- **Common ancestor**: a shared evolutionary origin.

## Card: How natural selection works
image: naturalSelectionAnimalsHero
The logic is simple but powerful. Individuals within a species **vary**. Some variations help survival and reproduction in a given environment. Those individuals tend to have more offspring, passing on the helpful traits. Over many generations, helpful traits spread — the species has evolved. This is **natural selection**: nature "selecting" what works, not by design, but by who survives and reproduces.

## Card: Adaptation and shared ancestry
Over time, natural selection produces **adaptations** — traits exquisitely suited to a way of life (a bird's wings, a fish's gills, camouflage). Given enough time, populations change so much they become new species. And all animals share **common ancestors** — trace life back far enough, and every creature connects on one vast tree of life. The diversity of animals is the result of billions of years of this branching evolution.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking an individual evolves in its lifetime (evolution acts on populations over generations); imagining animals evolve "on purpose" or by trying (variation is undirected; selection isn't intentional); thinking evolution is "just a theory" in the casual sense (it's a well-supported scientific explanation); and seeing species as fixed and unrelated (all life shares ancestry). Evolution is the elegant explanation for life's diversity.

## Card: Quick recap
Evolution is how life changes over generations, driven by natural selection: individuals vary, the better-suited survive and reproduce more, and helpful traits spread. Over time this produces adaptations and new species, all connected through common ancestors on one tree of life. It's biology's unifying explanation, turning the diversity of animals into a connected, understandable story.
