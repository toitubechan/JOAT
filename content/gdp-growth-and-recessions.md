## Card: What it is
image: econGdpGrowthAndRecessionsHero
Economies don't grow in a straight line — they move in **cycles** of growth and contraction. A period of falling output is a **recession**. Understanding this up-and-down rhythm, and what recessions are, explains the economic ups and downs you'll experience and hear about throughout your life.

## Card: Why it matters
Recessions affect jobs, businesses, and everyday finances, and they recur throughout history. Understanding the economic cycle helps you make sense of "boom and bust" news, prepare for downturns, and avoid panic — recessions are a normal (if painful) part of how economies work, and they're followed by recovery.

## Card: Key terms
- **Economic cycle**: the recurring pattern of growth and contraction.
- **Recession**: a period of significant economic decline.
- **Boom**: a period of strong growth.
- **Recovery**: the return to growth after a downturn.

## Card: The economic cycle
image: econGdpGrowthAndRecessionsTheEconomicCycleHero
Economies move through an **economic cycle**: periods of **boom** (strong growth, low unemployment, rising output) followed by slowdowns and sometimes **recessions** (shrinking output), then **recovery** and growth again. This up-and-down pattern recurs over years. It's driven by many factors — confidence, spending, shocks — and while the timing is unpredictable, the *cyclical* nature is a persistent feature.

## Card: What a recession means
A **recession** is a significant, sustained decline in economic activity (often defined as GDP shrinking for a couple of consecutive quarters). In a recession, businesses earn less, some cut jobs, spending falls, and times get harder. It's painful but normal and temporary — recessions are followed by recovery. Understanding this helps avoid panic and informs sensible preparation, like having savings.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking growth should be constant (economies are cyclical); believing a recession means permanent collapse (they're temporary, followed by recovery); panicking at every downturn; and not preparing for the cycle's inevitable dips. Recessions are a recurring, normal part of the economic cycle, not a one-off catastrophe.

## Card: Quick recap
Economies move in cycles: booms of strong growth, then slowdowns and sometimes recessions (shrinking output), then recovery. Recessions are significant, sustained declines — painful but normal and temporary, always followed by recovery. Expecting the cycle's ups and downs helps you avoid panic and prepare sensibly, like keeping savings for leaner times.
