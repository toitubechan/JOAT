## Card: What it is
image: negotiationPreparingHero
**Preparation** is the work you do before a negotiation — knowing what you want, what the other side wants, and your alternatives. It's the single biggest factor in negotiating well. The better-prepared side usually does better, regardless of who's naturally more confident or charismatic.

## Card: Why it matters
Going in unprepared means reacting on the spot, accepting whatever's offered, or being talked out of your position. Preparation gives you confidence, clarity, and leverage. Most negotiation outcomes are shaped before anyone sits down — by who did their homework. It turns nerves into a steady, informed position.

## Card: Key terms
- **Target**: the outcome you're aiming for.
- **Walk-away point**: the worst terms you'd accept.
- **BATNA**: your Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement.
- **Research**: knowing the facts and the other side's position.

## Card: Know your numbers
Before negotiating, decide three things: your **target** (what you want), your **walk-away point** (the worst you'd accept before walking), and ideally your aim to open. Knowing these keeps you from being pushed below your limit or anchored by the other side's first offer. Clarity on your own numbers is the foundation of a strong position.

## Card: Know your BATNA
image: batnaNumbersHero
Your **BATNA** — Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement — is what you'll do if no deal is reached. A strong BATNA (another job offer, another buyer) is your greatest source of leverage, because you can walk away. Knowing your alternative removes desperation and lets you negotiate from strength. The side that can walk away usually holds the power.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: walking in with no target or walk-away point, so you accept whatever's offered; not knowing your BATNA, leaving you desperate; failing to research (market rates, the other side's needs); and revealing your walk-away point too early. Do the homework, and you negotiate from clarity and strength rather than nerves.

## Card: Quick recap
Preparation decides most negotiations. Know your target and your walk-away point, research the facts and the other side's needs, and understand your BATNA — your alternative if no deal happens, which is your real leverage. The prepared side, able to walk away calmly, usually wins, regardless of who's more naturally confident.
