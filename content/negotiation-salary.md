## Card: What it is
image: salaryNegotiationHero
**Salary negotiation** is discussing your pay — whether for a new job offer or a raise. It's one of the highest-value negotiations most people ever have, yet many skip it out of fear. A single successful negotiation can be worth a great deal, and its effect compounds over a career.

## Card: Why it matters
Failing to negotiate salary can cost enormous sums over time, because future raises and offers often build on your current pay. Many people accept the first number out of discomfort, leaving money on the table. Learning to negotiate pay calmly and reasonably is one of the most financially impactful career skills there is.

## Card: Key terms
- **Market rate**: the typical pay for a role and experience level.
- **Anchor**: the first number stated, which shapes the range.
- **Total compensation**: salary plus benefits and perks.
- **Counteroffer**: a response proposing different terms.

## Card: Know your worth
image: knowWorthHero
Before negotiating, research the **market rate** for the role, your experience, and location — this gives you a factual, confident basis rather than a guess. Knowing the typical range means you can ask for a reasonable number and justify it. Employers expect some negotiation; a well-researched, polite request rarely offends and often succeeds.

## Card: How to negotiate pay
When given an offer, it's usually fine to ask if there's flexibility rather than instantly accepting. Aim a little high but reasonably (the first number can **anchor** the range). Consider **total compensation** — benefits, bonuses, flexibility, time off — not just base salary, since these have real value and may be negotiable when salary isn't. Stay polite, positive, and factual throughout.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: accepting the first offer without asking (the most common and costly); not researching the market rate, so you ask blindly; focusing only on base salary and ignoring total compensation; negotiating aggressively or apologetically rather than calmly; and naming a number too low, anchoring yourself down. A polite, researched ask is the sweet spot.

## Card: Quick recap
Salary negotiation is high-value and often skipped from fear, costing people dearly over time. Research the market rate to ask confidently, consider total compensation (not just base pay), and politely ask about flexibility rather than instantly accepting. Employers expect it. A calm, well-researched request frequently pays off — sometimes hugely.
