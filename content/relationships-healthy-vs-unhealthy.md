## Card: What it is
image: psychRelationshipsHealthyVsUnhealthyHero
Not all relationships are good for us. Knowing the signs of **healthy** versus **unhealthy** relationships helps you recognize which connections support you and which drain or harm you. This applies to all relationships — friendships, family, romantic, and work — and is important self-protective knowledge.

## Card: Why it matters
Unhealthy relationships harm wellbeing, sometimes seriously, yet their signs can be hard to spot from the inside — especially when they develop gradually. Knowing the signs helps you recognize problems, set boundaries, seek support, or step away if needed. This is genuinely important knowledge; if you're in a harmful situation, reaching out to trusted people or professionals is wise.

## Card: Key terms
- **Healthy relationship**: one built on respect, trust, and mutual support.
- **Unhealthy relationship**: one marked by disrespect, control, or harm.
- **Red flag**: a warning sign of an unhealthy dynamic.
- **Mutual support**: both people genuinely supporting each other.

## Card: Signs of a healthy relationship
image: psychRelationshipsHealthyVsUnhealthySignsOfAHealthyRelationshipHero
**Healthy relationships** share clear signs: mutual respect and trust, open communication, **mutual support** (both genuinely there for each other), respected boundaries, the freedom to be yourself, and the ability to disagree without fear. You feel generally safe, valued, and energized. Healthy relationships have ups and downs, but the overall pattern is supportive and respectful.

## Card: Signs of an unhealthy relationship
**Unhealthy relationships** show warning signs (**red flags**): persistent disrespect, control or manipulation, broken trust, contempt, one-sided effort, feeling drained, anxious, or "walked on eggshells" around the person, and not being able to be yourself. A single rough patch isn't the issue — it's persistent harmful patterns. If a relationship consistently diminishes you, that's a serious sign worth heeding.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking healthy means conflict-free (it means handled well); ignoring red flags or excusing them; assuming you can "fix" someone or a harmful dynamic alone; and not seeking outside perspective when something feels wrong. If a relationship is consistently harmful, recognizing it — and seeking support from trusted people or professionals — is important and wise.

## Card: Quick recap
Healthy relationships show respect, trust, mutual support, respected boundaries, and the freedom to be yourself — you feel safe and valued. Unhealthy ones show red flags: control, disrespect, broken trust, one-sided effort, and feeling drained or on eggshells. A rough patch isn't the issue; persistent harmful patterns are. If consistently harmed, seek support — that's wise, not weak.
