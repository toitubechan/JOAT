## Card: What it is
image: cutsBurnsHero
Cuts, bleeding, and burns are among the most common injuries you'll encounter. Knowing the basic, widely-taught responses helps you act calmly and correctly. This is general educational guidance — serious wounds, deep burns, or anything you're unsure about need professional medical care, so call for help.

## Card: Why it matters
Acting correctly in the first moments can reduce harm, control bleeding, and ease a burn. Doing the *wrong* thing — like a common burn myth — can make injuries worse. A little accurate knowledge means you help rather than accidentally harm, and know when an injury is beyond home care.

## Card: Key terms
- **Direct pressure**: pressing firmly on a wound to slow bleeding.
- **Dressing**: a clean covering placed over a wound.
- **Severe bleeding**: heavy, fast bleeding — a medical emergency.
- **Cooling**: running cool water over a burn.

## Card: Cuts and bleeding
image: directPressure
For a bleeding wound, apply firm **direct pressure** with a clean cloth or **dressing**, and raise the area if you can. Keep pressing — don't keep lifting to peek. Most small cuts stop with pressure, then can be cleaned and covered. **Severe bleeding** that won't stop, spurts, or is heavy is an emergency: call for help and keep applying pressure.

## Card: Treating burns
For a minor burn, **cool it** under cool (not ice-cold) running water for around 20 minutes, then cover loosely with a clean, non-fluffy covering or cling film. Remove tight items like rings before swelling starts. **Don't** apply butter, creams, ice, or burst blisters — these are myths that worsen burns. Serious or large burns need urgent medical care.

## Card: Common mistakes
Classic mistakes: repeatedly lifting a dressing to check bleeding instead of maintaining pressure; putting butter, toothpaste, or ice on a burn (all harmful); bursting blisters; and underestimating serious injuries. Any deep, large, or won't-stop wound, or a burn that's big, deep, or on the face or hands, needs professional care.

## Card: Quick recap
For bleeding: firm direct pressure with a clean dressing, raise the area, and call for help if it's severe. For minor burns: cool under running water for about 20 minutes, cover loosely, and never use butter, ice, or burst blisters. Serious wounds and burns need urgent medical care.
