## Card: What it is
image: conflictMistakesHero
In conflict, certain reactions reliably make things worse — escalating, stonewalling, or attacking the person. This card set gathers the common conflict mistakes, so you can recognize and avoid them. Often, *not* doing the wrong thing matters more than doing the perfect thing.

## Card: Why it matters
Conflicts spiral mostly because of how people react, not the original disagreement. These mistakes turn solvable issues into damaged relationships. Knowing them helps you catch yourself before reacting badly. Since many are automatic, emotional responses, awareness is what gives you the chance to choose better.

## Card: Key terms
- **Escalation**: ramping up the intensity of conflict.
- **Stonewalling**: shutting down and refusing to engage.
- **Personal attacks**: criticizing the person, not the issue.
- **Winning vs. resolving**: trying to beat them vs. solving the problem.

## Card: The damaging reactions
image: damagingReactionsHero
The big destructive moves: **escalation** (raising your voice, getting aggressive); **personal attacks** (criticizing character — "you always," "you never") instead of the issue; **stonewalling** (shutting down, going silent, refusing to engage); and contempt (mockery, eye-rolling). These predictably worsen conflict and damage trust, regardless of who was originally "right."

## Card: The mindset mistakes
Beyond reactions, two mindset traps: trying to **win** rather than **resolve** — treating conflict as a battle, so even "winning" leaves resentment; and being unwilling to admit any fault or see the other's view, which keeps you stuck. The goal is a resolution both can live with, which sometimes means compromise or acknowledging you were partly wrong.

## Card: Common mistakes
To gather them: escalating and raising your voice; personal attacks on character; stonewalling and shutting down; dragging up old grievances; contempt and mockery; trying to win instead of resolve; and refusing to see the other side. The fix is consistent: stay calm, focus on the issue, listen, and aim to resolve, not win.

## Card: Quick recap
Conflicts worsen mostly through how we react: escalating, personal attacks, stonewalling, contempt, and trying to win rather than resolve. These damage relationships regardless of who was right. Catch yourself before reacting badly — stay calm, focus on the issue not the person, listen, and aim for a resolution both can live with.
