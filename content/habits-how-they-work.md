## Card: What it is
image: growthHabitsHowTheyWorkHero
A **habit** is a behavior you do automatically, with little conscious thought, because you've repeated it many times. Brushing your teeth, checking your phone, your morning routine — all habits. A huge portion of daily behavior is habitual, which makes habits one of the most powerful forces shaping your life.

## Card: Why it matters
Because habits run automatically, they quietly determine your health, productivity, and happiness over time — for better or worse. Understanding how they form lets you build helpful ones and weaken harmful ones deliberately, rather than being run by autopilot. Small habit changes compound into large life changes.

## Card: Key terms
- **Habit**: an automatic, repeated behavior.
- **Autopilot**: acting without conscious decision.
- **Repetition**: doing something enough that it becomes automatic.
- **Compounding**: small actions adding up greatly over time.

## Card: Why we have habits
image: growthHabitsHowTheyWorkWhyWeHaveHabitsHero
The brain turns repeated actions into **habits** to save energy — it automates routines so it doesn't have to consciously decide every time. This is hugely efficient (imagine relearning to walk daily), but it's neutral: the brain automates *whatever* you repeat, good or bad. Habits are a tool, and you choose what you feed them.

## Card: Small habits, big results
The power of habits is **compounding**. A single instance of a habit barely matters — one cigarette, one workout, one saved euro. But repeated daily over months and years, tiny habits add up to dramatic differences. This is why focusing on small, consistent habits beats relying on occasional bursts of willpower.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: underestimating habits because each instance seems trivial; trying to change through willpower alone rather than habit design; expecting instant results instead of trusting compounding; and only noticing bad habits while ignoring the chance to build good ones. Habits are shaped by design, not just determination.

## Card: Quick recap
Habits are automatic behaviors formed by repetition, and they run a huge share of daily life. The brain automates whatever you repeat — good or bad — to save energy. Their power is compounding: tiny actions add up hugely over time. Shape habits deliberately, and small changes become big results.
