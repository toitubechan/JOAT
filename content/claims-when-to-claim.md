## Card: What it is
image: whenToClaimHero
Not every loss is worth claiming on insurance. Sometimes claiming can cost you more in the long run than paying yourself. Knowing **when to claim** — and when not to — is a surprisingly important skill that affects your future premiums and costs. (General guidance; weigh your own situation.)

## Card: Why it matters
People often assume they should claim for every covered loss, but small claims can backfire — raising future premiums or losing a no-claims discount by more than the payout is worth. Understanding when claiming makes sense helps you use insurance wisely, saving money over time. It's a subtlety many overlook.

## Card: Key terms
- **Deductible / excess**: what you pay before insurance pays.
- **No-claims discount**: a discount for not making claims.
- **Premium increase**: higher future costs after a claim.
- **Cost-benefit**: weighing the payout against the downsides.

## Card: When claiming may not be worth it
Consider a small loss just above your **deductible**. If you claim, you pay the deductible anyway, *and* you may lose a **no-claims discount** and face a **premium increase** at renewal — which over time can exceed the small payout. So for minor losses, paying out of pocket is sometimes cheaper overall. Always weigh the payout against the deductible plus the likely future cost.

## Card: When claiming makes sense
image: largeLossClaim
Claiming clearly makes sense for **large losses** you couldn't comfortably absorb yourself — which is exactly what insurance is for. The bigger the loss relative to your deductible and any premium impact, the more worthwhile the claim. The rule of thumb: claim for the big, genuinely unaffordable losses; think twice about small ones where the costs of claiming might outweigh the benefit.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: automatically claiming for every minor loss (sometimes it costs more long-term); not factoring in the deductible and likely premium increase; the opposite, not claiming for a large loss out of confusion or fear; and not knowing how a claim affects your no-claims discount. Do a quick cost-benefit check before claiming.

## Card: Quick recap
Claiming isn't always worth it: for small losses, the deductible plus a likely premium increase or lost no-claims discount can exceed the payout, so paying yourself may be cheaper. For large, genuinely unaffordable losses, claiming is exactly what insurance is for. Weigh the payout against the deductible and future costs before deciding.
