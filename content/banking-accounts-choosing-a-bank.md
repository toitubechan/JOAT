## Card: What it is
image: choosingBankHero
**Choosing a bank** means picking where to keep and manage your money — and they're not all the same. Banks differ in fees, interest rates, app quality, branch access, and customer service. The right choice fits how *you* actually use money, not just which bank has the biggest billboard.

## Card: Why it matters
You'll interact with your bank constantly for years, so small differences add up. The wrong fit means annoying fees, a clunky app, or poor service when you need help. The right fit quietly saves money and frustration. And switching is easier than most people fear, so you're not stuck with a bad choice.

## Card: Key terms
- **Fee schedule**: the full list of a bank's charges.
- **Interest rate (APY)**: what a savings account earns per year.
- **Branch vs. online bank**: physical locations versus app-only.
- **Direct debit**: automatic recurring payments linked to your account.

## Card: What to compare
image: bankComparisonHero
Weigh the things you'll actually use: the **fee schedule** (maintenance, ATM, overdraft), the **interest rate** on savings, the quality of the app, and whether you need physical **branches** or are happy fully online. Online-only banks often have lower fees and better rates; traditional banks offer in-person help. Match it to your habits.

## Card: How to switch
Switching is mostly admin. Open the new account, then move over your **direct debits** and recurring payments (salary, bills, subscriptions) one by one. Keep the old account open briefly until you're sure everything has moved, then close it. Many banks offer a switching service that does much of this for you automatically.

## Card: Common mistakes
The biggest mistake is staying with your first-ever bank out of pure habit, never checking if it still fits. Another is chasing a flashy sign-up bonus while ignoring ongoing fees that cost more long-term. And when switching, forgetting to redirect a direct debit can cause a missed bill — so move them carefully.

## Card: Quick recap
Pick a bank that matches how you use money: compare fees, savings rates, app quality, and branch access. Online banks often win on fees and rates; traditional ones on in-person help. Switching is simple admin — move your direct debits carefully and don't stay put out of habit alone.
