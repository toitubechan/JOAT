## Card: What it is
image: growthTimeProcrastinationHero
**Procrastination** is putting off what you know you should do, often despite knowing it'll cost you. It's not laziness — it's usually an emotional reaction, avoiding a task that feels boring, hard, or anxiety-inducing. Understanding *why* we procrastinate is the key to actually overcoming it.

## Card: Why it matters
Procrastination causes stress, missed deadlines, and guilt, and the dread often hurts more than the task itself. Almost everyone struggles with it, so practical strategies to beat it are genuinely valuable. Tackling procrastination frees up not just time, but the mental weight of looming undone tasks.

## Card: Key terms
- **Procrastination**: delaying a task despite knowing the cost.
- **Task aversion**: avoiding something because it feels unpleasant.
- **The two-minute rule**: starting with a tiny first step.
- **Momentum**: the ease of continuing once you've started.

## Card: It's emotional, not lazy
image: growthTimeProcrastinationItSEmotionalNotLazyHero
Procrastination is usually **task aversion** — the task triggers a negative feeling (boredom, fear of failing, overwhelm), and delaying it gives instant relief. So we avoid the feeling, not the work. This is why "just be disciplined" rarely works: the real fix is making the task feel less threatening or easier to start.

## Card: Beating it: start tiny
The most reliable trick is to shrink the starting step. The **two-minute rule**: commit to just two minutes of the task. Starting is the hardest part, and once begun, **momentum** usually carries you forward. Other helpers: break big tasks into small steps, remove distractions, and forgive past procrastination rather than piling on guilt.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: calling it laziness and just trying to "force" yourself (which ignores the emotional cause); waiting to "feel motivated" (action usually creates motivation, not the reverse); facing a huge vague task with no small first step; and beating yourself up, which adds stress and makes avoidance worse.

## Card: Quick recap
Procrastination is an emotional avoidance of unpleasant-feeling tasks, not laziness. Beat it by shrinking the first step (the two-minute rule), since starting is the hardest part and momentum follows. Break big tasks down, cut distractions, and skip the guilt. Action usually creates motivation — so just begin small.
