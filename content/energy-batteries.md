## Card: What it is
image: energyBatteriesHero
A **battery** stores energy chemically and releases it as electricity on demand. From phones to electric cars to grid storage, batteries let us *store* electricity for when we need it. As renewable energy grows, batteries have become crucial — solving the problem of using power when the sun isn't shining or the wind isn't blowing.

## Card: Why it matters
Batteries are everywhere and increasingly vital. They power our devices and electric vehicles, and — critically — they're key to the clean-energy transition, storing intermittent solar and wind power for later use. Understanding the basics demystifies the technology in your pocket and explains why battery improvements are so important for a renewable future.

## Card: Key terms
- **Battery**: a device storing energy chemically for later use.
- **Charge and discharge**: storing energy and releasing it.
- **Rechargeable**: a battery that can be refilled with energy.
- **Capacity**: how much energy a battery can store.

## Card: How batteries work
image: energyBatteriesHowBatteriesWorkHero
A battery stores energy in chemical form. When you use it (**discharge**), a chemical reaction inside releases electrons as electric current. When you **charge** a rechargeable battery, electricity reverses that reaction, restoring the stored energy. So a battery doesn't "contain electricity" sitting still — it holds chemical energy that converts to and from electricity. Its **capacity** is how much energy it can hold.

## Card: Why batteries matter for clean energy
Batteries are the key to the **storage** problem of renewables. Solar and wind are intermittent — they generate when the sun shines or wind blows, not necessarily when power is needed. Batteries store that energy for later, smoothing the gap. As battery technology improves and costs fall, relying more on renewables becomes feasible. This is why battery progress is so central to the energy transition.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking a battery "stores electricity" directly (it stores chemical energy that converts to electricity); not realizing rechargeable batteries degrade over many cycles (capacity slowly drops with age); and underrating batteries' role in clean energy. Also, batteries have their own resource and recycling challenges. They're crucial but not magic.

## Card: Quick recap
A battery stores energy chemically and releases it as electricity when discharged; charging reverses the reaction. It doesn't hold electricity directly but chemical energy that converts to and from it. Batteries power devices and electric vehicles, and crucially store intermittent renewable energy for later — making them central to the clean-energy transition. Improving them matters enormously.
