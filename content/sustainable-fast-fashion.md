## Card: What it is
image: sustainFastFashionHero
**Fast fashion** is the business model of producing cheap, trendy clothing quickly and in huge volumes, designed to be replaced fast. It makes fashion affordable and accessible, but carries large hidden environmental and social costs. Understanding it helps you make more thoughtful clothing choices.

## Card: Why it matters
Clothing is something everyone buys, and fast fashion has made us buy far more, far more often. Its environmental footprint (water, waste, pollution) and labor concerns are significant. Understanding the impact lets you decide how you want to engage with it — without guilt or extremes, just awareness.

## Card: Key terms
- **Fast fashion**: cheap, trendy clothes made fast and replaced often.
- **Textile waste**: discarded clothing, much of it landfilled.
- **Cost per wear**: an item's price divided by times worn.
- **Slow fashion**: buying fewer, lasting clothes.

## Card: The hidden costs
image: sustainFastFashionTheHiddenCostsHero
Fast fashion's low prices hide real costs. Environmentally, clothing production uses huge amounts of water and energy, creates pollution, and generates mountains of **textile waste** as cheap clothes are quickly discarded. There are also concerns about labor conditions in some supply chains. The cheap price tag doesn't reflect these wider costs, which are paid elsewhere.

## Card: A more sustainable approach
You don't need to never buy clothes — just buy *better*. **Slow fashion** means choosing fewer, better-quality, durable items, wearing them longer, and caring for and repairing them. A useful idea is **cost per wear**: a pricier item worn many times can be cheaper *and* greener than a cheap item worn twice. Secondhand clothing is also a great low-impact option.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: buying lots of cheap clothes that quickly wear out and get discarded; judging clothing by price alone rather than cost-per-wear and durability; throwing clothes away rather than donating, repairing, or reselling; and assuming you must buy expensive to be sustainable (secondhand is often best). Buy less, choose durable, wear longer.

## Card: Quick recap
Fast fashion makes clothes cheap but carries hidden environmental costs (water, pollution, textile waste) and labor concerns. A more sustainable approach is slow fashion: fewer, better-quality items worn longer, judged by cost-per-wear, plus secondhand and repair. You needn't be extreme — just buy less, choose durable, and wear clothes longer.
