## Card: What it is
image: realestateBuyerMistakesHero
**Common buyer mistakes** are the predictable errors people make when buying property — one of life's biggest purchases. Many are avoidable with awareness. Knowing the common pitfalls in advance helps you sidestep costly, stressful mistakes. This is general educational knowledge, not professional advice.

## Card: Why it matters
Buying property involves huge sums, so mistakes can be enormously costly — financially and emotionally. The good news: many buyer mistakes are common and predictable, so knowing them lets you avoid them. This awareness can save you from overpaying, buying the wrong thing, or being caught out. Forewarned is forearmed in a high-stakes purchase.

## Card: Key terms
- **Buyer mistakes**: predictable errors when buying property.
- **Emotional buying**: deciding with feelings over judgment.
- **Overstretching**: buying beyond what you can comfortably afford.
- **Due diligence**: thorough checking before committing.

## Card: Money and emotion mistakes
image: realestateBuyerMistakesMoneyAndEmotionMistakesHero
Two big categories. **Overstretching** — buying at the very top (or beyond) of what you can afford, leaving no cushion for costs, rate rises, or emergencies — is a common, dangerous mistake. And **emotional buying** — falling in love with a property and letting feelings override judgment, leading to overpaying or ignoring problems. Big purchases stir strong emotions, but staying within a sensible budget and keeping a clear head are crucial to avoiding costly errors.

## Card: Process and diligence mistakes
Other common pitfalls: skipping proper **due diligence** (not inspecting the property or researching the area, then hitting costly surprises); forgetting closing costs and the true total cost; rushing the decision under pressure; not comparing options or shopping around for the mortgage; and ignoring future needs (buying something that won't suit you in a few years). Most of these come down to acting too quickly or incompletely — slowing down and being thorough prevents them.

## Card: Common mistakes
The key common buyer mistakes to avoid: overstretching your budget (leave a cushion); emotional buying that overrides judgment; skipping due diligence and inspections; forgetting the true total cost including fees; rushing under pressure; and not shopping around. Nearly all are avoidable by staying within budget, keeping a clear head, doing thorough checks, and not rushing one of life's biggest decisions.

## Card: Quick recap
Common property-buying mistakes are predictable and avoidable: overstretching your budget (leaving no cushion), emotional buying that overrides judgment, skipping due diligence and inspections, forgetting closing costs and true total cost, rushing under pressure, and not shopping around. Given the huge sums involved, errors are costly — but awareness lets you sidestep them. Stay within budget, keep a clear head, be thorough, and don't rush.
