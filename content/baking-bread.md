## Card: What it is
image: breadBasicsHero
**Bread** is one of humanity's oldest and most fundamental foods — and at its simplest, it's made from just flour, water, salt, and often a leavening agent like yeast. Understanding the basics of how bread is made (and rises) demystifies a food many find intimidating to bake, revealing a simple, ancient process anyone can learn.

## Card: Why it matters
Bread is a staple across the world, yet baking it can seem daunting. Understanding the basics — what makes bread rise, the role of each ingredient, and the simple process — removes the mystery and makes home baking achievable. It also deepens appreciation of a food central to human history and daily life. Bread baking is simpler and more rewarding than it appears.

## Card: Key terms
- **Bread**: a staple food made from flour, water, and usually leavening.
- **Yeast**: a leavening agent that makes most bread rise.
- **Gluten**: the protein network giving bread structure.
- **Kneading and proofing**: developing structure and letting dough rise.

## Card: How bread rises
image: breadRiseHero
Most bread rises thanks to **yeast** — a living organism that ferments, producing gas (carbon dioxide) that gets trapped in the dough, making it rise and become light. This trapping works because of **gluten** — a stretchy protein network that forms when flour is mixed with water and worked, creating an elastic structure that holds the gas bubbles. So flour + water develops gluten (structure), yeast produces gas (rise), and the gluten traps it — that's the essence of how bread becomes light and airy.

## Card: The basic process
Bread-making follows a simple process: mix the ingredients into a dough; **knead** it (work it) to develop the gluten structure; let it **proof** (rest and rise) as the yeast produces gas and the dough expands; then bake it, where heat sets the structure, creates a crust, and finishes the rise. While there's craft and variation, this core process — mix, knead, proof, bake — underlies most bread. It's an ancient, forgiving process that rewards practice.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking bread-making is too difficult or mysterious for home bakers (it's a simple, ancient, forgiving process); not understanding the roles of yeast (rise) and gluten (structure); skipping or rushing kneading or proofing (which develop structure and rise); and being intimidated. Bread is flour, water, salt, and leavening, made by mixing, kneading, proofing, and baking — achievable for anyone willing to learn.

## Card: Quick recap
Bread, at its simplest, is flour, water, salt, and usually yeast. It rises because yeast ferments and produces gas, which is trapped by gluten — the stretchy protein network that forms when flour and water are mixed and worked. The basic process is mix, knead (develop gluten), proof (let it rise), and bake (set the structure and crust). It's an ancient, forgiving process, simpler and more rewarding than it appears.
