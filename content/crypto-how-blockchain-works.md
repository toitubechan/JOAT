## Card: What it is
image: financeCryptoHowBlockchainWorksHero
A **blockchain** is a shared digital record book that lists every transaction, copied across thousands of computers worldwide. Instead of one bank keeping the official ledger, everyone in the network holds a matching copy. New transactions are bundled into "blocks" and linked in a chain — hence the name.

## Card: Why it matters
The blockchain is the clever idea that makes crypto possible without a central authority. Understanding it — even loosely — demystifies the whole field and helps you tell genuine technology from buzzword-laden scams. You don't need the math, just the core concept of a shared, tamper-resistant record.

## Card: Key terms
- **Blockchain**: a shared chain of transaction records.
- **Block**: a batch of transactions added to the chain.
- **Ledger**: the record of who owns and sent what.
- **Verification**: the network agreeing a transaction is valid.

## Card: How it stays honest
image: financeCryptoHowBlockchainWorksHowItStaysHonestHero
Because thousands of computers each hold a copy of the **ledger**, no one can secretly change it — the other copies would disagree. To add a new **block**, the network must reach agreement through **verification**. Altering past records would mean fooling the entire network at once, which is practically impossible on a large blockchain.

## Card: Why blocks are "chained"
Each block contains a fingerprint of the one before it, so they're linked in order. Change anything in an old block, and its fingerprint changes, breaking the chain visibly. This linking is what makes a blockchain **tamper-resistant**: the records build on each other, so the past is locked in by everything that came after.

## Card: Common mistakes
A common confusion is thinking blockchain and Bitcoin are the same thing — Bitcoin uses a blockchain, but blockchains have other uses too. Another is assuming "blockchain" automatically means trustworthy or valuable; the word is often slapped on projects as marketing. The technology is real, but its presence alone guarantees nothing.

## Card: Quick recap
A blockchain is a shared, tamper-resistant ledger copied across many computers, with transactions bundled into linked blocks. No central authority is needed, because the network verifies and agrees on records together. It's the engine behind crypto — but the buzzword alone never proves something is safe or worthwhile.
