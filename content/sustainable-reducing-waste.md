## Card: What it is
image: sustainReducingWasteHero
**Reducing household waste** means cutting down how much you throw away — through using less, reusing more, and wasting less in the first place. It's the most effective form of "green" living because the best waste is the waste never created. It starts before the bin, not at it.

## Card: Why it matters
The average household throws away a surprising amount, much of it avoidable. Reducing waste saves money, cuts the resources and energy used to make and dispose of stuff, and lightens your environmental impact. It's one of the most practical, immediate ways to live more sustainably — and it often saves money too.

## Card: Key terms
- **Reduce, reuse, recycle**: in that order of priority.
- **Single-use**: items used once then thrown away.
- **Food waste**: edible food that's binned.
- **Refuse**: declining things you don't need.

## Card: The order that matters
image: sustainReducingWasteTheOrderThatMattersHero
The famous "reduce, reuse, recycle" is a *priority order*, not a list of equals. **Reduce** first (buy and use less), then **reuse** (use things again), and only then **recycle**. Many people jump straight to recycling, but reducing and reusing prevent far more waste. Even better, add "**refuse**" at the start: decline freebies and packaging you don't need.

## Card: The biggest wins
Two areas offer the biggest waste reductions: **food waste** (plan meals, store food well, use leftovers — see also meal planning) and **single-use items** (swap disposables for reusables: bags, bottles, containers). These two account for a large share of household waste, so targeting them first gives the most impact for the least effort.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: relying on recycling while ignoring reducing and reusing (the higher priorities); buying "eco" products you don't actually need (the greenest purchase is none); not addressing food waste, a huge avoidable category; and assuming small individual actions are pointless — they add up and build habits.

## Card: Quick recap
Reducing household waste means using less and reusing more, not just recycling. Follow the priority order — refuse, reduce, reuse, then recycle. Target the biggest wins first: food waste and single-use items. It saves money and resources, and small consistent habits genuinely add up. The best waste is the waste never created.
