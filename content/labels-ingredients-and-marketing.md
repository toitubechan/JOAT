## Card: What it is
image: ingredientsMarketingHero
The **ingredients list** tells you what a food is actually made of, and front-of-pack **marketing claims** try to shape your impression. Learning to read the list and decode the claims lets you see past the packaging hype to what you're really buying.

## Card: Why it matters
Marketing is designed to make food seem healthier or more natural than it is. The ingredients list is harder to spin, so it's your best truth-teller. Knowing how to read it, and which buzzwords are meaningless, helps you make genuinely informed choices rather than being led by clever packaging.

## Card: Key terms
- **Ingredients list**: contents listed in order of amount, most first.
- **Order by weight**: the first ingredient is the largest by quantity.
- **Marketing claim**: front-of-pack wording designed to appeal.
- **Unregulated term**: a word like "natural" with no strict legal meaning.

## Card: Ingredients are listed by amount
image: ingredientOrder
The key rule: ingredients are listed in **order of weight**, most first. So if sugar is near the top, the food is largely sugar. This instantly reveals what a product mainly *is*. A long list of unrecognizable additives, or sugar appearing under several different names, are both worth noticing.

## Card: Seeing through claims
Many front-of-pack words are **unregulated** marketing. "**Natural**", "wholesome", or "artisan" often mean little legally. "No added sugar" can still mean naturally high sugar. "Made with real fruit" might mean a tiny amount. Treat front-of-pack claims as advertising, and let the ingredients list and nutrition panel give you the real answer.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: trusting words like "natural" or "healthy" as if they're guaranteed; not noticing sugar listed under multiple names to push it down the list; assuming a short ingredient list or "real fruit" claim means healthy; and reading the front of the pack but never the back. The list and panel are where truth lives.

## Card: Quick recap
The ingredients list reveals what a food really is — items are ordered by weight, so the first few dominate. Watch for sugar high up or under several names. Treat front-of-pack claims like "natural" and "no added sugar" as marketing, not fact. To know what you're buying, read the list and the nutrition panel.
