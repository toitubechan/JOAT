## Card: What it is
image: psychRelationshipsTrustAndBoundariesHero
**Trust** and **boundaries** are two pillars of healthy relationships. Trust is the confidence that someone is reliable and honest; boundaries are the limits that define what's okay and not okay for you. Together they create the safety and respect that healthy relationships need to thrive.

## Card: Why it matters
Without trust, relationships feel unsafe and anxious. Without boundaries, people get overwhelmed, resentful, or taken advantage of. Understanding both helps you build relationships that are secure and respectful, where both people feel safe and valued. They're essential and often-overlooked foundations of healthy connection.

## Card: Key terms
- **Trust**: confidence in someone's reliability and honesty.
- **Boundary**: a limit defining what's acceptable for you.
- **Consistency**: reliable behavior that builds trust over time.
- **Respecting boundaries**: honoring others' limits.

## Card: How trust is built (and broken)
image: psychRelationshipsTrustAndBoundariesHowTrustIsBuiltAndBrokenHero
**Trust** is built slowly through **consistency** — reliable, honest behavior repeated over time. Small kept promises, honesty, and showing up build it; broken promises and dishonesty erode it. Importantly, trust is hard to rebuild once broken, which is why it's worth protecting. Healthy relationships run on this confidence that you can rely on each other.

## Card: Why boundaries are healthy
**Boundaries** — limits about what's okay for you (your time, energy, body, values) — are not selfish or walls against people; they're essential for healthy relationships. Clear boundaries prevent resentment and burnout, and **respecting** others' boundaries shows care. Saying "no" or "I'm not comfortable with that" is healthy. Relationships where boundaries are communicated and respected are stronger and more sustainable.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: thinking boundaries are selfish or unkind (they're healthy and prevent resentment); not communicating your boundaries, then feeling resentful when they're crossed; ignoring or pushing others' boundaries; and assuming trust is automatic rather than built through consistency. Trust and clear, respected boundaries are what make relationships safe.

## Card: Quick recap
Trust (confidence in someone's reliability and honesty) is built slowly through consistency and easily broken, so it's worth protecting. Boundaries (your limits on what's okay) aren't selfish — they prevent resentment and burnout, and respecting others' boundaries shows care. Together, trust and clear boundaries create the safety and respect healthy relationships need.
