## Card: What it is
image: smartphoneBasicsHero
A **smartphone** is a pocket computer that happens to make calls. Beneath the apps and screens, it works on a few consistent ideas — a home screen, settings, apps, and gestures. Once these basics click, any phone becomes far less intimidating, even when you switch to a new one.

## Card: Why it matters
Phones run much of modern life — communication, payments, navigation, photos, information. Feeling confident with the basics means you get the benefits without frustration or fear of "breaking something." The core concepts are simple and transfer across devices, so a little understanding goes a long way.

## Card: Key terms
- **Home screen**: the main screen showing your apps.
- **App**: a program that does a specific task.
- **Settings**: where you control how the phone behaves.
- **Gesture**: a touch action like tap, swipe, or long-press.

## Card: The core building blocks
image: phoneBuildingBlocks
Every smartphone shares a structure: a **home screen** holding your **apps** (each a tool for one job — messaging, maps, camera), a **settings** area to control the phone, and **gestures** to navigate. Learn these four and you can find your way around almost any phone. The brands differ in style, not in these fundamentals.

## Card: You won't break it
A big barrier for beginners is fear of doing something wrong. In reality, phones are hard to truly break by tapping around — you can almost always undo, go back, or close an app. Exploring the settings and apps is how you learn. Worst case, restarting the phone fixes most temporary glitches.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common beginner mistakes: fear of exploring, which slows learning; ignoring **settings** entirely, missing helpful adjustments; not knowing simple gestures like swiping back or closing apps; and assuming features are "not for me" without trying. Curiosity and a little experimentation are the fastest route to confidence.

## Card: Quick recap
A smartphone is a pocket computer built on simple shared ideas: a home screen of apps, a settings area, and touch gestures. These transfer across devices. You're very unlikely to break it by exploring, and restarting fixes most glitches. Curiosity beats caution — poke around, and confidence follows quickly.
