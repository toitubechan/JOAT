## Card: What it is
image: digestiveSystemHero
The **digestive system** is the body's food-processing line. It breaks down what you eat into tiny pieces your body can absorb and use for energy and building, then disposes of the rest. It's a long tube running from mouth to exit, with several organs helping along the way.

## Card: Why it matters
Everything your body does is fueled by what this system extracts from food. Understanding it explains why fiber, chewing, and gut health matter, and demystifies common experiences like hunger and digestion. It's the system that turns a meal into the energy and materials that keep you alive.

## Card: Key terms
- **Digestion**: breaking food into absorbable pieces.
- **Stomach**: where food is churned and broken down.
- **Intestines**: long tubes where nutrients are absorbed.
- **Absorption**: taking nutrients into the bloodstream.

## Card: The journey of a meal
image: mealJourneyHero
**Digestion** starts in the mouth — chewing and saliva begin the breakdown. Food travels to the **stomach**, which churns it with acids into a soupy mush. It then enters the **intestines**, where most **absorption** happens: nutrients pass into the bloodstream to be delivered everywhere. What's left becomes waste.

## Card: Helpers and gut health
Organs like the liver and pancreas add juices that help break down food. Your gut is also home to trillions of helpful bacteria — your **gut microbiome** — that aid digestion and health. **Fiber**, found in plants, keeps things moving smoothly and feeds these good bacteria, which is why it matters so much.

## Card: Common mistakes
A common misunderstanding is thinking digestion is only the stomach's job, when the intestines do most of the absorbing. Another is ignoring fiber, which keeps the system running well. And many underestimate how much chewing, eating speed, and gut bacteria affect digestion and how they feel after meals.

## Card: Quick recap
The digestive system breaks food into absorbable pieces and takes in nutrients: chewing starts it, the stomach churns it, and the intestines absorb most nutrients into the blood. Helper organs and gut bacteria assist, and fiber keeps it all running smoothly. It's how a meal becomes your fuel.
