## Card: What it is
image: insectsHero
**Insects** are a vast group of small invertebrates — animals without backbones — defined by six legs, three body segments, and usually wings. They include beetles, ants, bees, butterflies, flies, and countless more. Insects are by far the most numerous and diverse group of animals on Earth, quietly running many of the planet's vital processes.

## Card: Why it matters
Insects vastly outnumber all other animals and are essential to ecosystems — pollinating plants, recycling waste, and forming the base of many food chains. Despite often being overlooked or disliked, they're crucial to life on Earth (including our food supply). Understanding them reveals how much the living world depends on these tiny creatures.

## Card: Key terms
- **Insect**: a six-legged invertebrate with three body segments.
- **Invertebrate**: an animal without a backbone.
- **Pollination**: transferring pollen so plants can reproduce.
- **Metamorphosis**: dramatic transformation (e.g., caterpillar to butterfly).

## Card: What makes an insect
**Insects** are **invertebrates** (no backbone) with a defining body plan: six legs, three main body segments (head, thorax, abdomen), and usually wings — making them the only invertebrates that can fly. Many undergo **metamorphosis**, transforming dramatically as they grow (a caterpillar becoming a butterfly). Not every small crawling creature is an insect — spiders (eight legs) are not insects, for example.

## Card: Why insects rule
image: insectSuccessHero
Insects are staggeringly numerous — they make up the majority of all known animal species, and their total numbers are almost unimaginable. More importantly, they're ecologically vital: bees and other insects **pollinate** much of the food we eat; insects recycle dead matter, enrich soil, and feed countless other animals. A world without insects would collapse ecologically. These tiny creatures are quietly essential to life.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: calling all small crawling creatures "insects" (spiders, with eight legs, aren't insects); seeing insects as merely pests (most are harmless or beneficial, and many are essential); underrating how numerous and ecologically vital they are; and not knowing many transform via metamorphosis. Insects are the most successful, important animal group despite their small size.

## Card: Quick recap
Insects are invertebrates with six legs, three body segments, and usually wings (the only flying invertebrates). They're by far the most numerous and diverse animals, and ecologically essential — pollinating crops, recycling matter, and feeding food chains. Many undergo metamorphosis. Spiders aren't insects (they have eight legs). Tiny but mighty, insects quietly keep the living world running.
