## Card: What it is
image: psychEmotionsUnderstandingYoursHero
**Understanding your emotions** is the skill of noticing, identifying, and naming what you're feeling and why. It sounds simple, but many people struggle to pinpoint their emotions, which makes the emotions harder to handle. This self-awareness is the essential first step before managing emotions effectively.

## Card: Why it matters
You can't manage what you don't understand. Being able to recognize and name your emotions reduces their power over you, improves decisions, and helps you communicate. People who understand their own feelings handle stress, relationships, and challenges far better. It's the foundation of emotional skill, and it's learnable.

## Card: Key terms
- **Emotional awareness**: noticing what you're feeling.
- **Naming / labeling**: putting a word to an emotion.
- **Granularity**: distinguishing emotions precisely.
- **Trigger awareness**: knowing what set the emotion off.

## Card: Name it to tame it
image: psychEmotionsUnderstandingYoursNameItToTameItHero
A well-supported idea: simply **naming** an emotion reduces its intensity ("name it to tame it"). When you label what you feel — "I'm anxious," "I'm frustrated" — you engage your thinking brain and create a little distance from the raw feeling. Vague, unexamined emotion overwhelms; a named emotion becomes something you can observe and address. The first step is always: notice and name.

## Card: Get specific and find the trigger
Improve your **granularity** — distinguishing emotions precisely. "Bad" is vague; is it anxious, disappointed, lonely, or resentful? More precise naming gives more useful information and clearer next steps. Also practice **trigger awareness**: ask what *set off* the emotion. Understanding the trigger ("I'm anxious because of tomorrow's meeting") turns a confusing feeling into something specific you can actually work with.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: ignoring or pushing away emotions instead of noticing them; labeling everything vaguely ("I feel bad") rather than precisely; not asking what triggered a feeling; and judging yourself for having emotions instead of simply observing them. The skill is to notice, name precisely, and identify the trigger — with curiosity, not judgment.

## Card: Quick recap
Understanding your emotions means noticing, naming, and identifying the triggers of what you feel. Simply naming an emotion reduces its intensity ("name it to tame it"). Get specific — "anxious" beats "bad" — and ask what set it off. This self-awareness, approached with curiosity rather than judgment, is the essential first step to managing emotions.
