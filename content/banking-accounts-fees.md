## Card: What it is
image: bankFeesHero
**Bank fees** are charges for using certain services or for slipping outside the rules — like spending more than you have, using the wrong ATM, or just holding the account. Most are small, but they repeat, and many are completely avoidable once you know the rules of your account.

## Card: Why it matters
A few euros here and there feels trivial, but fees are pure loss — money gone with nothing to show for it. Over a year they can add up to a meaningful sum. Knowing the common fees, and the simple moves that dodge them, keeps more of your money where it belongs: with you.

## Card: Key terms
- **Overdraft fee**: charged when you spend more than your balance.
- **Maintenance fee**: a regular charge just for having the account.
- **ATM fee**: charged for using a machine outside your bank's network.
- **Foreign transaction fee**: a charge for spending in another currency.

## Card: The big ones
The costliest is usually the **overdraft fee** — triggered when a payment pushes your balance below zero. **Maintenance fees** are a flat monthly charge some accounts levy. **ATM fees** hit when you use an out-of-network machine. And **foreign transaction fees** apply when you spend abroad or in another currency.

## Card: How to avoid them
image: avoidBankFeesHero
Most fees are dodgeable: keep a small buffer so you never overdraw, and turn off overdraft "coverage" if your bank allows. Choose an account with no maintenance fee, or meet its waiver conditions. Use your own bank's ATMs, and for travel, look for cards with no foreign transaction fees. A quick read of the fee schedule reveals the rules.

## Card: Common mistakes
The first mistake is never reading the fee schedule, so charges feel random. Another is opting into overdraft coverage, which lets payments go through but at a steep fee — sometimes declining the payment is cheaper. And many people stay with a fee-heavy account out of habit when fee-free options exist.

## Card: Quick recap
Bank fees — overdraft, maintenance, ATM, foreign — are mostly avoidable. Keep a buffer, pick a no-fee account, use in-network ATMs, and read the fee schedule. They seem tiny, but they're pure loss, so a little awareness keeps that money in your pocket.
