## Card: What it is
image: religionBuddhismHero
**Buddhism** is a major world tradition based on the teachings of the **Buddha** ("the awakened one"), who taught a path to understand and end suffering through wisdom, ethical living, and mental training like meditation. Originating in ancient India, it has spread across the world. This is a respectful, general overview — descriptive, not promoting or judging.

## Card: Why it matters
Buddhism has shaped the cultures and lives of much of Asia and increasingly influences the wider world — its practices like mindfulness and meditation are now widely adopted, including in secular forms. Understanding its basics builds cultural literacy and offers insight into a tradition that approaches life's big questions quite differently from many Western religions.

## Card: Key terms
- **Buddhism**: a tradition based on the Buddha's teachings.
- **The Buddha**: "the awakened one," whose teachings Buddhism follows.
- **Suffering**: a central focus — understanding and ending it.
- **Meditation**: mental training central to Buddhist practice.

## Card: The path to end suffering
image: religionBuddhismThePathToEndSufferingHero
At the heart of Buddhism is the insight that life involves **suffering** (dissatisfaction, craving, change), and a path to understand and overcome it. The **Buddha** taught that suffering arises largely from attachment and craving, and that by following a path of wisdom, ethical conduct, and mental discipline, one can find peace and ultimately spiritual awakening (often called enlightenment). The focus is practical — transforming the mind and how one relates to life.

## Card: A distinctive approach
Buddhism is notable for often being *less* focused on a creator God than many religions, centering instead on personal spiritual development and understanding the mind. It shares concepts like karma and rebirth with other Indian traditions. **Meditation** and mindfulness — training attention and awareness — are central practices, and these have spread globally, often adopted in secular wellbeing contexts. Like other major faiths, Buddhism has diverse branches and cultural forms.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking Buddhism centers on worshipping a creator God (it often doesn't — it focuses on the path to awakening); seeing it as merely "the religion of meditation" (meditation is one part of a broader path); treating it as uniform (it has diverse branches); and stripping mindfulness from its context. Buddhism is a rich tradition focused on understanding the mind and ending suffering, best understood respectfully.

## Card: Quick recap
Buddhism, based on the teachings of the Buddha ("the awakened one"), centers on understanding and ending suffering through wisdom, ethical living, and mental training like meditation. Often less focused on a creator God than other religions, it emphasizes personal spiritual development and the mind, sharing concepts like karma and rebirth. Its meditation and mindfulness practices have spread worldwide. A diverse tradition best understood respectfully.
