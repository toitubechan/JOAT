## Card: What it is
image: energyOilAndGasHero
**Oil** and **natural gas** are fossil fuels that power much of transport, heating, electricity, and industry — and are raw materials for countless products (plastics, chemicals). They're hugely important economically and geopolitically. Understanding the basics explains a lot about energy, prices, and world affairs.

## Card: Why it matters
Oil and gas shape the global economy, geopolitics, and prices you pay (petrol, heating, even goods made from them). They're behind major world events and headlines. Despite the energy transition, they remain central for now. Understanding them helps you grasp energy markets, news, and why these resources carry such economic and political weight.

## Card: Key terms
- **Crude oil**: unrefined oil pumped from the ground.
- **Refining**: processing crude oil into usable products.
- **Natural gas**: a gaseous fossil fuel used for heat and power.
- **Petrochemicals**: products made from oil and gas (like plastics).

## Card: From ground to product
image: energyOilAndGasFromGroundToProductHero
**Crude oil** is pumped from underground and then **refined** — separated and processed into many products: petrol, diesel, jet fuel, heating oil, and the raw materials for **petrochemicals** (plastics, chemicals, fertilizers). **Natural gas** is extracted similarly and used for heating, cooking, electricity generation, and industry. So oil and gas aren't just fuels — they're woven into countless everyday products too.

## Card: Why they're economically and politically powerful
Oil and gas are traded globally in massive markets, and their prices ripple through the whole economy (affecting petrol, heating, transport, and the cost of goods). Because supplies are concentrated in certain regions, they carry huge **geopolitical** weight — influencing alliances, conflicts, and national wealth. This is why oil and gas news so often dominates headlines and shapes world events.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking oil is only used for fuel (it's also the basis of plastics and chemicals); not realizing how much oil and gas prices affect the broader economy; underrating their geopolitical importance; and assuming the energy transition has already replaced them (they remain central for now). They're fuels *and* materials, with vast economic and political reach.

## Card: Quick recap
Oil and natural gas are fossil fuels powering transport, heating, electricity, and industry — and oil is refined into countless products including plastics and chemicals. Their prices ripple through the whole economy, and because supplies are geographically concentrated, they carry huge geopolitical weight. Despite the energy transition, they remain central, which is why they so often dominate the news.
