## Card: What it is
image: tireCareHero
Your **tires** are the only parts of the car touching the road — four patches of rubber, each about the size of your palm, carry the whole vehicle. Looking after them means keeping the right **pressure** and enough **tread**. It's basic, quick, and directly tied to your safety.

## Card: Why it matters
Tires affect braking, grip, steering, and fuel use. Under-inflated or worn tires lengthen stopping distances, handle badly in rain, and can fail dangerously at speed. Because they're your only contact with the road, tire care isn't optional upkeep — it's one of the most safety-critical things you can check.

## Card: Key terms
- **Tire pressure**: how much air is in the tire, measured in PSI or bar.
- **Tread**: the grooved pattern that grips and clears water.
- **Tread depth**: how deep the grooves are; too shallow is unsafe.
- **Wear**: gradual loss of tread from use.

## Card: Getting pressure right
Correct **tire pressure** is listed in your manual or on a sticker in the door frame — not on the tire itself (that's the maximum). Check when tires are **cold** for accuracy. Too low wears the edges, wastes fuel, and risks failure; too high reduces grip. Most fuel stations have an air pump to top up.

## Card: Tread and safety
image: pressureTread
**Tread** channels water away so the tire grips in the wet; worn tread means skidding and longer stopping. Many places set a legal minimum **tread depth**. A quick check: tread wear indicators (small raised bars in the grooves) show when a tire is worn out. Bald, cracked, or bulging tires must be replaced — they can blow out.

## Card: Common mistakes
A common error is checking pressure when tires are hot, giving a misleading reading. Another is inflating to the number on the tire's sidewall (the maximum) instead of the car's recommended pressure. People also ignore uneven wear, which signals alignment problems, and drive on dangerously worn or damaged tires to delay the cost.

## Card: Quick recap
Tires are your only contact with the road, so keep them right: check pressure cold against the car's recommended figure, and watch tread depth using the wear bars. Replace bald, cracked, or bulging tires promptly. Good tires mean shorter stops and better grip — basic care with major safety payoff.
