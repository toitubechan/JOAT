## Card: What it is
image: interviewBodyLanguageHero
In an interview, *how* you come across — your **body language**, tone, and overall impression — matters alongside what you say. People form judgments partly from non-verbal signals, so managing these helps you project the confidence and warmth that make a strong impression.

## Card: Why it matters
You can have great answers but undermine them with nervous, closed-off body language — or boost them with calm, positive signals. First impressions form fast and are hard to shift. Being aware of the impression you give lets you reinforce your words rather than accidentally contradict them.

## Card: Key terms
- **Body language**: non-verbal signals like posture and gestures.
- **Eye contact**: looking at the interviewer appropriately.
- **First impression**: the rapid initial judgment others form.
- **Rapport**: a friendly, comfortable connection.

## Card: Project calm confidence
image: calmConfidenceHero
A few signals convey confidence: good posture (sitting upright, not slumped), appropriate **eye contact** (engaged, not staring), a genuine smile, and a steady, clear voice. A firm greeting and avoiding nervous fidgeting help too. You don't need to fake a personality — just present a calm, attentive, positive version of yourself.

## Card: Build rapport and first impressions
**First impressions** form within moments, so start warm — a friendly greeting and a smile. Throughout, aim to build **rapport**: listen actively, show interest, and treat it as a friendly conversation. People warm to those who seem genuinely engaged and likeable. Being personable is itself part of the "culture fit" employers assess.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: nervous habits (fidgeting, avoiding eye contact) that signal anxiety; closed body language (crossed arms, slumping); a weak or overpowering greeting; speaking too fast from nerves; and being so focused on answers that you forget to be warm and personable. Awareness and a few deep breaths go a long way.

## Card: Quick recap
Beyond your words, your body language and demeanor shape the impression you make. Project calm confidence with good posture, appropriate eye contact, a genuine smile, and a steady voice. Make a warm first impression and build rapport by being engaged and personable. How you come across reinforces — or undermines — what you say.
