## Card: What it is
image: tripPlanningHero
**Planning a trip** is the groundwork before you go — choosing where and when, setting a budget, and sorting essentials like documents and bookings. Good planning prevents stress, saves money, and avoids the surprises that can derail a trip. It's the foundation of enjoyable, hassle-free travel.

## Card: Why it matters
Poor planning leads to overspending, missed bookings, document problems, and avoidable stress. A little upfront planning makes travel cheaper, smoother, and more enjoyable. Especially for first-time or international travelers, knowing the basics of what to sort in advance prevents the common mistakes that ruin trips before they start.

## Card: Key terms
- **Itinerary**: a plan of your trip's schedule and stops.
- **Budget**: the money allocated for the whole trip.
- **Documents**: passport, visa, ID, and bookings.
- **Off-season**: cheaper, quieter times to travel.

## Card: Budget and timing
Start with a realistic **budget** covering everything — transport, accommodation, food, activities, and a buffer for surprises. Then consider **timing**: traveling in the **off-season** (outside peak holiday times) is usually cheaper and less crowded. Booking flights and stays in advance often saves money too. Setting the budget and timing first shapes all your other decisions sensibly.

## Card: Sort documents early
image: documentsEarly
Don't leave **documents** to the last minute. Check your passport is valid (many countries require it to be valid for months beyond your trip), find out if you need a **visa** for your destination (and apply early — they can take time), and arrange any required vaccinations or travel insurance. Document and entry-requirement problems are a top cause of trips going wrong, so handle them well ahead.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: an unrealistic budget with no buffer for surprises; leaving passport/visa checks too late (a top trip-ruiner); booking nothing in advance then overpaying; traveling at peak times unnecessarily; and over-planning every minute, leaving no flexibility. Plan the essentials early — budget, timing, documents — while leaving room for spontaneity.

## Card: Quick recap
Plan a trip by setting a realistic budget (with a buffer), choosing timing (off-season is cheaper and quieter), and sorting documents early — check passport validity and visa requirements well ahead, as these are top trip-ruiners. Book key things in advance to save money, but leave some flexibility. Good groundwork makes travel smooth and affordable.
