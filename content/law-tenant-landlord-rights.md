## Card: What it is
image: lawTenantLandlordRightsHero
Renting involves legal **rights and responsibilities** on both sides — for tenants and landlords. Laws typically protect tenants (e.g., a safe home, fair treatment, deposit protection) while also protecting landlords (e.g., timely rent, care of property). Knowing the basic balance helps you rent fairly and confidently. This is general educational knowledge; tenancy law varies significantly by location, so check your local rules.

## Card: Why it matters
Many people rent, and disputes between tenants and landlords are common — often arising because one or both sides don't know their rights and responsibilities. Understanding the basic legal balance helps you avoid problems, stand up for fair treatment, and meet your own obligations. It protects both your money and your living situation.

## Card: Key terms
- **Tenant rights**: legal protections for renters.
- **Landlord rights**: legal protections for property owners.
- **Deposit protection**: rules safeguarding a tenant's deposit.
- **Responsibilities**: obligations both sides must meet.

## Card: Tenant rights
image: lawTenantLandlordRightsTenantRightsHero
Tenants generally have **rights** including: a safe, habitable home (working essentials, no serious hazards); reasonable privacy (the landlord usually must give notice before entering, not just turn up); protection from unfair eviction (proper process and notice required); fair handling and often **deposit protection** (rules on holding and returning deposits, and not charging for normal wear and tear); and protection from discrimination. These prevent tenants from being mistreated or exploited.

## Card: Landlord rights and shared responsibilities
Landlords also have **rights**: to receive rent on time, to have the property treated with reasonable care, and to take proper action if a tenant breaches the agreement. Both sides have **responsibilities** — tenants to pay rent and not damage the property; landlords to maintain it and follow the law. Renting works best when both understand and meet their obligations. Most disputes come from one side not knowing or honoring this balance.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: tenants not knowing their rights (accepting unsafe homes, unfair deposit deductions, or landlords entering without notice); landlords overstepping (entering without notice, unfair eviction); either side not meeting their responsibilities; and assuming the rules are the same everywhere (tenancy law varies a lot). Know the basic balance of rights and responsibilities, document things, and check your local laws.

## Card: Quick recap
Renting involves legal rights and responsibilities on both sides. Tenants generally have rights to a safe home, reasonable privacy (notice before entry), protection from unfair eviction, and deposit protection. Landlords have rights to timely rent and proper care of the property. Both have responsibilities to uphold. Most disputes arise from not knowing this balance. Document things, know your local laws, and seek help for serious issues.
