## Card: What it is
image: exerciseStartHero
Getting started with exercise is where most people either build a habit or quit. Doing it right means starting small, progressing gradually, and avoiding the early injuries and burnout that derail beginners. The goal at the start isn't fitness — it's building a habit you can keep.

## Card: Why it matters
Most people who quit exercise do so in the first few weeks, usually from doing too much too soon — getting injured, exhausted, or discouraged. Starting sensibly protects both your body and your motivation. A gentle start that becomes a lasting habit beats an intense start that collapses in a fortnight.

## Card: Key terms
- **Warm-up**: gentle movement to prepare the body.
- **Progressive overload**: increasing difficulty gradually over time.
- **Rest day**: recovery time between sessions.
- **Form**: doing an exercise with correct technique.

## Card: Start small, build slowly
The biggest beginner error is going too hard. Begin with manageable amounts and apply **progressive overload** — increase intensity, duration, or weight *gradually* as you adapt. Your body needs time to strengthen. A modest start you can sustain and slowly build on far outperforms an exhausting one you abandon.

## Card: Warm up, rest, and use good form
image: warmupRestForm
Begin sessions with a **warm-up** to prepare muscles and reduce injury risk. Use good **form** (correct technique) over heavy effort — bad form causes injuries and wastes effort. And take **rest days**: muscles grow and repair during recovery, not just during exercise. Rest is part of training, not a failure of it.

## Card: Common mistakes
The classic mistakes: doing too much too soon and getting hurt or burnt out; skipping warm-ups; chasing heavy weights or speed before mastering **form**; and never resting, leading to exhaustion and injury. Beginners also compare themselves to experienced people, get discouraged, and quit. Progress is personal.

## Card: Quick recap
Start exercise safely by starting small and building gradually (progressive overload), warming up, prioritizing good form over intensity, and taking rest days for recovery. The early goal is a sustainable habit, not peak fitness. Avoid the too-much-too-soon trap, and don't compare your start to others' middle.
