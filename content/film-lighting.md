## Card: What it is
image: filmLightingHero
**Lighting** in film is the deliberate use of light to shape how a scene looks and feels. It's one of the most powerful — and most overlooked by viewers — tools in filmmaking. The same scene can feel cheerful, sinister, or romantic purely through how it's lit. Understanding lighting basics reveals a hidden language that profoundly affects every frame you watch.

## Card: Why it matters
Lighting dramatically shapes the mood, look, and emotional feel of a film, usually without viewers consciously noticing. It's central to a film's quality and atmosphere. Understanding it makes you a more perceptive viewer and is essential for anyone making video — good lighting is often what separates amateur-looking footage from professional. It's a high-impact, underappreciated craft.

## Card: Key terms
- **Lighting**: the deliberate use of light in a scene.
- **Mood**: the emotional atmosphere lighting creates.
- **Hard vs. soft light**: harsh, defined light vs. gentle, diffused light.
- **Direction of light**: where the light comes from, shaping the look.

## Card: Light creates mood
image: filmLightingLightCreatesMoodHero
The core power of **lighting** is creating **mood**. Bright, even light feels open, cheerful, or safe; dim, shadowy light feels tense, mysterious, or ominous; warm light feels cozy or romantic; cold light feels stark or unsettling. By controlling light, filmmakers set the emotional atmosphere of a scene before a word is spoken. The same set and actors can feel completely different under different lighting — which is why lighting is such a powerful storytelling tool.

## Card: Quality and direction
Two key aspects beyond brightness. **Hard vs. soft light**: hard light (a small, direct source) creates strong shadows and a dramatic, harsh look; soft light (diffused, spread out) is gentle and flattering, with soft shadows. And the **direction of light** — where it comes from — dramatically changes the look: lighting from the front, side, below, or behind a subject creates very different effects and feelings. Skilled use of light's quality and direction shapes both the look and the emotional impact of every shot.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking lighting is just about making things visible (it powerfully shapes mood and feeling); not noticing lighting's effect as a viewer; assuming any light will do (quality and direction matter enormously); and underrating it as a craft (it's often what separates amateur from professional-looking footage). Lighting is a deliberate, high-impact tool shaping how every scene looks and feels.

## Card: Quick recap
Lighting is the deliberate use of light to shape how a scene looks and feels — a powerful, often-overlooked filmmaking tool. It creates mood (bright feels cheerful, dim feels tense, warm feels cozy, cold feels stark). Beyond brightness, the quality (hard vs. soft) and direction of light dramatically change the look and emotional impact. The same scene can feel completely different through lighting — which is why it's central to a film's atmosphere.
