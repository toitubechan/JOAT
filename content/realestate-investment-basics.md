## Card: What it is
image: realestateInvestmentBasicsHero
**Property investment** is buying property to make money — through rental income, the property rising in value over time, or both. It's a popular way to build wealth, but it's not the guaranteed, easy money it's sometimes portrayed as. Understanding the basics and real risks helps you think about it realistically. This is general educational knowledge, not financial advice.

## Card: Why it matters
Property is one of the most common ways people try to build wealth, surrounded by both genuine opportunity and a lot of hype. Understanding how property investment actually works — and its real risks and costs — helps you evaluate it sensibly rather than believing it's effortless riches. It's important knowledge for a major financial avenue many consider.

## Card: Key terms
- **Property investment**: buying property to make money.
- **Rental income**: money earned by renting the property out.
- **Capital growth**: the property rising in value over time.
- **Risk**: the genuine ways property investment can lose money.

## Card: Two ways to profit
image: realestateInvestmentBasicsTwoWaysToProfitHero
Property investors typically make money two ways. **Rental income** — renting the property out for more than its ongoing costs, providing regular income. And **capital growth** — the property rising in value over time, so it's worth more than you paid when you sell. Some investments aim for one, some for both. Understanding which you're relying on matters, since a property might give good rental income but little growth, or vice versa.

## Card: Real risks and costs
Property investment isn't guaranteed money. Real **risks** and costs include: property values can *fall* (not just rise); rental income isn't guaranteed (vacancies, problem tenants, non-payment); ongoing costs (maintenance, taxes, fees, insurance, mortgage interest) eat into returns; property is **illiquid** (hard to sell quickly if you need cash); and it often requires significant money and effort. Done well it can build wealth, but it carries genuine risk and is far from the effortless riches sometimes promised.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: believing property investment is guaranteed, easy money (values can fall and there are real risks); ignoring the ongoing costs that eat returns; assuming rental income is guaranteed (vacancies and problem tenants happen); underrating how illiquid property is; and not accounting for the effort involved. Property can build wealth, but only with realistic understanding of its genuine risks and costs.

## Card: Quick recap
Property investment means buying property to make money via rental income (renting it out profitably) and/or capital growth (the property rising in value). It's a popular wealth-building route but not guaranteed easy money: values can fall, rental income isn't assured, ongoing costs eat returns, and property is hard to sell quickly. Done well it can build wealth, but with genuine risks and costs — understand them realistically before investing.
