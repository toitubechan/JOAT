## Card: What it is
image: growthLearningSpacedRepetitionHero
**Spaced repetition** is reviewing material at increasing intervals over time, rather than all at once. Combined with how memory naturally fades, it's one of the most powerful, evidence-backed ways to remember things long-term. It works *with* your brain's forgetting, not against it.

## Card: Why it matters
Cramming everything the night before works for a test tomorrow and is forgotten by next week. Spaced repetition gets information into long-term memory efficiently, so it actually stays. For anyone learning a language, studying, or building knowledge that lasts, it's a genuine game-changer.

## Card: Key terms
- **Spaced repetition**: reviewing at increasing time intervals.
- **The forgetting curve**: how memory fades over time without review.
- **Cramming**: massed studying all at once.
- **Spacing effect**: better retention from spreading out study.

## Card: Working with forgetting
image: growthLearningSpacedRepetitionWorkingWithForgettingHero
Memory follows a **forgetting curve** — we lose information over time unless we revisit it. Spaced repetition times reviews to catch material *just* as you're about to forget it. Each well-timed review flattens the curve, so you forget more slowly. Reviewing right before forgetting strengthens the memory far more than reviewing when it's still fresh.

## Card: Spacing beats cramming
The **spacing effect** is clear: the same total study time produces much better long-term retention when *spread out* than when **crammed**. Five short sessions over a week beat one long session. Tools like flashcard apps automate this by scheduling each item's review at the optimal interval — which is why they're so effective for memorization.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: cramming all study into one session, then forgetting it; reviewing material when it's still fresh (low value) instead of when nearly forgotten; not spacing reviews over days and weeks; and abandoning a topic entirely after one study session, guaranteeing it fades. Spread the work out, and far more sticks.

## Card: Quick recap
Spaced repetition reviews material at increasing intervals, working with the forgetting curve to lock knowledge into long-term memory. The spacing effect means spread-out study beats cramming for lasting retention. Review things just as you're about to forget them — flashcard apps automate this, making memorization dramatically more efficient.
