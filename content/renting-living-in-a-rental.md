## Card: What it is
image: rentalLivingHero
Once you've moved in, **living in a rental** well means maintaining a good relationship with your landlord, handling repairs and issues properly, and meeting your responsibilities. Doing this smoothly makes your tenancy pleasant and protects your deposit and references. (General guidance for everyday renting.)

## Card: Why it matters
A good tenancy isn't just about the paperwork — it's the day-to-day. Handling issues well, paying on time, and keeping a good landlord relationship leads to fewer problems, faster repairs, your deposit back, and good references for the future. Knowing how to manage the living phase makes renting far less stressful.

## Card: Key terms
- **Tenant responsibilities**: your duties, like paying rent and basic care.
- **Reporting repairs**: notifying the landlord of issues promptly.
- **Documentation**: keeping records of communications and issues.
- **Reference**: a landlord's recommendation for future rentals.

## Card: Meet your responsibilities
As a tenant, your core **responsibilities** typically include paying rent on time, keeping the place reasonably clean and undamaged, not causing nuisance, and reporting problems. Meeting these keeps the relationship smooth and protects your standing. Paying rent on time especially matters — it's the foundation of trust and a good **reference** later.

## Card: Handle repairs and keep records
image: repairRecords
When something needs fixing, **report repairs** to the landlord promptly and in writing (so there's a record) — don't let small issues become big ones, and don't attempt repairs that are the landlord's responsibility. Keep **documentation** of all communications, agreed actions, and the property's condition. This protects you in any dispute and shows you handled things properly.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: paying rent late (damages trust and references); not reporting repairs promptly (small issues worsen, and you may be blamed); communicating only verbally with no record; ignoring your responsibilities then losing your deposit; and damaging the landlord relationship unnecessarily. Meet your duties, report issues in writing, and keep records.

## Card: Quick recap
Living in a rental well means meeting your responsibilities (pay rent on time, basic care, no nuisance), reporting repairs promptly and in writing, and keeping records of communications and the property's condition. This keeps the landlord relationship smooth, gets repairs done, protects your deposit, and earns good references for future rentals.
