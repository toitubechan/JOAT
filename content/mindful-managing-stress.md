## Card: What it is
image: stressHero
**Managing stress** is understanding what stress is and using practical ways to handle it healthily. Stress is the body and mind's response to demands or pressures — normal and even useful in small amounts, but harmful when chronic and unmanaged. Understanding it helps you handle stress better rather than being overwhelmed by it. This is general wellbeing knowledge, not medical advice.

## Card: Why it matters
Stress affects nearly everyone and, when chronic and unmanaged, harms both mental and physical health. Understanding what stress is and how to manage it is a vital life skill that protects wellbeing and quality of life. Since stress is unavoidable, knowing how to handle it healthily — rather than being controlled by it — is broadly valuable for everyone.

## Card: Key terms
- **Stress**: the response to demands or pressures.
- **Acute vs. chronic stress**: short-term vs. ongoing stress.
- **Stress response**: the body's reaction to perceived threats.
- **Coping strategies**: healthy ways to manage stress.

## Card: Stress isn't all bad
image: acuteVsChronic
A key understanding: **stress** is the body and mind's natural **stress response** to demands or pressures — and it's not inherently bad. *Acute* (short-term) stress can be useful, sharpening focus and energy to meet a challenge. The problem is *chronic* stress — ongoing, unrelieved pressure — which wears on mental and physical health over time. So the goal of stress management isn't eliminating all stress (impossible and unnecessary) but preventing it from becoming chronic and overwhelming, and handling it healthily.

## Card: Healthy ways to manage stress
Effective **coping strategies** help manage stress. These include: addressing causes where you can (problem-solving what's within your control); relaxation and calming practices (like mindfulness, deep breathing, or activities that relax you); physical activity (which genuinely reduces stress); adequate sleep and self-care; connecting with others and support; and managing your thinking (since how we *interpret* situations affects our stress). Different strategies work for different people. The key is having healthy ways to release and manage stress, rather than letting it build up unaddressed.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking all stress is bad or must be eliminated (some is normal and useful; the issue is chronic stress); ignoring stress until it becomes overwhelming; using unhealthy coping (which worsens things long-term); and not having healthy strategies to release it. Managing stress means understanding it, preventing it from becoming chronic, and using healthy coping strategies — a vital skill, since stress is unavoidable. (For persistent or severe stress, seek support.)

## Card: Quick recap
Managing stress starts with understanding it: stress is the response to demands or pressures, and acute (short-term) stress can be useful, while chronic (ongoing) stress harms health. The goal isn't eliminating all stress but preventing it from becoming chronic and overwhelming. Healthy coping strategies — addressing causes, relaxation and mindfulness, physical activity, sleep, connection, and managing your thinking — help. Having healthy ways to handle stress is a vital life skill.
