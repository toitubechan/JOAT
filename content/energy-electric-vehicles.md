## Card: What it is
image: energyElectricVehiclesHero
**Electric vehicles (EVs)** run on electricity stored in batteries and powered by electric motors, instead of burning fuel in an engine. They're a fast-growing part of the transport shift away from fossil fuels. Understanding how they work and how charging works helps you cut through the hype and confusion around them.

## Card: Why it matters
Transport is a major source of emissions, and EVs are central to reducing it. They're increasingly common, and many people will own one. Understanding the basics — how they differ from fuel cars, how charging works, and the real pros and cons — helps you make informed decisions and follow a major shift in how we get around.

## Card: Key terms
- **Electric vehicle (EV)**: a car powered by a battery and electric motor.
- **Charging**: refilling the battery with electricity.
- **Range**: how far an EV can go on a full charge.
- **Range anxiety**: worry about running out of charge.

## Card: How EVs differ
image: energyElectricVehiclesHowEvsDifferHero
An **EV** replaces the fuel engine with an electric motor and a large battery. Instead of refueling with petrol or diesel, you recharge the battery with electricity. EVs have far fewer moving parts (no engine, gearbox in the traditional sense), so less can go wrong mechanically, and they produce no exhaust emissions while driving. They're also quiet and deliver instant acceleration.

## Card: Charging and range
**Charging** can happen at home (slower, often overnight) or at public charging stations (faster). **Range** — how far an EV goes per charge — has improved greatly, though it varies by model and conditions. **Range anxiety** (fear of running out) is a common concern, easing as ranges grow and charging networks expand. Charging is different from refueling: it takes longer, so it's usually planned around parking (home, work) rather than quick stops.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking EVs are emission-free overall (they emit nothing while driving, but their total footprint depends on how the electricity was generated and battery production — still typically cleaner than fuel cars); overestimating range anxiety as ranges and chargers improve; and assuming charging works like refueling (it's slower and planned differently). EVs are cleaner but not magic.

## Card: Quick recap
Electric vehicles run on a battery and electric motor instead of a fuel engine — recharged with electricity, with no exhaust while driving, fewer moving parts, and instant acceleration. Charging happens at home or public stations and is slower than refueling, so it's planned around parking. Their overall footprint depends on the electricity source, but they're typically cleaner than fuel cars.
