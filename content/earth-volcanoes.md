## Card: What it is
image: volcanoesHero
A **volcano** is an opening in Earth's surface where molten rock, gas, and ash escape from deep below. Beneath the surface, intense heat keeps rock molten (**magma**); when it reaches the surface, it's called **lava**. Volcanoes are dramatic evidence of the immense heat and energy within our planet.

## Card: Why it matters
Volcanoes shape landscapes, create new land, affect climate, and pose hazards to people living nearby. They also reveal what's happening deep inside the Earth. Understanding them satisfies natural curiosity about one of nature's most powerful spectacles, and explains both their dangers and their role in shaping the planet.

## Card: Key terms
- **Magma**: molten rock beneath the surface.
- **Lava**: molten rock that has reached the surface.
- **Eruption**: the release of magma, gas, and ash.
- **Plate boundary**: where volcanoes most often form.

## Card: How volcanoes work
Deep inside Earth, heat keeps rock molten as **magma**. This magma is less dense than solid rock, so it rises, collecting in chambers below the surface. When pressure builds enough, it forces its way out in an **eruption**, releasing **lava**, gas, and ash. Most volcanoes form at **plate boundaries**, where Earth's plates meet and magma can reach the surface more easily.

## Card: Why eruptions differ
image: eruptionTypesHero
Not all eruptions are alike. Some ooze runny lava gently; others explode violently with ash and rock. The difference comes largely from the magma's thickness and gas content — thick, gas-rich magma traps pressure and erupts explosively, while runny magma lets gas escape and flows more gently. This is why some volcanoes build gentle slopes and others blast dramatically. Volcanoes also create new land and fertile soil over time.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking all volcanoes erupt the same explosive way (it varies with magma type); confusing magma (underground) with lava (surface); imagining volcanoes appear randomly (most cluster at plate boundaries); and seeing them as purely destructive (they also create land and fertile soil). Volcanoes are windows into Earth's molten interior.

## Card: Quick recap
A volcano is where molten rock (magma below, lava above), gas, and ash escape from deep within Earth. Magma rises because it's less dense, and erupts when pressure builds — most often at plate boundaries. Eruptions range from gentle flows to violent explosions, depending on the magma's thickness and gas. Volcanoes both endanger and create land, revealing Earth's inner heat.
