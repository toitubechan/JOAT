## Card: What it is
image: aiPatternFinderHero
**Artificial intelligence (AI)** is technology that performs tasks we'd normally think need human intelligence — understanding language, recognizing images, making predictions, or answering questions. It's not a thinking robot with feelings; it's clever software that finds patterns in huge amounts of data and uses them to respond.

## Card: Why it matters
AI now sits behind everyday tools — search, recommendations, voice assistants, chatbots, and photo tagging. Understanding what it really is cuts through both hype and fear, helping you use it well and judge claims sensibly. As AI spreads, basic literacy about it is becoming as useful as knowing how the internet works.

## Card: Key terms
- **Artificial intelligence (AI)**: software that does tasks needing "intelligence".
- **Algorithm**: a set of steps a computer follows.
- **Data**: the information AI learns patterns from.
- **Model**: the trained system that makes predictions or responses.

## Card: Pattern-finding, not thinking
image: patternNotMind
At its core, modern AI is a powerful **pattern-finder**. Show it enough examples — millions of photos, or vast amounts of text — and it learns the statistical patterns, then uses them to predict or generate. A chatbot, for instance, predicts likely next words. It's not "understanding" like a human; it's extremely sophisticated pattern-matching.

## Card: AI is a tool, not a mind
It helps to think of AI as a tool, not a being. It has no beliefs, feelings, or true understanding — it produces outputs based on patterns in its training **data**. This is why it can be impressively capable and confidently wrong at the same time. Treating it as a smart-but-fallible tool sets the right expectations.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: imagining AI thinks and feels like a human (it doesn't); assuming it's either magic or useless, rather than a capable tool with limits; and trusting its outputs as always correct. Equally, some dismiss it entirely. The balanced view: a powerful pattern-based tool, useful but needing human judgment.

## Card: Quick recap
AI is software that does tasks needing "intelligence" by finding patterns in huge amounts of data — not a thinking, feeling mind. It powers everyday tools and can be both impressive and confidently wrong. Treat it as a capable but fallible tool that still needs human judgment, and you'll use it well.
