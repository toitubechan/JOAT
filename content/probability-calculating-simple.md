## Card: What it is
image: dataProbabilityCalculatingSimpleHero
**Calculating simple probability** is easier than it sounds: for equally likely outcomes, it's just the number of ways something can happen divided by the total number of possible outcomes. With this one idea, you can work out the odds of dice, coins, cards, and many everyday situations.

## Card: Why it matters
Being able to work out basic probabilities helps you judge games, risks, and chances realistically rather than by gut feeling. It demystifies odds you encounter and builds the foundation for understanding more complex probability. It's practical numeracy for reasoning about chance.

## Card: Key terms
- **Favorable outcomes**: the results you're interested in.
- **Total outcomes**: all the possible results.
- **Equally likely**: each outcome has the same chance.
- **Odds**: another way of expressing likelihood.

## Card: The basic formula
image: dataProbabilityCalculatingSimpleTheBasicFormulaHero
For **equally likely** outcomes, probability is simple: count the **favorable outcomes** (the results you want) and divide by the **total outcomes** (all possible results). A coin: 1 way to get heads ÷ 2 possible outcomes = 1/2 (50%). A die: 1 way to roll a 4 ÷ 6 faces = 1/6. Rolling an even number: 3 favorable (2,4,6) ÷ 6 = 1/2. That's the core idea.

## Card: Putting it to use
This simple formula handles many everyday probability questions. The chance of drawing a specific card, rolling a certain number, or picking a particular item — all are favorable outcomes ÷ total outcomes (when outcomes are equally likely). The main skill is carefully counting both numbers: how many ways can it happen, and how many total possibilities are there?

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: applying the formula when outcomes *aren't* equally likely (it only works when they are); miscounting the total possible outcomes; confusing "favorable" with "total"; and overcomplicating simple problems. Also, this basic formula is for single events — combining events needs more care. Count carefully, and check outcomes are equally likely.

## Card: Quick recap
For equally likely outcomes, probability = favorable outcomes ÷ total outcomes. A coin's heads is 1/2; a die's "4" is 1/6; an even roll is 3/6 = 1/2. The skill is carefully counting both the favorable and total outcomes, and checking the outcomes really are equally likely. This simple idea handles many everyday chance questions.
