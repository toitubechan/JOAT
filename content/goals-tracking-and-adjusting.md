## Card: What it is
image: growthGoalsTrackingAndAdjustingHero
**Tracking** is measuring your progress toward a goal, and **adjusting** is changing your approach (or the goal itself) based on what you learn. Goals aren't set-and-forget; they're living things that need monitoring and occasional course-correction to stay realistic and on track.

## Card: Why it matters
Without tracking, you don't know if you're making progress or drifting, and motivation suffers. And rigidly sticking to a goal that no longer fits — or an approach that isn't working — wastes effort. Tracking and adjusting keep goals alive, realistic, and effective as circumstances change.

## Card: Key terms
- **Tracking**: measuring progress regularly.
- **Review**: periodically checking how a goal is going.
- **Course-correction**: adjusting your approach based on results.
- **Sunk cost**: clinging to something just because you've invested in it.

## Card: Track to stay on course
image: growthGoalsTrackingAndAdjustingTrackToStayOnCourseHero
**Tracking** progress — a journal, an app, a simple checklist — does two things: it shows whether you're actually advancing, and the visible progress itself motivates. Regular **reviews** (weekly or monthly) let you catch problems early. What gets measured tends to improve, simply because you're paying attention to it.

## Card: Adjust without giving up
When tracking shows something's off, **course-correct**: maybe the *approach* needs changing (a different method), or the *goal* needs adjusting (it was unrealistic, or your priorities shifted). Adjusting isn't failure — it's smart. But beware the **sunk cost** trap: don't cling to a goal just because you've already invested, if it no longer serves you.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: setting a goal then never checking progress; treating any adjustment as failure and quitting instead; the opposite — abandoning goals at the first setback rather than adjusting the approach; and clinging to outdated goals out of sunk cost. Track regularly, adjust the approach before the goal, and let goals evolve sensibly.

## Card: Quick recap
Track your progress to stay on course and stay motivated, with regular reviews to catch issues early. Adjust intelligently when something's off — change the approach, or the goal itself if it no longer fits — without treating adjustment as failure. Avoid clinging to outdated goals out of sunk cost. Goals are living things.
