## Card: What it is
image: futuretechSelfDrivingHero
**Self-driving cars** (autonomous vehicles) use sensors and AI to drive themselves, with little or no human input. They sense their surroundings, decide how to respond, and control the vehicle. The technology has advanced dramatically but isn't yet fully solved everywhere. Understanding it cuts through hype to the real state and challenges of a much-discussed innovation.

## Card: Why it matters
Self-driving cars could transform transport, safety, and cities — and they're heavily hyped and debated. Understanding how they work, what "levels" of automation mean, and the genuine challenges helps you follow the technology realistically, neither over-believing nor dismissing it. It's a flagship example of AI moving into the physical world.

## Card: Key terms
- **Autonomous vehicle**: a self-driving car.
- **Sensors**: cameras, radar, and lidar that perceive surroundings.
- **Levels of autonomy**: stages from driver assistance to fully self-driving.
- **Edge cases**: rare, tricky situations that are hard to handle.

## Card: How they work
image: futuretechSelfDrivingHowTheyWorkHero
A self-driving car uses many **sensors** — cameras, radar, and often lidar (laser-based sensing) — to build a constant picture of its surroundings (other cars, pedestrians, signs, lanes). AI software interprets this and decides how to steer, accelerate, and brake, controlling the car. It's the sense-think-act loop of robotics applied to driving, requiring the AI to handle the immense complexity of real roads in real time.

## Card: Levels and the hard part
There are **levels of autonomy**, from simple driver assistance (lane-keeping, adaptive cruise) up to full self-driving with no human needed. Most cars today are at lower levels; true full autonomy everywhere isn't yet achieved. The hardest challenge is **edge cases** — rare, unpredictable, or unusual situations (odd road conditions, unexpected behavior) that humans handle intuitively but are very hard to program for. Handling the rare 1% safely is what makes full self-driving so difficult.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking fully self-driving cars are already everywhere (most are at lower assistance levels; full autonomy isn't solved); assuming any car with "autopilot" features fully drives itself (it doesn't — driver attention is still needed); underrating the edge-case challenge; and either over-hyping or dismissing the tech. The reality is rapid progress with real, unsolved challenges remaining.

## Card: Quick recap
