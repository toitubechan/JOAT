## Card: What it is
image: dataMentalmathWhyItMattersHero
**Mental math** is doing calculations in your head, without a calculator or paper. It's not about being a human computer — it's about a handful of clever **tricks** and habits that make everyday sums quick and easy. Anyone can learn them, and they're genuinely useful in daily life.

## Card: Why it matters
Despite having calculators everywhere, quick mental math is hugely practical — checking a bill, splitting a cost, estimating a discount, judging whether a figure makes sense. It saves time, builds number confidence, and helps you catch errors. It also keeps your mind sharp. The tricks are simple and the payoff is daily.

## Card: Key terms
- **Mental math**: calculating in your head.
- **Trick / shortcut**: a faster method than the standard one.
- **Estimation**: getting a close-enough answer quickly.
- **Number sense**: an intuitive feel for numbers.

## Card: It's tricks, not talent
image: dataMentalmathWhyItMattersItSTricksNotTalentHero
A reassuring truth: people who are good at mental math usually aren't geniuses — they've just learned a few **tricks and shortcuts** and practiced them. Mental math isn't an innate gift; it's a learnable skill built from simple methods (rounding, breaking numbers apart, useful patterns). You don't need to compute like a machine — just know a handful of techniques for common situations.

## Card: Building number sense
Beyond speed, mental math builds **number sense** — an intuitive feel for how numbers work and roughly what answers should be. This helps you catch mistakes (a total that's clearly too big), judge whether figures and statistics are plausible, and make quick decisions. Even rough **estimation** — getting close without exact precision — is often all you need and is a core part of practical mental math.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking mental math requires special talent (it's learnable tricks); believing you must get exact answers (estimation is often enough); avoiding it because calculators exist (mental math is faster for many everyday checks); and not realizing it builds valuable number sense. Mental math is practical, learnable, and worth the small effort.

## Card: Quick recap
Mental math is doing calculations in your head, using a handful of learnable tricks — not innate talent. It's practical for everyday life (bills, tips, discounts, checking figures), saves time, and builds number sense: an intuitive feel for numbers that helps you spot errors and judge plausibility. Even rough estimation is often all you need.
