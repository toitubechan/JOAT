## Card: What it is
image: cryptoHero
**Cryptocurrency** is digital money that exists only electronically and isn't issued or controlled by any government or bank. Instead, it runs on a shared, public record maintained by a worldwide network of computers. Bitcoin was the first; thousands of others followed. The "crypto" part refers to the cryptography (advanced math) that secures it.

## Card: Why it matters
Crypto is talked about everywhere, often with big promises and bigger risks. Understanding the basics helps you cut through hype and scams, whether or not you ever own any. Important: crypto is highly volatile and largely unregulated — this is general knowledge, not investment advice, and you should never risk money you can't afford to lose.

## Card: Key terms
- **Cryptocurrency**: digital money secured by cryptography.
- **Blockchain**: the shared public record of all transactions.
- **Decentralized**: run by a network, not a central authority.
- **Bitcoin**: the first and best-known cryptocurrency.

## Card: What makes it different
image: blockchainChain
Normal money is controlled by central banks and moved through commercial banks. Crypto is **decentralized** — no single institution runs it. Transactions are recorded on a public **blockchain** that thousands of computers verify together. Supporters value this independence; critics point to the lack of oversight, protection, and stability.

## Card: What it's used for
People use crypto in a few ways: as a speculative investment (hoping the price rises), as a way to send value across borders, or out of belief in money outside government control. But it's far less accepted than regular money for everyday purchases, and its wild price swings make it impractical as stable cash for most people.

## Card: Common mistakes
A big mistake is treating crypto as a guaranteed way to get rich — prices can crash as fast as they rise. Another is investing money you actually need, given the volatility. And many don't realize crypto largely lacks the protections of banks: if it's lost, stolen, or scammed, there's often no one to refund you.

## Card: Quick recap
Cryptocurrency is decentralized digital money, secured by cryptography and recorded on a public blockchain, with no central bank in charge. It's used for speculation, transfers, and ideology — but it's volatile, largely unregulated, and unprotected. Treat it as high-risk, never bet essential money, and beware the hype.
