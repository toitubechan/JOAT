## Card: What it is
image: reduceFoodWasteHero
**Reducing food waste** means using more of what you buy and throwing away less. A huge amount of bought food ends up binned — money straight in the trash. A few simple habits around storing, using up, and rethinking dates can dramatically cut this waste.

## Card: Why it matters
Wasted food is wasted money — the average household bins a striking share of its groceries. It's also an environmental issue: all the resources to grow, transport, and store that food are wasted too. Cutting waste is a rare win-win, saving you cash while reducing your impact, with minimal effort.

## Card: Key terms
- **Food waste**: edible food that's thrown away.
- **Use-it-up**: cooking from what needs eating soon.
- **Best-before**: a quality date, food often fine after it.
- **Storage know-how**: storing food to make it last.

## Card: Use what you have
image: useItUpMeal
A core habit is the **use-it-up** mindset: before shopping or cooking something new, use what's already there, especially items nearing their date. A "leftovers night" or a fridge-clear-out soup or stir-fry turns odds and ends into a meal. Planning meals around what needs eating soon prevents food quietly expiring at the back of the fridge.

## Card: Store smart and rethink dates
**Storage know-how** extends food's life: keep things in the right place, freeze items before they spoil, and store leftovers properly. Also, don't over-trust **best-before** dates — they're about *quality*, not safety, so plenty of food is fine to eat after (use your senses). Confusing best-before with use-by causes a lot of needless waste.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: buying more than you'll use; letting food expire forgotten at the back of the fridge; binning food at its best-before date when it's still fine; not freezing things before they spoil; and ignoring leftovers. Small habits — buying sensibly, using up, freezing, and reading dates correctly — cut most waste.

## Card: Quick recap
Reducing food waste saves money and resources. Use what you have first, especially items near their date, with leftover meals and clear-out dishes. Store food well and freeze before it spoils. Remember best-before is about quality, not safety, so don't bin good food. Small habits add up to real savings.
