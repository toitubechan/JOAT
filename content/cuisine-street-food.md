## Card: What it is
image: cuisineStreetFoodHero
**Street food** is the inexpensive, ready-to-eat food sold by vendors in public places — and around the world, it's often some of the most authentic, delicious, and beloved food a culture offers. Far from being lesser food, street food is frequently where a place's true everyday culinary culture lives. Understanding it reveals a vibrant, accessible window into world cuisines.

## Card: Why it matters
Street food is how much of the world actually eats, and it's often the most authentic and flavorful expression of a local cuisine. Understanding it deepens appreciation of food cultures, makes travel and exploration richer, and challenges the assumption that "good food" means expensive restaurants. It's a vibrant, democratic, and delicious part of global food culture worth understanding.

## Card: Key terms
- **Street food**: inexpensive ready-to-eat food sold by vendors.
- **Authenticity**: reflecting genuine local food culture.
- **Everyday cuisine**: the food people actually eat daily.
- **Food culture**: how food reflects a place's life and traditions.

## Card: Authentic and beloved
image: cuisineStreetFoodAuthenticAndBelovedHero
A key insight: **street food** is often the most **authentic** food a culture offers — the **everyday cuisine** people actually eat, made with local ingredients and time-honored recipes, refined by vendors who often specialize in a single dish for years. In many places, the best, most beloved local food is found not in fancy restaurants but at street stalls and markets. Street food reflects genuine local **food culture** directly and deliciously — it's everyday eating elevated by tradition and specialization.

## Card: A window into cultures
Street food around the world is incredibly diverse, each place with its own iconic specialties reflecting local ingredients, history, and tastes. Exploring street food is one of the best ways to experience a place's **food culture** — it's accessible, affordable, social, and authentic. It also challenges the idea that quality food must be expensive: some of the world's most delicious, skillfully made food is cheap street fare. Street food is a vibrant, democratic celebration of everyday culinary culture worldwide.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking street food is lesser or just "cheap food" (it's often the most authentic and delicious food a culture offers); assuming good food requires expensive restaurants (some of the best food is street fare); overlooking street food as a window into local culture; and not appreciating the skill behind it (vendors often perfect a single dish over years). Street food is authentic, skillful, beloved everyday cuisine worldwide.

## Card: Quick recap
Street food is inexpensive, ready-to-eat food from vendors — and around the world, it's often the most authentic and delicious expression of a local cuisine, the everyday food people actually eat, refined by specialist vendors. Far from lesser, it's frequently where a place's true culinary culture lives, challenging the idea that good food means expensive restaurants. Exploring street food is an accessible, vibrant window into world food cultures.
