## Card: What it is
image: coffeeBasicsHero
Good coffee starts with understanding a few basics: the **beans**, how they're **roasted**, how they're **ground**, and the **brewing method**. Each step shapes the final taste. You don't need to be a barista — just to grasp these few levers that turn so-so coffee into a genuinely good cup.

## Card: Why it matters
Most people make coffee on autopilot and wonder why café coffee tastes better. Knowing the basics lets you improve your home cup dramatically and order with confidence. Small changes — fresher beans, the right grind — often make a bigger difference than expensive machines.

## Card: Key terms
- **Roast**: how long beans are roasted (light to dark).
- **Grind**: how finely beans are ground.
- **Brewing method**: how water meets the grounds (filter, espresso, etc.).
- **Freshness**: how recently beans were roasted and ground.

## Card: Beans, roast, and freshness
Coffee flavor starts with the beans and their **roast** — lighter roasts taste brighter and more acidic, darker roasts bolder and more bitter. But the biggest everyday factor is **freshness**: coffee tastes best soon after roasting, and especially soon after grinding. Pre-ground coffee fades fast, which is why freshly ground beans are a noticeable upgrade.

## Card: Grind and brewing methods
image: grindBrewMatch
The **grind** must match the **brewing method**: espresso needs a fine grind, filter a medium one, and methods like French press a coarse one. The wrong grind gives weak or bitter coffee. Common methods include filter/drip (clean, easy), French press (full-bodied), and espresso (concentrated, the base for lattes and cappuccinos).

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: using stale, long-opened pre-ground coffee; the wrong grind for the method (causing bitterness or weakness); water that's boiling-hot (just off the boil is better, as boiling water scorches coffee); and wildly guessing amounts. Freshness, the right grind, and slightly-cooled water fix most home coffee.

## Card: Quick recap
Good coffee comes from fresh beans, a roast you enjoy, a grind matched to your brewing method, and water just off the boil. Freshness — especially grinding just before brewing — is the biggest everyday upgrade. Match grind to method (fine for espresso, coarse for French press), and you'll beat most café autopilot at home.
