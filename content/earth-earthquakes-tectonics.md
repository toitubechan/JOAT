## Card: What it is
image: earthquakesTectonicsHero
**Earthquakes** are sudden shaking of the ground, caused by the movement of Earth's giant plates. **Plate tectonics** is the theory explaining how these plates move and interact — the foundation of modern Earth science. Earthquakes are the dramatic, sometimes destructive, result of these slow-moving plates suddenly slipping.

## Card: Why it matters
Earthquakes are major natural hazards affecting millions, and plate tectonics is the grand theory tying together earthquakes, volcanoes, and mountains. Understanding it reveals how the planet's surface really works — as a set of moving pieces — and explains why earthquakes happen where they do, and why they can't be precisely predicted.

## Card: Key terms
- **Plate tectonics**: the theory of Earth's moving plates.
- **Fault**: a crack where plates or rock can slip.
- **Fault line**: where earthquakes commonly occur.
- **Seismic waves**: the energy waves that shake the ground.

## Card: Plates that move
Earth's surface is made of huge **tectonic plates** that slowly drift, driven by heat from deep inside. They meet at boundaries, where they pull apart, push together, or grind past each other. This slow movement builds enormous stress in the rock. Plate tectonics — the understanding that the surface is made of moving plates — unified Earth science, explaining earthquakes, volcanoes, and mountains all at once.

## Card: Why the ground shakes
image: faultEnergyReleaseHero
Where plates meet, rock can get stuck along a **fault** (a crack) even as the plates keep trying to move. Stress builds until the rock suddenly slips, releasing the stored energy as **seismic waves** that shake the ground — an earthquake. This is why earthquakes cluster along **fault lines** at plate boundaries. The sudden release of built-up stress is what makes them so abrupt and hard to predict precisely.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking earthquakes happen randomly anywhere (they cluster at plate boundaries and faults); imagining we can precisely predict them (we can't — only assess risk in zones); thinking the ground is perfectly solid and still (plates are always slowly moving); and not connecting earthquakes, volcanoes, and mountains (plate tectonics links them all).

## Card: Quick recap
Earthquakes are sudden ground shaking caused by Earth's tectonic plates slipping. Plate tectonics — the surface made of slowly moving plates — explains them: stress builds along faults until rock suddenly slips, releasing energy as seismic waves. Quakes cluster at plate boundaries, can't be precisely predicted, and tie together earthquakes, volcanoes, and mountains in one unifying theory.
