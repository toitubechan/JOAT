## Card: What it is
image: financeInsuranceTypesYouMightNeedHero
There are many types of insurance, each protecting a different part of life — your health, your home, your car, your income, your loved ones. Not everyone needs every type. This is a general map of the common ones so you can recognize what they cover and judge what's relevant to you.

## Card: Why it matters
Buying insurance you don't need wastes money; lacking insurance you *do* need risks disaster. Knowing the main types helps you prioritize — covering the big, likely-to-matter risks first. What you need depends on your life stage, dependents, and what you own, so it's personal, not one-size-fits-all.

## Card: Key terms
- **Health insurance**: covers medical costs.
- **Liability insurance**: covers harm you cause to others.
- **Life insurance**: pays your dependents if you die.
- **Contents / home insurance**: covers your home and belongings.

## Card: Protecting your body and income
image: financeInsuranceTypesYouMightNeedProtectingYourBodyAndIncomeHero
**Health insurance** covers medical treatment, which can otherwise be ruinously expensive. **Income protection** or **disability insurance** replaces some earnings if illness or injury stops you working. These matter most because your ability to earn is often your biggest financial asset — losing it hurts more than losing any object.

## Card: Protecting property and others
**Home or contents insurance** covers your dwelling and belongings against fire, theft, and damage. **Car insurance** covers vehicles — and **liability** cover (often legally required) pays for harm or damage you cause to others. **Life insurance** matters if people depend on your income, paying out to support them if you die.

## Card: Common mistakes
The first mistake is insuring possessions heavily while leaving health, income, or liability — the truly catastrophic risks — uncovered. Another is buying cover for risks that don't apply to you (life insurance with no dependents). And many never review policies as life changes, staying over- or under-covered for years.

## Card: Quick recap
Insurance types cover health, income, home, car, liability, and life. Prioritize the catastrophic risks — your health and earning ability — over replaceable objects. Match cover to your actual life: dependents, assets, and stage. Review it as things change, since needs shift over time.
