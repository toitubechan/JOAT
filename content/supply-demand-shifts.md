## Card: What it is
image: econSupplydemandShiftsHero
Supply and demand aren't fixed — they **shift** as conditions change, and when they do, prices move. A new trend boosts demand; a bad harvest cuts supply. Understanding how these shifts move prices lets you explain and even anticipate price changes in the real world.

## Card: Why it matters
Prices change constantly, and shifts in supply or demand are usually why. Understanding this lets you make sense of why something suddenly got more expensive or cheaper — from fuel to housing to concert tickets. It turns confusing price swings into understandable responses to changing conditions.

## Card: Key terms
- **Demand shift**: a change in how much people want something.
- **Supply shift**: a change in how much is available.
- **Driver**: an underlying cause of a shift (trends, costs, events).
- **Price response**: how price moves when supply or demand shifts.

## Card: When demand shifts
image: econSupplydemandShiftsWhenDemandShiftsHero
If **demand** rises (a new trend, more buyers, higher incomes) while supply stays the same, prices tend to rise — more people competing for the same goods. If demand falls (the trend fades, fewer buyers), prices tend to drop. Think of a hyped new product: surging demand pushes prices up, until the hype fades and they fall.

## Card: When supply shifts
If **supply** falls (a bad harvest, a factory closure, a disruption) while demand stays the same, prices tend to rise — less to go around. If supply rises (new producers, better technology, a bumper crop), prices tend to fall. This is why disruptions to oil, crops, or manufacturing often spike prices: supply dropped while demand held steady.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: blaming all price rises on greed rather than supply/demand shifts; not distinguishing a demand-driven rise from a supply-driven one (they have different causes); assuming prices change randomly; and forgetting that both sides can shift at once. Look for the **driver** — what changed in supply or demand — and the price move makes sense.

## Card: Quick recap
Supply and demand shift as conditions change, moving prices. Rising demand or falling supply pushes prices up; falling demand or rising supply pushes them down. Real price changes — fuel, food, housing — usually trace to such shifts. Identify the driver behind a shift, and confusing price swings become understandable.
