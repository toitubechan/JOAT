## Card: What it is
image: gettingSupportHero
**Getting support** for mental wellbeing means recognizing when self-help isn't enough and reaching out — to professionals, trusted people, or support resources. Knowing when and how to seek help, and that doing so is a sign of strength, is genuinely important. This is general wellbeing knowledge; for mental health concerns, qualified professionals and appropriate services are the right resources. If you're in crisis, contact emergency or crisis services.

## Card: Why it matters
Many people struggle with mental wellbeing far longer than necessary, avoiding help due to stigma, shame, or not recognizing they need it. Knowing when to seek support — and that it's a sign of strength, not weakness — can profoundly improve (or even save) lives. Understanding this is one of the most important pieces of mental wellbeing knowledge anyone can have. Reaching out at the right time matters enormously.

## Card: Key terms
- **Getting support**: reaching out for help with mental wellbeing.
- **Strength, not weakness**: reframing help-seeking positively.
- **Warning signs**: indicators that it's time to seek help.
- **Crisis support**: urgent help for severe situations.

## Card: Seeking help is strength
image: helpIsStrength
A vital reframe: seeking support for mental wellbeing is a sign of **strength, not weakness**. Just as you'd see a doctor for a persistent physical problem, getting help for mental and emotional struggles is sensible and healthy — not shameful or a failure. Stigma stops far too many people from getting help that genuinely works. Understanding that reaching out is a brave, sensible, strong choice — and that effective help exists — is the first and most important step toward getting support.

## Card: When to reach out
Consider seeking support when you notice **warning signs**: persistent low mood, anxiety, or distress that doesn't lift; struggles that interfere with daily life, work, or relationships; feeling unable to cope; withdrawing from others; or simply struggling more than you can manage alone. You don't have to wait until things are severe — earlier support often helps more. Support ranges from talking to trusted people, to professional help (therapists, doctors, counselors). And crucially: if you're in **crisis** or having thoughts of harming yourself, reach out urgently to crisis or emergency services — immediate help is available.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: viewing seeking help as weakness or failure (it's strength); waiting until things are severe before reaching out (earlier help often works better); not recognizing warning signs; thinking you should handle everything alone; and not knowing crisis support exists. Seeking support is a strong, sensible choice, and effective help exists. If you're in crisis or at risk of harming yourself, contact crisis or emergency services immediately.

## Card: Quick recap
Getting support for mental wellbeing means recognizing when to reach out — and crucially, that doing so is a sign of strength, not weakness, despite the stigma that stops too many people. Watch for warning signs: persistent distress, struggles interfering with life, or feeling unable to cope; you needn't wait until things are severe, as earlier help often works better. Support ranges from trusted people to professionals. If in crisis or at risk of self-harm, contact crisis or emergency services immediately.
