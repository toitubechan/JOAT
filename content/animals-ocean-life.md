## Card: What it is
image: oceanLifeHero
**Ocean life** is the incredible diversity of creatures living in the seas — from microscopic plankton to the largest animal ever, the blue whale. The ocean covers most of the planet and holds an enormous variety of life, much of it still unexplored. It's a vast, largely hidden living world right here on Earth.

## Card: Why it matters
Oceans cover most of Earth and are central to life — producing much of our oxygen, regulating climate, and feeding billions. Ocean life is astonishingly diverse and important, yet much remains unexplored. Understanding it reveals how vital and mysterious the seas are, and why protecting them matters for the whole planet.

## Card: Key terms
- **Plankton**: tiny drifting organisms at the base of ocean food chains.
- **Marine**: living in the sea.
- **Coral reef**: a diverse ecosystem built by tiny organisms.
- **Food chain**: who eats whom in an ecosystem.

## Card: From plankton to whales
image: planktonToWhalesHero
Ocean life spans an immense range of sizes. At the base are **plankton** — tiny drifting organisms, including microscopic plants that, remarkably, produce a large share of the world's oxygen. Plankton feed small creatures, which feed bigger ones, up to giant predators and whales — the largest animals ever to exist. This **food chain** links the tiniest to the biggest in one connected web beneath the waves.

## Card: Reefs and the unexplored deep
**Coral reefs**, built by tiny coral organisms over ages, are among the most diverse ecosystems on Earth — teeming with life, though sensitive to environmental change. Beyond the reefs and surface waters lies the deep ocean — vast, dark, and so under-explored that we've mapped less of it than the surface of some other worlds. New ocean species are discovered regularly. The seas hold much we've yet to understand.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking forests produce most of our oxygen (ocean plankton produce a large share); underrating ocean biodiversity (it's immense); assuming the oceans are well-explored (much of the deep remains a mystery); and not realizing how connected ocean life is, from plankton to whales. The ocean is a vast, vital, largely hidden living world.

## Card: Quick recap
Ocean life ranges from tiny plankton (which produce much of our oxygen and base the food chains) up through fish and predators to the giant blue whale. Coral reefs are among Earth's most diverse, sensitive ecosystems. The deep ocean is vast and largely unexplored, with new species found regularly. Covering most of the planet, the oceans are a vital, mysterious living world worth protecting.
