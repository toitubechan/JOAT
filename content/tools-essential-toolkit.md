## Card: What it is
image: toolkitHero
A basic **toolkit** is a small set of versatile tools that cover the vast majority of household tasks. You don't need a garage full of gear — just a well-chosen handful. Knowing what belongs in a starter kit, and what each tool does, makes you ready for most everyday fixes.

## Card: Why it matters
Having the right tool turns a frustrating, postponed job into a five-minute fix. Many people put off small repairs simply because they lack a screwdriver or a tape measure. A modest, sensible kit removes that excuse and pays for itself the first time it saves a call-out.

## Card: Key terms
- **Toolkit**: a curated set of basic tools.
- **Fastener**: anything that joins things, like a screw or nail.
- **Manual tool**: one powered by your hand, not electricity.
- **Versatile**: useful across many different tasks.

## Card: The starter set
image: starterSet
A solid beginner kit: a **screwdriver** set (flat and cross-head), a **hammer**, an adjustable **wrench**, **pliers**, a **tape measure**, a **utility knife**, and a **spirit level**. Add some tape, screws, and nails. These cover hanging, fixing, measuring, and assembling — most of what a home throws at you.

## Card: Buy decent, not fancy
For a starter kit, mid-range tools beat both the cheapest and the most expensive. Bargain-bin tools bend, slip, and frustrate; top-end professional gear is overkill for occasional use. Reliable mid-range tools last for years of household use. Buy good basics once rather than replacing cheap ones repeatedly.

## Card: Common mistakes
A common mistake is buying a giant pre-packed kit full of tools you'll never use, while missing a few key ones. Another is choosing the cheapest tools, which perform poorly and wear out fast. And many buy tools only in a crisis, paying a premium and grabbing whatever's available rather than the right thing.

## Card: Quick recap
A good toolkit is small and well-chosen: screwdrivers, a hammer, wrench, pliers, tape measure, utility knife, and level cover most jobs. Buy reliable mid-range tools once rather than cheap ones twice. Having the basics on hand is what turns postponed chores into quick, satisfying fixes.
