## Card: What it is
image: coffeeTeaWorldHero
**Coffee** and **tea** are the world's most popular drinks beyond water, each made by steeping a plant in hot water. Coffee comes from roasted **coffee beans**; tea from the leaves of the **tea plant**. Both span a huge range of flavors and styles, but the basics are simple and worth knowing.

## Card: Why it matters
Most people drink these daily yet know little about them. A little understanding helps you make a better cup at home, order confidently, and appreciate what you're drinking — often improving your daily ritual for free. It also demystifies the sometimes intimidating world of cafés and specialty drinks.

## Card: Key terms
- **Coffee bean**: the roasted seed used to make coffee.
- **Tea plant (Camellia sinensis)**: the single plant most true teas come from.
- **Brewing**: extracting flavor by steeping in hot water.
- **Infusion**: a drink made by steeping, like tea.

## Card: From plant to cup
image: plantToCup
Both drinks are an **infusion** — flavor pulled from a plant by hot water. For coffee, beans are roasted, ground, and **brewed**. For tea, leaves are processed and steeped. Remarkably, most true teas (green, black, white, oolong) come from the *same* plant, **Camellia sinensis** — the differences come from how the leaves are processed.

## Card: One plant, many teas
A surprising fact: green, black, white, and oolong tea all come from one plant. What makes them different is **processing** — mainly how much the leaves are allowed to oxidize (react with air). Herbal "teas" like mint or chamomile are technically different — they're infusions of *other* plants, not the tea plant at all.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking each tea type comes from a different plant (most come from one); assuming herbal teas are "real" tea (they're infusions of other plants); and believing good coffee or tea requires expensive gear. The basics — decent ingredients, hot water, and a little care — matter far more than fancy equipment.

## Card: Quick recap
Coffee and tea are infusions — flavor steeped from a plant in hot water. Coffee uses roasted beans; most true teas come from a single plant, with processing creating green, black, and the rest. Herbal teas are other plants entirely. You don't need fancy gear — good basics and a little care make a great cup.
