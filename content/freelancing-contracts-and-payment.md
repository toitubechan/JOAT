## Card: What it is
image: freelanceContractsPaymentHero
**Contracts** and **getting paid** are the protective, practical side of freelancing. A contract sets out what you'll do and how you'll be paid; good payment practices ensure you actually receive the money. Both protect you from the all-too-common problem of doing work and then struggling to get paid for it.

## Card: Why it matters
Freelancers regularly face late payment, scope disputes, and occasionally non-payment. A clear agreement and sensible payment terms prevent most of these headaches. This is general guidance, not legal advice — but knowing the basics protects your income and saves enormous stress compared to working on vague, handshake terms.

## Card: Key terms
- **Contract / agreement**: a written record of the terms.
- **Scope**: exactly what work is (and isn't) included.
- **Deposit / upfront payment**: money paid before work begins.
- **Invoice**: a request for payment with clear terms.

## Card: Why a contract protects you
image: contractPaymentProtectionHero
A written **contract** (or even a clear written agreement) spells out the **scope** (what's included), the price, deadlines, and payment terms. It protects both sides and prevents disputes — especially **scope creep**, where a client keeps adding work that wasn't agreed. "It's in the contract" is far stronger than "I thought we agreed." Always get the key terms in writing.

## Card: Getting paid reliably
Protect your income with smart practices: ask for a **deposit** or partial upfront payment (especially with new clients), send clear **invoices** with due dates, and state your payment terms in advance. For larger projects, milestone payments reduce risk. These steps dramatically cut the chances of doing work and then chasing payment for months.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: working with no written agreement, then disputing what was promised; not defining scope, so it creeps endlessly; never taking a deposit, then risking non-payment; vague or late invoicing; and being too shy to chase overdue payments professionally. Clear written terms and upfront payment protect you from most of these.

## Card: Quick recap
Protect yourself with a written contract setting out scope, price, deadlines, and payment terms — preventing disputes and scope creep. Get paid reliably by taking deposits, invoicing clearly with due dates, and using milestones for big projects. Working on vague verbal terms is the root of most freelance payment troubles.
