## Card: What it is
image: hygieneHotspotsHero
Kitchens and bathrooms are the two rooms where cleaning is about **hygiene**, not just looks — they're where germs concentrate. Cleaning them well means knowing the real hotspots and the difference between *cleaning* (removing dirt) and *disinfecting* (killing germs). Both rooms reward a little know-how.

## Card: Why it matters
These rooms are where illness-causing germs spread most — through food prep and bathroom use. Surfaces that *look* clean can still harbor bacteria. Knowing where germs hide and how to actually kill them protects your health, not just your home's appearance. This is cleaning that genuinely keeps you well.

## Card: Key terms
- **Cleaning**: removing visible dirt and grime.
- **Disinfecting**: killing germs on a surface.
- **High-touch surface**: spots many hands touch, like handles and taps.
- **Hotspot**: an area where germs concentrate.

## Card: The real germ hotspots
The dirtiest spots aren't always obvious. In the kitchen: sponges and cloths (often germ-ridden), chopping boards, the sink, and **high-touch surfaces** like the fridge handle and tap. In the bathroom: the toilet area, taps, light switch, and door handle. Focus disinfecting effort on these, not just the visible floor.

## Card: Clean, then disinfect
image: cleanThenDisinfect
**Cleaning** and **disinfecting** are different steps. Clean first to remove dirt and grime, because disinfectant works poorly on a dirty surface. *Then* disinfect high-touch and food-related areas to kill germs, giving the product its dwell time. On surfaces that touch food, rinse afterward if the product requires it.

## Card: Common mistakes
A big one is using a filthy sponge or cloth to "clean," spreading germs around — replace or sanitize them regularly. Another is disinfecting a visibly dirty surface, which doesn't work; clean first. People also forget high-touch spots like handles and switches, and use the same cloth in the bathroom and kitchen, cross-contaminating.

## Card: Quick recap
Kitchens and bathrooms need hygiene, not just tidiness. Target the real hotspots — sponges, boards, sinks, taps, handles, switches. Clean to remove dirt first, then disinfect to kill germs, allowing dwell time. Keep cloths clean and separate per room. This protects your health, not just appearances.
