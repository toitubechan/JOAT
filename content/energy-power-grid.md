## Card: What it is
image: energyPowerGridHero
The **power grid** is the vast network that delivers electricity from where it's generated to homes and businesses. It's an engineering marvel that most people never think about — a continent-spanning system that must balance supply and demand instantly, every second, to keep the lights on.

## Card: Why it matters
The grid underpins modern life — almost everything depends on reliable electricity. Understanding how it works explains blackouts, why renewable energy poses challenges, and how the system stays balanced. It's the hidden infrastructure behind the simple act of flipping a switch, and it's central to energy and climate discussions.

## Card: Key terms
- **Grid**: the network delivering electricity.
- **Generation**: producing electricity at power plants.
- **Transmission**: carrying electricity long distances at high voltage.
- **Supply and demand balance**: matching electricity made to electricity used.

## Card: From plant to plug
image: energyPowerGridFromPlantToPlugHero
Electricity travels in stages: **generation** (power plants produce it), **transmission** (high-voltage lines carry it long distances efficiently), then **distribution** (lower-voltage lines deliver it to homes). High voltage is used for long-distance transmission because it loses less energy; it's stepped down before reaching you. This network of generation, lines, and substations is the grid.

## Card: The constant balancing act
The grid's biggest challenge: electricity is hard to store at scale, so **supply must match demand in real time**, every second. Grid operators constantly adjust generation to match how much everyone is using — more on a hot evening, less overnight. If supply and demand fall out of balance, blackouts can result. This constant balancing is why the grid is such an impressive, delicate system.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking electricity is stored in the grid waiting to be used (mostly it's generated as needed, balanced in real time); not realizing why renewables pose a challenge (their output varies with weather, complicating the balance); and underrating the grid's complexity. The need for instant supply-demand balance is the key to understanding it.

## Card: Quick recap
The power grid delivers electricity through generation, high-voltage transmission, and distribution to your home. Its defining challenge is that electricity is hard to store, so supply must match demand instantly, every second — a constant balancing act. This is why variable renewables are challenging and why the grid is a remarkable, delicate piece of hidden infrastructure.
