## Card: What it is
image: histReligionsAbrahamicHero
The **Abrahamic religions** — **Judaism**, **Christianity**, and **Islam** — are three of the world's major faiths, sharing common roots traced to the figure of Abraham. Together they have billions of followers. This card set gives a respectful, basic overview of each and what connects them.

## Card: Why it matters
These three religions have profoundly shaped world history, culture, and the lives of billions. Understanding their basics and shared heritage builds cultural literacy and fosters respect. Because they're so widespread and historically intertwined, a basic grasp helps you understand much of the world's past and present.

## Card: Key terms
- **Abrahamic**: tracing roots to the figure Abraham.
- **Monotheism**: belief in one God.
- **Sacred text**: a central holy scripture.
- **Shared heritage**: common roots and figures across the three.

## Card: Shared roots
image: histReligionsAbrahamicSharedRootsHero
All three are **monotheistic** (believing in one God) and trace their heritage to **Abraham**, sharing some common figures and stories. **Judaism** is the oldest, centered on the covenant between God and the Jewish people, with the Torah as a central text. The three are historically and theologically connected, which is why they're grouped as "Abrahamic" — though each is distinct.

## Card: The three faiths in brief
- **Judaism**: the oldest, emphasizing the covenant with God and living by its teachings; central text the Torah.
- **Christianity**: centered on the life and teachings of Jesus, whom Christians regard as the son of God; central text the Bible. It's the world's largest religion.
- **Islam**: centered on the teachings revealed to the prophet Muhammad; central text the Quran; followers are called Muslims. Each has many branches and traditions within it.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking these religions are entirely unrelated (they share roots and figures); assuming each is monolithic (all have diverse branches and interpretations); confusing the religions' core teachings with the actions of some followers; and judging them by stereotypes. A respectful, accurate basic understanding serves far better than assumptions.

## Card: Quick recap
The Abrahamic religions — Judaism, Christianity, and Islam — are monotheistic faiths sharing roots traced to Abraham. Judaism is the oldest (Torah); Christianity centers on Jesus (Bible) and is the largest religion; Islam centers on teachings revealed to Muhammad (Quran). They share heritage yet are distinct, and each contains diverse branches. A respectful overview builds cultural literacy.
