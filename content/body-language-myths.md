## Card: What it is
image: bodyLanguageMythsHero
Body language is surrounded by **myths** — oversimplified "rules" from pop psychology that don't hold up. This card set debunks the common ones, so you can use body language as the genuinely useful (but imperfect) skill it is, rather than as a mind-reading superpower it isn't.

## Card: Why it matters
Believing body-language myths leads to confident misjudgments — assuming someone's lying or disinterested based on a single gesture. That damages relationships and decisions. Knowing the myths keeps you humble and accurate, using body language as helpful insight rather than a false science that misleads you.

## Card: Key terms
- **Myth**: a popular but false belief.
- **Single-signal fallacy**: reading one gesture as definitive.
- **Lie detection myth**: the false idea that gestures reliably reveal lies.
- **Context and culture**: factors that override simple "rules."

## Card: Common myths busted
image: mythsBustedHero
- "Crossed arms always means defensive" — it might just mean cold or comfortable (the **single-signal fallacy**).
- "You can spot a liar from body language" — the **lie detection myth**; even experts can't reliably do this from cues alone.
- "Body language is X% of communication" — that famous statistic is widely misquoted and oversimplified.
- "Power poses transform you" — effects are far weaker and shakier than claimed.

## Card: Use it wisely
The truth is more modest and more useful: body language offers *clues*, read in clusters and context, never certainties. It varies by person and **culture**. Treat it as one input among many — combined with words, situation, and someone's baseline — not as a decoder ring for people's true thoughts. Humility makes you more accurate, not less.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: believing you can detect lies from body language (you mostly can't); reading single gestures as definitive; quoting the misleading "X% of communication" figure as fact; over-trusting "power poses"; and ignoring context and culture. The fix: treat body language as suggestive clues, read in context, with humility about certainty.

## Card: Quick recap
Body language myths — that crossed arms always mean defensiveness, that you can spot liars, that it's a fixed percentage of communication, that power poses transform you — are oversimplified and often false. Use body language as suggestive clues read in clusters and context, not as a mind-reading science. Humility keeps you accurate.
