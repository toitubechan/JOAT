## Card: What it is
image: financeInvestingCommonMistakesHero
New investors tend to lose money the same handful of ways — not through bad luck, but through predictable behavior traps. This subtopic gathers the most common mistakes so you can recognize and sidestep them. Avoiding errors is often more valuable than picking winners.

## Card: Why it matters
In investing, your own emotions are frequently your biggest enemy. Fear and excitement drive the very mistakes that destroy returns. Knowing these traps in advance helps you stay calm and rational when markets get dramatic — which is exactly when good behavior pays off most.

## Card: Key terms
- **Panic selling**: dumping investments in fear during a dip.
- **Timing the market**: trying to buy low and sell high precisely.
- **Hype / FOMO**: buying because everyone else is.
- **Fees**: ongoing costs that erode returns over time.

## Card: Emotional traps
image: financeInvestingCommonMistakesEmotionalTrapsHero
The costliest mistake is **panic selling** — markets dip, fear strikes, you sell low, then miss the recovery. Its twin is **FOMO** buying — piling into something only because it's soaring and everyone's talking about it, often right before it falls. Both are emotion overriding plan. The fix: decide your strategy in calm times and stick to it.

## Card: The "I can time it" trap
Many beginners try **timing the market** — selling before drops, buying before rises. Even professionals rarely do this consistently; for most people it just means buying high and selling low. The steadier approach is investing regular amounts over time, regardless of the headlines, so you're not betting on perfect guesses.

## Card: Common mistakes
Beyond emotions: ignoring **fees**, which quietly compound against you over decades; chasing past performance as if it predicts the future; and putting in money you'll soon need, forcing a sale at a bad time. Each is avoidable with a simple, boring, long-term plan — which usually beats clever, frantic activity.

## Card: Quick recap
The big investing mistakes are emotional: panic selling in dips, FOMO buying in hype, and trying to time the market. Add ignoring fees and chasing past winners. The antidote is dull but effective — a long-term plan, regular investing, low fees, and the discipline to ignore the noise.
