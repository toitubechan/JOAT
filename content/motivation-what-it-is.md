## Card: What it is
image: psychMotivationWhatItIsHero
**Motivation** is the internal drive that moves us to act — to start, continue, or pursue a goal. It's what gets you out of bed, working toward things, and overcoming inertia. Understanding what motivation really is (and isn't) is the first step to harnessing it rather than waiting around for it.

## Card: Why it matters
Motivation shapes whether we achieve our goals or abandon them. Most people misunderstand it — treating it as a magical feeling that strikes randomly. Understanding how it actually works helps you take action even when motivation is low, and build systems that don't depend on always "feeling motivated." It's central to getting anything done.

## Card: Key terms
- **Motivation**: the internal drive to act.
- **Drive**: the underlying push behind behavior.
- **Action-motivation loop**: action creating motivation, not just the reverse.
- **Inertia**: the resistance to starting.

## Card: More than a feeling
image: psychMotivationWhatItIsMoreThanAFeelingHero
We often think of motivation as a *feeling* that must arrive before we act. But that's backwards much of the time: **action often creates motivation**, not the other way around. Starting a task — even reluctantly — frequently generates the momentum and motivation to continue. Waiting to "feel motivated" before starting is one of the biggest traps; often, you must start *first*.

## Card: What drives us
Motivation springs from various sources: needs (hunger, safety), desires and goals, values, rewards, and a sense of progress or purpose. Different things drive different people and different tasks. Understanding *your* drivers — what genuinely energizes you — helps you set up goals and tasks in ways that tap into real motivation rather than relying on willpower alone.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: waiting to "feel motivated" before acting (action often comes first); treating motivation as a fixed trait you either have or don't; relying on motivation alone instead of building habits and systems; and not knowing what genuinely drives you. Motivation is something you can influence and generate, not just passively await.

## Card: Quick recap
Motivation is the internal drive to act — but it's not just a feeling that must strike before you start. Often, action *creates* motivation, so starting (even reluctantly) generates momentum. It springs from needs, goals, values, and purpose. Understanding your drivers, and not waiting to "feel motivated," lets you harness it rather than await it.
