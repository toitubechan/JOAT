## Card: What it is
image: maintenanceHero
**Home maintenance** is the ongoing care that keeps your living space working — fixing small problems before they grow, and doing simple upkeep regularly. It's the difference between a home that quietly serves you and one that surprises you with expensive emergencies. Most of it is simpler than people fear.

## Card: Why it matters
Small issues rarely stay small. A tiny leak rots wood; a loose hinge wears a door; ignored gunk clogs a drain. Catching these early costs minutes and pennies; ignoring them costs days and a fortune. Basic upkeep also keeps your home safer and more pleasant to live in.

## Card: Key terms
- **Maintenance**: routine care to prevent problems.
- **Wear and tear**: gradual damage from normal use.
- **Preventive fix**: a small repair that stops a bigger one.
- **DIY**: "do it yourself" — handling a task without a pro.

## Card: The mindset that saves money
image: preventiveFix
Treat your home like a car that needs servicing: a little regular attention prevents breakdowns. Walk through occasionally and notice small changes — a new stain, a sticking window, a drip. Acting on a **preventive fix** while it's tiny is the single most cost-effective habit a homeowner or renter can build.

## Card: Build a simple toolkit
You don't need a workshop. A basic kit handles most small jobs: a screwdriver set, a hammer, pliers, a tape measure, an adjustable wrench, and some tape and filler. Knowing you *have* the tools makes you far more likely to fix small things promptly instead of letting them slide.

## Card: Common mistakes
The biggest mistake is ignoring small problems, hoping they'll resolve themselves — they almost never do. Another is attempting a repair beyond your skill or safety (gas, major electrical, structural) instead of calling a pro. And many lack basic tools, so even a two-minute fix gets postponed indefinitely.

## Card: Quick recap
Home maintenance is regular, simple care that stops small issues becoming big bills. Notice problems early, keep a basic toolkit, and act on little fixes promptly. Know your limits, too — some jobs need a professional. A little attention now saves a lot of money and stress later.
