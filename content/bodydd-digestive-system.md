## Card: What it is
image: bodyddDigestiveSystemHero
The **digestive system** breaks down the food you eat into tiny components your body can absorb and use for energy, growth, and repair. It's essentially a long, muscular tube from mouth to exit, with helper organs along the way. Everything your body does is powered by what this system extracts from food.

## Card: Why it matters
The digestive system fuels your entire body and is closely tied to overall health, energy, and even mood (via the gut). Understanding it explains hunger, digestion, why fiber and gut health matter, and how food becomes the energy you run on. It demystifies a system working constantly inside you.

## Card: Key terms
- **Digestion**: breaking food into absorbable pieces.
- **Absorption**: taking nutrients into the bloodstream.
- **Gut microbiome**: the helpful bacteria living in your gut.
- **Enzymes**: substances that speed up the breakdown of food.

## Card: The journey of food
image: digestiveJourneyHero
**Digestion** starts in the mouth (chewing and saliva), continues in the stomach (churning with acid and **enzymes** into a soupy mush), then moves to the intestines, where most **absorption** happens — nutrients pass into the bloodstream to be delivered everywhere. Helper organs like the liver and pancreas add juices and enzymes that aid breakdown. Whatever isn't absorbed becomes waste.

## Card: The gut microbiome
Your gut hosts trillions of helpful bacteria — the **gut microbiome** — that assist digestion, help produce certain vitamins, and influence health in ways science is still uncovering (including links to immunity and even mood). **Fiber** (from plants) feeds these good bacteria and keeps the system moving smoothly. A healthy, varied diet supports a healthy microbiome, which is increasingly seen as important for overall wellbeing.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking digestion is only the stomach's job (the intestines do most absorbing, with help from the liver and pancreas); ignoring fiber's importance (it feeds gut bacteria and aids digestion); underrating the gut microbiome's role in health; and imagining food is absorbed instantly. Digestion is a coordinated, multi-organ process with surprising links to overall health.

## Card: Quick recap
The digestive system breaks food into absorbable pieces: chewing starts it, the stomach churns it with acid and enzymes, and the intestines absorb most nutrients into the blood, helped by the liver and pancreas. Trillions of gut bacteria (the microbiome), fed by fiber, aid digestion and influence health. It's a coordinated process turning food into the fuel and materials your body runs on.
