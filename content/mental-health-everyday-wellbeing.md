## Card: What it is
image: everydayWellbeingHero
Just as you can build physical fitness, you can support your **mental wellbeing** with everyday habits. These aren't cures for serious conditions, but they're protective foundations — small, regular practices that help you feel steadier, more resilient, and better able to handle life's ups and downs.

## Card: Why it matters
Mental wellbeing isn't only about coping in a crisis; it's something you can nurture proactively. Simple daily habits build resilience, so challenges hit less hard. Many of these overlap with physical health, meaning one habit can help both body and mind at once. Small, consistent actions add up.

## Card: Key terms
- **Wellbeing**: feeling and functioning well overall.
- **Resilience**: the ability to recover from difficulty.
- **Connection**: meaningful contact with other people.
- **Mindfulness**: paying gentle attention to the present moment.

## Card: The big foundations
image: wellbeingFoundationsHero
Several basics strongly support mental wellbeing: regular **sleep**, **exercise** (a powerful mood booster), and decent nutrition. The body and mind are linked, so caring for one helps the other. These aren't optional extras — they're among the most reliable, evidence-backed ways to feel mentally better day to day.

## Card: Connection and presence
Humans are social, and **connection** — real contact with friends, family, or community — is one of the strongest protectors of mental health. So is doing things that give meaning or enjoyment. **Mindfulness**, simply paying gentle attention to the present rather than spiraling in worry, can also calm a busy mind.

## Card: Common mistakes
A common mistake is neglecting these basics when stressed — exactly when they help most — because there's "no time." Another is isolating during hard periods, which usually worsens things. And many treat wellbeing as something to fix only once it breaks, rather than maintaining it like physical fitness all along.

## Card: Quick recap
Support your mental wellbeing daily: prioritize sleep, exercise, and nutrition (body and mind are linked), stay connected to people, do things that bring meaning, and practice present-moment attention. These build resilience so challenges hit less hard. Maintain wellbeing proactively, especially when life gets stressful.
