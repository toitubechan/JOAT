## Card: What it is
image: accountProtectionHero
Protecting your accounts goes beyond a single strong password. It's a set of habits — keeping software updated, watching for breaches, recognizing takeover attempts, and managing old accounts — that together keep your digital identity secure over time. Good security is ongoing, not a one-time setup.

## Card: Why it matters
Attackers have many routes in, not just guessing passwords. A layered set of habits closes those routes. Since a compromised account can lead to fraud, lost data, or impersonation, these habits protect your money, reputation, and peace of mind. Most are simple and quickly become second nature.

## Card: Key terms
- **Software update**: a fix that often patches security holes.
- **Breach alert**: a notice that your data appeared in a leak.
- **Account recovery**: the process to regain a locked account.
- **Dormant account**: an old account you no longer use.

## Card: Keep things updated and monitored
**Software updates** often fix security holes attackers exploit, so installing them promptly genuinely protects you. It also helps to monitor for **breach alerts** — services and some browsers warn you if your email appears in a known leak, signaling it's time to change that password. Staying current and informed closes many gaps.

## Card: Spot takeover attempts and tidy up
image: accountTakeoverWarning
Watch for signs of an **account takeover**: unexpected login alerts, password-reset emails you didn't request, or being logged out suddenly. Act fast if you see them. Also close **dormant accounts** you no longer use — each old account is a potential weak point, and forgotten ones often hold personal data you've lost track of.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: ignoring software updates for months; dismissing breach alerts instead of changing the affected password; leaving countless old accounts open and forgotten; and using a weak, guessable answer for **account recovery** questions (which can bypass your strong password). Security is only as strong as its weakest link.

## Card: Quick recap
Protect accounts with ongoing habits: install software updates promptly, heed breach alerts and change leaked passwords, watch for takeover signs like unexpected reset emails, close old dormant accounts, and don't undermine strong passwords with guessable recovery answers. Layered habits keep your accounts truly yours.
