## Card: What it is
image: financeCreditCardsDoneRightHero
A **credit card** lets you borrow small amounts repeatedly, up to a limit, and repay monthly. Used well, it's one of the easiest ways to build a good credit history and earn perks. Used badly, it's one of the fastest ways into expensive debt. The card is neutral — the habits around it decide everything.

## Card: Why it matters
Credit cards are most people's first and most frequent form of credit, so they shape your credit history more than anything else early on. They also carry some of the highest interest rates around. That combination — powerful for building credit, punishing if misused — makes good card habits genuinely worth learning.

## Card: Key terms
- **Credit limit**: the maximum you can borrow on the card.
- **Statement balance**: the total you owe at the end of a cycle.
- **Grace period**: time to pay in full before interest applies.
- **Minimum payment**: the smallest amount you must pay each month.

## Card: The golden rule
image: financeCreditCardsDoneRightTheGoldenRuleHero
Pay your **statement balance in full** every month, within the **grace period**. Do that, and you pay zero interest — the card is essentially a free, convenient tool that builds your credit. The moment you carry a balance, interest kicks in at a high rate, and the card flips from helper to drain.

## Card: Keep utilization low
Lenders like to see you using only a small slice of your limit — ideally under about a third. This is your **credit utilization**, and low usage signals you're not financially stretched, which lifts your credit score. So even if you can spend up to the limit, keeping balances well below it is the smarter play.

## Card: Common mistakes
The deadliest is paying only the **minimum payment**, which keeps you in debt for years as interest compounds. Another is treating the limit as a spending target rather than a ceiling. Cash advances (withdrawing cash on the card) usually carry brutal fees and instant interest — best avoided entirely.

## Card: Quick recap
A credit card builds credit and offers perks if you pay the full balance every month and keep utilization low. Never settle for the minimum payment, don't treat the limit as a goal, and skip cash advances. Handled with discipline, it's a tool; handled loosely, it's a trap.
