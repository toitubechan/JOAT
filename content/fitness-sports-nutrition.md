## Card: What it is
image: fitnessSportsNutritionHero
**Sports nutrition** is how what you eat and drink supports physical activity, performance, and recovery. Food is the body's fuel, and nutrition affects how you perform and recover. Understanding the basics helps you fuel activity sensibly — without falling for the hype around endless supplements and fads. This is general educational knowledge, not medical, dietary, or professional advice.

## Card: Why it matters
Nutrition significantly affects exercise performance, recovery, and results, yet the field is full of confusing hype, fads, and expensive supplements. Understanding the basics helps you fuel your body sensibly, support your activity and recovery, and avoid wasting money on overhyped products. It cuts through marketing to the fundamentals that actually matter for active people.

## Card: Key terms
- **Sports nutrition**: how diet supports activity, performance, and recovery.
- **Fuel**: food providing energy for activity.
- **Recovery nutrition**: eating to support repair after exercise.
- **Fundamentals over fads**: basics mattering more than supplements.

## Card: Food is fuel
image: fitnessSportsNutritionFoodIsFuelHero
The foundation of sports nutrition is that food is **fuel** — activity requires energy, which comes from what you eat. Carbohydrates are a key energy source for activity; protein supports muscle repair and growth; fats, vitamins, minerals, and especially water all play roles. Eating enough, and a balanced mix of nutrients, fuels performance and supports the body. The basics — eating adequately and reasonably balanced, and staying hydrated — matter far more for most active people than any specific trick or product.

## Card: Fundamentals over fads
A crucial insight: **fundamentals over fads**. The supplement and sports-nutrition industry is full of hype, expensive products, and fads promising dramatic results — but for most people, the *basics* matter overwhelmingly more: eating enough quality food with adequate protein and carbs, staying hydrated, and supporting **recovery nutrition** (eating to refuel and repair after exercise). Most supplements are unnecessary for the average active person. Getting the fundamentals right consistently beats chasing the latest product or fad. Don't let marketing distract from the basics.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: chasing supplements and fads while neglecting the basics (fundamentals matter far more); under-fueling activity (not eating enough to support exercise); ignoring hydration (water matters greatly); overlooking recovery nutrition; and believing marketing hype about dramatic results from products. Sports nutrition fundamentals — adequate, balanced eating, hydration, and recovery nutrition — matter most; most supplements are unnecessary.

## Card: Quick recap
Sports nutrition is how diet supports activity, performance, and recovery — food is fuel, with carbs providing energy, protein supporting muscle repair, and water and other nutrients playing key roles. The crucial insight is fundamentals over fads: eating enough quality food, staying hydrated, and supporting recovery matter far more than the hyped, expensive supplements the industry promotes. Get the basics right consistently rather than chasing fads.
