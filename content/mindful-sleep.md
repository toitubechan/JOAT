## Card: What it is
image: sleepHero
**Sleep** is far more than rest — it's an active, essential process during which the body and brain repair, consolidate memories, regulate emotions, and restore themselves. Sleep profoundly affects nearly every aspect of health and wellbeing. Understanding its importance, and basic sleep habits, helps you protect this crucial foundation. This is general wellbeing knowledge, not medical advice.

## Card: Why it matters
Sleep is one of the most important — and most neglected — pillars of health, affecting physical health, mental wellbeing, mood, focus, and more. Many people undervalue it or struggle with it. Understanding why sleep matters so much, and the basics of good sleep, helps you protect a foundation that influences nearly everything else. Good sleep is foundational to feeling and functioning well.

## Card: Key terms
- **Sleep**: an active, essential process for repair and restoration.
- **Sleep's functions**: the vital things sleep does for body and mind.
- **Sleep hygiene**: habits that support good sleep.
- **Consistency**: regular sleep timing supporting quality.

## Card: Sleep is active and essential
image: sleepRepair
A key insight: sleep isn't just "doing nothing" — it's an *active, essential process* with vital **functions**. During sleep, the body repairs and restores itself, the brain consolidates memories and learning, emotions are regulated, and much physical and mental maintenance happens. This is why too little sleep impairs mood, focus, judgment, physical health, and wellbeing across the board. Sleep is foundational: it influences nearly every other aspect of health, which is why neglecting it has such wide-ranging effects.

## Card: Supporting good sleep
**Sleep hygiene** — habits that support good sleep — can help. Key ones: **consistency** (regular sleep and wake times help your body's internal clock); a wind-down routine and limiting stimulation (like bright screens) before bed; a dark, quiet, comfortable sleep environment; limiting caffeine (especially later in the day) and other disruptors; and getting daylight and activity during the day. These habits support better sleep quality. Prioritizing sleep — treating it as essential rather than expendable — is one of the highest-impact things you can do for wellbeing.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: treating sleep as "doing nothing" or expendable (it's an active, essential process); undervaluing it and sacrificing it for other things; not realizing how widely poor sleep affects mood, focus, and health; and ignoring sleep hygiene. Sleep is a foundational pillar of health — protecting it through good habits (especially consistency) is one of the highest-impact things you can do for overall wellbeing.

## Card: Quick recap
Sleep is an active, essential process — not just rest — during which the body repairs, the brain consolidates memories, and emotions are regulated, profoundly affecting physical health, mood, focus, and wellbeing. It's a foundational pillar of health that influences nearly everything else, yet it's often neglected. Good sleep hygiene (especially consistent timing, a wind-down routine, a good environment, and limiting caffeine/screens) supports it. Prioritizing sleep is high-impact for wellbeing.
