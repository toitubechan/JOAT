## Card: What it is
image: cvMistakesHero
Many CVs get rejected for avoidable reasons that have nothing to do with the candidate's actual ability. This card set gathers the most common CV mistakes — so you can check yours and avoid being filtered out unfairly. Fixing these is often the easiest way to improve your results.

## Card: Why it matters
A great candidate can be passed over for a sloppy or poorly structured CV. Since these mistakes are easy to make and easy to fix, knowing them protects you from rejection you don't deserve. Often, simply removing these errors lifts a CV from "skip" to "shortlist."

## Card: Key terms
- **Typo**: a spelling or grammar error.
- **Generic CV**: an untailored, one-size-fits-all CV.
- **Padding**: filler that adds length but not value.
- **Proofreading**: carefully checking for errors.

## Card: Presentation errors
image: cvPresentationErrorsHero
Top of the list: **typos** and grammar mistakes, which signal carelessness and sink credibility instantly — always **proofread**, and ideally have someone else check too. Other presentation killers: cluttered or hard-to-read layouts, inconsistent formatting, unprofessional email addresses, and CVs that are far too long. First impressions are visual.

## Card: Content errors
Common content mistakes: a **generic CV** not tailored to the job; listing duties instead of achievements; vague claims with no specifics; **padding** with irrelevant filler to seem impressive; unexplained gaps handled awkwardly; and including outdated or irrelevant information. Each weakens your pitch and wastes the recruiter's limited attention.

## Card: Common mistakes
To summarize the worst offenders: typos and sloppy formatting; a generic, untailored CV; duties instead of achievements; vagueness and buzzwords without evidence; excessive length and padding; and lying or exaggerating. Almost all are easy to fix with proofreading, tailoring, and an honest, results-focused rewrite.

## Card: Quick recap
CVs often get rejected for avoidable reasons: typos and sloppy formatting, being generic instead of tailored, listing duties not achievements, vagueness, padding, and dishonesty. Proofread carefully, tailor to each job, focus on results, and keep it concise and honest. Fixing these lifts many CVs from "skip" to "shortlist."
