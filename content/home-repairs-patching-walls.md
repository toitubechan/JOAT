## Card: What it is
image: wallPatchHero
Walls pick up damage over time — nail holes from old pictures, dents, and small cracks. **Patching** means filling these so the wall looks smooth again, ready to paint. For small holes, it's one of the easiest and most satisfying home fixes, needing only basic materials and a little patience.

## Card: Why it matters
Beyond looks, filling holes and cracks keeps walls tidy and can stop small cracks from widening. It's also essential when moving out of a rental — unfilled holes often mean losing part of a deposit. Learning this simple skill saves money on both decorators and lost deposits.

## Card: Key terms
- **Filler / spackle**: a paste that fills holes and dries hard.
- **Putty knife**: a flat tool for spreading filler smoothly.
- **Sanding**: smoothing the dried filler flush with the wall.
- **Primer**: a base coat that helps paint stick evenly.

## Card: The basic method
image: patchSandPaint
For a small hole: scrape away any loose bits, then press **filler** in with a **putty knife**, slightly overfilling. Let it dry fully — rushing ruins the result. Then **sand** it smooth and flush with the wall. Finally, **prime** the patch before painting, so the repair blends in instead of showing through.

## Card: Matching the finish
The repair only disappears if the surface and paint match. Sand until you can't feel the edge of the patch with your hand. Prime so the filled spot doesn't soak up paint differently and show as a dull patch. When painting, feather the edges outward rather than dabbing just the hole.

## Card: Common mistakes
The most common mistake is not letting filler dry fully before sanding or painting, which cracks and crumbles. Another is skipping the **primer**, so the patch shows through as a different texture. And many over-sand, gouging the surrounding wall, or under-fill, leaving a dent that catches the light.

## Card: Quick recap
Patching a wall: clean the hole, fill with spackle using a putty knife, let it dry fully, sand smooth, then prime before painting. Patience while drying and a coat of primer are what make the repair vanish. It's a cheap, beginner-friendly skill that protects deposits and looks.
