## Card: What it is
image: insuranceClaimHero
An **insurance claim** is a formal request to your insurer to pay for a loss covered by your policy — after an accident, theft, damage, or other covered event. Understanding how claims work turns a stressful situation into a manageable process. (General guidance; follow your policy and insurer's instructions.)

## Card: Why it matters
The whole point of insurance is the payout when something goes wrong — but many people don't understand the claims process, so they make mistakes, get less than they should, or have claims denied. Knowing how claims work helps you get the cover you've paid for, when you need it most. It's where insurance proves its worth.

## Card: Key terms
- **Claim**: a request for the insurer to pay for a covered loss.
- **Claimant**: you, the person making the claim.
- **Assessment**: the insurer reviewing whether and how much to pay.
- **Payout**: the money the insurer pays you.

## Card: The basic claims process
image: claimProcessFlow
A claim typically follows steps: an event happens; you notify your insurer (often promptly is required); you provide details and evidence of the loss; the insurer **assesses** the claim against your policy; and if approved, they pay out — often minus your deductible/excess and up to your policy limits. Understanding this sequence helps you know what to expect and prepare what's needed at each stage.

## Card: Why claims get reduced or denied
Claims can be paid less than expected, or denied, for reasons rooted in the policy: the loss wasn't actually covered (an exclusion), you didn't meet a condition (like reporting in time), the deductible reduced the payout, or there wasn't enough evidence. This is why understanding your policy *before* a loss — and documenting and reporting properly — matters so much. Most claim problems trace to these.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: not knowing what your policy actually covers until you claim; delaying notifying the insurer (often a requirement); providing weak evidence; not understanding the deductible will reduce the payout; and assuming any loss is automatically covered. Knowing the process and your policy upfront prevents most claim disappointments.

## Card: Quick recap
An insurance claim is a request for your insurer to pay for a covered loss, following steps: notify them, provide evidence, they assess against your policy, then pay out (minus deductible, up to limits). Claims get reduced or denied mainly due to exclusions, unmet conditions, deductibles, or weak evidence. Knowing your policy and the process upfront is key.
