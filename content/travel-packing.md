## Card: What it is
image: packingSmartHero
**Packing smart** means bringing what you need without overpacking or forgetting essentials. Good packing makes travel easier and lighter, saves baggage fees, and avoids the stress of missing important items. A few simple principles transform packing from a chaotic scramble into a quick, reliable routine.

## Card: Why it matters
Overpacking is heavy, costly (baggage fees), and inconvenient; forgetting essentials causes stress and expense abroad. Smart packing makes your trip smoother and cheaper. It's a small skill with an outsized effect on travel comfort — and most people swing between bringing too much and forgetting something important.

## Card: Key terms
- **Packing list**: a checklist of what to bring.
- **Carry-on**: luggage you take into the cabin.
- **Essentials**: items you can't easily replace (documents, medication).
- **Versatile items**: clothes that mix, match, and layer.

## Card: Pack light and versatile
The golden rule: pack *less* than you think you need. Choose **versatile items** — clothes that mix and match and layer — rather than a separate outfit for every possibility. You can often re-wear and do laundry. Packing light saves baggage fees, makes you mobile, and reduces stress. Many experienced travelers manage with just a **carry-on**, even for longer trips.

## Card: Use a list and protect essentials
image: carryOnEssentials
Use a **packing list** (or a saved reusable one) so you forget nothing important — relying on memory while rushing is how essentials get left behind. Keep true **essentials** — passport, documents, medication, valuables, a change of clothes — in your **carry-on**, not checked luggage that could be lost or delayed. These are the items hardest or impossible to replace en route.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: overpacking "just in case" (heavy, costly, unnecessary); packing without a list and forgetting essentials; putting irreplaceable items (medication, documents) in checked baggage that can go missing; and not considering the destination's climate and needs. Pack light and versatile, use a list, and keep essentials in your carry-on.

## Card: Quick recap
Pack smart by bringing less than you think — versatile, mix-and-match clothes you can re-wear. Use a packing list so you forget nothing, and keep true essentials (passport, documents, medication, valuables) in your carry-on, never in checkable luggage that could be lost. Light packing saves money and stress and keeps you mobile.
