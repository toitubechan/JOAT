## Card: What it is
image: ecosystemHero
An **ecosystem** is a community of living things interacting with each other and their environment. No organism lives in isolation — all life is connected in webs of dependence, where each part affects the others. Ecosystems are how the living world organizes itself into interdependent systems.

## Card: Why it matters
Understanding ecosystems reveals how nature works as connected systems, not separate parts. It explains food chains, why biodiversity matters, and how human actions ripple through the environment. This is foundational for understanding conservation, climate, and our own place within the web of life. Everything in nature is linked.

## Card: Key terms
- **Ecosystem**: a community of organisms and their environment.
- **Food chain / web**: who eats whom, transferring energy.
- **Biodiversity**: the variety of life in an ecosystem.
- **Interdependence**: organisms relying on one another.

## Card: Everything is connected
image: foodWebConnections
Within an **ecosystem**, organisms form a **food web** — a network of who eats whom, transferring energy from plants up through animals. But connections go beyond eating: plants provide oxygen and shelter, decomposers recycle nutrients, insects pollinate. This **interdependence** means organisms rely on each other, often in ways that aren't obvious until something is disturbed.

## Card: Balance and biodiversity
Ecosystems tend toward a balance, but it's delicate. **Biodiversity** — the variety of life — makes ecosystems more resilient, like a sturdy web with many strands. Removing one species can ripple through the whole system (remove a predator, and prey may overrun the area). This is why losing biodiversity, and human disruption, can have outsized, surprising effects.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: seeing organisms as independent rather than deeply interconnected; thinking removing one "pest" species is harmless (it can disrupt the whole web); underestimating how human actions ripple through ecosystems; and viewing nature as separate from us, when we're part of these systems too. Everything in an ecosystem is linked.

## Card: Quick recap
An ecosystem is a community of living things interacting with each other and their environment, connected through food webs and many forms of interdependence. Biodiversity makes ecosystems resilient, but the balance is delicate — disturbing one part can ripple through the whole. All life, including us, is linked in these connected systems.
