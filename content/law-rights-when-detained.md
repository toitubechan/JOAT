## Card: What it is
image: lawRightsWhenDetainedHero
If you're ever stopped, questioned, or detained by authorities, you generally have certain **legal rights** designed to protect you. These vary significantly by country, but most legal systems provide some basic protections. Knowing that you *have* rights, and the general categories, is valuable — though the specifics depend heavily on where you are. This is general educational knowledge, not legal advice.

## Card: Why it matters
Being detained or questioned is stressful, and not knowing your rights can leave you vulnerable. Understanding that basic protections generally exist — and the kinds they cover — helps you stay calm and act sensibly. While the specifics vary by country and you should know your local laws, the general awareness that rights exist is genuinely empowering and protective.

## Card: Key terms
- **Rights**: legal protections you're entitled to.
- **Right to remain silent**: not being forced to incriminate yourself (where it applies).
- **Right to legal help**: access to a lawyer (in many systems).
- **Know your local laws**: specifics vary greatly by jurisdiction.

## Card: Common protections
image: lawRightsWhenDetainedCommonProtectionsHero
While they vary by country, many legal systems provide protections such as: a **right to remain silent** (you generally can't be forced to incriminate yourself — though rules differ); a **right to legal help** (access to a lawyer); being informed of why you're being detained; and protection from mistreatment. Some systems have specific procedures authorities must follow. The key general point: you typically aren't powerless, and these protections exist for good reason.

## Card: Staying calm and sensible
Beyond knowing rights exist, general sensible conduct: stay calm and polite (escalating rarely helps); don't resist physically; understand you may have the right to stay silent and to ask for legal help; and remember that the specifics depend on your location. Crucially, **know your local laws** in advance, since rights and procedures differ significantly. The combination of staying calm and knowing your basic rights is your best position.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: assuming you have no rights when detained (most systems provide some); assuming rights are identical everywhere (they vary greatly by country); panicking or escalating (staying calm and polite is wiser); and not knowing your local laws in advance. The general lesson: you typically have basic protections, but learn the specifics for your own location. For real situations, seek qualified legal help.

## Card: Quick recap
If detained or questioned, you generally have basic legal rights — often including a right to remain silent (not to incriminate yourself), access to legal help, being told why you're detained, and protection from mistreatment — though these vary significantly by country. Stay calm and polite, don't resist physically, and crucially, know your local laws in advance. The key awareness: you typically aren't powerless. For real situations, seek qualified legal help.
