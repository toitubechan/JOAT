## Card: What it is
image: financeCryptoScamsAndSafetyHero
Because crypto is fast, irreversible, and lightly regulated, it's a favorite playground for scammers. **Crypto scams** trick people into sending funds or revealing their keys, with little chance of recovery. Knowing the common tricks is your best defense — and far more useful than any "hot tip."

## Card: Why it matters
Once crypto leaves your wallet, it's almost always gone for good — there's no bank to call, no charge to reverse. That irreversibility means prevention is everything. A few minutes learning the warning signs can save you from losses that, in crypto, are usually permanent and unrecoverable.

## Card: Key terms
- **Phishing**: fake sites or messages stealing your keys or logins.
- **Rug pull**: creators hype a coin, then vanish with the money.
- **Guaranteed returns**: a classic promise that signals a scam.
- **Impersonation**: scammers posing as celebrities or support staff.

## Card: The biggest red flags
image: financeCryptoScamsAndSafetyTheBiggestRedFlagsHero
Treat these as alarm bells: promises of **guaranteed** or "risk-free" high returns (real investing has no such thing); pressure to act *now*; anyone asking for your **private key** or seed phrase; and giveaways claiming to "double your crypto" if you send some first. If it sounds too good to be true, it is.

## Card: Common scam types
**Phishing** uses fake apps, sites, or messages to steal your login or keys. **Rug pulls** hype a new coin, then the creators disappear with investors' money. **Impersonation** scammers pose as celebrities, support agents, or even friends to request funds. The theme is always urgency plus a request for money or access.

## Card: How to protect yourself
Never share your private key or seed phrase with anyone — no legitimate service asks. Double-check website addresses and only use official apps. Be deeply skeptical of unsolicited offers, DMs, and "guaranteed" returns. Verify a coin or platform independently before sending anything, and slow down — scammers rely on you rushing.

## Card: Quick recap
Crypto scams thrive on irreversibility and hype. Watch for guaranteed returns, urgency, key requests, and "send to receive more" giveaways. Guard your seed phrase, use official apps, verify before sending, and never rush. In crypto, prevention is the only real protection — once funds are gone, they rarely come back.
