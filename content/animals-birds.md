## Card: What it is
image: birdsHero
**Birds** are a group of animals defined by feathers, beaks, and laying eggs — and most (though not all) can fly. From tiny hummingbirds to giant ostriches, they're a hugely successful and diverse group. Remarkably, birds are the living descendants of dinosaurs. They're among the most visible and beloved wild animals.

## Card: Why it matters
Birds are everywhere, easy to observe, and play key ecological roles. Understanding what defines them — and surprising facts like their dinosaur ancestry and flight adaptations — enriches your appreciation of the natural world around you. Birdwatching is also one of the most accessible ways to connect with wildlife.

## Card: Key terms
- **Bird**: a feathered, beaked, egg-laying animal.
- **Feathers**: the defining feature, used for flight and warmth.
- **Adaptations for flight**: features enabling birds to fly.
- **Dinosaur descendants**: birds' evolutionary origin.

## Card: What makes a bird
The single defining feature of **birds** is **feathers** — no other living animal has them. Birds also have beaks (no teeth), lay eggs, are warm-blooded, and have lightweight skeletons. Most can fly, with bodies superbly **adapted for flight**: hollow bones, powerful chest muscles, and wings. Feathers serve double duty — for flight and for warmth. These features make birds instantly recognizable.

## Card: Dinosaurs that survived
image: birdsDinosaurLinkHero
A remarkable fact: birds are the **living descendants of dinosaurs**. When most dinosaurs went extinct, one group survived and evolved into today's birds — so dinosaurs aren't entirely gone; they're singing in your garden. Birds are also incredibly diverse: some don't fly (ostriches, penguins), some swim, and they range from tiny to huge, filling habitats worldwide. Their flight and adaptability made them enormously successful.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking all birds can fly (ostriches, penguins, and others can't); thinking feathers are just for flight (they're also for warmth, and flightless birds have them too); not knowing birds descend from dinosaurs; and assuming all birds are small. Feathers — not flight — are the true defining feature of every bird.

## Card: Quick recap
Birds are defined by feathers (unique to them), along with beaks, egg-laying, warm blood, and lightweight bodies. Most fly, with adaptations like hollow bones and wings, though some (ostriches, penguins) don't. Feathers serve flight and warmth. Astonishingly, birds are living dinosaur descendants. Hugely diverse and successful, they're among the most visible and beloved wild animals.
