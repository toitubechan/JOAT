## Card: What it is
image: colorTheoryHero
**Color theory** is the study of how colors relate, combine, and affect us. It explains which colors work well together, how to create harmony or contrast, and how color influences mood and perception. Far from arbitrary, color choices follow understandable principles. Understanding color theory helps anyone make more effective, intentional, and pleasing visual choices.

## Card: Why it matters
Color profoundly affects how designs look and feel, and how people respond emotionally — yet color choices often seem mysterious. Understanding color theory takes the guesswork out, helping you combine colors that work, set the right mood, and avoid clashing combinations. It's useful for design, art, presentations, and even choosing what to wear or decorate. Color is one of the most powerful, and learnable, visual tools.

## Card: Key terms
- **Color theory**: how colors relate and combine.
- **Color wheel**: a tool showing how colors relate.
- **Harmony**: color combinations that work well together.
- **Color psychology**: how colors affect mood and perception.

## Card: How colors relate
The **color wheel** is the basic tool of **color theory** — it arranges colors to show their relationships. From it come principles of **harmony**: certain combinations reliably look good together. For example, *complematary* colors (opposite on the wheel) create strong contrast and pop; *analogous* colors (next to each other) create a harmonious, calm feel. Understanding these relationships lets you choose color combinations intentionally — harmonious or high-contrast — rather than guessing or clashing.

## Card: Color and emotion
image: colorEmotionHero
Color also has powerful psychological effects (**color psychology**) — it influences mood and perception, often associated with feelings: warm colors (reds, oranges) can feel energetic, exciting, or warm; cool colors (blues, greens) can feel calm, trustworthy, or fresh. While associations aren't absolute (and vary by culture and context), color reliably affects how a design *feels*. Choosing colors that match the mood and message you want — not just colors you like — is a key part of using color effectively.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking color choices are arbitrary or pure taste (they follow learnable principles of harmony and contrast); combining colors randomly (causing clashes); ignoring color's emotional effects; and not considering the mood colors create. Color theory — using the color wheel, harmony principles, and color psychology — lets you choose colors intentionally for both visual appeal and the right feeling.

## Card: Quick recap
Color theory explains how colors relate, combine, and affect us. The color wheel shows relationships, guiding harmony — complementary colors (opposite) create contrast and pop, analogous colors (adjacent) feel harmonious. Color also affects emotion (warm colors feel energetic, cool colors calm), though associations vary by context. Understanding these principles lets you choose colors intentionally — for visual appeal and the right mood — rather than guessing.
