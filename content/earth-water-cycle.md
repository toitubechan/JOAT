## Card: What it is
image: waterCycleHero
The **water cycle** is the continuous movement of water around the planet — from oceans and lakes into the air, to clouds, down as rain or snow, and back again. The same water has been cycling for billions of years. It connects oceans, sky, land, and living things in one endless loop driven by the sun.

## Card: Why it matters
The water cycle provides the fresh water all life depends on, drives weather and climate, and shapes landscapes. Understanding it explains rain, rivers, droughts, and why fresh water is renewable yet limited. It's one of the most fundamental natural cycles, linking many Earth processes you experience daily.

## Card: Key terms
- **Evaporation**: water turning to vapor and rising into the air.
- **Condensation**: vapor cooling into clouds.
- **Precipitation**: water falling as rain, snow, or hail.
- **Collection**: water gathering in oceans, lakes, and rivers.

## Card: The cycle's stages
image: waterCycleStagesHero
The cycle, powered by the sun, flows through stages. **Evaporation**: the sun heats water in oceans and lakes, turning it into invisible vapor that rises. **Condensation**: as vapor rises and cools, it forms clouds. **Precipitation**: water falls back as rain, snow, or hail. **Collection**: it gathers in oceans, lakes, rivers, and underground — then evaporates again. Round and round, endlessly.

## Card: Why it matters for life and weather
The water cycle is what makes fresh water available — evaporation leaves salt behind, so rain is fresh, replenishing rivers and groundwater. It also drives much of our weather (clouds, rain, storms are all part of it). The cycle means water is *renewable* — it keeps circulating — yet the usable fresh water at any time is limited, which is why conserving it still matters.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking water is created or used up (it cycles continuously — the same water is reused endlessly); not connecting the water cycle to weather (rain and clouds are part of it); assuming fresh water is unlimited because it's renewable (usable fresh water is still limited); and not realizing evaporation is what makes rain fresh. The cycle endlessly recycles Earth's water.

## Card: Quick recap
The water cycle is water's endless journey, powered by the sun: evaporation (water rises as vapor), condensation (clouds form), precipitation (rain or snow falls), and collection (water gathers, then evaporates again). It provides fresh water (evaporation leaves salt behind), drives weather, and means water is renewable yet limited. The same water has cycled for billions of years.
