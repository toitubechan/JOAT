## Card: What it is
image: beforeSignHero
Before signing any contract, a few simple **steps** protect you: reading it fully, asking questions, negotiating if you can, and keeping a copy. These habits take little time but can save you from costly mistakes. This is about what to *do* before you commit. (General guidance, not legal advice.)

## Card: Why it matters
The moment before signing is your point of maximum power — once you sign, you're bound. A few smart steps at this stage prevent the regrets that come from rushing. Building these habits means you commit to agreements with eyes open, rather than discovering problems too late.

## Card: Key terms
- **Due diligence**: checking things carefully before committing.
- **Negotiation**: discussing terms before agreeing.
- **Cooling-off period**: a window to cancel after signing (where it exists).
- **Keeping a copy**: retaining the signed agreement.

## Card: Read, understand, and ask
image: askBeforeSign
The basics of **due diligence**: actually read the whole contract (especially the key parts and fine print), make sure you understand it, and *ask questions* about anything unclear — a fair party will explain. Don't be embarrassed to take your time or ask for clarification. If terms are confusing or significant, getting advice (or a professional's review for major contracts) is wise.

## Card: Negotiate, and keep records
Two often-missed steps. **Negotiation**: many contracts are more flexible than they appear — you can often ask to change or remove terms before signing (it costs nothing to ask). And always **keep a copy** of the signed agreement, so you have proof of what was agreed. Also check for a **cooling-off period** — some contracts allow cancellation within a short window after signing.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: signing without reading or fully understanding; being too shy to ask questions or take your time; assuming terms can't be negotiated (often they can); not keeping a copy of what you signed; and not knowing whether a cooling-off period applies. A little time and these steps before signing prevent most contract regrets.

## Card: Quick recap
Before signing: read the whole contract and understand it, ask about anything unclear (and seek advice for major ones), negotiate terms where you can, keep a copy of what you sign, and check for any cooling-off period. The moment before signing is your point of maximum power — use these simple steps to commit with eyes open.
