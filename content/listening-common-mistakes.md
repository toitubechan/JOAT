## Card: What it is
image: listeningMistakesHero
Even well-meaning people fall into listening habits that make others feel unheard. This card set gathers the common listening mistakes — so you can catch and correct them. Since being heard is something people deeply value, avoiding these habits noticeably improves your relationships.

## Card: Why it matters
These mistakes are easy to make unconsciously, and they quietly damage conversations and relationships — the other person feels dismissed even if you didn't intend it. Knowing them helps you become the kind of listener people trust and open up to. Small corrections make a big difference.

## Card: Key terms
- **Interrupting**: cutting people off before they finish.
- **One-upping**: topping their story with a bigger one of your own.
- **Unsolicited advice**: offering solutions they didn't ask for.
- **Half-listening**: attending partially while distracted.

## Card: Conversation-killers
image: conversationKillersHero
The big ones: **interrupting** (signals their words matter less than yours); **one-upping** (responding to their story with a bigger one of your own, making it about you); and giving **unsolicited advice** when they wanted to be heard, not fixed. Each, even if well-intentioned, leaves the speaker feeling dismissed rather than understood.

## Card: Subtle failures
Quieter mistakes: **half-listening** while distracted (people can tell); waiting to talk rather than actually listening; judging or dismissing what they say; and faking attention without genuine engagement. These are less obvious than interrupting but just as corrosive — the speaker senses you're not truly with them, even if you say the right words.

## Card: Common mistakes
To gather them: interrupting; one-upping; unsolicited advice; half-listening while distracted; waiting to talk instead of understanding; judging; and asking nothing, so people feel ignored. The cure is consistent: give full attention, hold your response, ask before advising, and resist making it about you. Let people feel genuinely heard.

## Card: Quick recap
Common listening mistakes — interrupting, one-upping, unsolicited advice, half-listening, and waiting to talk — make people feel unheard, even when you don't mean to. They quietly harm relationships. The fix: give full attention, hold your response, ask before giving advice, and don't make it about you. Let people feel heard.
