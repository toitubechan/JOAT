## Card: What it is
image: clearWritingHero
**Clear writing** is writing that's easy to understand — the reader gets your meaning quickly, without effort. It's not about big words or elaborate sentences; in fact, it's the opposite. Clarity is the single most important quality of good practical writing, far more than sounding clever or sophisticated.

## Card: Why it matters
Most writing exists to communicate, and unclear writing fails at its one job. Clear writing gets read, understood, and acted on; murky writing gets skimmed, misunderstood, or ignored. Whether it's emails, reports, or messages, the ability to write clearly makes you more effective and more credible at work and in life.

## Card: Key terms
- **Clarity**: being easily understood.
- **Plain language**: simple, direct words and sentences.
- **Jargon**: specialist terms that confuse outsiders.
- **Reader effort**: how hard the reader works to understand.

## Card: Clarity over cleverness
image: clarityOverClevernessHero
A common myth is that good writing sounds sophisticated — long words, complex sentences, formal tone. The truth is the reverse: the best practical writing uses **plain language** and is effortless to read. Trying to sound clever usually makes writing *harder* to understand. Your goal is to transfer meaning easily, not to impress with vocabulary.

## Card: Minimize reader effort
Think of clear writing as minimizing **reader effort**. Every confusing sentence, unnecessary word, or piece of unexplained **jargon** makes the reader work harder, and they'll give up or misread. Clear writing does the work *for* the reader: simple words, short sentences, one idea at a time. If they have to reread to understand, it's not clear enough.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: using big words and complex sentences to sound impressive; unexplained jargon that confuses; cramming multiple ideas into one tangled sentence; and writing to show off rather than to communicate. The fix is a mindset shift: aim to be understood effortlessly, not to sound clever. Simple and clear wins.

## Card: Quick recap
Clear writing is easy to understand — and that, not sophistication, is the goal of good practical writing. Clever-sounding writing usually just makes the reader work harder. Use plain language, minimize reader effort, and avoid unexplained jargon. If readers have to reread to get your meaning, it's not clear enough yet.
