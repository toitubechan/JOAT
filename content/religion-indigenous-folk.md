## Card: What it is
image: religionIndigenousFolkHero
**Indigenous and folk beliefs** are the traditional spiritual traditions of native peoples and local cultures around the world. Diverse and often ancient, they're frequently tied to nature, ancestors, community, and place. Often overlooked beside the largest world religions, they're a rich and significant part of humanity's spiritual heritage. This is a respectful, general overview.

## Card: Why it matters
Focusing only on the largest religions misses the world's full spiritual diversity. Indigenous and folk traditions have shaped countless cultures and carry deep wisdom, especially about living in relationship with nature and community. Understanding that they exist and matter fosters respect for cultural diversity and a fuller picture of how humans have approached the sacred.

## Card: Key terms
- **Indigenous beliefs**: the spiritual traditions of native peoples.
- **Folk beliefs**: traditional local spiritual practices.
- **Animism**: the view that nature and objects have spirit.
- **Oral tradition**: passing beliefs down by spoken word.

## Card: Connected to nature and community
image: religionIndigenousFolkConnectedToNatureAndCommunityHero
Many indigenous and folk traditions share certain themes (though they're hugely varied). A common one is a deep connection to **nature** — viewing the land, animals, and natural world as sacred or alive (a view sometimes called **animism**). Ancestors, community, and place often hold central spiritual importance. Many are passed down through **oral tradition** — stories, rituals, and teachings shared by spoken word rather than written scripture — and woven into daily life and culture.

## Card: Diverse and significant
These traditions are enormously diverse — there are countless distinct indigenous and folk belief systems across the world, each unique to its people and place. Historically, many were marginalized or suppressed, yet many continue and are being revitalized. They carry valuable perspectives, especially on living sustainably and in harmony with nature. Recognizing them gives a fuller, more respectful picture of the world's spiritual life beyond the largest organized religions.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: ignoring indigenous and folk traditions entirely (focusing only on big religions); lumping them all together (they're hugely diverse and distinct); dismissing them as "primitive" (they're rich, meaningful traditions with deep wisdom); and not realizing many continue today. Indigenous and folk beliefs are a diverse, significant, living part of humanity's spiritual heritage, deserving respect.

## Card: Quick recap
Indigenous and folk beliefs are the traditional spiritual traditions of native peoples and local cultures worldwide — diverse, often ancient, and frequently tied to nature (sometimes animism), ancestors, community, and place, passed down through oral tradition. Often overlooked beside major religions and historically marginalized, many continue today. They carry deep wisdom and are a significant, living part of humanity's spiritual heritage, deserving respect.
