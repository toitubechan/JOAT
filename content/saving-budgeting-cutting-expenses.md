## Card: What it is
image: financeSavingCuttingExpensesHero
**Cutting expenses** means reducing what you spend so more stays with you — but done smartly, not painfully. The goal isn't to strip away every joy; it's to find spending that gives you little real value and redirect it toward things you actually care about. Spend less where it doesn't matter, so you can spend on what does.

## Card: Why it matters
Every euro you stop wasting is a euro you can save or use on something meaningful — and unlike earning more, cutting waste is fully in your control today. The trick is doing it sustainably: harsh, joyless cuts tend to snap back into binge-spending. Smart trimming sticks because it barely hurts.

## Card: Key terms
- **Recurring cost**: a repeated charge, like a subscription.
- **Value spending**: money on things that genuinely matter to you.
- **Subscription creep**: slowly accumulating unused subscriptions.
- **Big wins**: large, infrequent costs worth optimizing.

## Card: Target the big wins first
image: financeSavingCuttingExpensesTargetTheBigWinsFirstHero
People obsess over small treats while ignoring the **big wins** — rent, insurance, loan rates, phone and energy plans. Negotiating or switching one of these can save more in a day than cutting coffee for a year. Review your largest recurring bills first; that's where the real, painless savings hide.

## Card: Kill subscription creep
**Subscription creep** is the slow pile-up of small monthly charges you forgot about — streaming, apps, memberships you don't use. List every recurring charge on your statement and cancel what you don't truly value. This is some of the easiest money you'll ever recover, because you weren't enjoying it anyway.

## Card: Common mistakes
The first mistake is cutting only tiny pleasures (the daily coffee) while leaving huge bills untouched — big effort, small reward. The second is slashing so deeply that life feels grim, guaranteeing a rebound. The aim is **value spending**: cut ruthlessly where you get little joy, and protect the few things you love.

## Card: Quick recap
Cut expenses smartly: attack the big recurring bills first (rent, insurance, plans), kill forgotten subscriptions, and protect the spending that genuinely brings you joy. Painless, value-focused trimming sticks; harsh deprivation rebounds. Spend less where it doesn't matter so you can afford what does.
