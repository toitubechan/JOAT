## Card: What it is
image: cavesHero
**Caves** are natural underground hollows, often carved slowly by water dissolving rock over thousands or millions of years. Inside, mineral-rich water creates stunning **formations** like stalactites and stalagmites. Caves are hidden landscapes shaped by patient natural processes, revealing the slow power of water and time.

## Card: Why it matters
Caves are among nature's most fascinating hidden environments, and understanding how they and their formations arise reveals the slow, persistent power of water and chemistry. They also preserve climate records and unique ecosystems. Beyond science, caves capture the imagination — and knowing how they form deepens the wonder.

## Card: Key terms
- **Cave**: a natural underground hollow.
- **Stalactite**: a formation hanging from a cave ceiling.
- **Stalagmite**: a formation rising from a cave floor.
- **Dissolution**: water slowly dissolving rock.

## Card: How caves form
Most large caves form through **dissolution**: slightly acidic water (rainwater absorbs gases that make it mildly acidic) seeps into certain rocks like limestone and slowly dissolves them. Over enormous spans of time, this carves out passages and chambers. Some caves form other ways (by lava, waves, or ice), but water dissolving rock is the classic, slow process behind most spectacular cave systems.

## Card: Formations that grow
image: caveFormationsHero
Inside caves, dripping mineral-rich water creates **formations** over ages. As water drips from the ceiling, it leaves tiny mineral deposits that slowly build downward into icicle-like **stalactites**. Water dripping onto the floor builds deposits upward into **stalagmites**. A simple memory trick: stalac**t**ites hold **t**ight to the ceiling; stalag**m**ites grow from the **g**round. These grow incredibly slowly, drop by drop.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking caves are carved quickly (they form over thousands to millions of years); mixing up stalactites and stalagmites; imagining all caves form the same way (water dissolution is common, but lava and other processes also make caves); and not realizing formations are still slowly growing. Caves showcase the patient, drop-by-drop power of water and time.

## Card: Quick recap
Caves are natural underground hollows, most formed by slightly acidic water slowly dissolving rock like limestone over vast time. Inside, dripping mineral water builds formations: stalactites hang from the ceiling (holding "tight"), stalagmites rise from the ground. These grow drop by drop over ages. Caves reveal the slow, patient power of water, chemistry, and time.
