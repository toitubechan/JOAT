## Card: What it is
image: financeCryptoRisksAndVolatilityHero
**Volatility** means how much a price swings up and down, and crypto is famous for extreme swings — a coin can rise or fall by large percentages in a single day. Combined with light regulation and no safety net, this makes crypto one of the riskier places to put money. Understanding why protects you from painful surprises.

## Card: Why it matters
The same volatility that fuels stories of huge gains also causes huge losses, and most people hear only the success stories. Knowing the real risks lets you make calm decisions rather than emotional ones. This is general knowledge, not advice — but the core caution is universal: never put in money you can't afford to lose.

## Card: Key terms
- **Volatility**: how sharply a price rises and falls.
- **Speculation**: buying mainly hoping the price goes up.
- **Unregulated**: lacking the consumer protections of banks.
- **Liquidity**: how easily you can buy or sell without moving the price.

## Card: Why prices swing so hard
image: financeCryptoRisksAndVolatilityWhyPricesSwingSoHardHero
Crypto prices are driven heavily by **speculation** — buying on hope rather than steady underlying value — plus news, hype, and online sentiment. With no central bank to stabilize it and sometimes thin **liquidity**, prices can lurch fast. A single tweet or rumor can move markets, which is rare for established assets.

## Card: The protection gap
Banks come with deposit insurance and fraud reversal. Crypto is largely **unregulated**: if an exchange collapses, you're hacked, or you send funds to the wrong address, there's often no one to refund you and no transaction to reverse. This lack of a safety net is a defining risk — gains and losses are yours alone.

## Card: Common mistakes
The classic mistake is investing money you need, then being forced to sell in a crash. Another is FOMO — buying because a coin is soaring and everyone's hyping it, often right before it drops. And many bet on tiny, obscure coins promising huge returns, which are especially prone to collapse and manipulation.

## Card: Quick recap
Crypto is highly volatile, driven by speculation and sentiment, with no central stabilizer and few protections. There's usually no refund for hacks, errors, or collapses. Only ever risk money you can afford to lose, ignore hype, and treat obscure "moonshot" coins with deep suspicion. Risk here is real and personal.
