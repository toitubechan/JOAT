## Card: What it is
image: mathGeometryHero
**Geometry** is the branch of math dealing with shapes, sizes, space, and the relationships between them — points, lines, angles, areas, and forms. It's one of the oldest and most visual areas of math, and it's all around us in design, building, art, and nature. Geometry makes the abstract concrete because you can often *see* it.

## Card: Why it matters
Geometry underlies architecture, engineering, art, design, navigation, and computer graphics — and it's deeply practical (calculating areas, understanding shapes and space). Being visual, it's often more intuitive than other math. Understanding the basics helps with everyday tasks and reveals the mathematical structure in the world around you.

## Card: Key terms
- **Geometry**: the math of shapes, sizes, and space.
- **Point, line, angle**: the basic building blocks.
- **Area and perimeter**: space inside a shape, and its boundary length.
- **Dimensions**: 2D (flat) versus 3D (solid).

## Card: The building blocks
image: mathGeometryTheBuildingBlocksHero
Geometry builds from simple elements: a **point** (a position), a **line** (connecting points), and an **angle** (where lines meet, measuring turn). From these come shapes — triangles, squares, circles in 2D, and cubes, spheres, and more in 3D. Understanding these basics lets you describe and measure almost any shape or space. Geometry is essentially the language for talking precisely about shapes and how they relate.

## Card: Measuring shapes and space
Two everyday geometric measures: **perimeter** (the distance around a shape's edge) and **area** (the amount of space inside it). For 3D objects, there's also volume (space inside a solid). These are genuinely practical — calculating how much paint for a wall (area), fencing for a garden (perimeter), or water in a tank (volume). Geometry also covers **dimensions**: flat 2D shapes versus solid 3D objects, each with its own measures.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking geometry is just abstract school exercises (it's deeply practical and visual); confusing area (space inside) with perimeter (distance around); mixing up 2D and 3D measures; and not seeing geometry in the everyday world. Geometry is the practical, visual math of shapes and space, useful for countless real tasks.

## Card: Quick recap
Geometry is the math of shapes, sizes, and space, built from points, lines, and angles into 2D shapes and 3D objects. Practical measures include perimeter (distance around), area (space inside), and volume (space within a solid) — used for everything from painting walls to building. Visual and intuitive, geometry reveals the mathematical structure in the world all around us.
