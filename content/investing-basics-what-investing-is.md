## Card: What it is
image: investingHero
**Investing** means using money to buy something you expect to grow in value or pay you income over time — like a slice of a company or a loan to a government. Unlike saving, where money sits safely, investing puts it to work, accepting some risk in exchange for the chance of greater growth.

## Card: Why it matters
Over long periods, money left only in cash tends to lose buying power to **inflation** (rising prices). Investing is how people aim to grow wealth faster than prices rise, funding long-term goals like retirement. It's general knowledge worth understanding — though specific choices are best discussed with a qualified professional.

## Card: Key terms
- **Investing**: putting money into assets expecting growth or income.
- **Asset**: something of value you can own, like shares or bonds.
- **Return**: the gain (or loss) on an investment.
- **Inflation**: the gradual rise in prices over time.

## Card: Saving vs. investing
**Saving** keeps money safe and accessible, with little growth — ideal for short-term needs and emergencies. **Investing** trades some safety and access for the potential of higher long-term **returns**. The rough rule of thumb: save for money you'll need soon, consider investing for money you can leave untouched for years.

## Card: Why time is the key ingredient
image: timeGrowth
Investing rewards patience. Markets rise and fall in the short term, which feels scary, but historically they've trended upward over long stretches. The longer your money stays invested, the more those bumps tend to smooth out — and the more your returns can build on themselves. Time, not timing, does the heavy lifting.

## Card: Common mistakes
A frequent mistake is investing money you'll need next month, then being forced to sell during a dip. Another is expecting quick riches; investing is a slow, long-game tool, not a lottery ticket. And many never start, fearing complexity — yet broad, simple approaches exist precisely for beginners.

## Card: Quick recap
Investing puts money to work in assets expecting long-term growth, accepting risk that saving avoids. It aims to outpace inflation over years, not get rich overnight. Save for the short term, invest for the long term, and let time do the work. For specific decisions, seek qualified advice.
