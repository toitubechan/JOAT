## Card: What it is
image: histReligionsEasternHero
The major **Eastern religions** — including **Hinduism** and **Buddhism** — originated in Asia and have hundreds of millions of followers. They often differ from Abrahamic faiths in important ways, such as their views on the divine and the goal of spiritual life. This is a respectful, basic overview.

## Card: Why it matters
These traditions have shaped the cultures, philosophies, and daily lives of much of the world, especially Asia. Their ideas (like karma, meditation, and mindfulness) have also spread globally. Understanding their basics builds cultural literacy and broadens your sense of the diverse ways humans approach meaning and the sacred.

## Card: Key terms
- **Hinduism**: one of the world's oldest religions, originating in India.
- **Buddhism**: a tradition based on the teachings of the Buddha.
- **Karma**: the idea that actions have consequences.
- **Reincarnation**: the belief in rebirth into new lives.

## Card: Hinduism in brief
image: histReligionsEasternHinduismInBriefHero
**Hinduism**, one of the world's oldest religions, originated in the Indian subcontinent. It's highly diverse — not a single founder or creed, but a rich family of traditions, texts, and practices. Many Hindus believe in concepts like **karma** (actions have consequences) and **reincarnation** (rebirth across lifetimes), and a single divine reality that may be worshipped in many forms. Its diversity is part of its character.

## Card: Buddhism in brief
**Buddhism** is based on the teachings of the Buddha ("the awakened one"), who taught a path to end suffering through wisdom, ethical living, and mental training like **meditation**. It also features ideas of **karma** and **reincarnation**, but notably is often less focused on a creator god and more on personal spiritual awakening. Its practices, especially meditation and mindfulness, have spread worldwide, including in secular forms.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: treating Hinduism as a single uniform creed (it's a diverse family of traditions); assuming all Eastern religions are the same; thinking they all center on a creator god like Abrahamic faiths (many focus differently); and confusing borrowed practices (like secular meditation) with the full religious traditions. They're rich and varied, deserving accurate understanding.

## Card: Quick recap
Eastern religions like Hinduism (one of the oldest, a diverse family of Indian traditions) and Buddhism (based on the Buddha's path to end suffering through wisdom and meditation) often differ from Abrahamic faiths — featuring ideas like karma and reincarnation, and less focus on a single creator god. Their practices have spread globally. A respectful overview builds cultural literacy.
