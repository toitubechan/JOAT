## Card: What it is
image: psychRelationshipsHandlingConflictHero
**Conflict** is inevitable in any close relationship — and handling it well, rather than avoiding or mishandling it, is what keeps relationships healthy. The goal isn't to never disagree, but to disagree in ways that resolve issues and even strengthen the bond, rather than damaging it.

## Card: Why it matters
How couples, friends, and families handle conflict predicts whether relationships last and thrive. Poorly handled conflict erodes connection; well-handled conflict builds understanding and trust. Since conflict can't be avoided, learning to navigate it constructively is one of the most valuable relationship skills there is.

## Card: Key terms
- **Healthy conflict**: disagreement handled constructively.
- **Repair**: reconnecting after a conflict.
- **Contempt**: disrespect that badly damages relationships.
- **Problem vs. person**: tackling the issue, not attacking each other.

## Card: Conflict isn't the enemy
image: psychRelationshipsHandlingConflictConflictIsnTTheEnemyHero
A reframe: conflict itself doesn't harm relationships — *how it's handled* does. **Healthy conflict** can clear the air, surface needs, and deepen understanding. Relationships with no conflict often have avoidance or suppressed resentment, not perfect harmony. The aim is to address disagreements directly but respectfully, treating it as you both versus the **problem**, not against each other.

## Card: What helps and what harms
What helps: staying calm, listening to understand, using "I" statements, focusing on the issue not attacking the person, and **repair** afterward (reconnecting, apologizing where needed). What seriously *harms*: **contempt** (mockery, disrespect, eye-rolling), stonewalling (shutting down), constant criticism of the person, and bringing up old grievances. Avoiding the harmful patterns matters as much as doing the helpful things.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: avoiding conflict entirely (resentment festers); attacking the person rather than the problem; using contempt or shutting down; trying to "win" instead of resolve; and skipping repair afterward. Healthy conflict means addressing issues calmly and respectfully, tackling the problem together, and reconnecting after — not avoiding disagreement or fighting to win.

## Card: Quick recap
Conflict is inevitable; handling it well keeps relationships healthy. Healthy conflict (calm, listening, "I" statements, problem-not-person, and repair afterward) can deepen bonds. Contempt, stonewalling, personal attacks, and trying to "win" badly damage them. The goal isn't to avoid disagreement but to disagree constructively, as a team against the problem.
