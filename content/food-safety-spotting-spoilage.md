## Card: What it is
image: spoilageHero
Knowing when food has **spoiled** — and when it's still fine — saves you from both getting sick and throwing away good food. It combines using your senses with understanding their limits, since some dangerous bacteria leave no visible or smelly trace. A balanced judgment keeps you safe without wasting food.

## Card: Why it matters
Two opposite errors cost you: eating genuinely spoiled food and getting ill, or binning perfectly good food out of over-caution. Learning the real signs of spoilage, and where your senses can't be trusted, helps you cut waste while staying safe. It's about informed judgment, not guessing.

## Card: Key terms
- **Spoilage**: food deteriorating to where it shouldn't be eaten.
- **Off signs**: clues like smell, color, and texture changes.
- **Mold**: fungal growth, often fuzzy, on spoiling food.
- **When in doubt, throw it out**: the safety-first rule for uncertainty.

## Card: Trusting your senses (mostly)
Your senses catch a lot of **spoilage**: a sour or "off" smell, slimy texture, unusual color, or visible **mold** all signal food has gone bad. For many foods, these obvious **off signs** are reliable warnings. If something clearly smells, looks, or feels wrong, don't eat it — your senses are doing their job.

## Card: Where senses fail
image: sensesAndDates
The catch: some harmful bacteria (the food-poisoning kind) leave *no* smell, taste, or visible sign. This is why a use-by date matters more than the sniff test for risky foods like meat and dairy. So combine senses *and* dates: obvious spoilage means bin it, but "looks fine" doesn't override a passed use-by date on risky food.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: relying only on the sniff test for risky foods that can be dangerous without smelling off; eating food past its use-by date because it "seems fine"; scraping mold off and eating the rest (mold can run deeper than it looks, especially in soft foods); and, conversely, wasting good food at the first tiny doubt without checking signs.

## Card: Quick recap
Spot spoilage with your senses — off smell, sliminess, odd color, mold all mean bin it. But remember some dangerous bacteria leave no trace, so respect use-by dates on risky foods even if they look fine. Combine senses and dates, don't just scrape off mold, and when genuinely in doubt, throw it out.
