## Card: What it is
image: soilErosionHero
**Soil** is the thin layer of loose material covering much of the land, made of broken-down rock, water, air, and organic matter. **Erosion** is the wearing away and movement of soil and rock by wind, water, and ice. Soil feeds nearly all land life, and erosion both shapes landscapes and threatens this precious resource.

## Card: Why it matters
Soil grows the food the world depends on — yet it's a thin, slowly-formed, easily-lost resource. Erosion can strip it away faster than it forms, threatening agriculture and ecosystems. Understanding soil and erosion reveals why this overlooked layer is so vital, and why protecting it matters for food and the environment.

## Card: Key terms
- **Soil**: the layer of loose material that supports plant life.
- **Erosion**: the wearing away and moving of soil and rock.
- **Organic matter**: decomposed plant and animal material in soil.
- **Topsoil**: the fertile upper layer of soil.

## Card: What soil is and does
**Soil** forms slowly over long periods as rock breaks down and mixes with **organic matter** (decomposed plants and animals), water, and air. The fertile upper layer, **topsoil**, is where most plant growth happens — it holds nutrients and water for roots. Soil isn't just "dirt": it's a living, complex system teeming with organisms, and it underpins nearly all the food we grow.

## Card: Erosion's threat
image: erosionThreatHero
**Erosion** — wind, water, and ice wearing away and carrying off soil — is natural and shapes landscapes over time. But human activity (clearing land, poor farming) can speed it up dramatically, stripping fertile topsoil faster than it can reform. Since soil forms so slowly, losing it is a serious, long-term problem for food production. Protecting soil (with plant cover, careful farming) is key to sustaining agriculture.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: dismissing soil as just "dirt" (it's a living, vital, slowly-formed resource); thinking soil is endlessly renewable (it forms very slowly and can be lost fast); not realizing how human activity accelerates erosion; and underrating how much our food depends on healthy topsoil. Soil is a precious, easily-lost foundation of life.

## Card: Quick recap
Soil is the thin, living layer of broken-down rock plus organic matter, water, and air that grows nearly all our food — especially the fertile topsoil. Erosion (wind, water, ice) wears it away; human activity can accelerate this, stripping soil faster than it slowly forms. Protecting soil is vital for food and ecosystems. It's far more than just "dirt."
