## Card: What it is
image: endangeredFoodWebHero
A **food web** is the network of who-eats-whom in an ecosystem, connecting all its species. **Endangered species** are animals at risk of extinction. The two are linked: because species are connected through the food web, losing one can ripple through the whole system. Understanding this reveals why conservation matters beyond individual animals.

## Card: Why it matters
Biodiversity is declining, and many species are endangered. Understanding the food web shows why this matters: ecosystems are interconnected, so losing species can have cascading effects on many others — including, ultimately, us. It reframes conservation from saving cute animals to protecting the connected systems that sustain life.

## Card: Key terms
- **Food web**: the network of feeding relationships in an ecosystem.
- **Endangered species**: an animal at risk of extinction.
- **Biodiversity**: the variety of life in an ecosystem.
- **Keystone species**: a species many others depend on.

## Card: Everything is connected
image: connectedFoodWebHero
A **food web** links the species in an ecosystem: plants feed plant-eaters, which feed predators, while decomposers recycle the dead — all interconnected. Because of this, species depend on each other in ways that aren't always obvious. Some are **keystone species** — so central that many others depend on them, so their loss has outsized effects. Remove or add a species, and effects can ripple unexpectedly through the whole web.

## Card: Why endangered species matter
When a species becomes **endangered** and is lost, it's not just one animal gone — the food web is disrupted, potentially harming many connected species (predators lose prey, prey lose control, plants lose pollinators, and so on). Declining **biodiversity** also makes ecosystems less resilient, like a web losing strands. This is why conservation aims to protect whole ecosystems and biodiversity, not just individual species in isolation.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking losing one species doesn't matter much (the food web means losses can cascade widely); seeing conservation as just about saving individual cute animals (it's about protecting connected ecosystems); not knowing about keystone species; and assuming ecosystems are robust to any loss (declining biodiversity reduces resilience). Everything in an ecosystem is connected.

## Card: Quick recap
A food web connects all of an ecosystem's species through who-eats-whom, so losing one species can ripple through many — especially a keystone species many others depend on. Endangered species being lost disrupts these webs and reduces biodiversity, making ecosystems less resilient. This is why conservation protects whole connected systems, not just individual animals. Everything in nature is linked.
