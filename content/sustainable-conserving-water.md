## Card: What it is
image: sustainConservingWaterHero
**Conserving water** means using less of it in daily life — through small habit changes and fixing waste. Clean water is a precious, limited resource (and energy is used to treat and deliver it), so using it wisely matters environmentally and often saves money on bills too.

## Card: Why it matters
Fresh water is finite and, in many places, increasingly scarce. Treating and pumping water also uses energy, so saving water saves energy too. For households on metered water, it cuts bills directly. Conserving water is a simple, low-effort sustainability habit with both environmental and financial benefits.

## Card: Key terms
- **Water footprint**: the total water your lifestyle uses.
- **Leak**: water wasted through drips and faults.
- **Low-flow**: fixtures designed to use less water.
- **Hidden water**: water used to produce goods and food.

## Card: Easy household savings
image: sustainConservingWaterEasyHouseholdSavingsHero
The biggest everyday water uses are showers, toilets, taps, and laundry. Easy savings: shorter showers, fixing dripping **leaks** (a small drip wastes a surprising amount), turning off the tap while brushing or scrubbing, running full loads, and installing **low-flow** fixtures. These small habits, repeated daily, conserve a meaningful amount over time without real sacrifice.

## Card: The hidden water footprint
Beyond the tap, much of your **water footprint** is **hidden water** — the water used to *produce* what you consume, especially food (it takes a lot of water to produce meat, for example) and goods. So eating and shopping choices affect water use as much as your taps do. Reducing food waste, in particular, saves the hidden water that went into producing that food.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: ignoring leaks (a steady drip wastes a lot); long showers and running taps; only thinking about direct tap use while ignoring the larger hidden water in food and goods; and assuming individual saving is pointless. Fix leaks, build simple habits, and remember reducing food waste saves hidden water too.

## Card: Quick recap
Conserving water uses this finite resource wisely, saving energy and often money. Cut direct use with shorter showers, fixing leaks, mindful taps, and low-flow fixtures. But remember the hidden water footprint — much water is used producing food and goods, so reducing food waste and mindful consumption matter too. Small habits add up.
