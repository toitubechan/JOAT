## Card: What it is
image: growthNotesOrganizingAndReviewingHero
Taking notes is only half the job — **organizing** and **reviewing** them is what makes them useful. Notes you can't find, or never look at again, are wasted effort. A little structure and the habit of revisiting turn a pile of notes into a genuinely useful resource.

## Card: Why it matters
Most people's notes go into a black hole — written once, never seen again. Organizing them so you can find what you need, and reviewing them so the information sticks, is what unlocks their value. Otherwise note-taking is just a feel-good ritual that produces nothing lasting.

## Card: Key terms
- **Organization**: structuring notes so they're findable.
- **Review**: revisiting notes to reinforce and use them.
- **Tags / folders**: ways to categorize digital notes.
- **Capture vs. organize**: separating quick capture from later tidying.

## Card: Keep notes findable
image: growthNotesOrganizingAndReviewingKeepNotesFindableHero
The point of **organization** is retrieval — being able to find a note when you need it. Use a simple, consistent system: folders or **tags** by topic, clear titles, and dates. Don't over-engineer it; a system so complex you won't maintain it is worse than a simple one. The test is whether future-you can find things quickly.

## Card: Capture first, organize later
A useful principle: separate **capture from organizing**. In the moment, just capture the information quickly without worrying about where it goes — speed matters when ideas are fresh. Then, periodically, tidy and file your notes properly. Trying to perfectly organize while capturing slows you down and you miss things; do them as two separate steps.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: never organizing, so notes become an unfindable pile; over-complicating the system until you abandon it; writing notes then never reviewing them (so nothing sticks); and trying to organize perfectly *while* capturing, which slows you down. Simple organization plus regular review is what makes notes pay off.

## Card: Quick recap
Notes only deliver value if you can find them and revisit them. Use a simple, consistent organization system (folders or tags, clear titles) focused on findability, not complexity. Separate quick capture from later organizing. And actually review your notes, so the information sticks. Otherwise note-taking is wasted effort.
