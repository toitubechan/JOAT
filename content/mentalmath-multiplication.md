## Card: What it is
image: dataMentalmathMultiplicationHero
Multiplying in your head seems hard, but a few **shortcuts** make many multiplications surprisingly easy. By breaking numbers apart and using simple patterns (like multiplying by 10 or doubling), you can handle multiplications that look intimidating. These tricks turn mental multiplication from scary to doable.

## Card: Why it matters
Multiplication comes up often — working out quantities, prices, areas, or scaling things up. Quick mental shortcuts save time and help you estimate and check figures. While you won't multiply huge numbers in your head, these tricks cover the everyday multiplications that actually come up, building real number confidence.

## Card: Key terms
- **Multiply by 10**: just add a zero (or shift the decimal).
- **Doubling and halving**: simple operations to build on.
- **Breaking apart**: splitting a number to multiply in pieces.
- **Multiply by 5**: multiply by 10, then halve.

## Card: Easy building blocks
image: dataMentalmathMultiplicationEasyBuildingBlocksHero
A few moves unlock lots of multiplication. **×10** is trivial: add a zero (7 × 10 = 70). **×5** is ×10 then halve (7 × 5 = 70 ÷ 2 = 35). **×4** is double, then double again. **×9** is ×10 minus the number (7 × 9 = 70 − 7 = 63). Knowing these patterns turns awkward multiplications into a couple of easy steps.

## Card: Breaking numbers apart
For bigger ones, **break apart**. To do 6 × 23, split 23 into 20 + 3: (6 × 20) + (6 × 3) = 120 + 18 = 138. To do 12 × 15, use 12 × 15 = 12 × 10 + 12 × 5 = 120 + 60 = 180. **Doubling and halving** also helps: 14 × 5 = 7 × 10 = 70. Breaking numbers into friendly pieces is the master trick behind most mental multiplication.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: trying to multiply big numbers whole in your head (break them apart instead); not using easy patterns (×10, ×5, doubling); thinking you need to memorize huge tables; and giving up because it "looks hard." The key is decomposition — split numbers into easy pieces — and a few handy patterns. Most everyday multiplication then becomes manageable.

## Card: Quick recap
Mental multiplication is easier with shortcuts: ×10 (add a zero), ×5 (×10 then halve), ×9 (×10 minus the number), and doubling. For bigger numbers, break them apart — 6 × 23 = (6 × 20) + (6 × 3) = 138. Decomposing numbers into friendly pieces, plus a few patterns, turns intimidating multiplications into a couple of easy steps.
