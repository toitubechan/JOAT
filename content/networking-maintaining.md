## Card: What it is
image: maintainingNetworkHero
**Maintaining your network** means staying in touch with your connections over time, not just making them once and forgetting. A network is like a garden — it needs occasional tending to stay alive and useful. The good news: maintenance is low-effort once you build it into a simple habit.

## Card: Why it matters
A network you only contact when you need something quickly becomes useless — and reaching out cold after years of silence feels awkward and self-serving. Light, ongoing contact keeps relationships warm, so support is there when you need it. The time to nurture your network is *before* you need it, not during a crisis.

## Card: Key terms
- **Staying in touch**: light, periodic contact with connections.
- **Warm relationship**: a connection kept active and friendly.
- **Reaching out cold**: contacting someone after long silence.
- **Reciprocity**: helping others, who then help you.

## Card: Keep relationships warm
image: warmRelationshipsHero
The goal is **warm relationships**, not dormant ones. **Staying in touch** doesn't require much: an occasional message, congratulating someone on news, sharing something they'd find useful, or a quick catch-up. These small touches keep the relationship alive, so when you do need advice or a connection, it doesn't feel like reaching out cold.

## Card: Give and reciprocate
Maintenance is largely about **reciprocity** — continuing to give, not just take. Help your connections when you can, make introductions, offer support. People naturally help those who've helped them. A network is a two-way thing kept healthy by mutual generosity over time, not a resource you simply draw down when needed.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: only contacting people when you need something; letting connections go completely silent for years, then reaching out cold and awkwardly; never giving back, only taking; and treating maintenance as a huge effort rather than small, occasional touches. A little regular tending keeps a network alive.

## Card: Quick recap
Maintain your network with light, ongoing contact — occasional messages, congratulations, useful shares — to keep relationships warm rather than dormant. Practice reciprocity by giving, not just taking. Nurture connections *before* you need them, so support is there when you do. Small, regular touches keep a network alive and useful.
