## Card: What it is
image: safeMedicineHero
Taking medicine **safely** is about a few simple habits: the right dose at the right times, in the right way, and avoiding dangerous combinations. Medicines help when used correctly and can harm when not. These are general safety principles — your doctor and pharmacist give advice specific to you.

## Card: Why it matters
Medicine errors are surprisingly common and can be serious — too much, too little, or the wrong mix. Good habits make medicines work as intended and prevent avoidable harm. Since many of us take something at least occasionally, these basics are genuinely useful for everyone.

## Card: Key terms
- **Dose**: the correct amount to take.
- **Interaction**: when medicines (or food/alcohol) affect each other.
- **Course**: the full set of doses you're meant to complete.
- **Expiry date**: when a medicine should no longer be used.

## Card: Right dose, right timing
image: doseTimingHero
Stick to the stated **dose** and timing — more isn't better and can be dangerous, while too little may not work. Some medicines need taking with food, some on an empty stomach, some at set intervals. For a prescribed **course**, finish it as directed even if you feel better, unless your doctor says otherwise.

## Card: Avoid dangerous combinations
**Interactions** can be serious: some medicines clash with each other, with alcohol, or even with certain foods, reducing effectiveness or causing harm. Always tell your doctor or pharmacist what else you take, including supplements. And check the label for warnings about alcohol or other medicines before combining anything.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common errors: taking more than the dose hoping for a faster effect; mixing medicines or alcohol without checking; stopping a prescribed course early; using expired medicines (past the **expiry date** they may be weaker or unsafe); and storing medicines badly (heat and damp degrade them; keep them away from children).

## Card: Quick recap
Take medicines safely: follow the exact dose and timing, take them the right way (with or without food as directed), finish prescribed courses, and check for interactions with other medicines, alcohol, and food. Don't use expired medicines, store them properly and safely, and ask a pharmacist when unsure.
