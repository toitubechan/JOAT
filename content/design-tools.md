## Card: What it is
image: designToolsHero
**Design tools** are the software and applications designers use to create visual work — for images, layouts, interfaces, and more. There's a range of tools for different purposes, from professional software to free and beginner-friendly options. Understanding the landscape helps you choose where to start, without being overwhelmed. The key: tools are just tools — skills matter more.

## Card: Why it matters
Beginners often feel overwhelmed by design software or assume they need expensive professional tools. Understanding the landscape — and that accessible options exist — removes a barrier to starting. It also clarifies that tools serve your skills, not the reverse. Knowing what kinds of tools exist for what purposes helps you choose sensibly and begin creating, rather than getting stuck before you start.

## Card: Key terms
- **Design tools**: software for creating visual work.
- **Categories**: tools grouped by purpose (image, layout, interface).
- **Accessible options**: free or beginner-friendly tools.
- **Skills over tools**: technique mattering more than the software.

## Card: Tools for different purposes
Design tools fall into broad **categories** by purpose. Some are for *image editing and creation* (editing photos, creating graphics and illustrations). Some are for *layout and print* (arranging text and images for documents, posters, publications). Some are for *UI/UX design* (designing app and website interfaces). And many general or beginner-friendly tools combine simple versions of these. You don't need to learn everything — choose tools matching what you actually want to create.

## Card: Skills matter more than tools
image: skillsOverToolsHero
The crucial principle: **skills over tools**. The most powerful, expensive software won't make a poor designer good, while a skilled designer can create excellent work with simple or free tools. Tools are just *means* to apply your design knowledge (principles, color, typography, composition). So don't get stuck thinking you need professional software to start — many **accessible options** (free or beginner-friendly) are plenty to learn on. Focus on developing design skills; the tools will follow.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking you need expensive professional software to start (accessible free options work fine for learning); believing the tool makes the designer (skills matter far more); trying to learn every tool rather than ones matching your goals; and getting stuck choosing tools instead of creating. Tools are just means to apply design skills — choose accessible ones for your purpose and focus on developing technique.

## Card: Quick recap
