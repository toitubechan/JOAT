## Card: What it is
image: philosophyEthicsHero
**Ethics** is the branch of philosophy that explores what's right and wrong, good and bad, and how we ought to live. It's not about imposing one set of rules, but about thinking carefully about morality — *why* some things are right or wrong, and how to reason about difficult moral questions. Everyone has ethics; philosophy examines them rigorously.

## Card: Why it matters
We face moral questions constantly — what's fair, what we owe others, how to act. Ethics gives tools to think about these clearly rather than just react. Understanding the main ethical approaches helps you reason about right and wrong, understand disagreements, and clarify your own values. It's perhaps the most directly relevant branch of philosophy to everyday life.

## Card: Key terms
- **Ethics**: the study of right, wrong, and how to live.
- **Consequences**: judging actions by their outcomes.
- **Duties / rules**: judging actions by principles or obligations.
- **Character / virtue**: focusing on being a good person.

## Card: Different lenses on right and wrong
image: philosophyEthicsDifferentLensesOnRightAndWrongHero
Philosophers have developed major approaches to ethics, each a useful lens. One focuses on **consequences** — an action is right if it produces good outcomes (the greatest good). Another focuses on **duties and rules** — some actions are right or wrong in themselves, regardless of outcome (like "don't lie"). A third focuses on **character and virtue** — asking not just "what should I do?" but "what kind of person should I be?" Each captures something important about morality.

## Card: Why ethics is hard (and useful)
Ethics is genuinely hard because these lenses can conflict — sometimes good outcomes require breaking a rule, or being honest causes harm. Real moral dilemmas have no easy answer, which is why ethics is about careful reasoning, not simple formulas. But this is also its value: it gives you tools to think through hard moral questions, understand why people disagree, and act more thoughtfully. It sharpens moral judgment rather than replacing it.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking ethics is just one fixed set of rules (there are different reasoned approaches); assuming moral questions have easy answers (real dilemmas are hard); confusing ethics with mere opinion or feeling (it involves careful reasoning); and dismissing it as impractical. Ethics gives genuine tools for thinking about right and wrong — valuable precisely because moral questions are difficult.

## Card: Quick recap
Ethics is the philosophy of right and wrong and how to live. Major approaches offer different lenses: judging by consequences (good outcomes), by duties and rules (some acts are right or wrong in themselves), or by character and virtue (what kind of person to be). These can conflict, making real moral dilemmas hard — which is why ethics is about careful reasoning, giving tools to think through what's right.
