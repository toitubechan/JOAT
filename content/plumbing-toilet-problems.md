## Card: What it is
image: toiletProblemHero
Toilets have a simple mechanism inside the tank, so most common problems — constant running, a weak flush, or a clog — are easy to understand and often fix yourself. Lift the tank lid and you'll see the few parts that do all the work. No special skill needed for the basics.

## Card: Why it matters
A running toilet silently wastes a surprising amount of water and money, sometimes gallons an hour. A clog at the wrong moment is a genuine emergency. Knowing the simple fixes saves you water, money, and the cost of a call-out for problems you can solve in minutes.

## Card: Key terms
- **Flapper**: the rubber seal that releases water to flush.
- **Fill valve**: refills the tank after a flush.
- **Float**: rises with the water to stop the tank overfilling.
- **Plunger**: the tool for clearing a clogged bowl.

## Card: The running toilet
image: flapperFloatChain
Constant running usually means the **flapper** — the rubber seal at the tank bottom — isn't sealing, letting water leak into the bowl so the tank keeps refilling. Flappers are cheap and easy to replace. Sometimes the **float** is set too high, sending water into the overflow; lowering it stops the trickle.

## Card: Clogs and weak flushes
For a clog, a good **plunger** with a firm seal and steady pushes clears most blockages — avoid flushing repeatedly, which can overflow the bowl. A weak flush can be caused by mineral buildup in the rim holes or a flapper closing too soon. Never flush wipes, cotton, or similar — they're a top cause of clogs.

## Card: Common mistakes
The first mistake is ignoring a running toilet as harmless when it's quietly wasting water and money. Another is flushing a clogged toilet again and again, risking an overflow. And many flush "flushable" wipes and other items that don't break down, which build up and block the pipe.

## Card: Quick recap
Most toilet issues are simple: a worn flapper or high float causes running (cheap to fix), a plunger clears clogs (don't re-flush), and buildup or a fast-closing flapper weakens flushes. Never flush wipes or non-dissolving items. Lift the lid — the parts are basic and beginner-friendly.
