## Card: What it is
image: writingStructureHero
**Structure** in writing is how you organize and order your ideas. Even clear sentences confuse if they're in a jumbled order. Good structure guides the reader logically from start to finish, so they follow your thinking effortlessly. It's the skeleton that holds clear writing together.

## Card: Why it matters
Readers can't follow a disorganized piece, no matter how well each sentence is written. Structure determines whether your point lands or gets lost. It also makes writing easier *for you* — once you know the order of your ideas, the writing flows. Most "hard to follow" writing is really badly structured writing.

## Card: Key terms
- **Structure**: the order and organization of ideas.
- **Main point first**: leading with your key message.
- **Logical flow**: ideas in an order that makes sense.
- **Paragraph**: a unit covering one idea.

## Card: Lead with your main point
image: mainPointFirstHero
A reliable principle for practical writing: put the **main point first**. Busy readers want to know your key message or request up front, not after three paragraphs of build-up. Leading with the point respects their time and ensures it's seen even if they only skim. (Save the slow build for storytelling, not emails and reports.)

## Card: Organize logically
Group related ideas and put them in a **logical flow** — an order that makes sense to the reader, where each point leads naturally to the next. Use a **paragraph** for each distinct idea, rather than one giant block. Headings, lists, and short paragraphs all help the reader navigate. Structure should make the path through your writing obvious.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: burying the main point at the end; presenting ideas in a random or tangled order; cramming everything into giant unbroken paragraphs; and no logical flow between points. The fixes: lead with your point, group and order ideas logically, break text into paragraphs, and use headings or lists where helpful.

## Card: Quick recap
Structure is how you organize ideas, and it determines whether readers can follow you. Lead with your main point (busy readers want it first), arrange ideas in a logical flow where each leads to the next, and break text into paragraphs and lists. Good structure makes the path through your writing effortless.
