## Card: What it is
image: yourBodyLanguageHero
Managing **your own body language** means being aware of the non-verbal signals you send, and shaping them to support your message. You're communicating non-verbally whether you mean to or not, so a little awareness helps you come across as confident, warm, and credible rather than accidentally nervous or closed-off.

## Card: Why it matters
Your body language affects how others perceive and respond to you — your confidence, warmth, and trustworthiness. Awkward or closed signals can undermine even good words. And interestingly, your body language can influence how *you* feel too. Managing it improves both your impressions on others and your own state of mind.

## Card: Key terms
- **Open posture**: relaxed, uncrossed, facing the other person.
- **Eye contact**: appropriate visual connection.
- **Mirroring**: subtly matching another's body language to build rapport.
- **Posture feedback**: how your posture can affect your own feelings.

## Card: Project openness and confidence
image: openConfidenceHero
A few habits convey confidence and warmth: an **open posture** (uncrossed arms, facing the person, relaxed shoulders), appropriate **eye contact** (engaged, not staring), a genuine smile, and steady, purposeful movements rather than fidgeting. You don't need to perform — just present a relaxed, open version of yourself. Closed, fidgety signals quietly undermine your words.

## Card: Mirroring and posture feedback
**Mirroring** — subtly matching the other person's posture and energy — naturally builds rapport and is something we often do unconsciously when we click with someone. Also notable: **posture feedback** — standing or sitting tall can actually help you *feel* more confident, not just appear it. Your body and mind influence each other in both directions.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: closed body language (crossed arms, hunching) that signals nervousness or defensiveness; too little or too much eye contact (staring); fidgeting that betrays anxiety; faking exaggerated "power poses" that look unnatural; and obviously copying someone (mirroring should be subtle). Aim for relaxed, open, genuine — not performed.

## Card: Quick recap
Manage your body language to support your message: open posture, appropriate eye contact, a genuine smile, and steady movements convey confidence and warmth. Subtle mirroring builds rapport, and standing tall can even help you *feel* more confident. Aim for relaxed and genuine, not performed — closed, fidgety signals undermine your words.
