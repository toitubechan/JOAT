## Card: What it is
image: histDigitalInternetHero
The **internet** connected computers worldwide, transforming them from standalone machines into a global network for sharing information. This connectivity may be the single most transformative development of the Digital Age — it changed how we communicate, learn, work, shop, and relate to each other. This card set covers its rise and impact.

## Card: Why it matters
The internet reshaped nearly every aspect of modern life within a couple of decades. Understanding its rise explains how the connected world emerged so fast, and why it's had such sweeping effects. It's the backbone of the digital age, so understanding it is key to understanding the world we now live in.

## Card: Key terms
- **Internet**: the global network connecting computers.
- **World Wide Web**: the system of linked pages on the internet.
- **Connectivity**: the state of being networked together.
- **Information age**: an era defined by easy access to information.

## Card: From isolated to connected
image: histDigitalInternetFromIsolatedToConnectedHero
Before the internet, computers were largely standalone, and information was hard to share across distances. The **internet** linked computers into a global network, and the **World Wide Web** made it easy to publish and access linked pages. Suddenly, information could flow instantly across the world. This shift from isolated machines to global **connectivity** unleashed the transformation we now call the **information age**.

## Card: What connectivity changed
The internet's impact was sweeping: instant global communication (email, messaging, video calls), access to vast information at our fingertips, e-commerce and online services, remote work, and entirely new industries and social platforms. It collapsed distances and democratized access to information and publishing. Few technologies in history have reshaped daily life so broadly and so quickly as global connectivity.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: confusing the internet (the global network) with the World Wide Web (the system of pages on it) — they're related but not identical; underrating how recent and rapid the internet's spread was; and treating constant connectivity as always normal, when it transformed life within a couple of decades. The connected world is a very recent achievement.

## Card: Quick recap
The internet connected the world's computers into a global network, and the Web made information easy to publish and access — unleashing the information age. This connectivity transformed communication, information access, commerce, work, and society within just a couple of decades. It collapsed distances and is arguably the most transformative development of the Digital Age.
