## Card: What it is
image: speakingFearHero
**Fear of public speaking** is one of the most common fears there is — and entirely normal. Managing it isn't about eliminating nerves (even experts feel them), but about understanding them and using practical techniques so they don't control you. The goal is confidence *despite* nerves, not their absence.

## Card: Why it matters
Public speaking comes up constantly — presentations, meetings, toasts, interviews — and avoiding it limits your opportunities. The fear holds back many capable people. Learning to manage it opens doors and builds a skill that improves with practice. The relief of realizing nerves are normal and manageable is itself liberating.

## Card: Key terms
- **Stage fright**: the nervousness before and during speaking.
- **Adrenaline**: the body's arousal response, which can be reframed.
- **Reframing**: interpreting nerves as excitement, not fear.
- **Preparation**: the biggest source of speaking confidence.

## Card: Nerves are normal
First, a relief: nearly everyone feels nervous speaking publicly, including seasoned professionals. **Stage fright** is just your body's **adrenaline** response — the same arousal as excitement. The goal isn't to feel calm and fearless; it's to perform well *while* nervous. Accepting nerves as normal, rather than fighting them, takes much of their power away.

## Card: Reframe and prepare
image: reframePrepareHero
Two powerful tools. **Reframing**: the physical signs of fear (racing heart, energy) are nearly identical to excitement — telling yourself "I'm excited" rather than "I'm terrified" genuinely helps. And **preparation** is the biggest confidence-booster: knowing your material well means there's far less to fear. Most speaking anxiety shrinks dramatically when you're well prepared and have practiced.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: trying to eliminate nerves entirely (impossible, and the effort adds stress); interpreting normal nerves as a sign you'll fail; under-preparing, which guarantees more anxiety; and avoiding speaking altogether, so you never improve. Accept the nerves, reframe them, prepare well, and speak anyway — confidence grows with each time.

## Card: Quick recap
Fear of public speaking is normal — even experts feel it, and the goal is to perform well despite nerves, not to eliminate them. Reframe the adrenaline as excitement, prepare thoroughly (the biggest confidence-booster), and speak anyway. Avoiding it only keeps the fear; facing it, repeatedly, is how confidence is built.
