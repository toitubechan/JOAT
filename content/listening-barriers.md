## Card: What it is
image: listeningBarriersHero
**Barriers to listening** are the things — internal and external — that stop us truly hearing others. From distractions and assumptions to the urge to fix or judge, these barriers are why good listening is so rare. Recognizing them is the first step to removing them.

## Card: Why it matters
You can intend to listen well and still fail because of unnoticed barriers. Knowing the common ones lets you catch yourself in the act and correct course. Most poor listening isn't from not caring — it's from these predictable, fixable obstacles getting in the way.

## Card: Key terms
- **Distraction**: anything pulling focus from the speaker.
- **Assumption**: deciding what they mean before they finish.
- **The urge to fix**: jumping to solutions instead of listening.
- **Judging**: evaluating rather than understanding.

## Card: Internal barriers
image: internalListeningBarriersHero
The trickiest barriers are inside your own head: **assumptions** (deciding what someone means before they finish); **the urge to fix** (leaping to advice when they just want to be heard); **judging** (evaluating their words rather than understanding them); and rehearsing your reply while they talk. These all pull your attention away from genuine understanding.

## Card: External barriers
External barriers are easier to spot: **distractions** like phones, notifications, noise, and multitasking; interrupting; and physical signals of inattention (looking away, checking the time). Removing these — putting the phone away, facing the person, giving undivided attention — is straightforward once you decide to, and immediately improves your listening.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: trying to listen while distracted by a phone; jumping in with solutions when someone wants to vent, not be fixed; assuming you know where they're going and tuning out; and judging silently instead of understanding. The fix is awareness: notice the barrier rising, and consciously return your full attention to the speaker.

## Card: Quick recap
Barriers to listening are internal (assumptions, the urge to fix, judging, rehearsing replies) and external (phones, distractions, interrupting). They're why good listening is rare. Most poor listening isn't from not caring but from these fixable obstacles. Notice them rising, remove the external ones, and consciously return your attention to the speaker.
