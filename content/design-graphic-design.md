## Card: What it is
image: graphicDesignHero
**Graphic design** is the craft of communicating visually — combining text, images, color, and layout to convey a message or idea effectively. It's all around us: logos, posters, packaging, websites, apps. At its heart, graphic design is about *communication*, not just making things look pretty. Understanding the basics reveals a purposeful craft behind the visuals we see everywhere.

## Card: Why it matters
Graphic design shapes how information and brands are communicated everywhere we look. Understanding it reveals that good design is purposeful communication (not just decoration), helps you create more effective visuals, and lets you appreciate (and judge) the design around you. It ties together the design principles, color, typography, and composition into a practical, widely useful craft.

## Card: Key terms
- **Graphic design**: visual communication of a message.
- **Communication**: the core purpose — conveying a message clearly.
- **Visual elements**: text, images, color, and layout combined.
- **Purpose-driven**: design serving a goal, not just decoration.

## Card: Design is communication
image: designCommunicationHero
The key insight: **graphic design** is fundamentally about **communication**, not just aesthetics. Every design has a *purpose* — to convey a message, inform, persuade, or guide. Good graphic design serves that purpose effectively, making the message clear and impactful for its audience. "Looking nice" is secondary to communicating well; in fact, the best-looking design that fails to communicate its message has failed. This **purpose-driven** mindset is what separates effective graphic design from mere decoration.

## Card: Combining the elements
Graphic design works by combining **visual elements** — text (typography), images, color, and layout (composition) — applying the design principles you've learned (hierarchy, contrast, alignment, balance) to communicate effectively. The designer makes choices about all these to serve the message and audience: what to emphasize, what mood to set, how to guide the eye. Graphic design is essentially the skilled, purposeful combination of these elements to communicate a clear message visually.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking graphic design is just making things pretty (it's purposeful communication); prioritizing looks over the message (a beautiful design that fails to communicate has failed); ignoring the audience and purpose; and not applying core principles (hierarchy, contrast, etc.). Good graphic design combines visual elements purposefully to communicate a clear message effectively — communication first, decoration second.

## Card: Quick recap
Graphic design is the craft of communicating visually — combining text, images, color, and layout to convey a message effectively. Its essence is communication and purpose, not just decoration: the best-looking design that fails to convey its message has failed. It works by skillfully combining visual elements and applying design principles (hierarchy, contrast, alignment, balance) to serve the message and audience. Communication first, aesthetics second.
