## Card: What it is
image: deliveryVoiceHero
**Delivery** is *how* you speak — your voice, pace, pauses, and presence — as opposed to *what* you say. Great content delivered poorly falls flat; decent content delivered well lands. Delivery is a learnable set of habits, not a gift, and small improvements make a big difference to how you come across.

## Card: Why it matters
Audiences respond strongly to delivery: a monotone voice loses them, while energy and clarity hold them. Since how you say something shapes how it's received, delivery can make or break a talk regardless of the material. The encouraging part is that these are simple, practical skills anyone can improve.

## Card: Key terms
- **Pace**: how fast you speak.
- **Pause**: a deliberate moment of silence.
- **Vocal variety**: changing tone, volume, and pace to stay engaging.
- **Filler words**: "um," "like," "you know" that clutter speech.

## Card: Slow down and pause
image: pausePaceHero
The most common delivery fix: **slow down**. Nerves make people rush, becoming hard to follow. A slower **pace** sounds confident and lets the audience absorb your points. **Pauses** are powerful too — a moment of silence after a key point gives it weight and gives you a breath. Silence feels long to you but reads as poised to the audience.

## Card: Voice and filler words
**Vocal variety** keeps people engaged — varying your tone, volume, and pace stops the dreaded monotone. Emphasize important words; let your voice rise and fall naturally. Meanwhile, reduce **filler words** ("um," "like," "you know"), which creep in from nerves and clutter your message. A confident pause is far better than an "um" — silence beats filler.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: speaking too fast from nerves; a flat, monotone voice that bores; never pausing, so points blur together; overusing filler words; and speaking too quietly to be heard. The fixes are simple: slow down, vary your voice, use pauses, and replace fillers with brief silence. Practice makes these automatic.

## Card: Quick recap
Delivery — how you speak — can make or break a talk. Slow down (nerves make us rush), use pauses to give points weight, and vary your voice to avoid monotony. Cut filler words; a confident pause beats an "um." These are learnable habits, and small improvements noticeably change how you come across.
