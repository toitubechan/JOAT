## Card: What it is
image: sleepWhyHero
**Sleep** isn't your body switching off — it's an active, essential process where your brain and body repair, reorganize, and recharge. Far from wasted time, sleep is when much of your most important maintenance happens. You can't function well, or for long, without it.

## Card: Why it matters
Sleep affects everything: memory, mood, focus, immunity, weight, and long-term health. Skimping on it impairs you more than most people realize — chronic short sleep is linked to serious health problems. Understanding why sleep matters helps you stop treating it as the first thing to sacrifice when busy.

## Card: Key terms
- **Sleep**: an active state of rest and repair.
- **Sleep deprivation**: not getting enough sleep.
- **Memory consolidation**: locking in what you learned that day.
- **Circadian rhythm**: your body's internal 24-hour clock.

## Card: What happens while you sleep
image: sleepBodyRepairHero
During sleep, your brain performs **memory consolidation** — sorting and storing what you learned, which is why sleep aids learning. Your body repairs tissues, balances hormones, and clears waste from the brain. It's a nightly tune-up: skip it, and the maintenance simply doesn't get done.

## Card: The cost of too little
**Sleep deprivation** does more than make you tired. It impairs concentration, decision-making, and reaction time (drowsy driving rivals drunk driving), worsens mood, weakens immunity, and over time raises health risks. Crucially, you adapt to *feeling* less tired while still being impaired — so you underestimate the damage.

## Card: Common mistakes
The biggest mistake is treating sleep as optional or a sign of laziness, cutting it to do more. Another is believing you can "catch up" fully on weekends — it helps a little but doesn't undo weekday debt. And many overestimate how well they function on little sleep, since the impairment hides itself.

## Card: Quick recap
Sleep is active, essential maintenance: it consolidates memory, repairs the body, and balances hormones. Too little impairs focus, mood, immunity, and safety — and the damage hides because you stop *feeling* as tired. It's not optional time to sacrifice; it's foundational to health and performance.
