## Card: What it is
image: evolutionHero
**Evolution** is the process by which living things change over many generations, and how the huge variety of life arose from common ancestors. Its main driver is **natural selection** — a simple but powerful idea that explains how life adapts to its environment over vast spans of time.

## Card: Why it matters
Evolution is the unifying theory of biology, explaining the diversity of life, why organisms are suited to their environments, and how species (including humans) came to be. It also has practical relevance — from antibiotic resistance to how diseases evolve. Understanding it is key to understanding the living world.

## Card: Key terms
- **Evolution**: change in living things over generations.
- **Natural selection**: survival and reproduction of the better-suited.
- **Variation**: natural differences between individuals.
- **Adaptation**: a trait that helps an organism survive.

## Card: How natural selection works
image: naturalSelectionHero
The logic is simple. Individuals vary (**variation**). Some variations help survival and reproduction in a given environment. Those individuals have more offspring, passing on the helpful traits. Over many generations, helpful traits become common — the population has evolved. This is **natural selection**: nature "selecting" what works, not by intention, but by who survives and reproduces.

## Card: Adaptation and common ancestry
Over time, natural selection produces **adaptations** — traits well-suited to an environment, like a polar bear's thick fur. Given enough time, populations change so much they become new species. All life shares **common ancestors**: trace back far enough, and every organism connects on one vast family tree. The variety of life is the result of billions of years of this branching change.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking evolution is an individual changing during its life (it's about populations over generations); imagining organisms evolve "on purpose" or by trying (variation is random; selection isn't intentional); seeing it as "just a theory" in the casual sense (in science, it's a well-supported explanation); and thinking humans descended from monkeys (we share common ancestors with them).

## Card: Quick recap
Evolution is how life changes over generations, driven by natural selection: individuals vary, the better-suited survive and reproduce more, and helpful traits spread. Over time this produces adaptations and new species, all connected through common ancestors on one family tree. It's biology's unifying explanation for life's diversity.
