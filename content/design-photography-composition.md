## Card: What it is
image: photoCompositionHero
**Photography composition** is how you arrange and frame elements within a photo — what to include, where to place it, and how to frame the shot. It's the single biggest factor in making photos look good, far more than the camera. Understanding a few composition principles can dramatically improve anyone's photos, even with a phone.

## Card: Why it matters
Almost everyone takes photos, but most never learn composition — the key to better photos. Understanding a few simple principles improves your photos immediately and noticeably, regardless of your camera. It's the most impactful, accessible photography skill, turning snapshots into stronger images. Composition, not gear, is what separates good photos from forgettable ones.

## Card: Key terms
- **Composition**: how elements are arranged and framed in a photo.
- **Rule of thirds**: placing key elements off-center for balance.
- **Framing**: deciding what to include and exclude.
- **Leading lines**: lines that guide the eye into the photo.

## Card: A few powerful principles
Some simple, high-impact composition principles: the **rule of thirds** — imagine the frame divided into thirds (a 3×3 grid) and place key elements along those lines or intersections, rather than dead-center, for a more balanced, dynamic photo. **Leading lines** — use natural lines (roads, fences, edges) to draw the eye toward your subject. And mindful **framing** — deciding what to include and, crucially, what to *exclude* (removing clutter and distractions). These few principles alone noticeably improve photos.

## Card: Composition over gear
image: compositionOverGearHero
The key insight: **composition matters far more than the camera**. A thoughtfully composed photo on a phone beats a poorly composed one on an expensive camera. Better photos come mainly from *how you frame and arrange the shot* — your composition choices — not from gear. This is empowering: you can take much better photos right now, with whatever camera you have, simply by applying composition principles and thinking about how you frame each shot. Skill, not equipment, is the difference.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: always centering the subject (the rule of thirds often looks better); cluttered framing with distractions (decide what to exclude, not just include); thinking a better camera is the key to better photos (composition matters far more); and not thinking about framing at all. A few composition principles — rule of thirds, leading lines, mindful framing — dramatically improve photos, regardless of gear.

## Card: Quick recap
Photography composition — how you arrange and frame elements in a photo — is the biggest factor in good photos, far more than the camera. A few principles help immediately: the rule of thirds (place key elements off-center on a 3×3 grid), leading lines (lines guiding the eye to the subject), and mindful framing (deciding what to include and exclude). Composition over gear is empowering — you can take much better photos right now with any camera.
