## Card: What it is
image: parentingSupportingLearningHero
**Supporting learning** is how caregivers help children develop their minds and a love of learning — through everyday interaction, play, reading, curiosity, and encouragement, not just formal schooling. Much of children's most important learning happens through ordinary experiences. Understanding how to support it helps caregivers nurture capable, curious learners. This is general educational knowledge, not professional advice.

## Card: Why it matters
A child's attitude to learning and their early development profoundly shape their future. Caregivers play a huge role — much learning happens at home and through everyday life, not just school. Understanding how to support learning (and that play and curiosity matter enormously) helps caregivers nurture children's minds effectively, often more through everyday moments than formal instruction. It's a high-impact, accessible role.

## Card: Key terms
- **Supporting learning**: helping children develop minds and curiosity.
- **Play**: a primary way children learn.
- **Reading together**: a powerful support for learning.
- **Love of learning**: fostering genuine curiosity and motivation.

## Card: Children learn through play and everyday life
image: parentingSupportingLearningChildrenLearnThroughPlayAndEverydayLifeHero
A key insight: much of children's most important learning happens through **play** and everyday experiences, not formal instruction. Play is how children naturally explore, experiment, problem-solve, and develop — it's not a break from learning but a primary *vehicle* for it. Everyday interactions — talking, exploring, answering questions, doing things together — are rich learning opportunities. So supporting learning is less about formal drilling and more about providing a stimulating, interactive environment full of play, conversation, and exploration.

## Card: Reading and a love of learning
Two powerful supports. **Reading together** is one of the most valuable things caregivers can do — it builds language, knowledge, imagination, and a bond, and supports learning broadly. And fostering a **love of learning** matters more than pushing facts: children who are curious and enjoy learning thrive far more than those pressured or made anxious. Encouraging curiosity, celebrating effort and discovery, and keeping learning positive nurtures lifelong, self-motivated learners — which is more valuable than any specific early academic push.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking learning only happens through formal instruction (play and everyday life are primary); undervaluing play as "not learning" (it's a primary vehicle for learning); pushing facts or academics anxiously rather than fostering curiosity (love of learning matters more); and neglecting reading together. Supporting learning is mostly about a stimulating, interactive environment, play, reading, and nurturing genuine curiosity.

## Card: Quick recap
Supporting learning is helping children develop their minds and a love of learning — largely through play, everyday interaction, reading together, and encouraging curiosity, not just formal schooling. Play is a primary vehicle for learning, not a break from it. Reading together is especially powerful. And fostering genuine curiosity and a love of learning matters more than pushing facts anxiously — it nurtures lifelong, self-motivated learners. Everyday moments are rich learning opportunities.
