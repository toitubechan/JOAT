## Card: What it is
image: musicReadingMusicHero
**Reading music** means understanding musical notation — the written system that tells musicians what notes to play, how long, and how. It looks like a mysterious code, but it's a logical system anyone can learn. Understanding the basics demystifies sheet music and opens the door to learning instruments and music more deeply.

## Card: Why it matters
Written music is a universal language that lets musicians share and preserve music precisely, across time and place. While you can make music without reading it, understanding notation greatly expands what you can learn and play, and lets you access centuries of written music. Demystifying it removes a barrier many find intimidating about learning music.

## Card: Key terms
- **Notation**: the written system for representing music.
- **Staff**: the set of lines notes are written on.
- **Pitch**: how high or low a note is (shown by position).
- **Duration**: how long a note lasts (shown by its symbol).

## Card: Notes on a page
image: musicReadingMusicNotesOnAPageHero
Musical **notation** represents two main things visually. **Pitch** (how high or low a note is) is shown by a note's *vertical position* on the **staff** (the set of horizontal lines) — higher on the staff means higher pitch. **Duration** (how long the note lasts) is shown by the note's *shape* (different symbols mean different lengths). So a note's position tells you *which* note, and its appearance tells you *how long*. Together, these capture a melody precisely on paper.

## Card: A learnable system
While sheet music looks like a daunting code, it's a **logical system** built from consistent rules, learnable step by step — not memorizing random symbols. It also shows more than pitch and duration: timing, dynamics (loud/soft), and other instructions. Like learning to read words, learning to read music takes practice but follows clear logic. Many musicians learn it gradually alongside playing. It's a skill, not a mysterious talent — approachable for anyone willing to learn the basics.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking reading music is an impenetrable code (it's a logical, learnable system); believing you must read music to make any music (many musicians play by ear, though reading expands possibilities); expecting to learn it instantly (like reading words, it takes practice); and being intimidated into not trying. Notation logically captures pitch (position) and duration (symbol) — learnable step by step.

## Card: Quick recap
Reading music means understanding notation — the written system showing what to play. A note's vertical position on the staff shows its pitch (higher position, higher pitch), and its symbol shows duration (how long it lasts). Notation also captures timing, dynamics, and more. It looks like a daunting code but is a logical, learnable system, built from consistent rules — approachable for anyone, like learning to read words.
