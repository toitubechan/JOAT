## Card: What it is
image: digitalFootprintHero
Your **digital footprint** is the trail of data you leave online — everything from posts and photos you share, to the searches, clicks, and locations recorded behind the scenes. It builds up over years into a surprisingly detailed record. Much of it is permanent and hard to erase.

## Card: Why it matters
Your footprint can be seen by people you didn't intend — employers, strangers, companies, even future you. It shapes how others perceive you and what information exists about you. Understanding it helps you manage your reputation and reduce what you unknowingly reveal, now and far into the future.

## Card: Key terms
- **Digital footprint**: the total trail of your online data.
- **Active footprint**: things you deliberately share or post.
- **Passive footprint**: data collected without you actively sharing it.
- **Permanence**: how online content can persist indefinitely.

## Card: Active vs. passive
image: activePassiveFootprint
Your footprint has two halves. The **active footprint** is what you knowingly share — posts, comments, photos, profiles. The **passive footprint** is collected without your direct action — sites you visit, searches, location, time spent. Most people think only about the active part, but the passive trail is often larger and less visible.

## Card: The internet remembers
A crucial feature is **permanence**: once something is online, it can be copied, screenshotted, archived, and resurface years later, even if you delete the original. "Delete" rarely means truly gone. This is why a useful habit is to pause before posting — assume anything you put online could be permanent and public.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: assuming deleted content is truly gone; forgetting that the passive footprint exists at all; posting in the moment without considering future consequences; and not realizing old accounts and posts still represent you. A periodic "self-search" — looking yourself up — reveals what others can find.

## Card: Quick recap
Your digital footprint is the lasting trail of everything you share (active) and everything quietly collected (passive). Online content is often permanent, so deleting rarely erases it fully. Pause before posting, mind the invisible passive trail, and occasionally search yourself to see what's out there about you.
