## Card: What it is
image: lifeOutThereHero
**Is there life beyond Earth?** is one of humanity's biggest questions, and a real scientific pursuit. Scientists search for life — from simple microbes to intelligent civilizations — across our solar system and beyond. We don't yet have an answer, but the search is grounded in genuine science, not just speculation.

## Card: Why it matters
The question of alien life touches profound questions about our place in the universe. It's also a serious scientific field, driving space missions and telescope searches. Understanding how scientists actually approach it — and what we do and don't know — separates real science from science fiction and tells you why this question is so compelling.

## Card: Key terms
- **Habitable zone**: where a planet could have liquid water.
- **Exoplanet**: a planet orbiting another star.
- **Microbial life**: simple, single-celled life.
- **SETI**: the search for intelligent signals from space.

## Card: Where scientists look
image: habitableSearchHero
Scientists search for life in two main ways. Within our solar system, they look for signs of **microbial life** — simple organisms — on places like Mars or the icy moons of Jupiter and Saturn (which may hide oceans). Beyond, they study **exoplanets** (planets orbiting other stars), especially those in the **habitable zone** where liquid water could exist. The discovery of countless exoplanets has made the search far more concrete.

## Card: Simple life vs. intelligent life
Two different questions. Finding *simple* microbial life (even fossilized microbes) would be a huge discovery and may be more likely. Finding *intelligent* life is far harder — efforts like **SETI** listen for signals from advanced civilizations. The universe is vast, with countless stars and planets, which makes some scientists think life could be common — yet we've found no confirmed evidence yet. It remains a genuine open question.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: assuming the question is unscientific (it's a real, evidence-based field); confusing the search for *any* life (often microbial) with the search for *intelligent* aliens; thinking aliens have been confirmed (no confirmed evidence exists); and assuming the vastness of the universe guarantees life (it makes it plausible, but unproven). It's a serious, open scientific question.

## Card: Quick recap
The search for life beyond Earth is real science: scientists look for simple microbial life in our solar system (Mars, icy moons) and study exoplanets in habitable zones around other stars, while efforts like SETI listen for intelligent signals. The universe's vastness makes life seem plausible to many, but no confirmed evidence exists yet. It remains one of science's great open questions.
