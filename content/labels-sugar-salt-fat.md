## Card: What it is
image: sugarSaltFatHero
**Sugar**, **salt**, and certain **fats** are the nutrients most health guidelines suggest keeping an eye on, because many processed foods contain more than people realize. Knowing how to spot them on a label — and the tricks used to hide them — helps you make informed choices about everyday foods.

## Card: Why it matters
Excess sugar and salt are linked to health issues, and they hide in surprising places — sauces, bread, "healthy" snacks. Labels let you see what's really there, but only if you know what to look for. This is general guidance for awareness, not medical or dietary advice for any specific condition.

## Card: Key terms
- **High/low thresholds**: rough guides for what counts as a lot or a little per 100g.
- **Hidden sugar**: sugar in foods you wouldn't expect.
- **Salt vs. sodium**: salt is often listed as sodium; they differ in number.
- **Traffic-light labels**: color codes (red/amber/green) some labels use.

## Card: Quick ways to judge
Many labels use **traffic-light** colors — green (low), amber (medium), red (high) — for fat, saturates, sugar, and salt, giving an at-a-glance read. Where there are no colors, the **per 100g** figure helps: there are rough **thresholds** for "a lot" versus "a little." Lots of red or high per-100g numbers signal a product to enjoy in moderation.

## Card: Hidden sugar and salt
image: hiddenSugarSalt
**Hidden sugar** is everywhere — sauces, bread, cereals, yogurts, drinks — often where you'd least expect it, which is why checking matters. **Salt** is sometimes listed as **sodium**, a different (smaller) number for the same thing, which can confuse comparisons. Savory processed foods are common salt culprits, so the label is worth a glance.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: assuming "savory" foods have no sugar (many do); confusing salt and sodium figures when comparing; trusting "healthy"-sounding products without checking; and judging only the front claim. A "low fat" food, for instance, is often high in sugar to compensate. The label cuts through all of this.

## Card: Quick recap
Sugar, salt, and certain fats are worth watching, and they hide in unexpected foods. Use traffic-light colors or per-100g figures to judge quickly — lots of red or high numbers mean moderation. Watch for hidden sugar in savory and "healthy" products, and note salt may appear as sodium. The label reveals the truth.
