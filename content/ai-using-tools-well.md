## Card: What it is
image: aiToolUseHero
AI chatbots and assistants are now everyday tools, but how you *use* them hugely affects what you get back. A few simple habits — clear instructions, giving context, and checking results — turn AI from a frustrating gimmick into a genuinely useful helper.

## Card: Why it matters
The same AI gives a vague, useless answer to a vague question and a great one to a clear, specific request. Learning to work with these tools well is quickly becoming a practical skill for study, work, and daily life. A little technique unlocks most of the value.

## Card: Key terms
- **Prompt**: the instruction or question you give an AI.
- **Context**: background details that help the AI respond well.
- **Iterate**: refining your request based on the response.
- **Verify**: checking the AI's output for accuracy.

## Card: Write better prompts
The quality of your **prompt** drives the quality of the answer. Be specific about what you want, give relevant **context** (who it's for, the goal, the format), and state any constraints. "Write something about dogs" gets a vague reply; "Write a friendly 3-sentence intro about dog adoption for a shelter's website" gets a useful one.

## Card: Iterate and verify
image: iterateVerifyAi
AI works best as a conversation. If the first answer isn't right, **iterate** — tell it what to change ("make it shorter", "more formal", "you missed X"). Treat its output as a draft, not a final truth. And always **verify** anything factual or important, since AI can sound confident while being wrong.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: vague prompts that get vague answers; accepting the first response instead of refining it; trusting facts without checking; and — importantly — sharing sensitive personal information with AI tools, which may store or use it. Treat AI chats as potentially non-private, and keep confidential details out.

## Card: Quick recap
Use AI well by writing clear, specific prompts with context and constraints, iterating like a conversation, and treating output as a draft to verify — especially facts. Don't share sensitive personal information, since chats may not be private. Good technique turns AI into a genuinely useful everyday assistant.
