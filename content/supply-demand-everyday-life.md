## Card: What it is
image: econSupplydemandEverydayLifeHero
Once you understand supply and demand, you start seeing them everywhere — in surge pricing, seasonal sales, ticket prices, housing, and wages. This card set connects the theory to real, everyday situations, making the concept genuinely useful for understanding the world around you.

## Card: Why it matters
Supply and demand isn't just textbook theory — it explains a huge range of daily experiences and decisions. Recognizing it in action helps you understand price changes, make better timing decisions (when to buy or sell), and see the logic behind situations that might otherwise seem unfair or random.

## Card: Key terms
- **Surge pricing**: prices rising when demand is high.
- **Seasonal pricing**: prices changing with predictable demand cycles.
- **Labor market**: supply and demand applied to jobs and wages.
- **Timing**: choosing when to buy or sell based on these forces.

## Card: Pricing you see every day
image: econSupplydemandEverydayLifePricingYouSeeEveryDayHero
**Surge pricing** (taxis or rides costing more at busy times) is supply and demand live — high demand, limited supply, higher price. **Seasonal pricing** follows demand cycles: flights and hotels cost more in holiday seasons, winter coats are cheapest in spring. Recognizing these patterns helps with **timing** — buying when demand (and price) is low, like off-season.

## Card: Jobs, wages, and housing
Supply and demand even shapes wages: in the **labor market**, skills in high demand but short supply command higher pay, while abundant skills pay less. Housing prices reflect it too — limited supply plus high demand in a desirable city means high prices. Seeing these as supply-and-demand situations explains a lot about careers, costs, and where things are expensive.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: seeing surge or seasonal pricing as pure "rip-off" rather than supply and demand (though it can feel unfair); not applying the concept to wages or housing; missing chances to time purchases around demand cycles; and thinking these forces are abstract theory rather than everyday reality. The forces are genuinely everywhere.

## Card: Quick recap
Supply and demand operate everywhere in daily life: surge pricing, seasonal sales, ticket and housing prices, even wages (high-demand, scarce skills pay more). Recognizing the patterns helps you understand price changes and time purchases wisely — buying off-season when demand and prices are low. The theory is genuinely everyday reality.
