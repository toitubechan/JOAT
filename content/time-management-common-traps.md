## Card: What it is
image: growthTimeCommonTrapsHero
Even with good intentions, certain habits quietly drain your time and undermine productivity. This card set collects the most common **time traps** — so you can spot and avoid them. Recognizing these is often more useful than any new productivity technique.

## Card: Why it matters
You can plan and prioritize well and still lose hours to these traps without noticing. They're sneaky because each feels harmless or even productive in the moment. Naming them makes them visible, and visible problems are fixable. Avoiding a few traps frees up real time and mental energy.

## Card: Key terms
- **Multitasking**: juggling several tasks, usually less effectively.
- **Distraction**: anything pulling attention from the task.
- **Perfectionism**: over-polishing beyond what's needed.
- **Shiny new system**: endlessly tweaking tools instead of working.

## Card: The big time traps
image: growthTimeCommonTrapsTheBigTimeTrapsHero
Watch for these: **multitasking** (slower and more error-prone than focusing); constant **distraction** from notifications and phones; **perfectionism** (pouring hours into polishing what's already good enough); and saying yes to everything, leaving no time for your own priorities. Each feels reasonable but quietly devours your hours.

## Card: The productivity-tool trap
A sneaky one: endlessly chasing the **shiny new system** — new apps, planners, and methods — as a form of productive-feeling procrastination. Tinkering with your setup *feels* like progress but isn't the work. The best system is a simple one you actually use. Don't let optimizing how you work replace actually working.

## Card: Common mistakes
To gather them: multitasking instead of focusing; leaving notifications on to interrupt you; perfecting things past "good enough"; over-committing and protecting no time for priorities; and fiddling with productivity tools instead of doing tasks. Also: never taking breaks, which leads to burnout and worse work over time.

## Card: Quick recap
Common time traps include multitasking, constant distraction, perfectionism, over-committing, and endlessly tweaking productivity systems instead of working. Each feels harmless but steals time. Spot them, cut distractions, aim for "good enough," guard your priorities, and remember: a simple system you use beats a perfect one you tinker with.
