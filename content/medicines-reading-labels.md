## Card: What it is
image: medicineLabelHero
A medicine **label** and its leaflet tell you everything you need to use it safely — the dose, how often, warnings, and what's in it. Learning to read them turns a confusing wall of text into the few key facts that keep you safe. This is general guidance; ask a pharmacist if anything is unclear.

## Card: Why it matters
Most medicine mistakes — wrong dose, dangerous combinations, missed warnings — come from not reading the label. The information is there precisely to protect you. A minute spent reading it can prevent an overdose, a bad interaction, or taking something you shouldn't. It's the simplest safety habit there is.

## Card: Key terms
- **Dose**: how much to take at one time.
- **Frequency**: how often to take it.
- **Active ingredient**: the substance doing the work.
- **Contraindication**: a reason you should NOT take it.

## Card: The key things to find
On any medicine, locate: the **active ingredient** (what it actually is), the **dose** and **frequency** (how much, how often, and the maximum per day), and any **warnings** or **contraindications** — reasons not to take it, like other conditions or medicines. These few facts cover most of what keeps you safe.

## Card: Watch the active ingredient
image: activeIngredientCheck
A crucial tip: different products can contain the *same* **active ingredient**. People accidentally overdose by taking, say, a painkiller *and* a cold-and-flu remedy that *also* contains that painkiller. Always check active ingredients across everything you're taking, so you don't double up without realizing.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common errors: ignoring the maximum daily dose; not checking for the same active ingredient in multiple products; skipping warnings about alcohol, driving, or other medicines; and not reading whether to take it with food. When the label or leaflet is confusing, a **pharmacist** can explain it for free — use them.

## Card: Quick recap
A medicine label tells you the active ingredient, dose, frequency, maximum amount, and warnings. Always check it, respect the maximum dose, and watch for the same active ingredient hiding in multiple products to avoid accidental overdose. If anything's unclear, ask a pharmacist — that's exactly what they're there for.
