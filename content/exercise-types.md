## Card: What it is
image: exerciseTypesHero
Exercise comes in a few main types, each training the body differently: **cardio** (heart and lungs), **strength** (muscles), and **flexibility/mobility** (range of movement). A well-rounded routine includes some of each, but you can start with whichever fits your goals and life.

## Card: Why it matters
Knowing the types helps you build balanced fitness rather than overdoing one and neglecting others. People often do only cardio or only weights; understanding what each offers helps you fill the gaps. It also means you can pick activities you enjoy within each type, making exercise far more sustainable.

## Card: Key terms
- **Cardio (aerobic)**: raises heart rate, trains heart and lungs.
- **Strength (resistance)**: works muscles against resistance.
- **Flexibility**: the range your joints and muscles can move.
- **Mobility**: moving joints well through their full range.

## Card: Cardio and strength
image: cardioStrengthMobility
**Cardio** — walking, running, cycling, swimming — raises your heart rate and builds heart, lung, and stamina health. **Strength** training — weights, resistance bands, or bodyweight moves like push-ups — builds and maintains muscle and bone. Both matter: cardio for endurance and heart health, strength for power, metabolism, and aging well.

## Card: Flexibility and mobility
**Flexibility** (how far muscles stretch) and **mobility** (how well joints move) are the most neglected types — and they keep you moving comfortably and prevent stiffness and injury. Stretching, yoga, and mobility drills help here. As you age, this becomes increasingly important for staying active and avoiding aches.

## Card: Common mistakes
A common mistake is doing only one type — endless cardio with no strength, or weights with no stretching. Another is skipping flexibility entirely until something hurts. And many think strength training is only for bodybuilders, missing its huge benefits for everyone, including healthy aging and metabolism.

## Card: Quick recap
The main exercise types are cardio (heart and stamina), strength (muscle and bone), and flexibility/mobility (comfortable movement). A balanced routine includes some of each, but start with what you enjoy and fits your goals. Don't neglect strength or flexibility — both matter far beyond athletes.
