## Card: What it is
image: financeSavingSinkingFundsHero
A **sinking fund** is money you set aside gradually for a specific future expense you know is coming — a holiday, car insurance, a new laptop, the festive season. Instead of one painful lump-sum hit, you save a little each month so the cash is ready when the bill arrives. It's planned saving with a target.

## Card: Why it matters
Big "surprise" costs often aren't surprises at all — they're predictable events we just don't prepare for. Sinking funds turn those budget-busters into non-events. They're also what keeps an emergency fund for true emergencies, instead of being raided every time a known cost rolls around.

## Card: Key terms
- **Sinking fund**: savings built up for a specific planned cost.
- **Target amount**: the total you're saving toward.
- **Monthly contribution**: the amount you set aside each month.
- **Time horizon**: how long until you need the money.

## Card: How to set one up
image: financeSavingSinkingFundsHowToSetOneUpHero
Pick the **target amount** and the **time horizon**, then divide. Need €600 for a trip in 12 months? Save €50 a month. That's your **monthly contribution**. Keep each sinking fund clearly labeled — many banking apps let you create named savings pots — so you can see each goal filling up separately.

## Card: Sinking fund vs. emergency fund
They're easy to confuse but different. An **emergency fund** covers the *unexpected* — job loss, a sudden repair. A **sinking fund** covers the *expected but irregular* — a planned holiday, annual fees, predictable replacements. Keeping them separate means a known cost never forces you to drain the cushion meant for genuine surprises.

## Card: Common mistakes
The first mistake is lumping all goals into one pot, so you can't tell what's "spoken for" versus free. The second is dipping into a sinking fund for unrelated spending, leaving you short when the real bill lands. And many forget recurring annual costs entirely — set those up as ongoing sinking funds that refill each year.

## Card: Quick recap
A sinking fund saves gradually for a known future cost, turning budget-busters into non-events. Set a target and timeline, divide into monthly contributions, and keep funds labeled and separate from your emergency fund. Plan for the predictable, and big bills stop being a shock.
