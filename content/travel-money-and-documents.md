## Card: What it is
image: travelMoneyDocsHero
Managing your **money** and **documents** abroad is essential to a smooth trip — accessing funds without huge fees, and keeping your passport and key papers safe. Mishandling either can cause serious stress far from home. Knowing the basics keeps you secure and saves money. (General travel guidance.)

## Card: Why it matters
Being stranded without accessible money, or losing your passport abroad, are among the worst travel situations — and largely preventable. Smart handling of money avoids painful fees, and protecting documents avoids a nightmare. This is practical knowledge that protects both your wallet and your peace of mind while traveling.

## Card: Key terms
- **Exchange rate**: the value of one currency in another.
- **Foreign transaction fee**: a charge for spending abroad.
- **Backup**: a second source of money or copy of documents.
- **Document copies**: spare copies of passport and key papers.

## Card: Money abroad without big fees
Accessing money abroad can be costly if you're careless. Watch for **foreign transaction fees** and poor **exchange rates** — airport currency exchanges and some cards charge a lot. Tips: use cards with low/no foreign fees where possible, be wary of "dynamic currency conversion" (paying in your home currency abroad often gives a worse rate), and carry a **backup** payment method in case one fails. Notify your bank of travel so cards aren't blocked.

## Card: Protecting your documents
image: documentCopies
Your passport and key **documents** are precious abroad. Keep them secure (a hotel safe, a money belt), and crucially, keep **document copies** — photos or scans stored separately (and digitally) — so that if the original is lost or stolen, replacing it is far easier. Know where your country's embassy or consulate is at your destination, as they help with lost passports and emergencies.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: using airport exchanges or high-fee cards (poor rates and charges); accepting "dynamic currency conversion" (worse rate); carrying only one payment method (disaster if lost or blocked); not notifying the bank (cards get frozen); and having no copies of documents (a nightmare if the passport is lost). Plan money and documents carefully.

## Card: Quick recap
Handle money abroad smartly: avoid high fees and poor rates (airport exchanges, dynamic currency conversion), use low-fee cards, carry a backup payment method, and notify your bank. Protect documents: keep the passport secure, store separate copies (digital and physical), and know your embassy's location. Good handling prevents the worst travel headaches.
