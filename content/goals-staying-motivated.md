## Card: What it is
image: growthGoalsStayingMotivatedHero
Motivation is the drive to pursue a goal — but it naturally rises and falls. Staying motivated isn't about feeling fired up all the time (you won't); it's about having strategies and systems that keep you going *especially* when the initial excitement fades. That gap is where most goals are won or lost.

## Card: Why it matters
Almost everyone starts a goal motivated; far fewer finish. The difference isn't who feels more inspired, but who keeps going when they don't. Understanding that motivation fluctuates — and how to act anyway — is what turns started goals into achieved ones. It's the real secret behind persistence.

## Card: Key terms
- **Motivation**: the drive to act toward a goal.
- **Discipline / systems**: structures that keep you going without relying on feeling motivated.
- **Intrinsic motivation**: drive from within (enjoyment, meaning).
- **Accountability**: being answerable to someone for your progress.

## Card: Don't rely on motivation
image: growthGoalsStayingMotivatedDonTRelyOnMotivationHero
Motivation is fickle — it comes and goes, and waiting to "feel like it" is a losing strategy. The reliable approach is **systems and discipline**: habits, schedules, and routines that carry you when motivation dips. Successful people aren't constantly inspired; they've built structures so they act regardless of how they feel that day.

## Card: Fuel that lasts
Some motivation fuels last better. **Intrinsic motivation** — pursuing a goal because it's meaningful or enjoyable to you — sustains far longer than external pressure. Connecting your goal to a deeper "why" helps. So does **accountability** (a friend, group, or public commitment) and celebrating small wins, which keep the drive topped up along the way.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: waiting to feel motivated before acting; relying on willpower alone instead of building systems; pursuing goals for purely external reasons that don't sustain you; and ignoring small wins, so the long middle feels joyless. Build structures, connect to your "why," and celebrate progress to keep going when motivation dips.

## Card: Quick recap
Motivation naturally rises and falls, so don't depend on it — build systems and discipline that carry you when it dips. Fuel lasting drive with intrinsic motivation (your deeper "why"), accountability, and celebrating small wins. Goals are won not by those who feel most inspired, but by those who keep going anyway.
