## Card: What it is
image: marketingMeasuringHero
**Measuring marketing** means tracking whether your marketing efforts actually achieve their goals — so you can do more of what works and stop what doesn't. Without measurement, marketing is guesswork. Understanding the basics of measuring results helps you spend your time and money wisely and improve over time, rather than flying blind.

## Card: Why it matters
A famous saying goes, "half my advertising is wasted, but I don't know which half." Measurement solves this — it tells you what's actually working so you can invest in it and cut what isn't. Understanding how to measure marketing helps you avoid wasting effort and money, and continuously improve. It turns marketing from guesswork into something you can refine and optimize.

## Card: Key terms
- **Metrics**: numbers that track marketing performance.
- **Goals**: what you're actually trying to achieve.
- **Conversion**: someone taking the action you want (e.g., buying).
- **Actionable metrics**: numbers that genuinely inform decisions.

## Card: Measure against goals
image: marketingMeasuringMeasureAgainstGoalsHero
Effective measurement starts with clear **goals** — what is this marketing actually meant to achieve (awareness, sign-ups, sales)? Then track **metrics** that reflect those goals. A key one is **conversion**: how many people take the action you want (buy, sign up, contact you). Measuring against goals tells you what's working. Without clear goals, you can't judge success — you'd just be collecting numbers without knowing if they matter.

## Card: Useful vs. vanity metrics
A crucial distinction: focus on **actionable metrics** that genuinely inform decisions, not "vanity metrics" that look impressive but don't reflect real results. Lots of views or likes mean little if they don't lead to your actual goals (like customers). Ask: does this metric help me decide what to do? Track what truly matters — conversions, results, return on spending — and use it to improve: double down on what works, fix or drop what doesn't. Measurement only helps if you *act* on it.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: not measuring at all (flying blind); tracking vanity metrics (impressive-looking numbers that don't reflect real results) instead of actionable ones; measuring without clear goals (so you can't judge success); and collecting data but never acting on it. Effective measurement means tracking meaningful metrics against clear goals — and using the insights to do more of what works.

## Card: Quick recap
Measuring marketing means tracking whether your efforts achieve their goals, so you can do more of what works and cut what doesn't — turning guesswork into improvement. Start with clear goals, then track metrics that reflect them (especially conversions — people taking the action you want). Focus on actionable metrics, not vanity numbers that look good but don't reflect results. And crucially, act on what you learn.
