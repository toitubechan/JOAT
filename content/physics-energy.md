## Card: What it is
image: energyHero
**Energy** is the capacity to make things happen — to do work, move, heat, or change. It comes in many forms (motion, heat, light, electrical, chemical) and constantly transforms from one to another. Energy is a kind of universal currency: everything that happens involves energy changing form.

## Card: Why it matters
Energy underlies all activity, from your body moving to your phone charging to the Sun shining. Understanding it ties together huge swaths of science and everyday life. The key principle — that energy is never created or destroyed, only transformed — is one of the most powerful and far-reaching ideas in all of physics.

## Card: Key terms
- **Energy**: the capacity to do work or cause change.
- **Forms of energy**: motion, heat, light, electrical, chemical, and more.
- **Energy transformation**: energy changing from one form to another.
- **Conservation of energy**: energy is never created or destroyed.

## Card: Energy comes in many forms
Energy takes many **forms**: a moving object has *kinetic* energy; a stretched spring or raised weight has stored *potential* energy; there's also *heat*, *light*, *electrical*, *chemical* (in food and fuel), and more. These are all the same fundamental thing — energy — just appearing in different guises. Recognizing the forms is the first step to seeing energy everywhere.

## Card: Energy transforms but is conserved
image: energyConservation
The big principle: **energy is never created or destroyed — only transformed** from one form to another (conservation of energy). Eating food gives chemical energy your body turns into motion and heat; a phone turns electrical energy into light and sound. Everything that happens is energy changing form. Tracking these transformations explains a huge amount of how the world works.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking energy gets "used up" and vanishes (it transforms, often into less useful heat); confusing energy with force (energy is the capacity for change; force is a push or pull); not seeing that food, fuel, and motion are all energy in different forms; and imagining energy can be created from nothing (it can't).

## Card: Quick recap
Energy is the capacity to make things happen, appearing in many forms — motion, heat, light, electrical, chemical. Its key principle is conservation: energy is never created or destroyed, only transformed from one form to another. Everything that happens is energy changing form, which makes it a universal currency for understanding the world.
