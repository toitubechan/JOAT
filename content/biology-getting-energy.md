## Card: What it is
image: lifeEnergyHero
Every living thing needs **energy** to stay alive — to grow, move, and run its processes. Where that energy comes from, and how it flows through life, is one of biology's big stories. Almost all of it traces back to a single source: the **Sun**.

## Card: Why it matters
Understanding how life gets energy connects everything from a leaf to your lunch. It explains why plants need light, why we eat, and how energy flows through the natural world. It's a unifying thread linking the Sun, plants, animals, and you into one big system of energy transfer.

## Card: Key terms
- **Photosynthesis**: how plants make food from sunlight.
- **Respiration**: how cells release energy from food.
- **Producer**: an organism that makes its own food (plants).
- **Consumer**: an organism that eats others for energy.

## Card: Plants capture sunlight
image: photosynthesisHero
Plants are **producers** — through **photosynthesis**, they capture the Sun's energy and use it to make their own food (sugar) from water and carbon dioxide, releasing oxygen. This is the foundation of almost all life: plants convert sunlight into stored chemical energy that the rest of the living world ultimately depends on.

## Card: Energy flows to everything else
**Consumers** (animals, including us) can't make their own food, so they get energy by *eating* — plants, or other animals that ate plants. Inside cells, **respiration** releases the stored energy from food to power life. So energy flows: Sun → plants → animals. Trace your dinner back far enough, and its energy came from sunlight captured by a plant.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking plants get their food from soil (they make it from sunlight, water, and air — soil mainly provides minerals and support); not realizing nearly all life's energy traces to the Sun; and confusing photosynthesis (plants making food) with respiration (all cells releasing energy from food). The Sun powers it all.

## Card: Quick recap
All living things need energy, and nearly all of it traces to the Sun. Plants (producers) capture sunlight via photosynthesis to make food, releasing oxygen. Animals (consumers) get energy by eating, and cells release it through respiration. Energy flows from Sun to plants to animals — even your dinner's energy began as sunlight.
