## Card: What it is
image: electricianBoundaryHero
With electricity, the line between DIY and professional work is much stricter than with most home jobs. A few simple tasks are safe; almost everything involving wiring is not. Knowing exactly where that line sits isn't being overcautious — with electricity, it's how you stay alive.

## Card: Why it matters
Electrical mistakes don't give second chances the way a leaky tap does — they can kill instantly or start a fire that appears later. In many places, electrical work is legally restricted to qualified professionals for this reason. Respecting the line protects your life, your home, and often your insurance cover.

## Card: Key terms
- **Qualified electrician**: a certified professional, often legally required.
- **Wiring**: the in-wall cables carrying electricity.
- **Live wire**: a wire currently carrying electricity — dangerous to touch.
- **Isolate**: to fully cut power before any work.

## Card: Safe to do yourself
The safe list is short and simple: plugging and unplugging devices, resetting a tripped breaker, replacing a plug-in bulb (with the switch off), and using extension leads correctly. These don't involve touching **wiring**. If a task means opening up anything wired into the wall, it's no longer a beginner job.

## Card: Always call a professional
image: qualifiedElectricianBoundary
Get a **qualified electrician** for any **wiring** work, installing or moving sockets and switches, anything behind the walls, a fuse box that trips repeatedly, burning smells, sparking, or after any water contact with electrics. These risk electrocution and fire, and are frequently illegal to do yourself.

## Card: Common mistakes
The deadliest mistake is attempting wiring work to save money — the risk dwarfs any saving. Another is assuming a switch being off means a wire is safe; only fully **isolating** the power makes it safe, and even then, wiring needs expertise. And some hire unqualified "handymen" for work that legally requires a certified electrician.

## Card: Quick recap
The electrical DIY list is tiny: plug in devices, reset breakers, change bulbs, use leads properly. Everything involving wiring, sockets, the fuse box, or anything behind the walls needs a qualified electrician. With electricity there's no room for guessing — when in doubt, always call a pro.
