## Card: What it is
image: lawEmploymentRightsHero
**Employment rights** are the legal protections workers have in their jobs — covering things like pay, working hours, safety, fair treatment, and protection from unfair dismissal. They vary by country, but most have laws protecting workers. Knowing your basic rights helps you ensure you're treated fairly. This is general educational knowledge; specifics vary by location, and for real issues consult a professional or relevant authority.

## Card: Why it matters
Most people work, and not knowing your rights can leave you accepting unfair treatment, underpayment, or unsafe conditions you could challenge. Understanding that workers have legal protections — and the general categories — empowers you to stand up for fair treatment and recognize when something's wrong. It's directly relevant to your livelihood and wellbeing.

## Card: Key terms
- **Employment rights**: legal protections for workers.
- **Minimum standards**: legal floors for pay, hours, and conditions.
- **Discrimination**: unfair treatment based on protected characteristics.
- **Unfair dismissal**: being fired in a way the law prohibits.

## Card: Common worker protections
image: lawEmploymentRightsCommonWorkerProtectionsHero
While they vary by country, common employment protections include: **minimum standards** for pay (like a minimum wage) and working hours; rights to safe working conditions; protection from **discrimination** (unfair treatment based on things like race, gender, age, or disability); entitlements like rest breaks and sometimes paid leave; and protection from **unfair dismissal** (being fired without proper grounds or process). These set a legal floor below which employers generally can't go.

## Card: Knowing and using your rights
The key is awareness: many workers don't know their rights and so don't realize when they're being treated unlawfully. If you understand your basic protections, you can recognize problems, raise them appropriately, and seek help if needed (from your employer, a union, or relevant authorities). Keep records of issues. Since rights vary by location and situation, learn the specifics for where you work, and seek qualified help for serious problems.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: not knowing you have employment rights (so accepting unfair treatment); assuming rights are the same everywhere (they vary by country); not keeping records of workplace issues; and not seeking help when treated unlawfully. The general lesson: workers have legal protections (pay, safety, fair treatment, dismissal), so learn yours, recognize problems, and seek appropriate help for serious issues.

## Card: Quick recap
Employment rights are legal protections for workers — commonly including minimum pay and hours standards, safe conditions, protection from discrimination, rest and leave entitlements, and protection from unfair dismissal — though they vary by country. Knowing your basic rights lets you recognize unfair treatment and seek help. Keep records, learn your local specifics, and consult a professional or relevant authority for serious issues.
