## Card: What it is
image: interviewPrepHero
Interview **preparation** is the work you do beforehand — researching the company, anticipating questions, and rehearsing answers. It's the single biggest factor you control in how well an interview goes. Well-prepared candidates come across as confident and genuinely interested; unprepared ones rarely fake it convincingly.

## Card: Why it matters
Most interview success is decided before you walk in. Preparation reduces anxiety (you know what's coming), helps you give strong answers, and signals real interest in the role. It's the clearest example of effort paying off: the prepared candidate beats the more talented but unprepared one again and again.

## Card: Key terms
- **Company research**: learning about the employer and role.
- **Anticipating questions**: predicting what you'll be asked.
- **STAR method**: a structure for answering with examples.
- **Practice**: rehearsing answers out loud.

## Card: Research and anticipate
image: researchPracticeHero
Do your **company research**: understand what they do, their values, and the role's requirements. This lets you tailor answers and ask smart questions. Then **anticipate questions** — most interviews cover predictable ground (your experience, strengths, why this role). Prepare strong answers in advance for the likely ones, so you're never caught flat.

## Card: Prepare examples and practice
Have specific **examples** ready — stories that prove your skills. The **STAR method** structures them well: Situation, Task, Action, Result. Rather than claiming "I'm a problem-solver," tell a brief story showing it. Then **practice** out loud (not just in your head), ideally with someone. Rehearsal makes answers flow naturally instead of rambling under pressure.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: doing little or no company research (it shows); not anticipating obvious questions, then fumbling them; having no concrete examples, so answers stay vague; only rehearsing in your head; and preparing answers so rigidly they sound robotic. Prepare well, but stay natural and conversational.

## Card: Quick recap
Interview success is largely decided by preparation: research the company, anticipate the predictable questions, ready specific examples (structured with STAR — Situation, Task, Action, Result), and practice answers out loud. This builds confidence, sharpens your answers, and shows genuine interest. The prepared candidate consistently wins.
