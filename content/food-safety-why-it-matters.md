## Card: What it is
image: foodSafetyHero
**Food safety** is handling, cooking, and storing food so it doesn't make you ill. Most food poisoning comes from invisible **bacteria** that grow on food when it's handled or stored wrongly. The good news: a few simple habits prevent the vast majority of foodborne illness.

## Card: Why it matters
Food poisoning is common, unpleasant, and sometimes dangerous — especially for young children, the elderly, pregnant people, and those who are ill. Yet it's largely preventable. Understanding the basics protects you and anyone you cook for, and the rules are simple once you know the few things that actually matter.

## Card: Key terms
- **Bacteria**: tiny organisms that can multiply on food and cause illness.
- **Food poisoning**: illness from eating contaminated food.
- **Danger zone**: the temperature range where bacteria grow fastest.
- **Cross-contamination**: spreading germs from one food to another.

## Card: The invisible enemy
Most food poisoning comes from **bacteria** you can't see, smell, or taste. They naturally exist on many raw foods (especially raw meat, poultry, eggs, and seafood) and multiply rapidly in the right conditions — warmth, moisture, and time. Food can look and smell perfectly fine while carrying enough bacteria to make you ill.

## Card: The four pillars
image: cleanSeparateCookChill
Food safety boils down to four simple habits, often remembered as **Clean, Separate, Cook, Chill**: keep hands and surfaces *clean*; *separate* raw and ready-to-eat foods; *cook* food hot enough to kill bacteria; and *chill* food promptly to stop bacteria growing. Master these four and you've covered most of food safety.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: trusting that food is safe because it looks and smells fine (bacteria are invisible); leaving food out at room temperature too long; not washing hands and surfaces between handling raw meat and other foods; and not cooking risky foods thoroughly. Each is easy to avoid once you know it matters.

## Card: Quick recap
Food safety is about preventing illness from invisible bacteria that grow when food is mishandled. Food can look fine yet be unsafe. Remember the four pillars — Clean, Separate, Cook, Chill — and you'll prevent most food poisoning. Simple habits protect you and everyone you cook for.
