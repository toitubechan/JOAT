## Card: What it is
image: balancedEatingHero
**Balanced eating** means combining the right mix of foods in sensible amounts — enough variety to cover your nutrients, in **portions** that match your needs. It's not a strict diet but a flexible, sustainable way of eating that most people can follow for life without counting every bite.

## Card: Why it matters
Extreme diets often fail because they're miserable and unsustainable. Balance lasts because it allows everything in reasonable amounts. Getting the proportions and portions roughly right matters far more than perfection at any single meal — and it's how healthy eating becomes a lifelong habit rather than a temporary punishment.

## Card: Key terms
- **Balanced plate**: a meal with sensible proportions of food groups.
- **Portion**: how much of a food you eat at once.
- **Whole foods**: minimally processed foods close to their natural state.
- **Moderation**: enjoying treats in reasonable amounts.

## Card: The balanced plate
image: balancedPlateHero
A simple visual: fill about half your plate with **vegetables and fruit**, a quarter with **protein** (meat, fish, beans, eggs), and a quarter with **carbohydrates** (ideally whole grains), plus a little healthy fat. You don't need to weigh anything — this rough proportion guides most meals toward balance.

## Card: Portions and moderation
**Portion** size matters as much as food choice — even healthy foods add up. A handy guide: use your hand (a palm of protein, a fist of veg, a cupped hand of carbs). And **moderation** means no food is forbidden; treats fit fine in reasonable amounts. Sustainable beats strict every time.

## Card: Common mistakes
A frequent mistake is huge portions of even healthy food, assuming "healthy" means unlimited. Another is all-or-nothing thinking — one "bad" meal ruins the day, so people give up. And many over-rely on heavily processed foods; leaning toward **whole foods** most of the time makes balance much easier.

## Card: Quick recap
Balanced eating is variety in sensible proportions: roughly half the plate veg and fruit, a quarter protein, a quarter whole-grain carbs, plus some healthy fat. Watch portions, lean toward whole foods, and allow treats in moderation. Aim for mostly-right and sustainable, not perfect.
