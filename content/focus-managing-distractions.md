## Card: What it is
image: growthFocusManagingDistractionsHero
**Managing distractions** means deliberately reducing the things that pull your attention away, rather than relying on willpower to resist them in the moment. The most effective approach is changing your *environment* so distractions don't reach you in the first place.

## Card: Why it matters
Willpower is unreliable and tiring — every time you resist a tempting notification, you spend mental energy. Designing distraction out of your environment removes the battle entirely. This is far more effective than trying to "be more disciplined," and it's the practical key to actually focusing in a distracting world.

## Card: Key terms
- **Distraction**: anything pulling attention from your task.
- **Environment design**: arranging surroundings to support focus.
- **Friction**: making distractions harder to access.
- **Notification**: an alert that interrupts and pulls attention.

## Card: Design beats willpower
image: growthFocusManagingDistractionsDesignBeatsWillpowerHero
Don't rely on resisting distractions — *remove* them. **Environment design**: put your phone in another room, close unrelated tabs, silence **notifications**, and use full-screen or focus modes. Each removed temptation is one you don't have to fight. The goal is making distraction require effort and focus require none — the reverse of the default.

## Card: Add friction to distractions
The flip side of making good habits easy: add **friction** to distractions. Log out of social media, remove apps from your home screen, keep the phone out of reach. Even small obstacles — a few extra taps — interrupt the automatic reach for distraction and give your intention a chance to win. Make the distraction the harder option.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: keeping the phone on the desk and trying to resist it (willpower loses); leaving notifications on while working; having many tabs and chats open; and blaming yourself for getting distracted rather than fixing the environment. Most focus problems are environment problems in disguise — fix the setup, not your character.

## Card: Quick recap
Manage distractions by designing your environment, not relying on willpower. Remove temptations — phone away, notifications off, unrelated tabs closed — and add friction so distractions take effort to reach. Make focus the easy default and distraction the hard option. Most focus problems are really environment problems.
