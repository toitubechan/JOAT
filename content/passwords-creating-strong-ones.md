## Card: What it is
image: strongPasswordHero
A **strong password** is one that's hard for both humans and computers to guess or crack. The good news: strong doesn't have to mean impossible to remember. A few simple principles let you create passwords that are tough for attackers but manageable for you.

## Card: Why it matters
Attackers use programs that guess billions of passwords per second, so weak ones fall instantly. The difference between a weak and strong password can be the difference between safe and hacked. Knowing what actually makes a password strong helps you protect your accounts without resorting to gibberish you'll forget.

## Card: Key terms
- **Length**: the number of characters — the biggest strength factor.
- **Passphrase**: several random words combined into one password.
- **Complexity**: mixing letters, numbers, and symbols.
- **Predictable**: easy-to-guess patterns attackers expect.

## Card: Length beats complexity
image: longPasswordStrength
The single biggest factor is **length**. A long password is exponentially harder to crack than a short one, even a "complex" short one. This is why a **passphrase** — several random words like `purple-tractor-window-coffee` — can be both very strong *and* easier to remember than something like `P@ss1!`.

## Card: Avoid the predictable
Attackers know the common tricks, so avoid the **predictable**: real words alone, names, birthdays, "password", "123456", or simple substitutions like "p@ssw0rd". Anything personal (pet's name, favorite team) can often be found on social media. Randomness and length, not clever-looking but common patterns, are what make a password strong.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common errors: short passwords padded with one symbol to feel "complex"; using personal info that's easy to discover; tiny variations across sites (`Netflix1`, `Netflix2`); and believing a hard-to-type password is automatically strong. Length and unpredictability matter far more than awkward symbols.

## Card: Quick recap
Strong passwords are mainly *long* and *unpredictable* — a passphrase of several random words beats a short complex one and is easier to remember. Avoid names, dates, common words, and predictable patterns. Make each important account's password unique. Length plus randomness is the real recipe.
