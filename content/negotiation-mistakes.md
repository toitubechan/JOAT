## Card: What it is
image: negotiationMistakesHero
Negotiations are often lost not to a tougher opponent but to your own avoidable mistakes. This card set gathers the common ones — so you can sidestep them and negotiate from a stronger position. Most are about mindset and preparation, not clever tactics.

## Card: Why it matters
A single negotiation mistake can cost you money, terms, or the deal itself. Since these errors are predictable and avoidable, knowing them is a quick way to improve your outcomes. Many people repeat the same handful of mistakes their whole lives without realizing it.

## Card: Key terms
- **Anchoring**: how the first number shapes the range.
- **Talking too much**: revealing more than you should.
- **Accepting too fast**: agreeing before exploring options.
- **Emotional reactivity**: letting feelings drive decisions.

## Card: Mindset and preparation errors
The biggest mistakes start before you speak: not negotiating at all; arriving unprepared with no target, walk-away point, or research; and not knowing your alternatives (BATNA), which leaves you desperate. Then, **accepting too fast** — saying yes before exploring whether better terms are possible. Often, simply asking "is there any flexibility?" yields more.

## Card: In-the-moment errors
image: inMomentErrorsHero
During negotiation: **talking too much** and revealing your limits or eagerness; letting **emotional reactivity** (anger, anxiety, desperation) drive decisions; making the first concession too quickly; and getting anchored by the other side's opening number without pushing back. Staying calm, listening more than you talk, and pausing before responding all strengthen your position.

## Card: Common mistakes
To gather them: not negotiating; poor preparation; accepting the first offer; talking too much and over-revealing; reacting emotionally; making fast unilateral concessions; and treating it as a hostile win-lose fight. The fixes are consistent: prepare, stay calm, listen, ask for more, and know your walk-away point.

## Card: Quick recap
Negotiations are usually lost to avoidable mistakes: not negotiating, poor preparation, accepting too fast, talking too much, and reacting emotionally. The fixes are simple and consistent — prepare thoroughly, know your walk-away point, stay calm, listen more than you speak, and don't accept the first offer without asking for more.
