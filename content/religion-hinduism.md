## Card: What it is
image: religionHinduismHero
**Hinduism** is one of the world's oldest religions and one of its most diverse — not a single creed with one founder, but a rich family of traditions, beliefs, and practices originating in the Indian subcontinent. It has hundreds of millions of followers. This is a respectful, general overview — descriptive, not promoting or judging.

## Card: Why it matters
Hinduism is one of the largest and oldest religions, deeply shaping the culture and life of much of South Asia and beyond. Many of its concepts (like karma and yoga) have influenced the wider world. Understanding its basics builds cultural literacy and appreciation of a tradition very different in structure from Western religions — broadening your view of belief.

## Card: Key terms
- **Hinduism**: a diverse family of traditions originating in India.
- **Karma**: the idea that actions have consequences.
- **Reincarnation**: the belief in rebirth across lifetimes.
- **Diversity**: the wide range of beliefs and practices within Hinduism.

## Card: A diverse tradition
image: religionHinduismADiverseTraditionHero
A key feature: Hinduism is remarkably **diverse**. Unlike religions with a single founder and creed, it's a vast family of traditions developed over thousands of years — with varied beliefs, deities, texts, and practices. Some Hindus focus on one God, some on many forms of the divine, some on a single underlying divine reality. This diversity is part of its character, making Hinduism hard to summarize as one fixed doctrine — and important to approach without over-simplifying.

## Card: Common concepts
Despite the diversity, some concepts are widely shared. **Karma** is the idea that actions have consequences, shaping one's life and future. **Reincarnation** is the belief in rebirth — the soul being reborn into new lives. Many Hindus seek spiritual liberation from this cycle. Practices include worship, festivals, meditation, and yoga (which originated in this tradition). These ideas have influenced spirituality far beyond Hinduism itself.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: treating Hinduism as a single uniform creed (it's a diverse family of traditions); assuming all Hindus believe and practice identically; thinking it has one founder or text like some religions (it doesn't); and stripping concepts like karma or yoga from their context. Hinduism is an ancient, richly diverse tradition best understood in its variety, not over-simplified.

## Card: Quick recap
Hinduism is one of the world's oldest and most diverse religions — a rich family of traditions from the Indian subcontinent, with no single founder or creed. Widely shared concepts include karma (actions have consequences) and reincarnation (rebirth across lifetimes), with many seeking spiritual liberation. Practices include worship, festivals, and yoga. Its diversity is central, so it's best understood in its variety, respectfully and without over-simplifying.
