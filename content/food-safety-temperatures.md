## Card: What it is
image: temperatureSafetyHero
Temperature is the single biggest factor in food safety. Bacteria thrive in a warm middle range and are killed by proper cooking or kept in check by chilling. Knowing the basics of hot and cold — cooking thoroughly and chilling promptly — prevents most food poisoning.

## Card: Why it matters
Get temperature right and bacteria can't multiply to dangerous levels or survive cooking. Get it wrong — undercooked meat, or food left warm too long — and bacteria multiply fast. This one factor, more than any other, decides whether food is safe. It's worth understanding clearly.

## Card: Key terms
- **Danger zone**: the warm range where bacteria multiply rapidly.
- **Core temperature**: the heat at the thickest center of food.
- **Thorough cooking**: heating food hot enough throughout to kill bacteria.
- **Prompt chilling**: cooling food quickly to slow bacteria.

## Card: The danger zone
image: dangerZone
Bacteria multiply fastest in the **danger zone** — roughly between fridge-cold and properly hot (about 5°C to 60°C / 40°F to 140°F). The goal is to keep food *out* of this range: hot food hot, cold food cold, and not left sitting in between. The longer food lingers in the danger zone, the riskier it becomes.

## Card: Cook hot, chill fast
**Thorough cooking** kills bacteria — especially important for higher-risk foods like poultry, minced meat, and seafood, which should be cooked through (a food thermometer checking **core temperature** is the reliable way). And **chill promptly**: refrigerate leftovers within a couple of hours, since food left out lets bacteria multiply.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: leaving cooked food or leftovers out for hours before refrigerating; undercooking risky foods like chicken and mince; judging "done" by color alone rather than checking it's piping hot throughout; and putting too much hot food in the fridge at once in a way that keeps it warm too long. Hot food hot, cold food cold.

## Card: Quick recap
Temperature is the key to food safety: keep food out of the danger zone (roughly 5–60°C) where bacteria thrive. Cook risky foods like poultry and mince thoroughly to the core, and chill leftovers promptly, within a couple of hours. The rule is simple — hot food hot, cold food cold, nothing lingering in between.
