## Card: What it is
image: lightSoundHero
**Light** and **sound** are both **waves**, but very different kinds. Light lets us see; sound lets us hear. Both travel as waves carrying energy, yet they behave differently — understanding how explains everyday experiences from echoes to rainbows to why you see lightning before hearing thunder.

## Card: Why it matters
Light and sound shape how we experience the world — sight and hearing. Understanding them as waves explains countless everyday phenomena and corrects common confusions. It connects physics directly to your senses and daily life, from why the sky is blue to why distant events look and sound out of sync.

## Card: Key terms
- **Wave**: a travelling disturbance that carries energy.
- **Light**: an electromagnetic wave we can see.
- **Sound**: a wave of vibrating air (or other material).
- **Speed difference**: light travels far faster than sound.

## Card: Light: fast waves that need no medium
**Light** is a wave of electromagnetic energy. It travels incredibly fast — about 300,000 km per second — and, crucially, it can travel through the vacuum of empty space (which is how the Sun's light reaches us). White light is actually a mix of colors, which is why a prism or raindrops can split it into a rainbow. Light lets us see by bouncing off objects into our eyes.

## Card: Sound: slower waves that need a medium
image: soundNeedsMedium
**Sound** is a wave of vibrating particles — usually air. Unlike light, sound *needs* a material to travel through, so there's no sound in the vacuum of space. It's also far slower than light. This speed difference explains a familiar experience: you see **lightning** instantly but hear the **thunder** seconds later, because light reaches you almost immediately while sound lags behind.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking sound can travel through space (it can't — there's no air; space is silent); imagining light and sound travel at similar speeds (light is vastly faster); thinking white light is a single "color" (it's a mix); and not realizing both are waves carrying energy. The lightning-then-thunder gap is the everyday proof of their speed difference.

## Card: Quick recap
Light and sound are both energy-carrying waves, but different. Light is electromagnetic, extremely fast, and travels through empty space (white light is a mix of colors). Sound is vibrating particles, much slower, and needs a medium — so space is silent. Their speed difference is why you see lightning before hearing thunder.
