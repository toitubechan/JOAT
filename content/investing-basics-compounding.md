## Card: What it is
image: financeInvestingCompoundingHero
**Compounding** is when your investment earns returns, and then those returns earn returns too. Instead of growing in a straight line, your money grows on an ever-larger base — a snowball rolling downhill, gathering more snow the bigger it gets. Over long periods, this effect becomes remarkably powerful.

## Card: Why it matters
Compounding is the single biggest reason starting early beats starting big. A modest amount left to compound for decades can outgrow a much larger amount invested late. Understanding it changes behavior: it turns "I'll invest later when I earn more" into "even a little, started now, is worth a lot."

## Card: Key terms
- **Compounding**: earning returns on your previous returns.
- **Principal**: the original amount you invested.
- **Reinvesting**: putting returns back in rather than spending them.
- **Time horizon**: how long your money stays invested.

## Card: How the snowball works
image: financeInvestingCompoundingHowTheSnowballWorksHero
Say your money grows a certain percentage each year. Year one, you earn on your **principal**. Year two, you earn on the principal *plus* year one's gain. Year three, on all of that again. Each year's growth sits on a bigger base, so the gains get larger over time — slow at first, then accelerating.

## Card: Why time matters most
Compounding's magic is back-loaded: the biggest growth happens in the later years, once the snowball is large. That's why **time horizon** matters more than the starting amount. Ten years of compounding looks modest; thirty years can look astonishing. The earlier you start, the more of that powerful late-stage growth you capture.

## Card: Common mistakes
The first mistake is waiting to start, robbing your money of its most valuable ingredient — time. The second is **not reinvesting** returns, spending them instead, which breaks the snowball. And many underestimate it entirely, assuming small sums "aren't worth it," when small sums plus decades are exactly the point.

## Card: Quick recap
Compounding is growth earning growth — a snowball that accelerates over time. Its biggest gains come in later years, so starting early matters more than starting big. Reinvest returns to keep it rolling, and never dismiss small amounts: given enough time, they can grow into something large.
