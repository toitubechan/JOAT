## Card: What it is
image: medicinesBasicsHero
**Medicines** are substances that prevent, treat, or relieve illness and symptoms. They work by interacting with your body's processes — easing pain, fighting infection, or correcting an imbalance. They range from everyday painkillers to prescription drugs. This is general knowledge; a doctor or pharmacist guides what's right for you.

## Card: Why it matters
Medicines are powerful tools that can hugely improve and even save lives — but only when used correctly. Understanding the basics helps you use them safely, ask good questions, and avoid common errors. It also helps you tell sensible health information from misleading claims.

## Card: Key terms
- **Medicine / drug**: a substance used to treat or prevent illness.
- **Over-the-counter (OTC)**: medicine you can buy without a prescription.
- **Prescription**: medicine a doctor must authorize.
- **Active ingredient**: the part that actually does the work.

## Card: How medicines work
image: medicineTargetPath
Most medicines contain an **active ingredient** that interacts with your body — blocking pain signals, killing germs, reducing inflammation, or replacing something the body lacks. The rest of a pill is often just filler and coating. Different medicines target different processes, which is why one drug helps one problem but not another.

## Card: Prescription vs. over-the-counter
**Over-the-counter (OTC)** medicines (like common painkillers) are considered safe enough to buy without a doctor, for minor issues. **Prescription** medicines require a doctor's authorization because they're stronger, riskier, or need monitoring. The line exists for safety — prescription drugs can be harmful without proper guidance.

## Card: Common mistakes
A common mistake is assuming OTC means "completely harmless" — even everyday medicines can harm if misused or overdosed. Another is taking medicine prescribed for someone else, which can be dangerous. And many don't realize that "natural" remedies are still substances that can have effects and interactions.

## Card: Quick recap
Medicines prevent, treat, or relieve illness via an active ingredient that interacts with the body. OTC medicines are sold without prescription for minor issues; prescription ones need a doctor for safety. Even common medicines can harm if misused. Always follow professional guidance and never take another person's prescription.
