## Card: What it is
image: contractPartsHero
Contracts can be long and dense, but a few **key parts** carry most of what affects you: what each side must do, the money involved, the duration, and how to get out. Knowing where to focus lets you understand a contract's essentials without needing a law degree. (General guidance, not legal advice.)

## Card: Why it matters
Most people either don't read contracts or get lost in the dense whole. Knowing the key sections to focus on makes contracts manageable — you can grasp the important parts quickly and spot anything concerning. It turns an intimidating document into a few understandable essentials.

## Card: Key terms
- **Obligations**: what each side must do.
- **Terms**: the conditions, including price and payment.
- **Duration**: how long the contract lasts.
- **Termination clause**: how the contract can be ended.

## Card: What to focus on
A few sections carry the weight. **Obligations**: what each side must actually do. **Terms**: the price, payment schedule, and any fees. **Duration**: how long it lasts and whether it auto-renews. And the **termination clause**: how you (or they) can end it, including notice periods and penalties. Reading these few parts gives you most of what you need to understand a contract.

## Card: Watch the exit and the money
image: exitMoneyTerms
Two areas deserve extra attention. The *money*: not just the headline price but all fees, charges, and what triggers them. And the *exit*: how to get out, what notice you must give, any penalties or lock-in periods, and whether it auto-renews (many contracts renew automatically unless you cancel in time). People are most often caught out by hidden fees and difficulty exiting — so check these especially.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: focusing on the headline price while ignoring fees and the termination terms; not checking the duration or whether it auto-renews; missing notice periods (so you're stuck another term); and being intimidated by the whole document instead of focusing on the key parts. Know where to look, and contracts become manageable.

## Card: Quick recap
The key contract parts are: obligations (what each side must do), terms (price, fees, payment), duration (length and auto-renewal), and the termination clause (how to exit, with notice and penalties). Pay special attention to the full money picture and how to get out. Focusing on these essentials makes any contract manageable.
