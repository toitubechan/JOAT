## Card: What it is
image: growthGoalsBigGoalsSmallStepsHero
Big goals are inspiring but daunting — and that's exactly why they often stall. The solution is breaking a big goal into **small steps**: a series of manageable actions that add up to the big outcome. Progress comes from the steps, not from staring at the summit.

## Card: Why it matters
A huge goal viewed all at once feels overwhelming, and overwhelm causes paralysis or quitting. Breaking it down makes it feel achievable, gives you clear next actions, and provides frequent wins that keep motivation alive. It's how big, intimidating ambitions actually get accomplished — one doable step at a time.

## Card: Key terms
- **Milestone**: a meaningful checkpoint on the way to a goal.
- **Next action**: the single concrete step you can take now.
- **Process goal**: a goal about the actions, not just the outcome.
- **Overwhelm**: feeling paralyzed by a task's size.

## Card: Break it down
image: growthGoalsBigGoalsSmallStepsBreakItDownHero
Take a big goal and split it into **milestones** (checkpoints) and then into a clear **next action** — the single concrete thing you can do right now. "Write a book" becomes "write 500 words today." The big goal sets the direction; the next action is what you actually do. Always know your next small step.

## Card: Focus on the process
A powerful shift is from **outcome goals** ("lose 10kg") to **process goals** ("exercise 3 times a week"). You can't directly control outcomes, but you *can* control your actions — and consistent actions produce the outcomes. Focusing on the process you control reduces anxiety and gives you something to succeed at every single day.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: staring at the huge goal and feeling overwhelmed into inaction; not breaking it into a clear next action; obsessing over the outcome you can't directly control while neglecting the process you can; and skipping milestones, so there are no wins until the distant end. Small steps and process focus keep big goals alive.

## Card: Quick recap
Big goals get achieved by breaking them into small steps — milestones and a clear next action you can take now. Shift focus from outcomes (which you can't directly control) to the process (which you can), giving you daily wins and less overwhelm. Always know your next small step toward the summit.
