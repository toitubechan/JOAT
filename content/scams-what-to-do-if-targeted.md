## Card: What it is
image: scamResponseHero
Knowing what to *do* — when a scam reaches you, and if you've already been caught — is as important as spotting one. Quick, calm action can stop a scam in its tracks or limit the damage afterward. This card set covers the right responses for both situations.

## Card: Why it matters
Even careful people get targeted, and anyone can have an off moment. Panicking or freezing makes things worse; knowing the steps lets you act fast to protect your money and accounts. And reporting scams helps protect others, since these crimes thrive on silence and embarrassment.

## Card: Key terms
- **Report**: alerting your bank, the platform, or authorities.
- **Freeze / block**: stopping a card or account quickly.
- **Verify independently**: checking via official contacts you find yourself.
- **Recovery scam**: a follow-up scam targeting previous victims.

## Card: When a scam reaches you
Don't engage. Don't click links, reply, or call numbers the message provides. Instead, **verify independently** — contact the company using a number from its official website or your card, not the message. Delete or report the message. If it claims to be someone you know, reach them through a known, trusted channel to confirm.

## Card: If you've already been caught
image: freezeChangeReport
Act fast and don't panic. If you shared card or bank details, contact your bank immediately to **freeze** cards or stop payments. Change passwords on any affected accounts (and anywhere you reused them), and enable two-factor authentication. **Report** it to the platform and relevant authorities. Speed limits the damage.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: staying silent out of embarrassment (scams happen to everyone — reporting helps); delaying contacting the bank, when minutes matter; not changing reused passwords elsewhere; and falling for a **recovery scam**, where fraudsters posing as "help" target you again, promising to recover lost money for a fee. Be wary of unsolicited "recovery" offers.

## Card: Quick recap
If a scam reaches you, don't engage — verify independently through official contacts and report it. If you've been caught, act fast: contact your bank to freeze cards, change affected and reused passwords, enable 2FA, and report it. Don't stay silent from embarrassment, and beware follow-up "recovery" scams.
