## Card: What it is
image: filmLanguageOfShotsHero
The **language of shots** is how filmmakers use different camera framings and angles to convey meaning, emotion, and story — beyond just what's being filmed. A close-up feels different from a wide shot; a low angle feels different from a high one. Understanding this "visual language" reveals how films communicate through *how* they show things, not just *what* they show.

## Card: Why it matters
Film is a visual language, and shot choices profoundly shape how we feel and what we understand — usually without us consciously noticing. Understanding the language of shots makes you a more perceptive viewer (you see *how* films manipulate emotion and meaning) and is essential for anyone making video. It reveals the deliberate craft behind every frame.

## Card: Key terms
- **Shot**: a single continuous piece of filmed footage.
- **Framing**: how much is shown (close-up to wide shot).
- **Angle**: the camera's height and position relative to the subject.
- **Visual storytelling**: conveying meaning through how things are shown.

## Card: Framing conveys meaning
image: filmLanguageOfShotsFramingConveysMeaningHero
**Framing** — how much of a subject the **shot** shows — carries meaning. A *close-up* (tight on a face) creates intimacy and emphasizes emotion; a *wide shot* (showing the whole scene) establishes setting and can make a subject feel small or isolated; medium shots sit between. By choosing framing, filmmakers guide your attention and feeling. The same moment feels completely different in close-up versus wide shot — that's the language of shots at work.

## Card: Angle and visual storytelling
**Angle** — where the camera is positioned — also shapes meaning. A *low angle* (looking up at a subject) can make them seem powerful or imposing; a *high angle* (looking down) can make them seem small, weak, or vulnerable; eye-level feels neutral. Through these choices, filmmakers practice **visual storytelling** — conveying emotion, power dynamics, and meaning through *how* they show things. Every shot is a deliberate choice that influences how you feel, usually below conscious awareness.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking the camera just neutrally records what happens (shot choices actively shape meaning and emotion); not noticing how framing and angle affect feeling; assuming "what" is filmed matters more than "how"; and missing that every shot is a deliberate choice. The language of shots — framing and angle — is how films communicate visually, guiding your emotions and understanding.

## Card: Quick recap
The language of shots is how filmmakers convey meaning through camera choices, not just what's filmed. Framing (close-up to wide) shapes feeling — close-ups create intimacy and emphasize emotion; wide shots establish setting or isolation. Angle shapes meaning too — low angles feel powerful, high angles feel vulnerable. Through these deliberate choices, films practice visual storytelling, guiding your emotions and understanding, usually below conscious awareness.
