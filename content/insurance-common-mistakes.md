## Card: What it is
image: financeInsuranceCommonMistakesHero
People waste money on insurance and get caught underprotected in the same predictable ways. This subtopic gathers the common mistakes — so you can get the protection you need without overpaying for the protection you don't. Insurance done well is quiet and cheap; done badly, it's costly either way.

## Card: Why it matters
Insurance mistakes are doubly painful: you can lose money on premiums for years *and* still be exposed when disaster hits. Spotting these errors helps you redirect money from pointless cover toward the protection that actually matters, and ensures a claim won't be denied when you finally need it.

## Card: Key terms
- **Under-insuring**: having too little cover for a real risk.
- **Over-insuring**: paying for cover you don't need.
- **Auto-renewal**: a policy renewing automatically, often at a higher price.
- **Loyalty penalty**: long-standing customers quietly charged more.

## Card: Too little, too much
image: financeInsuranceCommonMistakesTooLittleTooMuchHero
**Under-insuring** the big risks — health, income, your home's full rebuild cost — is the dangerous mistake, leaving a catastrophic gap. **Over-insuring** trivial, affordable items wastes premiums. The fix is the same principle as all insurance: cover what you couldn't afford to lose, and self-cover the small stuff.

## Card: The auto-renewal trap
Many policies **auto-renew** each year, often at a higher price than a new customer would pay — the so-called **loyalty penalty**. Loyalty is quietly punished, not rewarded. Shopping around or even just asking for a better rate at renewal can cut the cost significantly, sometimes for identical cover.

## Card: Common mistakes
Beyond those: buying on price alone and ending up with useless cover full of exclusions; not updating policies after big life changes (a new baby, a home renovation); and stacking duplicate cover you already get elsewhere, like travel insurance bundled with a bank account you forgot about.

## Card: Quick recap
The big insurance mistakes are under-insuring real catastrophes, over-insuring cheap items, and letting policies auto-renew at inflated prices. Cover what you can't afford to lose, shop around at renewal, update after life changes, and avoid duplicate cover. Smart insurance protects you without quietly draining you.
