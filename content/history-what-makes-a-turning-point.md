## Card: What it is
image: histTurningpointsWhatMakesHero
A **turning point** is a moment or development that dramatically changed the course of history — after which the world was never the same. From the invention of farming to the printing press to the internet, certain shifts reshaped how humans live. This card set explores what makes something a true turning point.

## Card: Why it matters
History can feel like an overwhelming jumble of names and dates. Focusing on turning points gives you a framework — the key shifts that explain how we got from prehistoric humans to today. Understanding these helps you grasp the big story of humanity and see patterns in how change happens, rather than memorizing disconnected facts.

## Card: Key terms
- **Turning point**: a development that dramatically changed history's course.
- **Era**: a distinct period of history.
- **Catalyst**: something that triggers major change.
- **Long-term impact**: effects that ripple across generations.

## Card: What makes a turning point
image: histTurningpointsWhatMakesWhatMakesATurningPointHero
A true **turning point** isn't just a dramatic event — it's one with vast **long-term impact** that reshaped how people lived, thought, or organized themselves. The invention of writing, agriculture, the printing press, the steam engine — each opened a new **era**. The test isn't how exciting it was at the time, but how profoundly and lastingly it changed the human story.

## Card: How change happens
Turning points often come from a few sources: new **technologies** (the wheel, printing, the internet), big **ideas** (democracy, scientific method, major religions), and major events (wars, revolutions, pandemics). Sometimes a single **catalyst** triggers cascading change. Recognizing these patterns helps you understand not just *what* happened in history, but *why* the world changes.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking history is just a list of dates rather than a story of pivotal shifts; assuming the most *dramatic* events were the most *important* (quiet developments like farming reshaped life more than many battles); and not seeing patterns in how change happens. Focusing on turning points reveals history's bigger meaning.

## Card: Quick recap
A turning point is a development with vast, lasting impact that reshaped how humans live or think — like writing, agriculture, the printing press, or the internet. They arise from new technologies, big ideas, and major events. Focusing on these pivotal shifts gives history a meaningful framework, revealing how and why the world changes.
