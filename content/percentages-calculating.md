## Card: What it is
image: dataPercentagesCalculatingHero
**Calculating percentages** — finding "X% of something" — is a practical skill you can do with simple methods, often in your head. There are easy tricks that make it far less daunting than it seems. This card set shows the straightforward ways to work out percentages in everyday situations.

## Card: Why it matters
Being able to quickly work out percentages helps with shopping discounts, tips, splitting bills, and judging figures in the news. It saves you from being caught out or overcharged, and builds number confidence. These are practical, everyday calculations worth being comfortable with.

## Card: Key terms
- **Of**: in percentages, "of" means multiply.
- **1% method**: find 1%, then scale up.
- **10% method**: find 10% by moving the decimal.
- **Estimation**: getting a close-enough answer quickly.

## Card: The easy building blocks
image: dataPercentagesCalculatingTheEasyBuildingBlocksHero
Two simple tricks unlock most percentages. **10%** is easy: just move the decimal point one place (10% of 250 is 25). **1%** is moving it two places (1% of 250 is 2.5). From these, you can build others: 20% is double 10%; 5% is half of 10%; 15% is 10% plus 5%. Breaking a percentage into easy chunks of 10% and 1% lets you calculate many in your head.

## Card: The flip trick and estimation
A handy trick: **X% of Y equals Y% of X**. So 8% of 50 is the same as 50% of 8 (= 4) — much easier! Swapping can turn a hard calculation into an easy one. And for everyday purposes, **estimation** is often enough — rounding to get a close answer (a roughly 20% tip on 38 ≈ 20% of 40 = 8). You don't always need exact precision.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking you always need a calculator (10% and 1% tricks handle a lot mentally); forgetting the flip trick that simplifies hard cases; aiming for exact precision when estimation would do; and being intimidated into not trying. With a couple of simple methods, most everyday percentage calculations are quite doable in your head.

## Card: Quick recap
Calculate percentages easily using building blocks: 10% (move the decimal one place) and 1% (two places), combined for others (20% = double 10%, 5% = half). Use the flip trick (X% of Y = Y% of X) to simplify hard cases, and estimate when exact precision isn't needed. A couple of simple methods make most everyday percentages mental math.
