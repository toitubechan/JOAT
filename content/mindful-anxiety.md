## Card: What it is
image: anxietyHero
**Anxiety** is a feeling of worry, fear, or unease — a normal human emotion that becomes a problem when it's excessive, persistent, or interferes with life. Understanding what anxiety is, why it happens, and how it works helps you relate to it more healthily. This is general educational and wellbeing knowledge, not medical advice; for persistent or severe anxiety, professional help is the right resource.

## Card: Why it matters
Anxiety is extremely common, and understanding it reduces fear and stigma, and helps people relate to it more healthily. Knowing that some anxiety is normal — and recognizing when it becomes a problem worth addressing — is valuable for everyone. Understanding how anxiety works also makes it less frightening and more manageable. It's important knowledge given how widespread anxiety is.

## Card: Key terms
- **Anxiety**: worry, fear, or unease.
- **Normal vs. problematic anxiety**: useful protective feeling vs. excessive, interfering anxiety.
- **Fight-or-flight**: the body's response behind anxious feelings.
- **The anxiety cycle**: how avoidance can worsen anxiety.

## Card: Anxiety is normal (to a point)
image: anxietyCurve
A key understanding: **anxiety** is a *normal* human emotion, not inherently a disorder. In appropriate amounts, it's protective — it's linked to the body's **fight-or-flight** response, which evolved to alert us to threats and prepare us to respond (the racing heart, alertness, and worry). The problem is **problematic anxiety** — when it becomes excessive, persistent, out of proportion to the situation, or interferes with daily life. Recognizing this distinction helps you know that *some* anxiety is normal and healthy, while persistent, life-interfering anxiety is worth addressing.

## Card: How anxiety can feed itself
image: anxietyCycle
Understanding **the anxiety cycle** helps demystify it. Anxiety often involves the mind overestimating threats and the body reacting as if to real danger. A key pattern: *avoidance* — avoiding what makes us anxious brings short-term relief but tends to *reinforce* and worsen anxiety over time (the fear grows, and we never learn the feared thing is manageable). Understanding this cycle (and that anxious feelings, while unpleasant, aren't dangerous in themselves) helps people relate to anxiety more healthily. For persistent or severe anxiety, effective help exists.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking all anxiety is abnormal or a disorder (some is normal and protective); not recognizing when anxiety has become problematic (excessive, persistent, interfering); believing avoidance helps (it often worsens anxiety over time); seeing anxiety as shameful (it's common and understandable); and not seeking help for persistent or severe anxiety (effective help exists). Understanding anxiety reduces fear and stigma and supports relating to it more healthily.

## Card: Quick recap
Anxiety — worry, fear, or unease — is a normal human emotion linked to the protective fight-or-flight response, becoming a problem only when excessive, persistent, or interfering with life. Understanding the anxiety cycle helps: the mind overestimates threats, and avoidance brings short-term relief but worsens anxiety over time. Recognizing this reduces fear and stigma. For persistent or severe anxiety, effective professional help exists and is the right resource.
