## Card: What it is
image: dataMentalmathEstimationHero
**Estimation** is getting a close-enough answer quickly, usually by **rounding** numbers to friendly values before calculating. It's often more useful than an exact answer in everyday life, and far faster. Estimation is arguably the most practical mental-math skill of all, because most real situations don't need precision.

## Card: Why it matters
In daily life — judging a bill, a discount, whether you have enough money, if a figure seems right — a quick estimate beats a slow exact calculation. Estimation also helps you sanity-check exact answers (catching errors) and judge whether statistics are plausible. It's fast, practical, and removes the pressure of needing perfect precision.

## Card: Key terms
- **Estimation**: a quick, approximate answer.
- **Rounding**: simplifying a number to a near, friendly value.
- **Ballpark**: roughly the right size of an answer.
- **Sanity check**: verifying an answer is plausible.

## Card: Round, then calculate
image: dataMentalmathEstimationRoundThenCalculateHero
The core of estimation is **rounding** before you calculate. To estimate 312 + 488, round to 300 + 500 = 800 (close enough). For 19 × 21, round to 20 × 20 = 400. For a 18% tip on €43, estimate 20% of €40 = €8. By rounding to friendly numbers first, you trade a little precision for a fast, easy answer that's usually good enough for the decision at hand.

## Card: Ballpark thinking and sanity checks
Estimation gives you a **ballpark** — roughly the right size — which is often all you need ("do I have enough for this?", "is this a good deal?"). It's also a powerful **sanity check**: after any exact calculation (yours or a calculator's), a quick estimate tells you if the answer is plausible. If you expected ~800 but got 80 or 8,000, you've caught an error. Estimation guards against big mistakes.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking you always need an exact answer (a ballpark usually suffices); not rounding to simplify before calculating; not using estimation to sanity-check exact results; and rounding so crudely the estimate is useless. Round sensibly, get the ballpark, and use it both to decide quickly and to catch errors.

## Card: Quick recap
Estimation means rounding numbers to friendly values for a quick, close-enough answer — usually all everyday situations need. Round first (312 + 488 ≈ 800; 19 × 21 ≈ 400), get the ballpark, and use it to decide fast and to sanity-check exact answers (catching big errors). It's the most practical mental-math skill, trading a little precision for speed.
