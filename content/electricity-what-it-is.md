## Card: What it is
image: electricityFlowHeroScience
**Electricity** is the flow of tiny charged particles — usually **electrons** — through a material. Those electrons exist in all matter, and when they move in a coordinated way, we get the electric current that powers our world. At its heart, electricity is just charge in motion.

## Card: Why it matters
Electricity powers nearly everything around us, yet most people find it mysterious. A basic grasp of what it actually *is* demystifies the technology you use daily and lays the foundation for understanding circuits, energy, and safety. It turns "magic from the wall" into something you can actually picture.

## Card: Key terms
- **Electron**: a tiny negatively charged particle.
- **Charge**: a property of matter that can be positive or negative.
- **Current**: the flow of electric charge.
- **Conductor**: a material that lets charge flow easily.

## Card: Charge and electrons
All matter is made of atoms, which contain **electrons** carrying a negative **charge**. Normally these are balanced, but they can move. When electrons flow through a material, that movement is electricity. Opposite charges attract and like charges repel — this push and pull of charge is the basic force behind all electrical phenomena.

## Card: Conductors and insulators
image: conductorsInsulators
Materials differ in how easily charge flows through them. **Conductors** (like metals) let electrons move freely, which is why wires are made of copper. **Insulators** (like rubber and plastic) resist the flow, which is why they coat wires for safety. This difference — conduct or insulate — is what lets us guide electricity where we want it and keep it away from us.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking electricity is a mysterious substance rather than moving charge; imagining electrons zip along wires at light speed (the *signal* moves fast, but individual electrons drift slowly); and not realizing why some materials conduct and others don't. Electricity is simply charged particles moving through conductors.

## Card: Quick recap
Electricity is the flow of tiny charged particles — usually electrons — through a material. All matter contains electrons carrying charge, and their coordinated movement is electric current. Conductors like metal let charge flow; insulators like rubber resist it. Picture charge in motion, and electricity stops being magic.
