## Card: What it is
image: weatherIngredientsHero
**Weather** is the state of the atmosphere at a place and time — temperature, wind, rain, sunshine. It's driven by a few basic ingredients: the **Sun** heating the Earth unevenly, **air** moving around, and **water** cycling through the atmosphere. Understanding these makes weather far less mysterious.

## Card: Why it matters
Weather affects daily life constantly — what to wear, travel, farming, safety. Understanding what drives it helps you make sense of forecasts and the sky above you. It also builds the foundation for understanding bigger topics like climate. The everyday becomes comprehensible once you know the basic engine behind it.

## Card: Key terms
- **Atmosphere**: the layer of air surrounding Earth.
- **Temperature**: how hot or cold the air is.
- **Air pressure**: the weight of air pressing down, which drives wind.
- **Water cycle**: water moving between sea, air, and land.

## Card: The Sun is the engine
Weather is ultimately powered by the **Sun**, which heats the Earth *unevenly* — more at the equator, less at the poles, more on land than sea, more by day than night. This uneven heating is the engine of all weather: it creates temperature differences that set air moving and water evaporating, driving everything else.

## Card: Air, pressure, and water
image: airPressureWaterCycle
Uneven heating creates differences in **air pressure**, and air flows from high to low pressure — that flow is **wind**. Meanwhile, the Sun evaporates water into the air, which later cools and falls as rain or snow (the **water cycle**). So weather is really the interplay of heat, moving air, and cycling water, all set in motion by the Sun.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking weather is random rather than driven by understandable physics; not realizing the Sun's *uneven* heating is the engine; confusing weather (right now, here) with climate (long-term patterns); and imagining wind comes from nowhere, when it's just air flowing between pressure differences. Weather follows logical, if complex, rules.

## Card: Quick recap
Weather is the atmosphere's state at a place and time, driven by the Sun heating Earth unevenly. That uneven heating creates temperature and air-pressure differences (which cause wind) and evaporates water (which falls as rain). Weather is the interplay of heat, moving air, and cycling water — not random, but driven by understandable physics.
