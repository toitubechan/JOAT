## Card: What it is
image: carDealHero
The final stage of buying a car involves **negotiating** the price, completing the **paperwork** correctly, and avoiding **scams**. Getting this right ensures you pay a fair price, the car is legally yours, and you don't get cheated. (General guidance for a major transaction.)

## Card: Why it matters
The closing stage is where you can save money through negotiation, where paperwork errors can cause legal trouble, and where scams catch the unwary — especially with private and online sales. Knowing how to handle it protects your money and ensures the purchase is legitimate and secure. It's the make-or-break final step.

## Card: Key terms
- **Negotiation**: agreeing a fair price.
- **Paperwork / title transfer**: legally transferring ownership.
- **Scam**: a fraudulent sale designed to cheat you.
- **Ownership documents**: proof the car is legally yours.

## Card: Negotiate the price
Car prices, especially used and dealer prices, are often **negotiable** — the asking price isn't always the final price. Research what the car is genuinely worth, point to any issues found during inspection, and be willing to walk away (your strongest tool). Don't be rushed or pressured. Even a modest negotiation can save a meaningful amount on a big purchase.

## Card: Paperwork and avoiding scams
image: paperworkSafety
Complete the **paperwork** properly: ensure ownership is legally transferred to you (title/registration), get a receipt, and verify the seller actually owns the car and it's not stolen or owing money. **Scams** to avoid: deals that seem too good to be true, pressure to pay before seeing the car, requests for unusual payment methods, and sellers who avoid meeting in person. For private sales, verify everything and be cautious.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: accepting the asking price without negotiating; rushing the paperwork or not properly transferring ownership; not verifying the seller owns the car (or that it's not stolen or financed); paying before seeing the car; and ignoring scam warning signs in private/online sales. Negotiate, complete paperwork correctly, verify everything, and never pay before seeing the car.

## Card: Quick recap
Seal the deal well: negotiate the price (it's often flexible, and walking away is your strongest tool), complete the paperwork to legally transfer ownership and get a receipt, and verify the seller genuinely owns the car. Avoid scams — deals too good to be true, pressure to pay early, or unusual payment requests. Verify everything, especially in private sales.
