## Card: What it is
image: privacyProtectionHero
Protecting your privacy online is a set of practical, mostly free steps that reduce how much of your data is collected and exposed. You can't be perfectly invisible, but you can dramatically cut your exposure with a few sensible habits and settings. Small actions add up to real protection.

## Card: Why it matters
Most people leak far more data than they need to, simply through default settings and habits. Taking a few deliberate steps shifts the balance back toward you — less tracking, fewer exposed details, lower risk in breaches. It's about reasonable control, not paranoia or going off-grid.

## Card: Key terms
- **Privacy settings**: controls limiting who sees your data.
- **Permissions**: what an app is allowed to access on your device.
- **VPN**: a tool that hides your connection's origin.
- **Data minimization**: sharing only what's necessary.

## Card: Settings and permissions
image: privacyPermissions
Two high-impact moves. Review the **privacy settings** on your accounts and devices — defaults usually favor sharing, so tightening them helps a lot. And check app **permissions**: many apps request access to your location, contacts, camera, or microphone they don't need. Grant only what's genuinely required, and revoke the rest.

## Card: Smart everyday habits
Practice **data minimization** — share only what's necessary; not every form needs your real birthday or phone number. Reject non-essential cookies, use strong unique passwords, and be cautious on public Wi-Fi (a **VPN** can help by hiding your connection). Think before granting access or filling fields, and you'll leak far less.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: leaving default privacy settings untouched (they favor data sharing); granting apps every permission they ask for; oversharing personal details on forms and profiles; and assuming privacy is all-or-nothing, so giving up entirely. Even a few changes meaningfully reduce your exposure — perfection isn't the goal.

## Card: Quick recap
Protect your privacy with practical steps: tighten privacy settings (defaults favor sharing), limit app permissions to what's needed, minimize the data you share, reject non-essential cookies, and use caution on public Wi-Fi. You can't be invisible, but a few habits dramatically cut your exposure. Aim for reasonable control.
