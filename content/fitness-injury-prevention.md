## Card: What it is
image: fitnessInjuryPreventionHero
**Injury prevention** is reducing the risk of getting hurt during exercise and sport — through sensible practices like warming up, progressing gradually, using good form, and resting. Many common injuries are preventable. Understanding how to avoid them helps you stay active consistently rather than being sidelined. This is general educational knowledge, not medical advice. (For injuries or concerns, consult a professional.)

## Card: Why it matters
Injuries are a major reason people stop exercising — they cause pain, setbacks, and discouragement, sometimes derailing fitness for months. Many are preventable with sensible habits. Understanding injury prevention helps you exercise safely and consistently over the long term, which matters far more for fitness than any intense short burst. Staying injury-free keeps you active.

## Card: Key terms
- **Injury prevention**: reducing the risk of getting hurt.
- **Warming up**: preparing the body before exercise.
- **Gradual progression**: increasing intensity slowly to avoid overload.
- **Form and recovery**: good technique and adequate rest.

## Card: Common causes and prevention
image: fitnessInjuryPreventionCommonCausesAndPreventionHero
Many exercise injuries come from a few preventable causes. *Doing too much too soon* — overloading the body before it's ready — is a top cause, prevented by **gradual progression** (increasing intensity, duration, or weight slowly). Skipping **warming up** (preparing muscles and joints before activity) raises risk. Poor **form** (incorrect technique) strains the body. And inadequate **recovery** (overtraining without rest) leads to overuse injuries. Addressing these — progress gradually, warm up, use good form, and rest — prevents many common injuries.

## Card: Consistency over intensity
A key principle for staying injury-free (and fit long-term): **consistency over intensity**. Pushing too hard, too fast — chasing rapid results — is a common path to injury that then sets you back further than going steadily would have. Sustainable, gradual progress keeps you exercising consistently over months and years, which produces far better fitness than intense bursts that lead to injury and breaks. Listen to your body, don't ignore pain, and prioritize steady consistency over reckless intensity. Staying healthy and active beats getting hurt and stopping.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: doing too much too soon (a top cause of injury — progress gradually); skipping warm-ups; using poor form; ignoring pain or warning signs; overtraining without adequate recovery; and chasing intensity over consistency. Most exercise injuries are preventable with sensible habits — gradual progression, warming up, good form, rest, and prioritizing steady consistency. (For injuries, consult a professional.)

## Card: Quick recap
Injury prevention keeps you active by reducing the risk of getting hurt — and many exercise injuries are preventable. Key practices: progress gradually (avoid doing too much too soon, a top cause), warm up, use good form, and allow adequate recovery. The guiding principle is consistency over intensity: steady, sustainable progress produces far better long-term fitness than intense bursts that lead to injury and breaks. Listen to your body and don't ignore pain.
