## Card: What it is
image: feedbackCommunicationHero
**Feedback** is information about how someone is doing, meant to help them improve or to reinforce what's working. Both giving and receiving it well are key workplace skills. Done badly, feedback creates defensiveness and hurt; done well, it drives growth and strengthens working relationships.

## Card: Why it matters
Feedback is how people and teams improve, yet most handle it awkwardly — givers are vague or harsh, receivers get defensive. Learning to do both well makes you more effective, more coachable (which managers value highly), and better at helping others. It's central to growing in any job.

## Card: Key terms
- **Constructive feedback**: input aimed at helping someone improve.
- **Specific**: pointing to concrete behavior, not vague judgment.
- **Defensiveness**: reacting to feedback as an attack.
- **Receiving well**: taking feedback openly rather than personally.

## Card: Giving feedback well
image: givingFeedbackHero
Good **constructive feedback** is **specific** and about behavior, not the person — "the report was missing the summary section" rather than "you're careless." Be timely, kind, and clear about what could be better and why. Balance is good too: acknowledge what's working, not only what isn't. The aim is to help, not to vent or judge.

## Card: Receiving feedback well
**Receiving well** is just as important. The instinct is **defensiveness** — feeling attacked — but feedback is a gift of information, even when clumsily delivered. Listen without interrupting, assume good intent, ask questions to understand, and separate the message from any awkward delivery. Being genuinely coachable is one of the most valued workplace qualities.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: giving vague feedback ("be better") with nothing actionable; making it personal and harsh; only ever giving negatives; and on the receiving side, getting defensive, taking it personally, or dismissing it. Both giving and receiving improve hugely when you focus on the behavior and the goal of improvement.

## Card: Quick recap
Feedback drives growth when handled well. Give it specifically and about behavior, not the person — timely, kind, and balanced. Receive it openly: resist defensiveness, treat it as useful information even if clumsily delivered, and stay coachable. Both skills make you more effective and are highly valued at work.
