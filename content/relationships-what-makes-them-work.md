## Card: What it is
image: psychRelationshipsWhatMakesThemWorkHero
Healthy **relationships** — friendships, family, romantic, or work — rest on a few common foundations: respect, trust, communication, and mutual care. While every relationship is unique, these basics underpin them all. Understanding what makes relationships *work* helps you build and maintain the connections that matter most.

## Card: Why it matters
Relationships are among the biggest sources of happiness — and stress — in life, and strong social connections are linked to better health and wellbeing. Yet we're rarely taught how relationships actually work. Understanding the foundations helps you build stronger bonds and recognize what a healthy relationship needs to thrive.

## Card: Key terms
- **Respect**: valuing the other person and their autonomy.
- **Trust**: confidence in each other's reliability and honesty.
- **Mutual effort**: both people contributing to the relationship.
- **Connection**: the sense of being understood and valued.

## Card: The common foundations
image: psychRelationshipsWhatMakesThemWorkTheCommonFoundationsHero
Across all relationship types, certain foundations recur: **respect** (valuing each other), **trust** (relying on each other's honesty and reliability), open **communication**, and genuine care. Add shared time and **mutual effort** — relationships need investment from *both* sides to thrive. When these are present, relationships tend to be strong; when they're missing, relationships struggle regardless of how it began.

## Card: Relationships need ongoing effort
A key truth: relationships aren't "set and forget." They need ongoing **mutual effort** — attention, communication, and care over time, not just at the start. Like a garden, they're maintained, not just planted. Strong relationships come from both people consistently showing up, listening, and investing. Neglect, even without conflict, slowly weakens a connection.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking a good relationship should be effortless (healthy ones still need work); expecting one person to do all the investing (it must be mutual); assuming connection maintains itself without attention; and focusing on grand gestures over consistent small care. Relationships thrive on steady, mutual effort and the core foundations of respect and trust.

## Card: Quick recap
Healthy relationships of all kinds rest on respect, trust, communication, and mutual care, sustained by ongoing effort from *both* people. They're maintained like a garden, not set-and-forget — neglect weakens them even without conflict. When the foundations are present and both invest consistently, relationships thrive; understanding this helps you build the connections that matter.
