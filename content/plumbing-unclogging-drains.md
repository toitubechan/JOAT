## Card: What it is
image: cloggedDrainHero
A **clogged drain** is one where water drains slowly or not at all, because something is blocking the pipe — usually hair, grease, soap buildup, or food scraps. Most clogs are near the surface and clear with simple tools, no plumber required. Knowing the right method avoids damage and frustration.

## Card: Why it matters
Blocked drains are one of the most common household annoyances, and a slow drain only gets worse. Clearing it yourself is quick and cheap. Knowing the safe methods also helps you avoid harsh chemical cleaners, which can damage pipes and are hazardous to handle and to the environment.

## Card: Key terms
- **Clog**: a blockage stopping water from draining.
- **Plunger**: a rubber cup tool that uses pressure to shift clogs.
- **Drain snake**: a flexible tool that hooks or pushes out blockages.
- **P-trap**: the curved pipe under a sink where clogs often gather.

## Card: Try these first
Start simple. For a sink, a **plunger** often shifts a clog with a few firm pushes (cover any overflow hole first for suction). Hot water can melt grease buildup. A **drain snake** or even a straightened wire can hook out hair clogs near the surface. These low-tech methods clear most everyday blockages.

## Card: The P-trap trick
image: pTrapCleanout
The curved **P-trap** under a sink is where gunk loves to collect. With a bucket underneath to catch water, you can often unscrew it by hand or with a wrench, clear the muck inside, and reattach it. It's messier but very effective, and reaches clogs a plunger can't.

## Card: Common mistakes
The biggest mistake is reaching for harsh chemical drain cleaners — they can corrode pipes, don't always work, and are dangerous if they splash. Another is plunging without blocking the overflow hole, which kills the suction. And many ignore prevention: a cheap drain strainer to catch hair and scraps stops most clogs forming.

## Card: Quick recap
Clear clogs with simple tools first: a plunger, hot water for grease, or a snake for hair. The P-trap under a sink can be opened and cleaned for stubborn blockages. Avoid harsh chemicals, and prevent clogs with a drain strainer. Most blockages never need a plumber.
