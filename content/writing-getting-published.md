## Card: What it is
image: gettingPublishedHero
**Getting published** means getting your writing out to readers — and today there are more paths than ever. The two broad routes are **traditional publishing** (through a publisher) and **self-publishing** (publishing it yourself). Understanding the options, and the realities of each, helps you navigate how to share your work. This is a general overview of the landscape.

## Card: Why it matters
Many writers dream of being published but find the process confusing or intimidating, or hold outdated assumptions. Understanding the real options — and that publishing has been transformed by self-publishing — empowers you to choose a realistic path for your goals. It demystifies how writing actually reaches readers and clarifies what each route involves.

## Card: Key terms
- **Traditional publishing**: a publisher produces and distributes your work.
- **Self-publishing**: you publish your own work directly.
- **Gatekeepers**: those who decide what traditional publishers accept.
- **Realistic expectations**: understanding the genuine odds and effort.

## Card: Two main routes
image: publishingRoutesHero
**Traditional publishing** means a publisher accepts your work, then handles production, distribution, and (some) marketing — often via an agent. It offers credibility, professional support, and wider reach, but is highly competitive (**gatekeepers** accept only a small fraction), slow, and gives the author less control and a smaller share of revenue. **Self-publishing** means publishing yourself (especially easy now with digital and print-on-demand) — you keep control and more revenue, but must handle (or arrange) editing, design, and marketing yourself.

## Card: A transformed landscape
Publishing has been transformed: **self-publishing** is now a legitimate, viable route, not a last resort, and many writers succeed with it. Neither path is "better" — it depends on your goals, genre, and willingness to handle the business side. But **realistic expectations** matter for both: most books sell modestly, success usually takes persistence and often marketing effort, and overnight success is rare. Getting published is more achievable than ever, but building a readership still takes work whichever route you choose.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking traditional publishing is the only "real" way (self-publishing is now legitimate and viable); assuming self-publishing means no work (you handle editing, design, and marketing); expecting easy success or instant riches (most books sell modestly; it takes persistence); and not matching the route to your goals. Understand both paths realistically and choose what fits your situation and ambitions.

## Card: Quick recap
Getting published means getting your work to readers, via two main routes: traditional publishing (a publisher handles production and distribution — credible and supported, but competitive, slow, and less control) or self-publishing (you publish yourself — full control and more revenue, but you handle editing, design, and marketing). Self-publishing is now legitimate and viable. Neither is "better"; it depends on your goals. Keep realistic expectations — success takes persistence.
