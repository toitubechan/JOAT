## Card: What it is
image: logosIdentityHero
A **logo** is a symbol or design that represents a brand, and **visual identity** is the broader set of visual elements — logo, colors, fonts, and style — that make a brand recognizable. A logo isn't the whole brand (which lives in perception), but it's a key part of how a brand presents itself. Understanding logos and visual identity reveals the craft behind brand recognition.

## Card: Why it matters
Logos and visual identity are how brands become recognizable and memorable — a powerful and valuable thing. Understanding them helps you appreciate good (and bad) branding, create a coherent visual identity for any project, and grasp how recognition is built. It connects design skills to the practical world of brands and how they present themselves visually.

## Card: Key terms
- **Logo**: a symbol or design representing a brand.
- **Visual identity**: the full set of visual brand elements.
- **Recognition**: being easily identified and remembered.
- **Consistency**: using identity elements coherently everywhere.

## Card: Logos and good design
A **logo** is a symbol or design that represents a brand at a glance. Good logos are usually *simple, memorable, and distinctive* — easy to recognize and recall, and versatile enough to work everywhere (small or large, color or black-and-white). Contrary to common belief, effective logos are often simple rather than elaborate — simplicity aids recognition and memorability. A logo's job is to be a clear, recognizable symbol of the brand, not to cram in lots of detail.

## Card: The whole visual identity
image: visualIdentitySystemHero
A logo is just one part of a brand's **visual identity** — the full set of visual elements: the logo, color palette, fonts (typography), imagery style, and overall look. The key to a strong identity is **consistency** — using these elements coherently across everything (products, website, packaging, communications). Consistent visual identity builds **recognition**: people come to instantly recognize and associate the look with the brand. It's the consistent whole, not just the logo, that creates a recognizable brand presence.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking a logo *is* the brand (the brand is the whole perception; the logo represents it); believing logos should be elaborate (simple, memorable ones usually work best); focusing only on the logo and ignoring the broader visual identity; and being inconsistent (which undermines recognition). A logo is a simple, distinctive symbol; visual identity is the consistent whole that builds recognition.

## Card: Quick recap
A logo is a symbol representing a brand — best when simple, memorable, and distinctive (simplicity aids recognition). Visual identity is the broader set of elements: logo, colors, fonts, imagery, and style. The key to a strong identity is consistency — using these coherently everywhere builds recognition. A logo isn't the whole brand (which lives in perception), but it and the consistent visual identity are how a brand becomes recognizable.
