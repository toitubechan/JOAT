## Card: What it is
image: contractMistakesHero
People get caught out by contracts in a handful of predictable ways. This card set gathers the most common contract mistakes — so you can avoid them. Most are simple to sidestep with a little awareness, yet they cost people money and stress regularly. (General guidance, not legal advice.)

## Card: Why it matters
Contract mistakes can be expensive and hard to undo once you're bound. Knowing the common ones helps you avoid the traps that catch so many people. Since these errors are predictable and largely preventable, awareness is a genuinely valuable, money-saving form of life-admin literacy.

## Card: Key terms
- **Signing blind**: agreeing without reading or understanding.
- **Verbal promises**: assurances not written into the contract.
- **Auto-renewal trap**: being re-locked by automatic renewal.
- **Record-keeping**: keeping copies and documentation.

## Card: The big contract mistakes
The most common and costly: **signing blind** (not reading or understanding before agreeing); relying on **verbal promises** that aren't written in (if it's not in the contract, it may not count — get it in writing); falling into the **auto-renewal trap** (forgetting to cancel and being locked in again); and ignoring fees, penalties, and exit terms until it's too late.

## Card: Records and assumptions
image: keepContractCopy
Two more: poor **record-keeping** — not keeping a copy of what you signed, so you can't prove the terms later. And making assumptions — assuming terms are standard or fair without checking, or assuming you can easily exit. Read what's actually written, keep the documentation, and verify rather than assume. These habits prevent disputes and nasty surprises down the line.

## Card: Common mistakes
To gather them: signing without reading; trusting verbal promises not in writing; getting caught by auto-renewal; ignoring fees and exit terms; not keeping a copy; and assuming rather than checking the actual terms. Nearly all are prevented by the same habits: read carefully, get promises in writing, keep records, and verify before committing.

## Card: Quick recap
Common contract mistakes: signing blind, trusting verbal promises not written in, getting caught by auto-renewal, ignoring fees and exit terms, not keeping a copy, and assuming instead of checking. They're costly but predictable and preventable. Read carefully, get key promises in writing, keep records, and verify before you commit.
