## Card: What it is
image: recipeFailHero
**Why recipes fail** is about understanding the common reasons baking goes wrong — and they're usually predictable and fixable, not mysterious bad luck. Most baking failures come from a handful of recurring causes. Understanding these helps you troubleshoot, prevent problems, and bake more reliably. It turns baking mishaps from frustrating mysteries into solvable, understandable issues.

## Card: Why it matters
Baking failures are discouraging, and many people give up thinking they're "bad at baking." But most failures have specific, common causes that anyone can learn to avoid. Understanding why recipes fail empowers you to diagnose and fix problems, bake more reliably, and stop blaming yourself for predictable, preventable issues. It's the troubleshooting knowledge that builds baking confidence.

## Card: Key terms
- **Measuring accurately**: a top factor in baking success.
- **Oven temperature**: a frequent hidden cause of failures.
- **Ingredient issues**: wrong, old, or wrongly substituted ingredients.
- **Following the method**: doing steps correctly and in order.

## Card: The usual suspects
image: bakingTroubleshootingHero
Most baking failures trace to a few common causes. **Measuring inaccurately** — since baking depends on balanced ratios, sloppy measuring throws off the chemistry. **Oven temperature** — ovens are often inaccurate (running hotter or cooler than the dial says), a frequent hidden cause of over- or under-baking. **Ingredient issues** — using the wrong ingredient, old/expired leavening (which won't make things rise), or bad substitutions. And not **following the method** correctly — skipping steps, wrong order, or wrong technique. These few causes explain most failures.

## Card: Troubleshooting and prevention
Because failures are usually predictable, you can troubleshoot and prevent them. If a bake fails, consider: Did I measure accurately? Is my oven actually at the right temperature (an oven thermometer reveals this)? Were my ingredients correct and fresh (especially leavening)? Did I follow the method properly? Checking these usually identifies the cause. Preventing failures means measuring carefully, knowing your oven, using fresh correct ingredients, and following the recipe's method — which makes baking far more reliable.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: treating baking failures as mysterious bad luck or personal inability (they usually have specific, fixable causes); not measuring accurately; assuming your oven matches its dial (it often doesn't); using old leavening agents (which won't rise); and not following the method. Most failures come from a few predictable causes — understanding them lets you troubleshoot and bake reliably.

## Card: Quick recap
Most baking failures aren't bad luck — they trace to a few common, fixable causes: inaccurate measuring (baking needs balanced ratios), incorrect oven temperature (ovens often differ from their dial), ingredient issues (wrong, old, or badly substituted — especially expired leavening), and not following the method correctly. Checking these diagnoses most problems. Measuring carefully, knowing your oven, using fresh ingredients, and following the recipe makes baking reliable.
