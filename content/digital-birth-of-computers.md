## Card: What it is
image: histDigitalBirthOfComputersHero
The **Digital Age** began with the invention and rapid evolution of the **computer** — machines that process information using code. In a few decades, computers went from room-sized, rare machines to devices in nearly every pocket. This is the story of one of the fastest, most transformative turning points in history.

## Card: Why it matters
Computers underpin virtually everything in modern life — work, communication, money, entertainment, and more. Understanding how they arose and evolved explains the foundation of the digital world we inhabit. It also reveals just how *recent* and *rapid* this transformation was, which puts the pace of modern change in perspective.

## Card: Key terms
- **Computer**: a machine that processes information using instructions.
- **Hardware and software**: the physical machine and the programs it runs.
- **Miniaturization**: shrinking technology over time.
- **Moore's law**: the trend of computing power roughly doubling regularly.

## Card: From room-sized to pocket-sized
image: histDigitalBirthOfComputersFromRoomSizedToPocketSizedHero
The first electronic **computers** were enormous — filling entire rooms, costly, and rare. Then came an astonishing trend of **miniaturization**: computers got smaller, cheaper, and vastly more powerful with each passing year. Captured loosely by **Moore's law** (computing power roughly doubling at regular intervals), this rapid improvement shrank room-sized machines into laptops and then phones within a single lifetime.

## Card: Hardware and software
A computer has two sides: **hardware** (the physical machine — chips, memory, screen) and **software** (the programs and instructions that tell it what to do). The explosion of both — ever-better hardware and ever-more-capable software — drove the digital revolution. Understanding this split clarifies how the same kind of machine can do countless different things, just by running different software.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking computers have been around a long time (electronic computers are very recent); underrating how astonishingly fast they improved (room-sized to pocket-sized within a lifetime); confusing hardware and software; and not realizing today's phone is a powerful computer. The computer revolution was remarkably recent and rapid.

## Card: Quick recap
The Digital Age began with computers — machines processing information via hardware and software. Early computers filled rooms; through rapid miniaturization (loosely, Moore's law), they became smaller, cheaper, and vastly more powerful, shrinking into laptops and phones within a lifetime. This remarkably recent, rapid evolution laid the foundation of the modern digital world.
