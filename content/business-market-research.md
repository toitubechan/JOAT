## Card: What it is
image: marketResearchHero
**Market research** is gathering information about your potential customers, competitors, and market — so you make decisions based on reality rather than guesses. It helps you understand who your customers are, what they want, and what you're up against. Understanding the basics helps any business (or aspiring entrepreneur) make smarter, evidence-based decisions.

## Card: Why it matters
Businesses fail when they misunderstand their market — building for customers who don't exist, mispricing, or ignoring competitors. Market research replaces assumptions with real understanding, dramatically reducing risk. It informs nearly every business decision, from what to build to how to price and position it. It's how you ground a business in reality rather than hope.

## Card: Key terms
- **Market research**: gathering information about customers, competitors, and the market.
- **Target customer**: the specific people a business serves.
- **Competitor analysis**: studying who else serves the market.
- **Primary vs. secondary research**: gathering new info vs. using existing info.

## Card: Know your customer and competitors
image: customerCompetitorHero
Two pillars of market research. First, understand your **target customer** — who they are, what they need and want, what they'll pay, and how they behave. Vague "everyone" is not a target; specific understanding is. Second, **competitor analysis** — who else serves this market, what they offer, their strengths and weaknesses, and how you'll be different or better. Knowing both your customers and competitors grounds your decisions in reality.

## Card: How to research
Market research can be **primary** (gathering new information yourself — surveys, interviews, talking to potential customers, testing) or **secondary** (using existing information — reports, data, studying competitors, online research). You don't need a huge budget: even talking to real potential customers and studying competitors closely is valuable research. The goal is replacing guesses with genuine insight about who you're serving and what the market is really like.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: skipping research and relying on assumptions; defining your customer as "everyone" (too vague to serve well); ignoring competitors; only seeking information that confirms your hopes; and thinking research requires a big budget (talking to customers and studying competitors is cheap and valuable). Market research grounds decisions in reality — know your specific customer and your competition.

## Card: Quick recap
Market research gathers real information about your customers, competitors, and market, replacing guesses with understanding. Its two pillars: knowing your specific target customer (their needs, wants, and behavior) and analyzing competitors (who else serves the market and how you'll differ). It can be primary (new info you gather) or secondary (existing info), and needn't be expensive. It grounds business decisions in reality, reducing risk.
