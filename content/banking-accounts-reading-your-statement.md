## Card: What it is
image: bankStatementHero
A **bank statement** is a summary of everything that happened in your account over a period — usually a month. It lists money in, money out, and where you ended up. It's like a diary of your account, and learning to read it turns a confusing list of numbers into a clear picture.

## Card: Why it matters
Checking your statement is how you catch mistakes, spot fraud, and notice spending patterns. Banks make errors, and thieves test stolen cards with small charges hoping you won't look. A two-minute scan each month is your simplest defense — and a reality check on where your money actually goes.

## Card: Key terms
- **Opening balance**: what you had at the start of the period.
- **Closing balance**: what you had at the end.
- **Transaction**: any single movement of money, in or out.
- **Available balance**: what you can actually spend right now.

## Card: Balances explained
Your statement shows an **opening balance** and a **closing balance** — the start and end points. But day to day, watch your **available balance**: it's what you can truly spend now, which may be less than the total if some payments are still clearing. The headline number isn't always the spendable one.

## Card: Reading transactions
image: transactionReviewHero
Each **transaction** line shows a date, a description, and an amount — usually with money out and money in shown separately or with a minus sign. Descriptions can be cryptic (an odd company name behind a familiar shop). If a charge looks unfamiliar, search the name before panicking, then contact the bank if it's truly unknown.

## Card: Common mistakes
The biggest mistake is never reading the statement at all, so errors and fraud go unnoticed for months. Another is trusting the top-line balance while pending payments are still due to clear, then overspending. Lastly, people ignore small mystery charges — exactly the ones scammers use to test a stolen card.

## Card: Quick recap
A statement records your account's activity: opening and closing balances, plus every transaction. Watch the available balance for what's truly spendable, scan transactions for anything unfamiliar, and never skip the review. A quick monthly check catches errors and fraud early.
