## Card: What it is
image: climateChangeHero
**Climate change** refers to long-term shifts in Earth's climate, especially the warming trend driven largely by human activity. It's one of the most discussed scientific topics, and understanding the basic mechanism — without the noise — helps you grasp what's actually happening and why scientists are concerned.

## Card: Why it matters
Climate change affects weather patterns, sea levels, ecosystems, and societies worldwide. It's widely discussed but often poorly understood amid noise and confusion. Grasping the core science — the greenhouse effect and the evidence — lets you understand the issue clearly. This is established science presented as general knowledge.

## Card: Key terms
- **Greenhouse effect**: gases trapping heat in the atmosphere.
- **Greenhouse gases**: gases like carbon dioxide that trap heat.
- **Global warming**: the long-term rise in average temperatures.
- **Fossil fuels**: coal, oil, and gas that release CO₂ when burned.

## Card: The greenhouse effect
image: greenhouseEffectHero
The core mechanism is the **greenhouse effect**. Certain **greenhouse gases** (like carbon dioxide) in the atmosphere trap heat from the Sun, like a blanket around the Earth. This is natural and necessary — without it, Earth would be frozen. The issue is that human activity has *increased* these gases, trapping *more* heat and warming the planet beyond its natural balance.

## Card: The human role and evidence
Burning **fossil fuels** (coal, oil, gas) for energy releases large amounts of carbon dioxide, thickening the heat-trapping blanket. The scientific evidence — rising global average temperatures, melting ice, rising seas, shifting patterns — is extensive, and the scientific consensus is strong that recent warming is largely human-caused. This is **global warming**, and it drives broader climate change.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: confusing a cold day with disproof (that's weather vs. climate); thinking the greenhouse effect is entirely bad (it's natural and necessary — the problem is the *excess*); assuming it's scientifically uncertain (the consensus is strong); and feeling it's too abstract to grasp, when the core mechanism is genuinely simple: more greenhouse gases trap more heat.

## Card: Quick recap
Climate change is long-term shifts in Earth's climate, mainly a warming trend driven by human activity. The mechanism is the greenhouse effect: gases like CO₂ trap heat, and burning fossil fuels has increased them, trapping more heat. The natural greenhouse effect is necessary; the problem is the human-caused excess, supported by strong scientific evidence.
