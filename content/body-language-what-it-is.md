## Card: What it is
image: bodyLanguageBasicsHero
**Body language** is the non-verbal communication we send through posture, gestures, facial expressions, eye contact, and tone. A large part of how we communicate and form impressions happens without words. Understanding it helps you read others better and become more aware of the signals you send.

## Card: Why it matters
We're constantly sending and reading body language, usually unconsciously. It shapes first impressions, signals confidence or nervousness, and can support or contradict our words. Becoming more aware of it improves how you come across and how well you understand others — a quiet but powerful communication skill.

## Card: Key terms
- **Body language**: communication through non-verbal signals.
- **Non-verbal**: anything communicated without words.
- **Congruence**: when words and body language match.
- **Cluster**: reading several signals together, not one alone.

## Card: We communicate without words
Much of communication is **non-verbal** — how we say things and what our bodies do, alongside the words themselves. A confident posture, a genuine smile, or crossed arms all send messages. Often these signals are unconscious, which is why body language can reveal what words conceal, and why it's worth learning to notice.

## Card: Read clusters, not single signals
image: bodyLanguageClustersHero
A crucial principle: never read one signal in isolation. Crossed arms might mean defensiveness — or the person is just cold. Meaning comes from **clusters** (several signals together) and **context**. Look for patterns, consider the situation, and watch for changes from someone's normal behavior. Reading a single gesture as a definitive "tell" is the most common body-language error.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: reading single signals as definitive (crossed arms always means X); ignoring context (cold, tired, the setting); assuming body language is a precise science (it's suggestive, not certain); and only thinking about reading others, while ignoring the signals *you* send. Read clusters in context, and stay humble about certainty.

## Card: Quick recap
Body language is the non-verbal communication of posture, gestures, expression, eye contact, and tone — a large part of how we connect and impress. Read it in clusters and context, never as single definitive "tells," and stay aware of the signals you send too. It's suggestive, not an exact science.
