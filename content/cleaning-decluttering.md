## Card: What it is
image: declutterHero
**Decluttering** is removing the things you don't need or use, so your space holds less stuff. It's the natural partner to cleaning: the fewer items you own, the less there is to tidy, dust around, and organize. Decluttering tackles the *cause* of mess, not just the symptom.

## Card: Why it matters
A cluttered home is harder to clean, easier to lose things in, and quietly stressful — visual mess weighs on the mind. Decluttering makes every future clean faster and a space feel calmer and bigger. It's often the highest-impact thing you can do for a home, and it's free.

## Card: Key terms
- **Declutter**: remove items you don't need or use.
- **One-in-one-out**: bring in a new item only as one leaves.
- **Donate / discard**: give away or dispose of unneeded items.
- **Clutter magnet**: a surface that attracts random pile-ups.

## Card: A simple method
image: keepDonateDiscard
Work in small areas, not the whole house at once. For each item, ask: do I use it, need it, or truly love it? If not, **donate or discard** it. Sort into keep, donate, and bin. Doing one drawer or shelf at a time makes a daunting task achievable and builds momentum without burnout.

## Card: Keep clutter from returning
Decluttering once isn't enough if stuff flows back in. The **one-in-one-out** rule keeps things balanced: bring in a new item only when one leaves. Give frequently used things a designated home so they don't pile up, and clear **clutter magnets** (that one chair or counter) regularly before they overflow.

## Card: Common mistakes
The first mistake is trying to declutter the entire home in one heroic session, then burning out. Another is keeping things out of guilt or "might need it someday," which defeats the purpose. And many declutter once but never adopt habits like one-in-one-out, so the clutter quietly creeps back within months.

## Card: Quick recap
Decluttering removes what you don't use, making cleaning faster and your space calmer. Work in small areas, ask if each item earns its place, and donate or discard the rest. Prevent re-cluttering with one-in-one-out and designated homes for things. Less stuff is the simplest route to an easier home.
