## Card: What it is
image: moleculesCompoundsHero
Atoms rarely exist alone — they join together to form **molecules** and **compounds**. When atoms bond, they create substances with completely new properties. This combining is how a hundred-odd elements produce the millions of different materials around us, from water to DNA.

## Card: Why it matters
Almost everything you encounter is a combination of atoms, not lone ones. Understanding how atoms join — and that the result can be utterly different from its parts — explains why water, salt, and air are what they are. It's the step from "atoms exist" to "here's how they make the actual world."

## Card: Key terms
- **Molecule**: two or more atoms bonded together.
- **Compound**: a substance of different elements bonded together.
- **Chemical bond**: the link holding atoms together.
- **Formula**: a shorthand like H₂O for a substance's atoms.

## Card: Atoms bond into molecules
A **molecule** is two or more atoms joined by **chemical bonds**. A **compound** is specifically made of *different* elements bonded together. We write them with a **formula**: water is H₂O (two hydrogen atoms, one oxygen). Atoms bond because it makes them more stable — this drive to bond is what builds all the substances around us.

## Card: The whole differs from the parts
image: compoundNewProperties
A striking idea: a compound's properties can be totally different from the elements that form it. Water (H₂O) is a life-giving liquid, yet it's made from hydrogen and oxygen — both gases, and hydrogen is flammable. Table salt is made from sodium (a metal that reacts violently with water) and chlorine (a poisonous gas), yet together they're harmless seasoning. Bonding transforms.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking a compound keeps the properties of its parts (it usually doesn't); confusing a mixture (substances just mingled, like sand and water) with a compound (atoms actually bonded); and not realizing how few elements produce so many substances through combination. Bonding creates genuinely new materials.

## Card: Quick recap
Atoms bond to form molecules and compounds, written with formulas like H₂O. A compound's properties can be completely different from its component elements — water from two gases, harmless salt from a violent metal and a poison gas. This combining is how a hundred elements build the millions of materials in the world.
