## Card: What it is
image: howworksInternetPhysicalHero
The **internet** feels invisible and wireless, but it's built on very real physical infrastructure: vast networks of cables (including giant ones under the oceans), data centers full of computers, and equipment routing data worldwide. Understanding the physical internet demystifies the "cloud" and reveals the massive hidden machinery behind every click.

## Card: Why it matters
We use the internet constantly while imagining it as something ethereal "in the air" or "the cloud." Knowing it's built on physical cables and machines reveals how it actually works, why it can fail, and where your data really travels. It's a striking reality-check on technology we take for granted.

## Card: Key terms
- **Undersea cables**: cables on the ocean floor carrying most global data.
- **Data center**: a building full of servers storing and serving data.
- **Infrastructure**: the physical equipment the internet runs on.
- **The cloud**: data stored on remote physical servers.

## Card: Cables, including under the oceans
image: howworksInternetPhysicalCablesIncludingUnderTheOceansHero
Most internet data doesn't travel by satellite or "through the air" — it travels through physical cables. Astonishingly, the backbone of the global internet is a network of **undersea cables** lying on the ocean floor, carrying the vast majority of international data between continents. On land, more cables connect cities, buildings, and homes. The internet is, at its core, an immense physical web of wires spanning the planet.

## Card: Data centers and the "cloud"
The **cloud** sounds ethereal, but it's just **data centers** — huge buildings packed with thousands of computers (servers), consuming enormous power, located around the world. When you store something "in the cloud" or use an online service, your data lives on these physical machines. Equipment like routers and switches direct data along the cables between you and these data centers. The "cloud" is very much made of concrete, metal, and electricity.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking the internet is mostly wireless or satellite (most data travels through physical cables, including undersea ones); imagining "the cloud" is ethereal (it's physical data centers); not realizing how much hidden infrastructure exists; and assuming data travels invisibly through the air. The internet is a massive, real, physical machine spanning the globe.

## Card: Quick recap
The internet is built on physical infrastructure: vast cable networks (including giant undersea cables carrying most international data), and data centers — huge buildings of servers that are the real "cloud." Routers and switches direct data along these cables. Far from being ethereal or wireless, the internet is an immense physical web of wires, machines, and electricity spanning the planet.
