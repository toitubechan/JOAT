## Card: What it is
image: financeCreditGettingOutOfDebtHero
**Getting out of debt** means paying off what you owe in a planned, deliberate way rather than drifting. It starts with seeing the full picture — every debt, its balance, and its interest rate — then attacking them in a smart order. With a method, even big debt becomes a series of manageable steps.

## Card: Why it matters
Debt with high interest grows against you every day you carry it, quietly eating money you could keep. A clear payoff plan turns a vague, stressful weight into a finish line you can see. People who follow a method pay off debt faster and spend far less on interest than those who pay randomly.

## Card: Key terms
- **Avalanche method**: pay highest-interest debt first.
- **Snowball method**: pay smallest balance first for quick wins.
- **Consolidation**: combining debts into one, often lower-rate, loan.
- **Extra payment**: anything paid above the minimum.

## Card: Avalanche vs. snowball
image: financeCreditGettingOutOfDebtAvalancheVsSnowballHero
Two popular methods. The **avalanche** targets the highest **interest rate** first — mathematically cheapest, saving the most money. The **snowball** targets the smallest balance first — less efficient, but the quick wins build momentum and motivation. Both work; pick the one you'll actually stick with, since consistency matters more than perfection.

## Card: The mechanics
List every debt with its balance and rate. Always pay at least the minimum on all of them to avoid penalties, then throw every spare euro (your **extra payment**) at the one target debt. When it's cleared, roll that freed-up money onto the next. **Consolidation** can help too — merging debts into one lower-rate loan simplifies and may cut interest.

## Card: Common mistakes
The biggest mistake is paying down debt while still adding new debt — like bailing a boat with a hole in it. Another is draining your entire emergency fund to clear debt, leaving you to borrow again at the next surprise. And many stop after early progress; the plan only works if you see it through to zero.

## Card: Quick recap
Getting out of debt starts with listing every balance and rate, then attacking one at a time — avalanche (highest rate) to save most, or snowball (smallest balance) for motivation. Pay minimums on the rest, stop adding new debt, keep a small cushion, and roll freed-up money forward until you're clear.
