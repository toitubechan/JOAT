## Card: What it is
image: movingOutHero
**Moving out** of a rental properly means giving correct notice, leaving the place in good condition, and following the right steps to get your deposit back. Done well, it ends your tenancy cleanly with your money and references intact. Done badly, it can cost you your deposit. (General guidance; rules vary by location.)

## Card: Why it matters
The end of a tenancy is where deposit disputes happen and where rushing causes costly mistakes. Knowing how to move out properly protects your deposit (often a large sum), secures a good reference, and avoids penalties for incorrect notice. A little care at this stage saves real money and hassle.

## Card: Key terms
- **Notice period**: how much warning you must give before leaving.
- **End-of-tenancy clean**: leaving the place clean and tidy.
- **Final inspection**: the landlord's check of the property's condition.
- **Deposit return**: getting your deposit back, minus valid deductions.

## Card: Give correct notice
First, give the correct **notice period** in the right way (usually in writing) as set out in your lease — leaving without proper notice can mean penalties or lost deposit. Don't just move out early or assume verbal notice is enough. Check your agreement for exactly how much notice is required and how it must be given. Getting this right is the foundation of a clean exit.

## Card: Leave it right and protect your deposit
image: cleanExitPhotos
To maximize your **deposit return**: do a thorough **end-of-tenancy clean**, repair any damage you caused (beyond fair wear and tear), and return it to the condition shown in your move-in inventory. Take timestamped photos of the cleaned, empty property as proof. Attend the **final inspection** if you can, and get any agreed deductions in writing. This documentation is your defense against unfair charges.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: not giving proper notice (penalties or lost deposit); leaving the place dirty or damaged (deposit deductions); not taking move-out photos as proof; not comparing against the move-in inventory; and not getting deduction disputes resolved in writing. Give correct notice, clean thoroughly, document everything, and you'll exit cleanly with your deposit.

## Card: Quick recap
Move out properly by giving correct written notice per your lease, doing a thorough end-of-tenancy clean, repairing damage you caused, and returning the place to its move-in condition. Take timestamped photos as proof, attend the final inspection, and get any deductions in writing. Careful exit protects your deposit and secures a good reference.
