## Card: What it is
image: marketingBrandingHero
A **brand** is far more than a logo or name — it's the overall *perception* and *feeling* people have about a business, product, or person. It's what people think and feel when they encounter you. Branding is the deliberate shaping of that perception. Understanding what a brand truly is reframes it from a visual design into something deeper and more powerful.

## Card: Why it matters
A strong brand builds trust, recognition, loyalty, and the ability to stand out and even charge more. Branding shapes how people perceive and choose between options. Understanding it helps any business (or individual) build a meaningful identity rather than just a logo. It's one of the most valuable, and most misunderstood, assets a business can have.

## Card: Key terms
- **Brand**: the overall perception and feeling people have about you.
- **Brand identity**: the visible elements (logo, colors, voice).
- **Reputation**: what people come to expect and trust.
- **Consistency**: a coherent brand across every touchpoint.

## Card: A brand is a perception
image: marketingBrandingABrandIsAPerceptionHero
The crucial insight: a **brand** isn't your logo — it's the *perception and feeling* people hold about you. Your logo, colors, and name (your **brand identity**) are tools that *express* the brand, but the brand itself lives in people's minds: what they associate with you, expect from you, and feel about you. As the saying goes, "your brand is what people say about you when you're not in the room." It's your **reputation** made deliberate.

## Card: Built through consistency
A strong brand is built through **consistency** — delivering a coherent identity, message, and experience across every touchpoint (your product, communication, service, visuals, and how you treat people), over time. Every interaction shapes the perception. Consistency builds recognition and trust: people know what to expect from you. A brand isn't created by a logo alone but earned gradually through consistent, repeated experiences that shape how people think and feel about you.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking a brand is just a logo or visual design (it's the whole perception in people's minds); believing you can simply *declare* your brand rather than earn it through experiences; being inconsistent across touchpoints (which confuses the perception); and ignoring that every interaction shapes your brand. A brand is the feeling and reputation people hold about you, built through consistency over time.

## Card: Quick recap
A brand isn't your logo — it's the overall perception and feeling people have about you, your reputation made deliberate ("what people say about you when you're not in the room"). Visual elements (logo, colors) express the brand, but the brand itself lives in people's minds. Strong brands are built through consistency across every touchpoint over time. Understanding this reframes branding from visual design into shaping perception.
