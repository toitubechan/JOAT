## Card: What it is
image: bankingOnlineSafetyHero
**Online and mobile banking** lets you manage money from your phone or computer — checking balances, paying bills, sending transfers. It's fast and convenient, but it also makes your account a target for fraud. A few simple habits keep the convenience without the risk.

## Card: Why it matters
Most banking fraud today doesn't involve breaking the bank's security — it involves tricking *you* into handing over access. The bank protects its systems; your job is to protect your login and stay alert to scams. Good habits here are the single biggest factor in keeping your money safe online.

## Card: Key terms
- **Two-factor authentication (2FA)**: a second check, like a code, on top of your password.
- **Phishing**: fake messages designed to steal your login or details.
- **Secure connection**: an encrypted link, shown by a padlock and "https".
- **Official app**: the bank's real app from a trusted app store.

## Card: Lock down access
Use a strong, unique password for your bank and turn on **two-factor authentication** so a stolen password alone isn't enough. Only log in through the bank's **official app** or by typing the web address yourself. Avoid banking on public Wi-Fi without a trusted connection, and never save your banking password in a shared browser.

## Card: Spot the scams
image: bankScamSpottingHero
**Phishing** messages pretend to be your bank, creating panic — "suspicious activity, click now" — to make you act before thinking. Real banks never ask for your full password or a 2FA code by message or phone. If a message pushes urgency or asks you to confirm credentials, stop. Contact the bank using the number on its official site or card.

## Card: Common mistakes
The biggest mistake is clicking links in unexpected "bank" messages instead of opening the app yourself. Another is reading out a verification code to a caller — that code is the key to your account, and no real bank employee needs it. Reusing the same password across sites is a third easy way to get caught out.

## Card: Quick recap
Online banking is safe if you guard access: strong unique password, two-factor on, official app only. Scammers attack you, not the bank — so treat urgent messages with suspicion, never share passwords or codes, and reach the bank through its official contacts when in doubt.
