## Card: What it is
image: realestateWhatToCheckHero
**Checking a property before buying** (due diligence) means thoroughly investigating it *before* committing to one of life's biggest purchases. Many costly surprises can be avoided by knowing what to look into. Understanding the key things to check helps you avoid expensive mistakes and buy with confidence. This is general educational knowledge, not professional advice.

## Card: Why it matters
Buying property is hugely expensive, and problems discovered *after* buying can cost enormous amounts or be impossible to fix. Thorough checks beforehand are your protection. Understanding what to investigate helps you avoid nasty (and costly) surprises, negotiate better, and make a sound decision. A little diligence up front can save a fortune and much stress later.

## Card: Key terms
- **Due diligence**: thorough checking before committing.
- **Inspection / survey**: a professional check of the property's condition.
- **Hidden problems**: issues not visible at a glance.
- **The surrounding area**: factors beyond the property itself.

## Card: Check the property itself
image: realestateWhatToCheckCheckThePropertyItselfHero
Investigate the property's actual **condition** — not just how it looks on a nice viewing. A professional **inspection or survey** can reveal **hidden problems** (structural issues, damp, faulty systems, needed repairs) that aren't obvious but can be very costly. Check the essentials work, the age and state of key systems, and any signs of trouble. The viewing shows the surface; due diligence uncovers what's beneath, which is where expensive surprises hide.

## Card: Check beyond the property
Look beyond the building too. Investigate **the surrounding area**: the neighborhood, noise, safety, amenities, transport, and future developments (a planned construction nearby could affect value or livability). Check the legal and financial side: ownership, any legal issues, ongoing costs (taxes, fees, maintenance), and how the price compares to similar properties. Understanding the full picture — property, area, legal, and financial — is what real due diligence means.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: relying on a nice viewing without a proper inspection (hidden problems can be very costly); ignoring the surrounding area and future developments; not checking the legal and financial details; rushing the decision under pressure; and skipping due diligence to save a little time or money up front. Thorough checking before buying protects you from far larger costs and regrets later.

## Card: Quick recap
Before buying property, do thorough due diligence: check the property's real condition (a professional inspection can reveal costly hidden problems beyond a nice viewing); investigate the surrounding area (neighborhood, amenities, future developments); and check the legal and financial details (ownership, ongoing costs, fair price). Buying is hugely expensive and problems found afterward can be devastating — diligence up front saves a fortune and much stress.
