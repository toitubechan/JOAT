## Card: What it is
image: bodyddFiveSensesHero
The **five senses** — sight, hearing, smell, taste, and touch — are how your body detects the world and sends that information to the brain. Each sense uses specialized organs to turn things like light, sound, and chemicals into signals the brain can interpret. Your entire experience of reality comes through them.

## Card: Why it matters
The senses are your only windows to the world — everything you know about your surroundings comes through them. Understanding how they work reveals that perception isn't passive recording but active interpretation by the brain, which explains illusions and why senses can be fooled. It's fascinating and foundational to understanding experience itself.

## Card: Key terms
- **Senses**: systems detecting aspects of the world.
- **Sense organs**: eyes, ears, nose, tongue, skin.
- **Stimulus**: something a sense detects (light, sound, etc.).
- **Perception**: the brain's interpretation of sense signals.

## Card: Detecting the world
Each sense has specialized **sense organs** that detect a particular kind of **stimulus** and convert it into nerve signals: **eyes** detect light (sight), **ears** detect sound vibrations (hearing), the **nose** detects airborne chemicals (smell), the **tongue** detects chemicals in food (taste), and the **skin** detects pressure, temperature, and pain (touch). These signals travel to the brain to be made sense of.

## Card: Perception is interpretation
image: perceptionInterpretationHero
A key insight: your senses don't directly show you reality — they send raw signals, and the brain *interprets* them into your experience (**perception**). The brain fills gaps, makes assumptions, and can be fooled, which is why optical illusions work. Taste and smell are deeply linked (much of "taste" is actually smell). So what you perceive is the brain's best construction from sense data, not a perfect recording.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking senses passively record reality exactly (the brain actively interprets, and can be fooled); believing there are strictly only five senses (we also sense balance, body position, and more); not knowing taste and smell are linked; and assuming perception is flawless. Your experience is the brain's interpretation of sense signals, not raw reality.

## Card: Quick recap
The five senses — sight, hearing, smell, taste, touch — use specialized organs to detect stimuli (light, sound, chemicals, pressure) and convert them into nerve signals for the brain. Crucially, the brain *interprets* these signals into perception, filling gaps and sometimes being fooled (hence illusions). Taste and smell are linked, and we have other senses too. Perception is interpretation, not raw recording.
