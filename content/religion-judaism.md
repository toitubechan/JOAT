## Card: What it is
image: religionJudaismHero
**Judaism** is one of the world's oldest religions and the first major **monotheistic** faith. Centered on the relationship (covenant) between God and the Jewish people, and on living according to its teachings, it's the root from which Christianity and Islam also grew. This is a respectful, general overview — descriptive, not promoting or judging.

## Card: Why it matters
Though smaller in numbers than Christianity or Islam, Judaism is historically enormous — the oldest of the Abrahamic faiths and the source of ideas central to all three. It has profoundly shaped Western civilization, ethics, and culture. Understanding its basics is key to grasping world history, the Abrahamic religions, and Jewish identity and culture.

## Card: Key terms
- **Judaism**: one of the oldest monotheistic religions.
- **Monotheism**: belief in one God (a Jewish contribution to history).
- **Torah**: the central Jewish sacred text.
- **Covenant**: the special relationship between God and the Jewish people.

## Card: Core beliefs
image: religionJudaismCoreBeliefsHero
Judaism is centered on belief in one God and the **covenant** — a special relationship and set of obligations between God and the Jewish people. A core text is the **Torah** (the first part of the Hebrew Bible), containing teachings and laws for how to live. Judaism emphasizes living ethically and according to these teachings, with study, tradition, and community central to the faith. It's notable as the first major monotheistic religion in history.

## Card: A people and a tradition
Judaism is both a religion and the heritage of a people, intertwining faith, culture, history, and identity. It has different movements and levels of observance (from more traditional to more liberal), and rich traditions of study, festivals, and practice. As the oldest Abrahamic faith, it gave the world influential ideas — including monotheism itself and ethical teachings — that shaped Christianity, Islam, and Western thought broadly.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking Judaism is just an "older version" of Christianity (it's a distinct faith with its own beliefs and ongoing tradition); treating it as uniform (it has varied movements and observance); confusing Jewish ethnicity, culture, and religion (they're intertwined but not identical); and underrating its historical influence. Judaism is a distinct, ancient, living faith and heritage, best understood respectfully.

## Card: Quick recap
Judaism is one of the oldest religions and the first major monotheistic faith, centered on belief in one God and the covenant between God and the Jewish people, with the Torah as a core text emphasizing ethical living. It's both a religion and a people's heritage, with varied movements and rich traditions. As the oldest Abrahamic faith, it shaped Christianity, Islam, and Western thought. Best understood respectfully as a distinct, living tradition.
