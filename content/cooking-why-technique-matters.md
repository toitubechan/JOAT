## Card: What it is
image: cookingTechniqueHero
A **cooking technique** is the *method* you use to apply heat to food — frying, boiling, roasting, and so on. The same ingredient becomes wildly different depending on the technique. Learning a handful of core methods is more useful than memorizing endless recipes, because techniques work across countless dishes.

## Card: Why it matters
Once you understand techniques, you can cook *without* a recipe, improvise with what you have, and fix dishes that go wrong. Recipes teach you one dish; techniques teach you to cook. This is the shift from anxiously following instructions to confidently understanding what you're doing and why.

## Card: Key terms
- **Technique**: the method of applying heat to food.
- **Dry heat**: cooking with hot air, fat, or radiation (roasting, frying).
- **Moist heat**: cooking with water or steam (boiling, steaming).
- **Heat control**: managing how hot and how fast you cook.

## Card: Two big families of heat
image: heatFamilies
Most techniques fall into two families. **Dry heat** (roasting, frying, grilling) uses hot air, fat, or direct heat — it browns food and creates rich flavor. **Moist heat** (boiling, steaming, braising) uses water or steam — it's gentler and good for tenderizing. Knowing which family suits a food is half the battle.

## Card: Heat control is everything
The most underrated skill is **heat control**. The same pan can gently soften an onion or burn it black — the difference is the heat level and your attention. Many cooking failures are really heat failures: too hot and food burns outside while raw inside; too low and it steams instead of browning. Mastering heat transforms your cooking.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: chasing recipes without understanding the underlying technique, so you can't adapt or troubleshoot; cooking everything on high heat, causing burning; and overcrowding the pan, which drops the temperature and steams food instead of browning it. Understanding *why* each step happens prevents all of these.

## Card: Quick recap
Cooking techniques are methods of applying heat, split into dry heat (browning, rich flavor) and moist heat (gentle, tenderizing). Learning core techniques beats memorizing recipes, because they transfer everywhere. And heat control is the master skill — most failures are really heat failures in disguise.
