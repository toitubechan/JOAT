## Card: What it is
image: bodyLanguageSettingsHero
Body language isn't one-size-fits-all — what works in a job interview differs from a casual chat or a video call. Understanding how to **adapt your signals to different settings** helps you come across appropriately, whether you're meeting a manager, making friends, or presenting on screen.

## Card: Why it matters
The same body language can read very differently depending on the context and culture. Awareness of the setting helps you avoid sending the wrong signal — too casual in a formal setting, too stiff in a relaxed one. Adapting appropriately makes you more effective and comfortable across the varied situations of life.

## Card: Key terms
- **Context**: the situation and its norms.
- **Cultural differences**: how signals vary across cultures.
- **Formal vs. informal**: more reserved vs. relaxed settings.
- **Virtual body language**: how you come across on video calls.

## Card: Formal vs. informal, and culture
Match your signals to the **context**. **Formal** settings (interviews, important meetings) call for more composed, attentive body language; **informal** ones (friends, casual chats) allow relaxed warmth. And mind **cultural differences** — eye contact, personal space, gestures, and touch norms vary significantly across cultures, so what's friendly in one may be rude in another. When unsure, observe and follow local norms.

## Card: Body language on video calls
image: videoCallBodyLanguageHero
**Virtual body language** is its own skill, now that so much happens on video. Tips: look toward the camera (not just the screen) to approximate eye contact, sit at a good distance and framing, ensure decent lighting so your face is visible, and remember your expressions and nods still matter — maybe more, since other cues are reduced. On video, a little extra expressiveness helps connection.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: using the same body language regardless of setting; being too casual in formal contexts or too stiff in relaxed ones; ignoring cultural norms around eye contact, space, and gestures; and neglecting video-call presence (looking off-camera, poor lighting, flat expression). Read the setting and adapt accordingly.

## Card: Quick recap
Adapt your body language to the setting: composed and attentive in formal contexts, relaxed and warm in informal ones, and respectful of cultural norms (which vary widely). On video calls, look toward the camera, frame and light yourself well, and be a little more expressive. Read the situation and adjust.
