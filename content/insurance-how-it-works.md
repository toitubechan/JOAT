## Card: What it is
image: financeInsuranceHowItWorksHero
Behind every insurance policy are a few moving parts that decide what you pay and what you get back. The main ones are the **premium** (what you pay in), the **deductible** (what you pay before cover kicks in), and the **claim** (how you actually collect). Knowing these makes any policy far less confusing.

## Card: Why it matters
These pieces determine the real cost and value of a policy — not just the headline price. Two policies with the same premium can behave very differently when you actually need them. Understanding the mechanics helps you compare fairly and avoid nasty surprises at the worst possible moment.

## Card: Key terms
- **Premium**: the regular payment to keep cover active.
- **Deductible / excess**: what you pay yourself before the insurer pays.
- **Claim**: your request for payment after a covered loss.
- **Payout limit**: the maximum the insurer will pay.

## Card: Premium and deductible
image: financeInsuranceHowItWorksPremiumAndDeductibleHero
The **premium** is your ongoing cost. The **deductible** (sometimes called the excess) is the slice you cover yourself before the insurer pays the rest. They trade off: a higher deductible usually means a lower premium, and vice versa. Choosing means balancing what you can pay monthly against what you could afford in one hit.

## Card: How a claim works
When a covered event happens, you file a **claim** — notify the insurer, provide details and proof (photos, receipts, reports), and they assess it. If approved, they pay out, minus your deductible and up to the **payout limit**. The smoother your documentation, the faster and easier this usually goes.

## Card: Common mistakes
A common mistake is choosing the lowest premium without noticing a sky-high deductible you couldn't actually afford to pay. Another is not keeping proof — receipts, photos — making claims harder. And many don't read the **payout limit**, assuming "covered" means "fully covered," when there may be a cap.

## Card: Quick recap
Insurance runs on premiums (what you pay in), deductibles (what you pay before cover starts), and claims (how you collect, up to a payout limit). Higher deductibles lower premiums but cost more when you claim. Keep proof, know your limits, and compare the whole package — not just the monthly price.
