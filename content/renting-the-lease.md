## Card: What it is
image: leaseHero
The **lease** (or tenancy agreement) is the contract between you and your landlord. It sets out the rent, the term, the rules, and each side's responsibilities. It's a binding legal document, so understanding it before signing is crucial. (General guidance; tenancy law varies by location.)

## Card: Why it matters
The lease governs your entire tenancy — what you pay, what you can and can't do, and what happens if things go wrong. Signing without understanding can trap you in unfair terms or unexpected obligations. Knowing what to look for protects you throughout your time as a tenant. It's one of the most important documents you'll sign.

## Card: Key terms
- **Lease / tenancy agreement**: the rental contract.
- **Term**: how long the tenancy lasts.
- **Rent and increases**: the cost and how it can change.
- **Responsibilities**: who handles repairs, bills, and upkeep.

## Card: Key things in a lease
image: leaseTerms
Check the essentials: the **rent** (amount, when due, and how/when it can increase), the **term** (fixed period or rolling, and notice required to leave), and **responsibilities** — who pays which bills, who handles repairs and maintenance, and rules (pets, guests, decorating, subletting). These determine your day-to-day life and costs as a tenant, so read them before signing.

## Card: Watch the exit and the rules
Pay special attention to: how to end the tenancy (notice periods, and penalties for leaving early during a fixed term), conditions for getting your deposit back, and any restrictive rules you must follow. Like any contract, a lease binds you — so understand the exit terms and rules *before* signing, when you can still negotiate or walk away, not after you're committed.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: signing the lease without reading it fully; not checking how and when rent can increase; missing the rules (pets, subletting) then breaching them; not understanding the notice required to leave or early-exit penalties; and not clarifying who's responsible for repairs and bills. Read the lease carefully and ask about anything unclear before signing.

## Card: Quick recap
The lease is your binding rental contract: it sets the rent (and how it can rise), the term and notice to leave, responsibilities for repairs and bills, and the rules. Pay special attention to exit terms, deposit conditions, and restrictions. Read it fully and clarify anything unclear *before* signing — it governs your whole tenancy.
