## Card: What it is
image: passwordKeyHero
A **password** is the secret that proves an online account is yours. It's the key to your digital life — email, banking, photos, social media. Because so much of life now lives behind passwords, how you create and manage them is one of the most important security skills you can have.

## Card: Why it matters
A weak or reused password is the most common way accounts get hacked. And one breached account often leads to others, especially your email — which can reset passwords for everything else. Getting passwords right protects your money, privacy, and identity. It's the single highest-impact thing most people can do for online safety.

## Card: Key terms
- **Password**: the secret key to an account.
- **Data breach**: when a company's stored passwords are stolen.
- **Reused password**: the same password across multiple accounts.
- **Credential stuffing**: hackers trying stolen passwords on other sites.

## Card: Why reuse is so dangerous
image: passwordDominoRisk
The biggest password mistake is **reuse**. When any site suffers a **data breach** and passwords leak, attackers try those same email-and-password combos on other sites — a tactic called **credential stuffing**. If you reused that password, your other accounts fall like dominoes. One leak shouldn't unlock your whole life.

## Card: Email is the master key
Your **email account** is special: it can reset the password of almost every other account. If someone gets into your email, they can take over everything linked to it. That makes your email password the most critical of all — it deserves your strongest password and extra protection.

## Card: Common mistakes
The top mistakes: reusing the same password everywhere; using weak, guessable ones (names, "123456"); never changing a password even after a known breach; and protecting trivial accounts well while neglecting the email account that controls them all. Each leaves an easy door open for attackers.

## Card: Quick recap
Passwords are the keys to your digital life, and reusing them is the most dangerous habit — one breach can unlock many accounts via credential stuffing. Your email is the master key, so it needs the strongest protection. Good password habits are the highest-impact step in online safety.
