## Card: What it is
image: pricingProductHero
**Pricing** is deciding what to charge for your product or service — one of the most important and often mishandled business decisions. Price affects revenue, profit, how customers perceive you, and whether the business survives. Understanding the basics of pricing helps you avoid the common trap of pricing too low and build a sustainable business.

## Card: Why it matters
Price directly determines whether a business makes money — and it's frequently set wrong, especially too low. Good pricing can make a business thrive; poor pricing can sink even a great product. Understanding how to think about price helps you capture fair value, stay profitable, and avoid undervaluing what you offer. It's a high-impact decision worth getting right.

## Card: Key terms
- **Pricing**: deciding what to charge.
- **Value-based pricing**: pricing by the value delivered to the customer.
- **Cost-plus pricing**: pricing based on costs plus a margin.
- **Underpricing**: charging too little to be sustainable.

## Card: Price reflects value, not just cost
image: valuePricingHero
A key insight: price isn't just "cost plus a bit" (**cost-plus pricing**). Better is **value-based pricing** — charging based on the *value* your product delivers to the customer. Something cheap to produce can command a high price if it delivers great value or solves a painful problem. Price should reflect what the offering is *worth to the customer*, not only what it costs you to make. Customers pay for value, not your costs.

## Card: The danger of pricing too low
**Underpricing** is one of the most common business mistakes. New businesses often charge too little, out of fear or to win customers — but this can mean not enough profit to survive, exhausting work for little reward, and even signaling low quality (very cheap can imply "cheap"). Pricing too low is often more dangerous than pricing a bit high. Charge enough to be sustainable and to reflect genuine value — don't undervalue what you offer.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: pricing too low out of fear (underpricing is a top killer); pricing only on cost rather than value to the customer; assuming the cheapest price wins (very low prices can signal low quality and erode profit); and not researching what the market will bear. Price for value and sustainability — don't undervalue your offering, and remember customers pay for the value they get.

## Card: Quick recap
Pricing — what to charge — is a high-impact business decision often set wrong, especially too low. Price should reflect the *value* delivered to the customer (value-based), not just your costs (cost-plus). Underpricing is a top business killer: it can mean too little profit, exhausting work, and signaling low quality. Charge enough to be sustainable and to reflect genuine value — don't undervalue what you offer.
