## Card: What it is
image: typographyBasicsHero
**Typography** is the art of arranging text — choosing typefaces (fonts), sizes, spacing, and layout so text is both readable and visually effective. It's everywhere (every word you read is set in some typography), yet usually invisible when done well. Understanding the basics reveals a hidden craft that hugely affects how text looks, reads, and feels.

## Card: Why it matters
Typography shapes how we read and perceive text — it affects readability, tone, and impression, often subconsciously. Good typography makes text clear and pleasing; poor typography makes it hard to read or unprofessional. Understanding the basics helps you create better documents, designs, and presentations, and appreciate a craft that's everywhere yet rarely noticed. It's a high-impact, learnable skill.

## Card: Key terms
- **Typography**: the art of arranging text.
- **Typeface (font)**: the design of the letters.
- **Readability**: how easy text is to read.
- **Hierarchy**: using type to show what's most important.

## Card: Fonts and readability
A core element is the **typeface** (font) — the design of the letters, which carries tone (some feel formal, others casual, modern, or playful). But the most important goal of typography is usually **readability**: text that's easy and comfortable to read. This depends on font choice, size, line spacing, line length, and contrast with the background. Good typography prioritizes clear, comfortable reading — which is why the best typography often goes unnoticed, simply letting you read with ease.

## Card: Type creates hierarchy and tone
image: typeHierarchyHero
Typography also creates **hierarchy** — using size, weight (bold), and style to show what's most important and guide the reader (headings stand out, body text is calm and readable). This structures information so readers navigate it easily. And type sets *tone* — the right typeface and styling convey formality, friendliness, modernity, or seriousness, shaping the impression before a word is read. Effective typography balances readability, clear hierarchy, and appropriate tone.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: choosing fonts purely for looks while ignoring readability; using too many different fonts (cluttered and unprofessional); poor spacing or cramped text (hard to read); no clear hierarchy (everything looks the same, nothing guides the eye); and a tone that doesn't fit the content. Good typography prioritizes readability, clear hierarchy, and appropriate tone — usually keeping things simple and consistent.

## Card: Quick recap
Typography is the art of arranging text — choosing fonts, sizes, spacing, and layout. Its top priority is usually readability (clear, comfortable reading), which is why the best typography goes unnoticed. It also creates hierarchy (size and weight showing what's important and guiding the reader) and sets tone (the right typeface conveys formality, friendliness, or seriousness). Good typography balances readability, hierarchy, and tone — a high-impact, learnable craft.
