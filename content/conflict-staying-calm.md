## Card: What it is
image: stayingCalmConflictHero
Conflicts trigger strong emotions, and **staying calm** under that pressure is the foundation of resolving them well. When emotions take over, we say things we regret and escalate the situation. Managing your own state — staying composed even when provoked — is what makes everything else in conflict resolution possible.

## Card: Why it matters
In the heat of conflict, your emotional brain can hijack your rational one, leading to reactions that make things worse. Staying calm keeps you thinking clearly, prevents escalation, and models the tone you want. You can't resolve a conflict well while flooded with anger — calm is the prerequisite for everything else.

## Card: Key terms
- **Emotional flooding**: being overwhelmed by strong emotion.
- **The pause**: taking a moment before reacting.
- **Trigger**: something that sparks a strong emotional reaction.
- **Self-regulation**: managing your own emotional state.

## Card: Don't react while flooded
image: floodedPauseHero
When conflict triggers anger or hurt, you can experience **emotional flooding** — overwhelmed, with clear thinking shut down. Reacting in this state almost always makes things worse. The single most powerful tool is **the pause**: take a breath, or even step away ("let's continue this in a few minutes"), until you can think clearly again. Responding calmly beats reacting hotly, every time.

## Card: Self-regulation in the moment
**Self-regulation** — managing your own state — can be practiced: slow your breathing, notice your **triggers** rising, and remind yourself of your goal (resolving the issue, not winning). Lowering your own voice and pace often calms the whole interaction, since emotions are contagious. Staying calm isn't weakness; it's the control that lets you handle conflict skillfully.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: reacting instantly while flooded with emotion; raising your voice to match the other person, escalating things; refusing to take a break when overwhelmed; and suppressing emotion so hard it bursts out later. The fix: notice when you're triggered, pause before responding, and step away if needed to cool down.

## Card: Quick recap
Staying calm is the foundation of conflict resolution — you can't resolve well while flooded with emotion. Use the pause: breathe, or step away, before reacting. Practice self-regulation by noticing your triggers and lowering your voice and pace, which calms the whole interaction. Responding calmly always beats reacting hotly.
