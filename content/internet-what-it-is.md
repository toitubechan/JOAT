## Card: What it is
image: internetNetworkHero
The **internet** is a giant network connecting billions of computers around the world, letting them share information. It's not a single thing in one place — it's a web of cables, wireless links, and machines all agreeing to talk in the same way. When you go online, you're joining this worldwide conversation.

## Card: Why it matters
The internet underpins how we communicate, work, shop, learn, and relax. Understanding its basics demystifies the technology you use constantly, helps you troubleshoot problems, and lays the groundwork for using it safely. You don't need to be technical — just to grasp the simple core idea.

## Card: Key terms
- **Network**: computers connected to share information.
- **Internet**: the global network of networks.
- **Server**: a computer that stores and serves information.
- **Device / client**: your phone or computer that requests it.

## Card: A network of networks
image: networkOfNetworks
The internet is best understood as a **network of networks**. Your home Wi-Fi is a small network; your internet provider connects it to bigger ones, which link to others worldwide. No one owns the whole internet — it's countless networks cooperating, using shared rules so a message can hop across the globe and arrive intact.

## Card: Clients and servers
Most online activity is a conversation between two roles. Your **device** (the **client**) *requests* something — a web page, a video. A **server** (a powerful always-on computer somewhere) *sends it back*. When you open an app or site, your device is asking a server for information, and the server is answering.

## Card: Common mistakes
A common confusion is thinking the internet lives "in the air" or in one place — it's mostly physical cables (including huge ones under oceans) plus machines. Another is mixing up the internet (the network) with the web or with apps (things that run *on* it). And many imagine their data just floats around, rather than traveling a real route.

## Card: Quick recap
The internet is a global network of networks — billions of devices connected by cables and wireless links, all using shared rules to share information. Your device (client) requests things; servers send them back. No one owns it; it's cooperating networks. It's physical infrastructure, not magic in the air.
