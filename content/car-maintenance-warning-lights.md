## Card: What it is
image: warningLightsHero
The symbols that light up on your dashboard are your car talking to you. **Warning lights** flag everything from a loose fuel cap to a serious engine problem. Knowing the basics — especially which colors mean "stop now" — helps you respond correctly instead of panicking or ignoring them.

## Card: Why it matters
Ignoring a warning light can turn a cheap fix into a wrecked engine, or a minor issue into a dangerous one. But overreacting to a harmless light causes needless stress. Understanding the color code tells you the difference between "check soon" and "pull over immediately."

## Card: Key terms
- **Warning light**: a dashboard symbol signaling a condition.
- **Red light**: usually means stop safely as soon as possible.
- **Amber / yellow light**: a caution; get it checked soon.
- **Check engine light**: a general engine or emissions warning.

## Card: The color code
image: redAmberCode
Colors work like traffic lights. **Red** means a serious problem — stop safely as soon as you can (for example, oil pressure or overheating). **Amber/yellow** means caution — something needs attention soon but isn't an instant emergency. **Green or blue** are just information, like headlights being on. Color first tells you urgency.

## Card: Common lights to know
A few you'll likely meet: the **check engine light** (a general fault — get it diagnosed), the oil pressure light (red — stop, low oil can wreck the engine), the temperature/overheating warning (red — stop and let it cool), the battery light, and the brake warning. Your manual explains every symbol for your specific car.

## Card: Common mistakes
The dangerous mistake is ignoring a **red** light and driving on — overheating or low oil pressure can destroy an engine in minutes. The opposite is panicking at an amber light and braking hard in traffic. People also reset or cover lights to make them go away, hiding a problem that's still there.

## Card: Quick recap
Dashboard warning lights signal conditions by color: red means stop safely soon, amber means check it soon, green/blue are just info. Learn the key ones — check engine, oil, temperature — and consult your manual. Never ignore a red light or hide a warning; respond to the urgency the color signals.
