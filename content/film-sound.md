## Card: What it is
image: filmSoundHero
**Sound in film** — dialogue, music, sound effects, and ambience — is half of the cinematic experience, yet it's the most overlooked. Often said to be "half the film," sound profoundly shapes emotion, atmosphere, and meaning. Understanding it reveals that what we *hear* is as crafted and powerful as what we *see*, even though viewers rarely consciously notice it.

## Card: Why it matters
Sound has an enormous, largely subconscious effect on how we experience film — music and sound create much of the emotion and tension we feel. It's often said good sound is half the experience, yet it's the element viewers least notice. Understanding it deepens appreciation and is essential for video makers (poor sound ruins footage faster than poor visuals). It's a hidden powerhouse of filmmaking.

## Card: Key terms
- **Sound design**: the crafting of all sound in a film.
- **Score / music**: music composed or chosen to shape emotion.
- **Sound effects**: created sounds that make the world feel real.
- **Ambience**: background sound that builds atmosphere.

## Card: The layers of film sound
image: filmSoundTheLayersOfFilmSoundHero
Film sound has several layers, all deliberately crafted (**sound design**). *Dialogue* — what characters say. **Music** (the score) — perhaps sound's most powerful emotional tool, shaping how scenes feel (think how music makes a scene tense, sad, or triumphant). **Sound effects** — created sounds (footsteps, doors, action) that make the world feel real and immersive. And **ambience** — background sound that builds atmosphere and place. These layers combine to create the full, immersive soundscape of a film.

## Card: Sound shapes emotion invisibly
The remarkable thing about film sound is how powerfully it shapes our emotions while we barely notice it. Music especially does enormous emotional work — the *same* scene feels completely different with different music (tense, joyful, scary, sad). Sound effects and ambience make worlds feel real and immersive. Much of what we feel watching a film comes from sound we're not consciously attending to. This is why sound is often called half the film — invisible, but emotionally central.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common misunderstandings: thinking film is mostly visual and sound is minor (sound is often called half the experience); not noticing how much music shapes emotion; underrating sound effects and ambience for immersion; and (for makers) neglecting sound, when poor sound ruins footage faster than poor visuals. Sound — dialogue, music, effects, and ambience — is a hidden powerhouse that profoundly shapes emotion and immersion.
