## Card: What it is
image: potsPansHero
**Pots and pans** are where most cooking happens, and different types suit different jobs. Understanding a few basics — which pan for which task, and how to use and care for them — makes cooking easier and helps your cookware last for years instead of warping or losing its coating.

## Card: Why it matters
Using the wrong pan, or using one badly, causes sticking, uneven cooking, and ruined cookware. Knowing the basics means food cooks properly and your pans stay in good shape. Since good cookware isn't cheap, treating it correctly saves real money over time.

## Card: Key terms
- **Frying pan / skillet**: a flat, wide pan for frying and searing.
- **Saucepan**: a deeper pan for sauces, simmering, and small batches.
- **Non-stick**: a coated pan food doesn't stick to.
- **Preheating**: warming the pan before adding food.

## Card: The right pan for the job
image: panTaskMatch
A **frying pan** (skillet) is wide and flat — ideal for frying, searing, and sautéing where you want surfaces exposed to heat. A **saucepan** is deeper — for sauces, simmering, boiling small amounts. A large pot handles pasta and soups. Matching the pan to the task affects how evenly and well food cooks.

## Card: Using and caring for non-stick
**Non-stick** pans are great for delicate foods like eggs, but the coating is fragile. Protect it: use medium heat (high heat damages the coating), use wooden or silicone utensils (not metal, which scratches), and avoid harsh scrubbing. Treated gently, a non-stick pan lasts; abused, its coating fails within months.

## Card: Common mistakes
Common mistakes: not **preheating** a pan, so food sticks; cranking non-stick pans to high heat, ruining the coating; using metal utensils on non-stick surfaces; using a too-small pan and overcrowding; and putting hot pans straight into cold water, which can warp them. Gentle, correct use keeps cookware performing for years.

## Card: Quick recap
Match the pan to the task: a wide frying pan for searing, a saucepan for simmering, a pot for pasta and soups. Preheat pans so food doesn't stick. Protect non-stick coatings with medium heat and soft utensils, and don't shock hot pans with cold water. Good care makes cookware last.
