## Card: What it is
image: financeTaxesCommonMistakesHero
Most tax trouble comes from a few avoidable mistakes — not from sneaky rules. This subtopic collects the common ones, from overpaying through missed deductions to landing penalties through carelessness. None require expertise to avoid; they just require a little awareness and organization.

## Card: Why it matters
Tax mistakes cost real money in two directions: overpaying what you didn't owe, or underpaying and facing penalties. Both are avoidable. A little knowledge keeps more money in your pocket and keeps you out of trouble with the tax authority — a stress few people enjoy.

## Card: Key terms
- **Deduction / allowance**: amounts that legally reduce your tax.
- **Penalty**: a fine for late or incorrect tax handling.
- **Record-keeping**: storing documents that support your return.
- **Tax avoidance vs. evasion**: legal reduction vs. illegal dodging.

## Card: Leaving money on the table
image: financeTaxesCommonMistakesLeavingMoneyOnTheTableHero
The quietest mistake is overpaying by missing **deductions and allowances** you're entitled to — work expenses, certain contributions, tax-free amounts. The system rarely volunteers these; you usually have to claim them. A little research, or a professional's help, can reveal savings you've been ignoring for years.

## Card: Avoidance vs. evasion
There's a crucial line. **Tax avoidance** is legally arranging your affairs to pay less — using allowances and reliefs as intended. **Tax evasion** is illegally hiding income or lying, which carries serious penalties. Reducing tax through legitimate means is fine; dishonesty is not. Know the difference and stay firmly on the legal side.

## Card: Common mistakes
Beyond those: missing deadlines and racking up **penalties**; poor **record-keeping**, so you can't back up your claims; assuming someone else (an employer or app) handled everything without checking; and panicking and ignoring tax letters, which only makes problems grow. Open the letter, keep records, and meet the dates.

## Card: Quick recap
Common tax mistakes: overpaying via missed deductions, missing deadlines and earning penalties, weak record-keeping, and blurring the legal line. Claim what you're entitled to, keep documents, file on time, and never confuse legal avoidance with illegal evasion. When unsure, a tax professional is worth it.
